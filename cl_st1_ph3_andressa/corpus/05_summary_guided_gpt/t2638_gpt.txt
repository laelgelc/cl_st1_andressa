I grew up with an extremely abusive, narcissistic mother. Because of trauma bonding, I kept going back to her, kept sharing things with her that felt important or special to me. It’s like I couldn’t stop handing her these “secrets,” hoping she’d finally treat me like someone worth caring about.

Back in 2013, when I was on welfare, I taught her about cryptocurrency. She invested $30k. It turned into over $2 million. When I was later homeless and living out of my car, I asked her for help. She flat-out refused and told me to just get a low-wage job. Knowing she had millions and still chose to watch me sleep in a car broke something in me.

Recently, a doctor mentioned vitamin D and how it can help with mood. I tried it, and the change in my depression was almost overnight. It felt like someone turned the lights back on in my brain.

Now I catch myself doing the same thing again: running around telling people about this “secret” that helped me so much. I’ll get excited and share it with strangers, but they usually don’t care, don’t respond, or don’t reciprocate with anything meaningful.

I don’t understand why I’m so driven to give away helpful information to people who don’t care about me, or why I still expect some kind of connection or gratitude in return. It makes me feel foolish and used, and I don’t know how to stop repeating this pattern.
