People talk about being alone like it’s automatically a bad thing, like it’s proof that something is wrong with you. I don’t really see it that way anymore. I think loneliness and pain are part of being alive, and as awful as they feel, they’re not meaningless.

I do historical fencing, and getting hit is just part of it. You get bruised, you get knocked down, you mess up your timing and you pay for it. Sometimes it really hurts. But you learn that pain is temporary. You ice the bruise, you rest, you adjust your stance or your guard, and you go back in. Every hit teaches you something, even if it’s just “don’t leave that opening again.”

I’ve started to see emotional pain the same way. Grief, regret, sadness, insecurity, heartbreak, creative failure, watching people you love get taken by cancer… none of that is clean or easy. You can’t just “move on” like people say. You don’t go *out* of pain; you go *through* it. You carry it, you stumble with it, you curse it, and slowly, it shapes you.

There’s nothing weak about needing time alone to heal. It’s okay to step back, to sit with your hurt, to admit that you’re not okay. It’s okay to be the person who’s still grieving years later, or still ashamed of mistakes, or still trying to make something meaningful and feeling like you’re failing.

But please don’t confuse hurting with being worthless.

You still matter, even if you feel broken. Your value doesn’t vanish because you’re struggling, or because you’re lonely, or because life has hit you harder than you think you can stand. Life is a brutal teacher, but it’s still a blessing to be here at all, to still have the chance to try again, to love again, to create again.

If you’re in that dark place right now, keep fighting. Take your time. Protect your heart, but don’t give up on it. You’re allowed to hurt. You’re allowed to rest. And you’re allowed to believe that, even in all this pain, your life is still worth living.
