I’ve been thinking a lot about how I relate to other people and how different it feels from what seems “normal.” I don’t really enjoy socializing. I don’t get that emotional comfort or happiness people talk about from hanging out, talking for hours, or having big friend groups. Most of the time, I just feel indifferent.

I’m pretty sure a lot of this comes from how I grew up. My mom was very controlling, and I saw her as abusive and unfair. My parents didn’t come to my school events, didn’t really celebrate milestones like graduations or birthdays, and the general atmosphere at home was pretty neglectful. On top of that, they constantly devalued my friendships, criticized the people I liked, and tried to limit my social life whenever they could.

Somewhere along the way, I just… detached. I’ve grown distant from people in general. Close friendships, celebrations, birthdays – they all feel unnecessary to me now. I don’t really feel a pull toward intimate relationships either. Being a loner usually doesn’t bother me; it actually feels easier and safer.

But every now and then, I catch myself wondering why I’m like this, why I feel so different from how others describe their need for connection. I’m curious if anyone else has gone through something similar – especially if you had a neglectful or controlling upbringing and ended up feeling this kind of emotional distance from socializing and relationships.
