I’m 19 and on paper I guess I’m supposed to feel lucky. I’m tall, people tell me I’m good-looking, I do well in school, I’m not stupid. But none of that seems to matter when I look in the mirror or walk into a room. I feel ugly, awkward, wrong. It’s like there’s this huge gap between how I “should” feel about myself and how I actually do, and I’m stuck in that gap.

I can’t seem to keep friends. People talk to me for a bit, then drift away, and it’s like I just vanish from their minds. I honestly feel like most people forget I exist as soon as I’m not standing right in front of them. I don’t have anyone I’d call a close friend, no one I can message when something good or bad happens. I don’t really have memories with people, just a bunch of days that all blur together because there was no one there to share them with.

My brothers are the complete opposite. They’re outgoing, social, always surrounded by people, always invited somewhere. I watch them and think, “Why can’t I just be normal like that? Why is it so easy for them and so hard for me?” I don’t even want a huge social circle, just a few genuine friends who actually care if I’m around.

I overthink everything. If someone doesn’t respond right away, I assume I said something wrong. If people laugh near me, I’m sure they’re laughing at me. I constantly feel like I’m being judged, even when logically I know most people probably aren’t thinking about me at all. It makes me shut down even more, and then I end up even more alone.

I feel like I’m just existing, not really living. I look back on my life so far and all I can think is how badly I wish I could redo it, be different, make different choices, somehow come out as a different person. I don’t even know who I am now. I feel empty and kind of hopeless, like if I died tomorrow, no one would really remember me for long. They’d just move on, and it would be like I was never really here.
