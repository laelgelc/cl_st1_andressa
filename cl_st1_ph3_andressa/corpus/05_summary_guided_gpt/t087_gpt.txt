I was bullied pretty much my entire childhood and teens, and it wrecked whatever social skills I might’ve developed. I’ve never really had what I’d call a “real” friend. As an adult, I just kind of… opted out. I live with very minimal social interaction and, most of the time, I’m actually okay with that. I’m used to being on my own, and I’ve built a lot of pride around being self‑sufficient and not needing anyone.

But every now and then it hits me how one‑sided things have always been. I’ve spent so much of my life being the person who listens, who comforts, who tries to be there for others when they’re falling apart. And when I finally get desperate enough to reach out myself, I get dismissed, ignored, or flat-out rejected. It’s like the moment I need something back, I become inconvenient.

I don’t need a huge friend group or constant attention. I just wish there was one person who genuinely cared, who would actually listen when I’m struggling, comfort me without making me feel like a burden, and tell me that things will be okay. I’m tired of feeling like I have to carry everything by myself all the time. I’m tired of always being the strong, self-sufficient one when, deep down, I just want someone to be that for me, even once.
