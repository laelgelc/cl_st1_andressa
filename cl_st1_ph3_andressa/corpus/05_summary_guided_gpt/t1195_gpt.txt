I’m usually the kind of person who spends most of my time alone – either at home or working night shifts. I’ve never been in a relationship, don’t really have a social life, and the holidays are always just… another couple of days to get through.

This past Christmas and New Year’s I ended up working over 100 hours. Literally just work and sleep, nothing else going on. When it was finally over, I realized I had some money saved and absolutely nothing in my life to look forward to. On a bit of a whim, I booked a solo trip to a luxury all‑inclusive resort in Mexico.

It was weird at first. The place was full of couples and groups of friends, and being the lone person at a table or walking around by myself definitely made the loneliness sting sometimes. But honestly, I still had a really good time. I read on the beach, ate good food, had drinks, visited Mayan ruins, and even had an entire golf course to myself one day. It was peaceful. For a while, I felt like a “normal” person doing normal vacation things, not just the lonely night‑shift worker.

I’m not saying a trip fixes loneliness, and I know not everyone can afford something like that. But if you ever get the chance, and you’ve got no one to go with, I just wanted to say: go anyway. Treat yourself if you can. Being lonely didn’t magically disappear, but it felt good to give myself something nice and step outside my usual empty routine.
