I’m in my 30s now, but a lot of my life has been shaped by growing up with a chronic lung disease. It made it hard to fit in, to keep up, and to feel “normal.” I spent a lot of time on the outside looking in, thinking about what loneliness actually is and why some of us seem to get stuck in it.

Over time I’ve become more comfortable in my own skin and a bit better at building community around me. That’s made me even more curious about where loneliness comes from—personally and culturally—and what we could change to make our modern lives more community-oriented and less isolating.

I’m trying to gather people’s experiences with loneliness to better understand the patterns, the roots, and maybe some ways forward. If you’re willing, I’d really appreciate it if you filled out a survey I’ve put together. I may also reach out to some people for interviews if they’re open to sharing more of their story.
