I’ve spent most of my life feeling like I don’t belong anywhere and I never really understood why. I’ve always been the one to pull away, to isolate, and I told myself it was just who I was. Lately I’ve realized there’s something deeper going on: I’m terrified of hurting people.

Somewhere along the way I decided everyone else’s well-being mattered more than mine. So whenever someone got close, whenever they showed real interest in me, I panicked. I’d convince myself that if I stayed, I’d ruin their life somehow. The “kindest” thing I could do was leave.

Of course, that’s the cruel irony. By running away to avoid hurting people, I ended up hurting them anyway. I’ve abandoned friends, let down my family, and broken lovers’ hearts. Every time I disappear, it just feeds this image I have of myself as a threat, as someone dangerous to care about. And then the cycle repeats.

I’m only just starting to see this pattern clearly. I don’t know how to fix it yet, but I want to stop drowning in shame and guilt. I want to be able to apologize to the people I’ve hurt, even if only in my own mind, and one day look back on those relationships with some kind of warmth and gratitude instead of just regret.
