I feel like I’ve spent my whole life being left behind by people who were supposed to love me.

My dad left when I was a baby and took my brother with him. He never contacted me or my mom again. No calls, no messages, nothing. It’s like I never existed to him. Then as I got older, my mom slowly stopped really being there too. There was no official “I’m giving you up,” but she basically left me with my grandma to raise me. My grandma was the only real stability I had.

At school I could never make friends, no matter how hard I tried. People would tell me I was pretty, but it didn’t matter because I couldn’t talk to anyone properly. I always felt socially broken somehow, like I missed some step everyone else learned as kids.

Now I’m in college and living with my mom again because my grandma moved away, and that really messed me up. I don’t like my mom, but I barely see her anyway because she’s either at work or out with her friends. I’m stuck in this remote area, can’t drive because no one ever taught me, and I can’t get a job. I’ve never had friends, never had a relationship. People tell me I’m not their “type,” and I feel like I still look like I’m 12.

I hate college, I hate living with my mom, and I feel completely trapped. I keep thinking the military might be my only way out, and if that doesn’t work, my mind goes to really dark places, including suicide.

Has anyone else grown up like this—alone, but not by choice? Like your whole life was just people slowly walking away and you never got to be part of anything?
