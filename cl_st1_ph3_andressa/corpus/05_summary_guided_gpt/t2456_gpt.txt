I’m coming out of a pretty rough stretch – a few years of grieving a parent and dealing with other heavy stuff – and for the first time in a long while I actually feel like celebrating. It’s a weird feeling after being in survival mode for so long.

The problem is: I don’t really know how to celebrate in a way that doesn’t end up making me feel worse. I’m used to doing things alone and, honestly, I usually prefer it to being around people who don’t really get me or make me feel uncomfortable. But when I go out to loud, social places where everyone’s paired up or in groups, it becomes intolerable. I end up feeling like I’m just standing there, hyper-aware of being alone, wasting time and money.

Most of the people I “know” are guys I’ve met through dating apps, and those connections are mostly online and pretty shallow. I don’t want that kind of dynamic anymore; it doesn’t feel right for where I’m at.

So I’m thinking of celebrating by doing something just for me: maybe a day at a ski resort or a spa day, plus some side work and studying. Something quiet, intentional. I guess I’m just wondering if anyone else has been in this in-between space—wanting to celebrate, but not wanting to be around people—and how you handled it.
