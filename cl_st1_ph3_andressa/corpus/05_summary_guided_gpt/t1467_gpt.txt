I’m 26M and I feel like I’ve lived two totally separate lives for as long as I can remember.

At home growing up, everything was chaos. My mom has always been mentally unstable and she went through a lot in a short time: cheating, divorce, someone close dying, our house literally burning down, and trying to raise three kids who were all close in age. I was the “overly sensitive” kid, the one who cried or reacted, and I kept getting told to stop being so emotional. Eventually I just… did. I shut it off as much as I could.

Now it’s like I built these walls so high that no one can see in. My friends know basically nothing about what my home life was like, and my family knows almost nothing about my life outside. I’m always the calm, collected, “strong” one. When I’ve tried to open up before, people brushed it off because it didn’t fit their idea of me. “You’re fine, you’re tough, you’ll handle it.” So I learned to stop trying.

These days I’m exhausted by it. During arguments or misunderstandings, people act like whatever we’re fighting about is my only problem, and I just stand there knowing they have no idea what’s going on in my head. I want someone to actually know me, to understand why I am the way I am. But I’m scared that if I share, they’ll see me differently, or pity me, or just get uncomfortable and pull away.

I’ve made some new friends who are genuinely kind, and I still can’t bring myself to open up. So I stay quiet, smile, joke around, and inside I feel completely alone. The thoughts I’m having lately are getting darker and more worrying, and I feel this weird sense of running out of time, like something bad is going to happen if I keep going like this.

I don’t really know what I’m looking for. I just needed to say this somewhere.
