I’m 18 and just finished my first semester of college. I got my GPA back and it’s 2.93/5. To keep my scholarship I need at least a 4.07 next semester. Seeing that number made my stomach drop.

All through high school I was “the smart one.” I got As, medals, awards. I pushed myself and it felt worth it. Now I’m at my dream university, in my dream major… and it’s like my motivation just vanished. I don’t recognize myself. I’m so close to actually failing something for the first time and I have no idea how to cope with that.

My mental health has been going downhill since I started here. I feel like I’m watching my life from the outside. I know I should be asking for help, talking to someone, making a plan. Instead I just freeze. I don’t even know where I’m going in life anymore. The version of me who was so sure of everything in high school feels like a stranger.

The weird thing is, I’m not actually alone. I have friends, my family cares, my boyfriend is supportive, there are counselors available. But I feel completely alone anyway. I can’t seem to open my mouth and say, “I’m not okay.” I keep silently hoping someone will notice on their own and ask the right questions, but no one does, and I just keep pretending I’m fine.

Emotionally, I feel… blocked. I want to cry so badly, but I can’t. It’s like sitting in this constant, low-level discomfort with these huge, muted emotions in the background. They’re there, but I can’t reach them. They just make my thoughts cloudy and heavy. I’m exhausted all the time, mentally and emotionally. I don’t know what to do anymore.

I guess I’m writing this because I needed to tell someone, even if it’s strangers on the internet. Maybe I just needed to put this somewhere outside my own head so I can breathe for a second and think.
