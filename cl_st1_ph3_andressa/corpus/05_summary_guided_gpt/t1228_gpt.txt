I’ve been thinking a lot about how differently loneliness can feel for each of us. A colleague told me they feel lonely because they don’t know how to talk to people, like there’s this missing “social manual” everyone else got.  

For me, it’s almost the opposite: I often feel like I’m “too much” for people. Too intense, too emotional, too talkative, too something. Even when I’m around others, I feel like I have to hold parts of myself back, and that makes me feel weirdly alone.  

I’m really curious about your experiences and hoping we can all learn from each other:  

What makes you feel lonely?  
What do you think about loneliness in general?  
What’s been hardest about trying to change it?  
What have you tried that didn’t help?  
What has actually helped, even a little?  
What do people often misunderstand about you?  
And what do you think is at the root of your loneliness?  

If you’re comfortable, I’d really like to hear.
