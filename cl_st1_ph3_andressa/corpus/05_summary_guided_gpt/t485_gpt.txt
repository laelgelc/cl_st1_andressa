I feel like every time I try to socialize, I just end up bothering people. It’s like I’m this constant source of annoyance, and I’m tired of feeling like I’m draining everyone around me.

I used to be more open with my feelings. If I was sad, I’d say it. If I was happy or excited, I’d share that too. But over time it started to feel like any emotion I showed was just “too much” for other people. I got the sense that my sadness was a burden and my happiness was somehow irritating or overbearing. So I made a decision: I’d just stop. I’ve been trying to stay emotionally neutral all the time, like a blank slate, so no one has to deal with anything from me.

I also used to talk a lot. I know I can ramble, and I’ve been told I talk too much, so I cut back hard. Now I mostly only speak when someone speaks to me first. The problem is that this just seems to create a new issue. When I’m quiet, people assume I’m mad, upset, or just cold and distant. But when I try to talk more and be engaged, I feel like I’m right back to being “annoying” again.

It feels like there’s no middle ground I’m capable of reaching. I’m either too much or not enough, and I don’t know how to exist in a way that doesn’t bother people.

Because of that, I’ve kind of decided to stop trying to socialize or make friends. I’d rather just fade into the background and not be noticed at all than keep feeling like a nuisance. The problem is, choosing that is making me more and more lonely, and I don’t really know what to do with that feeling either.
