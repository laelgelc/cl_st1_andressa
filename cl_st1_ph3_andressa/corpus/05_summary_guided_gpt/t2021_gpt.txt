I put so much effort into changing how I look because I thought that was the missing piece. I lost weight, worked on my style, hair, grooming – the whole “glow up” thing. And it did change how people reacted at first. I get more attention, more smiles, more “hey”s. But it never goes anywhere. No one actually sticks around.

It feels like my appearance opens the door, but my personality slams it shut.

I have ADHD and autism, and even when I mask as hard as I can, my real self eventually leaks out. I talk too much, get overly passionate about stuff no one else cares about, go on about niche interests, and say things impulsively that upset people without me even realizing why. People laugh at me, not with me. I get called “annoying,” “draining,” “too much,” “weird.”

It’s like I’m on a timer with every new person. At first I’m “interesting,” then I’m “a lot,” then I’m gone from their life.

I worked so hard to change the one thing I thought I could control – my looks – and now I’m stuck with the realization that the part pushing people away is just…me. I wish I were neurotypical. I’m tired of being the person no one stays for.
