This year I’m spending Thanksgiving, Christmas, and New Year’s completely alone. It’s a weird mix of emotions. I chose to distance myself from my family because being around them made me so anxious, like I was constantly walking on eggshells. Every visit felt like bracing for impact. 

Now that I’m not going back, I feel really sad and lonely, especially with all the holiday stuff everywhere reminding me of what I “should” be doing. But at the same time, I feel this quiet sense of relief, like I finally did something that an adult version of me would do—protecting my own peace instead of sacrificing it to keep other people comfortable.

I keep reminding myself that this choice is part of building something better. I want a healthy, safe, empowering home for my future kids, and that means breaking away from the patterns that hurt me.

I went on a solo date recently—just me, some food, and a walk—and I actually enjoyed my own company, even though the loneliness is still there in the background.

If anyone else is spending the holidays alone or in painful situations, I’m thinking of you. I’m praying that we all eventually find companionship that’s lasting, safe, and genuinely worth having.
