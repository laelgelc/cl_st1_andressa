I don’t really feel lonely in the usual sense. My days are full, I’m busy, I’m not craving “more people” in my life. What I really miss is deep, focused conversations about ideas – the kind where you dig into a topic, follow tangents, challenge each other, and then just… go back to your separate lives.

I don’t actually want more friends or an active social circle. I don’t have the time or energy to maintain that, and I don’t want to give people a half-hearted version of myself where I’m always “sorry, I’ve been so busy.” I don’t want group chats, constant texting, or the expectation to check in regularly.

I’ve tried a bunch of things: dating and friend apps, meetups, networking events, various online communities. But they all seem to come with strings attached. Either it’s small talk and surface-level stuff, or if I do connect with someone, it quickly turns into an expectation of ongoing contact that I can’t or don’t want to sustain. Even online spaces can get draining with the pressure to always be “on” or responsive.

What I’m looking for is something more like one-off or very occasional conversations where both people know it’s about the topic, not building a relationship. Almost like intellectual “drop-in” sessions: we talk about X for an hour, it’s good and thoughtful, and then no one feels hurt or rejected if we don’t talk again for months (or ever).

Does anyone else feel this specific kind of gap? Wanting meaningful, idea-based connection but not the ongoing relationship that usually comes with it? If so, how do you handle it or where do you find that kind of interaction without all the extra expectations?
