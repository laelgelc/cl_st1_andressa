I’m middle‑aged, have no friends, and lately it feels like something inside me is just…shutting down. I let this new roommate move in a while ago, someone I barely knew. She’s very social, always out, always chatting on the phone, always planning things with people. At first I thought maybe it would be good for me to have someone like that around. Now I mostly regret it.

Being around her feels like having a mirror held up to everything I don’t have. She seems so alive, connected, full of stories and memories with people. I feel like I have nothing to offer in comparison. No social life, no real history with anyone, nothing interesting about me. Just emptiness. It’s like watching a life I never learned how to live happening right next to me, through the wall.

I grew up in an extremely isolating, cult‑like home. We weren’t allowed friends, weren’t allowed to really be part of the world. Everything was about these apocalyptic beliefs, preparing for some end that never came. There was no room to explore who I was, what I liked, what I wanted. Now, as an adult, I feel like I never developed a personality. Just a bunch of fear and rules and blank space where a life should have been.

Lately the depression has gotten really bad. I’ve hit low points before, but this feels like a new level. I’ve lost my appetite, and I feel physically unwell a lot of the time, like my body is just tired of existing. I don’t drink anymore, but the hopelessness reminds me of how I felt when I used to drink heavily—like I’m drowning in something thick and dark and there’s no point trying to swim.

It genuinely feels like loneliness is draining my life force. Like it’s slowly killing me from the inside out. Does anyone else feel like that? Like the absence of connection itself is making you sick?
