I’m 36 and kind of stuck in my head about an old friend group and a wedding.

Most of my social life used to revolve around a high school friend group. Our thing was basically drinking heavily every weekend. That was just…what we did. Over the years, people started getting married, having kids, doing the whole “adult milestones” thing. I don’t relate to that at all, and honestly I don’t like that lifestyle. At the same time, my own focus shifted to working two jobs and spending my free time on hobbies none of them shared.

The more they leaned into family/kids talk and the same old drinking routine, the more out of place I felt. Eventually I just got tired of the dynamic and ghosted the group. It was a relief not to have to keep turning down invites or feel like the odd one out.

Recently, one of those old friends asked me to be a groomsman. I said yes in the moment, but now I’m really second-guessing it. We don’t have much in common anymore, and I’m not sure if I’m doing this out of guilt, nostalgia, or obligation.

I do have other friends now, but those connections are mostly built around shared activities, not deeper emotional stuff. So I don’t really have anyone I feel comfortable unpacking this with.

If you were in my position, would you follow through and be a groomsman, or back out?
