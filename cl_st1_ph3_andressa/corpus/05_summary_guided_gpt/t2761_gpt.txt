Lately I’ve been realizing how many hours I just burn on pointless YouTube videos. I’ll sit there watching random stuff, and it hits me that what I really want isn’t more content, it’s people to share it with. Not in some big, overwhelming way, but like a small group where you can just say, “hey, check this out,” and actually get a response.

I’m not looking for dating or anything like that, and I’m not really into huge platforms like Reddit or 4chan for this kind of thing. What I have in mind is more like a tiny online bar or terrace: 10–20 people, mixed, casual, where you can talk about music, art, games, movies, anime, random stories from your day. Somewhere that’s ongoing, not a one-off chat, but also not some super-focused niche community with strict topics.

I guess I want that feeling of going to a bar, where you’re not necessarily best friends with everyone, but you can sit down, show someone a song or a meme, talk about a movie you just watched, and it’s just… easy. No travel, no social anxiety, no pressure. Just a small, relaxed place online where people kind of recognize each other over time.

I’ve tried different forums and sites before, but it always goes the same way: either I stop showing up, or the community slowly dies. Facebook doesn’t work for me either—I barely use it, and I find it really hard to start conversations there. I feel like I’d actually be more myself if I could do it under a fake alias instead of my real name.

So I’m wondering: does something like this even exist? A small, casual, ongoing online hangout space—like a digital terrace—where people just share stuff and chat without it being about dating, clout, or some super-specific hobby? If anyone knows of places like that, or has found something similar, I’d really like to hear about it.
