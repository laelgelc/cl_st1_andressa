I don’t really know how to put all of this into words, but I feel like I’m disappearing and I don’t have anyone in my life I can actually say this to.

Since I had a psychotic break, everything has changed. My two best friends, who also live with me and are my caretakers, don’t really treat me like a friend anymore. We only talk during “office hours” when they’re doing caretaker tasks. Outside of that, it’s like I don’t exist. They’ll be in the same space as me and just… ignore me. No casual conversation, no hanging out, no joking around like we used to. I miss them so much even though they’re right here. It feels like I lost my friends and got staff instead.

On top of that, my parents, who have always been abusive, have somehow gotten worse since they moved in. They don’t come to see me to actually be with me or share anything I care about. They come to vent, dump their emotions, and then leave. When I’m very sick and scared and in distress, they just scream at me. There’s never any comfort, just more pressure and noise. I’m bedbound and disabled and I depend on other people to survive, but I feel like I’m just a burden everyone is stuck with.

I feel completely alone, even in a house full of people. I keep wondering what’s so wrong with me that this is how everyone treats me. Am I a bad person? Is this what I deserve? It’s hard not to internalize it when the only interactions I get are either clinical, like I’m a job, or abusive, like I’m something to yell at.

I don’t have anyone I can rely on emotionally. No one to talk to about my interests, no one who comes to spend time with me just because they want to. I’m stuck in bed with my own thoughts, needing people to survive, and at the same time feeling like I don’t matter to any of them beyond what I require from them. It’s a really lonely place to be.
