I’ve been struggling with depression for years, but it got really bad in college. I ended up in the hospital because I was seriously suicidal. What hit me the hardest wasn’t just the suicidal thoughts, it was what happened around them – or more accurately, what didn’t happen.

No one checked on me. No messages, no calls, nothing. People I thought were my friends just… disappeared. It made me realize I’d always been the one keeping the friendships alive, always the one reaching out, planning things, checking in. When I stopped, everything just went silent. That silence felt like confirmation that they never really cared in the first place.

I’m homosexual, and that’s another layer to all of this. I get mockery, side glances, those “jokes” people think I don’t notice. And when I actually ask for help or try to be vulnerable, some people act like I’m hitting on them, like my sexuality automatically makes every interaction suspicious. It’s humiliating and makes me want to shut down even more.

This kind of thing has followed me since school. I was bullied, left out of groups, always the extra person no one really wanted around. I still often end up eating alone, crying alone, just feeling like I’m on the outside of everyone else’s life looking in. It’s like I’m not seen as a real person, more like some weird “other” species people don’t want to get too close to.

I keep trying to tell myself it’s about them, not me – that their behavior says more about who they are than who I am. But even with that thought, the sadness and loneliness don’t really go away. I still feel excluded, unwanted, and tired of always being the one who has to prove I deserve basic human kindness.
