I don’t really know how to explain this properly, but I feel like I’m living in this weird in‑between space with people.

On the surface, I get along with others. People seem drawn to me, they talk to me, they laugh with me, they say nice things. I’m not some total outcast. I have “friends.” I have family. But everything stays so far away, like everyone is standing on the other side of a glass wall.

It’s like people are watching me from the stands, “cheering” for me from a distance, but no one is actually on the field with me. They like me, but they don’t really know me. They don’t really *show up* for me. That hit me especially hard on my 19th birthday. Not a single “happy birthday.” Not one. It made all of that surface‑level stuff feel fake and hollow, like I’m surrounded by people but somehow invisible at the same time.

What I really want is just one person who feels like a brother to me. Someone I can be completely comfortable with, no pretending, no worrying about saying the wrong thing. Someone I can hang out with without planning weeks ahead, who’d come over just to exist in the same space, who’d answer a late‑night message and not make me feel like a burden. Someone who’d be there without judgment, who actually *wants* to be close, not just “around.”

Instead, I’m here with all these “connections” that never go deeper. I feel deeply alone and I honestly don’t know what went wrong or what I’m doing wrong. I don’t really have anyone I feel I can say this to in real life without sounding dramatic or needy, so I’m just dumping it here.

I’m 19, surrounded by people, and somehow I’ve never felt more alone.
