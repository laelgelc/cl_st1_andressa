I’m 17 and I feel like I’ve been failing at making friends ever since I started high school.

In person, I’ve only made about two actual friends. I regularly talk to just one of them, and even with her I constantly worry I’m bothering her or being too much. I overthink every message, every silence, every time she takes a bit longer to reply. It makes me feel like I’m walking on eggshells in the one friendship I do have.

Because of that, I’ve been trying to make friends online for about a year now. It always goes the exact same way: I join a server or a group, or I directly ask someone if they want to be friends. At first things seem okay. We talk for a few days or maybe a few weeks, and I start to feel hopeful. Then they slowly stop replying, or just disappear completely. No explanation, just… gone. 

It makes me feel like people enjoy my presence in the moment, but don’t actually care to know me beyond surface level. Like I’m “fun enough” to talk to when they’re bored, but not worth sticking around for. Each time this happens, I end up thinking there must be something wrong with me. I’ve started to get really self‑deprecating about it, like I’m just not someone people want in their life long-term.

I keep going through this cycle: I put in effort, try to be friendly and open, get tired of being ignored or left behind, give up for a while, feel horribly lonely, and then start the whole thing over again. It’s exhausting.

I’ve posted about this kind of thing online before and it felt like no one really cared or responded, which just made me feel even more invisible. I do want advice, but I really don’t want to be told “just make more friends in real life” because it doesn’t feel that simple, and people don’t really walk up to others and befriend them out of nowhere anymore.

I just want to know if anyone else relates to this pattern, or has found a way out of it.
