I’m in this small online friend group, like 5–6 people, and I’ve known two of them, Jeff and Steve, for over six years. You’d think that much time would mean we’re close and comfortable with each other, but lately I’m really starting to feel like the odd one out, or even like I’m the punching bag.

Steve especially seems to have it out for me. He’ll call me corny, say he’s tired of my voice and my laugh, and just generally throw these little toxic comments my way. What gets to me is that he doesn’t talk like that to anyone else. It makes me feel like I personally annoy him, like there’s something about me that just grates on him and he doesn’t bother to hide it.

I tried bringing this up to Jeff, because I’ve known him the longest and I usually trust his judgment. But when I told him how Steve’s comments were making me feel, he just brushed it off as normal tension from knowing each other so long. He literally said “Steve’s just being Steve,” like that explains everything and I should just accept it.

That kind of dismissal stuck with me, especially because there was another thing Jeff said that’s been bothering me. We were joking about school stereotypes and he described me as the kind of kid who would sneak into the girls’ locker room. It was said as a joke, but afterward I couldn’t stop thinking about it. Does he secretly see me as creepy or weird? Is that how I come across to everyone?

Jeff’s also called both me and Steve “chaotic,” and said I’m just the louder one. I don’t know how to take that. Am I actually chaotic and annoying and just not seeing it? Or are they the ones being crappy friends and hiding it behind “jokes” and “banter”?

I keep going in circles wondering if I’m the problem or if they are. Something just feels off in the group dynamic. Part of me feels like if I just disappeared from the group one day, nobody would really notice or care. And that thought hurts more than I want to admit.
