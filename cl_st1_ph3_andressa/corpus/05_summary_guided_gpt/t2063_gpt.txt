I’m 22 and I’m realizing I don’t really have many close, long-term friendships, and it’s starting to bother me more than I want to admit.

I have a few friends from high school, but they’re all way closer to other people than they are to me. That’s mostly on me – I didn’t put in the effort to keep in touch, and now I feel like the “outer circle” person. I avoid old classmates because I’m insecure and scared they’ll judge me, or think I’m weird or awkward, so I just… don’t reach out.

In college, I started off really isolated. Eventually I joined some activities, pushed myself socially, and ended up with a lot of acquaintances. People seemed to think I was well-connected, but underneath that I rarely felt like anyone’s “favorite” person, or like I had that one really solid, close friend. I did get close to two people, but both friendships ended in falling outs. That really messed with my head.

Now I’m very self-conscious. I’m scared that when people get to know me better, they’ll like me less. It also freaks me out how friendships can just end out of nowhere, or because of incompatibility you didn’t see at first. It makes me hesitant to invest in people.

After graduating, it feels like I’m back to square one. I’ve tried texting some college friends, but a lot of conversations either get ignored or just fizzle out. At the same time, I genuinely enjoy being alone. I like focusing on myself and not dealing with drama. But I also worry that preferring isolation and not wanting to keep up with a ton of people is somehow “wrong” or socially unacceptable, especially when everyone else seems to have these tight friend groups.

I’m scared I come across as unlikeable or just… off. Does anyone else struggle with making and maintaining close friendships like this? Do you feel lonely or isolated even if you kind of prefer being alone? Any advice or perspective would be really appreciated.
