I moved out of my family home when I was 15, and now I’m 17, basically supporting myself on about $300 a month while my parents cover the bills. I thought the hardest part would be money or responsibilities, but it turns out the worst part is how alone I feel.

I’ve never really had close friends. I always kind of assumed I just wasn’t a “friend type” person, like there was something about me that just didn’t click with people. For a while I had this one girl I’d talk to once a week and sit with at lunch. It wasn’t some deep friendship, but it was something. Then this semester our schedules changed, and now I don’t see her at all. I eat lunch alone in the bathroom because I can’t stand sitting alone in the cafeteria feeling like everyone can see I have no one.

I also used to go to a youth group, and that was another place I could at least be around people. But for about a month now I haven’t been able to go, and that cut off another bit of human contact I had. It’s like every small connection I manage to make just disappears.

I’ve realized how much I actually need human contact. I feel so tired and empty all the time. I try so hard to be nice, to look presentable, to do my hair and makeup, to change little things about myself hoping someone will notice me and want to talk. But it doesn’t really happen.

The best interaction I’ve had in the past year was this silly, anonymous car “race” with a stranger on the road. We just sped up and slowed down around each other for a bit and then went our separate ways. It was nothing, but somehow it felt like more connection than I get most days.

The girl I thought might be a friend turned out to be using me to lie to her parents, and that really hurt. I just wish I had one person I could share myself with, someone who actually wants me around and isn’t just using me or drifting away.
