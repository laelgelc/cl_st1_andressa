I used to have this big circle of friends and a busy social life. Now it feels like everything shrank down to just me and my kid, and most days I feel completely alone.

Becoming a single mom flipped my whole life upside down. I’m exhausted almost all the time, constantly stressed, and my health has definitely taken a hit. Meanwhile, my old friends are still child-free and carefree. They travel, go out, make spontaneous plans, and I’m over here trying to figure out how to get enough sleep and pay the bills.

Somewhere along the line, they started seeing me as “too serious” or “negative” or “problematic.” And honestly, I’ve started to see them as kind of childish, like they don’t understand what real responsibility feels like yet. When I try to be my real self – tired, worried, not exactly the life of the party – people slowly drift away. When I force myself to be upbeat, fun, and positive, they pay attention again… but it feels fake and exhausting.

I feel trapped between these two versions of myself: the fake, “acceptable” one who gets to belong, and the real one who ends up alone.

Living in a rural town makes it worse. Everyone already has their established circles, family nearby, and long-term friendships. There aren’t many places to meet new people, and I don’t have any supportive family to lean on. It just feels like I’m stuck in this lonely little bubble with no real way out.
