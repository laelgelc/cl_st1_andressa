I’m around 40 and it feels like I’ve been slowly shutting down for years without really noticing it.

For most of my life I’ve been the listener, the helper, the one who absorbs everyone else’s pain. The “emotional sponge.” I was the person people came to when they needed to vent, cry, or unload. I took pride in being that person, but I think it’s taken a bigger toll than I wanted to admit.

When I hit 40 I had a big life reset and told myself I was going to “protect my peace.” At first it felt empowering, like I was finally setting boundaries. But somewhere along the way, that turned into complete isolation.

I reach out to people I thought were close, and it’s like shouting into a void. Nobody calls. Replies are short texts, sometimes just an emoji or a one-liner. I can go days, even weeks, without actually speaking to another human being. When I do message someone, I feel like I’m forcing the conversation, dragging it along so it doesn’t die.

It’s making me realize that the people I considered close might not see me the same way. Maybe I was just useful when they needed a listener. Now that I’m not constantly available, it’s like I’ve disappeared.

I’m exhausted from holding a smile for everyone else for 25 years. I still honestly believe a simple smile can brighten someone’s day, and I don’t want to lose that part of myself. But I’m struggling with this heavy, quiet loneliness and the difference between choosing to be a loner and feeling truly, painfully alone.

I don’t really know what I’m asking for. I just needed to put this somewhere.
