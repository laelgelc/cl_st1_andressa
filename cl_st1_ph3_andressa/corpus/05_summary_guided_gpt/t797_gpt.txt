I’m 23, married, with a two-year-old, and I feel so intensely lonely all the time that it’s starting to scare me.

My husband is 24 and works six days a week. Most days it’s just me and our toddler. His family only watches our kid when I’m out cleaning houses for clients, and my own family doesn’t help with babysitting at all. So when I’m not working, I’m home, doing everything: cleaning, cooking, managing the house, taking care of our child. I’m exhausted in a way that feels deeper than just being tired.

I only really see my husband for a few hours a day. We’re both drained, and it feels like we barely talk about anything real anymore. No deep conversations, no real emotional connection. It’s like we’re just surviving next to each other, not actually together.

What I really want is some kind of shared community. Shared land, shared space, with friends or family so no one is doing everything alone. I don’t even care about owning the land myself. I just want mutual support, shared responsibility, less financial and emotional pressure on one person or one couple.

But my husband is completely against that. He wants to be the sole owner, to have full control over everything. It feels like we’re on totally different planets. I’ve even started wondering if our star signs (I’m an Aquarius, he’s a Taurus) actually say something about how differently we see life.

I can’t pretend I’m happy. I feel like he’s repressing a lot just to keep functioning in this capitalist grind, and I don’t know where that leaves me. I don’t know what to do or how to move forward when what I need feels so opposite to what he wants.
