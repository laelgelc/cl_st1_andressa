I’ve been reading all these articles and posts about loners and introverts, and I keep getting stuck on this weird in‑between feeling. I definitely am a loner, but not the “cool, mysterious, self-sufficient” type they always seem to describe. I’m much closer to the “lives with his mom” stereotype than the confident, independent one who travels solo and eats alone in fancy restaurants like it’s nothing.

At the same time, I don’t really fit the “forced loner” thing either. I don’t hate people, I don’t think everyone is stupid or beneath me, and I’m not sitting here seething because nobody understands my genius. I’ve just… basically always been a loner, at least since I stopped being a little kid. It feels like my default setting rather than something that happened to me.

What complicates it is that I still get anxious doing some of the “classic” loner activities alone. Eating in restaurants by myself makes me incredibly uncomfortable. I get this tight feeling in my chest, like everyone is watching and silently judging me for being alone, even though I know logically they’re probably not. The anxiety is enough that I usually just avoid it altogether.

But other things I can do alone now without much issue. I go to museums by myself and feel completely fine wandering around. Same with the cinema – I actually enjoy watching a movie alone. I also go running alone. My sister, who has friends and is definitely not a loner, keeps telling me that going for solo runs is “brave,” which is funny because to me that feels normal, and it’s restaurants that feel like some huge social risk.

I also started learning to swim as an adult, and doing that alone brought up a lot of anxiety too. I stuck with it, but that nervous feeling was there every time I went.

I don’t feel like I’m on the verge of depression or anything, but I can’t help wondering if the isolation might catch up with me at some point. It’s like I’m a loner by nature, but not a confident one, and I’m not sure what that means for the future.
