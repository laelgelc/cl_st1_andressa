I’m 26 and in grad school, and lately I just feel so lonely and confused about what adulthood is supposed to look like.

Most of my friends have graduated. A lot of them are married now or in serious relationships, and it feels like once someone gets a partner, friendships just quietly… disappear. I know people say “of course you prioritize your spouse,” but it still hurts to see how quickly I’ve gone from being an important part of their lives to basically an afterthought.

Even my unmarried friends mostly talk about wanting a relationship, how to find one, how to keep one. It makes me feel like our friendship is just a placeholder until they couple up. I catch myself thinking, “Why should I invest in this if it’s just going to end once they get a partner?” I even feel weird about the idea of being involved in future weddings and all that, when deep down I’m expecting to be dropped afterward.

It’s making me really anxious and alone. I keep asking myself what the point of life even is if every connection is temporary and conditional. I’m also scared that once I finish school, things will get even worse — like I’ll just be stuck in some 9–5 with even fewer chances to connect, watching everyone else build couple-focused lives I’m not really part of.

Is this just what your mid-20s are like? Is it common to feel like friendships fade and everything becomes about relationships at this age?
