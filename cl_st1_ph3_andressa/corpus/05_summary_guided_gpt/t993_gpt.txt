It’s strange how being alone can shift so slowly that you barely notice it changing.

At first, the quiet felt like a relief. No expectations, no noise, just space to breathe and think. I remember feeling clearer, calmer, like I finally had room in my own head. But somewhere along the way, that same silence started to feel heavy. It’s like the walls are closing in, not physically, but mentally. The calm turned into this suffocating stillness that’s hard to put into words.

The worst part is how it messes with how I see myself. The longer I’m alone, the smaller I feel. It’s like my self-worth is being eroded grain by grain. Reaching out to people feels terrifying now, like I’m exposing something fragile that will just be ignored or rejected. I feel unseen, unheard, like I could disappear and it wouldn’t matter.

I’ve noticed I’ve become harder, more guarded. It’s not a choice so much as a reflex. I’m tired in a way that sleep doesn’t fix, like my sense of who I am is slowly wearing away. Sometimes I try to remember what it felt like to be genuinely understood by someone, and it’s like reaching for a memory that’s gone blurry around the edges.
