I’m 24F and I feel terrified of both romantic and platonic relationships. Everyone I’ve ever really let in has eventually left, and now it’s like my brain has decided that’s just the rule. I feel like I have too much trauma, bad social skills, and these weird, annoying traits that make me fundamentally unlovable.

One of the worst ones is that I sometimes lie randomly in conversations. Not about big things, just small, pointless lies that come out of my mouth before I can stop them. It’s like I get nervous and my brain panics, and then I say something untrue and immediately regret it. I also don’t really know what’s “normal” to say, so every interaction feels like a test I’m failing.

Most days I don’t talk to anyone. I recently started college again, but I go out of my way not to form any intentions with people because I’m so scared of getting hurt. In class I kind of dissociate and pretend it’s just me and the professor in the room. On the first day a guy asked for my number and I gave it because I didn’t want things to be awkward, and then I just felt depressed afterward, like I’d made a mistake by letting someone get even that close.

The only person I really talk to is my therapist once a week, but lately it feels like she’s emotionally checked out. She sighs loudly and just seems like she doesn’t care, which makes me feel boring, strange, and unwanted even in therapy.

Lately I feel oddly content with the idea of just disappearing. And just to be clear, I don’t want anyone messaging me with sexual intentions.
