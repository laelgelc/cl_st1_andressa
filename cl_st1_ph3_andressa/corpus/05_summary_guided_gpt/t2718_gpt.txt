I’m 24 and, honestly, my life hasn’t really moved since 2012. I live with my parents, I’m on disability, and I’ve been basically reclusive for years. I only go out when I absolutely have to or when I force myself to take a walk. Most days are just me in my room, killing time and trying not to think too hard about how stuck I am.

A big part of it is mental illness, but also just…misanthropy. I don’t like people much, and I know I come off as verbally aggressive. I say harsh things, I push people away, and then I end up alone again and pretend I don’t care. The few relationships or friendships I’ve had never stayed stable because of that. It’s like I’m wired to sabotage any connection I get.

Now with coronavirus, everyone’s suddenly getting a small taste of isolation, and it’s weird. For once I can actually feel some empathy, because people are talking about being stuck inside, losing their routines, feeling lonely. That’s been my normal for years. But I also know that once things go back to “normal” for them, they’ll move on, and I’ll still be here in the same spot.

I was actually planning to try therapy again this year, to see if I could work on some of this, but then the pandemic hit and everything shut down. It feels like even when I try to do something different, life finds a way to keep me exactly where I’ve always been.
