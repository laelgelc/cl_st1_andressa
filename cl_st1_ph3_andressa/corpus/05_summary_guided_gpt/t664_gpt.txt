I’m 25 and I feel like I’ve never had a real, genuine female friend in my life. I’ve had classmates, coworkers, people I say “hi/bye” to, maybe chat a little with, but it never goes deeper than that. When I see other girls who have those close, natural bonds – hanging out, sharing inside jokes, calling each other when something happens – I feel like I’m watching life from behind a glass wall.

I’m a caring person. I’m always the one who’s available to help, listen, support, give advice, show up when someone needs something. But it doesn’t feel like it’s ever really reciprocated. It’s like people like what I can *do* for them, not who I actually am. Even with my male friends, they mostly reach out when they need something, not just to talk or spend time.

I know I’m introverted and I have a hard time opening up, and I’m sure that plays a role. I don’t instantly click with people or overshare, and maybe that makes me seem closed off or uninterested. But inside I’m really craving a genuine connection where I can just be myself and not feel like I’m being used or tolerated.

It’s gotten to the point where I keep asking myself: is there something fundamentally wrong with me? Or are real, mutual friendships just becoming rarer in general? I don’t know if I’m expecting too much, or if I’ve just never met the right people.

Does anyone else feel like this – like you’re always on the outside of those deep bonds everyone else seems to have?
