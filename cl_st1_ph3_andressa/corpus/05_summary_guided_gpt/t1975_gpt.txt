I’ve been thinking a lot about what loneliness actually is. Is it just being physically alone, or is it that feeling of being invisible even when you’re surrounded by people?

For me, it’s the second one. I can do small talk, and sometimes I can manage slightly deeper conversations, but I never feel like I’m showing my real self. I don’t feel like anyone truly knows me, and I struggle to be vulnerable because it never seems to go anywhere. It feels like I’m always stuck at the surface, and the parts of me that matter most are hidden away.

On top of that, I’ve had a lot of romantic and personal rejection, and it’s wearing me down. Every time I think about opening up again, I wonder if it’s worth the emotional cost. Sometimes it feels easier to just stay “comfortably numb” and alone rather than risk more hurt.

If you’re over 40 (or any age, really), how does loneliness affect you mentally, physically, and emotionally? And how do you find the conviction to keep trying and opening up after you’ve been rejected so many times?
