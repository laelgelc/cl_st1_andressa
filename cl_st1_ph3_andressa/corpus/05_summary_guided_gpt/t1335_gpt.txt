I’ve been thinking a lot about how Christmas isn’t the warm, cozy movie scene for everyone. For some of us, it’s a reminder of who isn’t here anymore, or what’s changed in ways we never asked for.

If you’re grieving someone, the empty chair at the table can feel louder than any carol. It’s okay to honor them in your own way—light a candle, cook their favorite dish, look through old photos, or just sit with the memories. Missing them is part of loving them.

If you’re going through a divorce, all the “perfect family” images everywhere can sting. Maybe this is the year you start new traditions, even if they’re small or imperfect—different food, a new place, a quiet day instead of a big gathering. Your Christmas doesn’t have to look like anyone else’s.

For those whose kids are far away, or whose house feels too quiet: you poured love and values into them, and that doesn’t disappear with distance. Trust that what you gave them will carry on, even if they can’t be with you right now.

You don’t have to celebrate Christmas the traditional way. You’re allowed to shape the day around your actual life and your actual feelings, not what’s expected.
