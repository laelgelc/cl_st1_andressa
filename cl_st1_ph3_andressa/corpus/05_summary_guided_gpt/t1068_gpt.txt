Loneliness hurts like hell, but honestly, I still think it’s better than giving up on myself or begging for connection that isn’t real.

From what I’ve seen, most friendships and relationships are just temporary arrangements built on convenience, shared routines, and stability. People are around when your life lines up with theirs, when you’re mentally and physically okay, when you’re “easy” to be around. The moment you’re not stable, a lot of them vanish. Loyalty and “forever” love feel more like illusions than anything else.

And love itself? Half the time it just feels like chemicals in your brain tricking you into attaching to someone who can leave whenever it stops being fun or easy. Along with that comes drama, expectations, pressure, and pain. You’re supposed to perform a role, and if you slip, you’re suddenly “too much” or “not enough.”

So I’ve kind of decided: I’d rather be alone than keep trading pieces of myself for temporary company. I’m trying to put my energy into self-respect, learning things, building skills, and just grinding for myself. Not in a bitter, “screw everyone” way, but in a realistic way where I’m my own constant.

If people show up and want to be in my life, cool. If they don’t, that’s fine too. I don’t want my sense of worth to depend on who stays. I’d rather be lonely with my integrity than surrounded and hollow.
