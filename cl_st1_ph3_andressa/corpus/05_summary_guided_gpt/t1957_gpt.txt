I’m 26 and I feel like I’m in this weird in‑between place socially.

On paper, I’m “socially capable.” I can hold conversations, crack jokes, read the room. But actually being around people drains me so fast. I have low self-esteem and a lot of social anxiety, so every interaction turns into this performance where I’m trying really hard to keep everyone else happy and comfortable. I don’t feel like that effort is ever really reciprocated, and it leaves me exhausted.

I went through five years of college and somehow never made any real friends. Just acquaintances I’d talk to in class or group projects, nothing that lasted. Now I work fully remote, so there’s no built-in social interaction there either. I haven’t formed friendships with coworkers, and I don’t really talk to anyone online either.

I live with my girlfriend, and she’s great. I’m genuinely grateful for her. But outside of her, I have essentially no social life. No one I text regularly, no one I meet up with, no group chats, nothing. Most days, she’s the only person I actually talk to.

Part of me feels like I completely missed out on life, like I watched my twenties go by from the sidelines. But another part of me finds this isolated lifestyle… easier. Less painful. Fewer chances to feel rejected or like I’m not enough.

I don’t even know if what I’m feeling is normal. I guess I’m just wondering if anyone else relates to this kind of “chosen” isolation that still somehow feels like a loss.
