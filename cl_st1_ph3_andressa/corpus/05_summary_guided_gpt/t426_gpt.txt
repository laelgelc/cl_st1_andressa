I’m 41 and I feel like I’ve finally hit a weird kind of acceptance about being alone.

I was never popular or socially successful, but in my 20s I at least had a couple of friends and two girlfriends over the years. My career was going nowhere though, so I moved to a new city hoping a fresh start would help. I stayed in touch with my old friends and visited them, but no one ever came to see me. That hurt more than I admitted at the time.

In the new place I tried. I really did. I went on dates, tried to meet people, put myself out there. Over and over I got rejected for things I can’t change – being short, not earning enough. After a while it just wore me down. I haven’t had a date in 5 years, and I haven’t made a real friend in almost 10.

These days my life is basically my routine: gym, work, coffee. I’m planning to go back to school in late 2026, mostly to have some direction.

I’ve decided to stop trying to pursue relationships. It feels like I gave it my best shot and it just didn’t work out. I’m not happy with that, but the sharp sadness has mostly faded. What’s left is this dull acceptance and a bit of peace, like I’ve stopped fighting something I can’t change.
