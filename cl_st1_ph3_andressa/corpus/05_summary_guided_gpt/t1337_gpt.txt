Sometimes I feel like every person who’s ever “loved” me was in love with a character they wrote in their head, not with me. It’s like they saw a projection and decided that was enough, and the moment I didn’t match it anymore, it all fell apart. After almost 24 years of dating and breakups, I still feel this weird, hollow space inside that nothing seems to touch.

The few people who actually seem to understand me are always somehow out of reach—emotionally unavailable, far away, or just not really in my life. Meanwhile, my friendships feel so fragile and surface-level. I’m the “floater friend” who drifts between groups, gives a lot, listens, shows up… but doesn’t really belong anywhere.

What makes it worse is that from the outside, people assume I’m socially fine, that I have close friends and a solid support system. If only they knew how off that is. Sometimes I catch myself wondering what would happen if I just quietly disappeared—how long it would take for anyone to really notice.
