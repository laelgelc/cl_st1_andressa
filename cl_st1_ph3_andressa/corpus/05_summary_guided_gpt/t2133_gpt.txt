To anyone reading this who feels lonely, unappreciated, and distant from everyone around them: I see you.

I used to share my problems, hoping someone would really listen, but it felt like nobody cared. Eventually I just stopped. I felt alone even in a room full of people, like I was invisible, like my pain didn’t matter.

If that’s you right now, I want you to question the things you tell yourself in the dark. You are not a failure. Just because your hard work hasn’t been rewarded yet doesn’t mean it was pointless. It doesn’t mean you should give up; it might just mean it’s time to change your priorities, your direction, or where you invest your energy.

I gave my love to the wrong person and it hurt. But it taught me something important: don’t place all of your hope in other people. Focus on improving yourself and your environment instead. Some people are fake. Some will betray you. That says everything about them and nothing about your worth.

Only you get to define who you are. Not your age, not your race, not your gender, not the people who left, and not the ones who didn’t see your value.

Life will always be hard in some way. But if you start living on your own terms, you’ll begin to see a bit of light at the end of the tunnel. You are amazing, you are strong, and you are so much more than what you’re going through right now.
