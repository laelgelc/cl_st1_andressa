Sometimes it hits me so hard that I just… stop whatever I’m doing and sit with this heavy feeling that I might never experience romantic love.

I don’t mean some movie-style, dramatic love story. What I want is something quieter: a supportive partnership where we trust each other, grow together, and build a life side by side. Someone I can rely on, who can rely on me. Just that sense of “we’re in this together.”

I’ve never had that. I’ve never even had a relationship that felt loving in that way. Most of the time I’m used to being on my own. I actually like solitude a lot—I enjoy my own company, my routines, my space. But then there are those moments where the loneliness catches up with me and I just break down. It’s like the absence of that “person,” that soulmate, suddenly becomes unbearable.

I keep thinking: what if this is it? What if I stay like this for the rest of my life? I do have friends I care about and who care about me, and I’m grateful for them. But it doesn’t feel the same as having a partner who’s really sharing life with you in that deeper way.

I’m not looking for reassurance or advice or people telling me it will “definitely happen.” I guess I just want to know if anyone else lives with this same quiet fear—being mostly okay alone, but haunted by the thought that you might never be loved in that way.
