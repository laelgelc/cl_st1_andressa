Quick update from our little hackathon / community-building crew in case anyone’s curious or wants to get involved.

Josephe is working on a simple, functional website so we have something more “solid” than just scattered posts and a Discord server. Nothing fancy, but a place to collect what we’re doing and make it easier for new people to understand and join.

We’ve also got a new member, Steve, who’s going to pull together a list of literature and references related to loneliness, connection, and related topics for the site. The idea is to have a kind of “starting library” for anyone who wants to go deeper than just personal anecdotes.

We spent a lot of time brainstorming what the actual hackathon could look like: what kind of content or projects we might focus on, what format (online, hybrid, etc.), and how to involve more people. Meetup groups came up as a way to find others, and we even talked about having local, in-person branches instead of staying fully online. There are a lot of questions there, especially about how to keep things coherent without turning into some big centralized organization.

Another thing we realized is that our mission is still pretty fuzzy. The subreddit wiki has some basics, but we want a clearer mission statement and guiding principles. “Purpose” keeps coming up as a big theme—like, lonely people often struggle with a sense of purpose—and we’re leaning toward focusing on that because it feels more positive and constructive than just “loneliness.”

We also want to reach out to experts (like authors who write about loneliness and connection) to see if they’d be willing to help or at least share what we’re doing.

Action items for now: Josephe continues the website, okko7 works on the mission statement, and Steve prepares the reference list.
