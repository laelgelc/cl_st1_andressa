Sometimes I feel like I broke something inside myself without noticing.

A few years back I realized, way too late, that I’d actually been in love with someone. By the time I understood what I’d felt, it was already over and there was no going back. That realization hurt in a weird, delayed way, like grief that finally catches up to you.

After that, I got so desperate for affection that I jumped into a short, horrible relationship with someone I didn’t even really like. I just wanted to be wanted. It was bad enough that it taught me a hard lesson: I can’t afford to lower my standards like that again, no matter how lonely I get.

Later on, I started developing feelings for an old friend. For a while I thought it might be love, but looking back, I think it was mostly fear of being alone dressed up as something deeper. Nothing serious ever happened there, and now I’m kind of grateful.

Out of all this, I came up with two rules for myself:  
1) Don’t let fear, uncertainty, or despair make my choices.  
2) Don’t settle for relationships that harm me.

I’ve been alone for about a year now. A bit lonely, yeah, but mostly okay. I’ve made some kind of peace with it.

But now there’s this new fear: not “will anyone ever love me again?” but “will I ever genuinely love anyone again?” It’s like I’ve been “thinking” my emotions instead of actually feeling them, analyzing everything to death. I worry I’ve numbed myself so much that I won’t be able to fully feel love again, even if it’s right in front of me.
