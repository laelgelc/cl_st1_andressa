I’ve spent most of my life convinced that other people were the problem. That they didn’t love me enough, didn’t listen enough, didn’t “get” me. I’ve always cast myself as the victim in my own story. And I used to think that being openly loving was kind of weak or uncool, like caring too much made you pathetic.

Lately I’ve been hit with this really uncomfortable thought: what if the problem is that I don’t actually know how to give love?

It sounds obvious when I write it, but it hurts my pride more than I expected. I’ve always seen myself as the one who’s been hurt, not the one who might be failing others. I’m a loner by default, and sometimes I feel genuinely dysfunctional, like there’s something missing in how I connect with people.

I keep thinking of love like a currency everyone else seems to have and I somehow don’t. I want to pay, I want to participate, but I’m standing there empty‑handed. Or maybe I’m holding the wrong kind of money and I don’t even realize it.

I do want to love people. I really do. I just don’t know if I’m capable of loving them in the way they actually need, not just in the way that feels safe or comfortable for me. And I’m scared that I’ll never feel confident that I’m “doing it right,” that I’ll always be second‑guessing myself and wondering if I’m quietly failing the people who matter.
