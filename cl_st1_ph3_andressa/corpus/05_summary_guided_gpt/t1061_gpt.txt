I’m 22, nonbinary, and I feel like I’m rotting in place.

I keep telling myself that I’m lonely because I’m not trying hard enough, but then I remember how little freedom I actually have. I live with my parents, who are homophobic and basically keep tabs on where I go and who I’m with. There’s a city nearby with LGBTQ bars and spaces, and I can’t even go there without lying and risking a whole meltdown at home. It feels like the world where I might belong is just out of reach.

What hurts even more is that the people who say they care, or say they “relate,” usually end up being inconsiderate or just… assholes. I’ll start to trust someone and then they show they don’t actually see me at all.

I just want one person who genuinely understands me. A friend I can hug and hold, someone real. I want to wake up and not feel useless. Instead I feel like I’m watching life from behind glass, like I’m only ever going to be an observer, never someone who actually gets to connect.
