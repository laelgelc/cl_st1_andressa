I left Reddit about five months ago because I really wanted to work on myself and cut down on how much social media was affecting my mental health. For the most part it actually helped. I felt a bit calmer and less caught up in doomscrolling and comparing myself to everyone else.

A big part of my life during that time has been my support workers. I’ve had them for years, but one in particular really stood out. They were kind and honest in a way that actually pushed me to grow instead of just keeping me afloat. They believed in me, encouraged me, and made me feel like I wasn’t completely alone in dealing with everything.

Recently they changed jobs, and their last day hit me a lot harder than I expected. They’re not my worker anymore, but we’ve become good friends, which I’m grateful for. Still, that transition from seeing them in that supportive role to just… not having them there in the same way has been rough. I’ve been missing them for about a month now.

On top of that, I’ve had a pretty bad cold, and being physically unwell has just drained me emotionally. I technically have two close friends now, which is more than I’ve had in a long time, but I still feel really lonely. It’s like my brain can’t catch up with the fact that I’m not as alone as I used to be.

So I ended up back here, looking for some kind of comfort and a place to let this all out. I think I’ll probably feel more normal again in about a week, but right now I just needed somewhere to say all this.
