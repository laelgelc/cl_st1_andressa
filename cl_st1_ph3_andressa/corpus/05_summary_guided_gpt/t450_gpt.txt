I’m 59 and lately I’ve been hit with this deep, almost physical fear that I’m going to die without anyone ever really knowing who I am. On paper, I’m not “alone” – I have three grown kids I’m close to and I love them so much. But they’re busy, building their own lives, which they should be. I don’t want to weigh them down with all of this.

Extended family is around, but those relationships feel distant and kind of superficial. We talk, we see each other sometimes, but it’s all surface-level. No one really knows the things I’ve survived, the hard stuff that’s shaped me. Most of the people in my life have no idea what I’ve actually been through, and I don’t feel like there’s much space or interest for that. Everyone seems so wrapped up in their own worlds.

I do all the “right” things: I meditate, go to therapy, I run, I try to chat with neighbors when I can. But when I look ahead, my future feels bleak and shrinking, like it’s all just going to fade out quietly. I keep thinking: if I’m going to remain unseen and easily forgotten, what’s the point of continuing?

I’m not looking for platitudes. I just wonder if anyone else feels this way – like you’re here, but no one really sees you, and you’re scared you’ll leave this world as a stranger to everyone.
