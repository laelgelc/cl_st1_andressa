I’m in my late 30s, married with kids, and on paper my life looks good. I love my spouse, I love my kids, and we actually get out and do a lot together as a family. But I feel so lonely and friendless lately and I don’t really know what to do with that feeling.

I used to have a decent social life. Nothing wild, but I had regular plans with different friends – coffee, walks, random hangouts. I was usually the one initiating, but it was something. A few months ago I quit social media, and it’s like my social life just… evaporated. In the last six months I’ve only seen friends a handful of times.

One friend treated me pretty badly, which really stung. Others still technically “exist” in my life, but they take forever to respond to texts or give really vague “sometime!” answers when I try to make plans. I’ve tried to make new friends, too – like another parent I met, we had a good conversation, I texted them, and then nothing. Just ghosted. It’s embarrassing how much that still bothers me.

Meanwhile, my spouse has a solid group of friends and regular plans. My sibling too – they’re always chatting in group texts, going to events, having people over. I’m happy for them, but I also feel jealous and sad and left out. It makes me feel like there’s something wrong with me that I can’t seem to have that for myself.

I miss having people I can text on a random afternoon and say “want to grab coffee?” or “come over and hang out.” I don’t want to rely only on my spouse for adult connection. I just want my own close friends again, or a small friend group where I actually feel like I belong.
