I’ve always been the “keeper in touch” person. The one who remembers birthdays, sends check-in messages, tries to keep old friendships alive no matter how much time has passed. I honestly believed that if we were once close, we should always be able to be friends.

Lately I’ve realized how mentally draining that has been. It hurts to reach out to people from my past and get silence, or a half-hearted reply that goes nowhere. It feels like they don’t care about what we once had, and I’m the only one still holding on. It’s starting to feel ungrateful on their part, even if that’s not fair of me to think.

At the start of this year I decided I can’t keep chasing people. I stopped trying to revive old connections and limited myself to a small handful of current ones, even if those are pretty low-intensity. The result is that I’m practically alone.

The most I’ve spoken to anyone recently was with my therapist. Outside of that, I’ve said basically zero words to my so-called “friends.” I do genuinely like my therapist and I’m glad I have that space, but it’s also really sad that my main meaningful conversation is in a paid appointment.

It’s a strange mix of relief and grief: I’m not exhausting myself chasing people anymore, but I also feel more alone than ever.
