On paper my life is pretty great. I’ve got a good, flexible job with no money issues, a loving family, young kids I adore, some hobbies I genuinely enjoy, a dog, decent health, even a men’s group at church and a few friends I can message.

But I feel lonely most of the time.

I’m a very social person and for a long time I felt emotionally fulfilled by my wife. Lately though, she’s become really absorbed in her own hobbies and social media. She’s not mean or anything, just… elsewhere. Emotionally unavailable. Quality one-on-one time feels rare, and when it does happen it’s often distracted or cut short.

I miss feeling like I’m someone’s “person.” I want to feel desired, wanted, and looked forward to by someone, not just needed as a provider or parent. I don’t really feel that from anyone right now.

At the same time, my kids are young and I really want to prioritize time with them. That makes it hard to build deeper friendships or invest in other relationships in a meaningful way, even though I know I probably need that.

I feel stuck between not wanting to sacrifice family time and not wanting to keep feeling this alone while surrounded by people.

Has anyone else felt this kind of loneliness even with a “good” life? How did you deal with it or find a way through?
