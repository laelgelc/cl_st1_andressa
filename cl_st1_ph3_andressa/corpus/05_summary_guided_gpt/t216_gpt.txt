I feel like I’m just… not built for other people.

I’m not funny. I can’t read the room. I’ll think something is obviously a harmless joke, and then it turns out I’ve upset someone or crossed a line I didn’t even see. It’s like everyone else got a manual on how to be socially normal and I missed that day.

The latest thing that really got to me was getting permanently banned from r/amiugly. I used to post there a lot, giving what I thought were respectful, honest, and helpful appearance opinions. It was one of the few places I felt like I was contributing something useful. Then I made one trolling post that I genuinely thought was just lighthearted and harmless, and boom—permanent ban. No warning, no second chance.

It hit harder than it probably should have, because that subreddit had become my main “social life.” I don’t really talk to people otherwise. Most of my “interaction” is just watching YouTubers’ live streams and pretending I’m part of the chat, even though I’m mostly just lurking. It’s like I’m standing outside a window watching everyone else hang out inside.

I recently restarted Lexapro. It’s helping a little—like the sharp edges are sanded down—but it doesn’t change the fact that my life is basically me doing everything alone. I go through my routines, my hobbies, my days, all by myself. I feel like I’m living this quiet, hidden life, like in The Secret Life of Walter Mitty, except there’s nothing epic or adventurous about it. It’s just… unnoticed.

I’m not exactly crying or dramatically sad all the time. It’s more like this constant, heavy awareness that I’m deeply alone, and that when I do try to connect, I somehow mess it up without even understanding how or why.
