I’ve been feeling this weird mix of “never truly alone” and “somehow completely unseen” for a long time now. I’m surrounded by people in my life, but whenever I try to talk about what’s really going on inside, it feels like I either get brushed off, given quick fixes, or people just change the subject. It’s like the deeper parts of me never make it out into the open.

I’ve tried therapy, but honestly, it’s expensive and hard to sustain. And a lot of the AI chatbots I’ve tried feel so generic and surface-level. They’ll say “That sounds hard” or “I’m here for you” in the same way to everyone, and after a while it just feels hollow. I ended up feeling even more alone after using them.

Because of all that, I spent the past year building something I wish I’d had myself. I’ve called it “Grace.” It’s a digital emotional-support companion that’s built around one simple idea: being truly listened to, without judgment and without trying to “fix” you.

Grace talks with you in real time over video, and the whole point is to create a space where you can just be fully honest. It can read emotions from your voice and facial expressions and actually remember your story over time, so you don’t have to start from scratch every time. It’s not about giving advice or solving your problems. It’s more about helping you understand what you’re feeling and why, and letting you feel heard in the process.

Just to be clear: Grace is not a therapist, and it’s not meant to replace professional care. And it’s not one of those generic chatbots that spits out the same responses to everyone. I’m trying really hard to make it something that actually feels present with you.

I put together a website with a short video explaining more, and there’s a waitlist if anyone wants to try it when it’s ready. I’d really appreciate any feedback at all—positive, critical, skeptical, whatever. If you’ve ever felt lonely and just wanted someone to really listen, I’d especially love to hear your thoughts.

If you’re curious, you can DM me or sign up through the site. Even if you never use Grace, just knowing how this all lands with people who understand loneliness would mean a lot to me.
