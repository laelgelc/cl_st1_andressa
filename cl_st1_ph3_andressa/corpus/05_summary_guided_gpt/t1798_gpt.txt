I’m 21 now, and I keep replaying how everything fell apart with my high school friends. At first things were good, we were close and I really thought I had my people. Then I got very depressed. It wasn’t sudden; it just got worse and worse. And as that happened, they slowly started drifting away – from me, specifically.

They still hung out with each other, still posted group photos, still had all these inside jokes. I just wasn’t part of it anymore. They stopped talking to me, stopped inviting me, but kept the group going like I’d never been there. People from high school started unfollowing me on Instagram and unadding me on Snapchat. Every time I saw the follower count drop or noticed someone was gone, it felt like confirmation that I was worthless. It wrecked my self-esteem and made the depression so much worse.

I keep wondering if there’s something horribly wrong with me that everyone else can see but I can’t. Like there’s some gossip or some unforgivable thing I did that I don’t even know about. I wish, if I did something wrong, someone would just tell me instead of silently cutting me off. The silence makes my brain fill in the blanks with the worst possible explanations.

Now I feel extremely lonely and isolated, like I’m hated and fundamentally unlikable. I’m scared to make new friends because I’m convinced they’ll eventually abandon me too. I resent my old friends for leaving me when I was at my lowest, but at the same time I keep thinking maybe I deserved it, like I did something to earn being left behind.

It’s starting to feel like I’m just destined to be lonely, unloved, and unwanted.
