I’m 41 and I feel like my life has just…collapsed in on itself.

In the last little while I lost my best friend and my romantic partner. Saying it like that sounds almost casual, but it doesn’t feel casual at all. It feels like someone pulled the floor out from under me and I’m still falling. I’ve never felt this lonely in my entire life.

I finally started seeing a psychiatrist and a therapist and I know, logically, that it’s the right thing to do. I’ve had unresolved trauma hanging over me for years and I kept putting it off. Now I’m actually facing it and it’s brutal. It’s like everything I’ve buried is coming up at once, and I’m doing it without the two people who used to be my main support.

I’ve tried reaching out here and looking for friends. Some interactions have been nice, some have fizzled, some just left me feeling worse. I did meet someone I really admire, which surprised me, but even then I struggle to maintain any kind of friendship. I isolate, I forget to check in, I disappear. Then I feel pathetic and guilty and it just reinforces this idea that I’m bad at being a person.

When I force myself to go out, to be around people, it somehow makes me feel even lonelier. Watching other people laugh, connect, live their lives… it just highlights how empty mine feels right now.

My mental health has been so bad that life feels kind of pointless. I feel stuck in this self-destructive cycle where every attempt to reach out, to fix things, backfires or falls apart. I’m trying to do the “right” things and it still feels like I’m just spinning my wheels in the dark, alone.
