I’m 19, gay, and have never had a serious relationship. Lately I keep talking to my friends about these cheap, warped Valentine’s teddy bears at the pharmacy where I pick up my meds. I play it off like a joke, but I’m secretly hoping someone will think, “Oh, that’s cute, I’ll get him one,” just platonically. Then I feel pathetic for wanting that so badly, for wanting someone to notice and care about something so small.

With Valentine’s Day coming up, I’ve been thinking about buying myself this little gift basket: heart-shaped chocolate, one of those ugly teddies, and a bedside plant. It’s this old middle school dream of mine, like the kind of thing I imagined getting from a crush someday.

But I’m scared that if I do it, it’ll just make me feel worse, like a reminder that I had to buy it for myself because no one else will. I feel stuck between going through with it or just standing there every week, staring at the Valentine’s section and then walking away alone.
