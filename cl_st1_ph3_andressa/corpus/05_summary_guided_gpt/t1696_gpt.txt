I’m 24 and just got out of prison for drug-related charges I caught when I was younger. Before all this, my life felt completely different. I had a lot of people around me, especially women who wanted to be with me. On the surface it looked good, but underneath I was dealing with addiction and a lot of insecurity from how I grew up.

Now that I’m out, it feels like I have nobody. My phone almost never goes off, but I still keep checking it like something’s going to change. It hits hard realizing how quiet it is, how fast people disappear when you’re not that “fun” person anymore.

Talking to women now is rough. As soon as my past comes up, I can feel the energy shift. I get why they’d be hesitant, and I don’t blame them, but it still hurts. It makes me feel like my whole life is just my worst mistakes.

Even with all that, I honestly feel blessed to be out. I know I’ve got a second chance and I really want to do things right this time. It’s just hard doing it while feeling this alone.
