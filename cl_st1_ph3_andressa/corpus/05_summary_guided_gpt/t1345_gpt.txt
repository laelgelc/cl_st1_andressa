I used to really dread holidays and my birthday. Those days made my loneliness feel huge, like the whole world was celebrating and I was just… there, watching from the outside. Over time, though, something shifted. I started treating those days like any other day, building my own little routines and plans. Now they don’t sting as much.

People don’t really reach out to me, except on my birthday, and even then it’s just a few messages. It’s not ideal, and I won’t pretend I don’t still want more connection. I do. But I’ve realized I don’t actually want a big fuss or attention. For now, simple messages and small check-ins feel like enough, and I’m learning to be okay with that.
