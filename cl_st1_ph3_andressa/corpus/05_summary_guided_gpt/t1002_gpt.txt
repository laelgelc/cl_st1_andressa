I was cleaning and found the little gift I’d picked out for my young neighbor Gabby’s birthday, and it hit me harder than I expected.

Back in March, during that awful blizzard, I brought blankets over to her place when their power went out. I ended up sitting with Gabby and her brother Mario, reading to them while their parents were scared and stressed. It felt like this tiny, warm pocket of connection in the middle of a really dark night.

Then, about a month later, their family suddenly moved while I was out. Another neighbor told me afterward that Gabby had been crying for me, calling me “Marky” like my niece does.

Now her birthday is coming, I don’t know where they went, and I feel so alone. Losing them on top of my own family has been emotionally crushing.
