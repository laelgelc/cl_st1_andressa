I’m so tired of feeling this kind of loneliness that has nothing to do with being “alone.”

I’m not isolated. There are people around me, chats, DMs, random late-night messages. There’s flirting, jokes, memes, surface-level “how was your day?” stuff. But it all feels like eating air. I’m full of interactions and completely starved at the same time.

What I want is someone who actually wants to *know* me. Not just the fun, easy, or “attractive” parts, but the things that actually make me a person—my values, my fears, the pain I carry, the hopes I don’t say out loud because I’m scared they’ll sound stupid or “too much.” Every time I try to go deeper, it’s like I hit a wall.

People shut down. They suddenly become very “private.” Or they deflect with jokes. Or they turn it sexual, like that’s safer than being emotionally honest. I’m touch-starved, yeah, but what hurts more is being *understood*-starved. I’d trade a lot of physical closeness for one person who really sees me and still stays.

I also don’t get why distance is treated like such a dealbreaker for connection. You can feel crushingly alone lying next to someone who doesn’t really know you. And you can feel strangely seen and understood by someone miles away who’s actually willing to be real with you. It’s not about geography for me; it’s about honesty and depth.

I’m not looking for a savior or some fantasy soulmate. I don’t need perfection. I just want a connection that isn’t performative or conditional—where both of us are allowed to be real, messy, scared, and still choose to show up.

For those of you who feel this intense, chest-ache kind of loneliness: what helps, even a little? And for anyone who’s actually found the kind of depth I’m talking about—what made it different from all the emotional crumbs the rest of us are surviving on?
