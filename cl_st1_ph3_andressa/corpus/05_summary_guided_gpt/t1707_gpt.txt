I’m 23 and I feel like my body is already giving up on me.

I’m in constant physical pain. Even basic stuff like bending my knees hurts. I had to quit my sport, which used to be the one thing that made me feel alive and connected to other people. Now I just feel like a shell of who I was.

I can’t afford to see a doctor, get meds, or go to therapy. I’m so broke that I’m down to one packet of ramen a day. I avoid any social event that involves spending money, especially food. It’s humiliating to sit there and not order anything or make excuses about why I “already ate.”

My mental health is going downhill with my body. I’m exhausted all the time, physically and mentally. My friends and peers don’t really get it. They’re out living their lives, doing “normal” 23-year-old things, and I’m struggling with simple tasks that shouldn’t be this hard.

When I try to explain my pain or fatigue, I see it in their faces: confusion, pity, that awkward “I don’t know what to say” look. I end up feeling guilty for even bringing it up, like I’m dragging everyone down. I feel like a burden, like I’m making people uncomfortable just by being honest.

I hate that people look at me and see someone broken or “not normal.” I want to be seen as an equal, not as someone to be pitied. So I pull back. I don’t open up, I don’t get close, and then I feel even more isolated.

I don’t really know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I just feel so alone in this pain, in this body, in this life I can barely afford to live.
