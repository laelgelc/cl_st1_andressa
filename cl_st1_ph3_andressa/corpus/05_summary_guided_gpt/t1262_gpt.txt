I’m starting to feel like my personality is fundamentally mismatched with the kind of relationships I want.

I’m kind, smart, nerdy, and very serious. I live in my head, constantly thinking about goals, ambitions, ideas. My friendships usually revolve around emotional support or deep intellectual conversations. That part I’m actually good at. But when it comes to romance, I’ve often felt like I’m “too much in my head,” or just plain boring.

I’ve noticed that the relationships that seem to last – both in my life and around me – almost always have this element of humor and lightness. Recently I’ve really seen how powerful humor can be, especially in attracting women. It almost feels like a cheat code I just don’t have.

My “humor” has been pretty bad for 23 years. I can fake being funny for short bursts, but it drains me and feels unnatural. I have no idea how to be spontaneously funny without forcing it.

So I’m stuck: do I just accept that I’m a serious person, or should I actually try to change and become more humorous? And if I should change, how do you even do that without being fake?
