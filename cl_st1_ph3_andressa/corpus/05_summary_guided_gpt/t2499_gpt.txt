I’m 22 and I feel like I’m living in this weird in‑between where I’m not completely alone, but I still feel incredibly lonely most of the time.

I do have a few close friends, and I’m grateful for them. But I’ve never been in a relationship, not even close. It’s not like I’ve been rejected a bunch of times; I’ve basically never asked anyone out because the fear of rejection is so intense that I just freeze up. It feels safer to not try at all than to risk hearing “no.”

I’ve always preferred friendships with women. I just feel more comfortable around them in theory, but in practice I’m constantly second‑guessing myself. I worry they’ll think I’m being creepy or that I’m only talking to them because I’m trying to hit on them, so I end up avoiding trying to befriend them at all. When I do talk to women, I overanalyze every word and body language cue and convince myself I’ve made them uncomfortable, even if they seemed fine.

Socializing in general feels like I’m bothering people just by existing in their space. My awkwardness makes me feel like some kind of defective person who just doesn’t know how to be normal. I’m stuck between wanting connection and being terrified of making people uncomfortable or getting rejected, and it’s exhausting.
