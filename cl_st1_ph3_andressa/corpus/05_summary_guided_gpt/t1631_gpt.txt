I’m a 30-year-old woman from South Africa and lately the loneliness has become so intense it almost feels physical, like an ache in my chest and throat. The smallest things set it off. Sometimes it’s literally the first few seconds of a song and I’m suddenly back in high school or my early 20s, surrounded by people, laughing, feeling like life was actually moving somewhere.

I used to be social. Not the life of the party, but I had friends, plans, inside jokes. Then lockdown happened, I got COVID four times, and somewhere in all of that my social anxiety just exploded. I stopped seeing people, stopped reaching out, and slowly most of my friendships faded away. Now I watch everyone else getting married, having kids, building careers, and I feel completely stuck in place, like I got off the train and it just never came back.

I hoard memories of the “good times” in my head and replay them constantly. It’s like I live more in those memories than in my actual life. I’m studying full-time online to become a counsellor, which I’m passionate about, but I almost never see people in person. I recently hit burnout and it made everything feel even heavier.

I’ve gained weight over the past few years, and it’s wrecked my self-esteem. The only small “positive” is that I understand more deeply now how people use food to cope, and I feel more empathy. I used to drink to feel social and loosen up, but I quit because of antidepressants, a strong family history of alcoholism, some really bad decisions I made with lowered inhibitions, and my growing Christian faith.

I really want to overcome this social anxiety, maybe reconnect with old friends or somehow meet new people, but I feel so far behind and broken. If anyone has gone through something similar or has practical advice on how to start rebuilding a social life from basically zero, I’d really appreciate it.
