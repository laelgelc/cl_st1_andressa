I’m autistic and I’ve spent years trying to convince myself I was just “overthinking it,” that I was misreading how people treated me. But after a lot of careful reflection and professional help, I can’t hide from the reality anymore: people care about everyone except me.

Even during times when I was barely holding on, when I genuinely wasn’t sure I wanted to stay alive, it felt like everyone around me seemed to have someone who cared, someone who checked in, someone who showed affection. And then there was me. Invisible. It’s not that I needed to be anyone’s first choice or top priority. I just wanted to be “someone” to somebody.

I’ve always tried to be there for others, listening, supporting, showing up when I could. But it feels like I’m not even seen as a person, more like background noise. That kind of dehumanization sticks with you. It’s like carrying a weight around that never gets lighter.

I want to meet people like me, people who understand what it’s like to be overlooked and still want to learn how to be around others after years of being mistreated. I wish I had the strength to support others more, but right now I feel too weak, like I don’t even deserve basic self-worth.

I’m not trying to be a victim, and I don’t care about being popular or liked by everyone. I just want to feel human.
