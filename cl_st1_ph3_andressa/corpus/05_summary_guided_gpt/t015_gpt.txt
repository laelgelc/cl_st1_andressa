I know this is going to sound weird to a lot of people, but Star Wars stopped being “just movies” for me a long time ago.

Somewhere along the way it turned into an actual framework for how I want to live. I don’t mean I think I’m swinging a lightsaber or that I’m delusional about living in a galaxy far, far away. It’s more that the idea of being a Jedi clicked with something deep inside me in a way religion never did.

When I say “Jedi,” I mean a keeper of peace and history, someone who tries to live with integrity and calm, guided by something bigger than themselves. For me, that “something” is the Force, and the closest real-world word I have for it is intuition or instinct. That gut feeling that tells you when something is right or wrong, when you should move forward or step back.

Listening to that has actually changed my life. I used to live in what I’d call my own Darkside: laziness, lying to myself and sometimes others, giving in to despair and self‑hatred. I really hated who I was for a long time. Seeing that struggle through the lens of Light vs Dark helped me name it. It gave me a language for choosing differently: “Is this the Darkside talking, or is this the Light?” That simple question has pulled me back from some pretty bad places.

Traditional religion never gave me that. I tried, but it always felt like I was forcing myself to believe something external. With the Force, it feels like I’m finally listening to what was already inside me. It’s the first time I can honestly say I have faith in something, and it’s made me happier and more at peace than I’ve ever been.

What hurts is that when I talk about this, people assume I’m crazy or trying to look special or “deep.” They think I’m doing some kind of performance or chasing attention. I’m not. I don’t want fame. I’m just trying to share something that genuinely helped me stop hating myself and start choosing happiness.

If anyone else feels lost or stuck in their own Darkside and wants to hear more about how I see the Force, I made a YouTube video talking about it. I don’t expect everyone to get it, but if even one person feels less alone or finds a bit of light from it, that’s enough for me.
