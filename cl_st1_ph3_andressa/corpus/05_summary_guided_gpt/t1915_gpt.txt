I don’t even know how to describe what these last few years have done to me.

For the first two years of college, I basically lived in the lab. Not because I was some ultra-motivated genius, but because my roommate situation was so bad I would do anything to avoid being in my own room. I’d be there 10–15 hours a day, surrounded by groups of people laughing and talking, and still feel like a ghost hovering around the edges. Everyone had their people. I was just… there.

Classes weren’t much better. I could sit in the same lecture hall week after week and still feel completely invisible. There was this guy who, for a while, I thought might actually see me. He’d show interest, then suddenly disappear and act like I was a stranger when we crossed paths. It happened enough times that I finally had to let go of whatever hope I’d built up. That hurt way more than I expected.

Now college is almost over. On paper, things look fine. I have a good internship lined up. Career-wise, I guess I’m “doing well.” But emotionally, I feel like I’ve spent four years walking through this place without ever really existing in it.

My current roommate is the opposite of my old one: extremely extroverted, talks nonstop, calls me a close friend. But when I try to share anything real, it’s like my words just slide off her. She doesn’t really listen, just waits to talk again. Somehow that makes me feel even more alone than being physically by myself.

I’m tired of feeling unseen and unheard. I don’t know how to process all of this or how to go into the corporate world without just carrying this same emptiness with me. How do you cope with long-term loneliness like this? How do you keep going when you’ve spent years feeling like you don’t belong anywhere?
