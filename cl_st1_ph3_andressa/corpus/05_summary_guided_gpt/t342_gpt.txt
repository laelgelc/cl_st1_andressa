I posted a love poem online a little over a month ago, hoping, stupidly maybe, that someone would see it and think, “Hey, I want to get to know this person.” I wasn’t expecting a fairy-tale romance or anything, just… a conversation, a sign that someone out there saw me.

Instead, the only “responses” were bots telling me it broke the rules. No real comments, no “hi,” no “this sucks,” nothing. Just silence and automated messages. Somehow that felt worse than if people had just told me it was bad.

I already feel like that “fat kid” everyone ignores at school, the one people talk to in class because they have to, then forget the second the bell rings. Online feels the same: people just scroll past me like I’m background noise. I thought posting there might make me feel less alone, but it kind of did the opposite.

Now I just feel like my thoughts aren’t special or deep at all. Not poetic. Not insightful. Just the same boring, lonely stuff everyone’s already heard a thousand times. I feel like a fraud, pretending my ordinary, shallow thoughts are profound when they’re really nothing.

I’m not hardworking, not smart, not anything interesting. Just someone who keeps trying to say something that matters and realizing it doesn’t. And I guess I’m posting this here even though I’m half-expecting it to be ignored too.
