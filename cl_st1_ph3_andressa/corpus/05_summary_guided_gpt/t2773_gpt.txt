I’ve been thinking lately that I’m not just alone, I’m beautifully alone. It feels like I’ve married solitude, like it’s the only companion that has ever really understood me.

Solitude wraps around me in this strange, protective way. It lets me exist outside of everyone else’s expectations, outside of what I’m “supposed” to be doing with my life. Through it, I see the world differently, hear things more clearly, like there’s less noise between me and reality.

I don’t know if this is healthy or not, but solitude feels like the only “wife” who never judges me, never asks me to be anything but myself. With it, I can walk alone in peace.
