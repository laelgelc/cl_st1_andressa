I just said goodbye to my dog Blue, my best friend for the last 15 years. I got him when I was 21, so he’s been there for basically all of my adult life. I stayed up all night dreading the moment, holding onto every second I had left with him. When he finally passed in my arms, it felt like my chest caved in.

Now my days are just… somber. I move through them, but it’s like I’m not really there. I can’t seem to let go. The grief comes in waves out of nowhere – washing dishes, trying to sleep, hearing a random sound and thinking it’s his paws on the floor. Then it hits me all over again that he’s gone.

I’m scared about going back to work and pretending to be okay when I feel anything but. It’s like that Taylor Swift lyric: “I’ve never been a natural, all I do is try, try, try.” Everything feels dark and lonely without him, and I don’t really know how to do life without Blue.
