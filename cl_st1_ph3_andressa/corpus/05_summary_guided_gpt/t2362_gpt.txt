I’m 26 and I keep going back and forth on whether I’m actually lonely or just… disappointed. I’m not isolated in the classic sense: I have family, a partner, and a few friends I can hang out with. But a lot of my long-term friendships have faded, and that loss hurts in a way I don’t really know how to process.

What gets to me most is one friend in particular. We were basically inseparable from about 15–21. We’d stay up stupidly late gaming together, talking about everything. He was my “best friend” in the way you only really have in your teens.

Now he’s always “very busy.” When I ask if he wants to play something, I usually get a vague “yeah, we’ll play sometime” and then nothing. Meanwhile I can see he’s online playing other games with other people. We still meet up maybe once or twice a year, and when we do, it feels almost like old times – we get along, we joke, there’s no obvious tension. Then we go back to barely talking.

I’m finding I feel like I’m constantly having to chase some of my old friends. If I don’t message first, we just don’t talk. If I suggest meeting up, I get noncommittal answers or long delays. It makes me wonder if I’m expecting too much, or if I’m just not that important to them anymore and I’m refusing to accept it.

So I guess my question is: what’s actually reasonable to expect from friends as adults?  

- How often do you talk to your close friends?  
- How quickly do they usually respond?  
- How often do you see each other in person?  

I’m trying to figure out whether I’m holding onto an unrealistic, teenage version of friendship or if my feelings of being sidelined are valid and I should stop fighting so hard for people who don’t really show up anymore.
