I’m a 22-year-old guy and I feel like I’m doing everything I’m “supposed” to do, and yet I’m still completely alone.

I join clubs at uni, I go to events, I talk to people, I go to the gym, I ask girls out, I use dating apps. I’m not sitting in my room doing nothing. But none of it ever turns into an actual connection. People stay as acquaintances, matches don’t go anywhere, and I end up back in my own head wondering what the hell is wrong with me.

I’m pretty left-leaning, so it really messes with me when I see people online talking about lonely men like we’re all evil fascists in the making, or that we “deserve” to be lonely, or that we’re not actually lonely and should just “go outside.” I am going outside. I am trying. It makes me feel like I must secretly be some horrible person just because I’m still alone.

I don’t think I’m entitled to friends or a girlfriend. I know no one owes me anything. I just feel like I’ve rolled bad dice in life or maybe there’s something off about me (maybe I’m autistic, I don’t know). And when men are told to open up, then get mocked or have their feelings used against them, it makes it even harder to be honest about this.

I’m not angry at the world, just really sad and confused. I wish I knew what I’m doing wrong, or if I’m just fundamentally unlikable without realizing it.
