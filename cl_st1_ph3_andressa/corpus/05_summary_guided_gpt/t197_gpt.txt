Last year feels like it happened to a different person.

I was on track for a promotion, had a solid friend group, and there was this girl I really liked. For the first time in a long time, I actually felt like things were moving in the right direction. I started to picture a future where I wasn’t constantly lonely or doubting myself.

Then everything just fell apart.

The promotion fell through. I started school on top of working, and that completely wrecked my social life. I stopped seeing my friends as much, and they slowly drifted away. The girl I liked told me she only wanted something casual and that she doesn’t catch feelings. I really loved her, and hearing that just crushed me.

I ended up quitting my job, which meant I lost my work friends too. I got depressed, stopped working out, and isolated myself. It felt like I went from having momentum to just… nothing. No progress, no people, no purpose.

Now I’m trying to rebuild, but it doesn’t feel hopeful the way it did before. I have a new job, but I don’t really like the people there. I started working out again, but I feel weak and everything hurts. I’m planning to go back to school in the summer and give it another shot.

The thing is, I don’t really have many real friends anymore. My relationship with my family isn’t great. And a romantic relationship honestly feels unrealistic at this point. I’m trying to “improve myself” like everyone always says, but underneath it all I still feel this deep pain.

I feel worthless a lot of the time, like I’ll never be seen as worth anyone’s time. Like I’m never going to be good enough at anything — not as a partner, not as a friend, not in my career. It’s hard to keep going and keep trying when it feels like everything I do just proves that belief right.
