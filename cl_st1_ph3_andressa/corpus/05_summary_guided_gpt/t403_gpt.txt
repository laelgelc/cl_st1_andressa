From the outside, I probably look like I’m doing okay. I function, I show up, I make small talk. But inside, I feel like I’m constantly bracing for impact, like my body is always waiting for something bad to happen. It’s this permanent tension, like I’m never really safe, even when nothing obvious is wrong.

I’ve gone through different addictions in the past, thinking they were what I wanted. But none of them ever touched that deep, wired-in feeling of unsafety. Eventually it clicked for me that I wasn’t actually craving the substances or distractions themselves—I was craving a sense of safety, of being able to relax in my own skin.

Learning about trauma, attachment, and the nervous system shifted how I see my loneliness. It’s less about “I’m alone and have no one” and more about “I don’t feel safe in myself or with other people.” I can be physically around others and still feel completely alone because my body doesn’t trust that I’m okay.

I couldn’t really find anything that addressed this in a way that actually helped me, so I ended up putting together a small “nervous-system reset” thing for myself. It’s not therapy, it’s not motivational talk or mindset stuff—just something simple aimed at helping my body come out of survival mode for a bit.
