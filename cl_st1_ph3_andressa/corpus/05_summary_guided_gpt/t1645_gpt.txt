I’ve felt lonely for as long as I can remember. I grew up in a lower-middle-class, close-knit neighborhood where everyone knew each other, but my family kept getting pushed out by landlords who wanted us gone. We moved a lot, and every time we did, it felt like starting over from zero. That made me really cautious about people and unsure of myself. I still struggle with low self-esteem because of it.

At home, my siblings are very competitive. They always told me that no one is really your friend unless you “become someone” in life. That idea got into my head so deeply that I started seeing friendships as something transactional, like “friends for benefits.” Instead of just wanting kind, loyal people around me, I started looking for smart, well-off friends who seemed successful or useful in some way.

Of course, that mindset didn’t help. I struggled to make real friends and to keep the ones I did have. I’ve lost a lot of friendships over the years, and I can’t even say for sure if it was always my fault or just the way I approached people. Now I feel like I’m stuck: I’m lonely, but I’m also not around the kind of people I think I want to be friends with.

On top of that, I rarely go to social events because I’m always studying. It’s like I’m trying to “become someone” so I can finally deserve good friends, but in the process I’m isolating myself even more. I don’t really know how to break out of this pattern or how to see friendships in a healthier way. I just know I’m tired of feeling this alone.
