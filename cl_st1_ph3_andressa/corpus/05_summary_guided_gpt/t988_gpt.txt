I think what hurts me the most isn’t just that he cheated, but how easily he lied about it.

It started with small things. He suddenly became so protective of his phone, taking it everywhere, turning it face down, changing his passwords. He stopped really touching me, stopped initiating anything intimate, and whenever I tried to get close he had some excuse — tired, stressed, not in the mood. I kept trying to convince myself I was overthinking.

Then one night he said he was going out with his “cousin.” I didn’t question it. But later, a friend sent me a photo from a bar: him with a female coworker, way too close, obviously not just friends. The way he was looking at her, touching her, it was like watching a version of him I didn’t recognize.

When he came home, he acted completely normal. I asked how his night was and he just smiled and told me about this imaginary cousin, like it was the most natural thing in the world. No hesitation, no guilt that I could see.

That’s when it really hit me: he wasn’t just unfaithful, he was comfortable lying straight to my face. And somehow, that betrayal feels even heavier than the cheating itself.
