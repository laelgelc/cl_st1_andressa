I just started college and on paper things look great. I go out a lot, I have a decent number of friends, I get invited to parties pretty often. People tell me I’m handsome, interesting, fun to be around. From the outside it probably looks like I’m doing everything “right.”

But I feel insanely lonely.

What really messes with my head is watching other people have that one person they can text or call anytime. A best friend they can send random thoughts to, or lean on when they’re upset. I don’t have that. My conversations feel shallow. People reply slowly, or the chats just die off. I don’t feel like anyone is actually excited to talk to me or really wants to know what’s going on in my head.

I’m starting to wonder if the version of me everyone sees – the outgoing, social, party guy – makes people think I don’t *need* anything deeper. Like they assume I’m fine, so they keep things surface-level.

Two years ago I broke up with my ex, and she was also my only real best friend. Since then I haven’t had anyone I can really open up to. I crave that kind of connection so badly – someone I can talk to about anything, who won’t ignore me or make me feel like a burden. I want a best friend, or a girlfriend, or just one person who actually feels like “home.”

Right now it feels like I’m surrounded by people, but completely alone.
