I’m 26 and I feel stuck in this weird loop in my head. Everyone keeps saying, “Get hobbies, do things alone, put yourself out there, work on yourself,” and I’m trying to accept that. But I’m scared that even if I do all of that, I’ll still feel just as lonely and depressed as I do now.

I really believe humans are social, that it’s normal to want to share what you love with other people. I don’t want to feel pathetic for wanting connection, or like I’m “using” future friends as emotional crutches. I don’t want to be that person who’s just desperate for anyone to fill a void. But at the same time, I can’t pretend I’m okay with being alone all the time, either.

I’m trying to find friends, but it feels like either it’s not working or I’m not trying hard enough. I keep thinking I should be going to more events, talking to more people, joining more things. And then I worry that even if I do somehow manage to build a social life, it still won’t fix this empty feeling.

On top of that, I’m scared of hitting 30 or 40 and realizing I never did anything exciting or out of my comfort zone—stuff like hang gliding, ziplining, bungee jumping, karaoke, whatever. I don’t want life to just quietly pass me by.

My main question is: is it possible that even after getting hobbies and pushing myself socially, I’ll still feel this lonely? And if so, then what?
