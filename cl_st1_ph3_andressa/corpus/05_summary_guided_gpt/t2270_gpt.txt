I don’t really know why I’m writing this, because I don’t feel like I have anything good or helpful to say. But I guess I just need to let it out somewhere.

Being homeless is the loneliest thing I’ve ever experienced. It’s not just “being alone,” it’s this constant awareness that you don’t belong anywhere. I avoid people as much as I can, especially in person. My hygiene is terrible and I’m always afraid that people can smell me, see the dirt, notice how rough I look. I don’t want to see the disgust or pity on their faces. It’s easier to just stay away than risk that kind of rejection.

I even avoid people online. I feel like all I have to offer is negativity and misery, and I don’t want to drag anyone else down with me. Everyone seems to have at least one small good thing to share, and I don’t. I feel like a black hole that just sucks the light out of every conversation.

I honestly believe I deserve this. I see myself as a failure, someone who didn’t manage to become a real adult, someone who’s unworthy of friends or any kind of care. I have no friends. No family, really. My mom died, and after that my immediate family just sort of fell apart. My extended family exists, but there’s no emotional support there. When I do reach out, I feel like I’m just an obligation, a problem they have to deal with. A burden.

I’ve been living like this for almost seven years now. Seven years of drifting, hiding, trying not to be seen. The emotional pain of having “next to no one” in my life is constant, like this dull ache that never stops.

My 25th birthday is coming up, and instead of feeling hopeful or excited, I just feel… empty. Twenty‑five is supposed to feel like you’re starting your life. I feel like mine never really started at all.
