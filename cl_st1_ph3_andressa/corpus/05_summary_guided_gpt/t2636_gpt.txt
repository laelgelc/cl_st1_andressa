Lately I’ve been thinking that the comfort we get from depending on other people is kind of a trap. It feels nice in the moment, but it turns into a kind of slavery.

I keep picturing two dogs. One is a pampered pet: warm bed, full bowl, constant attention. The other is a street dog: hungry, cold, always struggling. On the surface, it seems obvious which one is better off. But the pet dog’s whole world depends on its owner. If the owner leaves, changes, or turns cruel, that dog is helpless. The street dog suffers, but its survival doesn’t hinge on someone else’s mood or presence. It’s free, in a harsh way.

I feel like our society trains us to become that pampered pet emotionally. We’re told we need relationships, constant validation, and external approval to be happy. Then when people drift away, change, or turn on us, it hurts like hell because our whole sense of self was tied to them.

I’m starting to think the only way to be truly happy and free is to be more of a lone wolf, a loner who doesn’t need anyone to be okay.
