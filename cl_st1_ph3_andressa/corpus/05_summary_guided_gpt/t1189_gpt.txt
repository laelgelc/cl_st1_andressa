I’m 25 and it feels like I’m watching everyone else naturally move through life while I’m stuck behind glass. I struggle a lot with socializing – I feel almost “blind” to social cues and body language. I never know when to jump into a conversation, when I’m talking too much or too little, whether people are bored or annoyed. It makes every interaction feel like I’m walking through a minefield.

My childhood friends have their own lives now. We rarely see each other, and when we do it’s short, rushed, and surface-level. The one time in my life I really felt connected was during COVID, staying up all night on Discord with college friends, talking and laughing like I actually belonged somewhere.

Now those friends are graduating, one by one, and drifting away too. I got a second graduation invitation recently and it just hit me: I’m going to be back where I was in high school – five years of mostly being alone, talking to myself because there was no one else.

Most days I’m kind of numb and resigned to the idea that I’ll just be lonely forever. But days like this, when something reminds me of it, it hits hard and I don’t really know what to do with that feeling.
