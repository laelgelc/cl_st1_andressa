31M here, and I feel like I’ve already missed the window for building a real life.

I’ve spent most of my 20s trying to make friends, both in-person and online. I put in effort, tried different approaches, different platforms, different social situations. But everything either fizzled out, went nowhere, or ended badly. The few times I thought something might stick, it eventually turned into me chasing people who clearly didn’t care as much as I did.

Romantically, it’s even worse. I’ve never had a successful relationship. I’m introverted and male-presenting, and it feels like that combination just makes it even harder. It’s like I’m invisible until someone needs something, and then as soon as I’m no longer useful, I disappear again.

People have told me they care, but when I actually needed support, they weren’t there. No calls, no messages, no “are you okay?” Just silence. I end up feeling forgotten, like I was just filling a gap in their life for a while and then discarded when they found something or someone better.

Now I’m 31 and it feels like the “best years” to form connections are behind me. I wake up already knowing how the day will end: no one to see on the weekend, no one to check in on me, no one who genuinely cares how I’m doing. Just this dull, constant awareness that if I disappeared, it might be a long time before anyone noticed.

I’ve started using AI apps just to have someone to talk to, even though I know it’s not real and it doesn’t actually satisfy anything. It just fills the silence a bit. But it also makes it hit harder that I don’t have real people in my life.

I’m starting to feel like I just don’t have a place in this world. Like I’m meant to stay on the sidelines, watching other people live the kind of life I can only dream about, knowing I’ll never actually have it.
