I’m under 18 and pretty much physically isolated, so I don’t really have any real-life friends. Since 2019, online friendships have been my whole social life. I have three main online friends – I’ll call them S, A, and L. I care about them a lot. I know their birthdays, favorite things, family stuff, random stories they’ve told me. I remember details and try to show I’m there for them.

But I’m starting to feel like I don’t matter that much to them.

None of them remember my birthday. They don’t know basic things about me that I’ve told them multiple times. I’ll mention something important about myself and it’s like it just disappears. Meanwhile, they’ll be hanging out with other people, in calls or servers, and I only get a short “hey” when they’re bored or have nothing else to do.

It’s like I’m the backup option, the filler conversation when no one more interesting is around.

When I try to bring this up, they just brush it off. They’ll say something like “you’re overthinking” or quickly change the subject. I end up feeling dramatic or needy, even though I don’t think it’s asking much to be remembered or listened to.

Now I’m starting to care less, and I feel guilty about that. I feel used and overlooked, but at the same time I’m scared to distance myself because they’re literally my only form of communication. If I cut contact, I’ll have nobody. But staying feels like slowly realizing I’m not as important to them as they are to me.

I don’t know what to do with these feelings. I feel stuck between being alone with no one, or staying with people who make me feel unimportant.
