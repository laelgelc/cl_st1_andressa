I’m 31 now and it feels like my whole adult life has just been one long lesson in being used and then discarded.

I met my ex-husband when I was 21. We got married, had a child, and for six years I stayed even though he cheated on me over and over. I kept trying to make it work for our son, kept hoping he’d change, but in the end he left us. He wants nothing to do with our child now. Watching my son grow up without a dad who even cares to know him is its own kind of heartbreak.

After a year on my own, I thought maybe I was ready to try again. I started dating someone long-distance. Eventually I moved to be with him, about two hours away from my family. It felt like a big risk, but I really believed in us.

We were together for 3.5 years. Between my son and his two kids, we had three children in the house most of the time. I was the one mostly supporting us. When he was unemployed and depressed, I spent hours helping him apply for jobs, redoing his resume, pushing him to not give up. He finally got a job he liked, then a promotion to manager, and even a company truck. I was genuinely happy for him.

And then he broke up with me.

It’s hard not to see a pattern: both men left right after they “got everything” they seemed to want—stability, support, someone to carry them through the hard parts. Once life started looking up for them, I was suddenly no longer needed.

Now I’m here, two hours from my family, feeling like most of my light is just…gone. I feel empty and stupid for trusting again. I feel like I poured myself into other people until there was nothing left for me, and now I’m alone with a child to raise and no one to lean on.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by posting this. I just feel so used and so alone.
