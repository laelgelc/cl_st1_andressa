I’m 26 and it’s been about two years since I’ve had any real friends or social contact outside of my family. Sometimes it hits me all at once how isolated I’ve become.

For about 10 years I had one close friend who was basically my whole social life. We were always together or talking, and I thought that was enough. Then we had a big argument and just stopped talking. At first I was devastated and blamed myself, but with some distance I realized they were actually pretty toxic in a lot of ways. Still, knowing that doesn’t make the loneliness any easier. It’s like losing them took my entire social world with it.

Right now I’m in my final year of an adult high school program. You’d think being surrounded by people my age (or at least around my age) would help, but I just can’t seem to connect with anyone. I try small talk sometimes, but it never seems to go anywhere. People are polite, but I always get the feeling they’re not really interested in knowing me beyond surface-level stuff. I end up sitting alone most of the time, both in class and during breaks.

Outside of school, I’m basically at home all the time. The only time I go out is with my family on trips or errands. I appreciate them, but it’s not the same as having friends of my own. I feel like my world is so small, and I don’t even know where to start to change that.

Is anyone else in a similar situation—no friends at all, just family, and struggling to make any new connections? How do you cope with it, or even start to break out of it?
