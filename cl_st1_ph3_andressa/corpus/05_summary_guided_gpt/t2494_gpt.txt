I realized the other day that I haven’t had a real, personal conversation in weeks. I talk on work calls, but that’s just tasks and deadlines. My family group chat is basically just memes and short reactions. Nothing that feels like actually being known by someone.

Meanwhile, my feeds are full of people going to dinners, parties, game nights. I honestly can’t remember the last time I was invited anywhere. It’s like life moved on without me and I’m just watching through a window.

I’ve gotten so used to being alone that when my neighbor tried to make small talk recently, I felt this rush of anxiety and just wanted to escape. I cut the interaction short and then felt weirdly ashamed about it afterward.

Before the pandemic, I did have friends and some kind of social life. When everything went online, I just… never found my way back. Now it feels like I’m stuck in this loop: I’m lonely, but I’m also anxious about reaching out, so I stay alone and feel even worse.

I guess I’m posting this because keeping it all in was starting to feel unbearable.
