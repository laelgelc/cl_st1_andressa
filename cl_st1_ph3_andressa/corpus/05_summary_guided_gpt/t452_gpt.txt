I’m 20 and I feel like my life has been on pause for as long as I can remember.

Everyone my age seems to be out there actually living – studying, going out with their friends, going on trips, dating, having all these “normal” experiences – and I’m just… here. Stuck. I have chronic illnesses on top of depression and anxiety, and I’m sick a lot. Hospital stays are such a regular part of my life that they almost feel more “normal” than anything else. Because of all this, I never really got to have a friend group, or parties, or any kind of romantic relationship. All the typical teen/young adult stuff just passed me by.

Social media makes it worse. I see ex-classmates and ex-friends posting their memories, their nights out, their relationships, and it feels like I’m watching from behind glass. It hurts more than I can explain. It just feels so unfair. I scroll and it’s like I’m being reminded over and over that I have “absolutely no life,” that I’m just here to suffer while everyone else gets to actually live.

This has been my reality since I was a kid, so it’s not even like I remember a time when things were truly different. I feel empty and left behind, like I’m years behind everyone else with no way to catch up.

I don’t even know what to do anymore. Is there anything I can genuinely do to escape this? To stop feeling like my existence is just hospitals, illness, and watching other people’s lives from the outside?
