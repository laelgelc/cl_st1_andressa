I keep getting told that “being alone is good for you” and that I just need to “love myself first.” I *do* understand the importance of self‑love, and I’m not against it. But I don’t want to spend my life alone. It doesn’t feel freeing or empowering to me; it just feels empty, unfulfilling, and honestly, boring.

I want to share my life with someone. I want that deep connection, the feeling of really being seen and understood. I miss human touch. I want to hold someone and be held, to have those little everyday moments that you just can’t have by yourself. There’s a kind of love and care I want to give and receive that I simply cannot provide for myself, no matter how much “self‑love” I practice.

For me, existing for and with someone else gives my life meaning. It’s not that I think I’m incomplete, but being connected to another person in that way feels like what I’m meant for.

Sometimes I wonder if this makes me worse than people who are genuinely content being single. Like I’m less “cool” or mature because I actually want and need someone. I don’t know. I just wish wanting love didn’t feel like something I have to defend.
