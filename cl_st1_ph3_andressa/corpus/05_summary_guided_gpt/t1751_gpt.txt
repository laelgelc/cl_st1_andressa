I’m 27 and I don’t think I’ve had a real friend or genuine connection since high school. I love my family, and I’m grateful that I have a job I actually enjoy, but underneath that I feel this constant, heavy loneliness that I can’t shake. I feel socially awkward all the time, like I’m always just slightly out of sync with everyone else.

My coworkers are nice, and on paper they seem like people I could be friends with. But they’ll make plans, go out together, and I only hear about it after the fact. It’s not like they’re mean to me, but I’m never actually included. It makes me feel like I’m invisible, or like there’s something fundamentally off about me that other people can sense.

I want friends. I want a romantic partner. But I don’t understand how I’m supposed to find someone to date when I can’t even manage to build and maintain basic friendships. The older I get, the more it feels like everyone else is moving forward while I’m stuck. My family members are starting their own families, building lives that feel full and connected, and I’m just… on the outside, watching.

I’ve tried opening up to people so many times, and every time I end up drifting out of their lives and being forgotten. It’s starting to feel like I’ve given up, like I’m quietly accepting that I’m going to be alone, even though that’s the last thing I actually want.
