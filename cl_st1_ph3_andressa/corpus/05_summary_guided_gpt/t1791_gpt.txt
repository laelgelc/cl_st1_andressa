I’m a 24-year-old guy from Italy and for most of my life I’ve felt like I was trapped inside my own head, watching the world from behind glass. I’ve had some painful experiences with people in the past that made me really afraid of others, to the point where I started believing it was safer to stay alone and build my own mental cage rather than risk being hurt again.

Because of that, I’ve become extremely isolated. When I’m around most people, I feel like I can’t be myself at all. I’m tense, overthinking every word, and I put on this mask just to seem “normal” and acceptable. It’s exhausting and draining, but I’ve started to realize that as uncomfortable as it is, it’s still better for my mental health than complete isolation.

Lately I’ve been trying to work on myself. It’s slow, and it’s not some big dramatic change, but I can see small improvements. I’m forcing myself to be around people more, even if it means masking and pretending to fit in for now. My hope is that if I keep doing this, I’ll rebuild some energy, gain more social experience, and eventually find people I can actually be myself with.

I’m also trying to put my ego aside and challenge this negative view I’ve developed about people in general. I’m trying to believe that there are others out there who are similar to me, who might understand what it’s like to feel this way.

Honestly, just being able to write this out and share it feels like a big step forward for me. It makes me feel like things are very slowly moving in a better direction, even if I’m still far from where I want to be.
