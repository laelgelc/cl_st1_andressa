I’m turning 21 soon and I feel like I’m just… floating while everyone else is on some clear path.

After high school I had no big passion or dream job. I tried a private university and bounced from marketing to psychology, but I never really connected with anyone there. My anxiety got so bad that I ended up dropping out. Since then I’ve done some online courses and short in-person classes, but I decided not to go back to college for now.

Instead, I’m planning to open a small business and focus on learning practical things like cooking, Italian, and first aid. Part of me is excited about that, but another part feels like I’m missing out on the “college experience” everyone talks about – the dorms, the late-night talks, the big friend groups, all of it.

I technically have 1–2 close friends, but they each have their own “person,” and I don’t really feel like anyone’s first choice. I don’t enjoy partying unless I really click with people, and right now I just don’t. Since I got back from a month-long trip recently, I haven’t gone out much at all.

I’m trying to change that: I want to find a new gym, go out alone more, maybe just sit in cafés or explore the city. But every time I go on social media and see people in big friend groups, at college events, or posting about “the best years of their lives,” I feel this heavy loneliness and like I’m falling behind.

I don’t know if anyone else is in a similar situation, but I just needed to get this off my chest.
