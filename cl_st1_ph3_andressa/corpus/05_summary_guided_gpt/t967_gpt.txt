I feel like I’m just… existing on mute.

I have this one friend I hang out with sometimes, but I’m always the one who has to text first, always the one who suggests meeting up. If I don’t reach out, we just don’t talk. It makes me feel like they don’t really want to see me, like I’m just something they tolerate when I appear, not someone they’d actually miss. I’m trying to push myself to go out alone, to sit in cafés or walk around so I’m not always trapped inside, but it just makes me painfully aware of how silent my life is. No one asks how my day was. No one is waiting to hear about the little things.

The loneliness is so draining. I cope by imagining being with someone, making up conversations in my head, or “watching” movies with YouTubers and pretending they’re friends sitting next to me. I know it’s not real, but it fills the silence a bit. Still, when the video ends, it’s just me again.

Work makes it worse. Everyone else seems to have partners, families, people to go home to. They talk about their weekend plans, their kids, their relationships. Then the day ends and they leave in pairs or groups, and I’m the one walking out alone, waiting for the bus alone, going home to no one. It makes me wonder what’s wrong with me. Am I too fat, too ugly, too annoying, too… something?

There’s also this boy I have feelings for, but he always seems to find someone else to love. I’m just there on the sidelines, watching it happen again and again. It hurts more than I want to admit.

I try to stay positive, to keep my brain busy with information, shows, random interests. I fill my time hoping that one day I’ll have someone to talk to about all of it. But even in my own thoughts, I feel lonely. It’s like there’s an echo where another person should be.
