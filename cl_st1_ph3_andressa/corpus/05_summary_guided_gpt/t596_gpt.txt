I’m around 30 and I honestly feel like I’m missing some crucial “friendship software” everyone else seems to be born with.

Unless someone else initiates and clearly shows interest first, I basically never manage to make friends. When I do push myself to talk to new people, it always follows the same pattern: it starts okay, then gets awkward, then slowly dies. This isn’t just with one type of person or in one environment – it happens with everyone I meet.

I really do try. I force myself out of my comfort zone, start conversations, ask questions, try to be friendly. But at some point there’s this weird shift and I can almost feel the other person pulling away. I have no idea what cues I’m giving off that make things feel off or uncomfortable.

I’ve read so many books and articles about making friends, influencing people, matching energy, being a good listener, etc. and I still can’t pinpoint what I’m doing wrong. I “have looks,” so it’s not that I’m completely unpresentable or anything.

I’m seriously asking: what could be going wrong here? How do I figure this out and actually fix it?
