I’m starting to realize I might be the “listener friend” and I don’t know how to feel about it.

Most of my friends are online. I’m always the one who starts conversations, checks in, remembers what they’re going through, asks how things went, etc. If someone says they have a test, I’ll remember and follow up. If they mention something important, I’ll bring it up later to show I was paying attention. I try to share their interests and be supportive.

But it doesn’t really go both ways. They rarely ask about my life unless I bring it up first, and even then it feels like the topic dies quickly. They don’t follow up on things I mention, even when they’re big things for me. It makes me feel like I’m just there to listen, not really someone they’re curious about.

A recent thing that really stung: I changed high school programs, which is a pretty big deal for me. I told my friends about it, but no one asked how it went afterwards. No “how are you liking it?” or “was it scary?” or anything. It was just… silence.

Another friend recently asked if I wanted to be a doctor, even though I’ve said multiple times that I want to study nursing. It’s not the end of the world, but it still hurt that they didn’t remember something that feels important to me.

All of this makes me feel unnoticed and kind of stupid for caring so much. It’s making me pull back emotionally. I don’t feel like opening up when it seems like no one is really paying attention anyway.

I keep wondering: am I wrong for withdrawing a bit? Should I just accept that my friends aren’t as caring or attentive as I am, and lower my expectations? Or does this mean these friendships aren’t as real as I thought they were?
