I’m 20, a music major, and I feel like I’ve somehow faded out of my own life.

Freshman year I was always around people. There were constant hangouts, practice room chats, late-night food runs. I felt like I belonged somewhere. Now it’s junior spring and I feel like a ghost on campus. I walk around and it’s like everyone else is plugged into some social network I can’t access anymore.

I’m not part of any tight-knit group. I don’t have friends in my classes. When I try to join conversations, I feel like I’m intruding, like I’m the extra chair someone forgot to put away. In groups of three, I always end up being the one left out while the other two naturally pair off. Even when I’m with two people I consider friends, they mostly talk to each other, and I’m just… there.

I tried to throw myself into other things. I got a job, I’m taking harder courses, trying to keep busy. But none of that really replaces having people who actually want you around. I feel like a “backup friend” now—the person you invite if no one else is free, the last one to hear about plans, if I hear about them at all.

What hurts most is that it wasn’t always like this. These same friends used to show they cared. They surprised me for my birthday, they comforted me when I was going through rough patches. Now they somehow don’t have time for me, but I see them making time for others.

I feel like I’m silently suffering and no one notices. Each semester it feels like I’m drifting further away from everyone, and my loneliness is just getting worse.
