I had my first panic attack in public today, on a train of all places, and I’m still shaken up. One minute I was just sitting there, the next my heart was pounding out of my chest, I couldn’t catch my breath, and it felt like my throat was closing up and I was choking. It was terrifying, and I completely lost control.

A stranger actually helped me snap out of it, which I’m grateful for, but I also feel really embarrassed that it happened in front of people. I needed a long time afterward to calm down, and even some water, just to feel halfway normal again.

The worst part is that this all happened about an hour before a job interview. Normally interviews don’t really stress me out, but my voice was so dry and shaky, and I kept stuttering. I’m pretty sure some external stuff triggered the whole thing, but I still feel like my body betrayed me.
