Lately my life feels like it’s been boiled down to two things: my kid and my job. I go to work, I come home, I take care of my child, I crash, and then I do it all over again. I rarely leave the house for anything else, and most days I don’t really talk to anyone beyond what’s necessary.

It’s not a dramatic kind of loneliness, more like a quiet, everyday ache. I miss the little things: someone genuinely asking how my day was and actually wanting to hear the answer, a random “thinking of you” message, a conversation that isn’t about logistics or responsibilities. I’m not looking for romance or anything like that. I just miss feeling like a person in my own right, not only “parent” or “coworker.”

I’d love to have a real conversation with someone who understands this kind of feeling. If you’re in a similar place—stuck in routine, feeling invisible in the middle of all your roles—maybe we could talk. I’m only asking for honesty and kindness. I’ll offer the same in return.
