I’m 46 and I’ve never had what I’d call a real romantic relationship.

Back in my early 30s I did try. I went to clubs, danced, made out with a few women, sometimes even got numbers. But I’d either overthink everything and never follow up properly, or I’d come on too strong and they’d ghost me. It always felt like I was missing some basic piece everyone else had figured out.

Most of my life has just been work and family. On paper it probably looks “fine,” but it’s been very lonely underneath. I don’t really expect that to change now. I’m not writing this to ask for advice on how to fix it. At this point I honestly doubt I’ll ever experience being truly loved and wanted in a relationship.

What I’m really curious about is: what does it actually feel like? For those of you who have been in relationships where you felt wanted, loved, chosen… how would you describe that? Not the Hollywood version, but the real, everyday feeling of it. Waking up next to someone. Being missed. Being held. Being known.

I’ve been in dark places over this before. I’ve had times when I was suicidal, when the emptiness felt unbearable. I don’t feel that way right now. I think I can probably just endure life to the end. It’s not a dramatic statement, just… resignation, I guess.

I know I’m not the only one living like this. A lot of us are watching life from the sidelines. You don’t get the relationship “battle scars” that people talk about, but you also don’t get the memories, the connection. You’re just left with this quiet, persistent emptiness.

In the end, everyone is alone, I get that. But some people at least get to be less alone for a while. I just want to understand what that feels like.
