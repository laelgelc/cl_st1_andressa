I don’t really know why I’m writing this, but I guess I just need to put it somewhere.

I’m a Black gay man, HIV positive but undetectable. I’ve been in this rocky relationship for almost two years. I love my partner, genuinely. But it hurts that he would rather get high and masturbate alone than actually be intimate with me. When we do have sex, it feels so one-sided, like it’s all about him and I’m just… there. It’s like I’m a prop instead of a person.

That feeling doesn’t stop when I’m outside the relationship either. In the dating scene, I feel completely objectified. Like I’m a fetish or a checkbox, not someone worth getting to know. On top of that, I can’t shake this feeling of being “dirty” because of my status, even though I’m undetectable and I know the science. My self-confidence is basically non-existent. I look at myself and just see someone broken.

I’m still so angry about how I contracted HIV. I replay it in my head, like somehow if I think about it enough I can rewrite it. I thought I’d be better at handling this as an adult, but I feel like I’m failing at coping. I don’t know how to carry all of this and still function.

There’s this deep, growing void inside me. It feels bigger every day. The depression, the loneliness, the sense that I’m not really wanted, just used. I’m in a relationship and still feel completely alone.

I’m not sure what I’m hoping to get from posting this. Maybe I just want to know if anyone else has felt anything like this—this mix of shame, anger, emptiness, and loneliness. If anyone relates, I’d really like to hear from you.
