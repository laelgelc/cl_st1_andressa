I feel weird even writing this because on paper my life looks really “social.” I’m super outgoing, I’m in a bunch of clubs, I have a mentor/leadership role, and I’m almost always around people. I’m not a shut-in, I’m not friendless, I’m not “awkward.” And yet I feel this constant, heavy loneliness that I can’t shake.

In my 20s I had a solid group of close friends. Then COVID happened, people moved away, life paths diverged, and now the contact is pretty minimal. I didn’t realize how much those connections grounded me until they were basically gone.

I also just ended a three-year relationship this year. My ex definitely loved me, but by the end it felt like they didn’t actually like me very much. That’s been a pattern in my life: people are drawn to me at first, think I’m fun or interesting, and then over time I become “too much.” Too much to handle, too much to deal with long term.

I have depression and ADHD, I’m medicated, I work really hard to manage them. I’m generally functional and reliable. But inside I feel so untethered. I’m scared to let anyone get close now because I’m convinced that if they really get to know me, they’ll eventually realize I’m not very likable and quietly pull away.

I feel guilty even complaining because I’m not physically alone and I know others have it “worse.” But I’m struggling. Being surrounded by people and still feeling this alone is starting to really wear me down.
