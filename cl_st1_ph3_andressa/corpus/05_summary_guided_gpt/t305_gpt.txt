I live with C-PTSD and I already spend a lot of time feeling unwanted, like there’s something fundamentally wrong with me. So when I thought I’d finally found “the one,” it felt like everything was finally lining up. People around us encouraged it, said we were good for each other. It sounded so right: two lonely people finding comfort together.

But it didn’t turn out that way. I ended up feeling used, like I was more of a temporary emotional crutch than a partner. What hurts the most is that we both genuinely wanted to be close. It wasn’t a lack of interest or affection. It was that our values and priorities just didn’t line up enough to actually build something stable.

Somehow that mismatch is more painful than outright rejection. It’s like seeing the possibility of love right in front of you and still not being able to reach it.
