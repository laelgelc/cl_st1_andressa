I really enjoy being alone. When I’m by myself, it’s like I can finally hear my own thoughts clearly. I don’t have to perform, I don’t have to keep up with anyone, and I don’t feel that constant pressure of being “on.” There’s a kind of peace in that silence that I can’t seem to find around other people.

But at the same time, I feel really conflicted about being a loner. Humans are social animals, right? I keep thinking about the whole “lone wolf vs. the pack” thing. A wolf on its own is weaker, more vulnerable, easier to outnumber. I can’t help but project that onto myself: if I’m alone, does that automatically make me socially weaker? Am I just isolating myself while everyone else is out there forming packs, building connections, getting stronger together?

I also feel overwhelmed by all these social obligations that I never really asked for: expectations to go out, to network, to always be available and responsive. It feels like I’m constantly struggling against my own identity. I love my privacy and my solitude, but I also kind of hate that this makes me feel “less than” in a world built for extroverts and groups.

So yeah, being a loner is a really complex thing for me. If you consider yourself a loner, could you give me some arguments in favor of it? What do you enjoy most about being a loner? How do you see it as a strength rather than a weakness?
