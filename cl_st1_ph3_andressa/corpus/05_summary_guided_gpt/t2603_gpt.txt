I don’t know how else to describe it except that I feel almost “alien” compared to what people call normal. All the stuff that seems to be second nature to others—small talk, hanging out, constant messaging, “staying connected”—just feels like a chore to me. My “social battery” is basically a tiny flickering light. Ten minutes of conversation and I already feel drained and irritated, wondering what the point of any of it is.

What really annoys me is how people insist that humans *have* to be social, that isolation is automatically bad, like it’s some moral failing or mental illness. The same people saying “you can’t live like that, it’s unhealthy” are often drowning in their own unhealthy habits—bad relationships, drama, addictions, nonstop distractions. But somehow I’m the one “doing life wrong” because I’d rather be alone and quiet.

I’m not clueless about the downsides. I know that if something serious happens—an emergency, a health crisis, losing a job—I don’t really have anyone to fall back on. There’s no built-in safety net of friends or family. That reality doesn’t scare me as much as people think it should. I’ve been managing my own life for a long time. I keep things simple on purpose, so I can handle as much as possible by myself.

I’ve decided I’m done forcing myself to be someone I’m not. I’m done pretending to care about conversations that feel empty. I’m done trying to express emotions on demand just to make others comfortable. I’m done chasing relationships I don’t actually want, just because “that’s what people do.”

If I’m quiet, aloof, asocial, and alone, then that’s what I am. I’d rather fully lean into that than keep acting like there’s something broken in me that needs to be fixed. I’m not asking for advice or a cure. I just needed to say out loud that I’m choosing this, consciously, and I’m okay with the trade-offs.
