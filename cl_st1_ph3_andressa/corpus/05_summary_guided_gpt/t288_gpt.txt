I feel like I’m walking around with this huge ache that nobody else can see, and whenever I try to show it to people, it just… doesn’t land. Most of the time, when I open up, people immediately start talking about their own experiences or giving advice that doesn’t really touch what I’m feeling. The only person who doesn’t do that is my dad. He actually tries to be there with me in it. But everyone else seems to turn it into something about them, and I end up feeling even more alone.

I really want a relationship and real, meaningful comfort. But every time I talk about my struggles, I never feel relieved or fulfilled afterward. It makes me wonder if maybe I’m the problem, like I just can’t be comforted. Maybe I never learned how to comfort myself, or even how to really comfort others, so I don’t know how to receive it either.

There was one person in my life who comforted me in a way that felt so uniquely caring and almost childlike, in a pure, gentle way. It was like being emotionally held. I fell in love with them because of that. Now I’m scared of living the rest of my life without that kind of comfort again. I crave that feeling so intensely that I’m starting to wonder if there’s something wrong with me for wanting it so much.

Lately I’ve been feeling like giving up on sharing my struggles at all, because it feels pointless when I walk away still empty. But at the same time, I really would like to talk to someone. Not for pity, not to have my pain turned into a story about them, just… to chat, to be heard a little, to not feel so alone for a moment.
