I’m 22 and I’ve spent almost half my life convinced I’m not allowed to be loved.

When I was 13 I found out I have a rare physical condition. I won’t go into details, but it made me decide back then that I should never be in any kind of romantic relationship. I told myself it wouldn’t be fair to anyone, that it would be selfish to let someone get close to me when I’m “broken” in this way. That decision absolutely crushed me, but I stuck to it.

Now I’m older and the desire for emotional and physical intimacy is so strong it hurts. I want to be held, kissed, wanted. I want to share my life with someone. But every time those feelings come up, I force myself to shut them down. If I start to feel attracted to someone, I immediately pull away, remind myself that I don’t deserve that kind of happiness, that it’s not meant for people like me.

Recently I became friends with someone I’m really drawn to. I think they might like me back. We get along well and there’s this… tension, or something. But I keep distancing myself because I feel like I’d be tricking them, like it would be cruel to let anything happen. I tell myself it’s better to hurt now than to hurt them later. I keep thinking some people just aren’t meant to be happy, and I’m one of them.

I don’t feel like I can talk to anyone about this. I don’t know how to explain my condition without feeling disgusting or ashamed. So I just stay quiet, pretend I’m fine, then cry alone when it gets too much. It’s exhausting.

I don’t know what I’m asking for exactly. Maybe tips on how to cope with wanting something you’ve decided you can never have. How do you live with that? How do you stop wanting love when it feels like your whole body is screaming for it?
