I’m not very good with words, and honestly I had a much better version of this written before, but it got deleted. So this is the messy version.

All I’ve ever really wanted is to be loved. I thought I had that with my husband. I really, genuinely believed he was “my person,” the one place in the world where I was safe and chosen and understood.

Now I’m starting to think I was just deluding myself.

I care more about us being okay than about being right. I’d rather apologize, compromise, swallow my pride, whatever it takes, if it means we’re on the same side. But it feels like he doesn’t see things that way. It’s like winning the argument matters more to him than protecting what we have.

Lately I can’t shake the feeling that he doesn’t love me anymore. Not really. Not in the way I thought. And that realization is gutting. There’s this quiet, heavy loneliness that comes from lying next to someone who might not actually be “your person” after all.

It hurts to think I might have built my whole sense of safety on something that wasn’t real. And I keep coming back to this: unspoken expectations turn into resentment. I see it happening between us, and I don’t know how to fix it, or if he even wants to. I just feel so alone.
