I feel like I’m doing life wrong and I can’t tell if what I’m experiencing is normal or if I’ve seriously messed up somewhere.

I moved to Boston for an engineering job thinking it would make me more social. Instead I spent all day basically talking to a computer, barely interacting with anyone. I was far from my (very small) family and the loneliness hit so hard that after three months I just quit and moved back home.

After that I went to Europe by myself for a while. I usually travel solo – I’ve done Europe alone before, and Japan too – and I do enjoy it on some level, but it also reinforces this feeling that I’m always on my own. Now I’m about to start a new job and I’m already dreading it. I’m expecting the same isolation, plus it’s an hour commute each way, so there goes even more time where I’m just… by myself.

Most weekends I’m alone. I bike or hike a lot, which I genuinely like, but it’s all solo. I almost never eat out because I’m trying to diet, and I don’t drink, so going to bars feels pointless and awkward. The one social thing I really look forward to, racing sailboats, is on hold for the winter, so that’s gone for months.

Dating is basically nonexistent. I get maybe one date every six months from apps, and nothing has turned into anything. Even when I’m out in public places, I still feel completely alone, like I’m just existing next to other people instead of with them.

I keep thinking that most people spend their weekends doing things together, with friends or partners or family, and I’m just… here. Is this level of isolation normal? Is this just adulthood, or is there something really off about the way my life is set up?
