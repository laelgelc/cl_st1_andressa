I feel like my whole life right now is just working to stay alive. I’m juggling two jobs, constantly exhausted, and then I come home to… nothing. No one is there, no one texts to ask how my day was, no one cares if I made it back safely. It’s like I’m just existing to pay rent and bills, not actually living.

The only thing that really keeps me going is this fragile hope that maybe one day it won’t be like this. I picture a nicer apartment, the kind that actually feels like home. I imagine cooking real meals instead of just throwing something together because I’m too tired to care, and sharing that food with someone who actually wants to be there with me. I imagine a partner who loves me, whose presence gives me energy and a reason to wake up and try again.

I know I would sacrifice so much for the people I care about, but it feels like I can’t even get the bare minimum from anyone. I just want to be special to someone, to matter in a real way. Instead I keep coming back to this feeling that there’s something fundamentally wrong with me, something that makes real connection impossible. Like everyone else has this invisible piece that I’m missing, and I can’t figure out what it is or how to get it.

I don’t understand how people can live just for themselves and feel okay. When I’m alone and sober with my thoughts, it feels like I’m slowly losing my mind. The silence gets so loud. I start spiraling, questioning everything about who I am and why I can’t seem to have what other people take for granted.

I don’t even know what I’m expecting by writing this. I just needed to say it somewhere, because carrying it around by myself all the time is starting to feel unbearable.
