I’ve felt socially excluded and awkward for as long as I can remember. As a kid I never really understood social cues, and over time I just got used to being the one left out. When I tried to be nice or friendly, people would give me these weird looks, like I was doing something wrong just by being myself. It made me start believing there was something fundamentally wrong with me, that I was ugly inside and out.

That feeling never really left. It’s like this negative image of myself got burned into my brain and I can’t shake it. I’ve never had anyone I truly felt safe opening up to. I’m scared that if I share how I feel, people will judge me or use it against me somehow. So I keep everything bottled up.

Not having friends has made everything worse. My mental health has taken a hit, even though I’ve gotten used to being alone. I don’t even know if I remember what it feels like to genuinely connect with someone without fear.
