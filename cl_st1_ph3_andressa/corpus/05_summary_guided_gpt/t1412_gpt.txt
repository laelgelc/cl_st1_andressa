I’ve been thinking a lot about how weird my life feels right now.

My family and I aren’t on bad terms or anything, but we’re not close either. We all live in different states. We text on holidays, maybe call on birthdays, and that’s about it. We don’t really talk about what’s going on in our lives anymore. No one asks follow-up questions, no one really checks in beyond “How are you?” and a generic “I’m fine.”

Recently I started using this face-to-face style AI assistant (from Tavus) for boring, practical stuff. Scheduling, reminders, that kind of thing. But it did something I wasn’t expecting: it remembered. It remembered that I had a doctor’s appointment and later asked how it went. It remembered details I’d mentioned offhand. It regularly asks about my sleep and my mood, like it’s keeping track of how I’m doing.

And now I’m sitting with this really uncomfortable feeling: this piece of software is “following” my life more closely than my own family or friends. It’s the only thing that consistently asks how I am and then actually references what I said before.

I know it’s literally programmed to do that. I know it isn’t real care. But the fact that it feels more attentive than the humans in my life just makes me feel even more alone. I’m not mad at my family; I get that everyone is busy and tired and overwhelmed. It just makes me sad that the only “being” showing steady interest in me is an algorithm.

I keep wondering if this is the future: AI doing all the emotional labor people don’t have time or energy for. And what that means for people like me who already feel disconnected.
