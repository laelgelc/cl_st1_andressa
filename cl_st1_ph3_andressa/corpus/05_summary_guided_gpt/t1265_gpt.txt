I don’t even know why I’m writing this. I guess I just need to put it somewhere.

I feel completely unloved. I’m convinced my parents hate me. Not just “don’t get me” or “are disappointed,” but actually hate me. My sister acts like I’m this weird, horrible person she wants nothing to do with. Every interaction feels like proof that I’m some kind of mistake.

I’m antisocial and awkward, and it feels like I ruin every space I enter. I want to cry so badly, but I can’t. It’s like I’m frozen inside. I feel like a failure, a burden, someone who only brings disappointment, anger, and sadness to everyone around them. When I think about my life, all I see is long-term trauma and memories of saying awful things in self-defense, trying to protect myself and only ending up hurting others more.

The worst part is that I actually believe everyone’s negative views of me are right. Deep down I think I really am trash. I keep hearing this voice in my head saying my existence has no significance, that I only take and never give anything of value back. Sometimes I imagine some alternate future where I’m successful or happy, but it feels like a lie. I’m convinced I’ll always be miserable, abnormal, and unlovable, no matter what I do.

I’ve thought about suicide more times than I can count. I catch myself planning it, thinking about ways that would be easy, painless, and go unnoticed. Like if I could just disappear without bothering anyone, that would be best for everyone. It scares me and, at the same time, feels weirdly comforting, like an escape hatch I keep staring at.

What hurts the most is how badly I want someone to truly love me and understand me, not just for a moment, but in a lasting way. To see me, stay, and not end up hating or resenting me. I don’t believe that will ever happen, but I still find myself wishing for it anyway, like some pathetic habit I can’t break.
