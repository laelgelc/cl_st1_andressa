It’s 1 AM and I’m sitting alone in this forest, and it just feels like the most accurate reflection of my inner world right now: dark, quiet, empty, and sort of indifferent to whether I’m here or not.

I keep thinking about how other people seem to be out there actually living. Laughing with friends, building families, forming these deep connections that give their lives shape and meaning. Meanwhile I’m here, feeling like a ghost who somehow still has to show up to work, pay bills, and pretend I’m fine. I don’t know if anyone really sees me, or if they just see the surface-level version I drag around to keep things functioning.

Life used to feel brighter. Back in school and college there was this sense of possibility, of shared experiences, of being part of something. I remember feeling hopeful, like the future was this big open space I could grow into. Somewhere along the way, that feeling just…evaporated.

I left a big tech job to build my own thing, thinking it would give me freedom and purpose. Instead, I’ve become a slave to achievement, to metrics, to the next milestone. I’ve traded whatever joy and sense of self I had for this constant pressure to “succeed.” On paper, I’m stable. In reality, I feel hollow. Materially okay, emotionally bankrupt.

Sometimes I walk through cemeteries and it hits me how brief and pointless this all seems. Rows of names, dates, and that tiny dash in between. That’s it. I wonder what they worried about, what they sacrificed, who they loved, and if they died feeling as unseen and unheard as I do now.

I don’t really know why I’m writing this except that I’m alone out here and the weight of all of this feels too heavy to carry in silence. Maybe putting it into words will carve out a tiny bit of space inside this emptiness. Maybe it won’t. But at least, for a moment, it feels like I’m not completely invisible.
