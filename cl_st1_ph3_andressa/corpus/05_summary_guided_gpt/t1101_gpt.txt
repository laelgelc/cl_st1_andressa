I’m 47 and I feel like I’ve spent my whole life trying to turn situational “friends” into something real, and it just never sticks.

I’ll meet someone at work or through some activity, we get along, share some laughs, and I think, “Okay, maybe this could be an actual friend.” I get their number or social media, we exchange a few messages, maybe hang out once… and then it just dies. They stop replying, or they give one-word responses, or you can just feel they’re not interested in continuing.

What really stings is watching those same people light up and talk eagerly with other, “cooler” people, while I’m standing there like I’m invisible. It makes me feel pathetic and resentful, like I’m trying way harder than anyone else just to be… tolerated.

This hasn’t just been a school thing or a phase. It’s been like this my whole life. Right now, I looked at my phone and realized I only have two contacts: my son and my ex-wife. That’s it. And it just makes me feel like nobody really cares if I exist or not.
