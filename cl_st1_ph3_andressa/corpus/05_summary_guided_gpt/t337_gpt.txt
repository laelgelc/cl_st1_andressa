I’m 31, live alone with my two cats, and I feel like I’m drifting further and further away from everyone.

I come from a huge family, but I’m estranged from all of them. Growing up was just constant emotional abuse, hatred, and disrespect, especially from my mother. My dad just… stood there. He never stepped in, never defended me, just let it all happen. By 23 I’d had enough and left. Nobody called. Nobody looked for me. It was like I just quietly disappeared and they were fine with that.

Now I bartend. Before that I was a tattoo artist. I meet tons of people, talk to them, listen to their stories, make them laugh. But when I go home, it’s just me and the cats and this heavy, empty feeling. I have some friends, but they’re married now, having kids, buying houses. Their lives are moving forward in this “normal” way and I feel like I’m standing still, watching them go. We still talk, but the distance keeps growing and I can feel myself sliding out of their inner circles.

What I want is so simple it almost feels pathetic to type out: I want someone I can call. Someone I can tell about my day, send stupid memes to, cook with, sit in silence with. Someone who actually cares if I got home safe, or if I’m having a bad day. I want to feel like I matter to at least one person in that way.

I’ve tried dating, mostly Tinder, and it just feels like the same dull job interview over and over. “What do you do? What are your hobbies?” People say they want connection but then they’re so passive, like they’re waiting for me to carry everything. I put in effort and it just fizzles.

I’m in therapy. My therapist says I’m in this “borderline state” where I won’t tolerate being treated badly anymore, but I still don’t really know how to accept care when it’s offered. I crave it and push it away at the same time. I don’t know what I’m doing wrong. I guess I’m writing this hoping for some kind of miracle, or at least to feel a little less alone.
