I’ve always been a loner and honestly… I like it. I enjoy my own company and I’m happiest when it’s just me and my boyfriend. Every time I try to “do the normal thing” and hang out with friends, it feels forced and awkward. I’m anxious the whole time, then afterward I replay everything I said and did, panic about how I came across, and end up feeling worse.

I keep thinking I “should” have friends, but I don’t feel like I actually need them. I’m fine with acquaintances, people I can be friendly with but don’t have to invest in deeply. At the same time, I worry about stuff like: what happens if I get married and I have no bridesmaids? Or big life events where you’re “supposed” to have a group of close friends.

I had a baby shower recently and felt really guilty and embarrassed that I had no close friends to invite, even though my boyfriend’s family kept encouraging me to invite people. It made me feel like there was something wrong with me.

Sometimes I wonder if people with lots of friends are just faking it and burning energy to maintain those relationships. Being stuck in quarantine has given me way too much time to overanalyze this, so I just needed to vent.
