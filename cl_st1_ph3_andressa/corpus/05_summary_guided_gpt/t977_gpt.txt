I’m 26 and honestly feel like I’ve already lived through the “worst case scenario” version of my life.

My whole family is gone. Most of the people I used to call friends are either dead or just… gone in their own ways. I don’t have a job, I don’t have hobbies, I don’t really have anything that gives my days structure or meaning. I even got kicked out of the military because I didn’t have a next of kin to list. That one still stings in a very specific way.

I’ve never dated, never been in a relationship. I’m painfully socially awkward, the kind that comes from years of being bullied as a kid and never really learning how to be around people without feeling like I’m doing something wrong just by existing. Emotionally, spiritually, physically – I just feel beaten down. I go to sleep a lot of nights hoping I don’t wake up, and when I do, it’s like, “Okay. Guess we’re doing this again.”

I’ve pretty much accepted that I’ll probably die alone. That sounds dramatic, but it feels more like a quiet fact at this point than some big tragedy.

But even with all that, I can’t shake this stubborn belief that life can still have beautiful moments. That maybe, somehow, things can get better, even if I can’t see how from where I’m standing.

I’m posting this anonymously just to get it off my chest. If you’re reading this and you’re struggling too, I genuinely hope your days end up being kinder to you than mine have been to me so far. Don’t give up.
