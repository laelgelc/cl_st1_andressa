I’m 25, male, and I have borderline personality disorder. Today I got a double promotion at work. Like, a really big one. My coworkers were all congratulating me, saying they’re proud of me, telling me I earned it. On paper it’s the kind of thing you’re supposed to feel over the moon about.

But I walked into my apartment afterward and it was just…silent. No one to text right away, no one to call, no one waiting for me, no “we should celebrate!” Nothing. Just me, my empty place, and this weird hollow feeling.

Everyone at work was proud of what I did, but it doesn’t feel like anyone is proud of *me*. I keep thinking how different it would feel if there was even one person who was genuinely excited about *me* getting this, not just about the achievement itself. Someone who would light up and say, “I knew you could do it,” and actually mean it, because they care about my life and not just my output.

Instead, it feels like I’m watching my own life from the outside. Objectively, I know this is a big deal. Subjectively, it just…falls flat. I can almost see the version of me who would go out to dinner with a partner or call a close friend and gush about it. But that’s not my reality. I came home, sat down, and the first thing that hit me was how alone I am.

I know a lot of this is my BPD. I crave that intense validation and affection. I want someone who is *into* me, who is excited about my wins, who makes me feel like I’m not just existing in a vacuum. When that’s missing, even good things feel kind of pointless, like they’re happening in a void.

I should be happy. I should be proud. Instead I’m just…numb and lonely, staring at a promotion I can’t emotionally access.

So yeah. Double promotion. Big step in life.

Go me, I guess.
