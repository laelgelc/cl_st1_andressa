I keep wondering if there’s something fundamentally wrong with me for wanting what I want.

I don’t think I’m ugly. People have told me I’m pretty, nice, fun to be around. I’ve had people be interested in me. But none of it touches the part of me that feels so painfully empty and unseen.

What I want feels… extreme. I want someone to want me in this overwhelming, all‑consuming way. To need me so much that I feel indispensable to them. Not just “you’re cute” or “you’re nice to talk to,” but this sense that if I disappeared, there would be a real hole in their life that no one else could fill.

It sounds clingy and “sick” even to me, and it scares me that this is what I crave. It makes me think I’m broken somehow. But at the same time, it feels like the most honest way to describe how lonely I am. Like nothing less intense could ever make this emptiness go away.
