I’ve moved around a lot, lived in different countries, and the time I spent in London really sticks with me. I was working and living alone there and the isolation was intense at first. Over time, though, I got used to it. I was busy, focused, had my own routines, and actually started to enjoy being by myself.

When I moved back to my home country, the first year hit me hard. Most of my old friends had moved away, so I basically had to start from scratch. Eventually I found a new friend group and I was genuinely happy for a while. But I slowly realized my self‑worth had become tied to their validation, their texts, their invitations. If they were around, I felt important. If they weren’t, I felt like nothing.

Now I’m trying to undo that. I’m making an effort to focus on myself, spend more time alone, not chase people, and it’s mostly working. I can feel myself getting more grounded. But there are still days when no one reaches out and I suddenly feel lonely and unimportant, and it confuses me because I don’t remember thinking like this before.

I’m trying to figure out how to be genuinely okay with being alone and not feel lonely, especially in a society that constantly talks about community and connection. How do you balance wanting people around with not basing your worth on whether anyone messages you?
