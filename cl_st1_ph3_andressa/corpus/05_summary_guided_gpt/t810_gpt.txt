I turned 20 today and it honestly just feels like any other lonely day, maybe a bit worse. So far only one person has said happy birthday to me. My own family hasn’t said anything. I’ve also had the flu for the last four days, so I’m stuck in bed feeling physically awful on top of everything else.

I’m kind of used to being alone, so I didn’t expect much, but it still stings more than I want to admit. I grew up in a family where we don’t talk about feelings at all. No “I love you,” no real conversations about what’s going on inside. Because of that, opening up to people and being social feels almost impossible sometimes. It’s like I never learned how.

About three years ago I met a girl on Discord. We were friends for a year, then we became long-distance partners. That year we were together was honestly the best I’ve ever felt. We had this really beautiful connection. I could tell her about my day, my dreams, my fears. We made plans together and had shared goals. For once, I felt like I wasn’t just drifting through life. I had direction, and a future that actually seemed worth moving toward.

She ended things around our one-year anniversary because of external reasons. I still loved her, but it didn’t matter. It’s been about ten months since then. I don’t miss her specifically as intensely anymore, but I really miss what we had. That feeling of being understood, of having someone I could be completely honest with. Someone who cared about my day and my future, and whose future I cared about too.

Now I just feel lost again. I miss having that deep emotional connection more than anything. I don’t even know how to find something like that again, especially when I struggle so much with opening up in the first place. I guess I just needed to put this somewhere, because I don’t really have anyone in my life I can say it to.
