I feel like I’m in this constant emotional crisis and I honestly have no idea what to do anymore. I’m so lonely and I don’t have anyone I can really talk to for reassurance or validation. Every time I try to open up to acquaintances or people I’m not super close with, they just… change the subject or brush past it. They don’t really acknowledge what I’m saying, and it makes me feel like my feelings don’t matter, like I’m being dramatic or annoying just for needing someone.

The thing that hurts is that I know if the roles were reversed, I would sit and listen. I would gladly be there for someone else in my position, try to help them sort through their thoughts, comfort them. But there’s no one in my life who seems willing to do that for me. It makes me feel disposable and so, so alone.

Sometimes I end up crying for hours, just trying to calm myself down. I keep wishing there was just one empathetic person who could spend even a little time with me, helping me untangle the mess in my head. I’ve tried going for walks around the city, jogging, just moving my body to cope, but it doesn’t really help. I don’t even like being alone that much; I find myself going to libraries or cafes just so I can be around people and not feel like I’m completely isolated.

What I really want is some kind of social interaction, someone I can “catch up” with, but I’m scared of burdening people or disturbing them, especially when I’m feeling like this. I don’t want to be “too much” for anyone.

Has anyone else felt like this? How did you get through it, or at least make it more bearable?
