Lately I’ve been questioning what human connection actually is. How much of “love” or attachment is just brain chemistry firing in certain patterns? If it’s all hormones and neural circuits, does free will exist in relationships at all, or are we just acting out a script biology wrote for us?

Another thing I keep wondering: if you learn to cope with loneliness on your own, does any romantic or non‑utilitarian relationship still have a real point? Or is it all just a sophisticated coping mechanism, a cycle of mutual validation that we call “meaning” because our urges push us there?

I know a lot of people bring in ideas like the soul, spirituality, or some higher purpose here. I’m not sure I buy any of that, but I also feel unsettled by the idea that it’s all just chemicals. How do you find purpose or value in social connections?
