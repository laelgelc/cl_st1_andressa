My birthday was recently and, like the past few years, I found myself dreading it instead of looking forward to it. It’s supposed to be the one day where you get a bit of extra attention from friends and family, but for me it just ends up shining this harsh spotlight on how lonely I really am.

Birthdays used to be about celebrating with people I cared about, but somewhere along the way they turned into a reminder of who’s drifted away, who doesn’t care, and how small my circle has become. I’ve had birthdays where no one showed up at all, and others where people did come but barely talked to me, like I was just an extra at my own party.

This year, only four friends remembered, and about a quarter of my family didn’t say anything. Seeing those numbers in my head, it just… stings. It’s like a quiet confirmation of something I try not to think about most of the year.

I keep telling myself I don’t care anymore. I think of that Phil Dunphy line about having zero expectations so people can’t disappoint you. It sounds kind of pathetic, but there’s a strange comfort in it. If I expect nothing, maybe days like this won’t hurt so much.
