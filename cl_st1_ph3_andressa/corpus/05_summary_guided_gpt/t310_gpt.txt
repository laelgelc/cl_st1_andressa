I’m 27 and living in Las Vegas, and the last few months have felt like my entire life got picked up and dropped somewhere else without warning.

My cousin passed away unexpectedly, and he wasn’t just family—he was my roommate and my main support system here. One day we were sharing a home and our routines, and then suddenly I was trying to process his death while also scrambling to find a new place for me and my dog. It all happened so fast that I still feel like my body moved but my mind hasn’t caught up.

I ended up in a new apartment that I actually really like, but sometimes it feels like I’m just… visiting my own life. I’ll be sitting on the couch with my dog and it hits me how different everything is now, and I feel this weird mix of gratitude and total displacement. Like I’m here, I’m functioning, but I don’t fully recognize my life anymore.

My family is back in Texas and they’ve been incredibly supportive emotionally, constantly checking in. I’m so thankful for them, but I also feel guilty that they’re worrying about me from so far away. I know they’d rather I be closer, and sometimes I wonder if I’m being selfish by trying to stick it out here.

In Vegas, my circle is really small—basically my dog, and my boyfriend when we’re together. I’m an aspiring nail tech and trying to build a future, but grief and all this instability make my motivation come and go. Some days I can push forward, other days I just feel stuck and exhausted.

If anyone has gone through a sudden loss, having to move or rebuild quickly, especially far from home—how did you cope? What actually helped you keep going when everything felt unfamiliar and unstable?
