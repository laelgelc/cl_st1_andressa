I just got out of an online hangout where we’re trying to figure out how to turn all this talk about loneliness into something more action-oriented and decentralized, and I wanted to share where things are at.

We spent a lot of time on what kind of “background organization” we’d even need. Not some big rigid structure, but enough of a backbone so people can plug in and do things without waiting for permission. Part of that is making sure we’re not just talking to ourselves, so we want input from all over: academics, practitioners, authors, even insurers, but also (and maybe most importantly) people who are directly dealing with loneliness in their own lives.

There was a surprisingly long debate about language. Do we lean into softer, “positive” words like “purpose,” or say “loneliness” out loud? We landed on naming loneliness explicitly, but always pairing it with something hopeful, especially “purpose,” so it doesn’t feel like a dead end.

On the practical side, we’re going to start using a shared Google Drive so ideas and documents don’t just vanish into chat logs. Someone’s putting together a very basic website too, even if the content will be “in progress” for a while. The plan is to just keep talking to people, collecting feedback, and folding it back into whatever we build.

The next meetup is on Saturday, May 12th at the usual time. We’re expecting a smaller turnout, but honestly, each time so far a few new people have shown up, and that’s been encouraging.
