I turned 25 today and I’m spending it the way I always seem to: feeling alone and kind of forgotten, even though I’m technically “around people.” I got a couple of texts, the usual “HBD” and maybe an emoji or two, and that’s pretty much it. I keep trying to tell myself it’s because of the holidays, that everyone’s busy or distracted, but it doesn’t make this pit in my stomach go away.

Since I was a kid, I’ve always secretly wanted people to be excited to celebrate me. Not in some huge, dramatic way, just… something. For other people, there are plans made weeks in advance, group chats, decorations, dinners, surprises. I see them being thought of and prioritized. And then my birthday comes around and it’s like I just quietly pass through it, while everyone else keeps going with their lives.

Every year I find myself wondering: is it just bad timing, or am I just not worth the hassle? I keep thinking that if people really cared, they’d show it without needing to be pushed. They’d remember on their own, they’d make some kind of effort. It doesn’t have to be big – just someone asking, “Hey, what are your plans? Want to do something?” or actually seeming excited for me.

But the idea of asking to be celebrated feels so wrong and fake. I don’t want to tell people, “Hey, can you make a fuss over me?” because then it feels forced, like they’re only doing it because I asked, not because they wanted to. I just want someone, anyone, to choose to make me feel special for one day. To remember without reminders. To care enough to plan something, or even just check in and mean it.

Instead, it ends up the same every year. A few messages, some polite words, and then… nothing. It’s like people care, but not enough to really show it. And I’m left sitting here on my own birthday, wondering what it would feel like to actually be celebrated, instead of just… existing.
