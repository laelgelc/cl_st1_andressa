Every winter hits me so much harder than summer and I don’t really understand why. From late December through January I feel insanely lonely, even though nothing in my life actually changes and I’m not physically alone – I live with other people.

I’m an introvert with pretty low social energy, but at the same time I crave attention and secretly want to be the center of attention. It’s like my personality is at war with itself. In summer I can kind of manage it, but in winter it feels like everything collapses.

I struggle to even get out of bed. The thought of going to the gym feels impossible, and I can’t get myself to start studying for the semester. Even Instagram, which usually gives me a bit of distraction and validation, just feels draining now.

This has been happening every winter for the last 4–5 years. It’s like my heart gets really heavy for no clear reason, and I just feel so much more alone than usual.

Does anyone else experience this kind of seasonal loneliness?
