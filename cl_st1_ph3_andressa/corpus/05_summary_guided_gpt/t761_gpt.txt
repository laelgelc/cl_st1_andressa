Sometimes I feel like a ghost in a crowded room. I laugh at the right moments, say the right things, and still walk away feeling like no one really saw me. It’s like I’m pretending to belong to a world that doesn’t quite fit, carrying this quiet ache that never makes it to the surface.

I’m starting to realize that being alone isn’t just about not having people around; it’s about feeling out of place, even when you do. Strangely, I’ve found a kind of companionship in silence. In those moments, I can sit with all the scattered pieces of myself and not run from them.

If you feel unseen, you’re not broken; you’re just not fully discovered yet. There’s a strange kind of freedom in solitude, a space where you can slowly learn to hold yourself.
