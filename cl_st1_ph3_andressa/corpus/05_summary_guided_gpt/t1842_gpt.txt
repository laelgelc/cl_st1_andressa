I’m 22 and recently moved to a new city for work, and it’s like this weird, heavy loneliness snuck up on me. I’ve always had “a lot of friends” back home, so I thought I was fine socially. But now that I’m away from them, I’m realizing those friendships were never really that deep.

We’d hang out, joke around, go to events, text in group chats, but I never actually opened up to them about anything real. And now that I’m here, I don’t feel like I can suddenly start doing that with them. It feels… off, like they’re not the kind of people I can say, “Hey, I’m really lonely and not okay” to. Or maybe it’s just me not knowing how to do that.

I’ve started to notice that I don’t really know how to open up to anyone. I’ve never had that one person who felt like a true safe space, where I could just be completely honest and vulnerable without overthinking every word. It hits me sometimes that I’ve gone my whole life without that kind of connection, and it makes this move feel even more isolating.

Seeing people on social media with their “besties,” their ride-or-die friends, all the inside jokes and deep talks and years of shared history… it stings more than I want to admit. I feel this quiet, kind of shameful jealousy and this emptiness, like I missed out on something everyone else got to have.

I don’t really have a point to this, I just needed to put it into words somewhere. Maybe just admitting it here is a tiny step toward not feeling so alone with it.
