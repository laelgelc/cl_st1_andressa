I’ve always felt like an outsider in my own “family.” My dad has a girlfriend whose relatives are this big, tight-knit group, and for a while I thought I was part of that. There’s this non-biological cousin I used to be closer to, but his dad died around 2016. Ever since then, it feels like something broke and never got fixed.

After that, I barely spent time with him or the rest of the cousins. They all started doing more things together without me, and now it feels like there’s this invisible barrier between us. They go on fun trips, like to Six Flags, and I only find out after the fact, or from social media. I’m usually only around them in the summer or if I sleep over at my uncle’s house. It feels like I’m just this extra person they tolerate, not someone they actually want around.

Most of the uncles and aunts barely talk to me. They don’t really invite me anywhere or ask how I’m doing. There’s one uncle who actually makes an effort, and I’m grateful for him, but everyone else just kind of… ignores me. It hurts more than I want to admit.

I ended up crying to my biological uncle on my mom’s side about it. He told me not to worry, that it’s their loss, not mine. I know he’s trying to make me feel better, but I can’t shake this feeling of being unwanted and left out.

I keep wondering: should I even care this much? Is it worth caring about people who clearly don’t care about including me? Or am I just setting myself up to be hurt over and over again?
