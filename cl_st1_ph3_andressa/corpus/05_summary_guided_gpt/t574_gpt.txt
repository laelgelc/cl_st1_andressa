I’m autistic and disabled, and I feel like I’m constantly being told to “fix” my loneliness in ways that just aren’t realistic for me. Go to the gym, join clubs, work on my social skills, put myself out there… it all feels impossible or exhausting.  

The awareness that I’m alone is always there, like background noise I can’t turn off. Any romantic scene in a show or movie can completely wreck my mood for the rest of the day.  

I used to be high all the time, and at least that numbed things. I’ve been sober for a year now, and the loneliness is just as bad, but I really don’t want to go back because it messes with the few things I still enjoy.  

I’m not asking how to be less lonely. I want to know: how do you accept being lonely and stop thinking about it so much?
