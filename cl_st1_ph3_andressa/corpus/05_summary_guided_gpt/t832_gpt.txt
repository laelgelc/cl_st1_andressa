I keep thinking about how fragile my social life actually is. I have two close childhood friends, and I’m really grateful for them, but it also scares me how much everything depends on them. If they weren’t in my life, I’d basically have no one. That thought just sits in the back of my mind all the time.

No one has ever really shown spontaneous interest in getting to know me. Every friendship I’ve ever had started because I went out of my way to approach someone, to start the conversation, to keep it going. Now I feel too scared and tired to do that again. The idea of walking up to new people and trying to make friends feels overwhelming, like I’m setting myself up to be ignored or rejected.

In groups I feel like I’m just… there. A background character. I could disappear and the dynamic wouldn’t change. I listen, laugh at the right moments, but I don’t feel like anyone would actually notice if I stopped showing up.

Because of that, the idea that someone could like me—romantically or even just be genuinely drawn to me as a person—feels unreal. When I imagine someone confessing feelings for me, it doesn’t feel flattering, it feels almost disgusting, like it’s obviously some kind of mistake or joke.

I still don’t want to be alone forever. I want things to change. But it feels like the only way that could ever happen is if I push myself to take all the initiative again, and I don’t know if I have it in me anymore.
