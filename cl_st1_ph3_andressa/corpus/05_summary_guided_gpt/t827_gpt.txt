I’m so tired of being “resilient.” It feels like that’s all anyone ever expects from me: keep going, keep bouncing back, keep trying. I’m exhausted. I deleted Instagram because it started to feel like I was performing my life for an audience instead of actually being seen. People watching, liking, reacting… but not really being there. It made the loneliness feel louder.

I know I “should” get a therapist. I know I “have friends.” But it feels like my problems are too heavy to hand to anyone. I don’t want to be the burden, the one who always needs something. Every time I try to heal or build something stable inside myself, it just collapses again. It makes me feel like I’m broken in some fundamental way.

Things with my mom are a mess, and it adds to this feeling that I’ve been messed up from the start. Even the fact that two therapists literally ghosted me after intake sits in my head like proof that I’m too much, too complicated, not worth the effort. Who gets ghosted by therapists? It makes me feel like there’s something wrong with me at my core.

Most of my friends are in relationships. They have someone to go home to, someone who chooses them first. I feel like I’m never anyone’s first choice. I’m the backup plan, the “when we have time” friend. It’s like I’m always on the outside of everyone else’s real life.

I know my life hasn’t been the absolute worst compared to what some people go through, but it’s been relentlessly difficult in a way that feels like no one really understands. I feel like my time is running out, like I’m only holding on for other people and not for myself. A part of me honestly believes that someday I will end my own life. And I don’t know what to do with that.
