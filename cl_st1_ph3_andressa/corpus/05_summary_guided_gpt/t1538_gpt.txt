My boyfriend broke up with me two weeks before my birthday. What hurts even more is that right before that he’d been saying everything was fine, nothing was wrong. It feels like the rug got pulled out from under me again.

Every time a relationship ends, it feels like a personal failure instead of some “lesson” I’m supposed to grow from. I keep thinking I’m doing everything right, trying to communicate, be supportive, be understanding… but none of my relationships have even made it to a year. It’s hard not to see that as proof that something is wrong with me.

On top of that, I’ve always imagined myself married with kids by 25–26. I’m 21 now, and I keep doing the math in my head: I’d have to meet someone soon, build a solid relationship, get engaged, actually get married… it feels like there’s barely any time left. That pressure is making every breakup feel even worse, like I’m falling behind on some invisible timeline.

I don’t know what I’m doing wrong. How do people actually build relationships that last? What should I be focusing on so I can be successful in a relationship?
