I’m in college right now and I feel like I’ve never really had a close friend in my entire life. I’ve only ever had what I’d call “companions” – people I talk to in class or during activities, but who don’t think of me as someone to actually include or rely on.

In elementary school I was pretty much the outcast. I’d watch other kids form their groups and I was just… there. In middle school it got more obvious: people would talk about parties or hangouts that I was never invited to. By high school, I had people I could talk to in clubs and sports, but it never went beyond that. One example that still stings is when a group I thought I was close to planned a prom outing. I couldn’t go on the specific day they chose, and instead of trying to find another time or including me in any other plans, they just went without me and never brought it up again.

I thought college would be different. I joined multiple student orgs, I make an effort to go out, talk to people, not just sit in my dorm. But it feels like everyone already has their circles, and I’m this extra person on the side. My roommates all have closer connections with others than with me, and I feel like I’m easy to forget or overlook.

On top of that, I’ve never had anyone show romantic interest in me. I’ve never dated, never even had any romantic physical contact. I suspect I might be aromantic, but it still hurts to feel like I might never experience that cheesy romcom-style relationship. It’s like I’m grieving something I’m not even sure I want.

I’m also an only child, and sometimes I get hit with this fear that when my parents die, I’m going to be completely alone. No siblings, no partner, no lifelong friends.

I don’t really know how to handle this or what I should be doing differently. How do you cope with this kind of loneliness and the fear of being alone forever?
