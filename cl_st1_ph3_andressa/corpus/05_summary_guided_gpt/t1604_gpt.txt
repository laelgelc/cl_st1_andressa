I don’t even recognize myself lately. I’ve always been the one people describe as positive, sociable, the one who finds a way to make things work. But right now I just feel overwhelmed and helpless.

I’m physically dependent and I live with my husband and my mother. None of us work; we survive on my pension alone. It’s already tight and stressful, but we’ve managed so far. Now my husband’s brother is probably going to move in with us. He had a stroke, is nearly blind, physically dependent, and on top of all that he has a really difficult personality.

He’s constantly having relationship drama with his partner, and every time something happens my husband gets dragged into the middle of it. The idea of that chaos now coming fully into our home, on top of the financial and physical strain, is making my chest feel tight. It would be four people living on one income, with two of us being physically dependent.

Part of me understands: he’s disabled, he doesn’t have anyone else but my husband, and it feels cruel to say “no” to someone in that position. But another part of me feels like he should find his own way somehow, or that there should be other options. I feel awful even thinking that, like I’m a bad person for not wanting to take this on.

I don’t feel like I can talk honestly with my husband. He keeps saying we don’t have a choice, that we have to help his brother. Whenever I try to express how scared and resentful I am, I either shut down or I worry I’ll explode and say something I can’t take back.

I can feel my mood getting darker and I’m scared I’ll end up saying something hurtful to the people I love just because I’m so tired and trapped. I don’t know how to balance compassion for his brother with protecting what’s left of my own mental health. I just feel so alone with all of this.
