Sometimes I feel like I missed out on that “stereotypical teenage” friendship everyone seems to remember so fondly. The kind where you stay up all night gaming, watching dumb movies, sneaking out, drinking or smoking, then suddenly you’re talking about books, art, philosophy, and the meaning of life at 4 a.m. I want someone to go to the cinema with, make art with, someone who actually gets excited about ideas and weird conversations.

Mostly I just feel drained and disappointed when I try to commit to people. I barely have the energy to socialize, and when I do push myself, it’s like no one actually wants to hang out beyond surface-level stuff. I’m extremely introverted, but every now and then I get this intense urge to just have fun with someone, to actually share experiences instead of watching life from a distance.

The worst part is I know I probably sabotage any chance I have. Even if I did “belong” somewhere, I’d probably find a way to ruin it or convince myself I don’t. I’ve tried to repress the need for connection, pretend I’m fine alone, but it still creeps back in.

Anyway, Merry Christmas or whatever. Hope Santa brings me a functioning sense of connection and maybe something stronger than hot chocolate.
