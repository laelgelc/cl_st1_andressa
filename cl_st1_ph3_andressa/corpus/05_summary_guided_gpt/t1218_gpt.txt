I’m 26 and I feel like I’ve spent most of my life wondering what’s fundamentally wrong with me socially.

It started in high school. I remember people actively avoiding sitting next to me. Even if there were only a couple of seats left, they’d pick somewhere else or suddenly “need” to do something. That feeling has followed me into adulthood. In group settings, everyone seems to naturally pair up or form little clusters, and I somehow end up alone. It’s not like I’m hiding in a corner; I try to be friendly, presentable, and engaging. I smile, ask questions, show interest. But people rarely choose me.

Recently I’ve also realized that most of the men who showed interest in me didn’t actually want to be my friend. They wanted something else. It makes me feel even more hollow, because what I really want is close friendships, especially with other women. I crave that comfort and connection, but it never seems to click.

What confuses me is the feedback I get. Some say I don’t try hard enough. Others say I try too hard. A few have even said my looks might intimidate people. I don’t know what to believe. I don’t know what to change, or how to fix whatever this is. I’m just tired of feeling like the person no one chooses.
