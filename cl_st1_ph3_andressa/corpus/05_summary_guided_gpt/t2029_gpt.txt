I’m 20, a sophomore, and I live in this tiny, chaotic apartment with six of my closest friends. We’re basically on top of each other all the time – someone’s always in the kitchen, in the hallway, on the couch, knocking on my door. It’s loud and cramped and messy, but it’s also the only thing that’s ever really kept my depression from swallowing me whole.

When I start to sink, there’s always someone to drag me out of my room, shove food into my hands, or just sit next to me and watch something dumb. It’s like I don’t get the chance to stay depressed for too long because there’s always some kind of energy around me. I know how rare this is – to have six people I genuinely love and trust, all living together, all wanting to be around each other almost constantly. It feels like I somehow stumbled into this tiny miracle.

But I’ve been thinking about studying abroad, and that’s what set off this whole spiral in my head. Looking at dates and programs suddenly made me realize: this isn’t permanent. In two years, we’re all going to graduate. People will move back home, or to other cities, or start jobs somewhere else. This apartment won’t exist anymore. The idea of walking into a quiet, empty place after being used to this constant presence honestly terrifies me.

I keep imagining coming home to no one, no voices in the other room, no random knock on my door, and it feels like this huge, dark emptiness waiting for me. I’m scared that without this specific group, this exact setup, I’ll just spiral. That I’ll go back to feeling completely alone and stuck in my own head.

So I’m in this weird place where I feel incredibly lucky and grateful, but also already grieving something that hasn’t ended yet. I’m trying to enjoy what I have now, but part of me is just scared for what happens after, and I don’t really know how to prepare myself for that.
