People always tell me I’m kind, fun, a good friend. They say they love having me around, that I’m “such a light” or “so strong.” But all of that only seems to apply when I’m healthy enough to be the version of me they enjoy.

As soon as my illness flares up, it’s like I stop existing. When I’m in the hospital, or too weak to get out of bed, or I need emergency help, suddenly everyone is “busy,” “overwhelmed,” or just silently disappears. Messages slow down, then stop. The same people who call me amazing are nowhere to be found when I actually need someone.

It makes all those loving words feel completely hollow. If you vanish when things get hard, how real was any of it?

I feel like I’m on a stage, forced to smile and perform while I’m hurting, because if I show the reality, the audience leaves. And when the curtain falls, I’m still there, alone in the spotlight, with no one left watching.
