I’m 31, living in Bangalore, decent-paying software job, from a very normal middle-class family. On paper, things look fine. I’m not a complete loner either – I do the “right” things. I have long calls with friends, I go to house parties when invited, I attend my piano classes, I go shopping with acquaintances. I’m not hiding in my room.

But underneath all of that, I feel this huge gap where something like a stable, close, almost family-like connection should be. I keep thinking: these people are nice, but how long will they really be here? Most of my friends are already married or on that path. I can’t shake the feeling that once they get fully busy with spouses and kids, I’ll just be… the extra person who doesn’t quite fit anywhere.

The weird part is I’m not even sure I want marriage myself. It’s not that I’m strongly against it, I just don’t feel that pull. And yet I’m terrified of what my life will look like if I don’t have that built-in “unit” of people. It makes me feel like I’m too soft for this – like I have this “soft soul” that just can’t handle the uncertainty and this recurring phase of loneliness.

I can feel myself overthinking everything, but knowing that doesn’t stop it. The anticipatory anxiety about an empty future is eating away at me. It’s like it’s consuming my passions, my ambitions, my personality. I used to have goals and interests I cared about, and now I just feel weak, frustrated, useless for not working toward anything.

I don’t know how to stop this spiral. I’m doing the social things I’m “supposed” to do, but nothing feels solid or safe. I’m scared that I’ll wake up one day and realize I’ve spent my whole life worrying instead of actually living.
