I’m 30 and on paper my life looks pretty decent. I work hard, my bills are paid, I help take care of my nephews, my cat is healthy, I have a good home and I’m financially stable. But underneath all of that, I feel completely abandoned and rejected.

It feels like nobody actually wants to spend time with me. I’ve felt this way for a long time, like people just… leave. Friends move on, lose interest, stop reaching out. My closest friends live in another state now, and I barely see them. I try to be proactive: I invite people to hang out, grab drinks, go out somewhere, anything. It’s always “maybe sometime,” “I’m busy,” or just no response at all. It’s starting to feel less like bad timing and more like a pattern I can’t ignore.

Recently I finally worked up the courage to ask out a girl who really seemed to be interested. She was flirting, gave off all the signs (at least I thought so), and I felt confident for once. Then she rejected me. I know rejection happens, but it shook me more than I expected. It felt like confirmation of this fear that there’s something fundamentally wrong with me.

I keep asking myself what that “thing” is. Am I missing something obvious? Is there something about me that pushes people away without me realizing it? I’m doing everything I’m supposed to do as an adult, but I still feel like no one actually wants me around. It hurts, and I’m tired of feeling this way.
