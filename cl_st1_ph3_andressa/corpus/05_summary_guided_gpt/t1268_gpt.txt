I keep seeing people talk about a “loneliness epidemic” like it’s this mysterious force falling from the sky, but honestly, a huge part of it is us. Ordinary users. We sit behind screens, scroll past each other’s lives, and when we do say something it’s usually anger, sarcasm, or some shallow one-liner.

Everywhere you look, platforms are more censored, more sanitized, and less open to real, messy, human conversations. The kind where you actually feel seen and heard. Try to go deep and it either gets buried, removed, or met with awkward silence. People put time into sharing projects, thoughts, ideas, and they get maybe one pity comment or nothing at all—unless they’re already a big name. All the attention funnels up, and the rest of us are just background noise.

Then we wonder why everyone feels invisible.

Most commenters don’t seem to care about real connection anymore. It’s either outrage or hollow pity, nothing in between. Genuine communication is slowly dying, and what’s left are the loud extremists and these emotionless “robot” responses that feel copy-pasted.

But I don’t believe that’s all there is. Real humans are still here, and there are a lot of us. We’re just drowned out. And if we keep acting like this is normal, we’re helping to make this loneliness permanent.
