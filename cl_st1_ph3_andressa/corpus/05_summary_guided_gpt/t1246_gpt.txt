I just read this quote about “chosen ones” who isolate not because they’re antisocial, but because they’re overstimulated and frustrated by “mediocrity,” by having to dumb themselves down, by seeing patterns others don’t. It says that authentic, high-frequency people often don’t have many friends because truth is expensive, and most people prefer distraction over growth. It compares people like that to lighthouses: they stand alone, shining, until the right ships find them.

This hit me really hard. It feels like my life in a paragraph. I’m tired of feeling like I have to shrink my thoughts or pretend I don’t see what I see just to keep the peace or avoid awkwardness.

What do you think about this quote? Does it resonate with you? And if it does… where are the people we’re supposed to resonate with? Where are my “ships”?
