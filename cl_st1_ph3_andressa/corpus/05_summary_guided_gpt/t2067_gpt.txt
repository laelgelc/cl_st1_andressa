I’m 21 and I feel like I accidentally chose this life of isolation.

It wasn’t some dramatic decision, more like a thousand tiny non-choices. Not replying, not reaching out, not making plans, not putting effort into people who maybe did care at some point. Now I look around and all I have are these shallow, surface-level “relationships” that don’t feel like anything. It’s like I used up all my chances without realizing it.

My self-image is pretty wrecked. I don’t even feel actively miserable most of the time, just… empty. Numb. I’ve started telling myself that people are better off without me, that stepping back is some kind of twisted “service” I’m doing for them. Like I’m protecting them from how disappointing or draining I think I am. I know that’s irrational, but it still feels true in my head.

What hurts is knowing, logically, that a better life is possible. I could make friends, build something real, try to improve myself. But I feel like I’ve lost the will to even take the first step. I just sit here, stuck between wanting more and not believing I deserve it or can handle it.

I’m not even sure what I’m trying to say with this. I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head.
