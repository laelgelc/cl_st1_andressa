I’m 22 and I feel like my whole world kind of collapsed this past year. I lost my four closest friends, one after another, and every single “breakup” happened over text. No conversation, no trying to work things out, just… blocks of text and then silence.

Two of them told me I should “go to therapy” in their final messages. I can’t stop replaying that in my head. It felt so dismissive, like I was some problem to be fixed and not a person they cared about. I always thought I was a good friend – I was the one planning hangouts, trying to make memories, wanting to see them more and be part of their lives.

What hurts even more is that, deep down, if any of them reached out and wanted to be friends again, I know I’d probably say yes. And that makes me feel pathetic and degraded, like I have no self-respect.

It breaks my heart that people who knew me so well decided their lives are better without me. Now it’s just… empty. I feel completely alone, like there’s something fundamentally wrong with me that I can’t see.
