Humans are social animals, wired to need others. I keep thinking about how, not that long ago, being cast out from the group meant you might literally starve or get killed by predators. Now, there’s no wolf at the door, no obvious life-or-death threat. Instead, it feels like a slow, invisible kind of cruelty. My body keeps sounding the alarm, like I’m about to die, but nothing ever happens. No resolution, no end—just this chronic state of panic and emotional pain that never fully lets up.
