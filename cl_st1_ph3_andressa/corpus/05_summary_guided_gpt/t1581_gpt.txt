I’m working on a short film about a lonely teenager in a suburban Indian city, and I’m trying to get a better emotional handle on the kind of loneliness I want to portray.

The character copes by recording video journals on their phone – not for YouTube or anything, just for themselves – and I’m really interested in that space where someone is “connected” online but still feels completely isolated. Fragile DMs, group chats where you’re technically in it but not really part of it, people living in the same neighborhood who don’t truly see or understand each other.

I’m looking for movies and books that deal with loneliness and solitude in a way that feels raw and somewhat depressing, but not totally cynical or bleak. I don’t want misery for its own sake. I’m more drawn to stories that acknowledge how painful loneliness can be, yet still hold on to some kind of hope, or at least a sense that the pain might mean something.

Age-wise, I’d prefer stories about teens or young adults, ideally in suburban settings, and especially anything modern that deals with social media, online relationships, and that weird in‑between space of being surrounded by people but emotionally on your own.

For reference: I really liked Rilke’s “Letters to a Young Poet” – the idea of solitude being painful but important for creators really resonated. On the other hand, “The Catcher in the Rye” didn’t quite match the tone I’m going for.

Any film (any language, but English is easiest) or book recs that balance melancholy with some hopefulness or even a bit of humor would really help.
