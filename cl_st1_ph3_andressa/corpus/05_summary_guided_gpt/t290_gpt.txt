I’ve started seeing a counselor while I wait to get in with a therapist and psychiatrist. One of the main things she keeps telling me is that I need to think and speak more positively about myself. People I’ve dated have said the same thing, that I put myself down a lot and I’m really negative about myself.

The thing is, it doesn’t feel like I’m “choosing” to be negative. It feels automatic, like I’m just stating facts. This is genuinely how I see myself. When I try to say something nicer about myself, it feels fake and almost embarrassing, like I’m lying or pretending to be someone I’m not.

Deep depression and self-hatred feel like my baseline. It’s my “normal.” I’ve been like this for so long that I honestly don’t know what it would feel like to not hate myself. I’m at this point where I don’t even really care anymore; it’s just this constant background noise of “you’re worthless” that I’ve gotten used to.

At my last primary care visit, they did one of those mental health screenings and I scored the highest level on everything—3’s across the board. I walked out thinking that if I didn’t have to work and make money, they probably would’ve pushed for hospitalization or something more serious. But I just keep going because I feel like I have to.

Everyone always says you have to “love yourself first,” but I honestly don’t know how. I don’t even believe the positive things people say they see in me. It all feels like they’re either being nice or they don’t really know me.
