I grew up with my parents’ expectations hanging over me like a script I was supposed to follow. Somewhere along the way, I lost the plot. I never really fit in, always the outsider watching everyone else connect so easily. 

Women especially have left some deep scars—rejection, indifference, sometimes cruelty. It’s made me feel invisible, like I could disappear and no one would notice.

Yet I’m still here, quietly hoping there’s a way to reappear someday, to finally be seen and accepted for who I am.
