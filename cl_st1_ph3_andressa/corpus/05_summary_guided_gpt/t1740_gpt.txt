I feel like every time I let someone get close to me, it ends in the same way: badly, and with me wondering what the hell I did wrong.

I’ve had long-term friends – people I trusted for years – turn around and share things I told them in confidence. Deep, personal stuff I only said because I believed they were “my people.” And then suddenly other people know, or it gets thrown back in my face during an argument. It makes me feel stupid for ever opening up.

I’ve also had people I’ve known for years basically tell me I don’t matter to them. Not always in those exact words, but the message is clear: I’m not a priority, I’m an afterthought. And then there are the ones who just vanish. One day we’re talking regularly, sharing our lives, and then it’s like a switch flips. No explanation, no “I need space,” just silence. I’m left replaying every conversation, trying to figure out what I did to deserve being ghosted like that.

It feels like I’m always the least important person in everyone’s life. I’m the one who reaches out, checks in, remembers things about them, but it’s rarely returned. Communication feels so one-sided that I’m tired all the time. When I finally react to being treated badly or speak up about how hurt I am, somehow I become “the bad guy.” I get told I’m overreacting, too sensitive, or making things about me.

All of this has made me feel miserable and fake. I don’t feel like I can be genuine anymore because every time I try, it backfires. I either get used, ignored, or punished for having feelings.

I don’t know what to do at this point. How do you cope when friendships keep ending like this? How do you protect yourself without completely shutting down? Is there something I should be doing differently, or is this just how it is for some people? Any advice or perspective would really help.
