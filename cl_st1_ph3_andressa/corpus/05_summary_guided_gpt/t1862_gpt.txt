I’m 30 and I feel so painfully alone lately that it’s starting to mess with my head.

When I moved cities for college, I kind of lost everyone from my “old life.” It wasn’t dramatic or anything, people just drifted away, and I was bad at keeping in touch. College itself wasn’t great socially either. I didn’t really fit in, and I only ended up with one real friend. We stayed close after graduation, and for a long time I told myself, “At least I have her.”

I stayed in the same city for work, but it never turned into a place where I actually felt like I belonged. My coworkers are nice enough, but they’re at completely different life stages and have different interests. They have families, kids, established friend groups. I’m just… there. No one I can text randomly or make spontaneous plans with. Most days I go to work, come home, and spend the rest of the time alone. I got used to it on the surface, like I went numb, but I guess it was always there under everything.

Now even that one close college friend has started to fade away. She leaves me on read a lot, barely replies, and never really initiates anymore. It hurts more than I want to admit. It feels like confirmation that I’m not worth staying in touch with, that I’m forgettable.

I wish I could make new friends. I’d love to join something like a DnD group or some hobby thing, but the thought of reaching out to strangers makes me freeze. I’m so shy it feels impossible.

I don’t really know what I’m hoping for. I think I just needed to say it somewhere, because holding it all in is starting to feel heavy.
