I feel like I’ve spent my whole life standing just outside of everyone else’s circle.

It started in grade school. My classmates didn’t talk to me unless they had to, and even some teachers acted like I wasn’t there. The only person who treated me like a real human being was the cleaning lady, who was kind and actually spoke to me. That pretty much set the tone for everything that came after.

I tried to make friends in childhood, in my teens, and later at university, but everything stayed on the surface. People would chat with me in class or in group projects, but they never actually included me in anything. No one invited me to any graduation parties, and nobody came to mine. It was like I was always “nice enough to talk to,” but never someone they thought to actually spend time with.

Romantic stuff hasn’t gone anywhere either. On the rare occasions someone showed interest, it fizzled out fast. I’ve been ghosted after a few dates more than once. It’s like as soon as someone gets a slightly closer look, they decide I’m not worth sticking around for.

Even therapy hasn’t really worked out. One therapist ended things after about five sessions, and another never even scheduled a second one. That hurt in a different way, like even the people whose job it is to listen and help don’t really want to deal with me.

My relationships with family and coworkers are the same: polite, functional, but shallow. I try to connect, but it never seems to go anywhere deeper. Over time, I’ve gone pretty numb to it. I just expect to be ignored now, and I’m actually surprised when someone doesn’t overlook me.

At this point, my only real friends are my parents. I’m grateful for them, but it’s hard not to feel like that says something about me that no one else ever really chooses me.
