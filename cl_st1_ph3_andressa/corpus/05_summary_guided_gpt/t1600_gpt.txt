Sometimes I wonder why I should care about anything at all — life, the future, any of it — when it all just ends anyway. I try to explain this to people, but the words never come out right. I feel like I’m speaking a different language and everyone just shrugs me off or misunderstands me.

I keep thinking about this image in my head: the moon quietly doing her job, pulling the tides, keeping people safe while they sleep. No one really notices or thanks her. She just hangs there, alone, doing necessary work that goes unseen.

Then there’s this lone wolf who finally looks up and howls, not in anger but in admiration. For the first time, the moon feels seen. She stays because he values her. He stays awake to keep her company. For a while, they give each other a reason to exist.

But even they can’t stay together forever. They have to part. And when I think about that, I circle back to the same question: if everything ends, what’s the point of caring in the first place?
