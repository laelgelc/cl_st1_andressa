I’m 16 and it honestly feels like I’ve never had a stable friendship in my entire life. My family has always moved around or switched my classes, so basically every year since kindergarten I’ve lost my “best friend.” I’d finally get close to someone, start to feel comfortable, and then suddenly I’d be gone, or they would. By 6th grade I just kind of gave up on trying to make friends at all, but even then I still ended up forming connections that eventually fell apart the same way.

Since I couldn’t rely on real life, I threw myself into online stuff. That just made things worse. I got into these toxic online friendships where people would constantly make fun of me for not having money, or for how I look. It got inside my head so badly that now I’m obsessed with money and my appearance, even though I know it’s unhealthy. It’s like my whole self-image is ruined. I feel like I’m always comparing myself to everyone else and coming up short.

COVID just made everything harder. It’s like the tiny chance I had to build real-life friendships disappeared. Now it feels like the world is against me. I catch myself being narcissistic and self-loathing at the same time, like I’m focused on myself in all the worst ways. I feel uncaring, boring, and just… wrong.

I just want one person I can depend on or talk to honestly. Someone who won’t disappear after a year or turn everything into a joke about how I look or what I don’t have. But I can’t shake this fear that it’s never going to happen for me.
