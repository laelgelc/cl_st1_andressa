I’m 18 and I feel like I’ve spent my whole life obsessing over how other people see me, and no matter what “version” of myself I try on, I end up feeling like a loser.

When I was around 11–13 I was really into anime and kind of “cringe egirl” fashion. It actually made me happy, but it also made me feel like everyone saw me as weird. I was already self‑conscious, and those interests just felt like proof that I was this embarrassing, geeky outsider.

Around 15 I decided that meant I was shallow and materialistic, so I tried to completely reinvent myself. I stopped watching anime, stopped listening to pop music, tried to be “cool” and “smart” and “social.” I pushed myself to socialize even though it honestly felt painful and confusing. I never understood why it was so hard for me when it seemed easy for everyone else.

Now I’m in college and I still force myself to be social, but it feels fake. I lean on alcohol to get through it. I recently rewatched Jujutsu Kaisen and it’s like I’ve been thrown back to square one. I feel lonely, disgusting, unwanted, like I’m just inherently wrong.

I want so badly to change, to be liked, to be someone people actually pursue and want around. I just have no idea how to do that without completely destroying myself in the process.
