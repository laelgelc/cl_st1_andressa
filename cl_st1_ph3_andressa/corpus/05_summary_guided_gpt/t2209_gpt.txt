Lately I’ve been trying really hard to build a better life for myself. I started taking pottery classes to get out of the house and maybe meet people. I’ve got a decent routine going now, I cook at home, and I’ve even restarted my spiritual journey. All of this has honestly helped my mental health a lot compared to where I was.

But I still feel so lonely.

Dating feels especially hard and kind of terrifying. The idea of being vulnerable makes me want to run the other way, even though I don’t want to be alone forever. I don’t have a huge list of demands, I just want someone with similar values, who communicates well, and treats me right. Somehow that still feels out of reach.

I’m considering therapy because I feel torn between craving connection and being scared of someone really knowing me.

To top it off, my sister called me crying about a bad breakup. I’m trying to be there for her, but it’s hard when I’m struggling with my own loneliness and confusion. I feel like I’m barely holding myself together.
