Humans aren’t meant to be alone. Look at how prisons use solitary confinement as the harshest punishment they have. It literally breaks people down. That’s what isolation does to us.

I get why some of us choose to be alone, though. When you’ve been disappointed, ignored, or hurt over and over, it feels safer to just retreat and stop trying. You start telling yourself you’re better off on your own.

But having a real, supportive community is honestly life-changing. There *are* people out there who will listen, uplift you, and have your back. It might not be where you expect, and it might take time, but they exist.

Connection isn’t some optional bonus in life; it’s psychologically essential. We need to be seen, heard, and understood. So please, don’t give up on people completely. Keep looking, keep reaching out in small ways, and try to find your people. They’re out there, and you deserve them.
