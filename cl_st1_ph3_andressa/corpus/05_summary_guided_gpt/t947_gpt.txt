I don’t even know where to start with this, other than that I feel like something went fundamentally wrong with me a long time ago and I never caught up.

I’ve basically had no friends since kindergarten. Through elementary school, high school, community college, and then a 4‑year university, I was always physically around groups of people but never actually *in* them. They’d talk, laugh, make plans, and I’d just be there on the edge, never invited, never asked, and too awkward and scared to try to insert myself. It felt like there was some invisible wall between me and everyone else.

Around 2019–2020 it got so bad that I printed out a video game character’s face, taped it to a box, and talked to it. That was the only “person” I had to talk to. I knew it was sad and ridiculous, but the silence was worse.

Now I’m 28. I finished a 4‑year degree, I have a job, and somehow nothing has changed where it actually matters. I still have no one. No actual friends, no one I can text, no one who would notice if I disappeared. I’ve never been on a date. Everyone else seems to be moving through life stages—parties, relationships, trips, all these “firsts”—and I can literally count my own “special” moments on one hand each year.

When I go out and see people with friends, partners, families, I feel like I don’t belong to the same species. I feel like people see me as an asshole, or just someone not worth knowing. The worst part is how starved I am for any kind of connection. A coworker making a bit of small talk with me can make my entire day, and that just makes me feel even more like a loser.
