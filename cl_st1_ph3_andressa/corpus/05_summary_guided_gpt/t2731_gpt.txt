I’ve realized I actually really like being alone. I enjoy my own company. It’s not that I’m socially awkward or anything – I can talk to anyone if I feel like it. At school I just… don’t. I avoid big crowds, sit away from everyone at lunch, and keep to myself most of the time.

Most people around me are just “associates” in my head. I’d say I only have about four real friends, and my brother is the only person I’d call a best friend. I’m close to him, but even then, I still prefer to be by myself most of the time.

I think a lot of this comes from how I grew up. I was bullied for being fat in elementary and middle school. It really messed me up and made me not want to talk to people at all. The weird part is that in 6th grade I was apparently considered “popular,” but that didn’t change how I felt inside. The bullying stuck with me and kind of trained me to pull away from people.

Now I really see a difference between being lonely and being alone. I’m not completely isolated; I have people I can talk to if I want. I just usually choose not to. I don’t feel this big urge to be surrounded by friends or to always be social.

I also don’t want to get married. The idea of a quiet, drama-free life by myself actually sounds really nice.
