I was in a relationship years ago that completely broke me. It was intense and messy and hurtful in ways I didn’t even have words for back then. When it finally ended, I felt like there was nothing left of me. It took years of picking myself up, therapy, and a lot of quiet nights alone to even start feeling like a person again.

Recently I stumbled across their profile online. They’re married now, living the exact kind of life we used to talk about having together. Seeing it hit me harder than I expected. I don’t want them back, and I don’t miss the chaos. But there was this strange mix of pain and jealousy, like I was watching some alternate version of my life that never happened.

The thing is, I’ve met someone new. Someone who makes me feel safe and loved without all the drama and toxicity. I’m older now, and I’ve had help, and I can actually recognize what healthy love looks like.

Writing this out, I think I’m finally ready to let go of whatever ghost of that old relationship I’ve been carrying. It still hurts a bit, but I feel more at peace than I have in a long time. Life feels less like something I ruined and more like a journey that’s just beginning for me.
