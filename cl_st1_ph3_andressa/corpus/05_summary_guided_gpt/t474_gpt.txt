Christmas was quiet this year. No visitors, no noise, no schedule. I just moved slowly through the day: made coffee, ate when I felt like it, lay down when I was tired. Mostly I just sat with my thoughts.

I kept drifting back to the past. Old memories felt close, but not crushing. More like pages I could turn through without getting stuck. I was aware that other people were together, doing the whole festive thing, but it didn’t sting the way it sometimes has before.

There was no ceremony, no big moment. Just a simple day that came and went. And somehow, that was enough. Being on my own didn’t feel like a failure or a punishment. It was just… what it was. I had the day, and I had myself, and—for once—that actually felt sufficient.
