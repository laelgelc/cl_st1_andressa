I’ve been trying to understand why I still feel lonely even though I technically “have people” in my life, and I ended up making my own Maslow-style hierarchy of needs for social connection.

I’m neurodivergent, demisexual, and somehow a socially anxious extrovert. I can do surface-level interactions, group chats, casual hangouts, but they almost never feel like enough. It’s like most people are comfortable staying at the lower levels of connection: small talk, memes, shared hobbies. Those feel so shallow and fleeting to me.

I’m craving deeper, more consistent, emotionally safe bonds, and I’m realizing that’s why my social life still doesn’t feel fulfilling. It’s still lonely, but understanding this makes it hurt a bit less. I’m going to keep searching for people who really fit me.
