Three months ago I ended a relationship and, honestly, the time since has been rough. I’ve been sitting with this constant fear that I’ll never find love again, that maybe that was my “one shot” and I blew it. I’ve been so scared of being alone that I’ve questioned whether I made the right choice, even though I know deep down why it had to end.

Recently I decided to do something that terrified me in a different way: I took an overnight solo trip. Just me, no backup, no one to hide behind if I got anxious. I even went to a haunted house alone, which is something I never would have imagined myself doing. I was nervous the whole way there, but it ended up being one of the best experiences of my life. I felt more like myself than I have in a long time—like I was meeting the version of me that isn’t defined by a relationship.

On the drive back, a friend called asking for relationship advice. As they described what they were going through, it sounded so familiar to my last relationship that it was almost eerie. But instead of feeling sad or jealous, I felt this huge wave of relief and gratitude that my own relationship ended when it did. It reminded me why I keep my ex blocked and why I keep choosing myself.

I’m not magically “fixed” now. I know loneliness is still going to show up, probably a lot. I’ll still have nights where I miss having someone next to me, where I doubt everything. But for the first time in a while, I feel hopeful that I will find what I want someday—and that I don’t have to abandon myself to get it.

I’m determined not to question who I am or the choices I make just because of another person again. Even if I’m lonely sometimes, I’d rather be lonely as myself than lost in a relationship that doesn’t fit.
