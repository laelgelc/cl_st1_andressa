I don’t even know how to start this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like an outcast everywhere I go, like I’ve always been on the sidelines watching everyone else live real lives. I’ve never felt like a “real person” to others. No one checks on me, no one really wonders how I am. I’m just… there. Background noise.

I tried the whole “be yourself” thing. I tried the opposite too—copying how others talk, act, dress, trying to blend in and be like everyone else. Nothing ever worked. It’s like there’s this invisible wall between me and the rest of the world. I honestly don’t believe I’ll ever be an ordinary person with an ordinary life.

On top of that, I live in a country at war. I lost my home. I lost relatives. Every day is this constant pressure of stress and danger. There’s no safe place to go, no realistic way to leave. I have only temporary housing and live with the fear that I’ll be evicted and end up homeless. And because I feel like nobody needs me, that fear feels even heavier, like I’d just disappear and it wouldn’t matter.

I’ve always had insecurities and stress-related illnesses, but now there’s PTSD from the war layered on top of everything. I see myself as just… ordinary, unattractive, not rich, socially anxious, terrified of conflict, and honestly not sure how someone like me is supposed to function in society. I feel like I came into this world without the manual everyone else got.

I live in a country with low living standards, corruption everywhere, low wages, high prices, and no real social security. It feels like the whole system is built to crush people who are already weak. I feel very bad, unsupported, unloved. I’m scared I won’t survive alone, in every sense of that word.

If anyone here feels even a little bit like this—like you’re on the outside of life looking in, especially in a place that feels broken and unsafe—I’d really like to chat. I don’t have anyone in my life who understands, and maybe we could at least not feel so alone together.
