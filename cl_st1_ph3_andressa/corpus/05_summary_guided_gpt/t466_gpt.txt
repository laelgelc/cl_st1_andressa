I’ve always been someone who genuinely enjoys my alone time. I like having my own space, doing my own thing, not constantly being “on” for other people. But lately, that same aloneness has started to feel really heavy and… lonely.

On paper, I shouldn’t feel this way. I have a husband, I have friends, I have family who cares about me. But my friendships all feel kind of surface-level. I don’t feel like I’m anyone’s “person” or “best friend.” I see other women in their 20s with that one really close friend they do everything with, who knows all their secrets, and I’ve never really had that. My husband is my best friend, but it’s different. I want that close female friendship too, and it feels like I’ve somehow missed out.

It’s been hitting me harder lately because there are things I don’t want to do alone anymore. Going to the gym, for example – it would be so much easier and more fun with a friend. And after church, I see people chatting and laughing in little groups while I kind of stand there, feeling awkward and out of place. I want to go up to people and talk, I want to make friends, but I freeze. I don’t know how to start, and I’m honestly scared of being rejected or just… not fitting.

I don’t want to stay stuck in this lonely cycle, but I also don’t know how to break out of it. I just feel really lost and isolated, even though I’m not technically “alone.”
