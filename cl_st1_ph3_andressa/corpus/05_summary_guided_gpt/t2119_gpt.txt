I’m 22 and lately it feels like my life is stuck on repeat, especially around Valentine’s Day. Every year it’s the same: I see couples holding hands, going on dates, doing all those cheesy romantic things, and it just hits me how I’ve never had any of that.

I spent most of my life focused on academics and this dream of becoming a millionaire. I kept telling myself relationships could wait, that I didn’t need them, that success would fill the void. But now I look back and realize I’ve missed out on so many experiences people my age talk about so casually: having a crush who actually likes you back, falling in love, going on dates, sharing those small, intimate moments that mean everything.

On the outside, I act like I don’t care about relationships, like I’m above all that. Inside, I want it more than anything. I want someone I can be my real self with, without feeling judged or like I have to perform. Just someone I can be comfortable and honest with.

But when I look at how dating seems to work now—especially on apps—it makes me feel even more hopeless. Everything feels so shallow and fast, like if you don’t look or act a certain way, you’re invisible. It’s hard not to feel like I’ve already missed my chance before I’ve even started.
