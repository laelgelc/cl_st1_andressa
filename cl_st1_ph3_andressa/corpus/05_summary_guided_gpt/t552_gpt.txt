I’m 29, originally from Iran, and I’ve been in the US for about four years now for my master’s. I just moved to Texas by myself because of immigration stuff, and it’s really hitting me how much “being alone” has become the default setting of my life.

It’s not like this started with moving here. Even back in college, at work, traveling, doing hobbies… I’ve always been the one who has to initiate everything. If I don’t text first, if I don’t suggest plans, nothing happens. I’ve gotten used to doing most things alone: going to museums, watching movies in the theater, trying out new places. I’m genuinely comfortable with that part; I don’t feel ashamed sitting alone at a café or going to a movie by myself.

But there’s this painful side to it. When I’m out, I see groups of friends laughing, couples sharing inside jokes, people taking photos together. It’s like there’s this invisible wall between my world and theirs. I can do the same activities, but the “together” part feels like something I just don’t have access to. It’s not even that I’m desperate to be surrounded by people all the time; it’s more this constant reminder that I’m on the outside of something everyone else seems to have.

Remote work has made all of this worse. At least with in-person work or school there was some built-in contact, even if it was shallow. Now my days are mostly just me, my laptop, my apartment. On the rare occasions I do spend time with people, I almost feel disoriented, like I’ve forgotten how to be around others. And then, inevitably, I go back to being alone again, like that’s the “real” state and everything else was a temporary glitch.

I’ve basically paused dating. I value my alone time a lot, and the constant messaging, check-ins, and small obligations of a relationship feel draining compared to the few shared moments they bring. It doesn’t feel like a good trade-off. So I end up choosing solitude, even though that same solitude hurts in its own way.
