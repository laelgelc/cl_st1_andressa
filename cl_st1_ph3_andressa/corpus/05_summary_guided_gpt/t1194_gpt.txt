I just graduated, earlier than I was even supposed to, and I thought I’d feel…something more than this.

I’m 31, a stay-at-home mom of three. For the past few years I’ve been juggling kids, the house, pets, and a boyfriend while cramming schoolwork into nap times, late nights, and any quiet moment I could steal. I really busted my ass to finish, and I actually did it.

But the excitement died almost immediately.

My parents basically told me I was selfish for going back to school, that I should’ve focused only on the kids. No “congratulations,” no “we’re proud of you,” just guilt. My boyfriend gave me a quick “I’m proud of you” without looking up from his phone and then went right back to whatever he was doing. That was it. No dinner, no cake, no “let’s celebrate.” Just another day.

I don’t have any friends to share this with. Somewhere along the way, between having kids and being home all the time, I forgot how to make friends. I don’t even know where to start anymore. Everything feels so online and shallow, and I miss real face-to-face conversations, having someone actually see me.

I thought this achievement would make me feel less stuck, less invisible. Instead, it’s like it happened in a vacuum. I’m proud of myself, but it hurts that no one else seems to care. I just wanted someone to be excited with me, and instead I feel more lonely than ever.
