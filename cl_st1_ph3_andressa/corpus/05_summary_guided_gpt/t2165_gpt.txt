I don’t really know who I am anymore.

I used to be that kid who was always joking around, never serious, just messing about all the time. About a year ago I decided I didn’t want to be that person anymore. I tried to become more mature, quieter, started focusing on working out and just keeping my head down.

Now I just feel miserable and completely unmotivated. At school people keep making fun of me, asking why I look so sad or why I’m acting “weird” now. When I try to explain, it always turns into a joke or they brush it off, so I’ve basically stopped trying. Even my parents don’t really take me seriously. It feels like no one believes I could actually be struggling.

So I put on this act like everything’s fine. I still crack jokes, still play the clown sometimes, but it feels fake and exhausting. It’s like I’m forcing this old version of myself just so people don’t ask questions or make it awkward.

I try to stay quiet and distant, just keep to myself, but people—mostly girls, for some reason—keep coming up to me and asking what’s wrong, why I look so down. I never know what to say. I don’t want to dump everything on them, but I also don’t want to keep lying and saying “I’m fine.”

I feel stuck between who I was and who I’m trying to be, and it just feels like neither version is actually allowed to be real.
