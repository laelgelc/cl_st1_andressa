My brother and I used to be really close. He was always the one I could rely on, and we’d talk about everything. A few months ago he agreed to pick me up from the airport, and I really held onto that – it felt like one of those small but meaningful things that showed we were still connected.

Now the trip is getting close, and I just found out he completely forgot and made vacation plans with his partner instead. There’s no one else who can help me out, and somehow that part hurts even more. It’s not just about the ride, it’s the feeling that I’m not really on his radar anymore.

Since he got his new job and started seeing his partner, he’s become busier and more distant. He takes a long time to respond to my texts, but I see how quickly he replies to his partner. I keep telling myself it’s normal that his life is changing and I’m genuinely happy for him, but I can’t shake this feeling of being pushed further and further down his list of priorities.

I don’t want to confront him because I know he carried a lot of responsibility as the oldest and did so much for me growing up. I don’t want to make him feel guilty for finally focusing on himself. But at the same time, I feel really empty and alone, like I’ve lost the one person I was closest to.

I don’t know how to cope with this mix of happiness for him and loneliness for myself. How do you deal with feeling left behind by someone you love, without resenting them for moving on with their life?
