I hit a pretty big milestone recently, like the kind of thing that’s basically the equivalent of finishing a postgrad degree. I’d imagined this day for years. I pictured my family being proud, taking nice photos, making it feel like a real celebration.

My siblings couldn’t make it, so it was just my parents. I asked my mom ahead of time to take some good pictures because I wanted at least a few memories of the day. She took nine photos total, and every single one is terrible – bad angles, blurry, my eyes half closed. It sounds shallow, but it really stung. This was a once‑in‑a‑lifetime moment and there’s basically no record of it.

We went out for a “celebratory” lunch afterwards, and my mom complained through most of it. Both my parents kept sniping at each other and scrolling on their phones. I just sat there in silence, feeling like a background character at my own celebration. At one point my mom did a quick, almost throwaway “cheers, congratulations,” then went straight back to complaining.

There were no flowers, no card, no small gift, nothing. They told me they “didn’t know what to get,” so they just… didn’t get anything.

I keep trying for my family, and it hurts that they don’t seem to put in the same effort for me, especially on a day that meant so much.

If anyone here wants a friend or someone to chat with, my DMs are open. I don’t want anyone else to feel this alone.
