I’ve always told myself I’d walk my own road. No one to lean on, no one to answer to. Make my own rules, carry my own weight, walk free. It sounds brave when I phrase it like that, but most days it just feels like standing on the edge of a cliff and pretending I’m not scared of the drop.

I move through crowds like oil in a bucket of water. Everyone else seems to blend, to mix, to just…belong. I don’t feel like I’m “wrong,” exactly. More like I’m in the wrong container, surrounded by people whose world makes sense to them in a way mine never has.

That’s when all the little things other people seem to value start to feel pointless. Small talk, polite smiles, the constant dance of “How are you?” “I’m fine.” What’s the point of that when I’m not even sure I have any real worth, when life itself feels kind of hollow?

I keep circling back to this thought: if I’m probably going to fail anyway, why not just try? If I assume things won’t work out, at least I can prepare for the crash. Maybe that way it’ll hurt less when I hit the ground.

And somehow, that tiny reduction in pain feels like the closest thing to success I can realistically aim for. Not winning, not thriving—just failing in a way that doesn’t completely break me.
