My whole life feels like it’s been designed for one person. I shop for one, order takeout for one, cook for one. I drive everywhere with an empty passenger seat that almost feels symbolic at this point. On my days off, there’s never anything on the calendar except sleep, errands, and trying to kill time.

My phone is mostly silent. I rarely get texts unless it’s something practical. No one really asks how my weekend was, if I’m seeing anyone, or what I’ve been up to. It’s like my life is happening off to the side of everyone else’s, and no one’s really looking over.

I go to the beach alone. I go to bars alone. I sit there and watch groups of friends or couples, and sometimes people look at me with that confused “you’re by yourself?” expression. I’m so used to it that I don’t even know how I’d act if someone actually wanted to come with me.

The messed up part is that I’ve become extremely self-sufficient. I can do everything on my own. But underneath that is this constant hunger for connection. When someone shows me the slightest bit of kindness, I latch on way too fast in my head. Then I panic, pull back, and convince myself it’s going to end badly anyway.

It feels like I’m stuck in this loop of wanting people so badly, but also expecting to end up alone every single time. It hurts more than I know how to put into words.
