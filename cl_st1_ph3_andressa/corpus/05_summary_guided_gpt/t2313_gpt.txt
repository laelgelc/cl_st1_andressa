I wrote this during a really rough, lonely stretch, and it still feels painfully true.

I’m so tired. I don’t mean physically, but that deep, soul-level exhaustion. I just want a place to rest that actually feels like home. A pair of arms that feel safe. Someone who will wipe my tears without me having to explain everything first. Someone who knows me and still chooses me, exactly as I am.

I don’t have parents to call for advice or simple kindness. No one to say, “You did your best,” or “I’m proud of you,” or “Come over, we’ll figure it out.” People tell me I’m mature and responsible, and most days I do manage on my own. I try to be my own home. But it doesn’t always feel like enough.

Holidays are the worst. Random days can be, too—when something good or bad happens and I instinctively reach for a phone I don’t have anyone to dial on. No long-term, blood-related family. No default person.

If family is what really counts in the end, then what does that make me? I don’t know where I belong, or who I am, without that anchor.
