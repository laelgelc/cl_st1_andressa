Two years ago my last remaining “friend” invited me over. I thought maybe we’d actually hang out like normal people. Instead, he and another guy spent the whole time mocking me. At one point he even bragged that my old high school crush had sent him nudes and offered to show me, fully knowing how badly she’d rejected me back then. It felt like someone twisting a knife.

That night kind of broke something in me. I started replaying my whole life and realized my “friends” never really included me in fun stuff. I was always the extra, the joke, the one they made fun of to entertain each other.

I think I’d been in denial for years, telling myself I had friends because the alternative—admitting I was basically alone—felt unbearable. Now I’m just left with this mix of loneliness and the realization that I was being bullied the whole time.
