I’m 26, currently recovering from strep throat, and I’ve basically spent the last few days alone in my room watching my favorite comfort movie on repeat. What’s surprising me is how genuinely content I feel.

I’ve always assumed I needed a partner to be truly happy, like that was the “end goal.” But sitting here, sick and kind of gross, just existing with myself and my movie, I’m realizing I might actually be okay long-term with just pets and occasional short visits with friends and family. And I do have plenty of those people in my life.

I had a dating situation crash and burn recently, and it kind of pushed this feeling into focus. It’s been about five years since I’ve had a serious relationship, and instead of panicking about that, I’m starting to wonder if maybe I’m just… meant for this “blissful solitude.”

It feels weirdly cool to actually like my own company. At the same time, it sometimes seems to unsettle other people when I say I might be fine never having a traditional relationship. I don’t know exactly what my future looks like, but for the first time, the idea of it being mostly me doesn’t feel sad. It feels peaceful.
