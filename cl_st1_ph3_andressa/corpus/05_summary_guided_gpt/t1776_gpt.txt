I’m in my 30s, working in London, and on paper things look good – decent job, stable life, all the usual markers. But I feel incredibly lonely and isolated, to the point it’s affecting my work and I’ve actually resigned from several jobs over it.

The pattern is always the same: I’m the one who shows interest. I’m the one asking how people’s weekend was, following up if someone’s been off sick, trying to remember little details about their lives and bring them up later. I’m the one who notices when someone is sitting alone and tries to include them in conversation or invite them along to things.

But it almost never seems to go the other way. Very few people ask me how I am, how my weekend was, or follow up if I’ve been away or unwell. If I don’t make the effort, the interaction just… doesn’t happen. In groups, at work or socially, people form their cliques and I’m just kind of there on the periphery. I can leave a group or a room and it feels like no one even notices.

It hurts more than I like to admit. I start to wonder what’s wrong with me, even though I know I’m trying to be kind and open. I value sincerity, humility, and shared interests, but it feels like I never even get to that stage with most people; they don’t seem interested in knowing me beyond surface-level small talk, if that.

I’ve lived in a few different countries and, for me at least, London has felt especially cold and cliquey. People seem busy, already “full” socially, or just not that bothered about connecting beyond their existing circles. I know that’s a generalisation, but it’s how it feels from where I’m standing.

I don’t really know if there’s a solution. I’m tired of always being the one who reaches out, and tired of feeling invisible. I’m mostly posting this to see if anyone else has gone through something similar – especially in London or in their 30s – and how you’ve coped with it. How do you keep going when it feels like you’re always on the outside looking in?
