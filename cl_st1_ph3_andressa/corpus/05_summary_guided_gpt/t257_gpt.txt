I’m 40 and I feel like I’m just running on fumes, going through the motions because I don’t know what else to do. My life hasn’t been some tragic nightmare compared to what a lot of people go through, but it’s always felt… flat. Like I missed some crucial step everyone else got.

I was born with learning disabilities and was always struggling just to function at what everyone else saw as a “normal” level. I grew up being shamed for it, abused for it, sometimes treated like I wasn’t even fully human. It wasn’t just “kids being mean”; it felt like a constant message that I was wrong at my core.

By 11, it really hit me that my peers genuinely loathed me for being different. So I just… stopped. I became a loner. I barely spoke, avoided people as much as I could—especially girls and women—because I thought that’s what everyone wanted from me, for me to disappear into the background.

That went on until I was about 26. Even when I joined the Army, nothing really changed in that sense. If anything, it felt confirmed: people seemed to like me more the less I talked, the less I was even there. The quieter I was, the smoother things went.

Now I feel like I naturally bore or repel people. I try not to “inflict” myself on anyone. I tell myself this is just the result of choices I made years ago, and that I have to accept it. But it’s a lonely way to exist, feeling like the best thing I can do for others is to stay out of sight.
