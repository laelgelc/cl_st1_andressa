I used to be totally fine with spending a lot of time alone, as long as I had a few regular social things to look forward to. I’ve never really wanted a romantic relationship – that kind of intensity just feels overwhelming to me – so my friendships were basically my main source of connection.

Over the last decade, that’s just… evaporated. The regular hangouts slowly became less frequent, then sporadic, and now I’m lucky if I get one night a month at a friend’s house. And even then, it’s usually with kids running around, constant interruptions, and it’s almost impossible to have a real conversation or relax. It feels more like I’m just there, observing their family life from the outside.

I bought a house two years ago and only one person has ever come over. Everyone else just says they’re busy. The friends without kids don’t invite me out because they “don’t want me to be a third wheel,” which just makes me feel like some kind of social burden.

I have pretty bad social anxiety, but I’ve been forcing myself to go out more since the pandemic restrictions eased. The problem is I do almost everything alone. I go to events, shows, whatever, and I’m just… by myself. I feel like a loser sitting or standing there alone while everyone else seems to have someone.

It feels like things are only going to get worse from here. I’m honestly close to giving up on even the long-term friendships I have left, because it feels like I’m the only one still trying, and I’m so tired of feeling like this.
