I’ve dealt with depression and intrusive negative thoughts for as long as I can remember. For years, instead of actually getting help, I kind of used being in a relationship as my “treatment.” Having someone there made the noise in my head quieter, or at least easier to ignore.

When that relationship ended, it felt like the floor dropped out from under me. Suddenly I was alone again, but this time it wasn’t my choice. Now it feels like maybe this is how it’s supposed to be, like I’m meant to be alone and I don’t deserve love, comfort, or another person’s calming presence.

I know, logically, that these thoughts are coming from an ill‑minded place. I can recognize that they’re probably not true, but they’re so loud and so constant that it’s exhausting. It’s like fighting with a part of myself all day.

I don’t really have a point to this post other than to vent. I’m just tired of feeling like I’m fundamentally unlovable and that being alone is all I’m ever going to get.
