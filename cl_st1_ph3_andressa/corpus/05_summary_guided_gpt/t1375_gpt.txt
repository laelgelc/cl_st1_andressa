I’m 20, a guy, and I work as a writer/director. Lately I’ve been thinking a lot about how everyone keeps calling this a “loneliness epidemic,” and how weird it is that something so invisible can be so universal. I’m lonely too, in my own way. Not always in the obvious sense of having nobody around, but in that deeper sense of feeling like there’s a space inside you that never really gets filled.

I’ve been trying to make some kind of peace with that, and the only way I’ve found is to believe there’s a kind of beauty in loneliness—if it isn’t wasted.

What I mean is: if you’re going to hurt anyway, if you’re going to feel that empty space anyway, you can try to pour it into something. Anything. Writing, drawing, photography, cooking, sports, science, whatever your thing is or could be. It doesn’t have to be “good.” Honestly, it doesn’t even have to be shared with anyone. The point isn’t to impress people, it’s just to create.

For me, that’s writing and directing. I take the feelings that I don’t know how to talk about in real life and I build little worlds out of them. It doesn’t fix the loneliness, but it does something important: it turns tragedy into a bit of beauty. It makes the pain feel like it’s at least going somewhere, like it’s not completely pointless.

I know this isn’t a cure. It won’t magically give you friends or a relationship or a support system. But I really believe a little beauty goes a long way. Making something—however small, however “bad”—can make your own world slightly softer, and maybe, in tiny ways, it makes the bigger world a little better too.

If any of this resonates with you, or if you think I’m totally off base or just crazy for romanticizing this, feel free to comment or message me. I’m genuinely curious how other people deal with their own loneliness.
