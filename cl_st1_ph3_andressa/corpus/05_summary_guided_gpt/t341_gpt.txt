Silence has this strange way of talking to me. In a big room where anything could happen, I still end up choosing a quiet corner, sitting there alone. It feels like time itself is speaking, passing softly between old friends and new ones I haven’t met yet. In that space, my mind finally slows down. The usual worries about people I care about, the questions about who’s drifting away or who still thinks of me—they all fade for a while. In that quiet, I actually feel okay.
