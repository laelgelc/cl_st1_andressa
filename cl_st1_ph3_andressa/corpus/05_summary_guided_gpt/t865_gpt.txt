I feel like my “best years” were just… taken from me.

Because of social anxiety I never went to public school, so I missed out on all the stuff people talk about as normal teenage life – parties, hanging out after class, awkward first dates, even just having a group chat to complain about homework. I’m starting my senior year now and it honestly feels like it’s way too late to fix any of this.

Most days I’m just in my room, alone, scrolling or watching things, feeling like an outcast. I don’t really know how to act around people my age because I never had those experiences where you slowly figure it out. It feels like my social development is stuck at some earlier point and everyone else is miles ahead of me.

I’m planning to start a job soon, which I know is at least some kind of step forward, but I’m worried that even there I won’t really meet anyone my age or that I’ll just be too awkward to connect. I keep imagining myself being the quiet coworker no one really knows, and it makes me feel even more hopeless.

If anyone has gone through something similar, how did you start building a life that feels “normal”? How did you make friends or catch up socially when you felt so far behind? I’m really struggling to see a way forward and would appreciate any advice or just hearing from people who get it.
