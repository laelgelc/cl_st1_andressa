I spent so long telling myself “later.” I’d avoid responsibility, avoid uncomfortable truths, and blame whatever circumstances were convenient. It felt easier in the moment, but all those “laters” added up to a life without real moments. The honest people around me slowly disappeared, and I didn’t even fight it. I laughed with others, but inside I felt completely empty.

At some point I looked at myself and didn’t recognize who I was anymore—not my face, but my intentions. I had become someone who ran from everything that hurt. I thought my mistake was the choices I made, but now I see the real mistake was refusing to face my pain. I let every unhealed wound just sit there and pile up.

Now I’m trying something different. I’m admitting my mistakes without excuses, without blaming anyone or anything else. I know I can’t fix the past, but I can choose a different path from here. This kind of honest self-acceptance feels strange, but it also makes me feel lighter, more real, even if I’m still far from perfect.
