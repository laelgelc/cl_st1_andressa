I grew up being “the annoying one.” The kid who talked too much, overshared, trauma dumped on anyone who’d listen. I didn’t have much self-awareness back then. I just knew I felt ignored most of the time and was desperate for any kind of connection, even if it meant overwhelming people or crossing boundaries I didn’t understand.

For a long time I thought the solution to my loneliness was more people, more talking, more attention. I chased that feeling of being heard so hard that I never stopped to ask myself what I actually needed.

Somewhere along the way, as I got older and finally met people who genuinely matched my energy, something unexpected happened: I realized I really, truly love being alone.

Now some of my happiest moments are just me, noise-cancelling headphones on, sitting in silence. Not doing anything “productive.” Not scrolling. Just existing and letting thoughts drift in and out. Sometimes it brings up sadness or memories I’d rather avoid, but somehow that feels healing instead of terrifying. It’s like I’m finally giving myself the attention I kept begging other people for.

I see so many people chasing happiness in relationships, parties, constant social plans. And there’s nothing wrong with that. But I’ve found a weird kind of contentment in what looks like “loneliness” from the outside.

If you’ve tried everything else and nothing feels right, maybe it’s okay to experiment with just being alone with yourself for a while. Maybe the connection you’ve been missing is actually with you.
