My apartment is a disaster right now. I know it would probably take eight solid hours to get it into decent shape, but every time I think about actually doing it, it feels emotionally painful, like a piece of me dies with every trash bag I fill. It sounds ridiculous even to me.

I keep thinking about just hiring a maid, but that feels “too easy,” like I’m cheating or doing something wrong. Same with the thought of paying for sex or whatever. It all feels so empty and transactional, like it’s missing the point: love, connection, an unconditional bond.

Part of me thinks, “If I were married, this would be solved. A clean home, sex, someone there.” But then I imagine losing my solitude for decades, and that scares me. I’m stuck in this weird catch-22: craving connection and help, clinging to my solitude, and feeling like every practical workaround is hollow.
