I’ve been texting this girl I’ve known for a while. I genuinely see her like a sister, not romantically. For a long time I thought she just didn’t like me because she takes ages to respond and leaves me on delivered a lot. Recently she told me that’s not true, that she does like talking to me and it’s not personal.

Logically I believe her, but emotionally it still really hurts.

I haven’t had any friends to talk to outside my house for about 7 months. No job, just stuck with my own thoughts and dealing with depression, ADHD, and anxiety. So when she doesn’t reply for a long time, it feels like my brain goes into overdrive. I start hyperfixating, rereading messages, sending follow-ups, and then hating myself for being “too much.”

When we do talk, it’s great. Our conversations are deep and genuine. But the gaps between replies mess me up. I get a flood of negative thoughts: that I’m annoying, that she’s pulling away, that I’m just some background character in her life while she’s my only real connection. It’s affecting my focus and even my sleep.

Can anyone relate to this? How do you deal with these irrational, obsessive thoughts when you know they’re not logical but they still feel so real? Any advice or coping strategies would mean a lot.
