Some time ago, a girl from this subreddit messaged me after one of my posts. At first I was kind of hopeful – it’s rare enough to get any kind of connection here. We started talking and it seemed okay, until the topic of AI came up.

She had an extreme dislike of AI. Not just “I don’t like it” but basically “if you use it at all, you’re part of the problem.” I tried to be honest: I’m critical of AI in a lot of ways, I see the issues and I don’t blindly trust it, but I still use it sometimes. To me that felt like a pretty normal, nuanced position.

To her, that was completely unacceptable. She basically demanded that I give it up entirely if I wanted to keep talking to her. No middle ground, no “agree to disagree,” just a hard line. I said I wasn’t willing to do that, and suggested we could just part ways calmly if it bothered her that much.

She blocked me anyway.

I’ve been thinking about it since. I still feel like I was being reasonable and she was just hostile. And it really makes me think that a lot of modern loneliness isn’t just about not meeting people – it’s about how little tolerance there is for any difference of opinion. One disagreement and people are ready to erase you from their life.

What’s ironic is that a lot of people and communities that loudly brand themselves as “very tolerant” can end up being the most rigid and radical about this kind of thing. My main takeaway from this is: we really shouldn’t be blocking people over trivial differences. It just cuts off potential connections before they even have a chance.
