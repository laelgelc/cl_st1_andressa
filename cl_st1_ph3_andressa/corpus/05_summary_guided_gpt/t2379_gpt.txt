Every day after work I just get on my scooter and ride. No destination, no plan, no idea when I’ll turn back. I just keep going until the city noise feels a little more distant, like I’m slipping out of the chaos without even knowing what, exactly, I’m trying to get away from.

It reminds me of that story about the penguin that leaves its herd and walks alone into the cold, unknown mountains. Everyone watches, confused, as it keeps going in the “wrong” direction, and no one really knows why.

That penguin feels weirdly relatable. Like something in me is wandering off too, away from what’s familiar, without a clear reason or goal. I wonder if anyone else feels like that—just quietly drifting away from everything, hoping the distance will somehow make sense.
