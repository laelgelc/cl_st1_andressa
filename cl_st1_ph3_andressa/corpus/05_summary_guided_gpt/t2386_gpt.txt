I grew up moving around a lot, never staying in one place long enough for anything to really stick. Every time I started to get to know people, it was time to pack up and leave again. I guess somewhere along the way I just stopped letting anyone in properly, because what was the point if I’d be gone soon anyway?

Now I’m older and technically I have “friends.” I have people I talk to, people I joke around with. But it all feels like I’m playing a character. It’s like there’s this mask I put on automatically, and that’s all anyone ever sees. No one seems to remember or notice who I am underneath it, and I don’t even know how to show that side anymore.

What I want sounds so simple: just one close best friend. Someone I can be fully open with, who I can feel comfortable around without constantly second-guessing myself. Someone I can just “do nothing” with—sit around, talk about our days, hang out in silence, and still feel connected. Just existing together without feeling like I’m performing.

Instead, I wake up, check my phone, and there’s nothing. No messages, no “good morning,” no “how are you?” It makes me feel so small and invisible, like I don’t really exist in anyone’s life unless I force my way in. That moment in the morning hits me harder than I want to admit. It just crushes me.

I keep wondering if my mind is too damaged to ever feel truly comfortable with anyone. Like all those years of moving and never belonging messed me up beyond repair. Sometimes I wish I had just been born as someone else entirely—someone who knows how to connect, who doesn’t feel this empty all the time.
