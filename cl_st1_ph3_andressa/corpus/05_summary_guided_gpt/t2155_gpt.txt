I’m 45 and I’ve never been in a relationship. No girlfriend, no dates that turned into anything, not even a casual coffee that led somewhere. I’m trying to change things: losing weight, working on my confidence, putting myself out there on dating apps. But so far, nothing. I can’t even seem to get a simple coffee date.

For most of my life I convinced myself I liked being alone. I told myself I was “built for solitude,” that I preferred my own company. The truth is, I said that because I didn’t have any other option. Recently I had a brief experience with someone in an open relationship, and for the first time I got a tiny glimpse of what it might feel like to be wanted, to be close to someone. It was short and not really “mine,” but it cracked something open in me. Since then, I’ve felt pretty depressed.

I’m still going to keep looking for love. In the meantime, I’ve started a kind of bucket list of things I’ve never experienced: holding hands while driving, kissing someone on New Year’s Eve, cuddling on the couch, making breakfast in bed, cooking a romantic dinner, traveling with someone, sharing an impromptu slow dance in the kitchen.

It hurts to want these simple things so badly and have them feel so far away.
