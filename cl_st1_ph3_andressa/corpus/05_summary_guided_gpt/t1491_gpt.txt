I keep thinking about how social media was sold to us as this magical cure for alienation, like all we needed was more connection, more messages, more “friends.” Yet here we are, scrolling endlessly, and people seem as lonely and isolated as ever.

When I look around online, I mostly see sad seekers of human connection, myself included. We’re all out here broadcasting pieces of ourselves, hoping someone will really see us, but it feels like we’re drifting further into our own little islands, tribes, and cliques. It’s like the more ways we have to talk, the less we actually understand each other.

I don’t think technology is fixing anything fundamental. It just puts a shiny filter over the same old human problems and calls it progress. The emptiness doesn’t go away; it just gets distracted for a while.

I’m starting to feel like if we really want to understand loneliness, we have to look past the apps and platforms and dig into something deeper, more psychological, maybe even something universal that we don’t really know how to name yet.
