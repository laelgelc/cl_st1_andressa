Sometimes I feel like I’m walking around with this invisible “contaminated” stamp on me. I know I’m damaged by things that happened in the past, but I can’t really talk about them. Every time I even get close to the details, people get uncomfortable, or the vibe changes, so I end up pulling back. It makes me feel like the truth of who I am is something that has to be hidden.

The weird thing is, I’m not completely alone. I have a supportive network, people who care, even a couple of close friends. On paper, I shouldn’t feel lonely. But I still do, constantly. It’s like there’s a layer of glass between me and everyone else. I can see them, they can see me, but nothing really touches.

I think a big part of it is that I genuinely struggle to believe anyone could like me if they really knew everything. So I keep people at a distance, even while I’m craving connection. It’s like I’m sabotaging myself and I know I’m doing it, but I can’t seem to stop.

I’m trying to work on myself and I know this kind of healing is slow. But a lot of the time all I feel is anger, loneliness, misery, bitterness, and this huge emptiness inside. I’m so tired of feeling like this broken version of a person that nobody can ever actually reach.
