I don’t even know where to put all this hurt anymore, so I’m putting it here.

I fought so hard to become a mom. Five miscarriages and an ectopic pregnancy before I finally had my miracle baby. I poured everything into her – my time, my love, my health, my sanity sometimes. She was my whole world. Now she’s 33, married, living in Georgia, and it feels like I barely exist to her.

My birthday came and went this year and she didn’t even acknowledge it. When I finally brought it up, she said she thought we “weren’t buying each other things anymore,” like it’s about money or gifts. She and her husband are well-off, so it’s not that. It’s the complete lack of effort. A call. A card. Even a text. Anything to show she remembered, that I still matter to her in some way. It just broke my heart.

She rarely calls. Visits are even rarer. I feel like I’m this obligation she resents, instead of the mother who would have walked through fire for her. I can’t help but think she’s selfish and doesn’t care, and then I feel guilty for even thinking that.

I’m still married to her father, but the marriage feels empty – no real emotional connection, no physical closeness. We coexist more than anything else. Between that and losing my mom to cancer nine years ago – my best friend, the one person who always made me feel truly loved – I feel like I’ve lost every anchor I had.

I dreamed of being a grandmother, of having the kind of close relationship with my daughter that I had with my own mom. That dream just… never happened.

I’m so lonely and heartbroken. I don’t know what else to do except hand it over to God and hope He sees how tired and sad I am.
