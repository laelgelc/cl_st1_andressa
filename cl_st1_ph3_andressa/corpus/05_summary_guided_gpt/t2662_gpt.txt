I’ve always genuinely liked being alone. Not in a “no one likes me so I guess I’ll be alone” way, but in a “this actually feels peaceful and right for me” way. I do have good friends, people I care about and who care about me, but I just haven’t hung out with anyone since around February. And honestly, I’m okay with that. I feel content. Solitude is fulfilling for me.

What’s really getting to me lately isn’t the being alone part, it’s how other people react to it. At the gym, at work, even online, people feel the need to comment on it and judge me. I’ve been called weird, awkward, gay, a douche, stuck up – like not constantly, but enough that it sticks in my head. I’ve had people tell me I’ll “regret being alone” someday, like they somehow know my future better than I do.

It just feels really closed-minded. Not everyone needs the same level of social interaction to feel okay. I’m not depressed, I’m not trying to get attention, I’m not secretly crying about it. I’ve actually found a kind of tranquility in being by myself. I wish people could just let others live the way that works for them instead of projecting their own fears or expectations.

I guess I’m just venting to people who might understand what it’s like to genuinely prefer your own company and still get judged for it. If you’re the same way, you’re not broken or “less than.” Some of us just function better with more quiet and less crowd.
