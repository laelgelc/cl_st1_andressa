I used to think I was just built differently. As a kid, I was completely fine being on my own. I didn’t feel lonely, I didn’t crave friends, I didn’t want to be included. I was socially apathetic and it honestly felt natural.  

But over the last three years something changed. Now, when I see other people get positive attention so easily, it hits me in a way it never did before. Compliments, affection, people laughing at their jokes, wanting to be around them… it’s like I’m watching a world I was never allowed into. I’ve never had that, and it makes me feel like some kind of pariah who’s been quietly rejected my whole life.  

Because I never cared much about others before, and because of bullying, I never developed social skills. I don’t know how to make friends, or how to present myself in a way people find appealing. I *think* I want a friend now, but every time I try to interact online, I just feel this mix of disinterest and distress. It feels fake, forced, or exhausting, and I end up withdrawing again.  

What I really want feels almost childish: to be loved and cared about without having to earn it, without having to constantly “provide value” or be entertaining or useful. The kind of unconditional love parents are supposed to give their kids. I don’t think I ever got that, and now I’m not sure how to live with this new feeling of loneliness or what to do with this late, awkward desire to finally be wanted.
