I don’t even know what I’m looking for by writing this, but I feel like I’m running on fumes and I don’t have anywhere else to put it.

On paper my life is “full.” I’m constantly busy, always doing something for someone. I’m surrounded by family. I have a wife, kids, responsibilities stacked on responsibilities. Every day is a checklist: work, chores, kids, errands, repeat. From the outside it probably looks like a normal, functioning life.

Inside, I feel completely empty.

My relationship with my wife feels… drained. We’re more like co-workers sharing a project than partners. I can see that she’s exhausted and empty too, and that just adds another layer of guilt on top of everything else. We’re both just trying to keep everything going, but the connection feels so distant. I don’t even know how to reach for it anymore.

I haven’t had a close friend in years. There’s just no room. Every bit of time and energy goes into obligations and raising the kids. Whatever hopes or personal happiness I had just got quietly pushed aside, one compromise at a time. Now it feels like there’s nothing left that’s really mine.

I sit alone sometimes and wonder what’s wrong with me. Why can’t I feel happy? Why does everything feel so numb and pointless when I’m doing all the things I’m “supposed” to do?

The worst part is how much I want something as simple as a hug. From almost anyone. Just to feel held, noticed, cared about. Even thinking that makes me want to cry and makes me feel like a horrible person, like I’m betraying my family somehow just by craving basic comfort.

I don’t know. I just feel so alone, even when I’m not.
