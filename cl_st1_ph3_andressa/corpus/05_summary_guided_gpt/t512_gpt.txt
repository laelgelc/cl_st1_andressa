I live with my family and technically I’m not “alone,” but it feels like I am. The only person I really talk to is my brother, and even with him, it doesn’t feel like that deep, close bond people talk about. We coexist, we talk, but I don’t feel truly known.

I don’t have any friends I can just casually text. I miss that so much – having someone to send random thoughts, memes, or “you won’t believe what happened today” messages to. The few friendships I’ve had felt one‑sided, or like I was just the rebound friend until they found someone “better.” My head is full of thoughts and emotions with nowhere to put them.

What makes it worse is that random strangers will DM me wanting to “vent.” I get that people need someone to listen, but I don’t want to be a temporary dumping ground. I want a best friend who actually knows me, enjoys me, and sticks around – not just someone who uses me as a one-time therapist.

Because I don’t have that, I retreat into my own mind. I talk to myself like I’m my own friend. It makes me feel like a lunatic sometimes, but honestly, it’s all I’ve ever had since I was a kid.

Making friends feels impossibly hard, and I’m so tired of trying and failing. It feels like I only ever had one good year of real connection. I keep thinking I should tell all this to a therapist, but I’ve never had that always‑available texting kind, and I’ve never really connected with any therapist I’ve seen.
