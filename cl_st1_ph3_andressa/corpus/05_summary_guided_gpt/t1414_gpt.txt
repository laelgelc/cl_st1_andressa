If you’re spending the holidays alone this year, it doesn’t automatically mean there’s something wrong with you. It can mean you’ve grown stronger, more independent, and more protective of your peace. It can mean your standards have risen, and you’re no longer willing to settle for just anyone to fill the silence.

Still, don’t isolate yourself completely. Get out there: visit a church or community event, join a social group, try online dating if you feel up to it. Put yourself in spaces where connection is possible—but don’t sacrifice your peace just to say you’re not alone.

Enjoy your own space. Light a candle, cook yourself a nice meal, put on a movie you love. Trust that the right person will come in time. You’re not “too much,” “too picky,” or hard to love. You’re simply loving yourself enough to wait for someone who appreciates your strength.
