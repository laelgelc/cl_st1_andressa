My boyfriend ended things over text about a month ago and I feel like my whole life has collapsed. I think about him constantly. I wake up with this heavy, sick feeling in my chest and go to bed with it. During the day I’m exhausted from pretending to be okay around people, nodding along, making small jokes, acting “functional” when inside I just want to scream or disappear.

Nothing seems to help. I’m in therapy and it feels like I’m just repeating the same pain over and over. I’m so tired of hearing that I’ll “grow from this” or that I’ll “find someone else.” I don’t want to keep forming these deep attachments only to have them ripped away and be told to “move on” like it’s some kind of life lesson. I’m angry that this is what relationships seem to be: get close, lose them, grieve, repeat.

I keep hearing that I should “learn to be happy on my own,” and it honestly just makes me feel worse. I don’t want to be happy alone. I don’t want to fill the gap with pets or hobbies or “self-love” quotes. Friends, family, and hobbies are fine, but they are not the same as having that one person who’s really there with you, long-term, who you build a life with. That’s what I want, and it feels like the universe is just laughing at me for wanting it.

I feel so intensely alone and hopeless about my future. The idea of waking up alone and going to sleep alone every day, indefinitely, makes me feel like there’s no point to anything. I’m angry, I’m sad, and I’m so tired. It feels like everyone else is saying “you’ll be okay” while I’m stuck in this dark, empty place that I can’t see a way out of.
