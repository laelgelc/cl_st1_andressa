I don’t really have close friends. There’s this one guy I’ve known for years, he’s very extroverted and has his own big social circle. We meet up a few times a year, and I enjoy it, but I don’t feel like I’m special to him. I’m just one more person he sees sometimes.

We recently celebrated my birthday together and afterward I had this sudden thought that I’m wasting my youth. I keep waiting for something to change without doing anything. So I pushed myself and went to a party alone.

Honestly, it felt lame and awkward. I just stood around, mostly staring at my phone, pretending I was busy. No one was rude or anything, it was just… nothing. I don’t even really blame the party. I blame myself for not knowing how to talk to people or join in. Now I’m a bit drunk and overthinking everything.

I don’t know if I should just accept that this is how my life is going to be, with one semi-friend and a lot of loneliness, or if there’s actually a way out of this. Has anyone else been in a similar place and managed to change it? How did you go from being the awkward person on their phone to someone who actually connects with people?
