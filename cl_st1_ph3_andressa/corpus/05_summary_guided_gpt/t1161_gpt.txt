I’m 22F and it feels like being left out has been the theme of my entire life.

As a kid I was the one not invited to birthday parties, the one walking around the playground alone at recess. I always assumed it would get better when I grew up, that I’d “find my people,” but here I am, an adult, and it’s still happening.

Recently my girlfriend of three years and I broke up. We still live together and share the same friend group, which is already awkward enough. But even before the breakup, I kept finding out about plans after the fact. They’d go out, have these fun nights, and I’d see it on social media or hear about it later.

The thing that really stung was New Year’s. I wasn’t invited to a party that everyone else went to, even though it’s something I’ve wanted to do since I turned 21. I just kind of assumed I was included because I’d been there in past years, and when I said something, I was told I “shouldn’t have assumed.” That hurt way more than I expected.

This keeps happening with other outings too, like clubbing or random hangouts. I get told I’m not annoying, that they like me, that I’m part of the group… but their actions don’t match their words at all. It makes me feel like there’s something obviously wrong with me that everyone else can see but I can’t. Sometimes I wonder if they’re almost doing it on purpose.

Honestly, it feels like it would be easier to have no friends than to constantly deal with this FOMO and rejection.

Has anyone else gone through this? How do you cope with being left out over and over?
