I feel like I’m constantly walking this line between “opening up to friends” and “oversharing because I’m desperate for connection,” and I honestly don’t know which side I’m on anymore.

I have a lot of people I’d call acquaintances. People I can chat with, hang out with occasionally, send memes to. But I don’t have that solid, close friend group where I feel like I’m anyone’s first choice. The few people who do check in on me seem to have their own circles they default to for holidays, trips, and special events. I end up being the extra, the afterthought, or not invited at all.

A recent example that really hit me: I wanted to go to this Renaissance festival so badly. It felt like exactly my thing – I love fantasy (LOTR, The Hobbit, Harry Potter, Game of Thrones, all of it) and I’ve been wanting to bring that into my real life more. I tried to make plans with some friends, but everything just kind of fizzled out. No one followed up, no one seemed excited, and I didn’t feel like I could push it without being “too much.” Now the tickets are sold out, so I can’t even go alone.

I’ve also been wanting to find or start a DnD group, but every group I’ve been part of either fell apart because people moved away or because of drama. It just reinforces this feeling that I’m not meant to have a stable group.

I feel so alone that sometimes I genuinely wish I were dead, because it feels easier than constantly sitting with this emptiness and rejection. I don’t really know what to do with any of this.
