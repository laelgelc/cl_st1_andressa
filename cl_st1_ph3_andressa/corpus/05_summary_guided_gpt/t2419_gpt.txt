Sometimes I feel like people genuinely believe loneliness is some kind of moral punishment.

You hear it all the time, directly or indirectly: “If you’re lonely, you must have done something wrong.” “Just work on yourself and it’ll fix itself.” As if life is this neat little equation where good people automatically get love and bad people automatically get abandoned.

What bothers me is how society can recognize one kind of victim – like victims of abuse – but when it comes to people who are just quietly isolated, it suddenly turns into, “Well, they must be weird, creepy, or broken somehow.” There’s this total refusal to consider that there might be “lonely saints” out there: decent people who just… slipped through the cracks.

I once had an online friend say, very confidently, that all lonely men are bastards who deserve it. When I pointed out that I’m lonely, they quickly backpedaled: “Oh, not you, you’re different.” But they still held onto the idea that everyone else out there in the same situation as me must somehow deserve their pain.

It’s like people are comfortable condemning “lonely people” in the abstract, but if you put an actual face and story in front of them, suddenly it’s more complicated. I can’t help thinking they cling to this karma-like narrative because the alternative – that the world is unfair and chaotic, and that good people can suffer for no reason – is too disturbing for them to sit with.
