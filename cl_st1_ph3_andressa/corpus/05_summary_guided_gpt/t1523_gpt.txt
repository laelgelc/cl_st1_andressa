I’m about to do something that makes me really happy and weirdly ashamed at the same time: I’m buying a 90 m² apartment near the sea for €200,000.

Where I live in Europe, that’s actually a good and affordable deal, especially compared to city prices. I’ve run the numbers over and over, and I can manage the mortgage on my own with my job. No parents helping, no partner, no second income. Just me. I’m actually kind of proud of that part.

But everyone around me seems fixated on one thing: “Why do you need that much space if you’re alone?”  
“Isn’t that too big for a single person?”  
“Wait until you have a partner or kids, then it makes sense.”

It’s like they can’t accept that a single person might just… want to live comfortably. I feel like I’m being subtly punished for not having a partner or a family. As if being alone should automatically mean I belong in some tiny shoebox, barely bigger than a bedroom, while I wait for my life to “really start.”

The truth is, if I had more money, I’d happily buy something even bigger. Not to show off, but because I like space, I like being near the sea, and I want a home that feels like a refuge, not a compromise. I’m already lonely enough; why should I also be cramped and uncomfortable at home just to fit other people’s expectations?

There’s this unspoken rule that comfort and “nice things” are reserved for couples and families, and singles should make do with less. It makes me feel like my life is seen as temporary or incomplete, and anything I do for myself is “too much” until someone else validates it.

I wish people could just say, “Good for you,” instead of making me feel like I’m overreaching just because I’m doing it alone.
