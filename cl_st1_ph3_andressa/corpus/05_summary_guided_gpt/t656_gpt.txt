I’m a first-year college student, living away from home for the first time. Before the holidays, I was really looking forward to going back. In my head, home was still this safe, warm place where I could reset and feel loved.

But when I got there, it was nothing like I imagined. Almost immediately my parents started asking why I was staying so long, and making comments about how it was a “waste” of rent since I’d already paid for my room in the city. It felt like they were keeping a mental calculator instead of being happy I was there. I ended up cutting my visit short because I just felt so unwanted.

Later, I tried to be “practical” and said maybe I’d just come home in the summer. But again, they focused on money — saying there was no point in coming for just a month, that the rent and travel costs made it a bad idea, that I should just stay there and eat at the mess. I do understand the financial logic. It’s not like I’m blind to that. But emotionally, it really hurt. It felt like they were basically saying I’m not worth the expense.

Home and my parents used to be my main source of comfort, and now I feel like I’ve lost that. I’m in a new city, don’t really have friends yet, and I feel completely broken and alone.

Has anyone else gone through something like this, where home suddenly doesn’t feel like home anymore? How did you cope with that feeling of being unwanted in the one place that was supposed to be your safe space?
