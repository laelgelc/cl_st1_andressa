I’m 30 and I honestly don’t understand what happened to me or to people in general.

In high school and university I made friends so easily, both in real life and online. Some of those online friendships were really deep – years of talking, supporting each other, being there through bad times. Back then I thought that if you showed up for people, loved them, listened, cared… that meant something. That it would matter in the long run.

Now I have no friends. Not “few,” literally none. A lot of those old friendships just ended, often without some big fight or obvious reason. People drifted, ghosted, just… vanished. I know, rationally, that not all of it was my fault, but it still feels like I was thrown away. Like everything I gave was meaningless.

I live with my parents and I’m trying to open a coffee shop at home. On paper, my life isn’t horrible. I know I have some privilege and I’m not in the worst possible situation. But inside, something feels broken. I’m in constant emotional pain, like this dull ache that never stops.

What really messes with my head is how easy it seems for people to discard long-term connections and walk away without any consequences. Years of friendship, shared secrets, late-night talks – and then nothing. Just silence. They go on with their lives, seemingly fine, while I’m left wrecked.

It feels like in this world, you can hurt people deeply and stay completely unscathed, but if you’re the loving, caring one, you’re the one who pays the emotional price. I don’t know what to do with that realization.
