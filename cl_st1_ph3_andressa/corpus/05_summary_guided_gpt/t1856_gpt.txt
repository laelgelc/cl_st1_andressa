Sometimes I feel like I got born into the wrong “age group,” like my soul is 20–30 years older than my actual body. People my age are into things I just don’t connect with, and I end up feeling like an alien sitting in the middle of a crowd.

My interests are really specific and intense. I can spend hours reading about aviation, history, maritime disasters, obscure old infrastructure… right now I’m hyperfixated on Sutro Baths. I’ll deep dive into old photos, maps, stories, structural details, all of it. That kind of thing lights my brain up in a way that small talk or typical “young person” activities just don’t.

I also like moving through life slowly. Walking by the ocean, just listening to the water and thinking, is my idea of a good time. I like long, reflective conversations, or even just comfortable silence. But most people my age seem to want constant stimulation, fast-paced plans, and surface-level chatter. I end up sitting there, feeling boring or “too much” at the same time.

I’ve tried to find people online who share my interests or mindset. At first it feels promising, but it almost always turns into me being the one who initiates everything—starting conversations, asking questions, keeping it alive. Eventually it just fizzles out, and I’m left wondering if I was ever really wanted there, or just tolerated.

I’m trying to accept that I’m “wired differently,” that it’s not something inherently wrong with me. But it still hurts that I rarely feel truly understood, and that I can’t seem to build connections that last. The loneliness feels heavier when it’s not just being alone, but being surrounded by people and still feeling like no one really sees me.
