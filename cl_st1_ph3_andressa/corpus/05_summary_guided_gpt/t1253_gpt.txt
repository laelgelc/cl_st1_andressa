There’s a phrase from an Argentine comic that stuck with me: “nobody saves themselves.” For a long time I thought it meant I just had to wait for someone to come and rescue me. So I built this emotional cocoon to protect myself from pain, fear, and people. It felt safe at first, but it slowly turned into a prison of loneliness.

Years went by and nobody came. That’s when it hit me: the phrase is incomplete. Nobody saves themselves, but no one will do it for you either. I do need help, I do need people, but they can’t walk for me.

So now I’m trying to accept that if I want to leave this lonely hell, I have to take the first step. My loved ones can walk beside me, but they can’t carry me out.
