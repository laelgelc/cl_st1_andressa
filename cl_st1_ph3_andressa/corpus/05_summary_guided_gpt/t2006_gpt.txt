I’m 32, living in the UK, and I’ve basically been on my own for a bit over two years now. The loneliness has really hit hard over the last year. On paper, things look “good”: I’m financially stable, I own my home and my car, I don’t have any debt, and I work my ass off. But lately it just feels… pointless.

All the stuff I’ve managed to achieve doesn’t feel like it means anything when there’s nobody to share it with. I come home to an empty place every day, and it’s like the silence is getting louder. There’s this constant, painful emptiness in my chest that I can’t shake, no matter how much I try to distract myself.

I’ve tried to meet people in real life, and I’ve tried dating apps, but most of the “connections” I’ve made have ended up with people asking me for money. That’s happened enough times now that it’s made me really distrustful. It’s hard not to feel like people just see me as a walking wallet rather than an actual person.

I want a family. I want a partner. I want someone I can build a life with and share the small, everyday things with. But the more time passes, the more it feels like it’s never going to happen, and my thoughts are starting to go down a pretty dark path.

Does anyone else feel like this? Like you’ve done everything “right” but it still feels empty because you’re doing it all alone?
