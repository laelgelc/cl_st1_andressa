I used to think I understood loneliness. For years it was about not having a boyfriend, and only having a couple of people I could really call true friends. I kept telling myself that if I could just find someone who loved me, someone who wanted me, that empty feeling would finally go away. I thought a relationship would fill that void and somehow fix all the parts of me that felt unwanted.

Nine months ago my mom died, and now I realize I had no idea what loneliness really was.

What I feel now is so much deeper and heavier. It’s like a part of me died with her, and there’s this permanent hole in my chest that nothing can reach. It isn’t just being alone anymore, it’s this constant sense of being hollowed out from the inside. I feel emotionally detached from people, from the world, even from myself. I go through the motions of life, but it’s like I’m watching someone else do it.

There are moments when I’m scared that if anything else bad happened, my heart just wouldn’t be able to take it. It already feels stretched to its limit, like one more loss or disappointment would just break me completely.

I don’t really know why I’m posting this except that I needed somewhere to put these feelings, somewhere to vent without having to pretend I’m fine. I guess I’m “okay but a little lost,” if that makes any sense.

Please don’t message me or try to “fix” me. I’m not trying to be rude, I just… feel like this is something I have to walk through on my own if I’m ever going to heal, even a little.
