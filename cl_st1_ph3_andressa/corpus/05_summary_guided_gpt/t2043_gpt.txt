I’m turning 38 soon and I don’t have any plans. No dinner, no get-together, nothing. I keep telling myself it’s “just another day,” but it hurts more than I want to admit. What makes it feel even worse is that I’m technically not alone – I’m in a relationship – and yet I feel incredibly lonely.

My partner is the only person I could even call a “friend,” but they don’t really know me. They forget basic things about me, my likes, my history, the little details that make me feel seen. I find myself craving someone who remembers my favorite coffee order, who knows I’d happily spend a whole weekend doing a Harry Potter marathon, who would go horseback riding with me or say yes to a random road trip just because.

I used to have friends like that. Then life happened. Depression, an abusive relationship, a psychotic break, and the constant demands of being a single mom to two kids. One by one, my friendships faded away. I didn’t have the energy, the time, or the mental space to keep up with anyone. Survival came first.

Now my kids are older and building their own lives and friendships. I’m proud of them, but it also highlights how empty my own social world is. I’m scared of what the future will look like if I keep going like this – just me, my partner who doesn’t really “get” me, and this huge hollow feeling.

I do want real connection again. I miss laughing over coffee, sharing quiet, cozy evenings, feeling known. But I’m also terrified to open up after everything that’s happened. It feels like it might be time to try, I just honestly don’t know where to start anymore.
