I don’t really know what I’m doing with this, but I guess I just need to put it somewhere.

Lately I’ve been feeling really alone and kind of directionless. Most of the people I used to call friends are basically gone from my life now. I can’t even blame it all on them – I know I played a part in it. Choices I made, ways I acted, things I didn’t say when I should have. It’s like I watched my own social life slowly fall apart and didn’t stop it.

I’ve been trying so hard to “stay positive,” to keep it all bottled up and just push through, but it’s exhausting. On the rare occasions when I try to open up to someone, it feels like they just… don’t get it. Either they change the subject, try to fix it in two sentences, or tell me to “look on the bright side.” I end up feeling stupid for even saying anything.

I keep circling back to this feeling that I haven’t really been of value to anyone for a long time. Not in a dramatic way, just this quiet sense that if I disappeared from their lives, nothing would really change. Sometimes I don’t even want advice or solutions. I just want someone to actually listen, or to sit next to me and let me lean on them, or give me a hug. Physical comfort like that feels like something from another life at this point.

I keep telling myself I’m on a path to recovery, and I do believe that, but it hurts knowing that I’ll probably get better without those old friends ever seeing me happy, or being there for the bad days on the way. There’s a weird grief in that.

I’m not asking for sympathy. I think I just needed to say this out loud somewhere, to someone, even if you’re strangers on the internet. If you read this, thanks for listening.
