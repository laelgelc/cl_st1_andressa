I remember one night looking at my phone and realizing I hadn’t gotten a meaningful message in… I don’t even know how long. Just random memes, work stuff, and the occasional “how are you?” that never went beyond small talk. It hit me that I hadn’t had a real conversation in weeks.

My days had all blurred into the same pattern: wake up, go to work, exchange a few surface-level comments with coworkers, come home, scroll on my phone, sleep. Repeat. I told myself I was “too tired” or “too busy” to do anything else, but really I was just stuck. My old friends are far away now, and we only talk every once in a while. I kept waiting for something to change on its own, like one day I’d just magically feel less alone.

Instead, everything just felt empty and stale. I’d catch myself doomscrolling, not because I cared about what I was looking at, but because there was nothing else going on. It scared me how normal that started to feel.

At some point I realized that if I didn’t do anything, this would just keep being my life. So I made a decision: stop waiting for destiny to hand me a social life and start putting myself in situations where one could actually exist. I didn’t force conversations or chase people, but I did show up more. I gave myself chances to meet others instead of hiding behind the same routines.

It wasn’t instant, but slowly my phone stopped being just a black hole of scrolling. It started to be something that buzzed with actual connection.

If loneliness is bothering you, don’t let isolation become your comfort zone. Nothing changes if nothing changes. Even small steps to put yourself out there can open the door to something better.
