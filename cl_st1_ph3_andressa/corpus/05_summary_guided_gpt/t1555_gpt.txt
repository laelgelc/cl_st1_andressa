I’m almost 40, never been in a relationship, never even had anything that comes close. My mom died when I was 3, so I grew up pretty much learning early that people can just…disappear, and life keeps going whether you like it or not.

I don’t go to bars or clubs, I don’t hit on coworkers, and I rarely go to meetups because they’re usually inconvenient or feel like a chore. I’m not socially terrified or anything, I just don’t care enough to push myself into those spaces. It feels like people treat companionship like some kind of human right, but I don’t think society owes anyone a partner or a friend. I’ve made it this far without close relationships; that kind of proves I don’t “need” anyone to function.

I see all the stuff online about declining birth rates, male loneliness, “epidemics” of isolation. Honestly, part of me just thinks: maybe this is how things are supposed to be now. Not everything has to be fixed. Maybe some of us are just going to live and die mostly on our own.

I’m not miserable. I still like things: seeing new places, eating good food, traveling when I can, playing video games. Those experiences are enough to fill the days. I just feel…detached from other people. Like they’re living a different kind of life that I was never really meant to be part of.
