I just wanted to share something that might be useful for anyone here who has an older relative, neighbour, or friend who’s feeling really lonely or isolated.

There’s a UK charity called Re-engage that runs a free “call companion” service for people aged 75 and over. They match older people with a volunteer phone buddy who calls them regularly for a friendly chat. It’s not counselling or anything formal, just someone reliable to talk to so they don’t feel so alone.

The volunteers are all DBS-checked and trained, which I know can be reassuring if you’re helping an older person sign up. You can refer someone (with their consent) or they can sign themselves up through an online form.

If you know someone who might benefit, please think about mentioning it to them and helping them with the form if they’re not confident online. Ellie, who shared this, also said people can email her directly if they have questions or need support with the process.
