I just got out of a 4.5-year relationship. For most of it, I did feel loved and cared for, but toward the end I realized I was the only one ever initiating physical intimacy. It started to feel like I was more of a roommate than a partner, and that’s been messing with my head a lot now that I’m single again.

Being out of the relationship has made me really rethink how important it is for me to feel desired and wanted physically. I’m realizing that’s actually a huge need for me in a relationship, and I feel kind of ashamed admitting that, like it makes me shallow or needy. But I can’t deny how much it hurts to feel unwanted.

During the relationship, I definitely “let myself go” by my own standards. Now I’m working really hard to lose weight and feel attractive again, but in the meantime I just feel uncomfortable in my body. I feel unworthy of love and intimacy, like no one could genuinely want me as I am right now. The loneliness that comes with that belief is… a lot.

What makes it worse is a past experience where a guy told me I shouldn’t use dating apps until I hit a certain weight. And the painful part is that when I did lose a lot of weight later, I actually had way more success on the apps. So a part of me really believes I *have* to change my body to be desired.

I know this mindset isn’t healthy and I do want to work on it, but right now I’m just frustrated and sad and tired. If anyone else is dealing with similar feelings around their body, being desired, and loneliness… I see you. You’re not alone.
