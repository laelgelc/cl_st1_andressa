I’m 24F and I feel like I’ve spent my whole life on the outside looking in. Even as a kid I never really fit with people my own age, and I always ended up gravitating toward the other “weird” or outsider kids. In college I finally started to form a few friendships, but then COVID hit and everything just kind of… evaporated. Since then it’s felt like I’ve been starting from zero over and over again.

I have ADHD, probably autism too, and really intense social anxiety, so social stuff already feels like trying to do everything on hard mode. At work I usually get along better with people way older than me, which just makes me feel even more out of place with my peers.

I started a new job about two months ago and I’ve been trying to be friendly, talk about non-work things, show interest in people’s lives, all that. Early on, one coworker mentioned a group chat and said they’d add me. They never did, but I tried not to overthink it.

Then recently that same coworker casually brought up a new group chat they’d made and said everyone had been talking in it, including about another coworker’s recovery from surgery. That’s when it hit me that I still wasn’t in it. Multiple coworkers and both managers have my number, so it’s not like they couldn’t add me.

I already feel so lonely in general, and now I feel lonely at work too. It just really stung to realize they all have this little social bubble I’m not part of, even when I’m trying. I know it’s “just a group chat,” but it makes me feel like there’s something fundamentally wrong with me that keeps me on the outside every time.
