I’ve always been the introverted, emotionally dependent one, but I was never really alone. Even when I was getting bullied in primary school, I still had a small circle of close friends around me. I got used to that – to having “my people,” to knowing that even if the day was awful, there’d be someone to talk to, someone who really knew me.

Now I’ve moved really far away for senior school and it feels like the rug has been pulled out from under me. I know literally no one here. I spend breaks by myself, with my headphones in, hiding in the library or even the toilets just so I don’t have to look like the obvious loner standing around with nowhere to go. It’s such a weird shift, going from always having friends to suddenly being “that person” who’s always alone.

My mental health has never been great, but this has made it so much worse. I swing between crying almost every day and then having these random moments where I feel weirdly free and independent, like I don’t need anyone. But the “independent” feeling never lasts, and when it fades I’m just left drained and empty. The emotional whiplash is exhausting.

The strange part is that in some ways, things are better. My relationship with my family has improved, and I actually enjoy the school itself – the classes, the environment. But it feels like that came at the cost of having any kind of companionship. Like I traded friends for everything else getting slightly better.

I feel so lonely and it genuinely hurts. It’s like this constant ache that I can’t really explain to anyone around me. More than anything, I just want a proper, caring hug from someone who actually cares about me and isn’t just being polite.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping to get out of posting this. I just feel like if I didn’t put it somewhere, if I didn’t say it out loud in some way, I’d just keep bottling it up until I completely fall apart.
