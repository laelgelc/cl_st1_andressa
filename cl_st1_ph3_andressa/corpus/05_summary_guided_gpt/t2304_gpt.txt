I just lost my job and I’m honestly not even sure how to process what happened to me today.

I’m usually pretty depressed and numb, but earlier I ended up pacing around my room for hours, chugging an energy drink, blasting music, and just… daydreaming. I imagined all these social situations with “friends” I don’t actually have. Hanging out, joking around, doing fun stuff together. It felt so vivid that for a while I actually felt happy. Like genuinely happy, socially fulfilled, like I finally belonged somewhere.

Then I started thinking, “Maybe I can turn things around. I’ll go to the gym, improve myself, get my life together.” I imagined those same imaginary friends reacting positively, cheering me on, being proud of me. That fantasy made me feel motivated in a way I almost never do.

And then it hit me: none of those people are real. They don’t exist. That whole little support system in my head is just… me talking to myself. As soon as I remembered that, the motivation just evaporated and I crashed right back down.

The worst part is that even when I do manage to make friends, real interactions almost always feel disappointing compared to the worlds I build in my head. People have told me I should be an author because of how detailed my imagination is, but most of the time it just feels like I’m better at writing fake lives than living my own.
