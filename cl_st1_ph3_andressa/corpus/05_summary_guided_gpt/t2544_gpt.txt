I’m 24 and I feel like I’ve wasted so many years being stuck in my own head. I’ve had depression since middle school, but somehow it feels worse now than ever. I look around and see all these things that seem like they could be fun or meaningful, but I feel completely unable to actually enjoy any of them because I’m lonely, grumpy, and have no friends.

I’m still living with my parents because I’ve never had a friend to move in with. I’ve never traveled because I’m scared to do it alone, especially with my epilepsy. It feels dangerous and overwhelming to imagine something going wrong when I’m by myself in a place I don’t know. My parents are nice, but I don’t want to keep doing everything with them. It just makes me feel even more behind in life.

Lately I’ve started forcing myself to do things alone, like going to the cinema, but I just feel empty and embarrassed sitting there by myself. When I go to social events alone, I feel like crying from the moment I arrive. That feeling makes me shut down and get grumpy, which obviously doesn’t help me make any friends.

I can list things I think I’d enjoy—ice skating, traveling, cooking classes—but the idea of doing them alone just feels pointless. I imagine myself there, going through the motions, and I just see myself coming home feeling even more empty than before.
