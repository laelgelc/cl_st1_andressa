I’m 23, still living at home, and I’ve been unemployed for three years since graduating. Every day feels like the same loop: wake up, stay in my room, scroll endlessly, watch random YouTube videos, try to make the hours pass. I’m not doing anything meaningful. I know that. My parents remind me of it constantly.

They don’t scream or anything, but the criticism and judgment is always there. Little comments about how I’m “doing nothing,” how I’m “wasting my life,” how I’m just spending my dad’s money. It feels like every move I make is being watched and silently disapproved of. I eat alone in my room because being around them makes me feel like a failure on display.

I barely go out. Most days I don’t even step outside the house. I sit on my bed with my phone or laptop, scrolling through social media or watching YouTube, not because I enjoy it, but because hearing other people’s voices makes me feel slightly less invisible. It’s like I’m trying to prove to myself that humans exist somewhere, even if I’m not really part of their world.

I don’t feel wanted or loved or even really seen here. I feel like a burden, like an extra cost on a bill they resent paying. At night it hits the hardest. I lie there and cry quietly because it hurts so much to exist in a place where I feel like I don’t matter at all. I’m not “enjoying my own company” or using this time to “find myself.” I’m just surviving. Getting through one empty day so I can do it again tomorrow.

I’m not asking for advice. I honestly don’t have the energy for it right now. I just want to know if anyone else feels this specific kind of heavy loneliness and judgment at home. That feeling of being trapped in your own house, in your own room, in your own head. If you relate to any of this, even a simple “me too” would mean a lot. It would make me feel a little less alone in all of this.
