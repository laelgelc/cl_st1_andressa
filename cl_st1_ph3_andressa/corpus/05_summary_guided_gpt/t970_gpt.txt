Being a new hire feels like one long humiliation ritual to me.

Every shift, it’s like I’m under a microscope. I work with animals, and I actually care a lot and pay attention. I’m observant, I’m intuitive, and I try to do things thoughtfully. But it doesn’t seem to matter. I get micromanaged over the tiniest things: how I latch a gate, how I give dogs their playtime, the way I wrap a hose, how I close a kennel door. It feels like nothing I do is ever just “fine” or “good enough” — there’s always some new tiny correction, some new lecture.

I know there are reasons for rules and safety and consistency, and I’m not against that. It’s just the way it’s delivered. Every interaction with a manager feels like being scolded or talked down to, like they’re waiting for me to mess up so they can swoop in and “fix” me. They take everything so seriously that it feels suffocating. There’s no sense of, “You’ll get the hang of it, it’s okay.” It’s just constant scrutiny.

I’m also completely exhausted. My shift changed recently, and ever since then I can’t sleep properly. Insomnia has just wrecked me. I’m running on fumes, and my tolerance for being nitpicked is basically gone. When I’m that tired, even a small correction feels like a personal attack, and I hate that it gets to me so much. I’m not trying to be defensive; I’m just worn down.

I’ve started trying to avoid being seen by the managers because it feels like every time they look in my direction, they find something wrong. I’ll literally alter where I walk or how long I stay in a certain area just to minimize contact. It’s like I’m always bracing myself for the next “You should do it this way instead.”

It makes me feel small and stupid, like I can’t be trusted to do anything on my own, even though I know I’m not an idiot. I just feel really alone in this and tired of feeling like I’m constantly being judged and corrected for every tiny thing.
