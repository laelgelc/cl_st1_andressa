I’ve always been really comfortable being alone, and for a long time I thought that meant there was something wrong with me.

Everyone around me seems to crave company, to feel energized by hanging out, texting, calling, making plans. Meanwhile, when my friends reach out – people I genuinely like and care about – my first reaction is anxiety. I start worrying about how to “socialize correctly,” what to say, how long I’m supposed to hang out, whether I’ll come off as weird or distant. Even if I do meet up, I usually end up feeling overwhelmed pretty quickly and just want to go home and be by myself again.

It’s confusing because it’s not that I hate people or that I don’t want friends. I do. I just feel this huge sense of relief when plans get cancelled or when a weekend passes and no one expects anything from me.

The COVID lockdown really highlighted this. While a lot of people were struggling with isolation, I felt… relieved. Staying home, not having to contact anyone, not feeling pressured to “keep up” socially – it was like I could finally breathe. That made me wonder if this is something more extreme than just being introverted.

Finding this community has been a weird mix of comforting and emotional. Reading posts from people who feel the same way makes me feel understood in a way I honestly didn’t expect. For once I don’t feel like a broken friend or a bad person for needing so much space.
