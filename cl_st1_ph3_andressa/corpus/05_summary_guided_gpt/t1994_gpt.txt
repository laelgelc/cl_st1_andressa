I’m 23, in uni and working, and I feel like I’m living in a completely different world to everyone around me. Most people my age seem to be into partying, drinking, smoking, that whole scene, and I just can’t relate. I end up feeling like the odd one out in every room.

My childhood was horrendous and I joined the RAF at 18 mainly to get away from sexual abuse at home. I ended up in Afghanistan, doing aid work. It was obviously a horrible situation in a lot of ways, but I weirdly felt more connected and useful there than I do now. There was a clear purpose, and people needed each other. Now I’m back, at university, and I feel completely alone.

People here come to me for advice and see me as this “wise” person, but it’s like I’m a character in their lives, not someone they actually know. I listen to everyone else, but no one really knows me or where I’ve come from. I have one best friend, an ex-Afghan soldier, and I care about him a lot, but the language, culture, and gender differences make that friendship complicated and sometimes exhausting.

On paper I’m “normal” – I like football, the gym, investing – but my past has messed with how I see everything. I just don’t know how to build real connections with people who haven’t seen or lived any of this. I feel like I’m here, but not really part of anything.
