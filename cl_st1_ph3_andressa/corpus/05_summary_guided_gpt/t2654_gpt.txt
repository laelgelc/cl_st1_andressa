It really bothers me how hypocritical society is about socializing. People constantly tell loners to “get out there,” “be more social,” “make friends.” But when you actually try, you’re judged or quietly pushed aside if you don’t fit the exact mold of what they consider “normal.”

It’s like there’s this unspoken rule: “Be social, but only if you think like us, act like us, and like what we like.” If you have different values, beliefs, or interests, suddenly you’re “weird,” “awkward,” or just not worth including.

To me, it just proves that a lot of this talk about caring and connection is really about conformity. Even people who seem kind still tend to gravitate toward their own kind and shut out anyone who doesn’t match. It makes “just be yourself” feel like a lie.
