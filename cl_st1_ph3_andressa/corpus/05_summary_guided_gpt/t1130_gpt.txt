I came across this article about loneliness and thought it was worth sharing and talking about.

One of the ideas that really stuck with me was that loneliness isn’t just some accident that happens later in life because of things like loss, moving, or relationships ending. The author says it’s actually one of the earliest facts of life, something that’s been there from the beginning, even if we only really notice it when things fall apart.

Another idea that hit me was this: if you “walk into” your loneliness and really face it, it can start to dissolve. Not by finding better company or fixing your social life, but by revealing more of who you are underneath all of that.

Has anyone here experienced that? Does this way of looking at loneliness resonate with you at all?
