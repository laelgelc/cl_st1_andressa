I’m 22 and I feel like my world has shrunk down to the walls of my house and a couple of screens.

I have panic-induced agoraphobia, so I barely leave. On a “good” week, my only in-person interaction is a quick, awkward exchange at the liquor store. I work an online job that doesn’t pay much and I know I need to find something else, but even the thought of change or interviews makes my chest tighten. I dropped out of high school early, and that just adds to this feeling that I’m stuck at the bottom.

I used to think I was just shy or introverted, but it’s turned into something worse over time. The last time I tried to go to a movie, I had a full-blown panic attack in an almost empty theater. Now I keep having nightmares about being trapped there, unable to breathe or get out. Since then, going anywhere feels impossible.

I haven’t talked to a friend in about a year. I only interact with strangers online now. I miss the most basic things—saying goodnight to someone, asking how they slept, having someone to ask me how my day was. I go to bed alone every night and it feels like this heavy empty space next to me is swallowing me whole. I crave physical affection so badly, just a hug or someone’s hand in mine, and I can’t even remember the last time I had that.

I smoke constantly, drink more than I can afford, and it all just makes me feel worse and more alone. Sometimes I wonder if having someone to come home to is just a myth people tell.

I made this account just to post here after realizing there are others who feel this alone too.
