I don’t understand what “we should hang out” is supposed to mean anymore.

It keeps happening: people say they miss me, or that we should catch up, or “we should totally hang out sometime,” but the second I actually try to make a plan, everything gets weird. Suddenly it’s “oh ummm” and “lol” and awkward pauses, like I’m the one being strange for taking their words at face value.

Most recently an old friend reached out to me, completely unprompted, saying they missed me and wanted to hang out. It actually made me kind of happy and hopeful. So I did what I thought you’re supposed to do: I suggested a specific day. And immediately the vibe shifted. They replied with that awkward “oh ummm” and “lol,” and it felt like they were almost annoyed or thrown off that I treated it seriously instead of as some vague, empty phrase.

This isn’t just a one‑time thing either. It’s a pattern. Over and over, people say they want to see me, but when I try to follow through, they back off, act distant, or seem irritated. And I’m left feeling stupid and embarrassed, like I misread everything.

It honestly makes me feel really lonely and isolated. I start wondering what I’m doing wrong just by… believing people? I’m getting to the point where I feel like giving up on trying to make friends, because I don’t understand these unspoken rules. I don’t know how to tell when “we should hang out” is real and when it’s just something people say to be polite. And I’m tired of feeling like a burden just for wanting to actually see someone.
