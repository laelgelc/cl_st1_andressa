When I was younger, being alone felt heavy and sad, but it was still something I could sort of control. I thought that was loneliness.

Then I was widowed at 39. No friends. No family nearby. No social media. That’s when I learned what a different, brutal level of aloneness really is. It’s not just quiet evenings or empty weekends; it’s a constant, uncontrollable emptiness that you can’t bargain with.

If you’re alone now, but still have your health, time, and options, please try to see the strange gift in that. You have a clean slate. You have agency. What feels like sad loneliness today might actually be a kind of “wicked cool” independence compared to what can come after everything shatters.
