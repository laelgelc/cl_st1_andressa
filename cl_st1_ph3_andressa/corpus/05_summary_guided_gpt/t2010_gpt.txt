Finished a big exam today and instead of feeling relieved, I just felt… empty. Walking back to my dorm, it hit me that my “reward” for getting through it was just going back to the same routine: sit in my room, play video games alone, maybe go to the gym, repeat. No one to text, no plans, nothing to look forward to except the next assignment.

I keep thinking about how different things were before. I had a solid friend group in college until I got into a relationship that I honestly regret now. That whole situation kind of blew everything up, and since then I’ve just been drifting. I still have family and friends back home and I see them during breaks, but it’s not the same as having people *here* who want to spend time with me.

I’ve tried to fix it. I joined some volunteering groups, but nothing stuck. My day-to-day life is basically university and gym, and that’s it. People don’t seem to dislike me – I feel like I’m generally liked or at least tolerated – but it never goes beyond that. I’m not the person anyone thinks of when they’re making plans. I’m never the one who gets invited out.

I wish I had the confidence to invite people myself, but I’m so insecure about it that I usually just freeze. For a while I blamed my looks, but objectively I know I’m at least above average, and it clearly hasn’t helped me form any real connection. So I start wondering if it’s my personality, but I’m pretty introspective and I still don’t know what exactly is “wrong” with me.

I just miss simple things – going bowling, walking around the city, going out for no reason other than to be with people. More than anything, I just want to be chosen, to be actively wanted. Writing this out feels a tiny bit better than just sitting with it in my head.
