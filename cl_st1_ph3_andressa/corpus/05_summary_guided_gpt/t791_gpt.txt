I used to be what I’d call a happy loner. I had friends, I could hang out when I wanted, then retreat and recharge by myself. Being alone felt like a choice, and it actually gave me energy.

Now I’m 36, divorced, and somehow I’ve ended up with no friends at all. It’s not just “a quiet phase” anymore. I can go literal months without speaking to anyone in person, outside of basic transactions. Most of my time is just me, a cigarette, some music, YouTube, anime. I’ve gotten really into solo vloggers because watching someone else go through their day makes me feel a tiny bit less like a ghost.

But underneath it all I feel completely hopeless about changing this. The idea of making new friends or having any kind of romantic relationship feels almost impossible. I worry I’ve forgotten how to socialize, like whatever muscle I used to have just atrophied. When I do have the chance to talk to someone, I feel weirdly out of practice and self-conscious.

I know some people choose to be alone and genuinely prefer it, and I respect that. And then there are people like me who ended up here without meaning to. To both groups: how do you cope with the silence? How do you fill the empty space where friendships and relationships would normally be?

What do you actually do with your days so it doesn’t just feel like time passing in front of a screen?
