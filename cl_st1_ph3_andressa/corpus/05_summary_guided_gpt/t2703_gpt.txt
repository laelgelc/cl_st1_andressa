I’ve realized more and more that being a loner isn’t just something I “decided” one day. It’s woven into who I am and how I’ve learned to cope with life. 

As I’ve gotten older, I’ve reached this weirdly peaceful place with my own company. I don’t feel the same pressure to force connections or pretend I’m more social than I am. There’s something kind of beautiful about just living my truth, even if it doesn’t match what people expect.

Part of it is also knowing that not everyone has good intentions, and a lot of people turn out to be temporary anyway. That used to hurt more than it does now.

I’m curious if anyone else feels this way. How did you come to terms with your own solitude or “loner” side?
