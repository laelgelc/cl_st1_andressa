26M here. Not long ago I went through a breakup that hit me harder than I expected. My ex slept with someone else, and that was the moment everything snapped into focus. I realized I wasn’t really in love with *her* – I was in love with the idea of her, of us, of the future I had built in my head.

After it happened, I still tried to fix things. I told myself that if I just worked harder, or forgave more, we could make it. But all those attempts just showed me how one‑sided everything was. We didn’t want the same things. I pictured simple family dinners, building a home together, enjoying walks, cheap dates, just existing together without needing constant excitement or money. She didn’t care about that. The more I looked back, the more it felt like she never really loved me, and maybe was just using me for what I could give.

The weird part is that this whole mess taught me something I should’ve learned a long time ago: being alone isn’t the enemy. My real mistake was being afraid of loneliness, clinging to someone just so I didn’t have to face myself.

Now I’m trying to accept being alone instead of running from it. I’m focusing on making friends, going out more – parks, clubs, parties, whatever gets me around people without turning it into some desperate search for a partner. When you do that, you stop being a slave to this idea of “love at any cost.”

You don’t need a relationship to learn about life. Friends can teach you a lot. Listening to other people’s stories can teach you a lot. You don’t have to grab the first “love” that shows up just because you’re scared of being on your own. Wait for something healthy, something that actually matches who you are and what you want.

And honestly, I’d rather be happy and kind than waste my time chasing this “alpha” image everyone online keeps selling.
