I’m 26F and lately I’ve been feeling this really heavy kind of loneliness that I don’t know what to do with.

I technically have friends, but none of them feel close. We don’t really share our lives or lean on each other. Most of my free time is spent alone. On Mondays when coworkers ask “How was your weekend?” I usually lie and say I was out or busy, because I’m embarrassed to admit I mostly just stayed home or did things by myself.

I do try to be proactive. I invite people out, suggest plans, try to keep in touch. But so often it ends with people cancelling, rejecting the invite, or just… forgetting. I tell myself it’s because we have different lifestyles and interests, but it still hurts and makes me wonder if there’s something wrong with me.

Personality-wise I’m introverted, but also welcoming, kind, soft-spoken, bubbly, and a bit quirky. I can start conversations easily and I don’t think I’m unpleasant to be around. I actually enjoy doing things alone—shopping, going to cafés, going to the cinema—but part of that is because there just isn’t anyone to go with. I don’t have a partner either, which adds to the loneliness.

Dating has been rough. I’m honest and open, and men say they like that, but their actions never seem to match their words. On my last date, the guy spent most of the time on his phone. I felt ignored and empty sitting across from him. He told me he was interested and wanted to meet again, then just stopped replying afterward.

Sometimes I feel misunderstood. I think my bubbly side makes people assume I’m stupid or shallow. I actually think I’m pretty and good at conversation, so I’m really confused about what I’m doing wrong. I’m trying to accept the idea that I might be alone for life, but that thought is incredibly painful.
