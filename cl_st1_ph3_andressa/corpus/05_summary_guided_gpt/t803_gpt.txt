I’m 22 and it feels like I’ve been completely invisible romantically my whole life. I’ve never had a girlfriend, never kissed anyone, never even been on a real date. The most “romantic” contact I’ve had was holding a girl’s hand recently, and even that felt like some sort of miracle.

What really messes with my head is that I’m not a complete recluse. I’m sociable, I go out, I have friends, and I don’t think I’m ugly. But for more than four years now, it’s like there’s this wall between me and any kind of real romantic connection. Women always seem to put me into this “gentleman” box – like I’m safe, polite, nice to talk to, but never someone they’d actually be attracted to.

I’ve tried changing how I act a bit, without becoming someone I’m not, but nothing changes. I stay stuck in this role. Meanwhile, I’ve got a lot of other problems in my life, and I just keep wishing I had someone to share it all with – to give love to and to feel loved by. I want a girlfriend not just for the physical stuff, but for the emotional connection.

People sometimes suggest escorts, but that’s not what I want at all. I don’t want to pay someone to pretend. I want to be accepted and loved for who I am. Lately I’m scared that in today’s world, that might never happen for me.
