First day of college, I saw this girl who honestly felt like something out of a dream. I’d never seen anyone that beautiful in my life. I don’t know what came over me, but I actually walked up to her, made some awkward small talk, and asked her out. She said no, politely, but still—no.

I didn’t stop thinking about her. Years went by, and that feeling never really faded. Two years later, I did something even more ridiculous: I actually proposed to her. No relationship, no buildup, just this stupid, blind hope that maybe she’d see how serious I was.

She said no again.

I’m left wondering what’s wrong with me that I could cling to someone like that for so long, and have nothing to show for it.
