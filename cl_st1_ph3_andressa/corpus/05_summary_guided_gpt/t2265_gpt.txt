I’m sitting here drinking and realizing how much of my life has just… passed by among strangers.

I can sometimes manage to have one or two in-person friends at a time, but only if I see them regularly and they’re the ones who keep things going. I honestly don’t know how to talk to people long term if we’re not physically around each other a lot. Once the regular contact stops, it’s like I forget how to be their friend, or it just slowly fades into nothing.

Since I turned 18, I’ve moved a lot for work. Every time I start over somewhere new, I meet people, but those relationships feel so surface-level. We hang out, we talk, but it never really goes deep. And the deeper connections I used to have are just… gone now. We drifted apart with distance and time, and I never figured out how to stop that from happening.

Now it feels like I only know how to collect acquaintances, not real friends. I’m watching the years go by, surrounded by people I barely know, and I don’t know how to change it. How do you actually maintain real friendships when life keeps moving you around and you’re not naturally good at staying in touch?
