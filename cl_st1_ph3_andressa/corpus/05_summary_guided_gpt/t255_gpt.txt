I realized on the drive back from a camping trip that I haven’t actually been in love for nine years. And even that feels… distant, like a worn-out VHS tape you watched too many times as a kid. I remember that it happened, but not what it *felt* like.

I just spent a few days out in the middle of nowhere with my best friend. We drank, smoked, listened to music, just existed away from everything. I even kind of liked the isolation, and I was really hoping it would snow. It was peaceful in a way I don’t get in my normal life.

But underneath it all, I kept thinking how much I wish I had a woman to share it with. Not in a “settle down, buy a house, start a family” way. I don’t want that, at least not now. I just want someone who loves me, who wants to come on these trips, share my hobbies, sit by the fire and just *be* there.

I’m 23, never been kissed, never held hands. I was on the phone with a friend my age in the exact same situation, and it just made everything feel even more surreal and lonely.
