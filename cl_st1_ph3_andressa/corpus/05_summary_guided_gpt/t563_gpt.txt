I’ve been thinking a lot about how I ended up here, at 25, with no friends and almost no contact with anyone. As a kid in primary school, I had a few friends. Nothing dramatic, just normal childhood connections. Eventually that shrank down to one close companion, the kind of person you assume will always be around. Then secondary school happened, we drifted apart, and now we don’t talk at all. No argument, no clear ending—just silence that never got reversed.

These days, I don’t really reach out to anyone. It’s not that I can’t; I just don’t. My solitude is intentional. Most of the time, it feels valuable, like a space where I can actually breathe. Still, there are moments when loneliness hits in short, sharp bursts. Sometimes I get this urge to talk to someone, to share something, and then it passes, and I’m back to my usual quiet.

Part of the problem is that I find being around people exhausting. They’re too loud, too wrapped up in themselves, not really listening. When people do try to support me, it never feels like it lines up with what I actually need. I also never seem to meet their expectations of how I’m supposed to be, what I’m supposed to want, or how I should act. It’s like we’re all speaking slightly different languages.

I have this sense that I’m basically unnoticed. If I disappeared tomorrow, it wouldn’t really change anything for anyone. That’s not written as self-pity, more as an observation. It’s strange to be alive and yet feel so inconsequential.

I’m writing this less as a cry for help and more as a kind of detached reflection, a small record that I existed and thought about these things—a personal memento mori, I guess.

If you’ve read this far, I genuinely hope life is kind to you, or at least kinder than it has to be.
