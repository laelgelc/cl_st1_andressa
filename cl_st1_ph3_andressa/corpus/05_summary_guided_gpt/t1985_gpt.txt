I’ve been depressed for as long as I can remember, even as a kid. I always felt like the odd one out, like there was something wrong with me that everyone else could see. Now, as an adult, I get really anxious when I have to talk to new people. It’s like my brain freezes and I overthink every word, so I just avoid it when I can.

The weird part is, I finally have people around me that I actually like. But I can’t shake the feeling that they laugh at me behind my back, or that they only keep me around because I’m friendly and helpful and easy to use. I’m scared I’m just some kind of convenient extra to them.

I also feel like I’m outgrowing my current friendships, but I have no idea how to make new friends when my social skills feel so broken. So I’m stuck in this place where I feel lonely even when I’m not technically alone.

All of this is making me more mean and angry, and I really hate that. I don’t want to turn into a bitter person, but I honestly don’t know how to change it.
