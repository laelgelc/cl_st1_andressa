I get this really intense wave of loneliness whenever I see people who just seem… normal together. Groups laughing, friends who are clearly comfortable around each other, people who belong somewhere. It makes me feel like some kind of freak who slipped through the cracks, like I’m always on the outside looking in.

I don’t feel like I truly fit in anywhere. Even when people are friendly or pleasantly surprised to see me, it still feels like I’m optional. Like they’re happy enough I showed up, but they never would’ve thought to invite me or ask where I was in the first place.

No one really seeks me out. No one texts first to see how I’m doing or asks if I want to hang out. I’m always the one who has to appear in front of them for them to remember I exist. And I’m tired of feeling like a background character in other people’s lives.

What I want feels so simple but impossible: for someone to actually miss me, to notice my absence, to come looking for me. But it feels like that’s asking too much, like that kind of connection just isn’t meant for me.
