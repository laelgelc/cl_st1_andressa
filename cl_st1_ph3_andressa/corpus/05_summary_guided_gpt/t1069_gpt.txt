I’m an almost 18-year-old guy and I’m ace/aro. I don’t want sex, I don’t want romance, and I’m honestly fine with that part. What I *do* want is closeness – talking, just being around someone, cuddling, physical contact that’s comforting but not sexual.

This is where I’m really stuck. On one hand, I’m scared of ending up completely alone because so many relationships seem to revolve around romance or sex. On the other hand, I’m scared that my need for closeness is “too much” or somehow wrong.

I’m afraid that if I ask for cuddling or more physical closeness, people will feel pressured, manipulated, or think I’m secretly after something sexual, even though I’m not. I do communicate clearly that it’s non-sexual and I absolutely respect “no,” but I still feel guilty for even *wanting* it. I keep wondering: do I even “deserve” that kind of closeness? Am I even allowed to ask? Or should I just suppress this need so I don’t make anyone uncomfortable?

All of this ties into a general inner restlessness, derealization, and anxiety. I’m not looking for therapy here, just honest perspectives.

If you’re ace/aro (or not) and feel something similar:  
– How do you handle wanting closeness without pressuring yourself or others?  
– Is this kind of physical closeness inherently problematic, or is it just about how you manage it?  
– Do I sound like a pervert, or is this… normal?
