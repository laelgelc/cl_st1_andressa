I’m so tired of living like this. My anxiety is ruining everything, even the smallest, most basic things. We had to introduce ourselves in class recently and my whole body was shaking. I could feel my voice wobbling, my hands trembling, and I was so sure everyone was staring and judging me. Afterward I just kept replaying it in my head, mentally tearing myself apart for how “weak” and pathetic I must have looked.

It feels like my mind is a prison of nonstop negative thoughts. I get stuck in these loops where I just want everything to stop, where I think it would be easier if it all just ended. Then I get scared of my own thoughts and of the possibility that this is just how I’ll always be, that I’ll never get better or be “normal.”

This anxiety is even stopping me from doing things I need to do, like applying for jobs. I keep imagining the worst-case scenario: I show up shaky and nervous, everyone notices, and they secretly (or openly) mock me for being so fragile. Just the idea of people seeing how anxious I am makes me want to hide forever. So I don’t apply, and then I hate myself for not even trying.

I feel so drained all the time. Drained, angry, and full of self-hatred. And on top of that, I feel guilty for even feeling this way because I know other people “have it worse.” That thought just makes me feel like I’m not even allowed to be struggling, like I’m weak for not being able to handle life.

I also feel really alone. I don’t know if people abandoned me or if I just never reached out enough, but either way I feel friendless. I look around and see everyone else with their groups and their support systems, and I’m just… here. Stuck in my own head.

I’m tired of feeling sorry for myself, tired of crying, tired of being this person. I don’t even know what I want from posting this. Maybe I just needed to get it out somewhere because keeping it inside is starting to feel unbearable.
