I keep thinking about this analogy: imagine you have a neighbor you genuinely like. You chat at the mailbox, you’re happy to see him at parties, you enjoy the small talk and the jokes. But you’d never invite him to your wedding, or call him when something big happens, or think of him when you’re planning a trip. He’s… nice. Just not someone you’d ever pull closer.

I feel like I *am* that neighbor to everyone.

People laugh with me, say they enjoy having me around, tell me I’m “great to talk to.” But when it comes to real life—birthdays, deep conversations, holidays, group trips—I’m not on the list. It’s like I’m permanently stuck on the outer ring of everyone’s life, and it hurts more because they genuinely seem to like me.
