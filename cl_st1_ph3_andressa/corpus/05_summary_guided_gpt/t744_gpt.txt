I feel like I’m stuck between two bad options and I don’t know which one hurts less.

On one hand, I really value the peace that comes with being alone. No drama, no pretending, no feeling like I’m bothering anyone. Just quiet. But then that quiet turns into something heavier, and I start to feel like I’m disappearing. I get scared that one day I’ll realize I have no one to talk to besides family, and even that feels fragile.

What really messes with my head is that I’m always the one who shows up. I care, I check in, I remember birthdays, I listen to people’s problems. But when I’m low, it’s just… silence. No messages, no “are you okay?”, nothing. It makes me wonder if these long-term “friends” are even friends, or if I’m just convenient when they need something.

So I’m torn: do I choose solitude for the peace and accept the loneliness, or do I keep these unsatisfying, one-sided connections just so I’m not completely alone? How do you cope when the quiet gets so loud that it leads to emotional breakdowns? How do you carry that weight without it swallowing you?
