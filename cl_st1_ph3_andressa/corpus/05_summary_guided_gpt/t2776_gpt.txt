I’ve always been pretty comfortable being on my own. I’m an only child, I live in a different country from my family, and I’ve kind of grown up emotionally independent. I like my own company, and I’ve never really felt the need to be constantly talking to someone.

When I started college, I met this girl who really wanted to be close friends. At first it was nice, but very quickly it turned into something that felt… intense. She wanted constant attention: long late-night phone calls, texting all the time, and going out frequently, even when our schedules didn’t really match. For me, time at home is my break from socializing, not an extension of it. I actually prefer bonding in person, not over the phone at 1 a.m.

Whenever I tried to set some boundaries or was just quieter, she’d get upset and say I wasn’t being open enough or attentive enough. It felt like I was always failing some invisible test of friendship. Eventually it got too overwhelming, so I ended the friendship.

Now I keep wondering if I’m the problem. Is there something wrong with me because I need so much space? Is this just going to keep happening every time I try to get close to someone? The whole situation feels kind of weird and I’m not sure if my reaction was reasonable or if I’m just bad at being close to people.
