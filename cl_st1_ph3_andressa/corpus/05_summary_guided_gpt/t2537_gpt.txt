Lately it feels like I’m living more in my memories than in my actual life.

I keep replaying these moments from the past that feel lighter, warmer, more meaningful. It’s like I’ve built this little museum in my head of “better times,” and I wander through it instead of being in the room I’m actually in. I miss people, places, versions of myself that don’t exist anymore, and there’s this ache that doesn’t really go away.

At the same time, I’m very aware that while I’m busy grieving what’s gone, the present is quietly slipping away too. I know that one day, I’ll probably look back on *this* time and miss parts of it, and that thought really messes with me. It’s like I’m watching sand fall through an hourglass and I’m too paralyzed to cup my hands under it.

I keep telling myself that love and meaning only really exist in the current moment, not in some idealized past. But I’m scared I’m taking what I have now for granted, that I’m not really seeing or feeling it properly. I don’t even know anymore if I truly miss what I think I miss, or if I’m just failing to appreciate what’s right in front of me.

I feel stuck between nostalgia and numbness, and I don’t know how to actually live where I am.
