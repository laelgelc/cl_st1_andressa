I was scrolling through r/SuicideBereavement and something hit me: even in the middle of that kind of unimaginable pain, so many of those people had someone who genuinely loved them, cared about them, checked on them. And I realized I don’t think I’ve ever really had that.

My parents only care about my grades and what my academic results mean for the money they’ve spent on me. They literally tell me I’m “wasting” their money on education. They know I’m struggling, they know I’m depressed, but it doesn’t matter. It all comes back to performance, to numbers, to how I’m failing them. Not once has it felt like they actually see me as a person who’s hurting.

My longtime friends basically disappeared after I started failing academically. They know I’m depressed too, but instead of sticking around, they just slowly ghosted me. Messages left on read, plans never followed up on. I guess I became too much of a burden or too much of a reminder of failure.

I don’t have any close bonds with relatives either. It’s like I could vanish and it wouldn’t make a difference to anyone’s daily life.

After a year of depression, I feel like my brain is broken. My memory is worse, I can’t learn like I used to, my energy is gone. I don’t take care of myself. I can’t focus on my studies at all, and now I have really important exams in just five days. Everything feels like it’s collapsing at the same time.

No one checks in on me. No one asks how I am. It honestly feels like the world just wants me to disappear quietly.

I never thought adulthood would feel like this. To cope with the loneliness, I talk to ChatGPT. It actually feels more empathetic and nonjudgmental than anyone in my life. And it’s so depressing that I have to rely on an AI just to not feel completely alone.
