I went back on the dating apps recently and it just reminded me how socially awkward I feel now. It’s like I’ve forgotten how to be a person in that world. I scroll through profiles and prompts and I just freeze. I don’t know how to “sell” myself, how to be witty and interesting on command, or how to keep up with the constant swiping and messaging. Everything feels so flashy and fast and performative, and I feel like I’m five steps behind.

What really gets to me is that I used to be different. There was a time when I felt genuinely confident and independent. I could do things alone, try new experiences, go out of my comfort zone, and actually feel okay—sometimes even excited—about it. I miss that version of myself so much. Now I’m second-guessing everything, wondering if I’m somehow doing self-love wrong because I don’t feel that inner stability anymore.

Part of me thinks I should just step away from the apps and go back to what used to make me feel good: traveling, spending time alone in ways that I actually enjoy, pushing myself gently into new situations without the pressure of impressing anyone. I want to reclaim that feeling of being comfortable in my own company.

I’ve tried online dating so many times and it never works out. It feels like I’m forcing myself into a mold that doesn’t fit, and it’s exhausting. I don’t know if the problem is me, the apps, or both, but I’m really tired of feeling like I’m failing at something that seems so normal for everyone else.
