I don’t understand what’s wrong with me. I actually like things – shows, books, music, movies – but the second someone asks, my brain just freezes. It feels like there’s this wall between me and my own interests. I know what I enjoy, but I can’t bring myself to say it out loud.

Instead I panic and say stuff like “I don’t really listen to music” or “I don’t watch much TV,” even though that’s not true at all. I’ve even done this with my parents. It’s like I’m more comfortable pretending to be this blank, boring person than risking any kind of judgment.

I’m so scared of humiliating myself, of saying the “wrong” thing or having someone think my tastes are stupid or cringe. The fear feels physical, like my throat closes up. It honestly feels safer to lie or stay silent than to just say, “I like this song” or “I’m into this show.”

But I’m also so tired of it. I know it makes me look like I have no personality, like there’s nothing interesting about me. I hate that people probably see me as dull or empty when I know there’s more going on in my head. I just wish I could feel safe enough to be honest about the things I like without this intense anxiety taking over.
