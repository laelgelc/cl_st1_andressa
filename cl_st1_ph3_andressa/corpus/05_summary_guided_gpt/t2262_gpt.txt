I’m 19 and I feel like I’ve already been pushed to the edge of life before it even really started.

Physically I’ve always felt “wrong.” I have a really severe overbite and recessed facial bones, and I’m shorter than anyone expected me to be based on my parents. I look in the mirror and just see someone that people are going to instinctively avoid. On top of that I’m neurodivergent, with what I’d honestly call a low IQ, and my social skills are terrible. I never know what to say, I misread situations, and I can feel the awkwardness radiating off me when I try to talk to people.

At school I was always the small, weird kid that nobody wanted to sit with. I never had real friends, just people who tolerated me for a bit and then drifted away. Eventually it got so painful that I dropped out around 13–14. It felt like every day was just proof that I didn’t belong anywhere.

I tried to find connection online, but that went the same way. The rare times I thought I’d made a friend, they either betrayed me or slowly started ignoring me until they were gone. It’s like I’m some kind of test people fail and then move on from.

I feel like I make people uncomfortable or even disgusted just by existing. I’m so tired of masking, of trying to twist myself into something likable. Deep down I honestly believe I’m just destined to end up as a lonely, homeless, mentally ill person that no one really cares about.
