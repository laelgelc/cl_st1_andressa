I feel like I’ve almost never had real friends. In high school I was always kind of there, but not really seen. The people I hung around with treated me more like a background character than an actual person. I was the “extra” in their lives, not someone they genuinely cared about. After graduation, they all just… disappeared. No messages, no attempts to keep in touch. It was like I’d never existed to them in the first place.

That whole experience really messed with my head. Since then, I’ve been incredibly lonely, and I’ve sort of internalized this idea that people don’t actually want me around—they just tolerate me until they don’t have to anymore.

Recently, though, I met this guy who actually seems interested in being my friend. When I asked if he wanted to see a movie, he said yes pretty quickly, bought his own ticket, and didn’t act like it was some huge favor. We talk, and he actually engages in the conversation instead of just waiting for his turn to speak or ignoring what I say. On the surface, it all looks normal and positive.

But I can’t shake this feeling that it’s not real. My brain keeps telling me he’s just being nice, or maybe he feels sorry for me, and eventually he’ll realize I’m not worth the effort and drift away like everyone else. It’s like there’s this constant voice in my head saying, “Don’t get attached. Don’t be stupid. You’re imagining this. No one actually wants you.”

Logically, I know this situation is different from high school. He hasn’t done anything to suggest he’s using me or that he doesn’t care. If this were someone else telling me the same story, I’d probably say, “Give it a chance, it sounds like a real friendship.” But when it’s me, it feels impossible to believe. I feel stuck in this cycle where I’m desperate for connection but also convinced it’s all going to fall apart.

I’m miserable and I don’t know what to do with these feelings. I want to trust that this could be something good, but I honestly don’t know how.
