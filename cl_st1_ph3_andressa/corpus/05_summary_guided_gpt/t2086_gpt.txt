A few months ago my neighbor moved out, and I’m surprised by how much it’s affected me.

We were never close friends or anything. We’d say hi in the hallway, hold the door for each other, occasionally help with small things. Just normal neighbor stuff. But somehow her being there made this place feel a little more alive. I could hear her sometimes through the walls, or her keys in the lock, and it was oddly comforting just knowing someone else was around.

Now my floor is completely empty except for me. No footsteps, no doors closing, no muffled sounds of life. It’s just… quiet. Constantly. The whole place feels kind of abandoned, and I guess I feel a bit abandoned with it.

I’m not working right now because of some difficulties, so my days are basically just me and my thoughts. There’s nothing to distract me, no background noise, no sense that life is happening around me. Everything feels bland and flat, like every day is the same grey “nothing.”

The quiet doesn’t feel peaceful, it feels unnatural. It’s like the silence has weight to it, pressing down on me and reminding me how alone I am. I never realized how much those tiny, casual interactions and sounds mattered until they were gone.

I’m wondering if anyone else has experienced this kind of emptiness from something that seems small on the surface, like a neighbor moving away. How do you cope with this kind of total solitude when it just feels wrong and you can’t seem to get used to it?
