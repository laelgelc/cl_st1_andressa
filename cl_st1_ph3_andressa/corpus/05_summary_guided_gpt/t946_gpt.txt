I’m 37, AMAB, and in this weird limbo where I’m pretty sure I have AuDHD and BPD, but I’m still working with my doctor to get an actual diagnosis. In the meantime, my emotions feel like they’re turned up to 100 all the time. I cry a lot, sometimes over “small” things, and it’s just… exhausting. It feels like my nervous system never gets a break.

Childhood trauma is still running the show in ways I hate. It’s made it really hard to keep jobs or get through school. Every time I try to move forward, I feel like I hit the same walls over and over again. I start to think I’m finally getting somewhere, and then I crash.

I’m also in the middle of socially transitioning. I’ve come out to a few close people, but my family is very likely to react with transphobia, and that fear keeps me quiet. I’ve pulled away from a lot of old acquaintances because I don’t feel safe being myself around them. Now I don’t really have any transgender friends at all, and it makes me feel like I’m doing this in a vacuum.

My main confidant isn’t speaking to me right now, which hurts more than I expected. And even though I live with my partner, she’s recovering from major surgery and has fibromyalgia, so she sleeps most of the time. I understand she needs rest, but it leaves me sitting alone with my thoughts, feeling like I’m disappearing.

I don’t really know how to cope with this level of loneliness and emotional intensity. I just feel very, very alone.
