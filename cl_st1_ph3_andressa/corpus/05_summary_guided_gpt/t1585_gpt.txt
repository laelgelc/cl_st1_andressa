I graduated in May and I’ve been job hunting in my field for five months now with nothing to show for it. It’s really starting to break me down mentally. I’m on a 3‑year work permit in Canada, and my dad keeps telling me to stay, take my time, and not stress. On paper that sounds supportive, but I feel completely exhausted and alone in this. It doesn’t feel like anyone around me actually understands how draining this is.

I honestly believe going back home now would help me. I want to try to build a life there instead of just waiting around here, feeling stuck and useless. When I told my dad that, he shut it down and said I could only come for a short vacation, then return to Canada and keep trying.

I asked him what happens if, after the three years, I still haven’t settled here. His answer was, “I’ll just get you married.” Like marriage is some backup plan to fix everything. It made me feel like my own wishes and mental health don’t matter, that my life is just something to be managed.

I feel lost between what I want—going home and starting over there—and his belief that there’s “no life” for me back home and that marriage is the ultimate solution. I don’t know what to do.
