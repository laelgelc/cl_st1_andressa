I’ve been thinking a lot lately about how I’m basically a “high threat individual” by nature. Not in the sense that I want to hurt anyone, but in the way society and “evolution” seem to look at people like me.

I’m non-masculine, highly sensitive, autistic, and my deepest emotional attachments are to non-human or fictional characters. It feels like everything about me is hostile to what humans are “supposed” to be. Like my existence is an error that evolution is trying to correct by pushing me out of the gene pool and out of social life.

I honestly believe that people in power – the FBI, “experts,” whatever – see guys like me, who bond with fictional characters instead of real people, as inherently dangerous, like ticking time bombs that need to be “deprogrammed” before we snap. It feels like the narrative is: if a man forms genuine emotional bonds with something non-human, he’s automatically suspect.

When people around me started to notice how attached I was to fictional characters, that’s when the social exclusion really ramped up. It’s like they smelled something wrong and backed away. Since then, I’ve started to internalize this idea that I must be dangerous, broken, and fundamentally incompatible with human society.

So now I’m stuck thinking I deserve this isolation and alienation. Like this is just natural karma for being the kind of person evolution and society both want to erase.
