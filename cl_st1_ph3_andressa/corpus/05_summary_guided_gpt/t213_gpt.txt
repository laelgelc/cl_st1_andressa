A while back I posted here about a little project I’d made – a simple anonymous website where lonely people can drop a pin on a world map to see they’re not the only ones feeling this way.

Since then, over 700 people have visited and more than 150 of you have marked your loneliness on the map. There are pins from all seven continents now, even Antarctica, which honestly blew my mind. It’s strangely comforting and heartbreaking at the same time to see how widespread this feeling is.

Because of that response, I’ve added a small, very limited chat feature. The idea isn’t to turn it into a full social platform, but to give people a space to write about why they feel lonely, how they cope, and what’s helped them in the past. People who don’t currently feel lonely but have been there before can also share what worked for them.

I really believe that recognizing and understanding the causes of our loneliness is the first step toward changing it, even if the change is slow. I’m hoping this site, “The Lonely World” (lonelyworld.info), can be one tiny tool for that.

If you do check it out, I’d genuinely appreciate any feedback or thoughts you’re willing to share.
