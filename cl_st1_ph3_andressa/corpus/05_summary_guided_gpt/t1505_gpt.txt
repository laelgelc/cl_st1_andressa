For the last four years I’ve been dealing with clinical depression. It got bad enough that I basically disappeared from my own life. I isolated myself, stopped reaching out, stopped replying, and eventually just lost all my friends. It wasn’t dramatic, just this slow fade where I woke up one day and realized there was no one left I could text or call.

The good part is that things are finally changing. Therapy, medication, some big lifestyle shifts, and honestly just sheer stubbornness have started to pay off. I feel more hopeful and energetic than I have in years. It’s like I’m finally getting myself back.

Most of that new energy has gone into a startup I’ve been building. It’s become really meaningful to me, and it’s closely related to the things that helped pull me out of depression. The beta should be launching in about 2–3 months, and working on it gives me a sense of purpose I didn’t think I’d feel again.

But now I’m running into a different kind of loneliness. I don’t really have anyone to talk to about the emotional and practical side of building this thing—no one to share the little wins with, or vent to when something breaks, or just bounce ideas off. I miss having people to talk to, especially people who get what it’s like to be obsessed with an idea and trying to turn it into something real.

If you’re interested in startups, working on one, or just curious about this kind of stuff and open to chatting, I’d really appreciate it if you commented or DMed me. I’d be grateful for even one or two genuine connections.

I’ll share more details about the startup itself once the beta is live, since it’s very tied to what helped me out of depression. For now, I’d just like some human connection around the journey.
