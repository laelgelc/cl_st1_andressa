I’ve pretty much accepted that I’m a loner. I never really fit anywhere, and I get the sense that people can only handle me in small doses. It’s like I’m this weird inconvenience they tolerate for a bit before they need a break. That feeling makes me think I’m just fundamentally unlovable.

I don’t care about being remembered or leaving some legacy. The idea of being “important” to the world doesn’t mean anything to me. I’d rather just be myself than twist into whatever shape society wants from me, even if that means being alone.

I have Tourette’s, ADHD, OCD, and other neurological and mental stuff going on, and it feels like that pretty much kills any realistic chance of finding a partner who would genuinely accept me as I am.

I’m not promoting self-harm or anything like that. It’s just… I find a strange comfort in knowing life is finite, that at some point all of this will end and the suffering won’t go on forever.
