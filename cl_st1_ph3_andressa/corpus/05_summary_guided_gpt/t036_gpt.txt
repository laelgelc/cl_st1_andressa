I feel like I’ve slowly turned into an introvert, but not the “happy alone” kind – more like the “I’m alone and I don’t know how to fix it” kind.

In middle school I had close friends I could be open with. We’d talk about random stuff, play games together, just exist around each other without it feeling awkward. I miss that so much. Now I barely have anyone I talk to regularly, and I really miss having people who actually text first, who want to call, who want to play something with me.

My whole social life kind of ended up revolving around my ex. Then we broke up, quarantine hit, and everything just… stopped. It made me realize how much I depended on her for basically all my social interaction. After that, I drifted from everyone else too. It’s like once that connection was gone, I didn’t know how to keep the others going.

I still play games and watch YouTube, but it all feels weirdly empty when I’m doing it alone. Stuff that used to be fun with friends just feels like I’m passing time now. I’ve been putting more energy into my grades, and I guess that’s one good thing, but it also feels like schoolwork is all I have. Wake up, school, homework, repeat.

I don’t really know what I want from posting this, I just needed to vent somewhere. It actually feels kind of good to put it into words. I’m trying to hold onto the hope that university will be better, that I’ll meet people I can actually connect with again and not feel this alone all the time.
