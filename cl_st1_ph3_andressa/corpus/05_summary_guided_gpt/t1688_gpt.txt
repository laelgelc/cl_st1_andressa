Late last year I hit this weird wall: I wasn’t “alone” on paper, but I felt deeply lonely and kind of…purposeless. I grew up around men who never talked about feelings. Nobody modeled vulnerability for me. The unspoken rule was: be strong, be useful, don’t complain, don’t get too real.

So I learned to show people the version of me that fit their expectations. I’d be the good listener, the problem-solver, the one who’s “fine.” Meanwhile, the stuff actually going on in my head stayed locked away. It’s not that I don’t have friends; it’s that I don’t feel like I can be fully honest with them without worrying I’ll be “too much.”

I realized I wanted a space where I could be vulnerable without all that pressure. That’s how I ended up building an app called BeHuman. The idea is simple: you send and receive one message a day with a stranger. Just whatever’s honestly on your mind. You can stay anonymous if you want. It’s small, but it’s real. For me it’s become like a little daily mindfulness check-in with another human.

I’ve already gotten some surprisingly meaningful messages from people I’ll probably never meet. Somehow it’s easier to open up to strangers than to people who know me. And, honestly, working on this app has started to feel like a purpose. I feel freer expressing myself through it than I usually do in my “real” life.

I don’t know if this will resonate with anyone here, but I’m hoping it might give others the same tiny dose of connection it’s giving me.
