I don’t really know where I fit, and most of the time it feels like I don’t have the right to claim any space at all. With Americans I feel like I’m not “really” one of them, and with Indians I feel like I’m not “really” one of them either. It’s like I’m always borrowing a corner from someone else’s room, never having a room of my own, if that makes sense. I constantly feel like I’m taking up space that actually belongs to other people.

So I stay in my own little cocoon. I read, I write, I watch movies, and I talk to myself a lot. That sounds weird written out, but it’s how I process things. I’ll replay conversations in my head, or imagine ones that might happen, or just talk myself through random thoughts and ideas. I actually don’t feel particularly lonely when I’m doing this. I’m comfortable being alone. It’s more like I feel… lost. Like a loser who’s just circling around the starting line.

I have all these ideas and I genuinely believe I have potential, but I don’t really pursue anything properly. It’s like I’m always “about to” start something, always preparing, researching, thinking, but never actually committing. Life feels like I’m training for a marathon that I might never get to run. I’m stretching, warming up, buying shoes, reading about running techniques, but I never step up to the line when the race actually starts.

I ask people for advice sometimes, but the truth is I don’t really value it. Not because I think I’m smarter than them, but because I don’t even know if I know myself well enough to recognize what advice would help. Half the time I wonder if I actually want change or if I just like the idea of it.

I assume other people look at me and see a loser, because I haven’t really achieved anything that feels concrete or worth pointing to. And the worst part is I don’t even have friends to test that assumption on. I literally have no friends. It’s just me, my thoughts, and this weird sense that I’m always on the outside of my own life.
