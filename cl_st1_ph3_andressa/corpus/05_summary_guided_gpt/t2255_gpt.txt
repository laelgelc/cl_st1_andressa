I don’t really feel like a person anymore. For the past nine months or so it’s like I’ve been on autopilot, just moving through these repetitive cycles of moments and boredom. Everything feels detached, like I’m watching my life from the outside instead of actually living it. I do things because I’m supposed to, not because I feel present in them.

When I talk to people, I get this strong sense that they’re not really listening. Their eyes drift, they interrupt, or they just move on like I never said anything. It makes me wonder what the point of talking is at all. If no one hears me, it feels like I don’t exist. So I talk less, and then feel even more alone.

Social interactions feel like an improv play where everyone is acting for some invisible audience. Everyone else seems to know their lines and their character, and I’m just stumbling through the scene, a bad actor who never got the script. I feel awkward and not normal, like there’s something fundamentally off about me that other people can sense.

I’m constantly tired but also restless, stuck in my head, overthinking every little thing I say or do. I feel self‑conscious all the time, like I’m failing at being human. “Not human” is honestly the closest way I can describe it. I’m scared that if something really bad or traumatic happens again, it’ll just push me over the edge into total isolation, where I stop trying altogether.

I’m not posting this looking for pity or dramatic sympathy. I just want to put this somewhere. I want someone to actually read it. Even if no one replies, at least I said it out loud instead of just letting it loop endlessly in my head.
