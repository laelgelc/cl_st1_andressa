There was a time when I was so isolated I genuinely felt like I was going insane. Days would pass without me saying more than a few words out loud. It got to the point where I could feel my brain melting from the lack of conversation, like I was slowly disappearing because no one ever reflected anything back at me.

Things are technically “better” now, but not in any way that actually fixes the core problem. I’m not in that extreme silence anymore, but I still don’t have real friends. What I have is brief online interactions, surface-level chats that never go anywhere deeper, and then… nothing. It’s like taking a sip of water when you’re dying of thirst. It just makes you more aware of how thirsty you are.

I need frequent contact and conversation just to feel human, but making friends feels almost impossible. I feel fundamentally different from what I think of as “regular” people. It’s not even about interests, it’s like we’re running on different operating systems. To get along with them, I’d have to pretend, smooth out my rough edges, act “normal.” And I can’t do that without feeling like I’m slowly destroying myself.

I can only really connect with people who are “like me,” who just naturally understand without me having to translate everything. But those people are rare, and the specific circumstances where I might meet them just… aren’t happening in my life.

When I try to befriend typical people, it feels fake and mentally harmful, like I’m forcing myself into a costume that doesn’t fit and then trying to live in it. I can’t fit in, I can’t conform, but I still desperately need people I can simply exist around without acting.

So I’m stuck in this painful contradiction: I can’t be like them, I can’t find people like me, and I still need connection like I need air. It’s a really lonely place to be.
