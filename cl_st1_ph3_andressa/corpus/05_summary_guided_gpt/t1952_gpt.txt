I feel like I’m just wasting away here. I live in this small “pseudo city” that’s basically suburban sprawl with a few tall buildings slapped in the middle. I’m a minority here and I never really fit in; I’m always hyper‑aware of how out of place I am. It’s like I’m surrounded by people but completely invisible at the same time.

What makes it worse is knowing what it *could* be like. I took a leap a while back and moved to Boston with no job lined up. It was risky, but I loved it there – the density, the public transit, just being able to walk outside and see people, hear life happening around me. Even if I was alone, I didn’t feel as alone. But the job market was awful, I couldn’t land anything, and eventually I had to swallow my pride and move back in with my parents.

Now it’s 2026, I’ve got a 4‑year degree and certifications, and I’ve been applying nonstop for months with zero offers. I keep dreaming about New York or Chicago, just being somewhere where you don’t need a car and there are people on the street at all hours. Instead I’m stuck in a place where you need to drive everywhere, and if you don’t, you’re basically trapped. I honestly believe all this car‑centric sprawl is terrible for mental health.

I’d be happy with just one close in‑person friend, or a relationship, or even just living somewhere that doesn’t feel so empty. But right now I feel like I’m giving up. I just smoke weed every day to pass the time and numb everything, watching my life drift by while I wait for something to change that never does.
