Lately I keep picturing myself as a car stuck in deep mud.

No matter how much I press the gas, whether I try to go forward or backward, the wheels just spin and dig me in deeper. Every attempt to “fix” things seems to make everything worse. I look around and there’s no tow truck, no one on the side of the road, nothing. Just me, stuck, slowly sinking.

What scares me is that even if I somehow got pulled out, I don’t know if I’d even work right anymore. It feels like I’ve been sitting here too long, engine corroding, parts rusting. I imagine myself belonging in some junkyard, lined up with other useless, broken-down cars that no one wants, just collecting dust and rust.

And yet, there’s still this tiny spark in the engine. Maybe it’s fear, maybe it’s stubbornness, maybe it’s just habit, but something keeps turning the key, keeps trying to start. It’s exhausting, because it feels like a vicious cycle: try to move, sink deeper, hate myself for it, then try again anyway.

I’m tired of feeling like this, but I don’t know how to get unstuck.
