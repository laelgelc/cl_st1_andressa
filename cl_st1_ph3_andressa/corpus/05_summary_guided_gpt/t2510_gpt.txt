I’m a 20F in college and I’ve always been the quiet, “weird” one. This year I’ve been pushing myself really hard to be more outgoing, start conversations, not just sit alone and wait for people to approach me. I thought that was what I was “supposed” to do.

But it feels like it’s not working. I’m on casual talking terms with some classmates, but it never goes beyond small talk before/after class. We don’t text, we don’t hang out. There’s one girl who messages me, but it’s only ever about homework. I know that’s something, but it also makes me feel like I’m just useful, not actually liked.

It honestly hurts more than when I just stayed in my shell. When I chose to be alone, at least I could tell myself it was my decision. Now I’m trying, and it feels like I’m just getting rejected over and over.

There’s this shy guy I sit next to that I’ve been trying to befriend. I thought maybe we could relate. But he seems irritated and barely talks, and I’m starting to think I’m bothering him. At the same time, he hasn’t moved seats, so a tiny part of me is hopeful.

I keep wondering: am I just pestering people? Do other quiet people just want to be left alone? Am I just…annoying?
