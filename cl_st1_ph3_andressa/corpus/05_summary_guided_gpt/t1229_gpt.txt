I don’t even know how to put this into words anymore, but it’s really starting to mess with my head.

I keep seeing this pattern where guys treat my friends one way and me a completely different way, and I don’t understand what I’m doing wrong. My friends get crushes who are… decent. Guys who buy them little gifts, do favors, invite them places, try to get to know them. Even when my friends aren’t interested, the guys might be a bit disappointed, but they don’t turn on them. They stay polite, sometimes even stay friends, or at least neutral.

With me, it’s like the opposite. When a guy shows interest and I say “no” but still offer friendship, it almost always goes bad. I make it clear I’m not trying to lead them on. I don’t flirt, I don’t keep things ambiguous, I’m honest and straightforward. But instead of just accepting it or backing off respectfully, they start excluding me, ignoring me, or suddenly becoming hostile.

Some will literally act like I don’t exist anymore, especially in group settings. Others get mean: making snide comments, insulting me behind my back, or trying to smear me to other people. It’s like the moment I’m not available to them in the way they want, I stop being a person and become some kind of enemy or joke.

What hurts is that I’m not doing anything different from my friends. They can just… exist. They can say no, and people still invite them places, still treat them with basic respect. Meanwhile I feel like I’m constantly being punished for having boundaries. I end up isolated, on the outside of social circles, wondering why I’m always the one who gets the ugly side of people.

I’ve been told I’m conventionally attractive, so it’s not like this is about me being “undesirable” or something. But the way people react to me makes me feel less than, like there’s something fundamentally off about me that I can’t see.

It’s starting to make me feel really lonely and confused. I’m tired of feeling like I’m the one person who can’t just exist without it turning into drama or hostility.
