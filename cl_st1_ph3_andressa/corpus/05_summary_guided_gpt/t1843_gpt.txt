I’m 24, introverted, and I feel like I somehow managed to lose my entire life in the process of trying to “fix” it.

My whole social world used to revolve around drugs and alcohol. Parties, afterparties, “hangouts” that were really just getting high or drunk together. It made it easier to talk, easier to feel less shy, easier to pretend I was this social, fun person. I had friends, boyfriends, things to do. It felt like I was part of something.

Last year I decided to quit drugs. I still drink a little, but that’s it. And it’s like the second I stepped away from that scene, everyone just… disappeared. No one checks in. No one invites me anywhere. I don’t really talk to anyone outside of work now.

My nights and weekends are just me. I go to the gym, I make art, I read. On paper, it sounds kind of nice and productive, but it doesn’t touch the loneliness. It’s like I’m just filling time so I don’t have to sit in the feeling of how empty everything is.

The thing that really messes with my head is that I know I’m physically attractive. But socially, I feel invisible. Men don’t approach me. Dating apps go nowhere – matches just fizzle out, or we never actually meet. Seeing my old friends on Instagram having fun without me hurt so much I deleted it. It destroyed my self-esteem.

I don’t know if people stopped talking to me because I quit drugs or because I’m actually just not fun or desirable. I’ve started wondering if I might be autistic or something, because I feel so out of sync with everyone. I’m so sad all the time. I even think about going to bars alone just in the hope that someone will talk to me.

I just wish people liked me. I wish I had a group, a community, a life like the girls on “Girls” on HBO. I’m so tired of feeling like I’m on the outside of everything.
