I’m 25 and I’ve been hit with this really uncomfortable realization: I’ve never actually had real friends. I’ve had people to chat with, classmates, coworkers, some “hang out” people, but no one I can be completely honest with. No one I can call when I’m falling apart.  

The only person who ever came close to that was my husband. He was my main emotional support, the one I shared everything with. Now we’re divorcing, and he’s with someone else. That part hurts in a way I don’t even know how to explain. It feels like the floor just disappeared under me.  

I feel so empty and alone. I don’t know what my future looks like now—what relationships will be like, how I’m supposed to raise my child without that support, or how to deal with all of this by myself. It’s scary not having a safety net.  

At the same time, I can’t ignore the truth that’s staring me in the face: I never really built supportive friendships. I let one person be my whole world, and now that he’s gone, there’s just this huge void.  

So I’m trying to accept that I have to learn to rely on myself. To find small things that make me feel okay, even if it’s just a quiet moment or a tiny bit of progress. And maybe, slowly, I’ll figure out how to open up to new people and, hopefully, real friends someday.
