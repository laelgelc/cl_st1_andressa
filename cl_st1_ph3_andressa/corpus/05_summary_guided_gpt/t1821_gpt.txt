I live in this small town on the edge of Greater London and I feel like I’m in a weird no-man’s land socially. I don’t have any friends nearby that I can just text to meet for a coffee or a walk. All my old school friends have either moved away or we’ve just drifted apart over the years. When I think about trying to reconnect, it just feels like too much effort for something that probably won’t be the same anyway, so I don’t really bother.

I’ve tried to do the “right” things. I’ve joined several hobby and interest groups in central London and I actually really like the people there. When I’m with them, I feel more like myself and less alone. But it’s an hour each way on the train, plus the ticket cost, plus how expensive everything is in London. It’s hard to justify going regularly, and because I can’t go often, it’s hard to build anything deeper than “see you next week, maybe.”

I’ve searched and searched for anything similar closer to home, but there’s basically nothing. No local groups, no clubs that match what I’m into, nothing that feels like “my people.” So I end up spending most of my time in my home area feeling completely isolated, knowing that the people I click with are a long, expensive trip away.

I also can’t afford to move into London, not even close, with how housing prices are. So I feel stuck between where I can afford to live and where my potential social life actually is.
