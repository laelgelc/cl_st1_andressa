I’m finally sitting down after a really intense 72 hours of storm prep, and it’s hitting me in a weird way.

I’m 36, homo-demi, a total movie buff and horror fan, and I work at a theater. The last few days have been nonstop: getting stuff ready at work, getting my place ready, and then having to go in today just to help close the theater before the big snow really started. It’s been all adrenaline and to-do lists.

Now I’m home, safe, checked in with my boss, family, and friends. Everyone’s good. The storm is doing its thing outside, and I’ve got my own little plan for tomorrow: watching the snow, making some treats, staying cozy with my Christmas tree still up like a stubborn little comfort object.

But as soon as I put my phone down, I feel… off. It’s like the moment the noise stops, I’m very aware of how alone I am. I’m genuinely excited for my snow day, but there’s this undercurrent of “Wow, it’s just me here,” and it feels heavier than I expected.

So I guess I’m using this quiet moment to reach out. I’d love to just chat with people, maybe make some friends, especially anyone else snowed in or just feeling that weird mix of cozy and lonely. If you’re out there in this storm too, I hope you’re safe and warm.
