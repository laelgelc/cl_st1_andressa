My last real relationship ended a little over a year ago. We were together almost three years, and when it ended it absolutely shattered me. For a long time I just kind of… shut down. I told myself I was focusing on work, hobbies, whatever, but really I was just trying not to feel anything.

Recently I finally felt like I might be ready to try again. I put myself out there and it’s like the universe immediately slapped me in the face twice.

First was a coworker. I thought we had a good rapport, some light flirting, so I worked up the courage to ask if she’d like to go out sometime. Her response was harsh and very “professional,” like I’d crossed some line I didn’t even realize was there. It stung a lot more than I expected.

Then there was a girl I met at a bar. We had a fun conversation, she seemed into it, so I asked for her number. A few days later I texted asking if she’d want to go out. Six days of silence, and then just a single word: “No.”

Those two things back-to-back have made me feel like an idiot for even trying. It’s like, why did I bother healing just to get kicked right back down? Part of me wants to just emotionally shut down again, hit the gym, eat junk food, and joke that heartbreak is my new preworkout.

But underneath the jokes, it really hurts. I’m starting to feel like I’m just meant to be alone, not because I want to be, but because that’s just how it is for me.

If anyone’s been through something similar, or has any advice on how to deal with this without totally closing myself off again, I’d really appreciate hearing it. Or if you just want to share your own story, I’m listening.
