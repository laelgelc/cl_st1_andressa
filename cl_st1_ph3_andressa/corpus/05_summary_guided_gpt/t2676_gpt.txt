I feel like I’m living in this weird contradiction. On the surface, people usually like me at first. I’m told I make a good first impression, I’m seen as popular, well liked, even attractive. But after a few hangouts, it’s like a switch flips. People start to detach, get cold or mean, and suddenly I’m the “annoying” or “untrustworthy” one.

It’s confusing because I know my intentions. When I compliment people or joke around, I’m coming from a place of love. I’m not trying to hurt anyone or be rude. But somehow, what I say lands badly. People get upset or offended, and I’m left wondering what exactly I did wrong. It makes me second-guess everything I say and do.

I honestly don’t think I’ve ever had a real friend who stayed when things got hard. When life gets messy or I’m not fun to be around, people seem to disappear. Recently, some close friends hung out for a whole day without me, and they talked about it right in front of me like it was nothing. It really hurt, especially because I thought I was part of that group.

On top of that, people often assume the worst about me. They think I’m trying to steal their girlfriend, or that I’m only around for money, food, or weed. It’s like they’re always looking for some hidden agenda in everything I do, when I’m just trying to connect.

I feel really lonely and honestly pretty lost about what I’m doing that makes people see me this way. But I’m trying not to give up on the idea that things can get better. I’m holding onto the hope that one day I’ll find people who actually see me for who I am and that happiness will eventually find me.
