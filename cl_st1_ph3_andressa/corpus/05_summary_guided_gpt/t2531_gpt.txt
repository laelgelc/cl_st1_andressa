I forgot my own birthday this year. It only hit me when my dad and grandma messaged me. No friends, no one else. And honestly, I don’t even like my birthday anymore. It just feels like a reminder that I’m getting older but not really doing anything with my life.

I just started a new job in construction after basically a year of doing almost nothing. It’s brutal. I’m outside in -15°C, my hands hurt, my whole body hurts, and I feel so weak. The older guys there just work, no complaining, no visible struggle. Meanwhile I feel like I’m about to collapse half the time. It makes me feel pathetic, like I’m not built for “real life” the way everyone else is.

The last few days I’ve felt like I’m in some kind of mental fog. Emotionally numb, like my brain is wrapped in cotton. I keep thinking there’s something deeply wrong with me. I look at “normal people” living their lives and I honestly don’t understand how they do it, how they just… exist and function.

I had one small moment where I felt something good: playing Minecraft with a friend. For a little while I forgot everything and felt warm and okay. But it ended, and the emptiness came back even stronger. And that same friend has already said that someday we should stop talking. So even that feels temporary and doomed.

I have friends in real life, but I can’t talk to them about this. I don’t know how to explain this emptiness. I’m asexual and even that makes me feel weird and different, and I’m not comfortable talking about it with anyone.

I live in Latvia, but I’m Russian, and I feel like people hate me just for that, especially now with the war. I feel like I don’t have a homeland. I can’t even comfortably use my own language; I have to use English instead of Russian. I cry a lot, but now it’s like I’m past crying and just… empty.

Life feels like some arthouse film in a foreign language. Things are happening, but I don’t understand the plot at all. I don’t even fully know why I wrote this. Maybe I just wanted it to exist somewhere.
