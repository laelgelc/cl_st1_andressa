I got a weekend pass from the ward – first time out for more than an afternoon since early December – and I thought I’d use it to finally unpack and organise my new bedsit. I’d been looking forward to feeling a bit more “normal”.

But when I got there, the silence just hit me. No ward noises, no staff, no other patients, just… nothing. It felt like the walls were closing in. I tried putting on music, then a show, just to have some background noise, but it didn’t touch the loneliness. It almost made it worse somehow, like I was just pretending someone else was there.

I started spiralling and something related to my PTSD set me off. I ended up dropping a picture frame, and the whole thing snowballed until I was in A&E and then back on the ward again.

I still feel so alone. I moved across the country about a year ago, so there’s no family nearby. How do you cope with this kind of crushing loneliness?
