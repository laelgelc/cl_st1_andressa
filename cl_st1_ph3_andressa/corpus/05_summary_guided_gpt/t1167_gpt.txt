I’ve been really frustrated lately with how “support” so often feels like a product. Psychologists with waitlists and fees I can’t afford, “gurus” selling courses and coaching packages… it all feels so transactional. You’re a client, a problem to be solved, not a person who just needs someone to sit in the dark with you for a while.

I don’t want to be that. I’m not a professional, I’m not here to diagnose or fix anyone, and I definitely don’t have all the answers. What I *can* offer is to listen. Really listen. No judgment, no agenda, no trying to steer you anywhere. Just another human being on the other side of the screen.

I set up a small system on Discord for 1-on-1 text chats. It’s private and anonymous: you don’t have to share who you are, and the conversations are automatically deleted by a bot after a while, so nothing lingers forever. It’s just a place to unload, ramble, cry, vent, or sit in silence if that’s what you need.

This is completely free. There’s an option to donate if someone wants to help with server costs, but that’s totally optional and not expected.

If you’re feeling overwhelmed, lonely, or like you’re screaming into a void and nobody hears you, you can DM me here and I’ll send you the Discord info. No pressure, no expectations. Just a space where you’re allowed to be exactly how you are.
