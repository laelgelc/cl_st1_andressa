I used to be that person who would sit alone in coffee shops and diners, buying myself a meal or a drink and trying to feel okay with the solitude. Lately, though, I’ve started to resent spending money on what feels like “a meal that could have been a date.” It makes the loneliness feel more concrete somehow, like I’m literally paying for the reminder that I’m by myself.

So I’ve changed my approach. I still go to “third spaces” — markets, coffee shops, parks — but I’m trying not to buy anything unless it’s part of a social interaction. The idea is that I’ll be more intentional about trying to strike up conversations, and if something actually clicks, I could invite someone to grab a drink or a meal right then and there.

For example, I was at my local market recently and deliberately skipped my usual coffee. I just wandered, people-watched, and tried to be more open to talking to someone. I’m thinking of combining these outings with grocery trips so it feels a bit more purposeful and less like I’m loitering.

I’m curious how this sounds to other people. Would you feel weird sitting in a coffee shop without ordering anything? How long would you stay if you didn’t have food or a drink in front of you? Does it feel wrong to take up space like that, or is it just in my head?
