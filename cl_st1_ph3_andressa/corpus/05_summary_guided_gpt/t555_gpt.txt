I ended a long-term relationship a little while ago, and I feel like I lost everything in one go. He wasn’t just my boyfriend, he was my best friend, my emotional support, the one person I could actually talk to and feel safe with. In a life and household that already felt heavy and difficult, he was kind of the only light. Now that he’s gone, it feels like someone switched that light off and I’m just stumbling around in the dark.

I don’t really have anyone I can turn to now. I can’t have normal conversations with my family, let alone talk to them about how I feel. It just doesn’t feel possible or safe to seek comfort from them. So I keep it all inside and pretend I’m fine.

Outside of that, I basically only have two people in my life. One friend I bonded with because we were both in relationships, but now we barely see each other or talk. The other friend hardly replies anymore and has become really distant. I can feel them both slipping away and I don’t know how to stop it.

I’m in university, and everyone always talks about this being “the time of your life,” but it doesn’t feel like that for me at all. I’ve always struggled to make and keep friends. I feel awkward and not very social, like I’m just fundamentally bad at this. It feels like it takes a very specific kind of person to tolerate me long term, and those people are rare.

I have tried to be open and start conversations at uni. Sometimes it seems to go okay at first, but then it just… fizzles out. People don’t follow up, don’t message, or even avoid sitting next to me. I don’t know what I’m doing wrong, or if there’s just something about me that puts people off.

Most days I just go to class, sit alone, eat lunch alone, and go home alone. Sometimes I go an entire day barely speaking to anyone, maybe just a quick comment here or there. I feel like a ghost walking around campus, watching everyone else form groups, laugh, and make memories while I just exist on the edges.

I feel out of place and I keep asking myself what the point of being there even is, when I’m just so lonely all the time.
