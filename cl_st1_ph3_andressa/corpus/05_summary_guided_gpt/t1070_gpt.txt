It feels like people are more emotionally disconnected than ever, even though we’re technically more “connected” online. I see so many of us scrolling, lurking, maybe posting here and there, but not actually feeling seen or heard. Loneliness is everywhere, and most apps don’t really fix it—they just distract us for a bit.

I’ve been involved with a platform called Vooz that’s trying to tackle this more directly. It’s a random video and text chat site, but with a focus on actually matching people based on shared interests instead of just throwing you at totally random strangers.

You enter your hobbies and interests, and Vooz pairs you quickly with people who like similar things. You can choose whether you’re in the mood for video or just text, and if it’s not a good fit, you can skip and get matched again right away. If you click with someone, you can add them as a friend and keep talking later, and there are also group chats you can join around specific topics.

It’s not meant to be like Omegle. There’s strong AI moderation that actively bans NSFW users, so you’re not constantly dealing with creepy or inappropriate stuff. Matching is pretty fast, and there are gender and location filters if that matters to you in making connections.

The platform has been live for about a year now and currently has around 250k monthly users. The whole idea is to give people a realistic chance to make genuine friends and feel less alone, instead of just endlessly scrolling through feeds. If you’ve been feeling isolated and want to talk to actual humans with similar interests, it might be worth checking out.
