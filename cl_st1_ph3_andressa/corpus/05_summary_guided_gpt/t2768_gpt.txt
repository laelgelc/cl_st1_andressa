I’ve been on both sides: having friends around all the time and being a complete loner. Honestly, being a loner is the hardest. It can feel like walking around with a big “loser” sign on your back when you eat out alone, go to the beach alone, or shop alone. People stare, or at least it feels like they do.

But there’s another side to it. When you’re alone, the experience is fully yours. No one is shaping your opinions, pressuring you, or filling the silence with small talk that doesn’t really mean anything. It’s just you and your own thoughts.

Being in a group is comfortable, almost like a safety net. But being alone in your own skin takes a different kind of strength. I honestly think loners don’t get enough credit for how brave that really is.
