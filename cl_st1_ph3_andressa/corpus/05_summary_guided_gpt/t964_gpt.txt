Sometimes I look back at my childhood and it feels like watching a horror movie in slow motion. I was just a kid, but I spent years depressed, thinking about killing myself every single day, already convinced that loneliness was going to be a permanent part of my life.  

What really eats at me is how the adults around me didn’t notice, or didn’t care enough to look closer. No one asked, no one really listened. I never got help, never had real friends, never heard anyone tell me that what I was feeling was serious and that I deserved support.  

Now I’m an adult and I finally understand what was happening to me back then… and it feels like it’s too late. I’m still alone. I still need help. And the fact that I want it now, after all this time, just feels bitterly ironic.
