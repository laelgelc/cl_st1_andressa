19M here. I’ve pretty much felt lonely my whole life. It’s this constant background noise I can’t fully get rid of. I’m not writing this because I’ve “fixed” it, but because I’m trying really hard not to give up, and maybe some of what I’m doing could help someone else.

The main thing that’s helped me is forcing myself to be genuinely curious about other people. Not in a fake “networking” way, but actually wondering what their life is like, where they’re from, what they care about.

I learn languages and I’ve started talking to some of the workers at my college who are from places like Rwanda and Kenya. It started with simple questions, then trying a few words in their language. Those tiny conversations turned into small but meaningful interactions, and even led to me meeting a couple other students through them.

I try to do the same in other places too. At the gym, I might just say something like, “Hey, your tattoos are really cool, what’s the story behind them?” It’s nothing special, but it opens a door. Most of the time it’s just a short chat, but sometimes it turns into something more.

I know this doesn’t guarantee deep friendships. I’m still lonely a lot. But I really believe it massively increases the chances of eventually finding people you click with.

If anxiety is stopping you from talking to people, try thinking of yourself like a main character in a movie. The main character is scared, but they still walk up and say something. That’s what I’m trying to do: feel the fear, and talk anyway.
