I’m 30 and I’ve been single basically my whole life. At this point I’ve just accepted that this is how it’s going to be. People keep telling me “you’ll find someone” or “you just haven’t met the right person yet,” but I don’t believe that anymore and I don’t even care to argue.

Everyone repeats that humans “need” social connection, love, affection, whatever. I honestly don’t feel that way. If every person in my life disappeared tomorrow and I never spoke to anyone again, I don’t think I’d miss it. I’d just keep going. I don’t crave hugs, dates, deep talks, any of that.

“Love” itself feels like a lie to me. When people do “loving” things, it usually looks like obligation or habit, or they’re hoping for something in return—validation, security, favors. I don’t see genuine feeling there, just transactions dressed up with pretty words. So I don’t see the point in chasing something I don’t even believe is real.

Most people would probably look at my life and call it miserable or lonely. But for me, this is just normal. I’ve always expected to be left alone, so I’m not scared of it. You can’t really lose what you never had.
