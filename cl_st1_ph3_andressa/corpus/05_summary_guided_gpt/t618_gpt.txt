Twelve years ago, I watched this movie surrounded by neighbors, their kids, my family – a whole noisy crowd. We filled up an entire row, passing popcorn, laughing together. Yesterday I went to the re-release… completely alone.

It’s strange how quietly life changes. Social circles shrink, people get busy, relationships get messy. You don’t even notice when the group chats die and plans stop happening. And yet, somehow, you just… survive and keep moving.

Honestly, it took a ridiculous amount of courage to go by myself. I kept hesitating, almost backed out at the last minute. When I got there, I saw mostly couples and families, no one else alone, and it stung a bit. But I still went in, found my seat, and decided I was going to enjoy it.

I ended up talking to the people sitting near me a little, and when the famous dialogues came, I couldn’t stop myself from repeating them. I was anxious I might be annoying, but then I realized others were doing it too. That helped me relax. I enjoyed it just as much as when I secretly rewatch it on my laptop.

My mom always tries to stop me from rewatching my comfort movies, but when I’m alone and need something warm and familiar, I still put on Chennai Express. It’s my fun, happy place. SRK’s line about not escaping problems but solving them hit me again. I really hope one day I can actually live that, not just quote it.
