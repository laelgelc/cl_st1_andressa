I’ve pretty much been a loner for as long as I can remember. A lot of that comes from some stuff that happened when I was a kid that kind of rewired how I move through the world. I got close to my siblings instead, especially my older sister – she’s probably the one person who really “gets” me without me needing to explain much.

What’s funny is, from the outside, I don’t look lonely at all. I actually have a lot of friends, and people generally treat me with kindness. I know I’m loved and cared for. But I still gravitate toward being on my own. Give me my music, my movies, my creative gear, and I’m honestly content.

I’m very empathetic and intuitive, which sounds nice on paper, but it means I absorb everything around me – moods, tension, unspoken things. It can get overwhelming. Keeping some distance is my way of protecting my own peace, and weirdly, the peace of the people I love too. If I’m drained or overstimulated, I’m not much good to anyone.

My connections are still real and deep. I’d go to great lengths for the people I care about and I don’t expect anything back. I just also really value my space. I prefer being the one who visits instead of having people in my space, and most people in my life have learned to respect that I just need a lot of solitude.
