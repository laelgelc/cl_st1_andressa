Lately I’ve been thinking a lot about solitude and what it actually is for me. Not scrolling, not half-watching a show, not filling the silence with shallow chats. Just being with myself. In those moments, I notice my thoughts get clearer, and sometimes genuinely beautiful ideas show up. I understand myself a little more.

I still believe one-on-one conversations can be deeply meaningful, but I’m starting to think we put too much weight on lost or missing connections. We stare at the closed door and forget there are other “doors” in life. Like taking a walk alone and noticing the way light hits a wall, or a tiny plant growing through concrete. Those things are small, but they’re real.

Sometimes it feels like social interaction is this dopamine-heavy addiction, and the sudden quiet of loneliness is just withdrawal. Maybe some “necessary loneliness” is like sugar in a diet: not zero, not endless, but enough to keep us honest with ourselves.

I’m slowly redefining happiness as keeping promises to my future self. Showing up for the person I’ll be in a year, or ten. If you have people depending on you, maybe it’s also keeping promises to them. And if you don’t, maybe it’s to something bigger than you, however you define that.

Life feels like a long marathon we each run alone, with no real audience. In the end, the only person who can judge our effort is ourselves.

Wherever you are in your own race, I hope you keep going. We’re all in this together—just not in the same place or at the same time.
