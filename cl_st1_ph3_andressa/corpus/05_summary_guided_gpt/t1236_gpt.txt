I’m 29, single, and live alone with my dog and cat. On paper, my life is “fine” – I have friends, hobbies, a therapist, and a job I actually enjoy. My family isn’t super far away, but they rarely visit. And yet, living alone is really starting to break me, especially at night.

I dread coming home to a silent house. I walk in, feed the animals, maybe put on a show, but there’s this heavy feeling of emptiness that settles over everything. When I finally get into bed, my brain just won’t shut up. My thoughts race and I get hit with this wave of loneliness that feels so intense it almost hurts physically. Sometimes I end up crying, or I feel this urge to just grab my keys and go for a drive because being alone in the house feels unbearable.

I stay up scrolling on my phone until way too late because I can’t relax enough to sleep. I’ve tried tea, sleeping pills, working out, making sure I work full shifts so I’m tired – none of it really touches that feeling. I love when people visit or stay the night; I feel so much lighter. But when they leave, the silence afterward feels even worse, like the contrast makes it hit harder.

I feel like I’m the loneliest person I know, which I know probably isn’t true, but that’s how it feels. Does anyone else experience this specific kind of nighttime loneliness? How do you cope with it? Any tips or routines that make coming home to an empty place less painful?
