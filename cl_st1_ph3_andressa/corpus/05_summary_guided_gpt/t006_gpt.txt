I grew up constantly moving – different cities, different countries, different schools and universities. I was “the new kid” so many times I lost count. I’d make friends, sometimes even good ones, and then we’d move again. At first we’d stay in touch, but over time it always faded. It’s just so much easier to keep a friendship going when you see each other regularly than when it’s all effort and planning and messages across distance. It slowly turned into this “out of sight, out of mind” pattern for me.

Now I’ve actually been in the same place for about five years, which is the longest I’ve ever stayed anywhere. But my friendships still feel temporary. I make acquaintances, have friendly conversations, sometimes hang out a few times, but it rarely goes deeper. I struggle to find the emotional energy or even the desire to really invest in people or to start new connections from scratch.

I’m starting to wonder if I’ve internalized the idea that everyone is transient, that no one sticks around. Maybe I’m subconsciously protecting myself from the hurt of losing people by never letting them get too close in the first place. On the surface I want deep, stable connections, but when opportunities appear, I almost… pull back. Or I just don’t follow through.

The result is that I feel really unmoored and lonely, like I’m floating through places and people without ever really belonging anywhere. I want to change this, but I don’t quite know how to override whatever’s going on underneath.

Has anyone else had a similar experience, especially people who moved around a lot growing up? Did you notice the same “everyone is temporary” mindset? If so, how did you manage it or start to change it? What helped you actually build and maintain deeper friendships instead of just letting everything stay on the surface and fade away?
