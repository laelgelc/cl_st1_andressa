It’s this constant, quiet ache of feeling like I’m always on the outside looking in. I keep showing up, caring, listening, trying to be there for people, opening my heart more than feels safe… and somehow I’m still the one who’s never really chosen. It feels like I’m the backup option, the person people talk to when they’re bored, not someone they genuinely want in their life.

Every time I get my hopes up, it ends in some form of rejection or drifting apart, and it just reinforces this feeling that I’m unwanted, replaceable, easy to overlook. I start wondering what’s so wrong with me that no one seems to see me and think, “I want them. I choose them.”

The worst part is having all this love and care inside me with nowhere safe to put it. I don’t want to become bitter or shut down, but it’s hard not to when it feels like opening up only leads to more hurt. I don’t want to just be tolerated; I want to feel genuinely wanted.

If anyone else feels this way, you’re not weak or broken for wanting connection so badly. Wanting to be chosen and cared for doesn’t make you pathetic. You deserve to be seen, valued, and loved for who you are.
