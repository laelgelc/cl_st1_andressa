I’m 33, single, and have been unemployed for six months now. I spend most days sitting alone in my apartment, watching the hours go by and feeling like I’m stuck in concrete. I know I “should” be applying for jobs, reaching out to people, maybe even asking a woman out, but I just…don’t. It feels pointless, like I’ve already failed before I start. Then I get angry at myself and my own brain for constantly sabotaging me.

I’m in therapy and I know a lot of this goes back to the bullying I went through in elementary school. It was really bad, and when I think about it now my body still reacts like I’m back there: heart racing, stomach tight, this heavy shame. It’s been decades and it still has that much power over me, which is depressing in itself.

I joined a sports club to try and be more social. I do talk to people there, but when I ask someone if they want to grab a coffee, they usually turn me down or just never follow up. I have two friends I see maybe once every two weeks, and I’m grateful for them, but the rest of the time it’s just me and my thoughts.

The only thing that sometimes helps is going to the gym. Seeing the numbers on the weights go up gives me a tiny bit of dopamine, like “okay, something is improving.” But even that doesn’t always work, and I can’t live at the gym.

I’m looking for very small, concrete steps that give quick positive feedback, because if I don’t see something working fast I lose heart and give up. Also, I’d really appreciate some understanding words so I don’t feel so alone in this. If you’re struggling too, I honestly wish you better days ahead.
