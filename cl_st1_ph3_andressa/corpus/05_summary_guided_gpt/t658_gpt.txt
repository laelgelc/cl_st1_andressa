I started this new job in February. It’s a year-long training program, and at first I was with the same four guys all the time. We had classes together, studied together, group chats, all of that. It felt kind of like being thrown into a new school and latching onto the first people you meet.

But over the months I started noticing things that really turned me off. They act super childish, make sexist comments, talk about cheating on their girlfriends like it’s no big deal, and are generally two-faced with our classmates – nice to their faces, trashing them behind their backs. It honestly reminded me of high school, and not in a nostalgic way.

So I pulled back. I stopped hanging out with them as much, stopped trying to force conversation, because I just don’t like who they are as people. I don’t want to pretend to be friends with people whose values are so different from mine.

Now it feels like they’ve noticed and retaliated in this really petty way. They made a new group chat without me. They stopped sharing notes. One of them even lied about having a folder I needed for class. It’s like I’ve been quietly exiled.

This is the first time I’ve felt like an outsider and a “loser” at work. I actually have a good life outside of this – friends, things I enjoy – but now I feel this dread and disgust when I think about going in. I just want these classes to be over so I don’t have to be around them so much.

I’m stuck between feeling like I did the right thing by distancing myself and wondering if I’m overreacting or being too rigid. Is it normal for this to bother me this much? Has anyone else dealt with feeling like the odd one out at work because you just don’t like the people? What helped you get through it?
