I’m 27 and I’ve basically been isolated for over 10 years. No family, no friends, no real social life. Most days it’s just me, my room, and the internet. I’ve been trying to push myself out of my comfort zone lately because I’m tired of feeling like a ghost.

So I went to a local anime convention. It was a huge deal for me just to be there. A few people complimented my jacket, which already felt surreal. Then this girl noticed it, sat down next to me, and we ended up talking for like 1–2 hours. It felt…normal. Natural. We talked about shared interests, and I wasn’t overthinking every word for once.

Before we parted ways, she asked to exchange contact info. Later she added me on a messaging app. I was honestly shocked. I thought, “Maybe this is it. Maybe I finally made a friend.” I sent her a message, tried to keep it casual and friendly. No reply. Waited a bit, sent a follow-up. Still nothing.

It’s been a few days now. I know people get busy, I know there are a million possible explanations, but it still hurts. I feel stupid for letting myself hope. It’s like the universe dangled the idea of connection in front of me and then yanked it away.

Now I’m stuck between wanting to try again someday and wanting to just shut down completely and go back to avoiding people. I feel foolish for thinking someone might actually want me in their life. I don’t even know why I’m posting this, I just needed to get it out somewhere.
