I’m still in high school and I feel like I’m chronically isolated, even though on paper it probably doesn’t look that way. I know a lot of people, I talk to different groups, I’m not totally mute or anything. But I rarely get invited out, I’m almost never in group chats, and I don’t feel like anyone actually wants me around in a real, close way. It’s like I’m everyone’s “school friend” and no one’s actual friend.

Middle school was so different. I had a solid friend group, we hung out a lot, and I felt like I belonged somewhere. Since starting high school, it’s like everything has slowly faded. Each year the invitations get fewer, people get closer to each other but not to me, and now I mostly just go home after school. I keep thinking about how much I used to laugh and look forward to things, and how empty high school feels now.

I feel like I’m just rotting away. My life outside of school is basically soccer and family stuff. I feel like I’m disappointing my parents because they probably expected I’d be going out with friends more, having “normal” high school experiences. Instead, I’m always home, scrolling, overthinking.

I did have one relationship, but even that felt more like we were just friends. I never really felt chosen or special. I’m quiet, yeah, but I’m not antisocial. I can talk to people, I have different interests, I know people in a bunch of different groups. But when it comes down to it, I still end up alone.

I keep wondering if this is just how it’s going to be. Does it actually get better after high school? I’m planning on a job that involves traveling a lot, so I probably won’t see any of these classmates again. Part of me hopes that means a fresh start, but another part of me is scared that this feeling of being unwanted is just going to follow me everywhere.
