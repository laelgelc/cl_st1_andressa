I’m trying really hard not to completely fall apart right now, but I also don’t want to throw away my self-respect just because I’m hurting.

I’m so tired of being “the strong one,” the helper, the mentor, the therapist, the teacher. I’m exhausted from always being the person who holds everyone else together. People come to me to vent, to be understood, to be soothed, and I do it because I know how much it hurts to feel alone. But when it’s my turn, when I need someone to really see me, it’s like I’m talking into a void.

I’m starting to realize that a lot of the people around me just don’t have the emotional intelligence to meet me where I am. They hear my words but don’t actually listen. They react, they give surface-level empathy, but they don’t sit with me in it. I’ve confused that basic, polite empathy with real, intimate care for so long that I don’t even know what genuine emotional safety looks like anymore.

My longtime best friend… I don’t think I feel love there anymore. Or at least not the kind I thought I did. It feels like I’m just this emotional wall they lean on. If I don’t respond the right way, if I don’t show up how they expect, somehow I’m the problem. Connection has turned into this obligation, something I “owe” people, not something warm or mutual.

I feel used, but also weirdly numb. My desire for company doesn’t even feel emotional now; it feels like a basic human need, like needing food or water. I’m lonely, but in this detached, mechanical way, like I’m watching myself go through the motions of wanting people without actually believing I’ll be met.

I don’t know what to do with all of this. I just know I’m so tired of managing everyone else’s emotions while mine sit in the corner, ignored—even by me.
