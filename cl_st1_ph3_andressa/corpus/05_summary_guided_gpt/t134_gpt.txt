On the outside I probably seem like a pretty cheerful, put-together person, but I feel weirdly hollow and out of place most of the time. I’ve just finished postgrad and instead of feeling excited, I mostly feel this heavy hopelessness about my social life.

In college, I coped with a lot of childhood stuff by throwing myself into romantic relationships. They became my emotional crutch. I didn’t really invest in friendships, and when those relationships ended, I’d look around and realize I didn’t have anyone I was truly close to. It’s like I kept repeating the same pattern without learning from it.

Now I technically have “friends,” but they’re casual, surface-level, or just not people I feel I can be fully myself with. I do have one good friend, but even with them I feel like there’s a line I can’t cross when it comes to being vulnerable. I always hold back, like I’m too much or I’ll scare them off if they see how lonely I really am.

I’ve been with my boyfriend for about five months. He’s kind and I care about him, but I don’t feel ready to lean on him for everything. I don’t want to turn him into my only source of emotional support just because I don’t have anyone else. That feels unfair to him and unstable for me.

I’m scared that I’ve missed the window for making real, close friends and that this is just how my life is going to be: smiling on the outside, but alone where it really counts.
