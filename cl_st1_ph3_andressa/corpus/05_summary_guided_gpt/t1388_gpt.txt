I’m 35 and living in London, and somehow I feel more alone here than I ever did in smaller places. It’s been months since I’ve heard from most of my family or old friends. Sometimes I go whole weekends without speaking to anyone in person beyond a cashier or someone at the gym.

Things really started going downhill around 2020. During the lockdowns I lost all my work and didn’t qualify for any of the support schemes, so I scraped by on minimal universal credit and what savings I had. It felt like my life just… stopped.

When things finally opened up and I got back to work, I caught COVID and it almost killed me. I ended up with pneumonia, spent 18 days in hospital, 11 of those in intensive care. It took six months to recover, and it felt like I’d aged years in that time.

Since then it’s been one thing after another: a bad foot injury/plantar fasciitis, several chest and lung infections, flu, COVID two more times. I developed feelings for someone that weren’t returned, which hurt more than I expected. Then in 2025 I was attacked during a pickpocket attempt and broke a finger trying to defend myself.

All of this, on top of being so isolated, has messed with my head. I sometimes catch myself wondering whether it would have been better if I hadn’t survived that first severe COVID infection. I don’t really want to die, but I’m struggling to see what I’m surviving *for*.

Right now my life is basically work, gym, and side hustles. I’m trying to “improve myself”, but it’s all happening in a vacuum. I want some kind of normality again – real-life connections, people I can message or meet for a coffee, and eventually a romantic partner. Just to feel like I exist in other people’s lives.

If anyone reading this is London-based and feels similarly alone, would you maybe like to meet up at some point – a walk, coffee, gym session, whatever? I don’t have many people left in my life, and it would be nice to try to change that.
