I’m starting to think I might actually be depressed. I rated my loneliness as a 10/10 the other day, and it felt painfully accurate. The weird part is that I genuinely like being alone most of the time, but lately it just feels… empty. Like I’m watching my own life from the outside.

I’m 20F and I’ve never dated. When people talk about their relationships or “situationships,” I just sit there quietly, feeling like an impostor. I nod and smile but inside I’m thinking, “You have no idea what you’re talking about, you’ve never even held someone’s hand like that.” Then I feel guilty for even wanting that kind of intimacy or physical touch, like I’m somehow dirty or pathetic for craving it.

It’s gotten to the point where trying to satisfy myself just makes me cry. The loneliness and pent-up desire for connection is so overwhelming that it doesn’t feel good, it just reminds me of what I don’t have.

Another thing that worries me: I’m most comfortable talking to men who are older than me, like 4–20 years older. Professors, colleagues, etc. I don’t know why. I feel safer or more at ease with them than with people my own age, and that scares me a little. I keep wondering what that says about me.

I joke with my best friend that if I were a guy, everyone would think I’m a “loser” because of my interests and lack of experience. But deep down, I already feel like a loser.

I related really strongly to the manga *Inside Mari*—the whole theme of identity and emptiness hit way too close. And recently I saw an old friend from middle school in passing. We made eye contact, didn’t say anything, and neither of us reached out afterward. That silence kind of summed everything up.
