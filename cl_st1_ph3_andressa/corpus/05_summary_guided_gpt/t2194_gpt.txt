I feel like I’ve been this way my whole life: social stuff always feels like a chore I have to gear myself up for. I usually end up having an okay or even good time once I’m there, but then I get this urge to leave early and go home. It’s like I always want what I don’t have in the moment. When I’m alone I wish I had people around, and when I’m with people I want to retreat. I keep wondering if I’m just choosing the wrong people, or if it’s just me.

In my teens and early 20s I actually had close friends. We’d see each other regularly, hang out without planning weeks in advance, just be stupid together. Then I moved abroad and those friendships slowly turned into occasional messages and “we should catch up sometime” that never really happens. Now I don’t have any in‑person friends I see regularly.

My life is basically work, work out, sleep, repeat. It feels like I’m just killing time waiting for “real life” to start, but it never does. Winter makes it worse – dark, cold, and nothing to distract me from how empty my days feel.

Looking back, most of my friendships came from school and university, where you’re kind of thrown together. A lot of them were initiated by other people, not me. Now I’m nearly 30, single, and I miss having close friends I can just be myself with. But I feel like I don’t have the mental energy or motivation to actually build that again from scratch. I don’t really expect solutions; I just needed to vent this somewhere.
