I’m so tired of hearing some version of “no wonder you’re alone.”

I’ve been trying to use the internet to meet people who are also lonely, or who share similar values or interests. I’m not looking for drama, just some kind of human connection. But over and over, when things don’t click perfectly, it escalates into that same insult. It’s like people know exactly where to stab.

Recently I reached out to someone because they made a public post about a topic I’m really into. I thought, “Hey, this is what the internet is for, right? Finding people who like the same things.” I messaged them, tried to be polite and genuine. Instead, they mocked me as “weird” for even contacting them. When I said I didn’t want to talk anymore because of how they were speaking to me, they hit me with the classic: basically “no wonder you’re alone.”

I get that not everyone has to like each other. I don’t expect to get along with every person I talk to. But this particular insult feels especially cruel. It’s like taking something you already struggle with—loneliness, isolation—and weaponizing it. To me it feels comparable to making fun of someone’s loved one being sick or their pet dying. It’s kicking someone in the exact place they’re already hurting most.

Every time it happens, it makes me question why I even try. If reaching out just gives people more ammo to hurt me with, what’s the point of attempting to connect at all, whether online or offline? I’m already lonely, and I’m making a vulnerable effort. Having that used against me makes me want to just stop trying entirely.
