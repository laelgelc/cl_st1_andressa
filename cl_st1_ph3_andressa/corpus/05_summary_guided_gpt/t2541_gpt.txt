I’m 24 and it feels like my life is already over. Like the main story ended years ago and now I’m just sitting through the epilogue, watching the credits roll on a movie no one liked.

I saw a TikTok the other day about how you should avoid lonely people because they’re “dangerous” or “draining,” and it hit me way harder than it should have. It felt like someone finally said out loud what I’ve always believed: my loneliness is my fault. I’m the problem.

I go out of my way to make myself unapproachable. I avoid eye contact. I keep my face in this permanent “don’t talk to me” expression that I know comes off as evil or pissed off. If someone does try to talk to me, I give short, boring answers so they’ll lose interest. I want to be invisible and forgettable, because I see myself as a failure and a burden. If people don’t notice me, they can’t be disappointed by me.

The idea of making friends feels pointless. I’m convinced that if anyone got close to me, they’d eventually realize how messed up I am and feel overwhelmed by my problems. Then they’d just ghost me, and I’d be left feeling even worse. It feels kinder to everyone if I just stay on the sidelines and never let anything start.

My mom died, and I felt… nothing. I know that sounds horrible. I look at myself and think I must be cold and unfeeling. I don’t even let myself use that as an explanation for why I am the way I am, because I know other people have way more tragic stories and they still manage to function, to care, to feel. So I just add “emotionless” to the list of things wrong with me.

Most days I feel absent from my own life, like I’m just running on autopilot. Wake up, go through the motions, go to sleep. Repeat. I get these severe stomach pains that have made me collapse before. Sometimes I catch myself thinking that maybe one day it’ll just be the end, and a part of me wouldn’t be surprised. Just another thing that happens to someone who was barely here in the first place.
