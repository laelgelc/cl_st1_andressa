I feel like my whole life only works when I’m distracted. As long as I’m studying, working on my side hustle, or doing my hobbies, I can kind of ignore it. But the second things get quiet, I just feel this huge emptiness and this aching loneliness. It’s like there’s this version of me that only comes out when I’m alone, and she’s miserable.

I crave emotional connection so badly, but I don’t trust people. I have friends, but they don’t really know me. I never open up to them. Growing up in a strict religious environment, I learned early that a lot of my thoughts and feelings were “wrong,” so I started living this double life. On the outside I say and do the “right” things, but inside I’m someone else completely. I got shamed a lot, and now I just assume if I show people who I really am, they’ll judge me or leave.

I feel so complicated, like I’m broken somehow. I wish I could just be “normal” and not have all these layers and secrets.

After my first heartbreak, everything got worse. I struggle so much with being alone now. I keep chasing male attention, going through these short situationships and temporary connections. For a little while it feels like they care, and I end up oversharing with them in ways I never do with my friends. Then it ends, and I’m back where I started, maybe even emptier.

I’m not even technically allowed to have a boyfriend because of my background, but I’m also losing faith in love anyway. Social media is full of “rules,” games, and unrealistic expectations. It makes genuine dating and love feel fake. I honestly doubt I’ll ever experience real love.

I don’t really want advice. I just needed to put this somewhere.
