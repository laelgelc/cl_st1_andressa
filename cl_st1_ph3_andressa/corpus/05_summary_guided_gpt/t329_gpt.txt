I’ve felt alone and unloved for over a year now, and I’m starting to lose the desire to keep trying. I keep pushing myself through anxiety to talk to people at work, trying to be friendly and open, but it feels like it just confirms everything I’m afraid of.

Recently a coworker made a joke about how much I talk. On the surface it was “harmless,” but it cut really deep. It made me realize how desperate I’ve become for connection, how much I’m clinging to any bit of interaction. I know I talk a lot because I’m lonely, and having that pointed out, even jokingly, made me feel pathetic and exposed.

The worst part is that I work as a mental health therapist. I spend my days encouraging people to find hope, to connect with themselves, to believe that they’re worthy of love and care. Meanwhile, inside I feel hopeless and ashamed. I do have my own therapist, but there’s this heavy sense of hypocrisy, like I’m a fraud for telling others things I can’t seem to believe for myself.

Since a breakup almost two years ago, I haven’t felt truly happy or excited about anything. I’ve tried to move on with someone new, but it’s not working. I’m pretty sure they don’t really want to be around me anymore, and honestly, I get it. I’m depressed, barely able to take care of myself, and I can’t imagine that being much fun to be around.

I want so badly to be cared for, to feel like someone genuinely wants me in their life. But I’m starting to doubt my ability to consistently care for others in the way they deserve. I feel haunted by my past and the person I’ve been, like there’s something fundamentally unlovable about me.

More and more, I catch myself wondering if I’m just meant to be alone.
