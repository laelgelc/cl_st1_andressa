I’m 20 and I feel like I’m already stuck in place, like my life stalled and never started back up again.

I can do basic stuff: shower, eat, scroll on my phone, watch videos, do chores if I really have to. But whenever I try to focus on anything that feels meaningful or “important,” it’s like my brain hits a wall. I get tired, overwhelmed, and just… shut down. I can’t even stick with something for 15 minutes without feeling this pressure in my chest and then I escape back into mindless things. Then I end up hating myself for it.

I’ve always wanted to create and do something that matters, but I feel like I have no real skills or talents. I kept jumping from hobby to hobby, never sticking with anything long enough to get good. Now it feels like I’m just left with a bunch of half-started things and nothing solid to show for it. I tried learning 3D recently and the same thing happened: I sat down, got overwhelmed so fast, and just quit. Then the cycle of “you’re lazy, you’re broken, you’re useless” starts all over again in my head.

Since COVID, I’ve basically lived in my room. It feels like everyone else’s life kept moving forward while mine just froze. I feel behind in every way — socially, mentally, emotionally. I don’t feel like a real adult, just someone who exists and watches life from the outside.

On top of that, I’ve never had any romantic or sexual experiences at all. It makes me feel unwanted and defective, like there’s something fundamentally wrong with me. I’m scared that if I ever do meet someone, they’ll think my inexperience at this age is weird or a red flag.

I can’t point to any big trauma or clear reason I’m like this. So I keep coming back to the same question: is this just who I am? Am I just lazy?
