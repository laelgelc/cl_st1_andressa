Lately I’ve been thinking about how I show up for people. I’m the one who listens, remembers details, checks in, tries to be dependable. I invest, I care, I make space for others. And then, slowly, I just…fade out of their lives.

It doesn’t feel like I was unimportant. It feels like I was useful. I was there when they needed support, company, a sounding board. But once that need is gone, it’s like I become invisible. Not hated, not rejected—just quietly unnecessary.

It makes me question what friendships and relationships actually are. Were they ever “real,” or were they just temporary stories that only lasted as long as I was serving some purpose? How many of the people we call friends would still be there if we stopped being useful to them?

I’m starting to think loneliness isn’t always about having no one around. Sometimes it’s about realizing how easily you’re replaced once your role is filled. And I suspect that feeling is a lot more common than most people are willing to admit.
