Last November my boyfriend of five years just…disappeared. I told him I loved him, that if he was unhappy I would let him go, and he just never replied. No “goodbye,” no “I’m done,” nothing. He was my whole world, and it feels like he just tossed me away like I was nothing.

I don’t feel like myself anymore. It’s like someone scooped everything out of me and left this empty shell walking around. I keep replaying everything, wondering how someone who meant so much to me could walk away so easily and not even care enough to say a single word. It makes me feel pathetic and unlovable.

Last June I actually tried to end my life. After that, he tried to break up with me. I begged him to stay, and he did, but he changed. He turned cold and cruel, like he resented me for still being there. I guess I should have seen this coming, but it still hurts so much that he could just vanish.

Since he ghosted me, the loneliness has been crushing. I’ve been coping in really unhealthy ways. I can see myself sliding toward alcohol abuse and just general self-destruction, like I’m watching myself from the outside and can’t hit the brakes.

Lately, thinking about dying gives me this weird, fragile sense of comfort, like an escape hatch. I have this voice in my head saying, “Everyone thinks like that sometimes, everyone gets tired of living,” and I don’t know if that’s true or if I’m just lying to myself so I don’t have to admit how bad it is.

I can’t talk about this to anyone in my life. I don’t want to scare them or be a burden. Am I overthinking this? Is this actually “normal,” or is something really wrong with me? I don’t know what to do anymore, and I feel so alone.
