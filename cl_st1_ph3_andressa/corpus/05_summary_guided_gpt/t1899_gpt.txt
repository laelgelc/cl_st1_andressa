Last night I caught myself staring at my phone, wanting to text my best friend Lin like I always do when I feel lonely. But I didn’t.

For a long time, he was the person I’d message late at night, just dumping my thoughts about how empty things feel. Over the past months, his replies slowly got shorter, more spaced out. Three months ago he finally told me he was really busy, under a lot of pressure at work, and couldn’t chat as much.

Since then, I’ve been replaying everything in my head. I think my late-night loneliness talks became another weight on him. He just got promoted, he has a two-year-old child, and his parents aren’t well. When I think about all that, my problems start to feel…small. I’m 30, single, working abroad. No partner, no kids, no one depending on me. Just me and my own thoughts.

So now, when I feel that urge to reach out, I stop myself. I tell myself he doesn’t need my loneliness on top of everything else. But staying silent doesn’t make the loneliness go away; it just makes it heavier, somehow.

I miss when I could just message him without overthinking it. Now I’m stuck between not wanting to burden him and not really having anyone else to talk to.
