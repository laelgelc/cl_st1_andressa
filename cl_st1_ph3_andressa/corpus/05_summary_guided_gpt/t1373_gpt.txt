I’ve been dealing with this chronic disability that makes walking really painful, and the worst part is that the doctors still don’t know what’s wrong. At the beginning I thought I could handle it, but after about six months it all started to hit me harder. Now I just feel empty and lonely most of the time.

I spend almost all day in my room. I don’t really have gaming friends or an online community. I try to distract myself with philosophy, anime, and learning new skills, but it feels like I’m just collecting interests with no one to share them with. It all feels kind of pointless and directionless.

Sometimes I join streams just to hear other people talk, but I’m scared of saying anything because I don’t want to make anyone uncomfortable or drag the mood down. My family keeps telling me to go outside, but walking hurts too much, and they don’t really get that.

Most of my real-life friends have graduated, moved away, and gotten busy with their own lives. I feel like I’d just be burdening them if I reached out. Around last December, when I graduated too, the emotional pain really ramped up, and since then I’ve just felt more and more lost.
