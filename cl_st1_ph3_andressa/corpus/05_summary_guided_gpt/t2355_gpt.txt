I grew up completely immersed in evangelical Christianity. Church multiple times a week, youth group, Bible studies, the whole thing. My “school” was a tiny Christian school where I barely had any real high school peers, and then I went straight into a Christian college. For most of my life, my entire social world was built around church and faith.

In November 2024, after a long and really painful process, I de-converted. It wasn’t some impulsive decision; it was years of questions, doubts, and internal wrestling. Finally admitting to myself that I don’t believe anymore was both a relief and a loss.

The problem is that no one in my real life knows. I still have a few Christian friends from college who live nearby, and they’re basically my only friends. I haven’t told them, and I haven’t told my family, because I’m terrified of what will happen—disappointment, distance, maybe outright rejection. I just don’t feel ready to deal with that yet.

So now I’m stuck in this weird limbo where I feel incredibly lonely and fake. I can’t be honest about who I am or what I think with the only people I have, and it makes every interaction feel strained. At the same time, I have no idea how to find new people I can actually connect with on a genuine level.

Right now, the internet feels like the only place I can say any of this out loud. I guess I’m just getting it off my chest, because keeping it all inside is starting to feel unbearable.
