I just got out of a behavioral health unit after being involuntarily admitted for four days, and I’m feeling really strange and lonely right now.

I had a really bad manic episode that crashed into a deep depression and some pretty serious suicidal thoughts involving pills. I didn’t act on it, but I came close enough that it scared me. I ended up reaching out to my psychiatrist instead, and that’s what landed me in the hospital.

The stay itself was… clinical. Very controlled, very structured. Not good, not exactly bad, just surreal. There were some genuinely kind people there, both patients and staff. There was this quiet sense of understanding, like we were all carrying our own private storms but could at least recognize that in each other. That part helped a little.

At the same time, being a trans woman in that environment was really rough. I couldn’t shave, couldn’t wear makeup, couldn’t dress in a way that felt like me, and my usual self-care routines were basically gone. It made me feel dehumanized and disconnected from my own identity, like I was just a body in a hospital gown instead of myself. The dysphoria hit really hard.

Before I went in, my mania had pushed me into some really unhealthy coping, especially with alcohol, and I ended up hurting people I care about. Since getting out, I’ve tried to take real accountability. I’ve had some hard conversations that were painful but also strangely healing. I feel a bit more grounded now, more aware of my warning signs, and more committed to reaching out for help instead of trying to destroy myself.

Still, now that I’m home, it’s really quiet. Too quiet. I feel isolated and kind of untethered, like I stepped out of this intense, shared experience into a vacuum.

If anyone relates to this or just wants to talk, I’d be open to that. I could really use some connection and maybe some new friends right now.
