I’ve been living by myself for a few months now and the quiet is starting to feel really loud. During the day I can distract myself with work or chores, but it hits me in those private moments – making dinner for one, going to bed, waking up – that I’m really, properly alone.

What makes it worse is knowing that a lot of this is on me. Over the last few years I’ve managed to screw up several friendships and two relationships. I won’t go into details, but I know I hurt people and broke trust, and I’m left with this heavy mix of guilt and anger at myself. It feels like this loneliness is the consequence I “earned,” like this is just what I get now.

My friends tell me things aren’t beyond repair. I am trying to rebuild trust, to show up, to be more consistent and honest. But even when we talk or hang out, I can’t shake the feeling that something is permanently different. The old closeness and effortless consistency we had just isn’t there anymore. I miss that so much it physically hurts sometimes.

I’ve always struggled with change, and this feels like my whole social world has shifted into a new, colder version of itself. I keep trying to stay in touch so I don’t just fade out of people’s lives, but sometimes it feels like I’m forcing it, like I’m clinging to what used to be instead of accepting what it is now.

What I find hardest is that this isn’t some random bad luck; it’s loneliness that came from my own decisions. It’s a really specific kind of pain to look around and know I played a big part in ending up here.

I can’t afford therapy right now, so I’m just trying to sit with this and accept that this might be my new reality for a while. I’m trying to forgive myself, but the regret and self-directed anger are still so strong. I don’t even know what I’m looking for posting this – maybe just to say it out loud somewhere, because keeping it in my head all the time is exhausting.
