English isn’t my first language, so I hope this makes sense.

I live with depression, a lot of sensitivity, sluggish cognitive tempo, social anxiety, and I have a diagnosed ASD. There is also a possibility of inattentive ADHD. Because of all this, it feels like my perception of reality is warped and very limited. I feel like I had to “abandon” my ego, because whenever I follow it, I just fall into some mental abyss. It becomes a kind of ignorant suffering that I can’t escape from.

Sometimes it feels like parts of my brain are simply inactive. Without a very strong self-awareness, life becomes almost unbearable. I don’t trust my own thoughts, feelings, or experiences. I reject them as if they’re all lies. As a coping mechanism, I even sometimes think other people might be “soulless,” like they are not really there in the way I am. I know it sounds strange, but it’s a way to survive mentally.

I always need stimulation. If there is none, I feel empty and really bad inside. My memory is poor and I doubt myself so much that I can’t keep stable beliefs about anything. I feel like I get swept along by other people’s opinions and emotions, like a toddler who doesn’t know what is happening. I don’t know what to believe, how to interpret emotions like guilt, happiness, sadness, or even how to see my own accomplishments.

I hate that my world is completely constructed by my own mind. It makes me feel like a forsaken existence, trapped inside my own head. I feel like I can’t truly communicate with anyone, because everything I say and think just turns into a self-referential loop. It all comes back to me and my broken perception.

Sometimes I fantasize about becoming a recluse, cutting myself off from society and just living a life of pure self-indulgence, where I don’t have to care about anyone or anything outside my small world.

Even with all of this, I don’t feel completely alone, because I believe in God. That belief is maybe the only thing that makes me feel like I am not totally abandoned.
