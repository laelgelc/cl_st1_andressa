I’ve been feeling this really heavy, persistent loneliness lately. My therapist keeps telling me a lot of their clients are saying the same thing, and I see it in people around me too. It’s not just single people or people who are “failing at life.” I have a married friend who’s deeply lonely. I know people who are basically millionaires and people who are on the edge of homelessness, and the common thread is still this sense of being alone.

That’s part of what makes it so confusing. It clearly isn’t just about money or “getting your life together.” I try to live with some kind of community mindset, to compromise, to not make everything about me. I don’t buy into the idea that loneliness is just a result of people being socially awkward, lazy, or not trying hard enough. It feels deeper than that.

To me, real connection means mutual risk and uncertainty. It means letting go of a bit of control, opening up your inner world and trusting someone else not to trample it. But it feels like society treats relationships like corporate risk management now. One “red flag,” one imperfect moment, and people just cut you off. Everyone’s protecting their inner world like it’s a fragile asset, and I get why, but it makes everything feel cold and transactional.

I’m not innocent in this either. I’ve definitely caught myself thinking in that same language: “This person doesn’t serve me,” and then pulling away. I hate that phrasing, but it’s in my head anyway. It makes me wonder if I’m giving up on people too quickly, if I should be more patient and leave more room for imperfection and growth.

At the same time, I feel weirdly disposable. A lot of interactions feel like people are more interested in my job or what I can do for them than in who I actually am. It’s like my career status is the main thing people see, and underneath that I’m just… interchangeable.

This is mostly a rant, but if anyone relates, I’d really like to hear how you cope with this. How do you deal with feeling used, or like everyone’s guarding themselves so tightly that nothing real can get through?
