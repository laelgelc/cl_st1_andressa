I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into any of the usual categories people talk about.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers I get along with, a couple of old friends I still message sometimes, family chats that light up my phone. But it all feels thin, like tracing paper versions of real connections. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m watching everything through glass.

What bothers me most is how hard it is to actually be known. Conversations stay on safe topics: work, shows, random memes. I don’t know how to transition from that to “here’s what I’m actually thinking about at 2 a.m. when I can’t sleep.” And when I do try to open up a little, I feel like I’m oversharing or making things awkward, so I retreat again.

There’s also this growing sense that I’m falling behind socially. Other people seem to build friendships and relationships that deepen over time. Mine feel like they stall out at “acquaintance” and just linger there indefinitely. I keep wondering if there was some stage in life where everyone else learned how to do this and I just…missed the class.

I’m not sure what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put it into words somewhere, instead of letting it sit in my head as this vague, unnamed ache that follows me through the day.
