I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m disappearing in plain sight.

I’m at that weird age where everyone around me seems to be moving on to the “next stage” – partners, kids, real careers, solid friend groups – and I’m just kind of…floating. I talk to people at work, I answer emails, I nod in meetings, I say “have a good one” when I leave. On paper it looks like I interact with people all day. But when I go home, it’s just silence. No one texts. No one calls. If my phone lights up, it’s a notification from some app, not a person.

The worst part is that I don’t even have a “reason” to give if someone asked what’s wrong. Nothing catastrophic happened. No big breakup, no huge fight, no move to a new city where I don’t know anyone. It was more like a slow erosion. Friends got busy, plans got cancelled, group chats went quiet, and I didn’t push back. I told myself I was fine being alone, that I preferred it. I think I repeated that so many times I started to believe it, right up until the loneliness became impossible to ignore.

I’ve tried the usual things – hobbies, meetup apps, saying yes more often – but it always feels like I’m on the outside of a joke I don’t get. People already have their circles, their shared history, their inside references. I’m the extra chair at the table.

What scares me is how quickly I’m getting used to it. I’ve started talking less at work because I know the conversation ends when I walk out the door anyway. I catch myself lying when people ask “How are you?” because the truthful answer feels like too much for casual small talk.

I don’t really know what I’m looking for by writing this. Maybe I just needed to put into words that I’m here, that I exist, even if it doesn’t feel like it most days.
