A 31-year-old woman says she’s doing all the “right” things—hobbies, working out, self-care, trying to make friends—but none of it feels fulfilling without being in love. She feels numb and partly “asleep” without a romantic connection, yet refuses to lower her standards just to have someone. She gives 100% when she likes someone and has “so much love to give,” but using that love on herself doesn’t feel the same, leaving her feeling both “too much and not enough.” She believes she’ll eventually find someone and that her love won’t go to waste, but the empty waiting for that person is very painful. She ends by wishing others warmth and comfort on a Friday night.
