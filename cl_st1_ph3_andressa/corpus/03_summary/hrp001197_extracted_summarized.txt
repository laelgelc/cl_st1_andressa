The writer is in a very emotional state and trying to keep their self-respect. They feel that people in their life lack emotional intelligence and that they no longer feel love for their longtime best friend. They’re exhausted from always being the helper—mentor, therapist, teacher—and never being truly heard or met with the same depth they offer others. They feel used as an emotional wall, blamed when they don’t meet others’ expectations, and see connection more as a duty than something warm or enjoyable. They confuse basic empathy with genuine intimate care and feel their role has always been to manage others’ emotions. Now their desire for company feels like a basic human need rather than something emotionally fulfilling, and they feel lonely in a detached, almost mechanical way.
