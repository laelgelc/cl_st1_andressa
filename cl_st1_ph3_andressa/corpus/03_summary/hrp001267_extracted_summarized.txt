The poster blames ordinary users for contributing to the “loneliness epidemic” by staying behind screens, giving only angry or shallow responses, and rarely offering meaningful discussion, likes, or support. They argue that platforms are heavily censored and no longer allow deep, human conversations that make people feel seen and heard. People share projects and ideas but receive almost no engagement unless they are famous, which concentrates attention at the top. The poster criticizes commenters for not caring about real connection and only showing anger or pity, saying genuine human communication is dying and leaving only extremists and emotionless “robots,” while insisting that real humans are still here and numerous.
