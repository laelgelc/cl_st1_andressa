The poster is 24 and recently got out of prison for drug-related charges they committed when they were younger. Before prison, life felt good socially: many people wanted to be around them, especially women, though they were also dealing with addiction and insecurity rooted in their upbringing. Now, after release, they feel like they have no one and keep checking their phone even though nobody contacts them. When they talk to women now, they feel rejected because of their past, which they understand but still find painful. Despite the loneliness and difficulty of starting over, they feel blessed to be out, happy to have a second chance, and determined to do things right this time.
