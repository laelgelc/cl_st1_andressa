The poster talks about how many young (and not-so-young) men struggle to find partners, despite not having anything fundamentally wrong with them. They note that self-help advice from male and female influencers doesn’t seem to solve the issue, and that women’s expectations are often vague and hard for men to understand or meet. They think it’s likely more people, especially men, will remain long-term single.

They point out that discussions usually focus on men’s grief, women’s disappointment, and coping with loneliness, and that there are many channels encouraging women to celebrate being single and thrive through activities and social/support groups. The poster asks what equivalent positive paths, activities, and networks could exist for single men with no dating prospects so they can not just cope but thrive.

They argue that calling this a “male crisis” is stigmatizing, and instead see men as being in a difficult position due to rapid technological and social changes beyond their control. They emphasize that this is an unprecedented time and invite answers from both men and women on how men can build supportive networks and fulfilling lives while single.
