A 34-year-old man describes feeling lonely for most of his adult life. After his father died when he was 17, he took on adult responsibilities, sacrificing his own interests to support his mother and younger sister financially. He feels that people in his life, including romantic partners and even his own family, have mainly wanted what he could provide rather than valuing him as a person. When he faced financial hardship and brief unemployment, his mother and sister treated him as disposable despite all he had done for them. He now lives alone, has not seen his family in about four years, and they rarely contact him. His experiences have damaged his views on love and marriage, and he cannot remember the last time he was happy. He shared his thoughts because he feels no one in his life wants to spend time with him.
