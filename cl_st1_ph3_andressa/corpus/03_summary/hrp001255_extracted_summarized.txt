A woman in her 30s says she has only ever had one relationship, a bad six-year one, and now has no family, no friends, and no social life. She feels completely alone and especially misses romantic connection and physical affection, like being touched or hugged. She’s frustrated when men claim women can easily find partners, because that doesn’t match her experience—when she goes out, no one seems interested in her, and she sees herself as not very interesting. At night she finds some comfort in sleep, because in her dreams she isn’t lonely and often dreams about her childhood family life.
