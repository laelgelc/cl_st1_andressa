The poster is a 60-year-old transfeminine person who only fully admitted being transgender about 8 months ago, though they’ve wanted to be a woman for over a decade. They grew up in a strict Catholic family and spent 44 years in the Army, a very masculine environment. They’ve been married for 30 years and have three adult sons. They believe their wife, mother, and most siblings would not accept them as trans, though some of their sons and most nieces and nephews (including one transmasc) might.

They feel unable to come out because their wife has serious physical and mental health issues, and they fear the consequences (like divorce or worse) for her. They are resigned to continuing to present a false persona for the rest of their life, feeling that the “real” self will remain hidden, unable to connect or be touched by anyone. This leaves them feeling profoundly lonely and destined to be alone internally, and they see no real solution beyond enduring it. Writing the post is a small attempt to ease that deep loneliness, even though they doubt it will help.
