The writer describes late-night moments of deep stillness when thoughts quiet down and heavy feelings emerge. They see their solitude as armor they’ve built from pain, giving them a sense of vast inner space and self-contained wholeness. But when the feelings linger, that solitude reveals itself as distance and loneliness, making their carefully constructed identity feel fragile and at risk of dissolving. The loneliness doesn’t attack but waits, which feels unbearable. Despite this, they refuse to seek temporary relief or compromise their integrity, choosing instead to endure as a way of remaining themselves.
