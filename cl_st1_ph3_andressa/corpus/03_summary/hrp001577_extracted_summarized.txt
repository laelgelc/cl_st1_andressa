The poster describes having been extremely isolated in the past to the point of feeling like they were losing their mind from lack of conversation. Things have changed somewhat, but they still have no real friends and only brief online interactions. They feel a strong need for frequent contact and conversation but find making friends nearly impossible because they see themselves as fundamentally different from “regular” people and unable or unwilling to conform or pretend. They can only connect with others “like them,” but that requires specific circumstances that aren’t present. Attempts to befriend typical people feel fake and mentally harmful, like forcing oneself into a role that doesn’t fit. They feel they cannot fit in, cannot conform, and yet desperately need people they can simply exist around, making the whole situation deeply isolating.
