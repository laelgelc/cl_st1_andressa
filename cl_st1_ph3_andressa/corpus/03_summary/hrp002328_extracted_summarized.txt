An 18-year-old woman describes being shy her whole life, but says it has recently become a bigger problem. She used to have a close childhood friend group, but they changed, got into bad influences, and constantly caused stressful drama, including bringing around guys who didn’t respect her boundary of keeping some distance from men. She eventually cut them off, which reduced her stress but left her feeling lonely. Now she only has some mutuals she’s comfortable with but not close to, and she sometimes misses her old friends despite remembering why she left. She’s trying to be more social and meet new people, and has met some genuinely kind people, but her shyness makes her seem uninterested: she struggles with eye contact, talking, overthinks a lot, and tends to avoid attention even when she doesn’t want to.
