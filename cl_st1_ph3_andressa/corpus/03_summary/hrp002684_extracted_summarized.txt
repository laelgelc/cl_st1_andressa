The post shares a quote from John O’Donohue (referencing Thomas à Kempis) about learning to inhabit one’s aloneness. It says that seeking distraction and false belonging in society leads to emptiness, but facing and accepting aloneness gradually transforms bleakness into a sense of true belonging and inner “home.” This inner grounding is not selfish; once you rest in yourself, you can connect with others more genuinely and creatively, without needing constant affirmation. The process is slow and can take years, but it is essential for coming into rhythm with your own individuality.
