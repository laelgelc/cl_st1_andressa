The poster, who has epilepsy usually controlled by medication, describes having one of their worst seizure episodes: they fell and injured their nose, then continued seizing so badly they had to crawl to their bedroom to reach their medicine and water, and lay there seizing until the medication took effect. They feel that no one knows or cares about what they go through and, in their mid-30s, they fear that their future holds only more illness and loneliness.
