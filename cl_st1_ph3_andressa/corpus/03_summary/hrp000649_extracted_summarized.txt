The writer is lying alone in a dark room late at night, crying and feeling deeply misunderstood and unsupported, even by their own family. They feel they have no one to call, no friends, no partner, and no one who truly understands them. Their family imposes decisions instead of trying to understand them. They feel empty, “dying inside,” and are struggling to even recognize basic needs like hunger because their head feels so heavy and painful. They are desperate for validation, kindness, understanding people, and someone who can help bring them into a “brighter world,” and they pray that God will send such a person into their life.
