The poster is a third-year math–computer science student who has worked extremely hard, becoming top of their class and even one of the top students in their country. Despite the recognition and praise, they feel empty and lonely. They describe how classmates only become friendly and seek them out during exam periods for help, then disappear afterward, including someone they had a crush on who seemed to like them only for their notes. This pattern has made them realize they have no real friends or love, just people using them for academic help. They feel their success cost them weekends, relationships, peace of mind, and a normal, happy youth. They say they would trade all their achievements for one genuine friend, real laughter, and true warmth or love, questioning the value of success when there’s no one to share it with.
