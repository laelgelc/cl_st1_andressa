A 30-year-old single dad feels deeply lonely and believes he’ll never meet “the one.” He sees others in relationships, including at church and among his siblings, while he has no real friends and even family rarely visits unless he makes the effort. He struggles with a poor job and no education, partly due to past suicidality and not expecting to live this long. Although people tell him he’s attractive, he’s miserable, refuses casual sex, and just wants genuine companionship, affection, and rest.
