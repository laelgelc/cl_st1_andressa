They’re in a small British school year where everyone knows each other; they’re on good terms with many classmates but don’t feel they have much in common, and with only about a year left before college, they don’t feel motivated to build new friendships.

Their main concern is that acquaintances often ask where their old friend group is when they see them alone, and they don’t want to explain the situation or revisit the drama. Because of COVID restrictions, their year is confined to a small area, so people will easily notice they’re by themselves. They’re asking how to deflect attention and questions while still being able to stay alone and “do their own thing.”
