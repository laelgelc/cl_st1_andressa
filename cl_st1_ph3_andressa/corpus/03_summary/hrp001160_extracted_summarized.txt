A 22-year-old woman describes a lifelong pattern of social struggles and feeling left out of friend groups, starting in childhood (not being invited to birthday parties, being alone at recess) and continuing into adulthood (not being invited to outings like a New Year’s party or clubbing, even when it’s something she’s wanted to do since turning 21). She recently broke up with her girlfriend of three years, with whom she shares friends and still lives, which complicates things, but she felt excluded even before the breakup. When she assumed she was included in plans based on past years, she was told she shouldn’t have assumed. This repeated exclusion makes her feel unlikable and as if something is wrong with her that others see but she doesn’t. Although friends verbally reassure her that she isn’t annoying and that they value her, their actions don’t match, and she wonders if it’s almost intentional. She says she sometimes feels it would be easier to have no friends than to constantly experience FOMO and rejection, and she asks if others have felt this way and how they cope with being left out so often.
