The poster was raised in extreme isolation by parents who believed friends were unnecessary and dangerous, linking friendship to “evildoers” and communism. As an adult, they have no real friends and have instead paid people for companionship, believing that anything others use friends for can be bought with money. Now that they are older and want to save money, they no longer want to pay for people’s time, have cut those people off, and are feeling what others call loneliness. They still pay an ex monthly because she treats their past arrangement like a job and threatens court if payments stop. The poster concludes that loneliness isn’t real in itself; they see the real issue as money—when they stop paying, the companionship disappears—so they believe what feels like loneliness is actually just not being able or willing to pay for others’ time.
