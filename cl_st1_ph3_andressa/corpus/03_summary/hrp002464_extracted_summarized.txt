A 31-year-old man with BPD says he’s accepted that he’ll be alone forever. He’s turned down casual sex because he wants a deeper relationship, but believes he can’t handle one: he loves too intensely, fears giving someone the power to destroy him, and feels his diagnosis means he can’t have a relationship. Once a hopeless romantic who loved romcoms and Twilight, he’s spent years trying to make peace with this “fate,” yet every week or two he’s overwhelmed by intense heartbreak and grief that can last from 30 minutes to 3 hours, leaving him lying in silence and crying. He’s exhausted by this recurring emotional pain.
