The poster is asking how others understand loneliness: whether it’s just being alone or also feeling unseen even around people. They describe their own loneliness as not having anyone with whom they can share their true self, only managing small talk or limited deeper talk, and never feeling truly known or able to be vulnerable. They also struggle with repeated romantic or personal rejection, which makes them question whether it’s worth opening up again and sometimes prefer feeling “comfortably numb” and alone. They specifically invite people over 40, though everyone is welcome, to share how loneliness affects them mentally, physically, and emotionally, and how they find the conviction to keep trying after rejection.
