A 34-year-old man, recently separated and co-parenting an 18‑month‑old child, is spending New Year’s Eve at home with the child while his ex is out celebrating with her new partner. He accepts the breakup, says co‑parenting is respectful, and doesn’t blame her, but feels mixed emotions: gratitude for time with his child alongside loneliness, sadness, and a sense of being left behind. He’s asking if these feelings are normal after a separation with a child involved, how others would feel in his place, and for advice on coping with nights like this in a healthy way.
