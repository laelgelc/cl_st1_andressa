The post summarizes an online hangout about organizing an action-oriented, decentralized effort around loneliness. Participants discussed creating a background organization, seeking input from experts (academics, practitioners, authors, insurers) and from people directly affected by loneliness. They debated whether to use positive wording like “purpose” versus explicitly saying “loneliness,” and agreed to mention loneliness explicitly but pair it with positive terms, especially “purpose.” They noted that new people have been joining each time. Next steps include using a shared Google Drive, developing a basic website with content in progress, and continuing to talk to people and integrate feedback. The next meetup is scheduled for Saturday, May 12th at the usual time, though attendance is expected to be low.
