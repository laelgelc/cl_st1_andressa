The poster describes becoming emotionally numb to their loneliness. They spent high school without friends and used to cry about it almost daily, but now they’re so used to being alone that they don’t cry over it anymore. They still sometimes think about what they’re missing—like having someone to sit with in lectures, a friend group to eat lunch with, or social events to look forward to—and find it hard to grasp that other people regularly see friends and have social lives. They feel unable to imagine their life being any different from this ongoing isolation.
