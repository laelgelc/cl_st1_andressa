The writer describes a recent breakup with a man she deeply loved and trusted. At first he was caring, but over time he changed: he became agitated, stopped calling, blocked her, threatened to remove her from his life, verbally abused her, and kept her in the dark about his whereabouts. This caused her intense emotional pain, panic attacks, and self-harm.

When he finally left and sent a last text, she simply turned off her phone, didn’t argue, and shed a single tear. Since then, she feels strangely “normal,” unable to cry or understand her own emotions. She recently saw him in their neighborhood (they live across from each other); he looked happy and was smiling, and she smiled too but hid so he wouldn’t see her. She still wants him back but is afraid to turn on her phone in case he hasn’t messaged her. She feels she has no one to talk to about this and suggests she might never come online again after posting.
