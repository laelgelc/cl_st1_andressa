The writer reflects on how they took having people around for granted during school, the military, and past jobs, only realizing their importance now that they’re older and running a startup alone for six months. Surrounded by people in public yet feeling unable to truly talk to anyone, they long to share their story and hear others’ stories but find it “impossibly hard.” They describe genuine listening and mutual sharing as rare and precious, question whether loneliness is poison or medicine, and end by quoting the idea that letting someone “talk alone” is the cruelest thing you can do—urging others to listen, because it might save a life.
