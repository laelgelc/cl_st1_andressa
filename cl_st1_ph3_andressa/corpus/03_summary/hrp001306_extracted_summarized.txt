An 18-year-old feels lonely and dissatisfied with their life compared to their mostly extroverted, happy-seeming friends. At school they look forward to going home to play video games and have recently tried working out, but once home they feel like they have nothing going on and struggle to naturally meet people, partly due to their school environment. A dating attempt with a girl they liked ended unexpectedly, after which they became addicted to nicotine. They’re now wondering if their loneliness is actually made worse by unhealthy coping habits like nicotine, porn (“g.oning”), laziness, and drinking, despite having a few good friends, a great family, and attending the school they wanted. They apologize for their English and for venting, and wish readers a good day.
