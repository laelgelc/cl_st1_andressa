The writer uses an extended metaphor of a bank and a vault to describe wanting to invest years of their life and hide themselves inside someone they can trust. They long for safety, security, and protection from a hostile world, but are deeply unsure whether this person is truly safe, open, or trustworthy. They question who has been let in or out before, whether their vulnerability will be protected or exploited, and fear being forgotten or reduced to something merely stored and counted rather than genuinely cared for.
