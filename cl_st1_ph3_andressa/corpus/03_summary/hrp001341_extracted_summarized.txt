The writer describes a deep, long-term relationship with loneliness, saying they have feared it, faced it, and even befriended it. Over time, loneliness has become a source of strength, comfort, and a kind of home. In response to people who say they can’t live alone forever, the writer questions what “forever” means and asserts that they have already lived alone for as long as they can remember and believe they can continue to do so indefinitely.
