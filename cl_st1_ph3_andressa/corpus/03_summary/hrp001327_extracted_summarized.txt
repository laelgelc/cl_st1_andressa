The poster longs for an idealized, “stereotypical teenage” close friendship: staying up all night gaming, watching movies, sneaking out, drinking, smoking, and talking deeply about books, art, philosophy, and life, as well as going to cinemas and making art together. They want a trustworthy person with a similar mindset who would support and motivate them. They feel lonely, drained, and disappointed when they try to commit to relationships, have little energy to socialize, and struggle to find people who actually want to hang out. They’re extremely introverted, sometimes feel a strong urge to have fun with someone, and recognize they tend to sabotage connections even if they did “belong” somewhere. Despite repressing their need for connection, they admit they still need it, and they end with a bleak, somewhat sarcastic Christmas wish and a reference to substance use.
