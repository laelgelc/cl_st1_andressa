A 19-year-old gay man, who has never had a serious relationship and feels awkward and lonely, keeps talking to his friends about the cheap, warped Valentine’s teddy bears at his local pharmacy, half-joking but secretly hoping someone will get him one platonically. He feels pathetic for wanting someone to notice and care about small things like that. With Valentine’s Day coming up, he’s considering buying himself a little gift basket with heart-shaped chocolate, an ugly teddy, and a bedside plant to fulfill an old middle school dream, but he worries it will just remind him of his loneliness and the fact he bought it for himself. He feels stuck between going through with it or just sadly staring at the Valentine’s section whenever he picks up his medication.
