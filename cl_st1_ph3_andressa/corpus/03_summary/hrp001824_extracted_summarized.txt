The writer describes feeling socially excluded and awkward since childhood, struggling to understand social cues and getting used to being lonely. They say people gave them weird looks when they were nice, which led them to believe something was wrong with them and to feel ugly inside and out. This negative self-image has stayed with them into the present. They’ve never had anyone to open up to because they fear judgment and having their feelings used against them, and not having friends has worsened their feelings and mental health, even though they’ve become accustomed to being alone.
