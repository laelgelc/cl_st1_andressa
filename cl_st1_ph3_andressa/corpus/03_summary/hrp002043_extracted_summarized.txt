A 25-year-old man with borderline personality disorder describes feeling utterly alone and socially rejected. He recently received a significant double promotion at work, but it feels empty because he has no one in his personal life to share the news with. Although coworkers are proud of him, coming home to silence makes the achievement feel meaningless. He longs for someone who is genuinely excited about him and his life, recognizing that this intense need for attention and affection is part of his BPD. He ends with a bittersweet, half-hearted “Go me, I guess.”
