The writer shares a poem written during a very difficult, lonely period. They long for rest, comfort, affection, and a sense of home and belonging—someone to hug them, wipe their tears, know them, and love them as they are. They have no parents to turn to for advice or kindness and feel they must be their own home, which often doesn’t feel like enough. Although others see them as mature and responsible, they have no long-term, blood-related family to call. Most of the time they manage alone, but holidays and random days hit especially hard, when the loneliness feels overwhelming. They conclude that if family is what truly counts, their lack of it leaves them questioning who they are.
