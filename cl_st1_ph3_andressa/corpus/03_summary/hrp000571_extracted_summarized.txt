The writer feels like an outcast everywhere and has always been on the sidelines, never treated as a real person or checked on by others. They tried to “be themselves” and also to be like everyone else, but nothing worked, and they believe they’ll never be an ordinary person.

They currently live in a country at war, where they lost their home and relatives and feel constant stress and danger with no safe place to go and no realistic way to leave. They have only temporary housing and fear eviction and homelessness, especially since they feel nobody needs them.

They describe having long‑standing insecurities and illnesses from stress, now worsened by PTSD from the war. They see themselves as ordinary, unattractive, not rich, and socially anxious, afraid of conflict and unsure how to live in society. They feel very bad, unsupported, unloved, and fear they won’t survive alone. They also mention living in a country with low living standards, corruption, low wages, high prices, and social insecurity. At the end, they say that if someone feels the same way, they’d like to chat.
