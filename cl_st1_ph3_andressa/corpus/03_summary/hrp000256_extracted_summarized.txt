The poster is a 40-year-old who feels worn out and is just going through the motions of life. They say their life hasn’t been especially hard compared to others, but it has always felt lackluster. They were born with learning disabilities, struggled to function at a “normal” level, and were shamed, abused, and sometimes dehumanized for it.  

At age 11 they realized their peers loathed them for being different, and they became a loner, barely speaking and avoiding others—especially girls/women—because they believed that’s what people wanted. This continued until age 26. Even after joining the Army, they found that people seem to like them more the less they talk or are seen. They feel they naturally bore or repel others and now try not to “inflict” themselves on people, accepting their situation as the result of choices they made earlier in life.
