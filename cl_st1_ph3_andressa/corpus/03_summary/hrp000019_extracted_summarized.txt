The writer feels like an outsider who can’t claim their own space and often believes they’re taking up space that belongs to others, both among Americans and Indians. They mostly stay in their own “cocoon,” spending time reading, writing, watching movies, and talking to themselves, which helps them process their thoughts. They don’t necessarily feel lonely and are comfortable being alone, but they feel like a loser and lost, believing they have potential yet not truly pursuing any of their many ideas. They seek advice but don’t really value it, question whether they know themselves, and assume others see them as a loser because they feel they haven’t achieved anything. They describe life as preparing for a marathon they may never get to run and end by saying they have no friends.
