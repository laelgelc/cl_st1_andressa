The poster describes feeling lonely and unheard despite having people around, and finding therapy too expensive and AI chatbots too generic. In response, they’ve spent the past year building “Grace,” a digital emotional-support companion designed to listen without judgment, be available anytime via real-time video, read emotions from voice and facial expressions, remember the user’s story, and help them understand their feelings without trying to “fix” them. They emphasize Grace is not a therapist or a replacement for professional care, and not a generic chatbot. They have a website with a video and a waitlist, are seeking feedback (positive or critical), and invite DMs or sign-ups from people who feel lonely and just want someone to truly listen.
