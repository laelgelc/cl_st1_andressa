The writer describes feeling deeply hurt and forgotten after no close friends wished them a happy birthday this year. A girl they’ve talked to for four years didn’t remember their birthday, and a close friend of nine years who knows their birthday also didn’t say anything, despite later asking for help sending a gift to his girlfriend. The writer feels replaced and no longer a priority in people’s lives, especially as others enter relationships. It’s not about gifts but about a simple acknowledgment that would show they still matter. They question whether they care or expect too much, but are pained by realizing they don’t hold the same place in others’ hearts anymore.
