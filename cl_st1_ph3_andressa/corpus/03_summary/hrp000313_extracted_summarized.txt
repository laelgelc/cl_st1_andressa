The poster reflects that people who perceive life’s depth and complexity often end up walking alone, suggesting solitude can be the “cost of awareness” rather than emptiness. As one becomes more aware of layers in people, intentions, moments, pain, and beauty, they may outgrow superficial “noise.” The writer argues that not everyone who is alone is lost; some are simply seeing more than others. They reassure anyone who is alone that they might not be behind in life, but instead ahead, quietly observing reality, and present this as their personal understanding.
