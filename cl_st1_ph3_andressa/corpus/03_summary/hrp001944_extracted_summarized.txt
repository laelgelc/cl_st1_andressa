29-year-old man feels extremely isolated, going days without speaking to anyone. He has no friends or acquaintances and very limited contact with family, who only reach out when they need something. He feels too drained to pursue social activities, and past attempts haven’t led anywhere. Academia (PhD, multiple master’s degrees) is the only thing that keeps him going, but he feels it may have been a waste and that he has no one to share it with. His 9–5 job drains his spirit, and he’s afraid his life will stay this lonely for the next 50+ years, even though he tries to tell himself he’s prepared for that.
