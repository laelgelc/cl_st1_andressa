A 26-year-old man feels constantly lonely and like he doesn’t fit in anywhere. He goes through a routine of college, gym, work, football, and home but feels he’s just going through the motions rather than really living. Although he talks to people at college, work, and football, he doesn’t see them as real friends and feels like a third wheel, as if people talk to him but not with him. Even in group settings he feels left out and isolated. He wonders if anyone besides his immediate family would notice if he disappeared for a while and believes the world would just keep going without him.
