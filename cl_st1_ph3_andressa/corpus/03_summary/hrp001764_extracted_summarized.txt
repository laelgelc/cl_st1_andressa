The poster is a 27-year-old who has felt intensely lonely their whole life and feels they never truly “fit” or become anyone’s priority. They’ve learned how to talk to people and sometimes make friends, but those connections never last and people always leave. They feel excluded, not thought of, not invited, and can’t understand what is wrong with them that others don’t seem to want them around. When they asked their current friend group directly if they’d done something wrong or why people don’t like hanging out with them, they were given vague “we’re too busy” answers, which they know aren’t true because those friends make plans and text each other without including them, even right in front of them. They are frustrated, hurt, and desperate to understand why no one seems to want them.
