A 20-year-old man, who is a writer/director, reflects on the global “loneliness epidemic” and his own loneliness. He believes there is a kind of beauty in loneliness if it isn’t “wasted.” He encourages people to channel their loneliness into any form of creativity—writing, drawing, photography, cooking, sports, science—emphasizing that the result doesn’t need to be good; the act of creating itself is what matters. He sees this as a way to turn tragedy into beauty and to make one’s own world, and possibly the larger world, a little better. He acknowledges this isn’t a cure for loneliness but thinks “a little beauty goes a long way,” and invites others to comment or message him, even if they disagree or think he’s “crazy.”
