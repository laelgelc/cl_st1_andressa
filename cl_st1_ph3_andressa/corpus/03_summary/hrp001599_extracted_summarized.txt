The writer reflects on “why care” about life and the future, feeling unable to express their thoughts and being misunderstood. They use a metaphor of the moon, whose unseen work keeps people safe but goes unappreciated, until a lone wolf howls in admiration. The moon stays because she feels valued, and the wolf stays awake to keep her company; they find mutual worth in each other but eventually must part. This leads the writer back to a sense of futility, questioning what the point of caring is if everything ends.
