The writer moved to another country to gain more freedom as a gay person but struggles to make friends and find dates, feeling uncool and unattractive. Others seem to like them only superficially as the “kind foreigner,” never as someone’s close person. They feel very lonely and say that what keeps them going is a sense of duty and attachment to their family and heritage, even though their parents are disappointed in them for being “not normal.” They express a wish to be reborn in a better situation in the next life.
