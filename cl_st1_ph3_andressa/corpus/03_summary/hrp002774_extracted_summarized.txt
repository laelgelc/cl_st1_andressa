The writer shares a poem and reflections about choosing a solitary, independent path: making their own rules, carrying their own weight, and “walking free,” even though it can be frightening and not as ideal as it seems. They describe feeling different from others, like oil in a bucket of water, suggesting they don’t fit in but aren’t inherently wrong—just in the wrong place. They question the value of small talk, doubt, and fear when they feel they lack self-worth and life feels meaningless. They conclude that if failure seems inevitable, one might as well try, using the knowledge of likely failure to prepare and reduce the pain, which they frame as a kind of success.
