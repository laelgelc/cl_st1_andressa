The writer recently broke up with their first lover, someone they met online 5 years ago and grew up with from their teens into young adulthood. Although they didn’t cry, they felt a deep, confusing chest pain. Their past includes sexual trauma and parents in a bad marriage, which made them doubt love was real until this relationship. They had long anticipated the breakup but didn’t expect it so soon, and the silence afterward highlighted how lonely their life has become.

They previously lost their best friends of 6 years in 11th grade and felt lonely then, but their family (mother and two brothers) supported them with trips and movies. Now, that family closeness has faded for reasons they can’t clearly identify, and time together feels formal and distant. This makes it hard when their therapist suggests spending time with family, because they feel it will never be the same.

They experience both pain and comfort in their loneliness. They want to meet new people and hear different perspectives but are afraid of being hurt again, since every relationship eventually ends and they fear how much that ending might hurt.
