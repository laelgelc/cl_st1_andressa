The poster has two close childhood friends but feels that without them they would be completely alone. They say no one has ever shown spontaneous interest in getting to know them; they were always the one to initiate friendships, and now they’re too afraid to approach new people. In groups they feel invisible, like a ghost whose absence wouldn’t matter. Because no one has ever been interested in them romantically or socially, the idea that someone might like them or confess feelings feels unreal and even disgusting to imagine. They still hope not to be alone forever, but believe that will only change if they themselves take all the initiative.
