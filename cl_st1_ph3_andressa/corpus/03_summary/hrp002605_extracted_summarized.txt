18-year-old woman describes growing up very shy with motor/kinetic difficulties, which kept her from playing and socializing with other kids. She spent time alone collecting rocks and living in a fantasy world, which led to bullying and alienation. She feels she never fully learned normal social etiquette and still doesn’t feel integrated into any community, often feeling like she’s “performing” femininity rather than genuinely expressing it. Small talk and everyday topics feel unnatural; she prefers deep conversations about philosophy or art, but others often go quiet when she tries. When she does meet people who enjoy such conversations, the connection feels rare and precious, though usually temporary. She believes people who grow up largely alone may develop different thinking patterns and ways of processing information, which can lead to strengths in academic, economic, or artistic areas rather than social skills. She sees these differences as inherent rather than simply “intelligence” and invites others to share their thoughts.
