The poster is about to turn 38 with no birthday plans and feels very lonely despite being in a relationship. Their partner doesn’t know or remember basic things about them and is their only “friend,” which makes the loneliness worse. They miss having close, genuine friends who truly know them, enjoy quiet, cozy activities together, and share their interests like Harry Potter marathons, coffee dates, horseback riding, and spontaneous trips. 

They lost their old friends years ago due to depression, an abusive relationship, a psychotic break, and the demands of being a single mother to two children. As their kids have grown and formed their own friendships, the poster feels increasingly alone and fears being completely isolated as life goes on. They long for real connection, feel afraid to open up again, but are starting to think it might be time—though they don’t know where to begin.
