30-year-old woman from South Africa describes intense, physically painful loneliness and nostalgia for her high school and 20s, often triggered by small things like the first seconds of a song. She used to be social but after lockdown and four cases of COVID, she developed severe social anxiety, lost most of her friends, and now sees others moving forward while she feels stuck. She hoards memories of happy times, struggles with depression and anxiety, studies full-time online to become a counsellor with almost no in-person contact, and recently experienced burnout. Weight gain has hurt her self-esteem but increased her empathy for others who overeat. She used to rely on alcohol to feel social but quit due to its interaction with antidepressants, fear of alcoholism (which runs in her family), past negative consequences of lowered inhibitions, and her growing Christian faith. She wants to overcome social anxiety, possibly reconnect with old friends or meet new people, and asks for any advice.
