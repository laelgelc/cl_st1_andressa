A 16-year-old boy describes long-term struggles with friendship due to frequent family moves and class changes, causing him to lose his best friend every year since kindergarten. By 6th grade he gave up on trying to make friends, though he still formed connections that kept ending. He then got very involved online and ended up in toxic friendships where people mocked his lack of money and his looks, making him obsess over wealth and appearance. This has damaged his self-image and makes it harder to form real-life friendships, especially with COVID. He feels like the world is against him, believes he’s become narcissistic, self-loathing, uncaring, and boring, and just wants someone he can depend on or talk to, but fears that will never happen.
