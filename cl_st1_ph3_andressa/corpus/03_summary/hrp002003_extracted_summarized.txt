A 27-year-old man who has been isolated for over 10 years, with no family or friends, recently started pushing himself out of his comfort zone. He went to a local anime convention, where a few people complimented his jacket. A girl noticed his jacket, sat next to him, and they had a natural, enjoyable 1–2 hour conversation about shared interests. She asked to exchange contact info and later added him on a messaging app. He messaged her but received no reply, even after a follow-up. After a few days of silence, he feels hurt and foolish for thinking he’d finally made a friend and feels like withdrawing again.
