The poster is a 30-year-old woman who has felt lonely her whole life and is now extremely tired of living, though she won’t act on it because she has a family. She can’t find a job that provides real human interaction and feels isolated even when working with clients. Newly diagnosed as autistic, she believes her difficulties making friends and finding people on her intellectual level are tied to this. She feels empty, like she makes no difference in the world, and worries that if she can’t form connections herself, she won’t be able to teach her kids how to connect and avoid feeling the same way. She recognizes others feel similarly lonely but questions how that knowledge actually helps.
