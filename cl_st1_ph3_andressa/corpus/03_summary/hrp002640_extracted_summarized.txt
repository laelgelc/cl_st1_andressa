The poster read articles about loners and introverts and concluded they are a loner but not a confident one. They relate more to the “lives with his mom” stereotype than to independent, self-assured loners. They don’t see themselves as a “forced loner” who hates people; they’ve basically always been a loner (except in childhood) and don’t think others are stupid or unworthy.

They experience anxiety doing some typical “loner” activities alone, especially eating in restaurants, though they can now go to museums and the cinema alone and feel comfortable there, and they go running alone. Their sister, who has friends and isn’t a loner, finds solo runs “brave,” while the poster feels similarly anxious about restaurants. They started learning to swim alone as an adult and felt anxiety then too. They don’t feel at risk of depression, but think isolation might be a concern.
