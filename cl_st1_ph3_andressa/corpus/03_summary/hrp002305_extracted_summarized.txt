A male high school senior describes feeling very lonely, empty, and like a “backup friend” who is left out of inside jokes and always catching up on what happened. He feels overly attached and jealous when friends have fun with others, and believes everyone else has a close friend or partner except him. Recently he has started to hate his own voice, laugh, and way of speaking, feeling like his jokes fall flat and create awkward silences. He went through similar issues over a year ago and self-harmed at that time, sometimes in ways that others might notice, which makes him worry he was doing it partly for attention. He has stopped self-harming since then but is feeling the urge again and doesn’t want to relapse. He expresses self-hatred, guilt for wanting better treatment, and apologizes for what he sees as rambling and attention-seeking.
