The poster, who has diagnosed autism, feels deeply hurt and undesired because others consistently show care and affection to everyone except them, even during times when they were struggling to stay alive. They say this isn’t just overthinking but something confirmed through careful evaluation and professional help. Despite always being there for others and not needing to be a priority, they just wanted to be “someone,” yet feel dehumanized and overlooked. They carry this pain constantly, want to meet people like themselves, and learn how to be around others after years of mistreatment. They wish they could support others but feel too weak and undeserving of self-worth. They emphasize they don’t want to be a victim or popular—only to feel human.
