The writer was given a weekend pass from a hospital ward, their first time out for more than an afternoon since early December, and planned to use it to unpack and organize their new bedsit. Once there, the silence and lack of noise made them feel intensely lonely, and attempts to use music and shows for background noise didn’t help. They began to spiral, were triggered by something related to their PTSD, dropped a picture frame, and ended up in A&E before being sent back to the ward. They still feel very alone, have no nearby family after moving across the country about a year ago, and are asking how people cope with crushing loneliness.
