The writer reflects on an Argentine comic phrase “nobody saves themselves,” realizing it’s incomplete: nobody saves themselves, but no one will do it for you either. They describe having built an emotional “cocoon” to protect themselves from pain, fear, and people, which turned into a prison of loneliness. After waiting years for someone to rescue them and realizing no one would, they understood they needed help but that real change had to be driven by themselves. They conclude that they must take the initiative to walk out of their “lonely hell,” with loved ones beside them for support, but not carrying them.
