The writer describes hiding their pain and exhaustion by staying quiet so others won’t notice they’re struggling. They believe being quiet makes them easier to ignore and possibly easier to “love.” They feel like they’re disappearing, more like a ghost than a distant person, and nothing feels like home anymore. They handle conversations carefully out of fear of driving people away, measure their worth by how others respond to them, and flinch at being noticed. Inside, they are desperate for someone to see through their silence and recognize they’re not okay, but no one asks, and they’re too tired to reach out. In the end, their unnoticed suffering becomes what they accept as love.
