The writer describes a painful school experience where they were isolated and rejected because of their appearance and labeled as “less than.” They were bullied, mocked, and humiliated by classmates, and even their best friend joined in and used them. Teachers compared them to “smart” students in front of the class, which led to more ridicule and name-calling. They struggled academically without guidance or support, developed deep insecurity and an inferiority complex, and still carry the hurt from those memories. Despite everything, they continued going to school because they had no choice.
