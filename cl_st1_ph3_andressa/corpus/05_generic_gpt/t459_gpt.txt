I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

I talk to people every day—coworkers, a couple of acquaintances, family over text—but none of it feels like it touches anything real. It’s all “How’s it going?” “Busy weekend?” “Did you see that show?” I can play the part just fine. I even make people laugh sometimes. Then I go home and it’s like the whole day never actually involved me, just some version of me on autopilot.

What I miss is the feeling that someone actually sees me and is curious about what’s going on in my head. I can’t remember the last time someone asked a question that went deeper than small talk and actually waited for the answer. I also don’t really give people the chance, because I’ve learned that when I start to open up, it either gets awkward or the conversation gets steered back to something lighter.

There are evenings where I sit on my bed, phone in hand, scrolling through chats with people I used to be close with, hovering over the keyboard, trying to think of something to say that doesn’t sound needy or out of nowhere. Then I lock the screen and put the phone down, telling myself I’ll try again tomorrow.

I’m not completely isolated. I’m just lonely in a way that feels hard to justify to anyone else, because from the outside my life looks normal. It just doesn’t feel like it belongs to me.
