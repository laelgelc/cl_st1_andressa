I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and exposed lately. Like I’m standing in a crowded room with a glass wall around me. People can see me, I can see them, but nothing really touches.

I have friends on paper. Group chats, occasional hangouts, people who’d say they care if asked. But the conversations stay on the surface: memes, work, “we should catch up soon.” I can’t remember the last time someone asked how I really am and stayed long enough to hear the messy answer.

What scares me is how used to it I’m becoming. I wake up, go through my routine, scroll through feeds full of other people’s lives, and then realize I haven’t actually spoken out loud to anyone all day. Sometimes I rehearse what I’d say if someone called, like practicing being a person.

I keep thinking I must be the common denominator. Everyone else seems to eventually find “their people,” and I’m stuck wondering what’s so off-putting about me that connections never quite stick. It’s like I’m always almost part of something, but not quite.
