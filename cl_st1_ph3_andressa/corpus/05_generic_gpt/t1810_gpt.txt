I’m starting to think I’ve forgotten how to be close to people.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I can chat with about weather, TV shows, work. But there’s this invisible wall when it comes to anything real. I watch other people slip into friendships so naturally—inside jokes, casual hangouts, shared histories—and I feel like I’m standing outside a house, looking in through the window.

What messes with my head most is that I *look* fine. I can be funny, social, “normal” in short bursts. No one suspects that after a day of talking to people, I go home and don’t receive a single message from anyone. My phone is either silent or full of group chats where I’m optional background noise.

I don’t even know what I’m craving exactly. Maybe just the feeling that if I disappeared for a few days, someone would actually notice and care enough to ask where I went. That I’m not just a placeholder person people talk to when no one else is around.

It’s a weird kind of grief, mourning connections that never really existed in the first place.
