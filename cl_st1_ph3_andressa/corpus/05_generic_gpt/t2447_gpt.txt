I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life without sounding dramatic, so I’m putting it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people but feel completely disconnected from them. I have coworkers, acquaintances, family chats, but no one I can message and say, “Hey, I’m not okay, can we talk?” without feeling like I’m burdening them or overstepping what the relationship actually is.

Most of my days are a mix of routine and distraction: work, scrolling, random YouTube, then bed. I keep trying to “put myself out there” but every interaction feels shallow or forced, like I’m auditioning for a role instead of actually being myself. When I do open up a little, I immediately regret it and replay the conversation for days.

The weirdest part is how invisible this all is. From the outside, I probably look fine, functioning, maybe even “busy.” Inside, it just feels like this constant low-level ache that never really goes away, just shifts into the background until something small brings it right back.
