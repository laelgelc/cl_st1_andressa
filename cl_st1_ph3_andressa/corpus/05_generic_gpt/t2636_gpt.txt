I’ve been feeling like a background character in my own life lately.

I go through the motions—work, errands, scrolling on my phone—but it all feels like I’m just filling time between going to bed and waking up again. I talk to people at work, but it’s all surface-level stuff. Jokes, small talk, “how was your weekend?” with answers no one really cares about. When the day ends, everyone goes home to their people, and I just…go home.

What bothers me is how invisible I feel. I can disappear from group chats and social media for days and no one checks in. I see people posting about dinners, trips, “game nights with the best friends,” and I’m not jealous exactly, but it stings. It’s like watching life from outside a window.

I’ve tried joining clubs, going to events, talking to strangers. I can start conversations, but they never seem to turn into anything deeper. People are “nice,” but then they drift back to their already full lives.

I don’t know if I’m doing something wrong, or if this is just how adulthood is for some of us—quiet apartments, one set of dishes, and the constant feeling that if I vanished, the world would barely ripple.
