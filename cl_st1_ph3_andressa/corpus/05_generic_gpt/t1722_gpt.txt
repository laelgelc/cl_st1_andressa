I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

Lately it feels like my life is technically “full” but emotionally empty. I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about TV shows and weekend plans, but I can’t shake the feeling that I’m just acting out a script everyone else wrote a long time ago. People seem to like me well enough on the surface, but nobody really knows me. I’m not sure anyone even wants to.

What gets to me is the contrast. I’m surrounded by people almost all day, but I feel more alone in a crowded room than I do in my apartment. I laugh at jokes, I nod along, I say the right things, and then I come home and feel this weird emotional whiplash, like I’ve been pretending to be a person all day. When the door closes behind me, it’s so quiet it almost hums.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like everyone always says. I’ve joined group chats, gone to a couple of meetups, tried to be more responsive when people message me. It always seems to stall at the same level: acquaintances who share memes, coworkers who talk about tasks, people who say “we should hang out sometime” and then never follow up. I can’t tell if I’m the one who’s broken or if this is just how adulthood works now.

The worst part is how it messes with my sense of self-worth. My brain keeps whispering that if I were more interesting, more attractive, more something, someone would have chosen me by now—for friendship, for a relationship, for anything deeper than surface-level interaction. I know logically that people are busy, that everyone has their own problems, but it still stings watching other people slide so easily into each other’s lives while I stay orbiting on the outside.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to put it somewhere that isn’t the inside of my head.
