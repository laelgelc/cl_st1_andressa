I’ve been feeling like I’m slowly fading out of other people’s lives and I’m not sure when it started.

On paper, I’m “not alone.” I have coworkers, a family chat, a couple of old friends I message every now and then. But none of it feels like it actually touches me. It’s like I’m surrounded by glass: I can see everyone, hear them, but there’s this barrier that never goes away.

I go through the motions: work, errands, scrolling, sleep. I talk to people about deadlines, memes, surface stuff. But there’s no one I feel comfortable saying, “I’m scared of how empty this feels,” or “I don’t remember the last time I felt really seen.” Even when I’m with people, I’m counting down until I can go home, then I get home and feel this heavy silence that almost has a physical weight.

I’ve noticed I’m starting to forget what it feels like to be genuinely excited to see someone. Most interactions feel like obligations or distractions. I catch myself rehearsing conversations in my head, trying to be “interesting enough” or “normal enough,” and then I end up saying very little because I’m anxious I’ll come off as weird or too much.

I’m also ashamed of how lonely I feel. Other people seem to just…live. They meet friends, date, hang out, and I’m stuck analyzing every tiny social moment for signs that I’m being tolerated instead of wanted. So I pull back, which obviously doesn’t help, but it feels safer than risking that confirmation.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once, so it’s not just me and the echo of it all the time.
