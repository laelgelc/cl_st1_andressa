It’s starting to feel like my life is happening behind glass.

I technically “have people” around me—coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up on holidays—but it all feels like background noise. I can go days without anyone saying my name out loud. When my phone lights up, it’s email or spam, not someone asking how I am just because they care.

I’ve tried joining things: a hobby group, a Discord server, even a local meetup. I show up, talk a bit, smile in the right places, but I always feel like an extra in everyone else’s movie. They already have their in-jokes, their shared history. I’m just… there. And when I leave, nobody is any different for having met me.

What messes with my head is that on paper I’m “fine.” I work, pay my bills, make small talk. People probably think I’m quiet but okay. But I go home and it’s like the silence has weight. I eat dinner alone, scroll endlessly, pretend I’m too tired to reach out when the truth is I don’t know who to reach out to anymore.

There’s this constant ache of wanting to be known, really known, and this equally strong fear that if people actually saw how empty and disconnected I feel, they’d pull even further away. So I just keep floating on the surface, hoping it’ll start to feel like living at some point.
