I’ve been feeling like I’m slowly fading out of everyone’s peripheral vision.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I talk to coworkers, I answer family group chats with the occasional emoji. But none of it feels like connection. It feels like I’m playing a character that says the right lines and then goes back into storage when the scene is over.

Most evenings I sit in my room scrolling, not even absorbing anything, just trying to not notice how quiet it is. I’ll think of people I used to be close to and hover over their names, but I never actually message them. It’s like there’s this heavy, invisible barrier between wanting to reach out and actually doing it.

I keep wondering what’s wrong with me that I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m behind glass. I miss having someone who really knows me, who I don’t have to translate myself for. I’m starting to forget what that even feels like.

Sometimes it scares me how easy it’s becoming to accept that this might just be it.
