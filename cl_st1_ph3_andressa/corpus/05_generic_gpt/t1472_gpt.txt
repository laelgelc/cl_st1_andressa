I don’t really know how to describe what I’m feeling lately except that it’s like being in a soundproof room while life is happening on the other side of the glass.

On paper I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers I chat with, a couple of old friends I text occasionally, family that checks in. But none of it feels like it actually reaches me. I can go through a whole day of talking to people and still come home feeling like I never actually connected with anyone.

I miss having someone who really knows me, or at least tries to. The kind of person you can message something stupidly small like “I saw this and thought of you” without overthinking if you’re being annoying. Right now every interaction feels like a performance: choosing the right words, the right tone, trying not to come off as too intense or too distant. It’s exhausting.

What makes it worse is this sense that everyone else has moved onto some next stage of life—relationships, families, tight friend groups—and I’m stuck in this in-between space. I’m not completely isolated, but I’m not really part of anything either. Just orbiting around other people’s lives.

I’ve tried joining groups, going to events, talking more, talking less. I always end up feeling like the extra person that no one would really miss if I wasn’t there. People are friendly enough, but the conversation never leaves the surface. I leave feeling more empty than before, like I’ve just been reminded of what I’m missing.

I don’t know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just wanted to put into words that this quiet, persistent ache is there, even if I hide it pretty well offline.
