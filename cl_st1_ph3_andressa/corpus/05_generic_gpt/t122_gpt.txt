I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

I have people around me: coworkers, some old friends I still text, family chats that light up my phone. But the second I try to talk about how I actually feel, everything turns shallow. Conversations slide back to work, memes, surface-level updates. I end up nodding along, laughing in the right places, and feeling completely disconnected.

What bothers me most is this sense that no one really knows me. Not the version that’s tired for no obvious reason, or that gets anxious walking into a room full of people who already know each other. I keep wondering if it’s my fault—maybe I’m too quiet, too guarded, not interesting enough. I’ve had chances to get closer to people, but I always pull back, afraid of being “too much” or getting brushed off.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through feeds, see everyone posting group photos, trips, inside jokes. I know it’s curated, but it still hits. I’m physically alone in my room, but the real loneliness is that feeling that if I disappeared from most people’s lives, their routines wouldn’t really change.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by writing this. I just needed to put it somewhere.
