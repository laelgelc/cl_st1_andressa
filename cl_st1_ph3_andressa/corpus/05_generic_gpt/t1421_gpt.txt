I’ve been feeling like a background character in my own life lately.

I go through the motions—work, commute, groceries, scrolling—but it all feels like I’m watching someone else’s routine from far away. I talk to people every day, but it’s all surface-level: “How was your weekend?” “Busy.” “Same.” Then silence. I can be surrounded by coworkers, family, even friends, and still feel like I’m sealed behind glass.

What really gets to me is how hard it is to admit this out loud. Loneliness feels embarrassing, like an admission that I somehow failed at being a person. I see others making plans, having group chats, inside jokes, shared histories. I have… acquaintances. People who are “happy to see me” but wouldn’t notice if I disappeared for a few weeks.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for. I just wanted to say it somewhere: I’m tired of being the one who listens, nods, smiles, and then goes home to a quiet room and a phone that doesn’t light up unless I start the conversation.
