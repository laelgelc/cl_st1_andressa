I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life without sounding dramatic, so I’m just dumping it here.

Lately I’ve started to notice that I can be “surrounded” by people and still feel like I’m behind glass. I go to work, I make small talk, I reply to group chats, I send memes, I say “lol” and “nice” and all the other filler words, but it all feels like I’m playing a character that vaguely looks like me. The real me is somewhere a few feet back, just watching.

What hits the hardest is how forgettable I feel. If I don’t text first, I don’t hear from anyone. If I stop joining calls, no one asks why, they just stop adding me. I’ve tested it a few times—pulling back, going quiet—and the world just… keeps going, completely unaffected. People say “you should reach out more,” but I’m tired of being the one who always reaches out. It starts to feel like begging for scraps of attention.

I miss having conversations that aren’t just surface-level updates. I miss when someone would message me just because they thought of me, not because they needed something or were replying out of politeness. I miss feeling like I mattered to someone’s daily life in a real way, not just as a name in a contact list.

I catch myself editing everything I say, even online, because I’m scared of being “too much” or “too boring” and pushing people away. Then I end up saying nothing, and of course that pushes people away too. It’s like I’m stuck between being invisible and being “annoying,” and I don’t know how to land in the middle.

The worst part is how this makes me doubt my own memories. I look back at times I thought I was close to people and wonder if I just imagined the closeness, if I read way more into it than they ever did. It makes me feel stupid, like I’ve been misreading the room my whole life.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head for a while.
