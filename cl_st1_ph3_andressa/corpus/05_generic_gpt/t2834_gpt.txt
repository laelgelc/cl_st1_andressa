I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t make much sense on paper. I’m not completely isolated. I have coworkers, a couple of people I’d call acquaintances, family I text with sometimes. But it all feels like background noise, not connection.

Most of my days blur together: wake up, scroll my phone, go to work or do what I have to do, come home, stare at screens until I’m tired enough to sleep. I keep telling myself I’ll change things “soon” – join a group, message someone first, say yes to something – but when the moment comes, I freeze. It’s like I’ve forgotten how to be around people in any real way.

What really gets to me is how invisible I feel. Not in a dramatic way, just… like if I quietly disappeared from everyone’s life, it would be an adjustment for maybe a week and then nothing. I’m the person people remember when they need something, but not the person they think of when they want to share good news or just hang out. I feel like an extra in everyone else’s movie.

I also notice how curated everyone’s lives look online. I know it’s not real, but seeing groups of friends, trips, inside jokes, even just people casually sitting together in a café – it hits this raw spot in me. I don’t want anything extravagant. I just want to feel like someone is genuinely glad I exist and wants me around without me having to perform or be “useful.”

Sometimes I try to talk about this and I end up minimizing it halfway through, like I’m afraid of sounding pathetic or needy. So I joke, or I change the subject, then go back home and feel even more alone because I couldn’t even be honest when I had the chance.

I don’t know what I’m expecting by writing this. I think I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
