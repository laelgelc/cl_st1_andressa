I’m starting to realize that I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s eating through my life.

My days are basically work, commute, screens, sleep. I talk to coworkers about tasks, nod at neighbors, send “lol” in group chats, but it all feels like I’m playing a background character in everyone else’s story. I don’t remember the last time someone asked how I really was and actually had the time or interest to listen.

What makes it worse is the shame. I’m an adult; I’m “supposed” to know how to make friends, date, build a life. Instead I sit in my room scrolling, watching other people’s connections like a fish staring at the world through glass.

Sometimes I wonder if people would notice if I quietly drifted out of their lives, or if I’ve already halfway disappeared.
