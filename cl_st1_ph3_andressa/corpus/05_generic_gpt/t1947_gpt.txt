I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here because it feels safer telling strangers than people who actually know me.

I’m lonely in this weird, quiet way. It’s not dramatic. I have a job, I have coworkers I can chat with, I have family I text sometimes. On paper, my life looks “fine.” But most days it feels like I’m just…passing through my own life, like a background character.

What gets to me is the silence around the stuff that actually matters to me. I don’t have that person I can message randomly just to say “this made me think of you.” I scroll through my phone at night and realize there’s no one I can text without overthinking whether I’m bothering them. I replay old conversations and imagine how I sounded, then convince myself that’s why people drift away.

I’ve had friendships, even close ones, but they always seem to fade. No big fight, no obvious reason. Just slower replies, then none. I start wondering if I’m boring, or emotionally high-maintenance, or just fundamentally forgettable. It’s like people meet me, stay for a while, then realize I’m not what they were hoping for.

I try to put myself out there—meetups, chats, online communities—but I always feel like I’m joining a story that’s already in progress. Everyone else seems to already have their people. I end up hovering at the edges of groups, smiling, nodding, making small talk, and going home feeling more alone than before.

The hardest part is that I’m not even sure who I am around other people anymore. I’m constantly adjusting: less of this, more of that, trying to be easier to like. Then I come home and feel like I’ve spent the whole day playing a character, and I don’t even like the character.

Nights are the worst. The day is over, the distractions stop, and it hits me how quiet it is. Not just the room—me. That’s when it feels like if I disappeared, my absence would be more of an inconvenience than a loss.
