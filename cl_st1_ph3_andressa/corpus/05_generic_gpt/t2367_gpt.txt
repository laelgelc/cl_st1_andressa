Lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

I talk to people every day — coworkers, neighbors, cashiers — but it all feels like small talk bouncing off the surface. I don’t have anyone I’d call at 2 a.m. if something really went wrong. I don’t even know who would notice if I disappeared from social media for a month, besides maybe an algorithm.

I’ve tried the “solutions” people suggest: hobbies, meetups, gym, volunteering. I go, I smile, I participate, and then I walk home alone and it’s like none of it really touched me. People already seem to have their circles, their inside jokes, their shared histories. I always feel like the extra chair at the table.

What gets to me most is how invisible the loneliness is. From the outside my life looks fine. I have a job, a place to live, I’m not completely isolated. But there’s this constant, quiet ache under everything, like I’m an observer in my own life instead of a participant.
