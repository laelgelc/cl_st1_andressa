I don’t even know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of inside it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I nod in meetings, I say “have a good one” to people I barely know. I come home, I scroll, I eat something forgettable, I go to bed too late. Repeat. The weird part is how invisible I feel in the middle of all that. Like I could just quietly disappear and it would only register as a scheduling inconvenience.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, acquaintances, family chats that light up with memes. But there’s no one I feel like I can text and just say, “I’m not okay, can you talk?” without worrying I’m being too much or annoying or needy. I always end up deleting the message before I hit send.

I miss the version of connection where you don’t have to perform. Where you can say something stupid or dark or honest and not immediately regret it. I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely known by someone in real life, not just “oh yeah, that person from work who likes coffee.”

The worst moments are at night, when everything’s quiet and I realize I haven’t actually spoken out loud in hours. It’s like the silence starts to press in from all sides. I keep thinking I should “put myself out there,” join something, talk more. But I feel so out of practice at being a person around other people that I don’t even know where to start.
