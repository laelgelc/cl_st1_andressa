I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living behind glass.

I have people around me. Coworkers I chat with, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family I see on holidays. On paper, it looks like I’m “not alone.” But I go home and it’s like the air changes. The quiet is so loud it almost hums. I’ll put on a show or a podcast just to hear voices, but it doesn’t touch that feeling in my chest that something is missing.

What bothers me most is how invisible it all is. No one sees it because I’ve gotten good at playing the “I’m fine” version of myself. I can make small talk, crack a joke, look busy on my phone so I don’t seem out of place. But underneath, I feel like I’m drifting further away from everyone else, like I’m watching life happen instead of living it.

I miss feeling known. I miss those effortless conversations where you don’t have to calculate every word, where you can say, “I’m not okay today,” and it doesn’t scare the other person off. I don’t even know how to get back to that. I’m tired of my own company, but also weirdly afraid of letting anyone close enough to see how empty I feel most of the time.

So I just keep going, doing the usual things, hoping something shifts, but mostly it doesn’t.
