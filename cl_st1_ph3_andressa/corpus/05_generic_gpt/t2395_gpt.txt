I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

On paper I’m “fine” – job, roof over my head, a couple of people I can text if I really need something. But I don’t feel connected to anyone. I can go days without using my voice for more than “thank you” at a checkout. When people ask how I am, I say “good, busy” and they accept it, and a small part of me is disappointed they don’t push further, even though I’d probably panic if they did.

I scroll through chats and see conversations that just… faded. No big fight, no reason. We just stopped talking and now it feels weird to reach out, like I’d be bothering them, or they’d feel obligated to respond. The longer I wait, the heavier the silence gets, and then I tell myself it’s too late.

I’ve tried going to events, joining groups, but I always feel like an extra. Everyone already has their people, their in-jokes, their shared history. I’m there physically, but mentally I’m hovering at the edge, waiting for a moment that never really comes. I end up going home more drained and more aware of how alone I am.

What messes with my head is how invisible loneliness is. I can look “normal” and still feel like I’m quietly falling apart. I don’t have a dramatic story or a clear reason; I just feel like I’m not really part of anything, like I could disappear from most people’s lives and it wouldn’t change much.

I don’t even know exactly what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just needed to say it somewhere.
