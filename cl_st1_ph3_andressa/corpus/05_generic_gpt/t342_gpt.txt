I’ve been feeling this really specific kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t quite fit into any neat category. I’m not totally isolated – I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up my phone – but I still feel like I’m standing behind glass watching everyone else actually live.

When people ask how I’m doing, I give the usual “I’m fine, just busy” answer, and they accept it. Part of me is frustrated that no one pushes further, but the other part knows I’ve trained everyone not to. I keep conversations on the surface because I’m scared that if I start talking honestly, it’ll come out messy and I’ll scare them away or sound pathetic.

What really hits me is those small moments: walking home at night and realizing there’s no one who’s expecting me, scrolling through my phone and not knowing who I could message without feeling like I’m bothering them, seeing group photos online and feeling like everyone else got some memo about how to connect that I missed.

I also feel weirdly “out of practice” with being close to people. When someone is actually kind or shows interest, I get awkward and pull back. I want connection so badly, but when it’s near, I freeze. It’s like I don’t know how to be known anymore.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for posting this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
