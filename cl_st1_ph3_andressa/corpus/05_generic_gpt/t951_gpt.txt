I’ve been feeling like a ghost in my own life lately.

I technically “have people” around me — coworkers, acquaintances, family I text occasionally — but I don’t feel actually known by anyone. Conversations feel like I’m reading from a script: “How’s work?” “Busy.” “How was your weekend?” “Fine.” And then silence, or jokes that don’t go anywhere deeper. I walk away from every interaction feeling emptier than before.

I used to think loneliness meant having no one at all, but now I realize it’s worse when you’re surrounded by people and still feel invisible. I can be in a room full of voices and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else live.

I keep thinking it’s my fault — that I’m too quiet, too awkward, too… something. I overanalyze every message I send, every word I say, and then I end up saying nothing. Friends drift away, and I just let them, because I don’t know how to ask someone to stay without sounding desperate.

Most days I just go through the motions: work, home, screen, sleep. I don’t know how to break out of this pattern, or if I even remember what genuine connection feels like anymore.
