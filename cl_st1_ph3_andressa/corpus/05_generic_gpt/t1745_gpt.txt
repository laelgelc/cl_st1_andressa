I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit the usual “I have no one” description. I technically have people: coworkers I chat with, family I can call, a couple of old friends I text once in a while. But I still feel like I’m floating just outside of everyone’s real life, like I’m watching through glass.

I keep realizing how surface-level most of my interactions are. Work talk, memes, “how was your weekend?” Small talk on repeat. I don’t remember the last time I felt genuinely seen by someone, where I could say what I actually think or feel without worrying I’m being too much or too weird. So I end up editing myself constantly. I give people the version of me that’s easy to handle, and then I go home and feel like nobody knows me at all.

Nights are the worst. I’ll scroll through social media and see people hanging out, going on trips, posting group photos with inside jokes in the captions. Even when I know those posts aren’t the whole truth, they still hit a nerve. It’s like proof that there’s this other world of connection happening, and I’m not really part of it.

I also feel stuck because I don’t even know how to fix it. I’m not great at initiating plans. I second-guess myself constantly: “They’re busy,” “They don’t actually want to hang out,” “You’ll just be awkward.” So I wait for other people to reach out, and when they don’t, it just confirms the story in my head that I’m not that important to anyone.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. Waking up, going through the motions, talking to people but never really connecting, then coming home to the quiet. It’s like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life, and no one would really notice the difference.
