I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’ve slowly disappeared from my own life.

I have people around me in a technical sense—coworkers, some old friends I still text occasionally, family I see on holidays. But there’s this weird emptiness between us, like there’s a sheet of glass I can’t get through. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes, “how’s it going?” / “fine.” I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely seen or emotionally close to someone.

What messes with my head is that from the outside I probably look “okay.” I show up to work, I respond to messages, I make small talk. But I come home and it’s just silence. I eat alone, scroll alone, go to bed alone. Even when I’m physically with people, I feel like an extra in the background of their lives.

I’ve tried to reach out more, but I always end up feeling like I’m forcing myself into spaces where I’m not really wanted, just tolerated. I worry I’m boring or draining, so I end up pulling back again. It’s this cycle: crave connection → try a little → feel awkward or rejected → retreat and tell myself I’m better off alone, even though I’m not.

The worst part is how it’s starting to change how I see myself. I used to think I was at least somewhat interesting, somewhat worth knowing. Now I’m not so sure. It feels like I’m slowly fading into this quiet, unnoticed existence, and no one is really close enough to notice that anything is wrong.
