I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m just… drifting through my own life as a background character.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I reply in group chats, I show up to family events, I say the right things. People would probably describe me as “nice” or “quiet” or “reliable.” But I don’t feel actually known by anyone. Not in the way where you can show up messy and unfiltered and still be understood.

Most of my interactions are surface-level. At work it’s small talk: “How was your weekend?” “Got any plans?” I always give the safe, short answers, then go home and overthink how I sounded. I scroll through social media and see everyone else going out, traveling, dating, having inside jokes and shared memories, and I feel like I’m stuck behind glass watching other people live.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like everyone says. I’ve joined a few online communities, tried chatting in Discord servers, even went to a couple of local meetups. But I always end up feeling like an extra who walked into the wrong scene. People already have their friend groups, their shared history. I’m just this person hovering at the edge of conversations, smiling and nodding while counting the minutes until I can leave without it being weird.

What hurts the most is that I don’t really have anyone I can message when something small but meaningful happens. Like, I’ll see something funny or have a random thought and realize there’s no one I feel close enough to send it to without worrying I’m being annoying or clingy.

Sometimes I think I’ve forgotten how to connect. Or maybe I never really learned. I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m in a soundproof room, watching my own life from behind a window. It’s exhausting, carrying this quiet heaviness around and having no one who would even notice if I put it down for a minute.
