I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I can make small talk with coworkers and neighbors. I answer “not bad, you?” when people ask how I’m doing. I laugh at the right moments. I send memes in group chats. But there’s this constant, heavy awareness that no one actually knows me, and I’m starting to wonder if I even know how to let anyone know me anymore.

I have people around me, but they feel like background characters and I probably feel like one to them too. We talk about work, shows, the weather, weekend plans. It never goes deeper. I don’t think anyone in my life could really describe what I’m struggling with, what I’m afraid of, what I actually care about. I don’t even know who I’d call if something really bad happened at 3 a.m. I scroll through my contacts and realize there’s no one I’d feel comfortable “bothering.”

Most evenings are the same: come home, heat something up, put on a show or YouTube, scroll, lie in bed, stare at the ceiling. It’s not like I’m doing anything terribly wrong, but the days just blur together. When something mildly good happens, I think “oh, I should tell someone about this,” and then I realize there isn’t really a “someone.” So it just stays in my head and fades away.

What messes with me is that I don’t think I’m completely unlikable. People say I’m “nice” or “easy to talk to,” but it never goes beyond that. I don’t know how to cross that invisible line between acquaintance and actual friend. I overthink everything I say, replay conversations, convince myself I sounded weird or needy, and then pull back even more.

It feels like I’m slowly disappearing while still being physically present. Like I’m here, but not really here for anyone, and no one is really here for me either.
