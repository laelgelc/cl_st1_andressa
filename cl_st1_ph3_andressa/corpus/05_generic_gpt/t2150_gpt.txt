I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s hitting harder as I get older.

On paper I’m “fine” – job, roof over my head, a couple of acquaintances I can message if I have to. But everything feels… thin. Conversations stay on the surface, like we’re all actors reading from a script. I can be funny, polite, helpful, but I never feel seen. When I leave a social situation I just feel more hollow, like I was playing the role of myself instead of being myself.

I scroll through chats and there’s no one I feel I can message without overthinking it. I’m either “too much” or “not enough.” I worry that if I really showed who I am – the messy, insecure parts – people would quietly back away.

Nights are the worst. I lie there imagining what it would be like to have someone I could text something mundane to, without rehearsing it ten times in my head. It sounds so small, but it feels impossibly far away.
