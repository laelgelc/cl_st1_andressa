I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m slowly disappearing from everyone’s lives, including my own.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up on holidays. But I don’t feel *with* anyone. Conversations feel like I’m playing a role: “How’s work?” “Busy, you?” “Haha, yeah.” I can hear myself answering on autopilot, and the whole time there’s this weird echo in my head like I’m watching someone else pretend to be me.

What really messes with me is how one-sided everything feels. If I don’t text first, days or weeks go by in silence. When I do reach out, I get short replies or “let’s hang soon” that never turn into actual plans. I know people are busy, but it’s hard not to read it as “you’re optional.” I start overanalyzing every interaction, wondering if I’m boring, annoying, or just… forgettable.

The worst part is nights. The day is noisy enough to distract me, but when it’s quiet and I’m just lying in bed, it hits me how long it’s been since I felt genuinely close to someone. Not just “talking,” but that feeling that someone actually knows you and wants to. I scroll through my phone trying to think of a single person I could message without feeling like I’m intruding. Usually I just put the phone down.

I’ve tried joining things—hobbies, online groups, whatever—but I always hit this invisible wall where everyone already seems to have their people. I show up, smile, participate, then go home and it’s like I was never there.

I keep wondering if this is just how life is going to be: present, functional, and completely disconnected at the same time.
