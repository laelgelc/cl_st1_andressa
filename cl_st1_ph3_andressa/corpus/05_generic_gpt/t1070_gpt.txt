I don’t really know how to describe what I’m feeling lately except that it’s like being in a room with the lights on but everything still feels dark.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I interact with coworkers, I text a few people every now and then. But none of it feels like it actually reaches me. Conversations feel like I’m reading from a script I’ve memorized, and as soon as it’s over there’s this hollow drop in my chest like I’ve just been pretending to be a person.

The weirdest part is that I’m not completely isolated. I’m around people. But I don’t feel *known* by anyone. I can be sitting at a table with others, laughing at the right moments, and still feel like I’m on the outside looking in through a window. I keep thinking, “If I disappeared, they’d notice for a bit, then just adjust.” And I’m not even sure that thought is irrational.

I’ve also realized I edit myself constantly. I water down my opinions, my interests, even my sense of humor, just to avoid that awkward silence when you say something real and the other person doesn’t get it. Over time it’s like I’ve erased so many parts of myself that I don’t remember what I’m actually like when I’m not trying to be acceptable.

Nights are the worst. That’s when everything slows down and there’s no noise to distract me. I scroll, I watch random videos, anything to avoid sitting with the feeling that there is no one I can message who would genuinely understand me, not just reply out of habit or politeness.

It’s starting to feel less like a phase and more like the way my life just is.
