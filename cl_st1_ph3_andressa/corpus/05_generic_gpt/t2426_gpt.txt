I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from other people’s lives while still being physically here.

I have people around me technically—coworkers, a few acquaintances, family I text occasionally—but I don’t feel actually *known* by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes, “how was your weekend?” I answer, they answer, and it’s like we both agree not to go any deeper. Then I go home and replay every interaction in my head, wondering if I said something weird that made them not want to talk more.

I keep thinking about how long it’s been since someone reached out to me first just to talk, not because they needed something. I’m always the one sending the first message, suggesting plans, checking in. When I stop, everything just…goes silent. It’s like I’m a background character in everyone else’s story.

The worst part is that I don’t even know how to fix it. I try to be friendly, I try to listen, I ask people questions about themselves. I laugh at the right times. But I feel like there’s glass between me and everyone else. I can see them, hear them, but can’t actually connect.

Nights are the hardest. I scroll through my phone, through chats that have slowly died off, through group photos I’m not in, and I get this heavy feeling in my chest. I want to send a message to someone—anyone—but I’m tired of feeling like I’m intruding or forcing a friendship that isn’t really there.

It’s a strange kind of loneliness, being surrounded by people and still feeling like if I vanished tomorrow, it would mostly just be an inconvenience, not an actual loss.
