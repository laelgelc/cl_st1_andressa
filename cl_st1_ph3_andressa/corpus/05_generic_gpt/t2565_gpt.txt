I’m starting to realize that I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m in some kind of soundproof box.

On paper, my life doesn’t look lonely. I go to work, I make small talk, I respond to group chats with memes. I even go out sometimes when people invite me. But it all feels like I’m playing a character who’s loosely based on me, rather than actually being myself. I keep conversations at “safe” levels because I’m scared that if I really open up, I’ll either overwhelm people or they’ll just…not care.

What hurts the most is that there isn’t a single person I feel I can call without rehearsing what I’m going to say first. I scroll through my contacts and realize almost everyone is an acquaintance or someone I used to be closer to but drifted from. I don’t know exactly when the drift started, just that one day I noticed I was always the one sending the first message, so I just stopped and watched everything go quiet.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” more, but I always end up feeling like an extra in other people’s lives. They have their inside jokes, their shared history, their group photos. I’m the one who takes the picture, then walks home alone.

The weirdest part is that I’m not even sure I know how to be close to someone anymore. When people ask how I’m doing, “I’m fine” comes out automatically, even when I’m not. I don’t know if I’m protecting myself or just making it worse, but it’s become a habit I don’t know how to break.
