I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m fading out of my own life.

On paper I’m “fine” – job, roof over my head, a couple of people I can text. But everything feels…thin. I’m always the one watching other people have conversations, laughing, planning things. I’m there physically, but it’s like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else.

I try to join in, but my mind blanks. I overthink every word, every expression. Afterward I replay everything in my head and convince myself I sounded weird or annoying. So I pull back, which just makes things worse. People eventually stop inviting you when you keep saying no.

What scares me is how used to it I’m getting. Nights alone don’t hurt as sharply as they used to; it’s more like a dull, constant ache. I scroll, I lurk, I watch other people’s lives instead of living my own.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to admit out loud that I’m lonely.
