I’ve been feeling like I’m watching my own life from the outside lately, like I’m a side character in a movie where everyone else has a script and I’m just improvising lines that don’t quite fit.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family I see on holidays. But the weird part is how lonely I feel even when I’m physically around people. It’s like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else. I can see them, hear them, laugh at the right times, but nothing actually gets through.

Most of my days look the same: work, go home, scroll, lie in bed with some show playing in the background. I keep telling myself I should “put myself out there,” but I don’t even know what that means anymore. Join a club? Go to events alone? Talk to strangers? It all sounds exhausting and, honestly, kind of humiliating. There’s this fear that if I try and fail, it will prove that I really am as unlikable as I suspect.

What hurts the most is not having “my person.” Not even in a romantic sense, just… that one person you can text without overthinking, who actually wants to hear the messy, unfiltered version of what’s going on in your head. I feel like I’m always the “acquaintance,” the “coworker,” the “old friend from school,” but never the person someone thinks of first.

I’ve also noticed how much I censor myself. I don’t share too much because I don’t want to be “too much.” I keep things light, make jokes, ask questions about other people so the conversation doesn’t drift back to me. Then I go home and realize no one really knows me, and that’s partly my own doing. It’s a strange loop: I’m lonely because I hide, and I hide because I’m scared of being lonely in front of people.

Sometimes I wonder if this is just how adulthood is for a lot of people and everyone’s pretending better than I am. Other times it feels like there’s something fundamentally off about me that I can’t quite name, and I don’t know what to do with that thought.
