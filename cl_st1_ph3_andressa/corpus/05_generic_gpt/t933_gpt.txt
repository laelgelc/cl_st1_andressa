I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding overdramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine” — job, roof, no huge crisis. But everything feels thin and far away. I go through the motions: wake up, work, scroll, sleep. I talk to people every day, but it’s like talking through glass. We joke, we share memes, we complain about work, and yet I walk away feeling like nothing actually touched me.

I used to have close friends, or at least I thought I did. People got busy, moved, paired off, drifted. I can’t even pinpoint when the shift happened; it was like one day I looked up and realized all my “we should hang out soon” messages never turned into actual plans. Now I hesitate to text anyone because I feel like I’m asking for a favor just by wanting their time.

The worst part is how embarrassing it feels to admit this. Loneliness sounds like a personal failure, like I wasn’t interesting or persistent enough to keep people around. So I play the role: “I like my own company,” “I’m just introverted,” “I’m too tired to go out anyway.” Sometimes that’s true. A lot of times it’s not.

Nights are the hardest. The silence gets heavy and my brain replays every small rejection or ignored message like evidence in a trial. I keep wondering if this is just how adulthood is now, or if I somehow missed the exit where everyone else turned off and found their people.
