I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m fading out of my own life.

On paper I’m “fine”: job, roof over my head, a couple of people I can text. But everything feels distant, like I’m watching myself from the outside. I go through the motions—work, commute, scroll, sleep—but there’s no sense of being actually connected to anyone. Conversations feel like I’m reading from a script I didn’t write.

What hits me the hardest is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped talking to people, I’m not sure anyone’s day would really change. I’m the one who initiates plans, checks in, remembers birthdays. If I stop doing that, things just go quiet. It makes me wonder if I’m actually wanted or just… convenient.

I’m also weirdly lonely even when I’m around others. I can be in a room full of people laughing and still feel like I’m behind a glass wall. I hear my own voice and it doesn’t even sound like me, just some version of myself I’ve built so I don’t come off as “too much” or “too quiet” or “too weird.” Then I go home exhausted from pretending to be a person who belongs.

Nights are the worst. That’s when everything slows down and there’s nothing to distract me from the fact that no one really knows me. Not the “funny” version or the “helpful” version—just me. I don’t even know how to let people see that person anymore without feeling like I’m burdening them.

I’m not sure what I want from posting this. I guess I just needed to put it somewhere that isn’t my own head for once.
