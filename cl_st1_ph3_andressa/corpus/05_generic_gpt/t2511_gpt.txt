I’ve been feeling like I’m fading into the background of my own life.

I go through the motions: work, commute, scrolling, sleep. I talk to people every day, but it all feels like small talk performed by an actor who forgot what it’s like to actually care about the script. I crack jokes, ask questions, nod along. Then I go home and realize nobody really knows me, and I’m not sure I know myself anymore either.

What messes with my head is that from the outside I probably look “fine.” I have coworkers, acquaintances, people I can text if I need something practical. But there’s no one I’d call at 2 a.m. There’s no one who would notice if I just quietly withdrew, as long as I still replied “lol” in group chats.

Sometimes I try to open up and it lands weird—people change the subject, or I feel like I’ve overshared and I retreat. It’s like I keep testing the water and finding it too cold to get in.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for writing this. Maybe just to put into words that this numb, invisible feeling is there.
