I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m disappearing while technically still being “around” people.

On paper, I’m not alone. I have coworkers, acquaintances, a few people I can text. I show up where I’m supposed to, I answer “I’m fine” when people ask how I’m doing, and I can make small talk about work, weather, whatever. But none of it feels like it touches anything real inside me. It’s like I’m acting out the role of a normal person, and everyone else believes it, but I don’t.

I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely understood. I can’t remember the last time I said something vulnerable to someone and didn’t immediately feel stupid or like I’d overshared. Most of my interactions feel like I’m holding my breath and waiting for it to be over so I can go back to my own head, even though my own head is the last place I want to be.

The weirdest part is the contrast: I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m behind glass. I watch them laugh and connect, and I can’t figure out what I’m missing. I try to join in and it just feels off, like my timing is wrong or my reactions are slightly delayed. I worry that I come across as cold or uninterested when really I’m just overthinking every single word.

Nights are the worst. The silence gets so loud. I scroll through my phone trying to think of someone I could message, but I either feel like I’d be bothering them or I realize the conversation would stay surface-level anyway. I want that feeling of “I can call this person and just exist with them,” but I don’t have it.

I keep wondering if this is just how life is going to be—physically present, emotionally distant, functionally invisible. And I don’t even know what I’m asking for by writing this. Maybe I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
