Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m watching my own life from the outside, like I’m behind a glass wall while everyone else is actually living. I technically have “people” in my life—coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats—but it all feels incredibly shallow. I can go days without anyone asking how I really am, and honestly I’m not sure what I’d even say if they did.

What bothers me most is that I can be physically surrounded by people and still feel completely alone. At work, I laugh at the right times, make small talk about TV shows, but I’m just performing a version of myself that feels acceptable. When I get home, the silence is loud. I scroll, I lurk, I read other people’s posts and think, “Yeah, that’s me,” but I rarely comment because I don’t want to sound desperate or weird.

I miss the feeling of being known. Not just having someone to hang out with, but someone who actually notices when I’m off, who remembers small details about me without being reminded. I don’t know how adults are supposed to make that kind of connection anymore. It feels like everyone already has their people, and I somehow missed the window.
