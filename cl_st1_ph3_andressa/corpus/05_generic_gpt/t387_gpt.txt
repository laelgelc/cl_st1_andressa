I’ve been feeling like I’m slowly fading out of everyone’s peripheral vision.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, a couple of people I can technically call friends. But most days it feels like I’m just orbiting around other people’s lives, never actually part of anything. I’m always the one who texts first, who suggests meeting up, who tries to keep conversations going. And when I stop putting in that effort, everything just…goes quiet.

What really messes with me is how lonely I feel even when I’m not physically alone. I can be in a room full of people, or in a group chat, and still feel like I’m behind glass watching everyone else connect. They joke, they share, they refer to shared memories, and I’m there trying to find a way in that doesn’t feel forced. Most of the time, I end up just going silent and scrolling.

I’m also realizing I don’t really let people see the real parts of me. I’ve learned to be “easy,” agreeable, low-maintenance. I downplay when I’m struggling because I don’t want to be a burden or the “dramatic” one. But then nobody knows I’m lonely, and I resent them for not noticing, even though I’ve made it almost impossible for them to notice.

Nights are the worst. That moment when you put your phone down and realize no one is expecting anything from you, no one is waiting to hear how your day went, no one would really notice if you just disappeared from social media for weeks. There’s this constant ache of being unnecessary.

I don’t even know exactly what I’m looking for by posting this. I guess I just needed to put it somewhere that isn’t the inside of my head, because keeping it there all the time is starting to feel heavier than I can quietly carry.
