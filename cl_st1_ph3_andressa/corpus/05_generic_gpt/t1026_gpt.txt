I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like my life is happening behind glass.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family that I see on holidays. I can make small talk, I can be “pleasant,” I can laugh at the right times. But none of it feels like it touches whatever part of me is actually starving.

I come home, close the door, and it’s like the world just…stops caring that I exist. No messages, no calls unless it’s something practical. If I disappeared from the internet tomorrow, I’m not sure anyone would notice for a while. I scroll through chats and realize I’m always the one who reached out last. I tell myself I’ll stop trying so hard and see who comes to me, and then the silence just confirms what I was afraid of.

What gets to me most is how hard it is to explain this kind of loneliness without sounding needy or pathetic. People say “you just have to put yourself out there,” but they don’t get that I do. I go to things. I ask questions. I show interest in other people. Somehow I stay on the outer ring of everyone’s life, never really moving inward.

I miss feeling known. I miss those late-night conversations where you say something raw and the other person doesn’t flinch. I miss being someone’s first choice to tell news to, good or bad. Now it feels like I’m always a background character in other people’s stories.

Sometimes I wonder if there’s just something fundamentally off about me that people can sense, even if they can’t name it. Like I’m out of sync with everyone else, and no amount of trying is going to fix that.
