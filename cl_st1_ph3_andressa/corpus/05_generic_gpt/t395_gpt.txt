It’s weird how you can be surrounded by people and still feel completely disconnected.

I have friends, technically. Group chats, occasional hangouts, coworkers who ask how my weekend was. But it all feels like I’m playing a role I memorized a long time ago. I know the right things to say, when to laugh, how to keep the conversation going. Then I go home and it’s like I vanish. No one notices, because they only really see the version of me that’s “on.”

What I’m missing is that feeling of being known. Not just “oh, you like this show and that coffee place,” but the deeper stuff: how my brain spirals at night, how I overthink every interaction, how I feel like a placeholder in other people’s lives. I don’t think anyone in my life would describe me as lonely, which somehow makes it worse.

I’ve tried joining clubs, going to events, being “more social.” I meet people, but the connection never seems to move past surface level. I can’t tell if I’m the one holding back, or if people just don’t have space for anything deeper anymore.

Mostly, it feels like I’m watching my own life from the outside, and no one realizes I’m not really in it.
