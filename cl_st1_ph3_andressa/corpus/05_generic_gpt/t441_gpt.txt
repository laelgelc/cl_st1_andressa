I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living behind glass.

I go to work, make small talk, answer emails, nod in meetings. People probably think I’m “fine” or even “doing well.” But I come home to an empty apartment, sit on the couch, and it’s like everything just… stops. No one to text “I’m home.” No one to ask how my day was. Hours pass and I realize I haven’t spoken out loud.

I have acquaintances, but no one who actually knows me. Conversations never get past surface level. I’ve tried joining groups, chatting in Discords, going to events, but I always feel like an outsider orbiting around other people’s existing connections.

What scares me is how normalized this has become. I’m getting used to the silence, to eating alone, to scrolling just to feel some kind of presence. Sometimes I wonder if this is just what adulthood is now, or if I somehow missed a step where everyone else learned how to actually connect.
