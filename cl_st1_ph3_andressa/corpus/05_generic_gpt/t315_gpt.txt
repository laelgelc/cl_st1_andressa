I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

Lately I’ve been feeling this weird mix of being surrounded by people and still feeling completely alone. I go to work, I reply to messages, I laugh at memes in group chats, but it all feels like I’m just acting out a version of myself that people expect, not actually connecting with anyone.

I’m at that age where everyone seems to be moving on to “real life” – partners, kids, houses, tight friend groups that go on trips together. I watch it all through screens. I get invited sometimes, but it feels more like a courtesy than “we really want you there.” Half the time I say no because I don’t want to be the extra wheel, the person people tolerate rather than miss.

What bothers me most is how replaceable I feel. When I’m not around, nothing changes for anyone. No one checks in if I go quiet for a few days. I can disappear from a group chat and the conversation just keeps flowing, like I was never there in the first place. There’s a part of me that wants to test it – turn everything off and see how long it takes for someone to notice – but I’m scared of what the answer would be.

I also don’t really know how to let people get closer. I keep conversations light and surface-level because I’m afraid if I show the “real” stuff, they’ll back away. But then I complain that no one really knows me. It’s like I’m building my own prison and then getting mad that the doors are locked.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to say it somewhere out loud, since there isn’t really anyone in my day-to-day life I can say it to.
