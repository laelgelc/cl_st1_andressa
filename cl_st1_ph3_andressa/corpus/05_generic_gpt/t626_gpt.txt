I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too visible at the same time, and it’s starting to mess with my head.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family group chats that light up with memes and random updates. But I feel like I’m just orbiting around everyone else’s lives, never actually part of anything.

I go entire weekends without speaking out loud to another person. I’ll realize Sunday night that the only voice I’ve heard is my own, maybe from mumbling to myself while cooking. I scroll social media, watch people post pictures with their friends, partners, kids, and I feel like I’m watching life through a window from the outside.

What’s hard to admit is how much of this feels like my fault. I’m bad at reaching out. I overthink every message: “Am I bothering them? Do they actually like me or are they just being polite?” So I don’t send it. Then time passes, and it feels even weirder to suddenly pop up, so I avoid it more. Meanwhile, I’m craving someone to just notice I’m quiet and ask how I’m doing.

I’m also ashamed of how needy I feel. I want someone who actually wants to know the stupid little details of my day. Not small talk, not “we should hang out sometime” that never happens. Just… being known. Being remembered. Having someone think of me without me having to prompt it.

Nights are the worst. That’s when everything goes quiet and the whole weight of “no one is really close to me” hits. It’s this heavy, physical feeling in my chest. I keep wondering if this is just how adulthood is for some people, or if I somehow missed a step everyone else figured out.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by posting this. I just needed to put it somewhere that isn’t just inside my head.
