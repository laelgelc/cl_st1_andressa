I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about the weather and whatever show people are watching. I laugh when I’m supposed to. But the second I’m alone again, it’s like someone turns the volume down on the world and I’m just… there. Not sad enough to cry, not busy enough to be distracted, just existing in this dull, heavy quiet.

What gets to me most is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped texting people, it would take a long time before anyone noticed. When I do reach out, it’s usually me initiating, me asking “how are you?” and then watching the conversation slowly die because they get busy or forget to reply. I don’t blame them, but every unanswered message adds to this feeling that I’m more of a background character than an actual person in anyone’s life.

I miss having someone who really knows me. Not the “how’s work going?” version of me, but the version who can say something halfway and be understood. I miss being able to send a random thought or a stupid meme without overthinking if I’m being annoying.

I’ve tried different things: hobbies, meetups, online communities. I show up, I smile, I participate. People are polite enough, but it never seems to move past that surface level. Everyone already seems to have their people, their history, their inside jokes. I always feel like the extra chair pulled up to a full table.

Most nights I just end up scrolling, watching other people’s lives through screens, and wondering how I somehow ended up on the outside of everything, looking in.
