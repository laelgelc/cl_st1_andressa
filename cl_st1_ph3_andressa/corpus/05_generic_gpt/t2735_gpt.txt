I’m starting to realize that I feel lonelier around people than I do when I’m actually alone. I can sit in a room full of coworkers, talk about projects, even crack a few jokes, and still have this weird sense that I’m not really there. It’s like I’m playing a role and everyone else got the script except me.

I have acquaintances, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something was really wrong. When I try to open up, I feel like I’m oversharing or being dramatic, so I pull back and keep things surface-level. Then I get frustrated that no one “really knows me,” even though I’m the one hiding.

Nights are the worst. I scroll, watch random videos, lurk here, anything to avoid the quiet. The quiet makes it obvious how disconnected I actually feel.
