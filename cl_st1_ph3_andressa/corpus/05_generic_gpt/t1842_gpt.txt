I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too visible at the same time.

Invisible, because no one really checks in. My phone is mostly silent unless it’s work or some group chat where I’m just a name in a list. Too visible, because when I’m out in public, I feel like everyone can see how out of place I am. Like there’s a sign over my head that says “doesn’t belong anywhere.”

I have acquaintances. People I can joke with, talk about shows, complain about work. But there’s no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something actually broke inside me. Sometimes I scroll through my contacts trying to imagine who I could message just to say “I’m lonely,” and every name feels wrong.

What bothers me most is how long this has been my “normal.” I’ve gotten so used to eating alone, going home to silence, filling the time with YouTube or games or just lying there. It’s not even dramatic sadness most of the time—just this steady, dull emptiness. Like background noise I can’t turn off.

There are moments when I try to fix it. I sign up for things, say yes to invitations, push myself to be more talkative. But I always end up feeling like an extra in other people’s lives. They have history, inside jokes, shared memories. I’m just… there. Replaceable.

I keep wondering if there’s something fundamentally off about me that other people can sense and quietly avoid. Or if I just missed some crucial stage where everyone else learned how to form real connections, and now I’m permanently behind.
