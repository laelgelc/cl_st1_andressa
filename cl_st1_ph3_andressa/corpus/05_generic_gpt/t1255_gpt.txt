I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats—but I don’t feel actually *known* by anyone. Conversations feel like I’m reading from a script: “How was your weekend?” “Fine, yours?” It’s all surface-level, and when I try to go a bit deeper, people politely steer it back to small talk. So I’ve stopped trying.

Nights are the worst. I’ll scroll through my phone, wanting to message someone, but I can’t think of a single person I could text without feeling like I’m intruding. I end up closing all the apps and just lying there, feeling stupid for wanting something as basic as a genuine connection.

What messes with my head is that from the outside, I probably look “normal.” I show up, I smile, I get things done. But inside, it feels like I’m slowly disappearing in plain sight and no one’s noticing, including me.
