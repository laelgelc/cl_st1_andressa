I’ve been feeling this weird, hollow kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my life on paper. I have coworkers I chat with, a couple of friends I see every now and then, family I text. But I still go to bed most nights feeling like I don’t really exist in anyone’s mind once I’m out of sight.

I keep realizing how replaceable I feel. If I stopped showing up, people might notice for a bit, then adjust. No one really knows me deeply enough to miss *me*, specifically—just the role I play. The “reliable one,” the “good listener,” the “person who always says yes.” I’ve gotten so used to being there for other people that I don’t even know how to ask for someone to be there for me.

It’s also embarrassing how needy I feel inside. I overthink every message I send, terrified I’m being annoying. So I hold back, wait for others to initiate. Then when they don’t, I take it as proof I’m not really wanted. It’s like I’m stuck between craving connection and being afraid of confirming that no one actually cares that much.

I miss having “my person,” or even just feeling like I belong somewhere specific. Right now I feel like a background character in everyone else’s story, drifting between groups but never really anchored. I don’t know how people build those close, steady bonds as adults. It feels like I missed a class everyone else attended, and now I’m just improvising, badly.
