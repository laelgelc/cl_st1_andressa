I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, people I can technically text. But everything feels shallow, like I’m always the extra in other people’s stories. I’m around others, but not really *with* them. I go to work, make small talk, come home, scroll, sleep. Repeat. Weekends blur together. I can go days without hearing my name spoken out loud by someone else.

What gets to me most is how hard it is to move past surface-level conversations. I can talk about work, shows, news, whatever. But the moment I try to be even a little more honest, it’s like the conversation hits a wall. People change the subject, joke it off, or send a heart emoji and disappear. I start feeling stupid for trying.

I’ve also noticed I’m starting to censor myself more. I don’t message people first because I don’t want to feel like I’m bothering them. I delete half my texts before sending because they sound “too needy” in my head. Then I sit there staring at my phone, pretending I’m okay with the silence I helped create.

The weirdest part is feeling lonely even when I’m physically with others. Sitting at a table surrounded by people laughing and still feeling like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else. I watch them connect so easily and wonder what exactly is wrong with me that I can’t seem to do the same.

I don’t really know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I just needed to put it somewhere that isn’t just the inside of my head.
