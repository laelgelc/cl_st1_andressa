I’ve started to realize that I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m on the outside of a glass wall, watching everyone else actually live.

Friends, coworkers, family – I have them, technically. I go to gatherings, I laugh at the right moments, I ask questions and listen. But it all feels like I’m playing a character who’s better at being human than I am. When I get home, it’s like the mask falls off and there’s just…silence.

I don’t really know how to let people in without feeling like I’m burdening them or being “too much.” So I keep it surface-level. Then I get frustrated that no one really knows me. It’s a loop I can’t seem to break.

Sometimes I scroll through this subreddit and see posts that sound like they were pulled from my own head. It’s weirdly comforting and depressing at the same time. I guess I’m just tired of feeling like a background character in my own life.
