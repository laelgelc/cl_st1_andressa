I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening in a soundproof room. I’m surrounded by people—coworkers, neighbors, the usual faces at the grocery store—but it all feels like background noise. No one really knows me, and I’m not sure anyone wants to.

I go through the motions every day: wake up, work, come home, scroll, sleep. I chat with people at work, joke in meetings, send memes in group chats, but none of it feels real. It’s like I’m playing a character who’s “fine” because that’s what everyone expects. When the workday ends, it’s just me and the silence. I’ll catch myself opening messaging apps, scrolling through my contacts, and realizing there’s no one I feel comfortable reaching out to without feeling like I’m bothering them.

I’ve tried “putting myself out there” like people always say—joined a couple of clubs, went to some social events, even tried apps meant for making friends. It always seems to stop at small talk. People already have their circles, their inside jokes, their shared history. I’m just this extra piece that doesn’t fit into any puzzle.

What bothers me most is how invisible I feel. I could disappear from social media, stop showing up to things, and I’m honestly not sure who would notice beyond a “hey, long time no see” months later. There’s this constant ache of wanting to be known, really known, but not having the energy or courage to keep trying when it never seems to go anywhere.

Sometimes I wonder if this is just how adulthood is for some people—always on the outside, watching everyone else live in a way that feels completely out of reach.
