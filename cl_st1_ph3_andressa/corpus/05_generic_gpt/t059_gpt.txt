I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s hitting harder as I get older.

On paper I’m “fine”: job, roof, bills paid. But most days I go home, sit in silence, scroll, and feel like I’m watching everyone else live from behind glass. I have acquaintances, but no one I’d call if something really bad or really good happened. I hesitate to message people because I’m convinced I’m an annoyance, so I wait for others to reach out… and they usually don’t.

I overthink every interaction. If someone takes a bit longer to reply, I replay the last thing I said and assume I sounded weird or needy. Then I pull back, which probably makes me seem cold or uninterested. It’s like I sabotage things before they can even become real.

I’m not sure what scares me more: that this is temporary and I’m just failing at this phase of life, or that this is it—that I’ll always feel slightly outside of everyone else’s world, watching but never really belonging.
