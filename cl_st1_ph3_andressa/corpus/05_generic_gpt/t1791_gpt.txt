I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m just… drifting next to my own life instead of living it.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers I chat with, a family I text with sometimes, a couple of old friends I swap memes with. But everything feels surface-level. We talk about work, shows, “how’s it going?” and I answer with the usual “fine” because the real answer would take too long and I’m not sure anyone actually wants to hear it.

The weirdest part is how physically present and emotionally invisible I feel. I can be sitting in a room full of people, laughing at the right moments, saying the right things, and at the same time there’s this heavy, quiet part of me that’s just watching from the corner, thinking, “None of these people really know me. And I don’t know how to let them.”

I overthink every attempt at connection. I’ll type out a long message to someone, then delete it because it feels “too much.” I worry I’m being clingy, or boring, or that I’m forcing intimacy that the other person doesn’t want. So I pull back, play it cool, and then feel hurt when the relationship never deepens. It’s like I’m building my own prison and then complaining about the bars.

Nights are the worst. When everything is quiet and I’m alone with my thoughts, it hits me how little I’m actually needed in anyone’s daily life. If I disappeared, people would notice, sure, but would it actually change anything for them long-term? That question sits in my chest like a stone.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to put it into words somewhere, because saying it out loud to people I know feels impossible. At least here I can admit it: I’m lonely, and I don’t really know what to do about it.
