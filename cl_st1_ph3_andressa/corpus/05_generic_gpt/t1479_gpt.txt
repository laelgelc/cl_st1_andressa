I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, and that scares me more than being alone itself.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I respond to messages, I show up when I’m supposed to. But everything feels like I’m playing a role. Conversations are small talk on autopilot. I laugh when I’m supposed to, ask the right questions, but it never goes deeper. When I get home, it’s like I don’t exist to anyone.

What messes with my head is that I’m not completely isolated. I have “friends,” but it feels like I’m a background character in their lives. They share real stuff with each other, but with me it’s memes and surface-level updates. I don’t know if it’s because I don’t open up enough or because I’m just… forgettable.

I’ve tried to change things—joined groups, forced myself to go to events, tried being more talkative—but I always end up on the edge of conversations, smiling and nodding while everyone else seems to already have their people.

Sometimes I wonder if I missed some crucial stage in life where everyone else learned how to build and maintain close relationships, and I just… didn’t. It’s like I’m permanently stuck on the outside, watching everyone else live in a way I can’t seem to access, no matter how much I want to.
