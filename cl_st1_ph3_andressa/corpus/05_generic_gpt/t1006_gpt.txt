I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing while still being physically present in my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills on time, I make small talk with coworkers about the weather and whatever show people are watching. I reply to group chats with the occasional meme so I don’t completely fall off the radar. But none of it feels like connection. It feels like I’m playing a role that doesn’t require anyone to actually know me.

The weirdest part is how lonely I feel even when I’m surrounded by people. I can be sitting in a room full of voices and laughter and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else live while I just...exist. I’m there, but not really there. I nod, I smile, I say the “right” things, but inside it’s just this dull ache and this constant thought of “no one here would notice if I stopped trying.”

I’ve tried to “fix” it in the ways everyone suggests. I joined a hobby group. I tried being more open in conversations, asking people about themselves, showing genuine interest. I even tried being a bit vulnerable a few times, hinting that I was struggling. People were polite, they listened for a moment, then the topic shifted back to something lighter. I don’t blame them, but it just reinforces this feeling that there’s no place for the heavier parts of me.

What hurts most is wanting to be known and not knowing how to let anyone in without feeling like I’m burdening them. I overthink every message I send, every silence, every “seen” with no reply. I tell myself I’m too much, or not enough, or just not the kind of person people choose.

So I go through my days, doing what I’m supposed to do, cracking small jokes, pretending I’m okay. Then I come home, sit in the quiet, and realize how loud the emptiness actually is.
