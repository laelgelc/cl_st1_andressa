I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness that doesn’t really match the story people tell about it. It’s not that I have zero people in my life. I have coworkers, a few old friends I text occasionally, family chats that light up on holidays. On paper, I’m “not alone.” But it feels like there’s glass between me and everyone else.

I can go through a whole day surrounded by people and still come home feeling like I never actually existed in anyone’s mind. Conversations feel shallow, like we’re all reading from a script. “How are you?” “Busy, you?” “Same.” Then we move on. I notice that when I try to go deeper, people get uncomfortable, or they just don’t know what to do with it, so I pull back and play the easygoing version of myself.

Part of me wonders if I did this to myself. I’ve spent years being the listener, the helper, the “I’m fine, don’t worry about me” person. Now I don’t know how to say, “I’m actually not fine” without feeling like I’m breaking some unspoken rule. The few times I’ve hinted at it, the response is usually a joke, a quick “you’ll be okay,” or silence.

I keep thinking there must be some point where you’ve been lonely for so long that it just becomes your default setting. Like you stop expecting to be understood and just learn to function around the emptiness instead.
