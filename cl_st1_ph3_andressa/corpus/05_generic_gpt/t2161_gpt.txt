I’m starting to realize I’m not just “spending time alone” anymore, I’m actually lonely, and it’s getting under my skin in a way I didn’t expect.

On paper, my life looks fine. I work, I pay my bills, I’m not in immediate crisis. But most days I go from silence at home to small talk at work and then straight back to silence. I don’t really have “people.” I have acquaintances who’ll like a meme I post, coworkers who’ll ask about my weekend in that polite, surface-level way, and that’s about it.

I miss having someone who actually knows the day-to-day details of my life. Someone I can text something stupid to without worrying I’m being annoying. I feel like I’m always “too much” or “not enough” for people, so I hold back, and then they drift away, and then I tell myself it’s my fault for not trying harder.

I keep thinking about how long it’s been since I’ve had a genuine conversation that didn’t end with me replaying every sentence in my head afterward, worrying I sounded weird. I watch other people slide so easily into friendships and relationships, and I feel like I’m on the outside of some joke I don’t get.

It’s a strange mix of wanting to be seen and being terrified of it at the same time. So I end up doing nothing, and the days just keep passing like this.
