I don’t really know how to talk about this in my real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

Lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass. I go to work, I smile, I make small talk about projects and TV shows, and then I come home and it’s like someone hits mute. The silence is so loud. I scroll my phone for hours just to feel like I’m around people, but it’s all surface-level, just noise.

What bothers me most is how invisible I feel. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m not really there. No one really knows me in any deep sense. I don’t even know how to let them. Every time I start to open up, I feel stupid and oversharing and I pull back. Then I sit alone and wonder why nobody knows the real me.

I’ve lost touch with most of my old friends, and I’m not even sure when it happened. We just slowly stopped talking. No big fight, no dramatic moment, just a gradual fade-out. Now it feels too awkward to reach out after so long, like I’m intruding on lives that moved on without me.

There’s this constant ache of wanting to be understood by someone, anyone, but also this fear that if they really knew me they’d just leave anyway. So I stay quiet. I say “I’m fine.” I keep everything on the surface. And then I come back to my room at the end of the day, sit on my bed, and realize there’s no one I could call, even if I wanted to.
