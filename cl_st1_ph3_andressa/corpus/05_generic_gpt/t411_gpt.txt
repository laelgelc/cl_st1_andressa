I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s rewiring my brain.

I have people around me technically—coworkers, acquaintances, a few old friends I text every now and then—but I don’t feel known by anyone. Most conversations stay on the surface: work, memes, “how was your weekend?” with answers everyone could predict. I can’t remember the last time I said something vulnerable out loud and didn’t immediately regret it or try to laugh it off.

What bothers me is how I’ve begun to adapt. I talk to myself way more. I rehearse conversations that never happen. I scroll for hours just to feel like I’m near other humans. When someone does invite me somewhere, I panic, overthink, and sometimes bail, then sit at home feeling even worse.

I miss feeling chosen. Not just included, but wanted. I miss having someone who notices when I go quiet and asks what’s actually going on, not just out of politeness.

Right now it feels like I’m fading into the background of my own life.
