I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here because at least it feels like someone might read it.

I’m surrounded by people and still feel completely alone. I go to work, I chat with coworkers about random stuff, I answer “I’m fine” automatically, and then I come home and it’s like the silence is waiting for me at the door. I scroll, I watch shows, I listen to podcasts just to have voices around, but it’s all just noise. No one actually knows me. I’m not even sure I know how to let anyone know me anymore.

I’ve had friends in the past, but everything feels temporary. People move away, get busy, find partners, start families. I feel like I’m stuck in place, watching everyone else’s life move forward while mine is just… maintenance. Go to work, come home, distract myself, sleep, repeat.

What makes it worse is that I’m starting to feel awkward in basic social situations. I overthink every message I send, every pause in a conversation. I replay things I said days ago and cringe at myself. It’s like the less I interact, the rustier I get, and the rustier I get, the more I avoid interacting.

Sometimes I wonder if this is just how it’s going to be: functioning on the outside, but quietly starving for real connection and not really knowing how to fix it.
