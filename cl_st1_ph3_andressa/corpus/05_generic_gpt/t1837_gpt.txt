I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… background noise.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family chats that light up my phone. But I walk around with this constant sense that if I disappeared tomorrow, it would be more of an inconvenience than a loss. Like people would say, “Oh wow, that’s sad,” and then keep scrolling.

The part that scares me is how used to it I’ve become. I go entire days without having a real conversation. I say the expected things at work, I laugh in the right spots, but I can feel myself watching it from the outside, like I’m just playing a character that nobody really cares to know.

I try to reach out sometimes, but it always feels one-sided. I’ll send a message, they’ll respond hours later with something short, and I immediately regret initiating. I overthink every word and then end up pulling back even more. It’s like I’m both desperate for connection and completely convinced I’m a burden.

Nights are the worst. I lie there scrolling through other people’s lives, seeing group photos, inside jokes, people tagging each other. I can’t remember the last time I was someone’s first choice to call, or the person someone thought of when something funny happened.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once, instead of just carrying it silently like I always do.
