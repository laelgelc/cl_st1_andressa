I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is something I’m just watching from the outside.

I talk to people every day – coworkers, neighbors, the barista who knows my order – but none of it feels like it lands anywhere. Conversations stay on the surface, like we’re all skimming across a lake that no one’s allowed to dive into. I laugh at the right moments, ask about weekends, make small jokes, and then I go home and it’s like I vanish.

What gets to me is that I’m not “isolated” in the obvious way. I show up. I reply to messages. I go to gatherings when invited. But I still feel weirdly invisible, like I could stop existing and it would take people a long time to notice, and even then they’d mostly remember me as “nice” or “quiet” or “easy to talk to,” not as someone they actually knew.

I keep thinking I must be doing something wrong without knowing what it is. I try to open up a little, but I always feel like I’m oversharing or making things awkward. So I retreat again, and everyone just assumes I’m fine because I’m functioning.

There’s this constant ache of wanting to be known by someone who actually wants to know me, not just pass time with me. And it’s embarrassing how hard that is to admit out loud, so I don’t. I just keep going, and the days all blur together in this weird, quiet way.
