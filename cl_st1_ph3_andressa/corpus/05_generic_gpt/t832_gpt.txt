I’m in that weird place where I’m technically “not alone,” but I feel completely alone anyway.

I have coworkers, I have family I could text, I even have a couple of people I’d probably call “friends” if someone asked. But none of it feels real or close. It’s like I’m always hovering on the edges of other people’s lives, never actually in the middle of anything.

I go through entire days without anyone asking me something that isn’t functional or surface-level. “How was your weekend?” doesn’t count, because no one waits for the real answer. I’ve started defaulting to “good, you?” even when I spent the whole weekend staring at my phone, trying to convince myself to message someone and then not doing it.

What makes it worse is that I can’t tell if this is my fault. I overthink every interaction. I replay conversations and convince myself I was awkward or boring, so I pull back. Then the distance grows, and I tell myself that proves I was never really wanted there in the first place. It’s this loop I can’t seem to break.

Nights are the hardest. That feeling when you want to reach out so badly, but you have no idea who wouldn’t see it as a burden or an intrusion. So you just lie there, scrolling, watching other people’s inside jokes and group photos, and it feels like there’s a glass wall between you and the rest of the world.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for. I just needed to put this somewhere.
