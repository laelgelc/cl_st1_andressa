I don’t even know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m fading out of my own life.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, a few old friends I text every now and then, family chats that light up my phone. But I feel like I’m behind glass. I can see everyone, I can hear them, I can even respond in the “right” way, but nothing ever feels like it actually reaches me, or like I reach them.

I’ve noticed that I keep editing myself down in conversations. I’ll start to say something real and then I hear this voice in my head: “No one wants to hear that. Keep it light.” So I make a joke, or I change the subject, and then later I’m lying in bed replaying the whole thing, wondering if anyone actually knows me or if they just know this carefully curated version.

The weirdest part is I don’t know how to fix it. I try to be more open and my brain instantly screams that I’m being “too much.” I try to reach out and then I stare at the message box until the moment passes. I’ll type “hey, are you free to hang out sometime?” and then delete it because I’m convinced I’m bothering them.

I’m at this point where I’m surrounded by noise but feel completely disconnected. I go days without having a conversation that feels real. Small talk at work, a meme in a group chat, a “how are you?” that isn’t really a question. I answer “I’m fine” so automatically that I almost believe it for a second, until I’m home again and it’s quiet and the emptiness hits me like a wave.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for posting this. Maybe I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
