I’ve been feeling like a ghost in my own life lately.

I technically have “people” around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats—but none of it feels like connection. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes, “how was your weekend?” followed by the same recycled answers. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m pressed up against a glass wall, watching everyone else actually live.

What gets to me most is how invisible this feels. From the outside, I probably look fine: I show up, I smile, I make small talk. Inside, it feels like there’s this heavy, constant ache that I can’t explain without sounding dramatic or needy. I don’t even know what I’d say if someone genuinely asked, “How are you really?” I’m out of practice being honest with anyone.

I miss feeling known. Not adored or admired, just… understood. The kind of comfort where you can send a half-finished thought and someone just gets it. I scroll through old messages with people I used to be close to and wonder at what point I became the person no one checks in on.

Sometimes I wonder if this is just how adulthood is now, or if there’s something fundamentally off about me that makes it hard for people to stay. I don’t have an answer.
