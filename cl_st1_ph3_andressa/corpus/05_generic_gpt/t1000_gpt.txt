I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I talk to coworkers, I answer emails, I make small jokes in meetings. People would probably describe me as quiet but friendly. The weird thing is how none of that seems to translate into actual connection. I leave work and it’s like someone hits mute on the world. No messages, no calls, no “hey, want to hang out?” Just silence and the same four walls.

I scroll through my phone sometimes just to prove to myself that there’s no one I can text without it feeling forced or awkward. Old group chats where I stopped replying and eventually no one noticed. Contacts that are basically just names attached to memories of when I still felt like I belonged somewhere.

What gets to me most is how invisible this all is. If you’re obviously struggling, people at least recognize it. But loneliness is so easy to hide behind “busy” and “tired” and “just staying in tonight.” I’ve become really good at saying, “Yeah, things are good,” while feeling like I’m floating outside of everything, watching other people have lives that somehow never intersect with mine.

I try to put myself out there occasionally—join a group, say yes to something—but it’s like I’m always one step out of sync. Everyone else already has their people, their inside jokes, their shared history. I’m just… there. Present but not really part of it. I leave those situations feeling more alone than before I went.

There’s this dull, constant ache of wanting someone to actually notice when I’m gone, to message me first, to remember something I said weeks ago and bring it up. I don’t need a crowd. I just want to feel like I exist in someone else’s mind when I’m not physically in front of them.

Right now it feels like I don’t.
