I don’t even know if what I feel counts as “lonely” anymore or just some weird default setting I’ve had for years.

I have people around me – coworkers, acquaintances, family chats that light up on holidays – but I don’t feel connected to any of them. It’s like I’m always slightly behind a glass wall, watching everyone else actually participate in life while I just…observe.

I try to join conversations, hangouts, whatever, but it always feels forced. I replay everything I say later and convince myself I sounded awkward or boring. Eventually I stop trying as much, then feel even more isolated, then hate myself for not trying. It’s a loop I can’t seem to break.

What scares me is how normalized it’s become. I can go days without a real, honest conversation and only notice it when I see other people casually sharing inside jokes or talking about their “person.” I don’t even know what it would feel like to be genuinely known by someone.
