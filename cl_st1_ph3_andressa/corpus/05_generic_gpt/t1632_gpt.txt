I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and “too much” at the same time.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats—but I don’t feel like I actually exist to anyone in the way I want to. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m pressed up against a glass wall, watching everyone else live.

I keep thinking about how long it’s been since someone asked me a genuine follow-up question, or remembered something small I said and brought it up later. I can’t remember the last time I felt like someone was really curious about me, not just filling silence.

What makes it worse is that I’m starting to forget how to connect. I overthink every message, every reply. I delete texts because I’m scared I’m being annoying or needy. Then I feel hurt when people don’t reach out, even though I’m the one pulling away. It’s like I’m sabotaging myself and I know I’m doing it, but I can’t seem to stop.

Nights are the hardest. That’s when the silence feels loud and my brain starts listing all the reasons I’m alone: not interesting enough, not attractive enough, not… enough. I scroll, I lurk, I distract myself, but the feeling just sits there, heavy and familiar, like it’s not going anywhere.
