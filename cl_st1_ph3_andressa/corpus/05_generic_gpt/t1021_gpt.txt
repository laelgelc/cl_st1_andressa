It’s weird how you can go through an entire day surrounded by people and still feel like you’re behind glass.

I talk to coworkers, I answer emails, I nod along in meetings, I even crack a few jokes. On paper, it looks like I’m functioning fine. But as soon as I step outside the office, it’s like the world goes silent. No one to text, no one to call “on the way home,” no one who’d really notice if I just disappeared into my room for a week.

I see people casually mention “my friend said…” or “I was talking to this person the other day…” and I realize I don’t have anyone I talk to like that. I have “people I used to be close to” and “people I’m friendly with,” but no one who feels like a real, present part of my life. I scroll through my contacts and there’s no one I feel comfortable messaging without overthinking it for an hour.

I keep wondering how much of this is my fault. I cancel plans sometimes because I’m tired or anxious, then feel hurt when people stop inviting me. I hold back what I really think or feel because I don’t want to be “too much,” and then I feel invisible because no one actually knows me. It’s like I’m slowly editing myself out of my own life.

Nights are the worst. That moment when you put your phone down and realize it hasn’t lit up all day unless you started the conversation. I start questioning whether I’m just fundamentally unlikeable, or if I somehow missed the window where everyone else learned how to form real connections.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put into words how heavy it feels to move through life like this, half-present, surrounded by people but not really belonging anywhere.
