I’m starting to realize that most of my “social life” is just background noise.

I have coworkers, classmates, people I chat with in group chats and comment sections. But I don’t feel like I actually exist to anyone once the context disappears. If I stopped showing up, people might notice for a week, then it would just… level out.

What really messes with me is how performative everything feels. I can be funny, helpful, “easy to talk to,” but it never crosses that invisible line into being someone’s person. I’m the side character who makes scenes smoother, not the one anyone thinks about when they go home.

Nights are the worst. I’ll scroll through my phone, rereading old conversations that just faded out, trying to figure out the exact message where I became “optional.” I keep wondering if I’m too quiet, too intense, too boring, too something. Or maybe just not enough of anything.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for anymore. I just want to feel like if I disappeared, it would matter to someone in a way that isn’t polite or obligatory.
