I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m fading out of my own life.

I have people around me—coworkers, family chats, the occasional “we should hang out sometime” from old friends—but it all feels like I’m watching through glass. I can go days without anyone asking how I actually am. When I try to open up, the conversation somehow shifts back to them, or to jokes, or to “you’ll be fine.”

I’m not completely isolated. I go to work, I answer messages, I smile when I’m supposed to. But the connection feels thin, like a bad Wi‑Fi signal that keeps dropping. I miss having someone who really knows me, who remembers small details without me reminding them, who notices when I go quiet.

Sometimes I catch myself rehearsing what I’d say if someone genuinely asked, “What’s going on with you?” Then no one asks, and the whole script just dissolves in my head again.
