I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here because it feels safer to say it to strangers.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m living next to my own life instead of actually in it. I go through the motions – work, errands, scrolling, sleep – but there’s this constant background feeling that I’m not really connected to anyone. I “interact” with people, but it all feels like acting out a script I never fully learned.

I have acquaintances, co-workers, people I joke with. On paper, I’m not isolated. But there’s no one I can text something small to, like “look at this stupid meme” or “I had a rough day, can I vent?” without overthinking it. I always feel like I’m intruding, like I’m the extra person in a group chat who could disappear and the dynamic wouldn’t change.

What makes it weird is that I’m not totally socially inept. I can be funny, I can hold a conversation, I can be the “good listener.” People tell me I’m “easy to talk to.” But the moment the conversation ends, it’s like I vanish from their minds. I’m there for other people, but no one seems to think of me first, or at all, when they need someone. It’s like I’m a side character in everyone else’s story.

Nights are the worst. I lie in bed and replay every small interaction, trying to figure out what’s wrong with me. Did I talk too much? Not enough? Was that joke weird? Did I come off as clingy? I end up convincing myself people are just being polite and secretly find me annoying or boring. Then I pull back, become more distant, and of course that just makes the loneliness worse.

I see friends from school getting married, having kids, going on trips together, and I’m just… here. Watching. Double-tapping photos of people who don’t really know me anymore, if they ever did. I keep telling myself I’ll “put myself out there” more, join things, reach out, but the fear of being unwanted is so strong that I usually talk myself out of it.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to say out loud that I’m lonely, instead of pretending I’m fine all the time.
