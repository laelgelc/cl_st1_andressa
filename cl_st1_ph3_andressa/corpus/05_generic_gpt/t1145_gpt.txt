I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I work, I answer emails, I make small talk about TV shows I don’t actually watch. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice.” No one would guess that when I get home and close the door, it feels like the air just drops out of the room.

I have people I text, but no one I’d call if something really went wrong at 3 a.m. I see friends occasionally, but it’s always in group settings, surface-level conversations where I play the role I’m used to: the listener, the one who nods and asks questions and laughs in the right places. I don’t think anyone actually knows what’s going on with me. I’m not even sure I know.

What hurts the most is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped showing up to things, I don’t know how long it would take anyone to notice. I’ve tested it a few times—stopped initiating, stopped reaching out—and everything just went quiet. People moved on with their lives like nothing was missing.

I keep wondering if I’m doing something wrong, like there’s some basic social skill everyone else got the manual for and I missed it. I overthink every interaction, replay conversations in my head, cringe at myself hours later. Then I get exhausted and withdraw even more, which obviously doesn’t help.

I’m tired of feeling like a background character in my own story, but I honestly don’t know how to step into the foreground without feeling like an intruder.
