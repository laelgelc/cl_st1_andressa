I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

Lately it feels like my life is technically “full” but emotionally empty. I have coworkers, I reply to messages, I show up to things I’m supposed to show up to. From the outside I probably look like I’m doing fine. Inside it feels like I’m walking around in a soundproof box. People see me, but nothing really reaches me and nothing I say seems to land anywhere meaningful.

I’ve noticed that most of my conversations are just logistics or small talk. “How was your weekend?” “Busy.” “You?” “Same.” Then silence. I keep waiting for that moment where it goes a layer deeper, where someone actually wants to know me, not just exchange noise until it’s polite to stop. It almost never happens unless I force it, and forcing it makes me feel needy and weird.

I also catch myself editing everything I say so I don’t come off as “too much” or “too negative.” I think I’ve built this careful, bland version of myself that’s easy to be around but impossible to be close to. Then I resent people for not seeing past it, even though I’m the one hiding.

Nights are the worst. That’s when the silence feels heavy and I start wondering if I’ve somehow missed the window where people form real friendships and relationships. Like the train left years ago and I’m just standing on the platform, watching other people post photos from inside.
