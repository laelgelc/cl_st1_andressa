I don’t really know how to describe what I’m feeling lately except that it’s like being surrounded by people and still feeling like a ghost.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers I talk to, a family group chat that occasionally pings, a couple of old friends I exchange memes with. But none of it feels like connection. It’s like everything in my life is stuck at this surface level, and I’m starving for something deeper while pretending I’m full.

I keep catching myself rehearsing conversations in my head that will never happen. Things I wish I could say to someone who actually wants to listen: how tired I am, how scared I am of wasting my life, how I feel like I’m always “too much” or “not enough.” But when I’m actually with people, I default to the safe script: work, TV shows, “yeah, I’m fine.” It’s like I’m editing myself in real time so I don’t make anyone uncomfortable.

The weird part is I don’t even know how to fix it. I’ve tried being more open and it usually gets awkward, or the other person just doesn’t know what to do with that level of honesty. I end up feeling exposed and stupid, like I misread the room. So I retreat, and every time I do, it gets harder to try again.

I’m at this point where I’m not sure if I’m lonely because there’s no one around me who really gets me, or because I’ve built such thick walls that no one can. I scroll through social media and see people hanging out, going on trips, having “best friends” and “soulmates,” and I can’t tell if that’s real or just curated. Either way, it makes me feel like I’m watching life through a window.

Most days I just keep going through the motions, doing what I’m supposed to do, hoping that at some point it’ll start to feel less empty. But right now it doesn’t.
