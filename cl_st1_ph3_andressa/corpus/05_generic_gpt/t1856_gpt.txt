I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s hitting me harder as I get older.

On paper, my life looks okay. I have a job, I pay my bills, I’m not completely isolated in the sense that I do interact with people. Coworkers, cashiers, neighbors. But it’s all surface-level, like I’m just playing a role. I can talk about work, weather, random stuff, but the moment it starts to feel like it could become real or vulnerable, I freeze up or pull back.

Most days I go through the motions, come home, and then it’s just... silence. I scroll through my phone, watch people’s stories, see them hanging out, getting married, having kids, going on trips with “their people,” and it feels like I’m watching life from behind a glass wall. I’m there, but not really part of it.

I’ve tried apps, meetups, hobbies. I’ll show up, make small talk, maybe even have a decent conversation. But then I go home and it’s like nothing sticks. No one follows up, or if they do, I get anxious and let the conversation die. I keep telling myself it’s because they’re not really interested, but I also know I’m part of the problem.

What bothers me most is the feeling that if I disappeared, my absence would be more of an inconvenience than an emotional loss to most people. Like, “Oh, that person from work isn’t here anymore,” and then life just goes on. Rationally, I know that’s true for most people in most situations, but it still hurts when I apply it to myself.

Anyway, I don’t really know what I’m looking for by posting this. Just needed to put it somewhere that isn’t just in my head.
