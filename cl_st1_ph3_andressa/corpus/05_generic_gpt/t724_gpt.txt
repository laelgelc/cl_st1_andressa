I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, and I’m not sure when that happened.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I can joke with, but it all feels like I’m playing a role. I can be “pleasant,” “helpful,” “funny enough,” but as soon as I walk away, it’s like none of it was real. No one really knows me, and I’m not even sure I’d know what to say if they asked.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through my phone, open and close apps, stare at chats I never start. I keep thinking, “If I message someone now, it’ll be weird” or “They’re probably busy,” and then another hour goes by and I’ve said nothing. Meanwhile, other people seem to just fall into friendships and relationships like it’s breathing.

I’ve noticed I censor myself constantly. I overthink every sentence, trying not to sound needy, boring, too intense, too quiet. By the time I’ve edited what I want to say in my head, the moment has passed. It’s like I’m always five seconds behind everyone else in every conversation.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. Waking up, going through the day, exchanging surface-level words, coming home, and sinking into that heavy, quiet feeling. I keep telling myself I’ll “try harder tomorrow,” but tomorrow looks exactly like today, and I don’t know how to break out of it.
