I’m starting to feel like I’m disappearing while still being technically “around” people.

On paper, my life doesn’t look that lonely. I have coworkers I chat with, family I text sometimes, a couple of old friends I can message if I really need to. But most of the time I just feel like I’m orbiting everyone instead of actually being connected to anyone.

I’ll have these moments where I look at my phone and realize I haven’t had a real conversation in days. Not the “how’s it going?” “fine, you?” kind of thing, but the kind where you feel seen, where you can say something messy and not have to clean it up right away. I can’t even remember the last time I spoke out loud without editing myself in real time.

I keep thinking about how my relationships feel like faded versions of what they used to be. Friends have moved, gotten married, had kids, gotten busy. I don’t blame them, life moves on. But I feel like I got left in this weird in-between space where I’m not important enough to be included, but not distant enough to be completely forgotten. I get invited to things occasionally, but it always feels like an afterthought or a “we should include them too” type of invite.

I’ve tried to put myself out there: joined groups, gone to meetups, tried talking to people online. It always stalls out in the “surface-level acquaintance” phase. I don’t know if it’s me being awkward or people just not being interested, but it’s exhausting to keep trying when everything stays shallow.

The worst part is how it messes with your sense of self. When you go long enough without anyone really checking in on you, you start to wonder if you’re actually as boring or unlikable as your brain says you are. I second-guess everything I say. I’ll send a message and then stare at the “seen” status and convince myself I said too much, or not enough, or the wrong thing entirely.

Most days I just go through the motions: work, come home, scroll, sleep. I keep hoping something will shift on its own, but nothing really does. It’s like I’m waiting for my own life to start, and in the meantime I’m just here, watching everyone else live theirs from the sidelines.
