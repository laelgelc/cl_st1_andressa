I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

I’m “fine” on paper. Job, roof over my head, people I can technically text. But I don’t feel connected to anyone. Conversations are surface-level, like I’m playing a character who knows the right lines but doesn’t feel any of it. I go home after work and there’s this heavy silence that almost feels physical.

I’ve tried the usual advice: hobbies, meetups, talking to coworkers, even therapy. I can show up, I can be polite, but I never get past that acquaintance stage. People already have their circles, their inside jokes, their shared history. I’m the extra chair at the table that no one really needs.

What messes with my head the most is how invisible it all is. From the outside I probably look normal, maybe even “busy.” Inside, it’s just this constant ache and this sense that if I disappeared from everyone’s lives, they’d adjust in a week or two and be fine.

Sometimes I wonder if I missed some crucial stage in life where people learned how to form real bonds, and now I’m just pretending. I can’t tell if I’m the one keeping people at a distance or if I’m just fundamentally forgettable.

There isn’t really a point to this post beyond needing to say it somewhere. I’m tired of hearing my own thoughts echo back at me.
