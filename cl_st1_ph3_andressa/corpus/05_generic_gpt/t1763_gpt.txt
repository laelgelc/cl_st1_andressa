I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore. I can be around others, talk, even joke a bit, but it all feels like I’m acting out a version of myself that people might tolerate. When I get home, it’s just silence and the feeling that nobody really saw me.

I watch other people form friendships so easily—group chats, inside jokes, casual hangouts—and I feel like I’m standing behind a glass wall. I’m not completely isolated, but the relationships I do have feel shallow or one-sided. I’m scared that if I showed how lonely I really am, I’d push people away.

The worst part is starting to believe this is just how my life is going to be: surface-level interactions, no one to really lean on, and too much time alone with my own thoughts.
