I don’t know when “being alone a lot” quietly turned into “feeling lonely almost all the time,” but it has.

My days are basically work, commute, screen, sleep. I talk to coworkers about tasks and deadlines, but no one really knows me. I have people I can message, but no one I feel comfortable calling at 2 a.m. if everything fell apart. I keep thinking, “If I disappeared, who would actually notice quickly?” and I don’t have a clear answer.

I try to join things—Discords, group chats, even local meetups—but I always end up feeling like the extra chair at the table: technically there, but not really part of anything. I’m either “too quiet” or I overshare from the desperation of finally having someone listen, and then I feel weird and pull back again.

It’s a strange mix of wanting connection so badly and being exhausted by every failed attempt. I look at other people’s casual friendships and inside jokes and it feels like a language I never properly learned.
