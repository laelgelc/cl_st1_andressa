I’m in that weird place where I’m never technically “alone,” but I feel completely disconnected anyway.

I have coworkers, a couple of acquaintances I see every few weeks, and family I text with out of obligation. On paper, it looks like I have people. In reality, I feel like I’m just rotating through roles: employee, sibling, “friend,” but never actually myself with anyone.

Conversations feel like performances. I know what to say to keep things light and normal, and I do it automatically. But as soon as I try to say anything real, I can see people pull back, or change the subject, or give a quick “that sucks” and move on. After a while, you just stop trying.

I scroll through chats and realize almost every thread is me reacting, not initiating. When I do reach out, it’s usually met with slow replies or short answers, and I end up feeling like I forced myself into someone’s day.

What scares me most is how normal this has started to feel. I go through days barely speaking to anyone outside of work, and it doesn’t even shock me anymore. It just feels like the default.
