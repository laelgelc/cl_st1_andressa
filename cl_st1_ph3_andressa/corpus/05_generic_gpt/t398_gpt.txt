I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of inside it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, a couple of people I can text if I really need something. But most days it feels like I’m just orbiting other people’s worlds, never actually part of anything. I’m always the extra person in a group chat, the one people forget to tag, the one who shows up to things and somehow still feels invisible.

I can make small talk. I can be “pleasant.” I can even be funny sometimes. But it never seems to turn into real connection. Conversations slide off the surface and then it’s back to silence. I leave social situations feeling more drained and more alone than before I went.

What scares me is how normal this has started to feel. I wake up, go to work, come home, scroll, eat something, lie in bed with my phone lighting up my face, and realize I haven’t actually spoken to anyone in a way that mattered. Days blur together. I keep thinking, “This can’t be it,” but I don’t know what to do differently.

I’m starting to feel like there’s something fundamentally off about me that other people can sense right away, like a quiet “do not get too close” signal I don’t know how to switch off. And I don’t know how long a person can go on feeling like this without it changing them into someone they don’t recognize.
