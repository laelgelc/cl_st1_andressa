I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… empty space with me echoing around inside it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I talk to coworkers, I answer “not bad” when people ask how I am. I send memes in group chats and people reply with laughing emojis. But underneath all of that, I feel like I’m watching my own life from behind glass. I’m there, but not really there.

What hits me the hardest is how replaceable I feel. If I disappeared from work, someone would fill my role. If I stopped messaging, the group chat would keep going without a glitch. I don’t think there’s a single person who would notice the *specific* shape of my absence, if that makes sense. Not just “oh, someone’s gone,” but “*you* are gone.”

I miss a kind of connection I’m not sure I’ve ever actually had. The late-night phone calls, the “I saw this and thought of you” texts that aren’t just politeness, the feeling that someone actually wants to know the boring details of my day. Most of my conversations feel like small talk stretched over a void.

I keep wondering if there’s something fundamentally off about me that people can sense, like a quiet warning sign: “Don’t get too close.” I try to be kind, I listen, I show up when people need help. But when it’s over, they go back to their lives, and I go back to my room, and the silence feels louder every year.
