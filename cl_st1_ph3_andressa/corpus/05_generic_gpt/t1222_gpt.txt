I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to be close to people.

On paper, I’m “fine” – job, roof over my head, a couple of acquaintances I can text memes to. But I don’t have anyone I feel safe being fully myself with. Every interaction feels like I’m performing a slightly edited version of me, and then going home and replaying every sentence in my head, cringing at things the other person probably didn’t even notice.

I keep thinking I want friends, but when opportunities show up, I freeze. I don’t know how often you’re “supposed” to message someone, how vulnerable is too vulnerable, when you’re crossing the line from friendly to annoying. So I default to silence, and then the connection fades, and I tell myself “see, they didn’t really like you anyway.”

Most days are just work, commute, screen, sleep. Weekends are the worst. I’ll scroll through social media and see people doing basic things together—coffee, movies, board games—and it feels like watching a life that’s behind glass.

What scares me is how quickly I’m getting used to this. The routines, the quiet, the way nobody really notices if I disappear for a while. It’s starting to feel normal, and I’m not sure if that’s better or worse.
