I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I say “not much, you?” when people ask how I’m doing. I have acquaintances I can joke with, but no one I can text at 2 a.m. when my chest feels heavy for no obvious reason. Most of my conversations stay at the surface: weather, work, memes. I can feel myself wanting to say something real, and then I just… don’t.

What scares me is how used to it I’m becoming. I move through my days like I’m on autopilot, then come home and scroll my phone until I’m too tired to think. The silence in my apartment is so loud, but the idea of reaching out and being brushed off feels worse than the quiet.

I keep wondering if I somehow missed the part of life where everyone else learned how to form real connections. I watch people talk so easily, make plans, have inside jokes, and I’m just there on the sidelines, smiling and nodding, feeling like an extra in my own story.

Sometimes I’m not even sure what I’d say if someone genuinely asked how I was. I don’t think “lonely” even covers it anymore.
