I don’t even know if what I feel counts as “loneliness” anymore or just some kind of permanent background condition.

I’m surrounded by people a lot of the time – coworkers, neighbors, random small talk – but it all feels like I’m acting in a play. I say the right things, smile at the right moments, but it never feels like anyone is actually seeing *me*. It’s like I’m a placeholder person in other people’s lives.

I have acquaintances, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something really bad happened. I scroll through my contacts and realize there’s no one I can message without feeling like I’m bothering them or overstepping some invisible boundary. So I just don’t. Then I feel even more isolated, and the cycle keeps feeding itself.

What bothers me most is how invisible this feels from the outside. I do my job, I crack jokes, I show up when I’m supposed to. People would probably be surprised if they knew how much time I spend in my own head, replaying old conversations and wondering if I somehow pushed everyone away without noticing.

I miss the feeling of being known. Not just “How was your weekend?” but someone remembering the weird specific things about me, or noticing when I’m off without me having to announce it.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for a change.
