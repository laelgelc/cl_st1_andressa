I’m starting to realize that I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m pressed up against a glass wall, watching everything instead of actually being part of it.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, a few people I can text memes to, but I don’t really have anyone I can message at 2 a.m. and say, “Hey, I’m not okay,” without feeling like I’m being a burden. Every time I try to open up, I either downplay it with jokes or panic afterward and disappear for a while.

What confuses me is that on the outside I probably look fine. I go to work, I make small talk, I laugh in the right places. But then I come home and it’s like all the noise shuts off and I’m left with this heavy silence that feels almost physical. I keep hoping that if I just “put myself out there” more, something will change, but I don’t even know what that practically means anymore.

Mostly I just feel tired of hearing my own thoughts echo back at me.
