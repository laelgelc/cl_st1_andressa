I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people, technically. Coworkers, family chats, a couple of old friends I send memes to. But I feel like a background character in my own life. I show up, do what’s expected, laugh at the right moments, and go home feeling like I didn’t actually connect with anyone.

The weirdest part is how invisible it feels. No one is cruel to me. I’m “liked,” I guess. But no one really knows me. I can’t remember the last time someone asked me a question that went deeper than work, hobbies, or weekend plans and actually stayed to hear the full, messy answer.

I’ve tried to open up a few times, but it always feels like I’m making things awkward, or oversharing, or being “too much.” So I pull back. Then I end up scrolling through my phone at night, checking if anyone messaged me first, pretending I don’t care when they don’t.

I miss feeling chosen. Not out of convenience or habit, but because someone genuinely wants me around. I miss long, meandering conversations that go late into the night. I miss feeling like if I disappeared for a week, someone would actually notice for real, not just because I didn’t answer an email.

Mostly, I’m just tired of talking to myself in my own head all the time.
