I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living in some kind of quiet bubble that no one else can see.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I answer messages, I show up when I’m supposed to. People would probably describe me as “nice” or “chill.” But most of my interactions feel like I’m playing a role. I laugh when I’m supposed to, ask questions back, keep the conversation going. Then I go home and it’s just…silence. Not peaceful silence, more like the sound of an empty house settling.

I’ve noticed that I’m always the one to initiate plans, and when I stop, everything just kind of stops. No one really checks in unless they need something or happen to remember I exist. I don’t think anyone actually knows what’s going on in my head because I’ve trained myself to say “I’m good, just tired” automatically.

What bothers me most is how replaceable I feel. Like if I disappeared from social media or group chats, people might notice for a bit, then adjust and move on. There’s this constant ache of wanting to be important to someone, to be the person they think of first, not last, not as an afterthought.

I’m not even sure what I’m asking for here. I just needed to put this somewhere outside my own head.
