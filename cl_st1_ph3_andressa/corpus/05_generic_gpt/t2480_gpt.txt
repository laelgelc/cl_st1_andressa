I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too exposed at the same time.

On paper, I’m “fine” — job, small apartment, a couple of acquaintances I sometimes see. But I don’t feel like I actually exist in anyone’s mind when I’m not physically there. No one texts just to check in. If I don’t reach out, days go by in complete silence except for work emails and random notifications.

When I do see people, I feel like I’m playing a role: the “okay” version of myself who can make small talk and laugh at the right spots. Meanwhile there’s this heavy, dull ache underneath, like I’m watching my own life from the outside. I leave gatherings feeling more alone than before, because it just highlights how shallow most of my connections are.

I’ve tried joining clubs, going to events, chatting online. It’s like there’s a glass wall — I can see everyone, hear them, but I can’t actually get through to the part where you become important to each other. I worry that I’m just fundamentally forgettable.

What scares me is how normal this has started to feel. Like this is just how my life is now: presentable on the surface, hollow underneath, and no one really noticing the difference.
