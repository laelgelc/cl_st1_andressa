I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of inside it.

I’m surrounded by people in the technical sense—coworkers, neighbors, the usual small talk—but none of it seems to touch anything real. I go to work, answer emails, nod in meetings, come home, scroll, sleep. Repeat. There’s no one I feel I can text just to say, “I had a weird day, can I talk?” I don’t even know who I’d put as an emergency contact if I had to fill out a form right now.

What bothers me most is how invisible this feels. From the outside I probably look fine: functioning adult, decent job, not a complete recluse. But there’s this constant ache, like being homesick for a place or person that doesn’t exist. I watch other people just… belonging to each other. Group chats, inside jokes, weekend plans. I’m happy for them, but it also feels like standing outside a house, watching a warm light through the window, knowing it’s not for you.

I’ve tried things—meetups, hobbies, messaging old friends—but everything fizzles out or stays surface-level. I start worrying I’m too quiet, too awkward, too “much” or “not enough” in some way I can’t quite fix. So I pull back, which obviously doesn’t help, and the cycle continues.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe just to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
