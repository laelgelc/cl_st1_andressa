I don’t really know how to talk about this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… background noise. Like I’m in a room full of people and somehow I’m the only one on mute.

I have “people” in my life, technically. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every few weeks, family chats where we send memes and birthday messages. But it all feels so surface-level. No one really knows what my days look like, what my nights feel like, or what’s going on in my head. And I don’t really know how to let them in without feeling like I’m burdening them or being weirdly intense.

The worst part is that I’m not even sure I remember how to be close to someone. I’ve gotten so used to saying “I’m fine” that it feels like a script I can’t break out of. When someone asks how I’m doing, there’s this split second where I want to say, “Actually, I feel completely disconnected and empty most of the time,” but then I hear myself say, “Yeah, I’m good, just busy,” and the moment passes.

Nights are the hardest. I scroll, I watch stuff, I try to distract myself, but there’s this heavy awareness that no one is thinking about me. No one’s waiting for my message, no one’s wondering how my day went. It makes me feel strangely invisible, like if I quietly disappeared from social media and stopped replying, it would just be a mild inconvenience at most.

I keep telling myself I should try harder—join something, message people first, be more open—but there’s this tiredness that hits every time I think about it. Like I’m already exhausted from rehearsing conversations that never actually happen.
