I’m not even sure if I’m lonely or just fundamentally disconnected from everyone around me.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, coworkers I can chat with, a family I text with, a few old friends I exchange memes with. But it all feels like I’m acting out a role instead of actually being part of anything. I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m on the other side of thick glass, watching life happen instead of living it.

What gets to me is how invisible this feels. If you looked at my life from the outside, you’d probably think I’m relatively social. I show up to work events, I laugh at jokes, I ask people about their weekend. No one sees that I go home and sit in silence for hours, scrolling through my phone not because I’m interested in anything, but because the alternative is just staring at the walls and feeling that heavy emptiness.

I don’t really know how to connect with people beyond the surface level. I want deeper conversations, the kind where you can be honest without worrying you’ll scare the other person away. But when I get the chance, I freeze. I default to small talk, I make it about them, I keep myself hidden. Then I go home and feel hurt that no one really knows me, even though I’m the one who never lets them.

There’s also this weird shame tied to it. I’m an adult; I feel like I should’ve “figured out” friendships and relationships by now. Admitting that I’m lonely feels like admitting I’m defective somehow. I see people forming connections so easily, like it’s breathing, and I’m stuck overthinking every text message.

Sometimes I wonder if this is just how it’s going to be: existing around people but not really with them, always slightly out of sync. I don’t have some big plan to fix it. Mostly I just needed to put this somewhere, because carrying it around in my head all the time is starting to feel heavier than I can handle on my own.
