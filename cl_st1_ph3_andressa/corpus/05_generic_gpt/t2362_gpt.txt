I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I exist in the negative space of other people’s lives.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I can make small talk about work or shows or whatever. But there’s this constant, quiet ache that never really goes away, like I’m always slightly out of focus in my own life.

My phone almost never lights up unless it’s a notification from an app or some automated email. If I scroll back through my messages, it’s mostly me reaching out, me asking “how are you?” and then the conversation dying after a couple of exchanges. I can’t remember the last time someone texted me first just to talk, not because they needed something.

I’ve had “friends” over the years, but it feels like I’m always the peripheral one. The person who gets invited if someone remembers, not the one people plan around. When I’m actually with people, I can laugh and join in, but there’s this weird sense of watching myself from outside, like I’m playing a role. As soon as I go home, the silence hits really hard and it’s like I was never really there.

What really gets to me is how hard it is to form anything deeper than surface-level connections as an adult. I don’t even know how to move from “we chat sometimes” to “we’re actually close.” I worry I’m too quiet, or too awkward, or just not interesting enough to stick in anyone’s mind once I’m out of sight.

Nights are the worst. That’s when everything catches up with me. I end up lying there replaying conversations, overanalyzing what I said, wondering if I came off weird or needy. I keep thinking there must be some invisible rulebook everyone else got about how to build real relationships, and I somehow missed it.

Mostly I just feel like I’m drifting. People pass through my life like background characters, and I can’t tell if it’s them keeping their distance or me not knowing how to cross the gap.
