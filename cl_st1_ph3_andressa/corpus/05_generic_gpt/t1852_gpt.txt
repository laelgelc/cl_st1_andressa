I’ve been feeling this really specific kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t quite fit into the usual “no friends / no relationship” boxes, even though those are part of it too.

On paper, I’m not completely alone. I have coworkers I get along with, a few old friends I text every now and then, family I can visit on holidays. But the weird thing is that I feel the most alone when I’m actually around people. It’s like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else. I can see them, hear them, laugh at their jokes, but it doesn’t feel like I’m really *with* them.

I notice it most in group conversations. People talk about their weekends, their partners, their kids, their trips, their plans. I chime in with something surface-level, they respond politely, and then the conversation moves on. No one is being mean. It’s just like I’m a background character in a movie, there to fill space so the main characters don’t look like they’re talking to themselves.

I’m also realizing how much of my “connection” with people is logistical or functional. “What time is the meeting?” “Can you send that file?” “Are you free Saturday?” When I try to go deeper—say something honest like “I’ve been feeling pretty off lately”—it usually gets brushed past, or people say “same” and then switch topics. I get it; everyone has their own stuff. But it reinforces this idea that my inner world isn’t really something anyone wants to linger on.

What makes it worse is that I don’t even know how to fix it. I’ve tried being more open, I’ve tried initiating plans, I’ve tried being the “funny one” or the “good listener.” I can play those roles, but none of them feel like me. And when I’m finally alone again, I feel this heavy emptiness, like I spent all this energy performing and still came back with nothing.

I keep thinking there must be some missing piece—some way other people naturally connect that I never learned. But I’m starting to worry that this might just be how my life feels: surrounded, but separate.
