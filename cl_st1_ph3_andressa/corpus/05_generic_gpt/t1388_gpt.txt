I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here because it feels like the only place where it might make sense.

Lately I’ve been feeling this really specific kind of loneliness that has nothing to do with how many people are around me. I have coworkers, acquaintances, group chats that light up all day, but I still feel like a background character in my own life. I show up, say the right things, do the tasks, laugh at the jokes, and then go home and feel like I was never actually there.

What bothers me most is that people seem to like the “version” of me I present, but I don’t feel like anyone knows the actual person underneath. When conversations start getting even slightly deeper than surface-level, I feel this panic and change the subject or make a joke. Then later I get upset that nobody knows me. It’s like I’m the one locking the door and then feeling abandoned when no one comes in.

I scroll through social media and see people posting about their “people” – best friends, partners, tight-knit groups – and I can’t think of a single person I’d call in the middle of the night if something really bad happened. I have people I could text, sure, but no one where I feel confident that I’m a priority, not just an option.

There’s also this weird shame attached to all of it. I’m an adult, supposedly functional, and on paper my life looks fine. Admitting that I’m lonely feels childish, like something I should’ve grown out of. So I end up pretending I’m just “busy” or “tired” instead of saying, “I feel completely disconnected and I don’t know how to fix it.”

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put into words that I feel invisible, even when people are looking right at me.
