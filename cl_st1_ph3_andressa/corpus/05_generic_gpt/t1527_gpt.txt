I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s hitting me harder as I get older.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I talk to coworkers, I answer messages in group chats. But it all feels like I’m playing a character who’s loosely based on me. I can be funny, helpful, “easy to talk to,” but as soon as the interaction ends, I feel this weird emptiness, like nothing actually touched me.

I don’t really have “people.” No one I can text without overthinking it. No one who would notice if I disappeared for a while, except maybe out of convenience. I scroll through my contacts and realize most of them are old classmates, ex-coworkers, or people I only talk to when they need something.

What messes with my head is that I don’t know if I’m the problem because I keep people at arm’s length, or if I learned to do that because people never stayed. I catch myself rehearsing what I’ll say before I send a simple message, then deleting it because it suddenly feels embarrassing or “too much.”

Nights are the worst. That’s when I feel the gap between the life I’m living and the life I thought I’d have by now. Friends, routines, inside jokes, someone who knows me beyond surface-level facts. Instead, it’s just me, my phone, and a silence that feels heavier than it should.
