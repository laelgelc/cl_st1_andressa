I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into the usual “I don’t have friends” box. I technically have people in my life – coworkers I chat with, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family group chats that light up with memes. But when I actually try to think of someone I could really be honest with, I kind of draw a blank.

It’s like I’m always playing a role: the “reliable one” at work, the “easygoing one” with friends, the “fine, just busy” one with family. I can’t remember the last time I said, “I’m not okay,” out loud to another human being and actually meant it. I’ll type something vulnerable into a message, stare at it for a few seconds, then delete it and replace it with a joke or a neutral response.

Nights are the worst. I’ll scroll through social media and see people hanging out, getting married, having kids, going on trips with “their people,” and I feel like I’m stuck behind glass, watching life happen to everyone else. I’m not totally isolated, but I feel weirdly…unclaimed. Like I exist on the periphery of everyone’s life, but not at the center of anyone’s.

I also feel ashamed of how lonely I am. Like I’ve somehow failed at being a person. I start thinking, “If you were more interesting, more fun, more something, you wouldn’t feel this way.” Then I get stuck in this loop of wanting connection but being afraid to reach out because I don’t want to seem needy or pathetic.

I don’t even know what I’m expecting by writing this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere instead of letting it sit in my head all the time.
