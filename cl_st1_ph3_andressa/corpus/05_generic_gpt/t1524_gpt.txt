I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people, technically. I have coworkers, some acquaintances, a family group chat that pings every now and then. But I keep having this feeling that if I disappeared, the first reaction would be confusion about missed messages or responsibilities, not actual concern about *me*.

I’m not completely isolated. I go to work, I respond to texts, I say “I’m good, you?” like everyone else. But it feels like I’m just playing a character I’ve been stuck with for years. I don’t think anyone really knows what’s going on in my head, and I’m not sure anyone wants to. When I’ve tried to open up in the past, people either change the subject, give quick advice, or get uncomfortable. After a while, I just stopped trying.

The weird part is how small the loneliness makes me. It’s like I’m constantly shrinking inside my own life. I second-guess every message I send, edit and re-edit before pressing enter, then reread it afterward to see if I sounded weird. Sometimes I delete messages mid-typing because I convince myself I’m being annoying.

Nights are the worst. I’ll scroll through social media, see people hanging out, group photos, inside jokes in comments. I know it’s curated and not the full story, but it still hits this raw nerve: I don’t have “my people.” I have people I orbit around, but no one I feel securely attached to.

I’m not even sure what I’m looking for posting this. I just needed to say out loud, somewhere, that I feel really alone, even when I’m technically not.
