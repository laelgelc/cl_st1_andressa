I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s getting harder to pretend otherwise.

On paper I’m “fine”: job, roof over my head, a couple of acquaintances I can message about surface-level stuff. But most days feel like I’m watching life through a window. I go to work, I come home, I scroll, I sleep. Repeat. Whole weeks pass where my voice barely gets used outside of meetings and “have a good one” to the cashier.

What hits me the most is how invisible I feel. I can disappear for days and no one checks in. I see people talking about group chats blowing up, friends dropping by unannounced, family dinners. My phone is mostly silent unless I’m the one initiating, and even then, conversations fizzle out quickly. It makes me feel like I’m forcing myself into other people’s lives.

I’ve tried joining clubs, going to events, making small talk. I can get through the “where are you from, what do you do” part, but it never seems to go deeper. Everyone already has their circles. I end up standing on the edge, pretending I’m fine with just observing.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. The quiet, the empty evenings, the sense that I’m just filling time. I don’t even know what I’d say if someone genuinely wanted to know me. I’m not sure there’s much to know anymore.
