I’ve been feeling like a ghost in my own life lately.

I’m surrounded by people a lot of the time—at work, on the train, even in my own family group chats—but it all feels like background noise. I can make small talk, crack a joke, look “fine,” but it never seems to go any deeper than that. When conversations end, it’s like I was never really there.

I scroll social media and see everyone posting about friends, partners, trips, inside jokes. I know it’s curated, but it still hits like a reminder that I’m not really part of anyone’s “we.” I’m just “me,” orbiting around other people’s lives.

I miss having someone who actually checks in, unprompted. Someone who remembers small details about me because they care, not because I repeated them three times. I’m tired of being the one who initiates, the one who reaches out and then overanalyzes every silence.

It’s starting to feel less like a phase and more like who I am now.
