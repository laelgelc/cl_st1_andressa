I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m fading out of everyone’s peripheral vision.

I have people in my life, technically. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I text once in a while, family chats that light up on holidays and birthdays. But I can go whole weekends without hearing my name spoken out loud. My phone is either silent or full of group notifications where my presence doesn’t really matter.

I try to initiate plans, conversations, anything. People say “we should definitely hang out” and then vanish. I’m tired of being the one who reaches out, but when I stop, everything just…stops.

I’m starting to feel like I must be fundamentally off-putting somehow, like there’s something about me that people tolerate but don’t choose. I can’t tell if I’m lonely because I’ve withdrawn, or if I’ve withdrawn because I’m lonely. It’s just this loop I can’t seem to break, and I’m not sure what to do with that anymore.
