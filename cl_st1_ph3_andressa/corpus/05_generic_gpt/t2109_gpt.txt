It’s weird how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re sealed off behind glass.

I’m not totally isolated. I have coworkers, a few acquaintances I can grab a drink with, family I text sometimes. On paper it looks like I shouldn’t feel lonely. But most of my interactions feel like I’m playing a character: the “fine, busy, doing okay” version of me. The real stuff never really makes it into the conversation.

I scroll through chats and realize how many are just dead threads. People I used to talk to every day are now names I’m almost afraid to message because I don’t know how to bridge the gap of silence. I overthink every “hey, how have you been?” and usually just don’t send it. Then I feel even more alone and blame myself for not trying.

What bothers me most is the lack of being known. Not just having someone to hang out with, but someone who actually understands how my brain works, what I’m afraid of, what I care about. I don’t know if it’s that I never learned how to open up properly, or if I’m just not the kind of person people feel close to.

Nights are the worst. That’s when everything goes quiet and it’s just me and the feeling that everyone else has somehow figured out how to build a life with connections, and I’m stuck on the outside looking in. I keep telling myself I’ll change something “soon,” but the days keep passing and it all stays the same.
