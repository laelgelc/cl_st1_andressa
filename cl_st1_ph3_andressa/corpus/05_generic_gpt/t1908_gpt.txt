I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

I feel like I’m living in this weird in‑between state where I’m not totally isolated, but I’m still lonely almost all the time. I have people I can text, a couple of friends I see every now and then, coworkers I joke with. On paper it looks like I’m “fine.” But it all feels thin, like I’m standing behind glass watching everyone else actually connect.

When I’m with people, I can play the role: I laugh, I ask questions, I nod in the right places. But it feels like I’m performing a version of myself that’s easier to digest. I can’t remember the last time I said exactly what I was thinking without softening it, editing it, or just swallowing it. And then I go home and replay every conversation, convinced I was too quiet, too weird, too much, or not enough.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through chats and social media, watching other people’s group photos, random hangouts, “we just dropped by” moments. My life doesn’t have many of those. Everything with me is scheduled, planned weeks in advance, and even then it’s often “something came up, can we reschedule?” until it just fades away.

I’m at this point where I don’t even know what I’m craving exactly. I say I want “real connection” but I don’t know what that looks like anymore. Someone I can message without overthinking the timing or the wording. Someone who actually notices when I disappear for a few days. Someone I don’t have to constantly monitor myself around.

What makes it harder is feeling like I’m the common denominator. Other people seem to just drift into friendships and relationships naturally, and I feel like I’m always trying to force it or hold it together with both hands. I can’t tell if I’m too guarded, too intense, too distant, or just fundamentally uninteresting.

I’m not looking for advice, really. I just needed to put this somewhere that isn’t the notes app on my phone. It’s strange how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like there’s this empty space no one even sees.
