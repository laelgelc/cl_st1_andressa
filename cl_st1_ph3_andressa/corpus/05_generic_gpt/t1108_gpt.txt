I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, a few people I can text if I really need something. But there’s this constant quiet ache, like I’m permanently a few inches away from everyone else, watching through glass. I go through the motions—work, errands, scrolling, sleep—but everything feels like filler between nothing and nothing.

I’m not completely isolated. I talk to coworkers, I answer “how are you?” with the usual “good, you?” and sometimes I even laugh at a joke. But none of it feels like it lands anywhere. It’s all surface-level. I don’t feel known. I’m not sure I even remember what it feels like to be known—like really known, where you can just exist around someone without performing.

What messes with me is that I’m not even sure I’d know what to do if someone actually tried to get close. I’ve spent so long numbing out and minimizing my own needs that when I do start to want connection, it feels embarrassing, almost childish. I second-guess every message I send, overanalyze response times, and usually end up backing off so I don’t “bother” anyone.

I see other people forming friendships, relationships, group chats, inside jokes. I watch it like a spectator sport. I tell myself they just got lucky, or they’re naturally more likable, or they met people at the “right time.” But underneath that rationalizing is this ugly fear that maybe I’m just fundamentally forgettable. The kind of person people are polite to, but don’t think about once I’m out of sight.

Nights are the worst. That’s when the silence gets heavy. I’ll put on a podcast or a show just to have voices in the room, but it doesn’t really help. It just highlights that they’re not talking to me. No one is.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
