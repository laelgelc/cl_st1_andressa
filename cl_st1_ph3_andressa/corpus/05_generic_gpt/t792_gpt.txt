I don’t know when “being alone a lot” quietly turned into “I feel like I don’t exist to anyone.”

I have people around me in the most technical sense—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats that light up with memes—but I can go days without anyone asking how I’m actually doing. And I’ve noticed I don’t really ask them either, at least not in a way that invites a real answer. It’s like we’re all skimming the surface and I’m sinking underneath it.

The worst part is how invisible the loneliness is. From the outside, I probably look fine: I go to work, I respond to messages, I post the occasional thing. Inside, it feels like there’s this constant ache that I’m not important to anyone’s daily life. If I stopped reaching out, I’m not sure how long it would take before someone noticed.

I keep catching myself rehearsing conversations in my head that never actually happen. Then I just put my phone down and sit in the quiet, waiting for a notification that doesn’t come.
