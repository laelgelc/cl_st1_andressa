I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into any simple category. It’s not just “I don’t have friends” (I technically do) and it’s not exactly “I’m depressed” (though maybe that’s in the mix too). It’s more like I’m surrounded by people but no one really sees me, and I’m starting to wonder if that’s my fault or just how things are.

I have a few friends I talk to online, and some coworkers I get along with. I can go out for a drink or send memes in group chats. On paper that sounds “connected,” but it all feels surface-level. I notice that when conversations start to dip into anything deeper than jokes or small talk, I either shut down or people just… change the subject. I don’t know if I’m giving off some kind of “don’t get close to me” vibe, or if I’m just not that interesting once you get past the basics.

What really gets to me is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped messaging people, I’m pretty sure I’d just fade out of their lives. No big fight, no drama, just a slow, quiet disappearance. I’ve tested it a couple of times—stopped initiating conversations—and the silence that followed was way louder than I expected. It’s like realizing you’re more of an optional extra than an actual part of anyone’s life.

I also feel like I’ve forgotten how to be genuinely open with people. When someone asks how I’m doing, I have this automatic script: “I’m fine, just tired.” I don’t even think about it. The idea of saying, “Actually, I feel really alone and disconnected most of the time,” feels almost embarrassing, like I’d be burdening them or making things awkward.

Nights are the worst. That’s when it really hits me that there’s no one I can just message and be fully honest with, without overthinking every word. I keep imagining what it would feel like to have someone who actually notices when I go quiet and cares enough to ask why, but it feels more like a daydream than something that’s actually going to happen.
