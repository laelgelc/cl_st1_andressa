I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and “too much” at the same time.

I’m surrounded by people in the technical sense—coworkers, neighbors, people on social media—but it all feels like background noise. I can go an entire week without anyone asking me a genuine question about my life. It’s always small talk, or logistics, or memes. I laugh, I respond, but it’s like I’m playing a character who has friends instead of actually having them.

What gets to me most is that I *used* to have close connections. I’ve had best friends, late-night talks, that feeling of being known. Somewhere along the way, people moved, got partners, had kids, got busy. I get it. But I never really replaced those connections. I just… thinned out.

Now I hesitate to reach out because I feel like I’m interrupting other people’s actual lives. I type messages and delete them. I tell myself, “If they wanted to talk, they would.” Then I sit there hoping my phone lights up for some reason other than a notification from an app I don’t care about.

I’m not even sure what I’m looking for anymore. I just know that the silence in my life is starting to feel heavier, like it’s pressing down on everything I do.
