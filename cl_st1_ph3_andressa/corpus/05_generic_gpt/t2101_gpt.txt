I’m starting to realize that I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m on mute.

I have coworkers, acquaintances, even a few people I could technically call friends, but everything feels…thin. Conversations never get past surface level. I listen, I ask questions, I nod in the right places, but I don’t feel known. If I disappeared, I’m honestly not sure anyone’s day-to-day life would change much.

I keep thinking it’s my fault. I’m bad at reaching out, I overthink every message, and I convince myself I’m being annoying before I even hit “send.” So I don’t send it. Then I feel even more isolated and resentful, like I’m watching life through a window I can’t open.

Nights are the worst. That’s when the silence feels heavy, like it’s pressing on my chest. I scroll through my phone just to feel some kind of connection, but it usually makes me feel more left out. Everyone else seems to have group chats, inside jokes, people who actually choose them.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
