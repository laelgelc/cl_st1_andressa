I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, or if I ever did.

On paper my life looks “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, coworkers I can chat with about projects or random stuff. But as soon as I go home, it’s like someone hits a mute button on my life. No messages, no calls, no one who would notice if I just stopped showing up online.

I keep replaying old friendships in my head, trying to figure out where I went wrong. People drifted away, got partners, moved, built new circles. I stayed in this weird in‑between space where I’m always “kind of” included but never really chosen. The person you invite if there’s room, not the person you plan around.

I’ve tried joining groups, going to events, making small talk, but it feels like everyone already has their people. I can’t shake the feeling that I’m always one step out of sync, like I’m speaking a slightly different language.

What scares me most is how normal this is starting to feel. Coming home to silence. Eating alone. Weekends that blur together. I’m not even sure what I’d say if someone genuinely asked how I’m doing, beyond: “I’m tired of feeling like a background character in my own life.”
