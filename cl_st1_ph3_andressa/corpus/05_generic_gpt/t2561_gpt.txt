I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing in slow motion.

My days are technically “full” — work, errands, scrolling, sleep — but there’s no one actually in them. I have people I talk to (coworkers, the barista, random group chats), but I don’t feel like I exist in anyone’s mind once the interaction is over. It’s like I’m just background noise in other people’s lives.

What really gets to me is how my phone can stay silent for days unless I’m the one initiating. And even when I do reach out, I start overanalyzing every pause in the conversation, every short reply, convincing myself I’m annoying or forcing something that isn’t really there. So I pull back, then feel even more alone, and the cycle just keeps repeating.

I see other people making plans, posting photos with “my people,” “my besties,” family gatherings, whatever. I know social media is a highlight reel, but it still stings to realize I don’t have anyone I’d naturally show up in a picture with. There’s no one who would randomly text me just to share something small, no one I’d call in the middle of the night without feeling like I’m imposing.

I miss being known. Not just talked to, but actually known — the way you remember someone’s weird habits and inside jokes and they remember yours. I miss feeling like my presence changes the shape of a room, even a little. Right now, it feels like if I quietly slipped out of my own life, very little would look different to anyone else.
