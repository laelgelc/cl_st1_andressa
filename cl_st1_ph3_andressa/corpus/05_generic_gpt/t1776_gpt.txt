I don’t really know how to talk about this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’ve slowly slipped out of everyone’s life and no one noticed, including me, until it was already done.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I send memes to, family group chats that light up on holidays. But there’s this constant sense that I’m just orbiting other people’s lives instead of actually being in them. Like I’m a background character who occasionally delivers a line and then fades out of the scene.

What’s messing with my head is how invisible it feels. Days go by where my phone doesn’t light up unless it’s a notification from some app or a spam call. If I don’t initiate, nothing happens. I’ve done experiments where I just… stop texting first. The silence that follows is so loud. It’s like I’m testing my own importance and failing every time.

I’ve also noticed how much I edit myself around others now. I keep things light, crack jokes, ask questions, listen. I rarely talk about anything that actually matters to me because I get this sense that people are half-listening, waiting for their turn, or glancing at their phones. After a while, you start to feel like your inner world is this private museum no one ever visits. The doors are open, but no one comes in, so you start turning off the lights room by room.

Nights are the worst. That’s when the thoughts get loud: “If you disappeared, how long until anyone really noticed?” I know logically that someone would, eventually, but emotionally it feels like I could just slip through the cracks and everyone would adjust pretty quickly. It’s a weird mix of wanting someone to care and being terrified that if I ask for that, I’ll just get proof that they don’t.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I guess I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head, because living with it in silence is starting to feel like carrying around a weight that keeps getting a little bit heavier every day.
