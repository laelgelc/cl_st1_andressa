I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, people I can technically contact if I need something. But I don’t feel connected to anyone. The conversations I have are all surface-level: work updates, small talk, memes. I can go days without anyone asking how I actually am, and if they did, I’m not even sure I’d know what to say.

I scroll through social media and see people going on trips with friends, having game nights, sending each other dumb selfies. I can’t remember the last time someone sent me a photo just because they thought of me. I’m always the one who reaches out first, and half the time I feel like I’m intruding or forcing something that isn’t really there.

What gets to me is how invisible this feels. I show up where I’m supposed to, I smile, I make jokes, and everyone assumes I’m okay. I don’t think anyone realizes that when I go home, it’s just silence. No one waiting, no one checking in, just me trying to fill the hours until I’m tired enough to sleep.

It’s a strange kind of grief, missing a connection I’ve never really had, and not knowing if it’s ever going to be different.
