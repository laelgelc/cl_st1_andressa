I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, and it scares me.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family chats that light up my phone. But everything feels thin, like I’m watching life through glass. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m not really there, like I’m just performing “normal person” and waiting to go home.

I’ve gotten so used to being the quiet one, the listener, the person who says “I’m fine” that now when someone actually asks how I’m doing, my mind just goes blank. I don’t know how to be honest without feeling like I’m dumping too much or being weirdly intense. So I keep it light, make a joke, change the subject. Then I go home and feel this heavy emptiness because nobody actually knows me.

I scroll through social media and see people hanging out, going on trips, having “their people.” I can’t tell if I’m jealous or just numb. I don’t even know what I’d talk about if someone invited me out. It feels like I’ve missed some crucial stage where everyone else learned how to form real bonds, and I just… didn’t.

It’s a strange kind of grief, missing something I’ve never really had.
