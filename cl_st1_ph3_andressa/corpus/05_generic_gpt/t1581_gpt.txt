I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and exposed lately. Like I’m walking around with a sign on my forehead that says “alone,” but no one actually sees it. I have people around me—coworkers, a couple of acquaintances I text sometimes—but I don’t feel *known* by anyone. It’s like I’m playing a character in my own life and everyone only interacts with the role, not the person.

I keep catching myself rehearsing conversations in my head that never actually happen. Imagining what it would be like to have someone I could message without overthinking every word, someone who wouldn’t see my name pop up and need a second to remember who I am. Most of my notifications are apps I don’t care about, not people.

What messes with me is that I don’t even know how to fix it. I try to be friendly, I try to show interest in others, but it feels like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else. They’re all living their lives, forming memories with each other, and I’m stuck behind the glass watching it happen.

Nights are the worst. The silence feels heavier then. I scroll through social media and see people hanging out, getting married, having kids, or even just grabbing coffee with friends. I know it’s curated, but it still stings. I can go whole weekends without speaking out loud to anyone. I start to wonder if this is just how it’s going to be—like I somehow missed the point where everyone else learned how to connect and I just…didn’t.
