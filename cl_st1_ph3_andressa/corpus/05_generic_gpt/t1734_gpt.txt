I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too exposed at the same time, and I don’t really know where else to put it, so I’m dumping it here.

On paper, my life looks fine. I have a job, I respond to messages, I show up where I’m supposed to. I can make small talk about work, weather, TV shows. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice.” But it all feels like I’m acting out a role that doesn’t belong to me.

The loneliness isn’t just “I don’t have plans on Friday.” It’s more like: there is no one on this planet who really has a clear picture of who I am. No one who would notice subtle changes in my mood. No one who’d say, “You’ve been off lately, what’s going on?” I could disappear from most people’s lives and it would register as a minor inconvenience at most.

I’ve tried to reach out before, to open up a bit more, but it feels like I’m always talking just slightly past people. I’ll say something honest and they’ll respond with a joke, or change the subject, or give some generic “you’ll be fine.” I don’t even blame them; I’m not sure I’d know what to say either. But each time it happens, I feel myself shutting down a little more.

There’s also this shame around being lonely as an adult. Like I missed some critical developmental step everyone else seems to have figured out. I’m old enough that “making friends” feels less like a natural process and more like an awkward group project no one wants to do.

I keep thinking about how much of my personality is shaped by trying not to be a burden. I don’t ask for favors, don’t initiate much, don’t show too much emotion. Then I sit here and wonder why no one seems close to me. It’s like I’m building my own isolation and then resenting it.

Anyway, I don’t really know what I’m looking for writing this. I just needed to say, out loud (or as close as it gets), that I feel really alone, even when I’m technically not.
