I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my life on paper.

I have people around me. Coworkers I joke with, a group chat that pings all day, family I talk to every week. I’m not “isolated” in the obvious sense. But there’s this constant sense that I’m just slightly out of sync with everyone, like I’m watching my own life from a few steps back.

Whenever I’m with others, I feel like I’m playing a role I’ve rehearsed: the “chill friend,” the “good listener,” the one who remembers birthdays and sends memes. I’m good at asking questions, keeping conversations going, but I notice that when it’s my turn to talk about myself, I kind of…glide over the surface. I don’t even know if I’m avoiding depth or if I just don’t believe anyone is actually interested.

What makes it worse is that no one would guess I feel lonely. People describe me as “social” or “easy to talk to,” which somehow makes the loneliness feel more illegitimate, like I’m not allowed to feel this way because I’m not visibly alone.

I miss having that one person I can text without overthinking, the kind of friend where silence isn’t awkward and you don’t feel like you’re burdening them if you say, “I’m not okay today.” I don’t have that right now. Interactions feel transactional or scheduled, not organic.

Sometimes I wonder if the problem is me—maybe I don’t let people in enough, or maybe I’m just not the kind of person people feel deeply connected to. It’s a strange thing to feel surrounded and unseen at the same time, and I don’t really know what to do with that feeling anymore.
