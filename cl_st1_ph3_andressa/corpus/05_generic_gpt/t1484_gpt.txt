I’m starting to realize how much of my life happens in silence.

I have people around me in the technical sense—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats that light up my phone—but almost no one who actually *knows* me. Most interactions feel like I’m playing a role: the helpful one, the listener, the “doing fine” person. It’s like I’m present, but my real self is always standing a few steps behind a glass wall.

What makes it worse is that I don’t even know how to fix it. I try to reach out sometimes, but conversations stay surface-level. I’m scared that if I push for more, I’ll come across as needy or awkward, so I retreat. Then I feel even more disconnected and weird, which just feeds the cycle.

Nights are the hardest. The day winds down, everyone else seems to disappear into their own lives, and I’m left with this heavy feeling that I’m missing something fundamental that everyone else figured out a long time ago.

I keep wondering if this is just how things are going to be.
