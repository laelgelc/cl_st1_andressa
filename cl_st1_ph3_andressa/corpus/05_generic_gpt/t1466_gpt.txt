I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into the usual “I have no friends” box. On paper, I’m not alone. I have coworkers I talk to, a couple of old friends I message sometimes, family that exists in the background. But it all feels like I’m interacting through glass.

Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes, “how was your weekend.” No one really asks how I’m actually doing, and when they do, I default to “I’m fine” because I don’t even know how to explain this without sounding dramatic or needy. It’s like there’s this whole inner world I carry around that never gets seen by anyone.

I scroll through social media and see people posting group photos, inside jokes, trips, even just dumb little daily things they share with “their people.” I don’t even know who I’d send a random photo to without it feeling forced or awkward. Most of my interactions feel like I’m a guest in other people’s lives, not someone who actually belongs there.

I’ve tried joining things—Discord servers, hobbies, group chats—but I always end up feeling like an extra in a movie where everyone else already knows their lines. I either talk too much and feel annoying, or I go quiet and fade into the background. Then I leave before anyone really notices I was there.

The worst part is how it makes me start doubting myself: maybe I’m just not the kind of person people feel close to. Maybe I missed some crucial stage where everyone else learned how to connect, and now I’m just stuck watching it from the outside.
