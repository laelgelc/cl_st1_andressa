Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m watching my own life from the outside, like a spectator instead of a participant. I go to work, I come home, I scroll, I sleep. I talk to people every day, but it all feels like small talk on top of an empty space.

What bothers me is that nothing is *bad* on paper. I have a job, a place to live, no huge crisis going on. But I can go days without anyone asking how I actually am, and if they did, I’m not sure I’d know how to answer without making it awkward. I’ve somehow become “the reliable one” who listens, helps, covers shifts, but when I close my door at night it’s just…quiet.

I’ve tried to be more social—saying yes to invitations, joining a couple of groups—but I always feel like the extra person, the one who could disappear and the dynamic wouldn’t really change. Everyone else seems to already have their people, their shared history, their inside jokes. I’m just orbiting.

What scares me is how easy it’s becoming to accept this as normal. To tell myself I’m “just independent” when actually I’m lonely in a way that feels physical, like a weight in my chest. I’m starting to wonder if there’s something fundamentally off about me that other people can sense, some reason I never quite make it from acquaintance to “someone who’d be missed.”
