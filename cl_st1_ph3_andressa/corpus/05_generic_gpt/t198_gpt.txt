I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about the weather or whatever show is trending. People would probably describe me as polite, maybe even “easy to get along with.” But it all feels like I’m playing a character that everyone accepts but nobody really knows.

The weirdest part is that I’m almost never actually alone. I’m surrounded by coworkers, neighbors, strangers on public transport. My phone is full of group chats that are technically active, but I still hesitate before sending anything because I’m not sure anyone is really interested in what I have to say. I scroll, I like, I react, but it’s like tapping on the glass of other people’s lives.

I miss the feeling of being genuinely seen. Of having someone ask how I am and actually want the real answer, not the edited version. I miss conversations that go past the surface, where I don’t have to calculate every word.

I keep thinking I should “put myself out there” more, join clubs, talk to people, whatever. But when I imagine walking into a room full of strangers, I already see myself standing off to the side, pretending to check my phone so I don’t look as out of place as I feel.
