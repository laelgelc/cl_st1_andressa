I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, coworkers I can make small talk with, a couple of old friends I text a few times a month. My family checks in. But there’s this constant sense that nobody actually knows me, not the way I wish someone would.

I’ll have these moments where I’m in a group, people are laughing, and I’m smiling too, but it’s like I’m behind a glass wall. I can see them, hear them, participate, but there’s no real connection getting through. When the evening ends, everyone goes home to someone or at least to a chat buzzing on their phones. I go home to silence and scrolling.

I keep thinking: if I disappeared, people would notice in a logistical way—missed messages, unanswered emails—but I’m not sure anyone would feel that deep, aching “something is missing” kind of absence.

It’s strange how heavy it is to carry so many words you never say out loud. I have so many thoughts, memories, stupid jokes, fears, and they just stay inside my head, like a room no one ever visits.
