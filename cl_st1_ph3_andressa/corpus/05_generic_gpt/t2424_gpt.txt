I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living in “observer mode” while everyone else is actually participating.

On paper, I’m not completely alone. I have coworkers I chat with, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family I see on holidays. But it all feels like I’m just rotating through roles: the polite coworker, the “doing fine” sibling, the friend who always says “we should hang out sometime” and then never does. None of it feels like it touches the real part of me that’s quietly screaming for someone to actually notice I’m not okay.

The weirdest part is how invisible this loneliness is. I go through my day, crack jokes, hit deadlines, make eye contact, and nobody would guess that I go home and just… sit. Scroll. Stare. Try to stretch out the evening so it doesn’t just collapse into another night of lying in bed with my phone lighting up my face and nothing meaningful coming through it.

I’m starting to feel like I missed some critical stage where people learned how to go from “small talk” to “real connection.” I get stuck at the surface. I don’t know if it’s fear of rejection, or just being out of practice, or something broken in me that I can’t name.

I keep thinking, “If I disappeared, who would actually notice quickly enough to matter?” and I genuinely don’t know the answer. And that thought just kind of hangs there, heavier every week.
