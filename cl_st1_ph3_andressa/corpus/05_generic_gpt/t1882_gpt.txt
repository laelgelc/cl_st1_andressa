I’m starting to feel like I’m disappearing in plain sight.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, even a few “friends” I see occasionally—but I don’t feel known by any of them. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, weekend plans. I keep waiting for someone to ask a real question and actually want to hear the answer, but it never happens, and I’m too afraid of being “too much” to force it.

I’ll scroll through my phone wanting to message someone, then convince myself they’re busy or don’t actually care. So I do nothing, and the silence becomes proof that I was right. It’s like I’m stuck between craving connection and being terrified of rejection.

Sometimes I wonder if I missed some crucial stage where people learned how to form close bonds. I’m in my own head constantly, replaying every interaction, trying to figure out what’s wrong with me that I can be surrounded by people and still feel this alone.
