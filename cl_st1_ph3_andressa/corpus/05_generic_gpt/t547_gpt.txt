I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, and it scares me.

I’m surrounded by people all the time – at work, on public transport, online – but it all feels like I’m behind a glass wall. I can talk about surface stuff just fine, even joke around, but as soon as things get remotely personal, my brain freezes. I either overshare and feel stupid afterward, or I shut down and say nothing.

Most of my “friendships” are just old chat threads I’m too embarrassed to revive. I watch the little green dots next to people’s names and still can’t bring myself to message them, because I convince myself they’ve moved on and I’d just be bothering them.

Nights are the worst. I lie there replaying every small social interaction, picking apart my tone, my face, my timing. It’s like I’m constantly auditioning for a role I never get. I’m not sure if I’ve become this way slowly or if I’m just finally noticing how alone I’ve been for a long time.
