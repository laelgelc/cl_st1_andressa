I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to my own life instead of inside it.

On paper, I’m not isolated. I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family group chats that light up on holidays. I go to work, I make small talk, I say “have a good one” to the barista. If someone looked from the outside, they’d probably think I’m just another quiet, normal person.

But I feel like a ghost. I walk around and nobody really sees me, not in the way that matters. Conversations never leave the shallow end. I can talk about the weather, deadlines, memes, but I can’t remember the last time someone asked me how I really am and actually wanted to know.

The worst part is how much of it feels like my fault. I hesitate before sending messages because I don’t want to “bother” anyone. I type out long texts and delete them. I tell myself, “If they wanted to talk to you, they would.” So I wait. And nothing happens. Then I feel even more pathetic for waiting.

Nights are the hardest. I lie in bed scrolling through other people’s lives: friends getting married, traveling, going to parties, posting pictures with big groups. I’m genuinely happy for them, but it also feels like I’m watching through a window at something I was never invited to.

I miss feeling important to someone. Not in a romantic way necessarily, just… like my presence actually matters. Like if I disappeared, someone would notice quickly, not weeks later when I haven’t replied to a meme.

I don’t really have a point to this. I just needed to put it somewhere, because keeping it all in my head makes it feel even heavier.
