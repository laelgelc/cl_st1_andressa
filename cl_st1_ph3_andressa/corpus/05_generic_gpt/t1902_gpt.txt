I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore. Not in a “no one likes me” dramatic way, just in a very quiet, practical sense: I don’t know what to say, when to say it, or how much of myself to show.

I have acquaintances. I reply in group chats sometimes, I show up to things occasionally. People are friendly enough. But there’s this invisible wall where everything stays surface-level. I can talk about work, TV shows, random news, but when the conversation shifts toward anything real, I freeze. I either overshare and feel stupid afterward, or I say nothing and feel like a ghost in the room.

What gets to me is how routine the loneliness has become. I go through the day, do my tasks, maybe laugh at something online, and then suddenly it’s 11 p.m. and I realize I haven’t had a single genuinely honest interaction. Just noise and small talk.

I keep thinking, “I should reach out to someone,” but I don’t know who that someone is anymore. The people I used to feel close to have their own lives now. I scroll through their updates and feel like I’m watching from behind glass.
