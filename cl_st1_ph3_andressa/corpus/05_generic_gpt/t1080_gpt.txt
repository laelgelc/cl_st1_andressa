I don’t even know where to start with this, but I’m so tired of feeling like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, I can pay my bills. I talk to coworkers, I answer emails, I nod and smile in meetings. But as soon as I walk out of the office, it’s like someone turns the sound off. No messages, no calls, no plans. Just the hum of the fridge and whatever noise I put on in the background so I don’t have to hear how quiet it is.

I scroll through my contacts sometimes, just staring at names, and realize there isn’t a single person I’d feel comfortable texting out of the blue to say, “Hey, I’m lonely, can we talk?” Either we’ve drifted too far or the relationship never really went beyond surface-level. I’ve been “the reliable one” for so long that I don’t even know how to be the vulnerable one without feeling like I’m breaking some unspoken rule.

What gets me is how invisible this all feels. From the outside, I probably look like I’m just “private” or “independent.” Inside, I feel like I’m standing behind glass watching everyone else do normal human things—group chats, weekend plans, inside jokes—and I’m just not… in any of it. I’m always the extra chair at the table that no one needs.

I keep thinking I should “put myself out there,” but it’s exhausting to keep trying to connect when most interactions stay stuck at small talk and then fade away. It makes me question if there’s something fundamentally off about me that people can sense, like a quiet “don’t get too close” sign I never meant to hang up.

Anyway, that’s where I’m at. Just needed to put this somewhere that isn’t my own head.
