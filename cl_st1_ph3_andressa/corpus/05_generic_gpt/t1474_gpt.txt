I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers I chat with, a family group chat that occasionally pings, a couple of old friends I exchange memes with. But it all feels so surface-level that I come home every day with this weird ache, like I’ve been around people all day and somehow still haven’t really been seen once.

Most of my days are a loop: wake up, work, small talk, scroll, sleep. I keep thinking, “This can’t be it,” but I don’t know what to do differently. I try to initiate conversations, but I always feel like I’m intruding or being too much. I’ll type out a message, stare at it, then delete it because I convince myself they’re just being polite and don’t actually want to talk to me.

I miss having real conversations—the kind where you talk about fears and hopes and stupid little details that don’t matter to anyone else. I don’t remember the last time I had that. I’ve become the person people vent to about their problems, but when it’s my turn, I feel like I’m burdening them. So I keep it all in, and then I feel even more disconnected because no one really knows me.

The worst part is how loneliness messes with your perception of yourself. I’ve started assuming people don’t like me before they even get the chance. If someone takes a while to respond, my brain immediately goes, “They’re bored of you.” I cancel plans sometimes because I convince myself they only invited me out of obligation.

I watch other people form friendships so easily—inside jokes, group photos, shared traditions—and it makes me feel like I missed some critical social lesson everyone else got. I’m not totally socially inept; I can hold a conversation, I can be funny in bursts. But I never seem to move from “acquaintance” to “actual friend.”

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by writing this. Maybe I just needed to say out loud that I’m tired of feeling like an extra in my own life.
