I’m starting to realize how much of my life happens in silence. Not the peaceful kind—more like the kind where you suddenly notice you haven’t spoken out loud all day aside from “thank you” to a cashier. I have acquaintances, group chats, people I send memes to, but it all feels like noise around a hollow center.

I keep wondering what’s wrong with me that conversations never seem to move past the surface. I want to say, “I’m lonely,” but it feels dramatic, like I’m asking for more than I deserve. So I just say I’m “tired” or “busy” and change the subject.

Nights are the worst. It’s like the quiet gets heavier. I scroll, I listen to podcasts, anything to drown out the feeling that if I disappeared, my daily absence wouldn’t really interrupt anyone’s life.
