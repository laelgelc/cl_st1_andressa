I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness that doesn’t really fit into the usual “no friends” narrative. I technically have people around me: coworkers, family I text sometimes, a couple of old friends I send memes to. But it feels like there’s this glass wall between me and everyone else.

I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m watching life happen instead of being in it. I laugh at the right times, I say the right things, and then I go home and it’s just…silence. Not peaceful silence, but the kind that makes you realize no one is really checking in on you in a real way. If I disappeared from social media tomorrow, I’m not sure how long it would take anyone to notice.

I’ve also started to notice how guarded I am. When conversations get deeper than surface-level stuff, I freeze up or deflect with humor. Then I go home and feel hurt that no one “really knows me,” even though I’m the one hiding. It’s like I want connection but I’m terrified of it at the same time.

What makes it worse is feeling like I’m too old to be feeling this lost, like everyone else has their circle and I somehow missed the window where people form those bonds. It’s a very specific kind of ache that just sits there, day after day.
