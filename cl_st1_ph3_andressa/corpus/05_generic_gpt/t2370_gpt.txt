I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and “too much” at the same time, and I don’t really know where to put it, so I’m putting it here.

Most days I go through the motions: work, errands, scrolling, sleep. I talk to people, but it all feels like background noise. Coworkers, small talk, “how was your weekend?”—I answer, they answer, but it’s like we’re all actors reading from a script. When I get home and close the door, it hits me how quiet it actually is.

I’m not completely isolated. I have acquaintances, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family group chats. But there’s no one I feel like I can message and say, “I feel really lonely right now, can you just stay on call with me?” without worrying I’m being needy or weird. So I keep it to myself, and the distance just grows.

I keep thinking that if I were more interesting, more funny, more something, I’d naturally fit into a group. Instead, I feel like the extra piece in a puzzle set—technically fine, but not belonging anywhere. I overthink every interaction: Did I talk too much? Not enough? Did I sound boring? So I retreat, then feel lonelier, then get rusty at social stuff, which makes me more anxious next time.

It’s like watching life happen through a window. I can see everyone else laughing, dating, making plans, but there’s this glass between me and all of it, and I can’t figure out how to open it.
