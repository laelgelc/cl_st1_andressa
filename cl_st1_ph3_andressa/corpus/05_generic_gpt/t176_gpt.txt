I’ve been feeling like I’m slowly fading out of everyone’s peripheral vision.

On paper, I’m “not alone” — I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every few weeks, family group chats. But none of it feels like it actually touches me. Conversations stay on the surface: work, memes, “how was your weekend?” I can’t remember the last time someone asked how I really am and actually wanted the messy answer.

I try to initiate plans, but people are “busy,” or we set something vague that never happens. After a while, you start to feel pathetic for even asking. So I stop, and then the silence just confirms what I was afraid of.

The worst part is how it warps my sense of self. I start wondering if maybe I’m just fundamentally forgettable, like background noise people don’t realize has gone quiet.
