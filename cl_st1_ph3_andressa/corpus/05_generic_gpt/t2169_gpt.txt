I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

Lately it feels like my life is technically “full” but emotionally empty. I go to work, answer emails, make small talk about TV shows and weekend plans, and everyone assumes I’m fine because I can hold a conversation and smile at the right times. But as soon as I’m alone, it’s like the bottom drops out and I realize how disconnected I actually feel from everyone.

I don’t have anyone I’d call in the middle of the night if something really bad happened. I have “friends,” but they’re more like people I see in group settings a few times a month. We joke, we share memes, but nothing ever goes deeper. When I’ve tried to open up a bit, it always feels like I’m oversharing or making things awkward, so I pull back and go back to the surface-level stuff.

What makes it worse is this sense that I missed some crucial stage where people learned how to build close relationships. I watch others make plans effortlessly, check in on each other, remember birthdays, share random thoughts throughout the day. I don’t seem to exist in anyone’s mind like that. If I don’t message first, my phone is usually silent.

The worst part is how it messes with my self-perception. I start wondering if I’m just fundamentally unlikeable, or boring, or too much work. I replay conversations in my head and critique every little thing I said, as if I can find the exact moment I pushed people away. It’s exhausting.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just wanted to say it somewhere, to admit that I feel really, really alone, even when I’m surrounded by people.
