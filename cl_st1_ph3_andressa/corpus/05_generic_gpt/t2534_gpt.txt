I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life without sounding dramatic, so I’m dumping it here.

I’m surrounded by people, technically. Coworkers, family chats, the occasional meetup. But I feel like I’m always slightly behind a glass wall. I can see everyone, hear them, even laugh along, but it never feels like I’m actually *with* them. It’s like I’m playing a version of myself that’s acceptable and easy to be around, and the real version is sitting in the back of my head, watching.

I scroll through my phone a lot, not because anything interesting is happening, but because I’m terrified of how quiet it gets when I put it down. The silence feels heavy, like it’s pressing on my chest. I tell myself I should reach out to someone, but then I convince myself they’re busy, or that we’re not “close enough” for me to bother them.

I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely known by anyone. People know facts about me, sure, but not the stuff that actually keeps me up at night. I don’t even know how I’d start that conversation. So I keep going through the motions, doing what I’m supposed to do, and hoping no one notices how empty it all feels.
