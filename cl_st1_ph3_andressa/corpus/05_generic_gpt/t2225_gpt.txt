I’m starting to realize that I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I don’t actually exist to anyone.

On paper, my life looks fine. I have coworkers I chat with, a family group chat that pings occasionally, a couple of old friends I send memes to. But none of it feels like real connection. It’s like I’m acting out the role of a “normal person” while feeling completely disconnected inside.

I don’t really have anyone I can message and say, “I’m not okay, can we talk?” without feeling like I’m being a burden or overdramatic. Conversations stay at the surface: work, shows, random news. The moment I try to say something genuine, I either get brushed off, joked away, or I regret saying it and retreat.

The worst moments are at night, when the day is over and there’s no more distraction. I scroll, I lurk, I watch other people’s lives unfold, and it just reinforces this feeling that I’m somehow behind, or broken, or fundamentally unlikable in a way I can’t fix.

I keep thinking that if I were more interesting, more attractive, more something, people would want to know me beyond the small talk. But I’m tired of being the one who initiates, the one who listens, the one who pretends it’s enough. It doesn’t feel like enough anymore.
