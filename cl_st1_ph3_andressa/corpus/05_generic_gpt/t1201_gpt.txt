I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m fading out of my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I occasionally meet people for drinks or dinner. I have group chats that light up with memes and random links. But I can go entire weeks without feeling like I’ve had a real conversation with anyone. I’m around people all the time and still feel like I’m behind glass.

I keep noticing how replaceable I am. If I stopped responding in the group chat, someone else would pick up the conversation. If I left my job, they’d post an opening and move on. Even in my family, I feel more like an extra than a main character. I show up to holidays, smile for pictures, make small talk, then go home and realize nobody actually knows what’s going on with me.

I’ve also realized I filter myself constantly. I don’t want to be “too much,” so I don’t share the heavier stuff. But then I end up feeling like people only know this watered‑down version of me. And when you’re only ever half‑seen, it’s hard to believe anyone could really care about the full version.

Nights are the worst. I scroll, I lurk, I watch other people’s lives through their posts and stories. Everyone seems to have some sort of anchor—partner, best friend, tight-knit group. I have…acquaintances. People I like, but who wouldn’t notice if I disappeared for a while unless I made it obvious.

I don’t even know exactly what I’m looking for writing this. Maybe just proof that I still exist outside my own head, that these thoughts are real enough to put into words, even if nothing actually changes after I hit “post.”
