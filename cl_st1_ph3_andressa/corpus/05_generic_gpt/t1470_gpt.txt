I don’t really know how to put this into words without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I respond to emails, I show up to things when I have to. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice.” I make small talk about the weather, weekend plans, whatever show is popular. I laugh in the right places. Then I go home and it’s like someone turns the volume down on the world and I’m just… there.

What’s messing with my head is that the loneliness doesn’t just happen when I’m alone. I can be sitting in a room full of people, or in a group chat that’s blowing up, and still feel like I’m not actually part of any of it. It’s like everyone else is speaking a language I never fully learned. I understand the words, but I don’t feel them the same way.

I keep thinking about how I don’t really have “my person” or even “my people.” I have acquaintances. Colleagues. People I used to be close to before life drifted us apart. But no one I’d feel comfortable texting at 2 a.m. just to say, “Hey, I’m not okay.” I don’t even know if I’d know how to act if someone suddenly wanted to be that close. I’m so used to being the background character that the idea of being genuinely seen feels almost threatening.

Sometimes I wonder if I did this to myself by always playing it safe, not reaching out, not risking rejection. Other times it feels like I just somehow missed the window where everyone else formed their circles and I was in the wrong room.

Mostly I’m just tired of feeling like a ghost in my own life.
