I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing in slow motion while everyone else keeps living.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I smile at the right times, I get asked “How’s it going?” and I say “Good, you?” and we both move on. But I can’t remember the last time I had a real conversation that wasn’t just logistics or small talk. I have people around me, but I don’t feel like I actually exist to any of them beyond my function.

I have “friends,” but they’re more like people I used to be close to who now only text in group chats about memes or plans I’m never really part of. I’ll send something and it gets buried under a dozen other messages, or just left on read. It’s not that anyone is cruel; it’s more like I’m optional. If I vanished, their lives would rearrange around the empty space in a day or two and keep going.

What really messes with me is how invisible this feels. I can be in a room full of people and feel like I’m watching life through glass. I laugh at the right times, nod along, but inside I’m thinking, “None of you actually know me. And I don’t know how to let you.” When I try to open up, I get this tight feeling in my chest, like I’m about to overshare and scare them away, so I pull back and make a joke instead.

Nights are the worst. That’s when it hits me that there’s no one I can text just to say, “Hey, today sucked,” without feeling like I’m being a burden. I end up scrolling until my eyes hurt, just to avoid sitting alone with the silence and the feeling that I’m somehow fundamentally out of sync with everyone else.

I’m not even sure what I want from posting this. I guess I just needed to put it somewhere that doesn’t immediately shrug it off.
