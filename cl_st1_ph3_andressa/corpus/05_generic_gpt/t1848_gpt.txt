I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone,” I’m actually lonely. I have coworkers, acquaintances, people I chat with online, but no one who really knows me or checks in unprompted. I overthink every interaction, replaying conversations and imagining I sounded weird or needy. I watch other people make plans and feel like I’m behind glass—visible but unreachable. I’m not sure when it got this bad, only that it quietly did.
