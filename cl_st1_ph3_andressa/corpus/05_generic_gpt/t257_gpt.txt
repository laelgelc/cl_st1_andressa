I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing from my own life.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, some old friends I text every now and then, family chats that light up my phone. But none of it feels like it actually reaches me. I’m always a little bit outside of everything, like I’m watching myself play the role of “normal person” and waiting for someone to notice it’s just an act.

I go through whole days without having a real conversation. I talk about work, schedules, random memes, but never about how I’m actually doing. And when someone vaguely asks “how are you?”, I can’t bring myself to answer honestly. I just say “I’m good, you?” and then feel weirdly resentful that nobody sees through it, even though I’m the one hiding.

What scares me is how quiet my life is when my phone is face-down. No one really checks in without a reason. If I don’t message first, the silence just stretches. I start wondering if I stopped trying altogether, how long it would take for anyone to notice I’d gone.

I’m also tired of feeling like the “extra” in every group. The person who can be invited or not, and it doesn’t really change anything. I’m there, but I’m not needed. Replaceable.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for. I think I just needed to put this somewhere, because keeping it in my head all the time makes it feel heavier than it probably should.
