I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s hitting harder as I get older.

On paper, my life looks fine: I have a job, a place to live, a few acquaintances I can make small talk with. But there’s nobody I can message when something good or bad happens. My phone is just silent unless it’s spam or work. When I scroll through my contacts, there’s no one I feel comfortable reaching out to without overthinking it.

I’ve tried going to meetups, talking more at work, even joining Discord servers. I end up feeling like an outsider watching everyone else already have their in-jokes and shared history. I don’t know how people move from “we talk sometimes” to “we are actually close.” I either overshare and feel stupid, or stay surface-level and feel invisible.

What scares me is how used to this I’m becoming. I fill the silence with YouTube, podcasts, background noise. Sometimes I go the whole weekend without using my voice except to order food. It makes me wonder if this is just how my life is going to be from now on.
