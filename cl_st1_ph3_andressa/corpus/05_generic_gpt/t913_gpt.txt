I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into the usual “I have no friends” box, but also… kind of does.

On paper, I’m not alone. I have coworkers I chat with, a family I text with occasionally, and a couple of old friends I still have on social media. My phone isn’t completely dead. But somehow it all feels thin, like eating cotton candy when what you actually need is a real meal.

I’m starting to realize how much of my “connection” is just surface-level maintenance. Work talk, memes, commenting “lol” on someone’s story, sending a like so they remember I exist. I don’t know when it happened, but the deeper conversations—the ones where you actually say “I’m not okay” and mean it—just kind of disappeared from my life. Or maybe I stopped offering that part of myself because I didn’t think anyone really wanted it.

What makes it worse is the sense that I’ve become forgettable. People don’t really check in unless I initiate. I can disappear from group chats for days and no one notices. I cancel plans and nobody asks if something’s wrong; they just accept it and move on. It makes me feel like a background character in my own social circle.

I’ve tried to “fix” it in the usual ways—joining a couple of Discord servers, trying to be more active in group chats, even forcing myself to say yes to invitations when I’d rather stay home. But it all feels like I’m performing the role of a social person, not actually being one. I come home from these interactions feeling more drained and somehow lonelier, like I just proved to myself again that I don’t quite fit.

There’s also this growing fear that I’m becoming harder to know the older I get. Like the longer I go without those close, honest connections, the more rusted shut that part of me becomes. I don’t really know how to reverse that. I miss feeling known. I miss feeling like someone is genuinely glad I exist, not just glad I answered their message.
