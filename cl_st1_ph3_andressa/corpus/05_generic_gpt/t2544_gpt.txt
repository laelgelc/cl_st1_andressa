I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every few weeks, family chats where we send memes. But none of it feels like connection. It’s like I’m playing an NPC version of myself that knows the right responses, laughs at the right times, and then goes home and just… powers down.

The weirdest part is that no one would guess I’m lonely. I come across as “fine.” I can hold conversations, crack jokes, talk about work or shows or whatever. But there’s this constant, heavy awareness that no one really knows what’s going on inside my head, and I don’t know how to let them in without feeling like I’m burdening them or being too intense.

I miss feeling important to someone. Not in a romantic way necessarily, just… like someone actually notices when I pull away, or checks in because they genuinely want to know how I’m doing, not just out of politeness. I keep imagining what it would be like to have “my person,” someone I can text about nothing and everything and not overthink every word.

Instead, I go through the motions, scroll, lurk, watch other people’s lives move forward. I keep telling myself I’ll try harder to reach out, join things, be social. Then the day ends, and I’m still here, sitting in the quiet, wondering how I became this invisible.
