I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my life “on paper.” I have a job, coworkers I get along with, a couple of old friends I still text sometimes, family I talk to here and there. But most of the time I feel like I’m watching my own life from the outside, like I’m in the audience instead of on the stage.

I don’t think I’ve had a truly honest, unguarded conversation in a long time. Everything feels…surface-level. Jokes, memes, small talk, “how’s work,” “we should hang out sometime” that never actually turns into plans. When I try to say something a little deeper, I can almost see the other person mentally flinch, like I’ve broken some unspoken rule of keeping things light.

There’s also this sense that everyone else has moved into the next phase of life without me. People are getting married, having kids, buying houses, going on trips with their “group.” My days all kind of blur together: work, commute, scrolling, sleep. I tell myself I like being alone, and sometimes I really do, but there’s a difference between solitude and feeling like no one would notice if you disappeared for a while.

I’ve tried joining clubs, group chats, Discord servers, whatever, but it always feels like I’m entering a movie halfway through. Everyone already has inside jokes, shared memories, a rhythm. I end up lurking on the edges until I quietly disappear. Then I tell myself it’s my fault for being awkward or too quiet or just fundamentally unlikeable.

What bothers me most is how this loneliness is starting to feel like part of my identity, not just a phase. Like I’m not just alone right now; I’m “a lonely person.” And I don’t really know what to do with that, other than keep going and hope something eventually shifts, even if I don’t quite believe it will.
