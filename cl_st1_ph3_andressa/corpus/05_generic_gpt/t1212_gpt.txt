I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore. I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I chat with online, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something really bad happened. I go through my day on autopilot: work, small talk, home, screen, sleep. It all feels like background noise.

When I try to open up, I either overshare and feel ashamed afterward, or I stay surface-level and walk away feeling invisible. I watch other people form friendships so naturally, like they’re speaking a language I never properly learned.

What messes with my head is that from the outside I probably look “fine.” I laugh at jokes, meet deadlines, even give decent advice. But it feels like I’m standing behind glass, watching everyone else live near each other while I just orbit around them, never really landing anywhere.
