I don’t really know where else to put this, so here it goes.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it. I go through the motions—work, errands, scrolling on my phone—but it all feels like background noise. When I try to think of someone I could text just to say “hey, want to hang out?” my mind comes up blank or lands on people I haven’t spoken to in months, which somehow feels even worse.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people who’ll chat about TV shows or complain about traffic, but nobody who actually knows me. I’m always “the reliable one,” “the quiet one,” “the one who’s fine,” and I’ve played that role so long that I don’t think anyone realizes how lonely I am. I’m not even sure how to change it without coming off as weird or needy.

Even when I’m around people, I feel like I’m on the outside of an inside joke that never ends. Everyone seems to have this invisible thread connecting them—group chats, plans, shared histories—and I’m just orbiting around it, close enough to see but not close enough to belong.

What scares me most is how normal this is starting to feel, like I’m getting used to the idea that this might just be it.
