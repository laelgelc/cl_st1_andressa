I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life without sounding dramatic, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people on paper—coworkers, family chats, a couple of old friends I text occasionally—but I feel like I’m living behind glass. Conversations stay on the surface: work, memes, random updates. No one asks how I *really* am, and I’ve forgotten how to offer that honestly without feeling like a burden.

I miss having someone who actually knows my day-to-day life, the stupid little details. Someone I can message without rehearsing what I’m going to say, without worrying I’m being “too much.” I scroll my phone hoping for a notification that never comes, then feel pathetic for caring so much.

I keep wondering if this is just adulthood, or if I somehow missed the point where everyone else figured out how to form real connections. It feels like I’m slowly fading out of people’s lives and no one notices, including me.
