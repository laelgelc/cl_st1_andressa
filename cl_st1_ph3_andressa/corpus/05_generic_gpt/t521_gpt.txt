I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… quiet in all the wrong ways.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I’m not in immediate crisis. But when I zoom out, there’s this constant low-level ache that I’m just existing parallel to everyone else instead of with anyone.

Most of my social interactions are surface-level. Work small talk, group chats that have mostly turned into people sending memes, the occasional “we should catch up sometime” that never becomes an actual plan. I scroll through old conversations and see this pattern of me trying to keep things alive and the other person slowly fading out. Eventually I stop reaching out because I don’t want to be the one who “does too much,” and then the connection just dies.

I’m not completely isolated; there are people who’d recognize my name, maybe say hi if they saw me. But there’s no one I’d text at 2am if something was really wrong. No one who knows the mundane details of my day, like what I had for dinner or the random thought I had on the bus. It’s like I’m missing that layer of life where you’re known in an unremarkable, everyday way.

I’ve tried to “fix” it: joined clubs, said yes to invites, forced myself to talk more. It always feels like I’m hovering at the edge of groups that already have their own history and inside jokes. I’m there, but I’m not really there.

What messes with me the most is how invisible this feels. From the outside I probably just look independent or introverted. Inside, it feels more like I’m slowly forgetting how to be close to anyone, and I don’t know how to reverse that.
