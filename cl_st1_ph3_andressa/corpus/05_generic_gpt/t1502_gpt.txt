I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind thick glass.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I reply to messages, I show up when I’m supposed to. People think I’m quiet but “chill.” Inside, it’s like I’m watching everyone else live in color while I’m stuck in grayscale.

I have people around me, technically. Co‑workers, some old friends I talk to occasionally, family chats where we send memes. But I don’t feel *known* by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, shows, “how was your weekend?” I answer automatically, then go home and feel this heavy emptiness that’s hard to put into words.

What gets to me is how invisible this feels. I can be sitting in a room full of people and feel like a ghost. I laugh at the right moments, nod along, but my brain is somewhere else, wondering if anyone would actually notice if I just stopped trying to connect at all.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like everyone suggests. Joined group chats, tried hobbies, even forced myself to message people first. It always fizzles out or stays in that weird acquaintance zone. I start to feel like there’s something fundamentally off about me that people sense and quietly back away from.

Nights are the worst. That’s when it hits how silent everything is. No one to text just to say, “today sucked” and have them actually care, not out of obligation, but because they genuinely want to be there.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for writing this. I guess I just wanted to say it somewhere.
