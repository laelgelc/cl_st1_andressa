Lately I’ve been feeling like a background character in my own life. I go through the motions—work, errands, scrolling on my phone—but nothing feels connected to anyone else. I have acquaintances, people I can joke with, but no one I can text when I’m falling apart at 2 a.m. I’m always the one listening, nodding, saying “I’m fine” when the conversation turns to me.  

What makes it worse is this sense that I missed some invisible checkpoint where everyone else learned how to form real bonds. I overthink every message, every silence. I’m tired of pretending I’m okay with being on my own so much, but I also don’t know how to change it without feeling pathetic or intrusive.
