I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I answer emails, I make small talk with coworkers about weekend plans I don’t actually have. I say “yeah, it was good, just relaxed” every Monday even though what I really did was stare at my phone, scroll, eat something forgettable, and then realize it’s suddenly midnight.

The weirdest part is that I’m not completely isolated. I have people around me. I can go days having conversations, sending memes, nodding along in meetings. But none of it feels like it lands anywhere real. It’s like I’m interacting through glass. I can see everyone, hear everyone, but there’s this invisible barrier where nothing really touches me and nothing I say really reaches anyone else.

I used to think loneliness meant having no one. Now I’m realizing it can also mean being surrounded by people and still feeling like a background character in your own story. I don’t feel actively disliked or rejected, just… unnecessary. Replaceable. Like if I stopped showing up, people would notice logistically, but not emotionally.

I’ve tried to “fix” it the way everyone suggests. Join things. Go out more. Say yes to invitations. I’ve gone to meetups, tried hobbies, even forced myself through some painfully awkward social events. I always end up standing on the edge of circles, smiling at the right times, but somehow never crossing that line into actually belonging. People remember my face but not my details. They forget my name, or they remember it but not anything I’ve shared.

It’s getting harder to share anything real anyway. When I do open up a little, I immediately feel exposed and stupid, like I overshared in a space that wasn’t meant to hold it. So I pull back again and make myself smaller, quieter, “easier.” Then I feel even more invisible, which just confirms the whole cycle.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for writing this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere that isn’t just inside my own head for once.
