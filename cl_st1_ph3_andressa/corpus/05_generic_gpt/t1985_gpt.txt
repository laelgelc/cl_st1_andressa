I’m starting to realize that my life is basically a long string of quiet days that no one else really sees.

I have people around me in the broad sense—coworkers, acquaintances, family chat groups—but I don’t feel truly known by any of them. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes. I can joke, I can be “pleasant,” but the second I try to say anything real, I feel like I’m making things awkward, so I pull back.

What bothers me most is the absence of being someone’s first choice. No one texts me just to tell me something funny they saw. No one says, “I thought of you when…” I’m always the one reaching out, and if I stop, everything just goes quiet. It’s like I exist in other people’s lives only when I make the effort to show up.

I keep thinking that maybe I’m just fundamentally forgettable. I’m not dramatic, not particularly interesting, not the “fun one.” I’m just… there. Background character energy.

Some nights I scroll through my phone, looking at old conversations that slowly faded out, trying to figure out the exact message where I became optional. I never can. It’s like people just gradually drift, and I never learned how to be someone worth staying for.
