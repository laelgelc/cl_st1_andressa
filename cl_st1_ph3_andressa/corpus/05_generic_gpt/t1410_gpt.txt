I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and “too much” at the same time.

On paper, my life looks fine. I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about weekends and TV shows. People would probably describe me as “nice” or “quiet” or “easy to get along with.” But it all feels like I’m acting in a play where everyone else got the script and I’m just improvising.

I don’t really have “people.” I have acquaintances, coworkers, a couple of old friends I text once in a while. But there’s no one I can message at 2 a.m. when my brain is loud. No one who really knows how my day actually went beyond “pretty good, you?” I can go days without a real conversation that isn’t transactional or surface-level.

What’s messing with me is that I’m starting to feel like I’ve forgotten how to connect. When I do talk to someone, I overthink every word, replay the conversation later, and convince myself I sounded weird or boring. So I pull back a little more. Then I feel more lonely. Then I pull back again. It’s this loop I don’t know how to break.

Sometimes I wonder if this is just how adulthood is for a lot of people and no one talks about it, or if it’s just me who somehow missed the part where everyone else formed real bonds and moved on with their lives.
