I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’ve just…slipped out of everyone’s life without anyone noticing.

I have people around me technically—coworkers, family I text occasionally, a couple of old friends in a group chat—but I don’t feel like I’m actually “in” any of it. Conversations stay on the surface. If I disappeared, they’d probably notice eventually, but mostly because of routine, not because they’d really feel the absence.

I keep catching myself rehearsing what I’m going to say before I send a message, then deleting it because it feels “too much” or like I’m bothering them. Then I scroll through our old chats and see that I’m always the one who initiates. It makes me feel weirdly ashamed, like I’m begging for scraps of attention.

What’s messing with me is how invisible this kind of loneliness is. From the outside I probably look fine: I go to work, I respond when spoken to, I laugh at the right moments. Inside I feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else live in a world that I can’t quite enter.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe just to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
