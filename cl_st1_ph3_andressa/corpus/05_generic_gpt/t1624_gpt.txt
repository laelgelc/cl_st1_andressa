I’m starting to realize I don’t really “exist” in anyone’s mind unless I’m physically in front of them.

People are nice enough when we’re together. At work, I can joke around, have decent conversations, even feel like I belong for a few minutes. But as soon as I go home, it’s like I vanish. My phone stays silent unless I’m the one reaching out first, and even then it’s usually a short reply hours later, if at all.

What messes with me is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped showing up, I think it would be noticed for a week, maybe two, then everyone would just… adjust. No one really knows what’s going on in my head, because I’ve learned that when I try to open up, people get uncomfortable or change the subject.

I’m not completely isolated. I have “people” in my life—coworkers, acquaintances, a couple of old friends I talk to occasionally. But there’s no one I can text at 2 a.m. when I’m spiraling, or even at 2 p.m. when I just want to share something small.

It feels like my life is happening in the background of everyone else’s, and I’m the only one who can hear it.
