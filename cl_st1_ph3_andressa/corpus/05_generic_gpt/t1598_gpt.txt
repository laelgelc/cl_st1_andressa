I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to be close to anyone.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats—but everything feels like I’m talking through glass. I can do small talk, I can be “pleasant,” but the second anything real might come up, I freeze or deflect with a joke. Then I go home and wonder why nobody really knows me.

What scares me is that I’m not even sure who the “real me” is anymore. I’ve spent so long trying to be easygoing and low‑maintenance that I’ve worn down whatever edges I had. When I imagine opening up, I feel this weird mix of shame and…emptiness? Like there’s nothing worth showing.

Nights are the worst. I scroll, I lurk, I watch other people’s lives happening in front of me, feeling like I’m pressed against the window of a party I was never invited to. And I honestly don’t know how to step inside, or if I even belong there.
