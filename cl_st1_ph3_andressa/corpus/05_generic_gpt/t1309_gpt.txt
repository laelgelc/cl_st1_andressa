I’m starting to realize that I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m on mute.

I have coworkers, I have acquaintances, I even have a couple of people I’d technically call friends. But everything feels…surface-level. We joke, we talk about work, about shows, about random stuff, and then everyone goes back to their lives. When I go home, it’s like I vanish. My phone is mostly silent unless I’m the one reaching out first.

What bothers me most is that I don’t think anyone really knows me. Not the way I wish someone would. I edit myself constantly—try not to overshare, try not to be “too much,” try to seem fine. Then I go back to my room and feel this heavy, stupid ache because I want to be understood, but I’m terrified of actually letting people see the messy parts.

Sometimes I wonder if I’m the problem, or if this is just how adulthood is now: everyone busy, everyone tired, everyone lonely, but no one saying it out loud.
