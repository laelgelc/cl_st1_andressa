I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing in plain sight.

My life, on paper, looks “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I say “not much, you?” when coworkers ask how my weekend was. I send memes in group chats and people react with laughing emojis. Technically, I “talk” to people every day. But I can’t remember the last time I felt actually seen by anyone.

I come home to a silent apartment. Sometimes I’ll stand in the doorway for a second, almost expecting someone to call out, and then I remember no one ever does. I eat dinner alone, scroll, maybe watch something, and the only sound is the fridge humming. It hits me weirdly hard when I realize that if I vanished, the first sign would probably be my manager emailing about missed deadlines.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” the way people always suggest. Joined a hobby group, went to a couple of meetups. Everyone already seemed to have their circles, their inside jokes, their shared history. I’m always the extra chair someone pulls up, never the person anyone is waiting for.

What messes with my head is that I’m not even sure I’d know how to be close to someone anymore. When conversations get even slightly deeper than surface level, I panic internally and default back to safe topics. Then I go home and feel lonely because no one knows the real me, but I’m also the one hiding.

I’m tired of being the background character in my own life, but I genuinely don’t know how to step into the foreground without feeling like I’m forcing myself on people.
