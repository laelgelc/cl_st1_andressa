I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore. Not in a “haha I’m so awkward” way, but in a “I genuinely don’t remember what it feels like to be known” way.

Most of my days are a loop: wake up, go to work, small talk with coworkers about weather and deadlines, come home, scroll, sleep. I have people around me, but it all feels like I’m acting in a play where everyone else got the script and I’m just improvising lines that sound acceptable.

I used to have friends I could text without overthinking every word. Now, any time I think about reaching out, I get stuck in this mental spiral: “They’re busy. It’s been too long. This will be weird. You’ll sound needy.” So I don’t text. I tell myself I’ll do it later, when I’m “less weird,” which never happens.

The worst part is how invisible it feels. On the outside, I look functional. I show up to work, I answer emails, I smile when I’m supposed to. No one sees that I come home to silence every day, that I eat most meals alone, that I sometimes go the entire weekend without speaking out loud to another person.

I’ve tried joining things—online communities, group chats, even a hobby group in my city—but I always end up feeling like the extra piece in a puzzle. People already have their circles, their inside jokes, their shared history. I’m the person who joins late and laughs half a second too late because I don’t actually get it.

What scares me is how comfortable the loneliness is starting to feel. Like I’m getting used to being the background character in my own life. I don’t know if I’m more afraid of staying this way forever or of trying to change it and confirming that maybe there really isn’t a place where I fit.
