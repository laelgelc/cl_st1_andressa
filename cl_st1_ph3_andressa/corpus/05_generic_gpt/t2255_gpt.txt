I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is something I’m just watching instead of living.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, some acquaintances, family I text in group chats. On paper I’m not “alone,” but I feel like there’s this invisible wall between me and everyone else. I can talk about work, memes, random surface stuff, but the second I try to say anything real, my throat kind of closes up. I either make a joke or change the subject, and then later I’m mad at myself for not being honest.

What makes it worse is that I used to have close friends. The kind where you stay up too late talking about everything and nothing. Over the last few years, people moved, got married, got busy, or just drifted away. There was no big fight, just fewer messages, longer gaps, and then that weird moment when you realize you haven’t spoken in months and it would feel awkward to reach out. It’s like grieving people who are still alive.

Nights are the hardest. I scroll my phone until my eyes hurt, not because I’m interested in anything, but because I don’t want to sit in the silence. I keep thinking about how nobody really knows what’s going on in my head, and how I don’t really know anyone else’s either. I’m surrounded by timelines and updates and still feel completely disconnected.

Sometimes I wonder if there’s just something fundamentally off about me that people can sense and slowly back away from. I replay conversations, overanalyze texts, try to figure out what I’m doing wrong. I tell myself I’ll try harder tomorrow, be more open, say yes to more things. Then tomorrow comes and I’m tired, or anxious, or just numb, and nothing changes.
