I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

I have people around me—coworkers, some acquaintances, family chats—but everything feels thin, like plastic wrap between me and everyone else. I can talk, joke, even seem “fine,” but the second I’m alone it’s like the volume drops to zero and I’m just… there. Quiet apartment, glowing phone, no one actually reaching out unless I start the conversation. And when I do, it feels forced, like I’m auditioning for a role I don’t even want.

I scroll through group photos of people my age doing normal things—game nights, weekend trips, stupid selfies—and I genuinely can’t figure out where that version of life is supposed to come from. I go to work, I go home, sometimes I run errands on autopilot, and the days blur. I try to join things, but I always end up hovering at the edge of groups, nodding along while everyone else already has their in-jokes and shared history.

It’s not that I want a crowd. I just want to feel like I actually exist to someone when I’m not physically in front of them.
