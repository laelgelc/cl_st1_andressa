I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

Lately it feels like my life is technically “full” but emotionally empty. I’m around people a lot – coworkers, classmates, random social obligations – but I still feel like I’m on the outside of a glass wall, watching everyone else actually connect while I just…perform being a person.

I have “friends” on paper. Group chats, people I can send memes to, people I see at events. But I don’t feel like anyone really knows me. And I’m not sure if that’s their fault or mine, because when conversations start getting deeper, I panic and steer it back to surface-level stuff. Then I go home and wonder why no one knows what’s really going on with me.

It’s like I’m scared of being fully seen and equally scared of never being seen at all.

The worst part is how shameful it feels to admit I’m lonely. I keep thinking, “You’re an adult, you shouldn’t be struggling with something as basic as having people.” I see others casually texting friends to hang out, planning trips, having inside jokes, and I can’t imagine being that comfortable with anyone. Even when I’m invited to things, I feel like the extra person, the one they added because it would’ve been rude not to.

I spend a lot of time on my phone just to feel less alone, scrolling through other people’s lives. But it usually makes me feel more disconnected. I try to tell myself social media isn’t real, but it still stings to see how easily everyone else seems to belong somewhere.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to say out loud that I’m lonely, that it hurts, and that I don’t really know how to fix it without feeling like I’m begging people to care about me.
