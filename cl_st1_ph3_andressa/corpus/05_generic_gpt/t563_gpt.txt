I don’t really know how to connect with people anymore, and I’m starting to wonder if I ever actually did.

On paper I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I nod in meetings, I say “weekend was good, you?” at the coffee machine. I have a couple of group chats that are mostly memes and “lol.” But it all feels like I’m acting out a role that everyone else understands instinctively, and I’m just copying the lines without feeling anything.

What messes with my head is how invisible this feels. No one sees anything wrong. I’m “quiet,” “reliable,” “easy-going.” But I go home and there’s this heavy silence that almost hums. I can go days without hearing my own voice outside of work. Sometimes I’ll put on a YouTube video just so there’s another human voice in the room, even if they don’t know I exist.

I don’t really have that “person” you text when something happens—good or bad. When something funny happens, I catch myself thinking, “I should tell—” and then there’s no one. Birthdays are the worst. My phone buzzes with obligatory “HBD!” messages, and I type “thanks!” to each one, and it all feels like a performance of having a life instead of actually having one.

I also feel out of sync with people my age. Everyone seems to be moving through the expected milestones: relationships, roommates turning into partners, trips with friends, weddings. I’m just… there. A background character in my own timeline.

The part that scares me is how normal it’s starting to feel. Like I’m getting used to this low-level ache, this constant awareness that I’m not really known by anyone. I keep thinking I should “put myself out there,” join something, talk more, whatever. But the energy it takes to break the pattern feels huge, and I’m not even sure I’d know what to do if someone actually tried to get close.
