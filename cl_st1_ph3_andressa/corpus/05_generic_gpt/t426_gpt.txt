I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I respond to messages, I show up to things when I have to. But there’s this constant sense that I’m just a background character in everyone else’s story. People talk to me, but no one really talks *with* me. I’m there to listen, to nod, to react. Then they go back to their group chats and inside jokes that I’m not part of.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there.” Joined a couple of clubs, said yes to invitations, forced myself through small talk. It always hits the same wall: that moment where everyone else shifts from polite conversation to actual connection, and I just… don’t get included. Like there’s some invisible barrier I can’t see but everyone else can.

What bothers me most is how it’s changing me. I used to want close friends; now I’m starting to feel numb about it, like I’m slowly accepting that this is just how it is. I scroll through my phone at night and realize there’s no one I can text without feeling like I’m bothering them.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for by writing this. Maybe I just wanted proof that I existed somewhere outside my own head, even if it’s just in a random post on the internet.
