I’ve been feeling this heavy, quiet kind of loneliness lately that’s hard to explain to people who don’t experience it. On paper, my life looks “fine.” I have a job, some acquaintances, family I talk to occasionally. But most days it feels like I’m just moving through scenes instead of actually being in them.

I’ll be in a room full of people, nodding, smiling, making small talk, and there’s this parallel track in my head where I’m painfully aware that no one really knows me. Not the unfiltered, messy, contradictory parts. I censor myself constantly because I’m scared that if I showed more of who I am, people would quietly step back.

What makes it worse is that I can’t even point to a dramatic reason. No big breakup, no huge falling out. Just this gradual drifting. Friends got busy, moved away, started families. I kept telling myself it was temporary, that we’d reconnect “when things calmed down.” They never really did.

I try to reach out, but it often feels like I’m forcing myself into other people’s already full lives. I’ll send a message, get a polite response, and then the conversation dies. It’s hard not to interpret that as a sign that I’m just… optional.

Nights are the worst. That’s when the silence feels loud. I scroll through my phone, not sure who I could message without feeling like an intrusion. I see people posting about their friends, partners, plans, and it just reinforces this sense that I’m on the outside looking in.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for here. Maybe I just needed to say it somewhere.
