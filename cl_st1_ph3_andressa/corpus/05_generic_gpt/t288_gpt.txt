I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly dissolving from the inside out.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, I’m not in immediate crisis. But I go entire days without having a real conversation with anyone. I say “hey” to coworkers, make small talk about weather or deadlines, and then go home to silence. The weirdest part is how normal it’s become. The quiet doesn’t feel like a break anymore; it feels like the default setting.

I scroll through group chats that have gone dead or moved on without me. People who used to send memes or ask to hang out just… stopped. No big fight, no drama, just gradual fading. I replay old messages and wonder at what point I became the person you don’t think to invite.

I’ve tried reaching out sometimes—“Hey, how have you been?”—and the replies are polite but distant, like I’m a former coworker rather than someone they once knew well. After a few of those, it’s hard not to feel pathetic for trying. So I stop, and then I feel worse for not trying.

I’m at this strange intersection of wanting connection desperately but also being exhausted by the idea of starting from scratch with new people. I don’t even know what version of myself to present anymore. The longer I spend alone, the less I feel like I have anything interesting or valuable to offer. My stories are all about work, chores, and what I watched on streaming. Who wants to hear that?

There’s this constant background fear that this is it—that I’ll just keep existing like this, orbiting around other people’s lives, close enough to see them but never really part of anything. And I don’t have a clear idea of what to do about it. I just know that I’m tired, and lonely, and I don’t really recognize my own life right now.
