I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up on holidays. But I feel like I’m living behind glass. I watch everyone else seem to connect so easily, and I’m just… adjacent to it.

I can’t remember the last time someone asked me how I really am and actually wanted the full answer. Most conversations are surface-level: work, memes, “how’s it going?” followed by “good, you?” and then it fizzles out. I try to open up a little and feel like I’m oversharing or being “too much,” so I pull back again.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through my phone pretending I’m busy, hoping someone will message me first. When no one does, I tell myself I prefer the quiet, but it feels more like I’m slowly disappearing. It’s a weird mix of craving closeness and being terrified of being seen.

I find myself rehearsing what I’d say if someone actually wanted to know me: my fears, the stuff that keeps me up, the random things I care about way too much. But when I’m with people, I default to the “safe” version of myself: agreeable, light, forgettable.

I don’t know if I’m hard to love or just hard to notice. I just know that I’m tired of feeling like a background character in my own life, but I genuinely don’t know how to step out of it without feeling like I’m bothering everyone.
