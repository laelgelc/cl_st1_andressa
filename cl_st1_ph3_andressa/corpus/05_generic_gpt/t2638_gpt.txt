I keep realizing how little evidence there is that I actually exist in other people’s minds.

People are “nice” to me. Coworkers chat, classmates say hi, relatives ask about work. But when I go home, my phone goes silent. No one messages first unless they need something. Group chats go on without me and I only see them when someone remembers to forward a screenshot. It feels like I’m an optional add-on to everyone’s life.

I try to initiate plans, and sometimes people say yes, but it always feels like I’m dragging them out of their real lives. If I don’t reach out, nothing happens. Days pass and I realize I haven’t spoken out loud to anyone except cashiers or people asking “paper or plastic.”

What gets to me most is how invisible this feels from the outside. I look “fine.” I function. I work, I answer emails, I make small talk. No one sees that I eat every meal alone, or that I sometimes delay going to bed just because I dread another morning of waking up to zero notifications.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for anymore. I’ve tried clubs, hobbies, therapy, “putting myself out there.” I meet people, but it never seems to move past that surface layer where you’re interchangeable with anyone else.

It’s like I’m standing behind a glass wall: I can see everyone, they can technically see me, but there’s no real contact. Just the constant, quiet reminder that if I disappeared, most people would only notice when something I do for them stopped getting done.
