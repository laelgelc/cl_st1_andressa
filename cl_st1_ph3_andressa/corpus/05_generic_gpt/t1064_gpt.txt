I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m watching my own life from the outside. I go to work, say the right things, joke with coworkers, answer “I’m good, you?” automatically. Then I come home and it’s just…silence. Not peaceful silence, more like the kind that echoes.

I have people around me, technically. Colleagues, acquaintances, a group chat that mostly sends memes. But I don’t feel known by anyone. It’s like I’m a background character in everyone else’s story. When I try to open up, I either feel like I’m oversharing or that they’re not really listening, just waiting for their turn to talk.

Nights are the worst. I scroll on my phone, see other people hanging out, dating, getting married, having kids, even just grabbing a drink with friends. I’m happy for them in theory, but it also makes me feel like I somehow missed a step that everyone else understood.

I’ve tried joining clubs, going to events, messaging old friends. Sometimes it goes okay, but nothing sticks. People already seem to have their circles, their “person,” their group. I always feel like the extra chair at the table that no one really needs but no one moves, either.

I don’t know if I’m doing something wrong socially or if this is just how my life is going to be. I can’t tell if I’m lonely because I’m isolated, or isolated because I’m lonely. It’s starting to feel less like a phase and more like a personality.
