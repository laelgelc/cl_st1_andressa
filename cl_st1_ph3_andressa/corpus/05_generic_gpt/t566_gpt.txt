I’ve been feeling this weird kind of invisible lately, like I’m living next to my own life instead of inside it.

On paper, I don’t look lonely. I have coworkers I talk to, a couple of old friends I text with sometimes, family chats that light up on holidays. But it all feels like noise around me instead of connection with me. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else actually participate while I just imitate it.

What gets to me most is how long it’s been like this. I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely understood by someone. I’ve had moments where I overshared a bit, hoping it would lead to a deeper conversation, and it just kind of… falls flat. People change the subject, or give some surface-level response, and I’m left feeling embarrassed for even trying.

I’ve also realized I’ve started editing myself down. I keep things light, make small jokes, nod along. I don’t talk about the stuff that actually matters to me because I’m scared it’ll just highlight how different I feel. But then I go home and feel resentful that nobody really knows me. It’s like I’m choosing to be unknown and then hurting because I’m unknown.

Nights are the worst. I’ll scroll through my phone, wanting to message someone, and then I talk myself out of it because I don’t want to be “too much” or “needy.” I end up just lying there with this heavy feeling in my chest, replaying old conversations and wondering if I somehow pushed people away without realizing it.

I don’t know if I’m lonely because I’m bad at connecting, or if I’m bad at connecting because I’ve been lonely for so long that it’s rewired my brain. I notice myself assuming people don’t really want me around, even when there’s no proof of that. I’m constantly waiting for the moment they get tired of me.

I don’t really know what I’m looking for posting this. Maybe just a place to say, out loud (or on screen), that I feel alone. Because most days I just keep it to myself and pretend I’m fine, and it’s starting to feel like I’m disappearing a little bit at a time.
