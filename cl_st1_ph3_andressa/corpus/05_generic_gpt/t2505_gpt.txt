I’ve been feeling like I’m slowly fading out of people’s lives and no one has really noticed, including me, until recently.

I’m not completely isolated. I have coworkers, some acquaintances, a family chat I respond to with emojis. But there’s this constant sense that I’m “around people” without actually being *with* anyone. I can go days without having a conversation that isn’t about logistics, work, or small talk. No one really asks follow-up questions. I’m not sure if I’ve forgotten how to open up or if I never really knew.

What bothers me most is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped showing up, I think people would just assume I’m busy. Maybe send one “you okay?” text, then move on. I don’t feel like I’m anyone’s first choice, or even second. I’m just… an option when others aren’t available.

I also notice how quiet my phone is. I’ll unlock it out of habit, then realize I’m only doing it because I’m hoping someone thought of me. Most of the time it’s just notifications from apps, nothing with my name in it. Then I feel stupid for caring so much.

I’ve tried reaching out more, suggesting plans, but I always feel like I’m forcing myself into other people’s schedules. It’s embarrassing how relieved I feel when someone says yes, like I’ve won some kind of competition I didn’t know I was in.

I don’t really know what I’m looking for by posting this. I just wanted to put it into words somewhere, so it’s not only in my head.
