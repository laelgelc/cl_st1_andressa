It’s weird how you can go through an entire day surrounded by people and still feel like a ghost.

I have coworkers, neighbors I nod at, family chats that light up my phone, but I don’t feel like I’m actually *in* any of it. I’m there physically, saying the right things, laughing at the right moments, but it feels like I’m watching myself do it from somewhere else. When I get home, it’s like the silence just rushes in and reminds me that none of it really touched me.

I used to think loneliness meant having no one. Now I’m realizing it can also mean having plenty of people around but no one who really knows you. Not the version you’ve polished for work or social media, but the messy, insecure, too-much, too-quiet version. I don’t think anyone in my life has that version of me. I don’t even know how to show it without feeling like I’m burdening people or scaring them off.

Sometimes I’ll type out a long message to someone, trying to explain how I’m feeling, then delete it because it suddenly seems dramatic or needy. I keep conversations light and surface-level, then later I’m upset that nobody sees what’s underneath. It’s like I’m building my own cage and then complaining about the bars.

The worst part is the sense that time is passing while I’m stuck in this loop. Birthdays, holidays, random Sundays—they all blur together into this quiet, numb sameness. I keep thinking, “Next month I’ll change things. I’ll reach out more. I’ll be braver.” But then the days go by, and I don’t. And nothing really changes.
