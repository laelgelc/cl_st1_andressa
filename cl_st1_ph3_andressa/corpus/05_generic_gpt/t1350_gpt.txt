I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, even when they’re right in front of me.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I can talk to about projects or random news. But it all feels like I’m playing a character who knows the right lines. As soon as the conversation ends, I’m back in my head, replaying everything I said and wondering if I sounded weird or boring. I leave interactions feeling more drained and somehow more alone.

I’m at that age where everyone seems to be pairing off, having kids, building little units of “we.” My weekends are mostly empty. I scroll through group photos of people I used to be close to and can’t remember when exactly I drifted out of their orbit, just that I did. I tell myself I’m “busy” or “tired,” but the truth is I’m scared to show up and be the awkward extra.

What bothers me most is how invisible I feel. I can disappear for days and no one checks in. When something good or bad happens, there’s no obvious person to tell first. I exist in other people’s lives only in small, replaceable ways, and I don’t know how to change that.
