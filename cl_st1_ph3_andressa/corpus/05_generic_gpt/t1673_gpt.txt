It’s weird how you can go through an entire day surrounded by people and still feel like a ghost.

I have coworkers, neighbors, a couple of people I’d technically call “friends,” but everything feels shallow and temporary, like I’m an extra in everyone else’s movie. Conversations are small talk about work, the weather, some show people are watching. I laugh in the right places, ask the right questions, but it’s like I’m playing a character I don’t even like.

When I get home, the silence hits hard. I scroll on my phone, watch videos, read other people’s posts here, but the apartment feels like a waiting room for a life that never actually starts. There’s no one who messages me first just to see how I am. If I disappeared from social media tomorrow, I’m not sure anyone would notice for a long time.

I’ve tried to “fix” it. Joined clubs, went to events, forced myself to be more social. I can get through the initial interactions, but I never seem to move past acquaintance level. It’s like there’s an invisible wall between me and everyone else. I don’t know if it’s me being guarded, boring, awkward, or just fundamentally out of sync with people.

What makes it worse is that I can remember times when I did feel connected—late-night talks, inside jokes, feeling like someone actually got me. Those memories almost hurt now because they prove that I’m capable of connection, but also underline how far away it feels.

Most days I just move through my routine, do what I’m supposed to do, and hope no one notices how empty it all feels. The hardest part is not having anyone in my real life I can say this to without feeling like I’m being dramatic or needy.
