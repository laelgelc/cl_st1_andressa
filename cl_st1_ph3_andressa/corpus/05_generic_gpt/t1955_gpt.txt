I don’t really know where to put this, so I’m putting it here.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m watching my own life from the outside. I go to work, I make small talk, I answer emails, and then I come home to a quiet apartment that feels less like “home” and more like a storage unit for my body. I have people around me, but I don’t feel like I’m actually connected to any of them.

I have “friends,” but they’re really just people I occasionally see in group settings. No one messages me first. If I don’t initiate, my phone just doesn’t light up. When I do reach out, I worry I’m annoying them or forcing myself into their lives. So I pull back, and then I feel even more invisible. It’s like I’m constantly stuck between wanting to be seen and wanting to disappear.

What hits hardest is how long it’s been like this. It’s not a temporary rough patch; it feels like a pattern that’s hardened into reality. I’m in my [age redacted] and I’ve never really had that “person” — a close friend or partner who genuinely chooses me. I’ve been the backup plan, the convenient option, the person people vent to and then forget about when they’re done.

I try to fill the space with hobbies, media, scrolling, but there’s this underlying sense that my life is happening in a vacuum. No shared memories, no “remember when we…” moments that belong to me and someone else. Just a lot of quiet.

I don’t know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to put into words that I exist, even if it doesn’t feel like it matters much to anyone offline.
