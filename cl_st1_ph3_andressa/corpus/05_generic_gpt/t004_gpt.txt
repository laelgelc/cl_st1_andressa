I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

I go to work, I chat with coworkers, we make small jokes, I nod along in meetings. On paper I’m “social.” But when I go home and close the door, it’s like all of that dissolves and I’m back to being this weird, hollow version of myself that no one actually knows.

I have people I can text, but almost no one I feel okay being honest with. Every time I try to open up a little, I hear myself oversharing and then I panic and pull back. I end up turning it into a joke or changing the subject. It’s like I’m constantly editing myself to be easier to be around, and then I feel even more invisible because the version of me people like isn’t really me.

Weekends are the worst. I’ll lie in bed scrolling, pretending I’m “resting,” but really I’m just waiting for a notification that never comes. Sometimes I deliberately stay up too late because going to sleep means admitting another day went by where nothing really changed.

I keep thinking this is something I should have figured out in my teens or early twenties, but here I am, older than that, still stuck in the same loop of wanting connection and not knowing how to exist in it without feeling like I’m faking everything.
