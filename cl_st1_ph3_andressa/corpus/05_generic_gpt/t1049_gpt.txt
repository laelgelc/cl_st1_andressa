I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

I feel like I exist next to people, not with them. I have coworkers, acquaintances, people I can make small talk with, but no one who actually knows me. I go through the whole day saying the right things, making the right jokes, and then I come home and it’s like I vanish. No messages, no calls, just silence and the sound of my own thoughts looping.

What makes it worse is that I *have* tried. I’ve joined groups, gone to social events, forced myself to be “outgoing” even when it feels fake. I can get through the surface-level stuff, but I can’t seem to cross that invisible line into real connection. People keep each other at arm’s length, and I don’t know how to be the one who pushes closer without feeling desperate or weird.

Sometimes I wonder if there’s just something fundamentally off about me that other people can sense, like an invisible sign that says “not worth the effort.” I see friendships that look so easy for others—inside jokes, spontaneous plans, years of shared history—and it feels like watching life through a window I can’t open.

I’m tired of always being the person no one thinks to invite, the backup plan, the one who’s “nice” but never really chosen. The loneliness feels less like an emotion and more like a permanent state I’m slowly getting used to, even though I don’t want to.
