I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, or maybe I never did.

I have “people” in my life on paper—coworkers I chat with, a group chat that lights up sometimes, family I see on holidays—but it all feels like I’m playing a role in someone else’s life instead of living my own. I show up, smile, say the right things, then go home and feel like I was never really there.

What scares me is how normal this emptiness is starting to feel. I’ll go days without a real conversation, just surface-level stuff. I scroll through social media, see everyone hanging out, getting married, traveling, and it’s like I’m watching through thick glass. I’m there enough to see it, but not enough to touch it.

I’ve tried joining clubs, messaging old friends, even forcing myself to say yes to invitations. But I always end up feeling like the extra person in the group photo, the one who could vanish and nothing would actually change.

Sometimes I wonder if people see me as quiet and independent, when in reality I’m just lonely and don’t know how to bridge the gap. And I honestly don’t know if this is just how my life is going to be.
