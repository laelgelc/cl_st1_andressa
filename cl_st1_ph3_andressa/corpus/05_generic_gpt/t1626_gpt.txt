I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life without sounding dramatic, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people, technically. Coworkers, acquaintances, a couple of old friends I text every now and then. But I feel like I’m standing behind glass all the time. I watch conversations happen, I participate just enough to not look weird, but it never feels like I’m actually *in* it. It’s like I’m acting out “normal person” instead of being one.

What really gets to me is how one-sided everything feels. I listen to people, remember their problems, check in on them, send memes, ask how they’re doing. When I hit a rough patch and try to open up, it’s like the room goes quiet. Replies get shorter, or they change the subject, or I get the classic “you’ll be fine” and that’s it. I end up apologizing for “being negative” and then feel stupid for even trying.

Nights are the worst. I lie there scrolling, watching everyone else post photos with friends, partners, family. Even when I know social media is fake, my brain still translates it as: “everyone else is wanted; you’re not.” I’ve had whole weekends where I didn’t speak out loud to another person. Just podcasts and background noise so the silence doesn’t feel so loud.

I’m starting to worry that I’ve forgotten how to actually connect. Like, if someone genuinely wanted to know me, I wouldn’t even know what to say. I’ve edited myself down for so long that I’m not sure what’s left that’s real, or if anyone would even like that version anyway.
