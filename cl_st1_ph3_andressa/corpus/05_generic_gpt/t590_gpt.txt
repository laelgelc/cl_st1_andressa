I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too visible at the same time, and I don’t know how to explain it to people in my life without sounding dramatic.

On paper, I’m “not alone.” I have coworkers, a couple of friends I see every so often, family chats that light up my phone. But most of the time I feel like I’m watching my own life from the outside, like I’m a side character in everyone else’s story. I show up, I play my part, I say the right things, and then I go home and it’s just silence.

What really gets to me is how hard it is to move from “acquaintance” to “actually close.” I can make small talk, I can be funny in a group, but it never seems to go deeper. People will tell me, “You’re easy to talk to,” but no one really *chooses* me. I’m the person you invite if there’s already a group, not the person you call first.

Nights are the worst. I’ll scroll through my phone trying to think of someone I can message without feeling like I’m bothering them. I usually end up not texting anyone because I convince myself they’re too busy, or that we’re not close enough for me to randomly say, “Hey, I’m lonely.” I end up just lying there, overthinking every interaction I’ve had, replaying conversations and wondering what’s wrong with me.

I don’t think people around me realize how lonely I am because I function pretty well. I go to work, I laugh at jokes, I make plans when invited. But there’s this constant background ache of “no one really knows me, and maybe no one really wants to.”

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just needed to put it somewhere that doesn’t talk back to me with “you’ll be fine” or “you just need to get out more.”
