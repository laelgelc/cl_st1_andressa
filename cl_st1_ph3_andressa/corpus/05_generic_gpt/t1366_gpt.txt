I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I exchange jokes with coworkers, I can make small talk at family gatherings. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice.” No one sees anything wrong. But I go home and it’s just… nothing. Silence, scrolling, eating, sleeping. Repeat.

It’s not that I don’t have anyone’s number. I do. I just don’t feel like I actually exist in anyone’s mind unless I’m physically in front of them. When I don’t reach out, my phone is dead quiet. Days pass and the only notifications I get are spam emails and random app alerts. I’ll stare at my contact list and can’t think of a single person I feel safe texting “hey, I’m not okay” without worrying I’m being too much.

The weird thing is I’m not completely socially inept. I can be funny, I can listen, I can carry a conversation. But it all feels like I’m performing a version of myself that people tolerate in short doses, not someone they’d actually want around long-term. Once the context (work, school, etc.) disappears, so do the connections.

I keep thinking maybe I missed some crucial phase where people learned how to turn acquaintances into real friends. Everyone else seems to have group chats, plans, shared histories. I have a bunch of almosts and used-to-bes.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for writing this. Maybe I just wanted to put into words that awful feeling of being surrounded by people and still feeling like a ghost.
