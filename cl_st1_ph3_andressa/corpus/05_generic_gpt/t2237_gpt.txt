I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

I go through the motions: work, errands, small talk with coworkers, scrolling through social media. People would probably describe me as “quiet but fine.” But I feel like there’s this constant dull ache in my chest that I never talk about because I don’t know how to bring it up without making everything awkward.

I have “people” in my life, technically. A few friends I text sometimes, family I see on holidays. But I don’t feel known by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface. I’m always the listener, the one who asks questions, nods, laughs at the right spots. I don’t know how to switch roles and say, “Actually, I’m not okay,” without feeling like I’m suddenly too much.

Nights are the worst. I’ll lie there replaying moments where I could’ve said something real and didn’t. Or I’ll imagine what it would be like to have someone I could message at 2 a.m. without rehearsing my words a hundred times first.

It’s weird how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re standing alone in an empty room, shouting silently, hoping someone somehow hears you without you having to make a sound.
