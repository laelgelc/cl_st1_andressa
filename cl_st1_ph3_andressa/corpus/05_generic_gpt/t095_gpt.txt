I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… happening in a vacuum.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, I’m not in immediate crisis. I talk to coworkers, I answer emails, I make small jokes in meetings. People probably think I’m normal, maybe even okay. But once I walk out of the office and the door closes behind me, it’s like the volume of my life drops to almost zero.

I go home to a quiet apartment. No one is waiting for me, no one texts to ask how my day went. I scroll through my phone more out of habit than hope. Group chats I used to be active in have basically moved on without me. I send a message sometimes, it gets a couple of replies, and then the conversation just dies. It feels like I’m always coming in late to a party that’s already over.

What bothers me most is how invisible I feel. I could disappear for days, and outside of work obligations, I’m not sure anyone would notice. I see people online talking about “their person,” or even just “my friend that I always call when something happens,” and I can’t think of a single person I’d feel comfortable calling randomly. I don’t really have that role in anyone’s life either.

I try to do things “right” – I’ve joined clubs, gone to events, tried apps (friendship and dating), made small talk at the gym. It always feels like I’m hovering at the edge of social circles, never quite crossing into the part where you become someone that’s actually thought of, included, or missed. People are friendly enough in the moment, but it stays surface-level. I leave, and they just go back to their real lives, the ones that don’t have a space for me.

The worst part is how it’s changing me. I find myself hesitating before messaging anyone because I don’t want to be annoying or “too much.” I downplay when I’m struggling because I don’t think I’ve earned the right to lean on anyone. I’m starting to believe that maybe this is just how my life is going to look: surrounded by people, but not really connected to any of them.

It’s a strange thing, realizing you can be known by many and still not really known by anyone.
