I’m starting to feel like I’ve become see‑through.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats that light up on my phone—but I still go entire days without feeling like I’ve actually connected with anyone. I say the right things, make the right small talk, and then go home and realize no one really knows me. Not the stuff that actually matters, anyway.

I keep trying to reach out in small ways, but it feels like everyone else is already booked solid with “their people.” I’m always the extra, the backup plan, the one they’ll see “when things calm down.”

Nights are the worst. I replay conversations and wonder if I’m the problem, or if I just somehow missed the point where everyone else found their place and I didn’t.
