I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life without sounding dramatic, so I’m putting it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people but I feel completely separate from them, like there’s an invisible wall. I can do small talk, I can joke around, I can function at work, but it all feels like I’m playing a character. When I get home, it’s like I just power down. No calls, no messages, nothing planned. Just me, my phone, and the constant awareness that no one is really “my person.”

What really gets to me is the asymmetry. I care about people more than they seem to care about me. I’ll remember things they said weeks ago, check in on them, send them stuff that reminded me of them. Meanwhile, I can disappear for days and nobody notices unless they need something. It makes me feel like I’m just… convenient background support.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like everyone suggests—joined groups, said yes to invitations, tried being more open—but the connections never seem to deepen. People already have their circles, their history, their inside jokes. I always feel like the extra chair someone dragged over to an already full table.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for. I just needed to say this somewhere, because pretending I’m fine all the time is starting to feel heavier than the loneliness itself.
