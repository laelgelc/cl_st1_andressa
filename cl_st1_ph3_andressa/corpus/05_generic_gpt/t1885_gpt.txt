I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing in slow motion.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, people I can technically text. But I don’t feel *connected* to anyone. Conversations feel like I’m playing a role—saying the right things, laughing at the right moments—while the real me is locked in another room, watching through a window.

I’m surrounded by people all day and still feel like a ghost. I go home, cook, scroll, lie down, and the silence gets so loud it’s almost physical. I keep thinking, “If I vanished, how long until anyone noticed something was actually wrong and not just ‘oh, they’re busy’?”

I’ve tried reaching out more, but small talk drains me and deeper conversations never seem to get there. Everyone else already has their circles, their shared histories, their in-jokes. I’m always the extra piece from another puzzle that doesn’t quite fit.

What scares me most is how normal this is starting to feel. I can’t tell if I’m getting used to the loneliness or just shutting down around it. I still want to be known by someone, really known, but I’m starting to wonder if I’ve forgotten how to let that happen—or if I ever knew in the first place.
