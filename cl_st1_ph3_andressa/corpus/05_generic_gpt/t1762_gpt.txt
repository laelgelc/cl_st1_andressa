I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine” – job, roof over my head, a couple of people I can text if I really need something. But I don’t feel connected to anyone. Conversations feel like I’m reading lines from a script. I laugh at the right moments, ask the right questions, and go home feeling like I was never really there.

I’ve noticed I’ve started avoiding invitations, even the rare ones I get. I tell myself I’m tired, or that I’ll go next time, but the truth is I’m scared of sitting in a room full of people and still feeling alone. That kind of loneliness feels worse than just staying in my apartment.

What messes with my head is that I can’t point to a single big trauma or event that “caused” this. It’s more like a slow drift. Friends moved away, life schedules changed, group chats went quiet, and I just never really replaced any of it. Now I’m here, scrolling, watching other people’s lives and inside jokes, feeling like a permanent extra in the background.

I keep thinking that maybe I missed some critical stage where everyone else learned how to form deep, lasting connections, and I just…didn’t. I’m not even sure what I want from posting this. Maybe I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
