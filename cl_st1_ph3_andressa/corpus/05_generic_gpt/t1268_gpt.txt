I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really make sense on paper. I have people around me—family, coworkers, a couple of friends I can text—but it still feels like I’m walking around in a glass box. I can see everyone, hear them, laugh at the right moments, but there’s this gap I can’t cross.

I don’t think anyone really knows me, and honestly I’m starting to suspect I don’t give them much to know. I hold back the “real” stuff because I’m afraid it’ll make things awkward or I’ll come off as too much. Then I end up resenting the fact that our conversations stay on the surface, like I’m starving and only getting crumbs.

What makes it worse is that I’m starting to lose the ability to imagine being genuinely close to someone. When I try to picture it, my brain just… blanks. I can’t tell if this is just a rough patch or if I’ve quietly slipped into a version of myself that doesn’t know how to connect anymore.

Most days I just go through the motions—work, home, scrolling, sleep—and tell myself it’s fine. But there’s this constant ache under everything, like I’m homesick for a place I’ve never actually been.
