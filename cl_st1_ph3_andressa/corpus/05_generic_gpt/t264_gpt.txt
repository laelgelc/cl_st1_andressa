I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into the usual categories. I’m not totally isolated: I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family group chats. But it all feels like background noise, like I’m standing in a crowded room with a glass wall around me.

I keep catching myself editing everything I say so I don’t come off as “too much” or “too needy.” Then I end up feeling fake, and that just makes the loneliness sharper. I miss the feeling of being known without having to carefully package every thought first.

Nights are the worst. I scroll, I lurk, I watch people joke and vent and share selfies with their friends. It feels like looking into a world that I somehow missed the sign-up sheet for. I’m not even sure what I’d say if someone actually asked how I’m doing, because “lonely” sounds so small compared to how it sits in my chest.

I don’t think I want a big social circle. I just want one or two people I can message without rehearsing, who’d notice if I went quiet for a while. Right now it feels like if I disappeared, everything around me would keep moving exactly the same.
