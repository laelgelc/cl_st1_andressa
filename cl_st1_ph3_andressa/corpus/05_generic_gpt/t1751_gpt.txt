I’m starting to realize that loneliness for me isn’t just “being alone,” it’s feeling like I’m permanently misaligned with everyone around me.

I have people in my life. I talk to coworkers, I answer family group chats, I even go out sometimes. But I always feel like I’m playing a role I learned by watching other people, instead of actually being there as myself. I can mimic small talk, laugh in the right places, ask the right questions, but it feels like I’m puppeteering my own body from somewhere far away.

The weirdest part is how invisible this feels. No one would describe me as “the lonely one.” Some people think I’m “put together.” Meanwhile I come home, close the door, and it’s like sound just stops. I sit on my bed scrolling, not because I enjoy it, but because I don’t know what else to do with the hours until I’m tired enough to sleep.

I don’t even know what I’m missing exactly. I say I want “real connection,” but when opportunities show up, I freeze. I worry I’m too intense, too quiet, too boring, too much of something I can’t quite name. So I stay on the surface, and then resent that nothing feels deep.

Sometimes I wonder if this is just how my life is going to be: surrounded by people, but somehow never quite reached by any of them.
