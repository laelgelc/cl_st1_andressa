I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life without sounding dramatic, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m “socially functional” on paper. I have a job, I can make small talk, I reply to memes in group chats, I show up to birthdays. But it all feels like I’m acting a part I don’t fully understand. I can be in a room full of people I’ve known for years and still feel like I’m pressed up against the glass, watching everyone else actually connect.

What really gets to me is the lack of someone who genuinely wants to know how my day was, not out of politeness or habit, but because it matters to them. I’m tired of being the one who listens, reassures, remembers details, checks in. When I go quiet, most people don’t notice. When I reappear, it’s like I was never gone.

I’ve tried hobbies, meetups, online communities, all of that. I can collect acquaintances easily enough, but that’s not what I’m missing. I’m missing the feeling that I’m not interchangeable. That if I vanished, it would actually leave a specific, person-shaped gap somewhere instead of just a slightly quieter room.
