I’m starting to feel like I’ve forgotten how to connect with people. I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I joke with, but no one I can text when something small but important happens. I scroll through my phone and realize there’s no conversation I can jump back into without it feeling forced or awkward.

What bothers me most is how invisible it feels. From the outside, I probably look fine—busy, functioning, “normal.” Inside, it’s just this constant dull ache. I overthink every interaction, then retreat, then feel even more isolated. It’s like watching life through glass: I can see everyone else forming bonds, but I can’t figure out how to step through.
