I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m drifting through my own life as a background character. I go through the motions — work, errands, scrolling, sleep — but there’s this constant sense that I’m not actually *in* anything with anyone. I’m just next to people.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, acquaintances, family chats, the occasional “we should hang out sometime” that never actually happens. But it all feels…surface-level. I can talk about tasks, shows, news, stupid memes, but not about the heavy stuff without feeling like I’m burdening them or making things awkward. So I don’t. And then I end up feeling even more alone because no one really knows what’s going on inside my head.

What messes with me is that from the outside I probably don’t look lonely. I show up, I make small jokes, I answer “I’m good, you?” automatically. I even get invited to things sometimes. But I feel like a visitor in other people’s lives, not someone who actually belongs. When I leave, it’s like the world just snaps back to normal and I was never there.

Nights are the worst. That weird quiet when your phone is completely still and you realize there isn’t a single person you could text without overthinking it. I scroll through my contacts and it hits me that I don’t have “that person” — the one you can message about nothing and everything. I either feel like I’m interrupting someone’s life or forcing a connection that isn’t really there.

I also feel stuck in this loop where the more lonely I feel, the more awkward and self-conscious I get, and then the harder it becomes to connect with anyone. I overanalyze every interaction: “Did I talk too much? Not enough? Was that weird?” Then I pull back, and people probably just assume I’m fine being distant.

Sometimes I wonder if this is just how it’s going to be for me — always on the edge of things, close enough to see other people’s connections but not really part of them. I don’t know how to fix it. I’m tired of my own company, but I also don’t know how to be anything else.
