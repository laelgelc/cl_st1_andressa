I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… empty background noise.

I technically have “people” in my life: coworkers I chat with, family I text out of obligation, a couple of old friends I exchange memes with. But none of it feels like it touches anything real. It’s all surface-level, like we’re all actors reading from a script that’s been used a thousand times before.

What’s bothering me most is the sense that no one actually *knows* me. Not the version of me that’s polite and makes small talk, but the version that’s tired, scared, bitter, sometimes hopeful, sometimes numb. I don’t have anyone I can message at 2 a.m. just to say, “I feel really off tonight,” and expect them to actually care, not just send a heart emoji and disappear.

I’m starting to notice how much of my life is just me waiting for something to happen. I come home, go through the motions, scroll endlessly, watch people post photos with their friends and partners, and it feels like I’m behind a glass wall watching everyone else live. I can’t tell if I’m invisible, or if I made myself invisible at some point and forgot how to come back.

There’s this weird shame attached to it too. I’m an adult. I’m “functional.” I show up to work, pay my bills, say “I’m good, you?” when people ask how I am. Admitting that I’m lonely feels like admitting I’ve failed at something basic that everyone else figured out in high school.

Sometimes I want to just spill everything to someone—anyone—about how disconnected I feel, how I’m scared this is just how it’s going to be. But I don’t even know where to start, or who would want to hear it without secretly thinking I’m pathetic.

So I keep it all inside, and then come online and type it to strangers instead. At least here I can say it out loud, even if it doesn’t change anything.
