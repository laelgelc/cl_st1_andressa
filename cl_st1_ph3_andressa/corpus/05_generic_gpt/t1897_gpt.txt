I’ve been feeling this weird combination of invisible and exposed at the same time. Like no one really sees me, but if they did, I’d have no idea how to act anyway.

I have people in my life, technically. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family group chats. But none of it feels like connection. It’s like I’m playing a character that delivers the right lines at the right time—“Haha yeah,” “All good, just busy”—and then I go home and it’s just silence and this heavy sense that I don’t actually exist in anyone’s mind when I’m not physically there.

I keep thinking about how I don’t have a “person.” No one I can message without overthinking whether I’m being annoying. No one whose first thought would be to tell me when something big happens. I’m always the one reaching out, trying to keep conversations alive, and when I stop, everything just…stops. It confirms this fear that I’m optional in everyone’s life.

What makes it worse is that I’m starting to withdraw more because of it. I turn down invitations, then feel hurt that I’m not invited the next time. I want closeness but I’m exhausted by the effort and terrified of rejection, so I end up doing nothing and then hating myself for it.

I don’t really know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just wanted to admit out loud that I’m lonely instead of pretending I’m fine all the time.
