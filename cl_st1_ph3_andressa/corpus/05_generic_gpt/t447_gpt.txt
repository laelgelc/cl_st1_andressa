I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, people I can technically text. But everything feels like I’m watching it through glass. I show up, I smile, I say the right things, and then I go home and just…collapse into this weird numbness. The part that scares me is how normal it’s starting to feel.

I’m surrounded by people but I don’t feel known by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, TV shows. I try to open up a bit sometimes, give a hint that I’m not okay, but it either makes things awkward or gets brushed off with a joke. After a while you just stop trying. I’ve reached this point where I feel more comfortable pretending I’m fine than risking that awkward silence again.

What really messes with my head is how replaceable I feel. If I disappeared from my job, someone else would be hired. If I stopped messaging the few people I talk to, I’m not sure anyone would notice for a while. I don’t feel like a “main character” in anyone’s life, just an extra in the background.

I also feel out of sync with everyone my age. People are getting married, having kids, building these big intertwined lives. I’m struggling to even ask someone if they want to hang out without overthinking it for days. I end up not asking at all, then feeling rejected by a rejection that never technically happened.

Most nights it’s just me, scrolling, half-watching something, trying to distract myself from the heaviness in my chest. The silence in my room feels louder than any noise. I keep wondering if this is just what adulthood is for some people: going through the motions, surrounded by others, but somehow still completely alone.
