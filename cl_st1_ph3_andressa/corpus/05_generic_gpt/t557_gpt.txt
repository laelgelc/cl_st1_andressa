I’ve been feeling like a ghost in my own life lately.

I’m surrounded by people in the most technical sense—coworkers, neighbors, family chats that light up my phone—but I don’t feel like I actually *exist* to anyone. It’s like I’m a background character in every room I walk into. I listen, I nod, I smile in the right places, but no one really asks anything that goes beneath the surface. And I’ve forgotten how to offer that part of myself without feeling awkward or needy.

I used to have friends I could send unfiltered thoughts to, people who knew my history, my weirdness, my bad days. Now most conversations are logistics, memes, “how’s work?” and then silence. When I try to open up a bit more, it either gets brushed off or I feel like I’m oversharing and pull back immediately.

The worst part is how it’s changing me. I catch myself editing everything I say, trying to be easy to deal with, not a burden. Then I resent that no one sees me, even though I’m the one hiding. I don’t even know what I’d say if someone genuinely asked, “How are you, really?” without expecting the usual “I’m fine.”
