I don’t really know how to connect with people anymore, and I’m starting to wonder if I ever actually did.

On paper, my life looks “fine.” I have a job, I answer messages, I show up when I’m supposed to. But everything feels like I’m playing a role that never quite fits. Conversations feel scripted, like I’m just recycling lines I’ve heard other people use. When I try to be more honest or vulnerable, it usually gets awkward or brushed off, so I retreat back into small talk and silence.

The weirdest part is that I’m not completely isolated. I have acquaintances, group chats, coworkers. I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else actually participate in life while I just mimic it. I laugh at the right times, nod along, but inside I’m thinking, “If I disappeared, would anything actually change for anyone?”

I miss having even one person I could message without overthinking every word. Someone I wouldn’t feel like a burden to. Lately, I’ve noticed I’m starting to pull away from the little connections I do have, because it feels easier to withdraw than to keep feeling this invisible. Then I get hit with the panic that I’m making it worse, that I’m actively building the cage I’m stuck in.

I’m also tired of my own story. I hear myself thinking, “Here we go again, you’re lonely, you feel disconnected,” and it’s like I’m bored of my own pain but still trapped in it. I don’t know how to break the pattern. I don’t know how people go from casual surface-level interaction to actually being close. It feels like everyone else got a manual that I missed.

I guess I just needed to put this somewhere, because keeping it in my head all the time is starting to feel heavier than usual.
