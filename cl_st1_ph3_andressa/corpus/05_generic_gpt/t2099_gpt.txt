I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing in slow motion.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about weekends and TV shows. People laugh at my jokes sometimes. I’m not completely isolated in the physical sense. But there’s this constant sense that I’m just… not really there for anyone in a way that matters, and no one is really there for me either.

The thing that hits the hardest is the silence at the end of the day. I come home, put my bag down, and that’s it. No “How was your day?” text, no one expecting me, no one to debrief with about the stupid little things that happened. I scroll through my phone and most of my conversations are me trying to keep them alive, or old group chats that slowly died out. I feel like I’m always the one reaching out, and when I stop, everything just goes quiet.

I’ve tried hobbies, meetups, online communities. I show up, talk, smile, participate, and then I go home and still feel like a guest in everyone’s life. Like I’m always orbiting other people’s worlds but never really part of any of them.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. The loneliness used to hurt sharply; now it’s more like a dull background noise I can’t imagine life without. I keep wondering if this is just how it’s going to be—functioning, visible, but fundamentally alone in any way that actually counts.
