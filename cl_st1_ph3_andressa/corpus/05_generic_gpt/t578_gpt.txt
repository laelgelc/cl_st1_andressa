I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, or if I ever did.

On paper I’m “fine”: I work, I pay bills, I answer emails, I say “not bad, you?” when people ask how I am. But it all feels like I’m acting out a role someone else wrote. I can be around people and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else have real conversations while I recycle the same safe, surface-level lines.

I scroll through my contacts and there’s no one I feel like I can message without overthinking it. I hesitate over every word, then put the phone down because I don’t want to be “too much” or “too needy.” Then I sit there wishing someone would check in on me without me having to ask.

It’s a weird mix of wanting to be seen and being terrified of actually being seen. I’m scared that if I show how lonely I am, people will either pull away or just not understand. So I keep it hidden and the distance just grows.

Most days I tell myself I’m just tired, or busy, or introverted. But underneath that, it feels more like I’m slowly disappearing from other people’s lives, and I’m not sure how to reverse it, or if I even can.
