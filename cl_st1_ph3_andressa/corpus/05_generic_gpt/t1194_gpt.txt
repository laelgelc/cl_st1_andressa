I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t fit neatly into the usual “no friends” box. I technically have people around me—coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats—but I still feel like I’m on mute in a crowded room.

Conversations feel so surface-level. I listen, I nod, I say the right things, but it’s like I’m playing a character of myself. I can’t remember the last time I said exactly what I was thinking without editing it in my head first. I always worry it’ll be “too much,” too dark, too intense, or just… boring.

What makes it worse is that it’s not like anyone is being cruel. They’re just busy, distracted, living their own lives. I’m the one who doesn’t seem to know how to actually connect. When I try to open up a little, the moment passes fast, or the topic changes, and I feel stupid for even trying.

Nights are the hardest. I scroll through my phone, debating if I should message someone, and then talk myself out of it because I don’t want to be “that person” who only reaches out when they’re not okay. So I end up lying there, replaying old conversations and imagining new ones that will probably never happen.

It’s a strange thing to be surrounded by people and still feel like if I quietly disappeared from their daily lives, it would barely register as more than a brief inconvenience.
