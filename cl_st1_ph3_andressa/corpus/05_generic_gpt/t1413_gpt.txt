I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I respond in group chats, I show up when I’m supposed to. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice.” But when I leave any social situation, it’s like the world shuts off and I’m just… parked in silence. No one texts me first unless they need something. Weekends stretch out in this weird gray way where I don’t actually do anything, I just scroll, nap, stare at the ceiling, repeat.

What messes with me the most is that I don’t feel truly known by anyone. Even when I’m around people, there’s this invisible glass wall. I edit what I say so much that conversations feel like small performances instead of real connection. Then I go home and replay everything I said, convinced I sounded awkward or boring.

I keep wondering if this is just how adulthood is, or if I somehow missed the point where everyone else learned how to actually belong.
