I don’t really know how to explain this feeling without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, acquaintances, a couple of old friends I text once in a while. I talk to people every day. But it all feels like background noise. It’s like being in a crowded room wearing noise-cancelling headphones: I see mouths moving, people laughing, but there’s this thick glass wall between me and everyone else.

What’s messing with me the most is that I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely known by someone. Not just “they know my favorite TV show” known, but the kind where you can say something half-formed and the other person still gets it. I censor myself constantly—tone it down, don’t be too intense, don’t be too quiet, don’t be too weird. Then I go home and feel resentful that no one knows the real me, even though I never actually show the real me.

I scroll through chats and see all these dead conversations: “we should hang out soon!” from months ago that never turned into anything. I stare at the message box, type something, delete it, type something else, delete that too. I’m tired of feeling like I’m begging for scraps of attention, but the alternative is more silence.

Nights are the worst. The day distracts me, but once it’s quiet, everything hits at once. I’ll lie in bed and imagine what it would be like if someone texted me just to say, “I saw this and thought of you,” or if there was someone I could call without rehearsing what I’m going to say beforehand. Instead, my phone stays dark unless an app reminds me to drink water.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I guess I just needed to put it somewhere outside my head for once, because carrying it around all the time is starting to feel heavier than it should for something that’s technically “invisible.”
