I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately I feel like I’m living in a glass box.

From the outside, my life looks fine. I have a job, I answer messages, I show up when I’m supposed to. Technically I “talk” to people every day. But it all feels like I’m playing a role and nobody actually knows me, and I’m starting to wonder if anyone ever really has.

I scroll through group chats where people send memes and quick updates, and I’ll type something out, then delete it because it feels off, or too much, or just…unnecessary. When I do send something, it usually gets a couple of reactions and then the conversation moves on like I never said anything. I’m there, but not really there. Background noise.

The weirdest part is how exhausting it is to be around people now. I can go to a gathering and talk and laugh and make small talk, but I’m counting the minutes until I can go home. And then, when I’m finally home, the silence hits so hard that I almost wish I was back in that room pretending again. It’s like I’m stuck between wanting connection and wanting to disappear.

I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely understood. Not “oh yeah, same” on a surface level, but that feeling where you say something honest and the other person really gets it. I keep thinking maybe I’m just not that likeable, or I’m too quiet, too awkward, too something. People say “you just have to put yourself out there,” but I don’t even know what “out there” is supposed to look like anymore.

Most nights I end up lying in bed, phone in hand, watching other people’s lives through a screen. Friends getting engaged, traveling, posting pictures with their “favorite people.” I’m happy for them, or at least I want to be, but there’s this heavy feeling in my chest like I’ve somehow missed a train that everyone else managed to catch. And I don’t know if there’s another one coming.
