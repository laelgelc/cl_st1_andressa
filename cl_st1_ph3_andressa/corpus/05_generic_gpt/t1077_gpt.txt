I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’ve become invisible in my own life.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, I talk to coworkers, I answer messages in group chats. But it all feels like background noise. The second I’m not physically in front of someone, it’s like I disappear from their mental radar. No one checks in unless I initiate, and even then the conversations feel shallow, like we’re all reading from a script.

What’s messing with me the most is that I can’t tell if I’m the problem. I overthink every interaction: Did I talk too much? Not enough? Was I boring? Annoying? I keep replaying moments where people drifted away from me, trying to find the exact line where I said or did something wrong, even though I never actually find it.

I miss feeling chosen. Not in a romantic sense necessarily, just… being someone’s first thought instead of their last resort. I watch other people make plans around me, tag each other in memes, send each other random “this made me think of you” stuff, and I’m just there, liking things from the sidelines.

The weirdest part is how lonely I feel even when I’m physically with people. I can be in a room full of voices and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else actually live while I just perform being “okay.” I’ve gotten so good at pretending that I don’t even know how to be honest in real time anymore. It only comes out here, to strangers I’ll never meet.
