I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really make sense on paper. I have coworkers I talk to, a few old friends I message every now and then, family chats that light up my phone. But it all feels like background noise, like I’m sitting behind a glass wall watching my own life happen.

What gets to me is how hard it is to be truly known. I can joke around, talk about work, complain about the weather, but the moment I try to say, “Hey, I’m actually not okay,” the conversation either gets awkward or redirected. It makes me feel like I’m too much when I’m honest and not enough when I’m not.

I scroll through social media and see people doing things in groups, having inside jokes, casually mentioning “my best friend” like it’s the most normal thing in the world. I can’t even remember the last time I felt like someone’s first choice.

I don’t really know what I’m looking for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to say it somewhere without having to soften it or make it sound better than it is.
