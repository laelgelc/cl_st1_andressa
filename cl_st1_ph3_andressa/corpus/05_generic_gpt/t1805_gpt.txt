I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into any of the usual categories people talk about. It’s not that I have *no one* in my life. I have coworkers I chat with, a couple of old friends I text here and there, family members who send the occasional “how are you?” But it all feels…surface-level. Like I’m surrounded by people but somehow still completely disconnected.

What’s been hitting me hardest is the sense that no one really knows me, not the version that exists in my head. I edit myself constantly in conversations—tone things down, keep it light, make sure I’m not “too much.” Then I go home and feel resentful that no one understands me, even though I never give them the chance. It’s like I’m building my own cage and then complaining about the bars.

I see other people having these close, almost effortless friendships where they can just show up as they are, and I honestly don’t know how they got there. I feel stuck in this loop where I want deeper connections but don’t trust that anyone would actually want the unfiltered version of me. So I stay quiet, then feel invisible, then withdraw more.

The worst moments are at night, when I realize there isn’t a single person I’d feel comfortable calling if I really needed to talk. Not because they’d be cruel or anything, but because I don’t feel like I occupy that space in anyone’s life. I’m always the “optional” person, never the first call. And it’s starting to feel like that might just be my permanent role.
