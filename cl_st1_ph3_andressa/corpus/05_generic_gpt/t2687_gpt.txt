I don’t really know how to talk about this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from everyone’s life, including my own.

Most of my days are just… quiet. Not peaceful quiet, more like “no one would notice if I vanished for a week” quiet. My phone barely buzzes unless it’s some app notification or a spam call. I scroll through chats and see conversations that just faded out months ago and never picked back up. I don’t even know how to restart them without it feeling forced or pathetic.

When I’m around people, I feel like I’m acting. I can make small talk, crack a joke here and there, but it feels like I’m watching myself from the outside. Everyone else seems to have these built‑in groups, inside jokes, plans, routines. I’m the person who gets invited as a courtesy once in a while, not the one anyone thinks of first. Sometimes not at all.

What really messes with my head is that I’m not completely isolated. I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I can talk to. It’s the lack of closeness that hurts. No one really knows me. No one checks in just to see how I’m doing. If I don’t initiate, nothing happens. It makes me feel like I’m the extra piece in a board game that no one needs.

I’ve tried joining groups, hobbies, online communities. I show up, try to be friendly, but it never moves past that “surface level.” I leave, go home, and the silence feels louder because I tried and still ended up alone.

I don’t know if I’m the problem—too quiet, too awkward, too something—or if this is just how adulthood is now. I just know that I’m tired of talking to walls, tired of pretending I’m fine with being “independent” when it’s really just loneliness with better branding.
