I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness that doesn’t really match what my life looks like on paper. I have coworkers, a couple of casual friends, family chats on WhatsApp. I’m not isolated in the obvious way. But I feel like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else.

Conversations stay on the surface: work, memes, small talk. I don’t know how to cross that invisible line into “real” connection without feeling like I’m being too much or too needy. I censor myself a lot, then end up feeling unknown and weirdly invisible.

Nights are the worst. I’ll scroll through my phone, not even wanting to message anyone because I assume I’m just an obligation, or that they already have their “real people” and I’m just an extra. I keep fantasizing about having that one person I can text anything to without overthinking every word.

I’m starting to wonder if maybe this is just how adulthood is for some of us: surrounded, but alone anyway.
