I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into the usual “I have no friends” narrative. I technically do have people in my life—coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up my phone—but it all feels distant, like I’m watching my own life through glass.

I keep noticing how most of my conversations are surface-level. “How’s work?” “How was your weekend?” I answer, they answer, and then it dies. I don’t know how to cross that invisible line into saying, “Actually, I feel really disconnected and kind of lost. Do you ever feel that way?” It feels too heavy, like I’d be breaking some unspoken rule of casual interaction.

What makes it worse is that I’ve started to feel lonely even when I’m physically with people. I’ll be sitting in a room full of chatter and laughter, and there’s this quiet part of me that’s just…not there. Like I’m playing a role, nodding and smiling on cue, while the real me is off to the side, watching.

It’s gotten to the point where I hesitate to reach out because I’m tired of small talk, but I also don’t know who, if anyone, would actually want to hear the real answer if I said, “I’m not okay.” So I just don’t say it.
