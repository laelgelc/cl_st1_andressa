I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it scares me more than being alone does.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I can chat with about shows or work. But it all feels like I’m acting out a script I memorized years ago. I laugh at the right times, ask the right questions, and then go home and feel like I was never really there. It’s like I’m a background character in my own life.

Nights are the worst. I’ll scroll through my phone, wanting to message someone, and then talk myself out of it because I’m convinced I’m just an obligation to them. I replay old conversations and pick apart every “we should hang out sometime” that never turned into anything. I don’t know if I’m the one pushing people away or if I was never really wanted in the first place.

What makes it harder is that from the outside, my life probably looks fine. Job, roof over my head, no obvious crisis. But I go days without a real, honest conversation. I’m starting to forget what it feels like to be known by someone, and I’m not sure how you fix that when you don’t even know where it went wrong.
