I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living in a glass box. People can see me, I can see them, we interact, but nothing actually touches me.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, coworkers I get along with, a couple of acquaintances I can text about surface-level stuff. I visit family on holidays. I answer “not much” when people ask what’s new. It all looks normal enough.

But there’s this constant, low-grade ache that never really shuts off. I can be standing in a room full of people and feel like a ghost hovering just outside of it. I hear myself laugh at the right times, say the right things, and it feels like I’m playing a character I don’t remember choosing.

What bothers me most is how hard it is to be known. I don’t mean in some deep poetic way, just… having someone who actually notices when I go quiet, or who cares about the little details of my day. I keep trying to open up a bit more with people, but the conversations always slide back to small talk. I walk home replaying everything I said, wondering if I’m just fundamentally bad at being close to anyone.

Nights are the worst. That’s when the silence gets loud and my brain starts listing all the people I used to talk to and somehow lost along the way. I don’t even know when it happened, just that somewhere between “let’s hang out soon” and “we should catch up sometime” everything faded.

I’m tired of feeling like an extra in my own life.
