I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life without sounding dramatic, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people on paper. Coworkers, acquaintances, family chats buzzing on my phone. But it all feels like I’m watching life through glass. I show up, smile, make small talk, do the “I’m fine, you?” routine, then go home and feel like I was never really there.

I keep thinking about how no one actually knows me. Not the stuff I pretend to like, but the things I’m really afraid of, the things that keep me up at night. I’ve tried to open up a few times and it always seems to land wrong—people change the subject, joke it away, or give advice that makes it obvious they didn’t really hear me.

It’s starting to feel easier to just stay surface-level with everyone. But the more I do that, the more invisible I feel. I scroll through social media and see people posting about their “people” and I can’t even picture what it would be like to text someone when something small but important happens and know they’d actually care.

Most days I just feel like background noise in my own life.
