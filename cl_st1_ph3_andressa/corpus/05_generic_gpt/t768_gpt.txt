I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m standing behind a glass wall while everyone else is living on the other side. I “function” fine – job, bills paid, small talk with coworkers, the usual. But it all feels like I’m playing a role instead of actually being part of anything.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, some acquaintances from school, family chats that are mostly memes and logistics. But I don’t feel like anyone actually knows me beyond the surface-level version I perform. When I try to open up, I either feel like I’m oversharing, or I get brushed off with “you’ll be fine” or “everyone feels like that sometimes.” So I just stop halfway and go back to nodding and smiling.

Nights are the worst. I’ll scroll through my phone, wanting to message someone, but I can’t think of a single person I’d feel comfortable being honest with. Or I’ll type something and delete it because it feels needy or weird. Then I end up just sitting in the dark, feeling this heavy ache in my chest, like I’m missing something essential that other people got by default.

It messes with my sense of self. If no one really sees me, do I actually exist as a person beyond my own head? I catch myself rehearsing what I’ll say before I say it, constantly editing myself, trying to be “easy to be around,” and then wondering why no one gets close to me. It’s like I’m simultaneously afraid of being alone and afraid of being seen.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by posting this. I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own brain. I feel tired of pretending I’m okay when the truth is that most days, I feel like a background character in my own life, watching everyone else move forward while I’m stuck in the same quiet, empty place.
