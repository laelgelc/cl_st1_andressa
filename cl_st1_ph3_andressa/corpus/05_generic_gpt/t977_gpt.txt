I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness that doesn’t really fit into the usual “I have no one” box. I technically have people in my life: family I see on holidays, coworkers I chat with, a couple of old friends I text every now and then. From the outside, it probably looks like I’m functioning fine.

But I feel like I’m living in a glass box. I can see everyone, and they can see me, but there’s this barrier I can’t seem to get through. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes, “how was your weekend?” I can’t remember the last time I felt like I really opened up to someone and they actually stayed, actually wanted to know more instead of changing the subject or going quiet.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like people say. I join group chats, go to events, comment on posts, but I end up feeling more alone afterward, like I’m forcing myself into spaces where I don’t actually belong. I’m either “too much” when I try to be real, or “too quiet” when I hold back. It feels like there’s no version of me that fits.

What scares me most is how normal this is starting to feel. I come home, scroll, distract myself, go to bed, repeat. When something good or bad happens, my first thought isn’t “who can I tell?” It’s “there’s no one to tell.” And then I just move on like that’s the most ordinary thing in the world.
