I’ve been feeling this weird combination of invisible and too visible at the same time, and it’s starting to mess with my sense of who I am.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, a few old friends I text every now and then, family chats that light up my phone. But none of it feels like it actually reaches me. It’s like everyone is interacting with a version of me that knows the right things to say, laughs at the right moments, and then disappears the second the conversation ends. The real me just sort of sits in the background, untouched.

Most days I go through the routine: wake up, work, come home, scroll, sleep. The only time I feel anything close to connection is when I’m consuming other people’s lives online—watching their group photos, inside jokes, spontaneous plans. I’m not jealous in the obvious way; it’s more like I’m watching a movie about a life I’m not part of and never learned how to audition for.

What makes it harder is that I don’t even know how to fix it. I’ve tried reaching out, suggesting plans, being more “open,” but everything feels forced, like I’m trying to push two magnets together the wrong way. I end up overthinking every message I send and every silence I get back. Eventually I just retreat and tell myself it’s easier not to try.

The loneliness isn’t always loud. Sometimes it’s just this quiet heaviness that sits in the room with me, especially at night, when I realize no one really knows how my day went, what I’m worried about, what I’m hoping for. It’s like my life is happening in a soundproof box. People can see me moving, but they can’t hear a thing.
