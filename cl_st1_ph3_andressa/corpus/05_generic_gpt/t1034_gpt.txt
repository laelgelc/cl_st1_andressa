I’m starting to feel like I’ve somehow slipped out of everyone’s peripheral vision.

People text me when they need something, or when a group is already forming and they want numbers. But no one really checks in. I can go days without a personal message that isn’t transactional. When I do reach out, I’m suddenly hyper-aware of every minute they take to respond, reading silence as a verdict on my worth.

In groups I feel like background noise—there, but not really part of anything. I talk, people nod, and the conversation drifts back to the same few louder voices. Then I go home and replay every interaction, wondering if I’m just fundamentally unlikeable, or if I somehow missed the point where everyone else learned how to be close to each other.
