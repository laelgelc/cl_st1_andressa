I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living parallel to everyone else instead of with them.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I say “hey, how’s it going?” to coworkers and neighbors. I even laugh at the right times in conversations. But none of it feels like it actually connects. It’s like I’m playing a character I’ve rehearsed for years while the real version of me sits in the back of my head watching.

The weirdest part is that I’m not technically alone that often. I’m around people almost every day. But I feel more alone in a crowded room than I do sitting in my apartment at night. I can’t seem to get past small talk with anyone. The moment things get slightly more personal, my brain tells me I’m being “too much” or “too weird,” and I pull back. Then I go home and feel invisible and wonder why no one really knows me.

I used to think I just hadn’t “found my people” yet, but I’m starting to suspect I’m the common denominator. I overthink every interaction, replaying conversations and cringing at things I said or didn’t say. I convince myself people are just being polite and would rather not deal with me if they had the choice. So I keep my distance, then feel hurt when no one pushes through that distance.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through chats where I’m the one who always reaches out first, or group photos I’m not in, and it just hits me that if I disappeared, most people’s lives would go on exactly the same. I don’t want to be the center of anyone’s world, but I’d like to feel like I matter somewhere, to someone, in a way that’s not so easily replaceable.

Right now it just feels like I’m drifting, and everyone else has somewhere to dock.
