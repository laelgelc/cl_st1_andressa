I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living in a glass box.

From the outside, my life looks fine. I have a job, I respond to messages, I go through the motions. But there’s this constant, heavy awareness that no one really knows me, and I’m not sure anyone actually wants to.

I have “people” in my life, technically. Coworkers, some old friends I text every few weeks, family chats that are mostly memes. But the second I try to be honest about how I feel, everything gets awkward. Jokes, quick subject changes, “you’ll be fine.” It makes me feel like my actual self is too much, so I retreat back into small talk and pretend that’s enough.

Most nights I come home, lie on my bed, and scroll until my eyes hurt. I keep thinking I’ll stumble onto something that makes me feel less alone, but it usually just reminds me how connected everyone else seems to be. I see people having inside jokes, trips, “my person” posts, and I can’t remember the last time I felt like someone’s first choice for anything.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for. I’m not sure I’d know how to handle real closeness if it showed up. I’ve spent so long convincing myself I’m fine on my own that I don’t really know how to be any other way.

It just feels like I’m here, existing parallel to everyone else, but never really with them.
