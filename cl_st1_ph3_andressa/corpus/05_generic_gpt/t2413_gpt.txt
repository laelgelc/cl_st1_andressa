It’s weird how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re in a soundproof box.

I have friends, technically. Group chats, occasional meetups, people I can text memes to. But none of it feels like it actually touches whatever part of me is starving for connection. I’m always the “easygoing” one, the listener, the one who laughs things off. I don’t think anyone really knows me beyond a surface-level sketch, and I’m not even sure I’d know how to let them.

I overthink every interaction: Did I talk too much? Not enough? Was that joke awkward? Then I end up withdrawing, which obviously doesn’t help. Weekends stretch out like this blank space I don’t know how to fill, and I end up scrolling, lurking, half-hoping someone will notice I’m gone if I stay quiet long enough.

Most days, it feels like I’m just watching my own life from the outside, waiting for something to change and not really believing it will.
