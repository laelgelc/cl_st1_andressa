I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… empty space with me echoing around in it.

I’m not completely isolated on paper. I have coworkers I chat with, a family I text with sometimes, a couple of old friends I exchange memes with every now and then. But there’s this constant sense that none of it actually touches me. It’s like there’s glass between me and everyone else. I can see them, they can see me, but nothing really gets through.

I’m at that point where I’m tired of my own company but also exhausted by the idea of trying to connect. Whenever I do talk to people, it feels like I’m playing a character: “normal, functioning, mildly funny version of me.” As soon as I’m alone again, the silence is so loud that I almost miss the small talk I claim to hate.

What messes with my head is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped reaching out, I’m pretty sure most people in my life would just… keep going, maybe notice a little less noise in their notifications. I don’t think anyone really sees me as a “first call” person. I’m always the one who asks “How are you?” and then just kind of fades into the background of their answer.

I’ve tried the usual stuff: hobbies, online communities, group activities. I can be present, I can participate, but I never quite cross that invisible line from “familiar stranger” to “someone who actually matters.” I don’t know if it’s something I’m doing wrong or if I just missed some crucial social development checkpoint years ago.

Nights are the worst. That’s when it hits me that no one is thinking of me in that quiet, soft way that people think of their favorite person. I’m not anyone’s favorite person. I’m just… around.

I don’t really have a point or a neat conclusion. I guess I just wanted to put this somewhere outside my own head for once.
