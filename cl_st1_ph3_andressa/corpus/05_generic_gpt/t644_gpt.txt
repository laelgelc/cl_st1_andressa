I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I talk to coworkers, I text in group chats, I answer “how are you?” with the usual “good, you?” But I feel like I’m living behind glass. I’m there, but not really reachable.

I don’t have anyone I can message at 2 a.m. when my brain won’t shut up. I don’t even have anyone I’d feel comfortable asking to just sit in silence with me. Every interaction feels like I’m playing a role: the “normal” version of me that doesn’t say too much, doesn’t seem too needy, doesn’t make things awkward.

I scroll social media and see people casually mentioning “my friend said…” and I realize I don’t really have those kinds of people. Acquaintances, sure. Surface-level conversations, plenty. But nothing that feels solid.

What scares me is how used to this I’m getting, like loneliness is becoming my default setting and I don’t know how to reset it.
