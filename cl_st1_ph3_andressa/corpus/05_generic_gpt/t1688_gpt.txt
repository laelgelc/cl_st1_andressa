I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… background noise.

I’m surrounded by people on paper. I go to work, I respond in group chats, I say “hey, how’s it going?” to coworkers and neighbors. But it all feels like acting. I don’t feel connected to anyone, and I’m starting to forget what it’s like to actually be known.

What gets to me is the small stuff. Nobody texts first unless it’s about logistics. If I stopped initiating, I honestly don’t know how long it would take before anyone noticed. I see people sharing memes and inside jokes in group chats I’m technically in, and I realize I’m just there to keep the numbers up.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like everyone says. Meetups, hobbies, even forcing myself to stay after events and make small talk. I can be friendly and “on” for a while, but it never moves past the surface. People leave with new acquaintances; I leave with a slightly more polished version of my loneliness.

What scares me is how much I’ve started editing myself down. I don’t share opinions, I don’t mention when I’m struggling, because I’ve learned that people get uncomfortable and drift away. So I end up being this muted version of myself that even I don’t like, just to avoid pushing people further away.

Sometimes I wonder if there’s something fundamentally off about me that I can’t see but everyone else can. Like there’s a sign on my forehead that says “temporary person” and people subconsciously treat me that way.
