I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and “too much” at the same time.

On paper, my life looks fine. I have a job, a place to live, people I can technically text. But most days it feels like I’m just orbiting other people’s lives, not really part of anything. I’m always the one listening, the one people vent to, but when I try to open up, the conversation drifts back to them or dies awkwardly. After a while you start to get the message: your inner world isn’t that interesting to anyone.

I scroll through group chats where I’m technically included, but the plans are always made between other people. I get the “oh we should hang out sometime!” messages that never turn into anything. I’m tired of being the backup plan, the “if no one else is free” person. It’s like I exist in the periphery of everyone’s life, just enough to not be completely alone, but not enough to feel wanted.

What hurts most is how this is changing me. I used to try so hard—initiating plans, sending long messages, remembering little details about people. Lately I’ve stopped. I leave messages on read because I don’t know how to keep pretending I’m okay. I say I’m busy when I’m not, because the idea of showing up and feeling invisible in person is worse than just staying home.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for writing this. Maybe I just needed to put into words that I’m lonely in a way that doesn’t look lonely from the outside.
