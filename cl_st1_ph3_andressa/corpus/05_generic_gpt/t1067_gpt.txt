I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it’s been hitting me how “out of sync” I feel with everyone around me.

On paper, I’m not completely isolated. I have coworkers I chat with, a few old friends I text every now and then, family I see on holidays. But most of the time it feels like I’m just drifting past people rather than actually connecting with them. I say the right things, make small jokes, ask about their weekends, but it all feels like I’m playing a role instead of actually being there.

What bothers me is how invisible this loneliness is. From the outside, I probably look fine. I show up, I function, I laugh at the right moments. But I go home and it’s like the volume of my life drops to almost nothing. I sit in silence, scrolling on my phone, half-hoping someone will message me and half-dreading having to respond if they do, because I’m so out of practice at being real with anyone.

I don’t really have “my person” or “my people.” There’s no one I can text randomly just to say, “I’m having a rough day,” without worrying I’m bothering them or being too much. When something happens—good or bad—my first instinct is to open a chat window, and then I close it because I don’t know who to send it to. It’s like everything important in my life just passes through me and disappears because there’s no one to witness it.

I also feel like I missed some crucial stage where people learn how to form deep connections. I watch others make friends so easily, join group chats, plan outings, and I honestly don’t understand how they do it. I overthink every interaction, replay conversations in my head, and then end up withdrawing because it feels safer than risking rejection or awkwardness.

The worst part is how tired I am of my own company. I used to think I liked being alone, but now it feels more like I’ve just adapted to it because I didn’t have another option. And I’m not sure what to do with that.
