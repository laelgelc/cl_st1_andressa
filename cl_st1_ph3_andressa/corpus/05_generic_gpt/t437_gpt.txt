I don’t know when “being alone” turned into “feeling lonely all the time,” but it’s like it crept up on me slowly.

My days are basically work, commute, screen, sleep. I talk to people, but it’s all surface-level stuff: “How was your weekend?” “Busy.” Then everyone goes back to their own little circles. I’m there, but not really part of anything.

What gets to me most is the silence after I get home. No one texts unless I text first. Group chats keep going without me. I scroll and see people hanging out, getting engaged, having game nights, and I feel like I’m watching life from behind glass.

I try to put myself out there sometimes, but I always feel like the extra chair at the table—technically included, but unnecessary. It makes me question what’s wrong with me that I can’t seem to turn casual interactions into actual connection.
