Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m living in a parallel universe to everyone around me. I go to work, I make small talk, I laugh at the right moments, and then I come home and it’s just…silence. Not peaceful silence, but the kind that makes you suddenly aware of how loud your own thoughts are.

I have people in my life, technically. Coworkers, acquaintances, distant friends I text every few weeks. But I don’t feel known by any of them. I’ve gotten really good at being “fine.” I can sound okay, look okay, function. Inside, it feels like there’s this huge gap between the person I show and the person I actually am, and I don’t know how to bridge it without scaring people away or sounding needy.

What bothers me most is how invisible this all is. There’s no obvious crisis, nothing dramatic. Just this constant, low-level ache of not having anyone I can message when something small but important happens. No one whose name pops into my head when I think, “Who would actually understand this?”

I try to reach out sometimes, but I overthink every message: Am I being annoying? Do they actually want to talk to me or are they just being polite? Then I end up deleting what I wrote and telling myself it doesn’t matter anyway.

It’s strange how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re on the outside of the glass, watching everyone else live their lives while you just sort of…exist alongside them.
