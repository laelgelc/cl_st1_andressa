I’ve started to realize that most of my “social life” is just background noise. I’m around people at work, I answer messages, I scroll through group chats, but I still feel like I’m standing behind glass watching everyone else actually connect.

I have acquaintances, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something really bad happened. I don’t even know who would notice if I disappeared from social media for a month. When people say “we should hang out sometime,” it never turns into anything. I can’t tell if I’m the one holding back or if they just don’t care that much.

What makes it worse is that I’m not completely isolated, so it feels invalid to say I’m lonely. On paper, I look “fine.” But I go home, sit in my room, and the silence feels heavy. I keep wondering if this is just how adulthood is for some people, or if I somehow missed a step everyone else figured out.
