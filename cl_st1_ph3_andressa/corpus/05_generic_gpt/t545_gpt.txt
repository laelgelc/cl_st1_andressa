I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, or maybe I never did.

I have “people” in my life—coworkers, acquaintances, some old friends I text a few times a year—but it all feels like I’m acting in a play where everyone else got the script and I’m improvising badly. I can do small talk, I can crack jokes, but as soon as anything goes even a little deeper, I freeze up or deflect. Then I go home and wonder why no one really knows me.

The worst part is how invisible this feels. From the outside, I probably look fine: functioning adult, decent job, not a complete recluse. Inside, it’s just this constant ache of wanting to be understood and having no idea how to let anyone close enough to do that.

I keep thinking I missed some critical step in life where people learned how to be vulnerable with each other, and now I’m permanently behind, watching from the outside.
