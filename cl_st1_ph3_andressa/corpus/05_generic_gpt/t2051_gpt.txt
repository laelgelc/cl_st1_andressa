I don’t really know how to describe this other than feeling like a ghost in my own life.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, even family—but it all feels like background noise. Conversations are small talk on repeat, like we’re reading from a script. I go home, close the door, and it’s like everything goes silent in a way that’s louder than any noise.

I scroll through chats and realize most of my messages are me reacting to other people’s lives, not anyone asking about mine. I’m “reliable,” “easygoing,” “always there,” but it doesn’t translate into anyone actually choosing me when it matters. I’m invited when it’s convenient, not when it’s intimate.

What gets to me most is how invisible this all is. From the outside, I look fine—job, health, hobbies. Inside, it feels like I’m slowly fading out of focus. I don’t know how to bridge the gap between being “around people” and actually being known by them.
