I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into any neat category. I’m not completely isolated: I have coworkers I chat with, a couple of old friends I text every so often, family I see on holidays. But I still feel like I’m standing behind glass watching everyone else actually live their lives together.

What bothers me most is that I don’t feel *known* by anyone. People know facts about me—where I work, what I like, some stories from my past—but it’s like no one has the full picture, and I don’t know how to bridge that gap. Whenever I try to open up even a little bit, I get this anxious feeling that I’m oversharing or being dramatic, and I end up pulling back or changing the subject. Then I go home and feel frustrated that I didn’t say what I really meant.

I see other people forming these deep connections that seem to grow almost naturally: inside jokes, regular plans, calling each other just to talk. I feel like I’m always on the outside of that, orbiting around other people’s closeness. I’m invited sometimes, but it feels more like they’re being polite than genuinely wanting me there. That might not be true, but it’s how it feels.

The weirdest part is that I’ve started to get used to it. I go through my routines, scroll on my phone, watch shows, and just kind of coexist with this constant low-level ache. When something good or bad happens, my first thought is usually, “Who would I even tell?” and then I realize I don’t really have an answer.

I don’t know if I’m doing something wrong socially, or if I’m just fundamentally not the kind of person people get close to. I keep thinking maybe this is just how my life is going to be: surrounded by people, but never really connected to them, always slightly out of sync.
