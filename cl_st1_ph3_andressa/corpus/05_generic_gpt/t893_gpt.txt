I’m starting to realize I’m not just “introverted,” I’m lonely in a way that feels like it’s hollowing me out.

My days are basically work → home → internet → sleep. I talk to coworkers, but it’s all surface-level. No one really knows anything real about me, and I don’t know much about them either. I go along with it because I don’t want to be the weird one oversharing in the break room.

What messes with my head is that I *do* have people I could technically message. Old friends, acquaintances. But the longer I wait, the heavier that “hey, how have you been?” message feels, like I need a whole explanation for disappearing, so I send nothing. Then more time passes, and it feels even more impossible.

I miss things I’ve barely had: casual invitations, inside jokes, being the person someone thinks to text when something funny happens. I feel like I’m watching life through a window—close enough to see, too far to touch.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by writing this. Maybe I just wanted to put this somewhere that isn’t my own head for once.
