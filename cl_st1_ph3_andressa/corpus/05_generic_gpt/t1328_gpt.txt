I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living in some kind of glass box. People can see me, I can see them, but there’s this invisible barrier I can’t get through.

I have “people” in my life, technically. Coworkers, some old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up my phone. But none of it feels real. Conversations stay on the surface: work, memes, “how was your weekend.” I can be in a group and still feel like an observer who wandered into the wrong room.

I keep thinking it must be my fault. I’m not interesting enough, not funny enough, not warm enough. I rehearse things in my head before I say them so I don’t sound weird, then end up saying nothing and just nodding along. Everyone else seems to connect so effortlessly, like they were handed a manual I missed.

What scares me most is how used to it I’m getting. The quiet evenings, the scrolling, the pretending I’m fine with the routine. When something good or bad happens, my first instinct is to just… keep it to myself, because I don’t know who I’d even tell or how to start.

I’m not sure what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
