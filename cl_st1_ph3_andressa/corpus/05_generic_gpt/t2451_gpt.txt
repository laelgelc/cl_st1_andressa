I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from other people’s lives while still being physically here.

I’m in that weird space where I technically have “people” around me—coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family chats that light up on holidays—but none of it feels like real connection. I can go days without anyone asking how I am in a way that isn’t just small talk. Most of the time I answer “I’m fine” so automatically that I don’t even know if I’d recognize what “not fine” looks like out loud.

I’ve started to notice how much of my day is completely silent. I wake up, get ready, commute, work, come home, eat, scroll, sleep. I might say a handful of sentences to actual people. No one’s mad at me. There’s no big drama. It’s like I’ve just slipped into the background of my own life.

I try to be social sometimes. I’ll send a message to someone, maybe suggest hanging out. Half the time I get “we should sometime” and then nothing happens. The other half I get ghosted or postponed until it just fades away. After a while you start to feel pathetic for even trying. Like you’re forcing yourself into spaces where no one really wants you, they’re just being polite.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. How I’m getting used to eating alone, doing everything alone, not expecting my phone to light up. I used to feel this sharp pain about it; now it’s more like a dull background hum I carry everywhere.

I don’t know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
