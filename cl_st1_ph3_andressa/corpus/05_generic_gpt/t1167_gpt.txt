I don’t really know how to describe this other than feeling like I’m standing behind a glass wall watching everyone else live.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, coworkers I can make small talk with, a family that texts occasionally, a couple of old friends I message once in a while. But I don’t feel connected to anyone. It’s like everything is surface-level, like I’m playing a role and no one actually sees me.

I go days without a real conversation. I talk, but it’s about tasks, schedules, memes, weather. Nothing where I feel safe enough to say, “I’m lonely,” without worrying I’ll sound needy or dramatic. When I do hint at it, people say “you’re not alone” and then change the subject, and I end up feeling more alone for having tried.

I’ve also started noticing how much I edit myself around others. I hide parts of my personality because I’m scared they’ll think I’m weird or too intense. Then I resent that no one knows the “real” me, even though I’m the one hiding. It’s a loop I can’t seem to break.

Nights are the worst. I scroll my phone just to feel some kind of presence, but it usually makes me feel more disconnected. Everyone else looks like they’re part of something—friend groups, relationships, families—while I’m just… here.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for. I just needed to put this somewhere that isn’t a notes app no one will ever read.
