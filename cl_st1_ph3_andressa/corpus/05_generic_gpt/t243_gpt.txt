I’ve been feeling this really specific kind of loneliness lately that’s hard to explain to people in my life, mostly because from the outside I don’t “look” lonely.

I have coworkers, a couple of casual friends, family chats on WhatsApp. I reply to memes, show up to birthdays, make small talk at work. But none of it feels like it actually touches me. It’s like I’m playing a character that knows all the right lines, while the real me is somewhere behind glass watching it happen.

I keep thinking how long it’s been since I’ve felt genuinely known by someone. Not just “how was your day” but the kind of knowing where someone can tell something’s off even when you say you’re fine, or remembers a small detail you mentioned months ago. I miss that more than anything.

I also feel weirdly ashamed of this loneliness, like I should have outgrown it by now. I’m not a teenager anymore. I’m “functioning,” paying bills, doing adult things. Admitting that I feel empty and disconnected makes me feel childish or ungrateful, so I usually keep it to myself and make jokes instead.

Most days I just go through the motions, come home, scroll on my phone, and then suddenly it’s midnight and I realize I didn’t have a single real conversation all day. And then I wonder if this is just how it’s going to be from now on.
