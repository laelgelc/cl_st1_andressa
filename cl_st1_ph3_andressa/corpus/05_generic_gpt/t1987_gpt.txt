I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and exposed lately.

I’m surrounded by people pretty often—work, public transport, family group chats buzzing—but I still feel like I’m living behind glass. I can see everyone else’s lives happening, but there’s this distance, like I’m not really part of anything. I show up, I talk, I laugh at the right times, but it feels like I’m just playing a role I learned from watching other people.

What gets to me most is how hard it is to move past surface-level stuff. I can talk about projects, memes, news, whatever, but when it comes to saying “I’m lonely” or “I feel disconnected,” my throat just closes up. I worry that if I actually say it out loud, people will either pity me or pull away.

There’s also this shame attached to it. Like, I’m an adult, I’m “functional,” I shouldn’t feel this empty. I tell myself I should join more things, message people first, be more open—but even when I try, it still feels like I’m orbiting around everyone else’s lives instead of actually being in them.

It’s exhausting, honestly.
