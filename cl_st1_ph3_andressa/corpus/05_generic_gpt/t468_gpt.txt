I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

I’m not completely isolated in the obvious sense. I have coworkers, I reply in group chats, I see people in stores, I nod at neighbors. On paper it might even look like I’m “social.” But it all feels like background noise. I’m there physically, I participate just enough, and then I go home and it’s like I vanish. No one really follows me back into the quiet.

What I miss is being known. I miss those random, “Hey, this made me think of you” texts. I miss someone remembering how I take my coffee, or asking about something I mentioned weeks ago because they actually cared. Most of my interactions now feel transactional: work talk, small talk, memes in a group chat that don’t mean anything. I can be in a room full of people and feel like a ghost.

I also feel stuck between wanting connection and being terrified of it. When I try to open up a little, I get this voice in my head that says, “You’re being too much, they don’t actually care, don’t burden them.” So I pull back, keep it light, laugh, make jokes. People tell me I seem “fine,” “chill,” “easygoing.” It’s weirdly painful to realize I’ve become really good at hiding the exact thing that’s eating me alive.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through my phone, not really looking for anything, just hoping someone will message first so I don’t feel like I’m forcing myself into their life. Most of the time, nothing. I’ll lie there thinking about old friendships that faded, conversations I wish I’d handled differently, people I probably pushed away by being distant because I was afraid of being left.

I don’t know what I’m expecting by posting this. I guess I just wanted to say it somewhere out loud, because keeping it all in my head is starting to feel heavier than the loneliness itself.
