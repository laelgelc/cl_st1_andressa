I don’t really know how to talk about this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I answer emails, I say “not bad, you?” when people ask how I’m doing. I even laugh sometimes. But it all feels like I’m playing a character that just happens to share my name. When the day is over and I’m back home, the silence hits in this really physical way, like a heavy pressure in the room.

I have people I talk to, technically. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I send memes to, family group chats. But it all stays surface-level. No one actually knows what my days feel like from the inside. I can’t remember the last time someone asked me a question and really waited for the answer.

What hurts the most is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped showing up anywhere, I think people would notice in a vague “oh yeah, where’d they go?” kind of way, but not enough to actually reach out more than once. I don’t feel rooted in anyone’s life.

I’m also starting to forget how to be close to people. When someone is kind to me, I get suspicious or awkward. I overthink every message, every pause in conversation. I don’t know how to move from “acquaintance” to “actual friend” anymore.

Mostly I just wanted to put this somewhere that isn’t inside my own head for once.
