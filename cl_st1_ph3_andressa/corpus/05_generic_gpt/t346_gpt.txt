I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing in slow motion.

On paper I’m “fine” – job, roof over my head, no major crisis. But my days feel like I’m watching someone else’s life from behind glass. I get up, go to work, make small talk that never goes deeper than “how was your weekend,” then come home to silence. The silence is the part that gets me. It’s so loud.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, acquaintances, even a group chat that still pings sometimes. But I can’t shake this feeling that if I stopped replying, or just quietly slipped out of their lives, it wouldn’t really register. Like I’m optional background noise.

What hurts most is not having that one person who knows the “unfiltered” version of me. I edit myself constantly: don’t be too negative, don’t overshare, don’t be weird. I end up giving people this watered‑down version of me and then feel rejected when no one seems to really see me. It’s a loop I don’t know how to break.

Nights are the worst. I scroll, I lurk, I see people posting about their friends, partners, families. I’m happy for them in theory, but it also hits this raw nerve that I’m missing something basic that everyone else got the manual for.

I don’t even know exactly what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just wanted proof that I still exist somewhere outside my own head.
