I don’t even know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… empty background noise.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers I joke with, family group chats that light up sometimes, a couple of old friends I send memes to. From the outside it probably looks like I’m “fine.” But I can go entire days without feeling like anyone actually sees me, or that anything I say really lands with another person.

What makes it worse is that I’m not completely isolated, so I feel like I don’t have the “right” to feel this lonely. I show up, I play the part, I make small talk, and then I go home and sit in my room and feel like I’m watching everyone else live real lives through a glass wall.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like people always say. Joined a couple of groups, tried to be more active in chats, even went on some dates. Every time, I end up feeling like an extra in someone else’s story. They have their close circles already, their inside jokes, their shared history. I’m just… there. Replaceable.

What stings the most is that no one really knows me. Not the stuff that actually matters. My fears, the thoughts that keep me up at night, the weird little things I care about way too much. I don’t have that person I can message at 2 a.m. without overthinking it. I don’t have a “safe” person at all.

Sometimes I catch myself rehearsing conversations in my head that will never happen, where someone actually asks and waits for the real answer. I don’t even know what I’d say anymore. I’ve gotten so used to saying “I’m good” that I’m not sure how to be anything else out loud.
