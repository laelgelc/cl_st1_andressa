I’ve been feeling this weird combination of invisible and exposed lately, like I’m standing in a crowded room under a spotlight and somehow no one can actually see me.

I have people around me. Coworkers, acquaintances, group chats that light up all day. I answer messages, send memes, do all the surface-level stuff you’re “supposed” to do to stay connected. But it all feels like I’m talking through a pane of glass. I can see everyone, hear them, but there’s this thickness between us that I can’t break through.

What really gets to me is how hard it is to admit this out loud. When I try to say “I feel lonely,” it either sounds dramatic or pathetic, even to my own ears. So I end up making jokes, changing the subject, saying I’m just tired or busy. Then I go home and sit in the quiet, scrolling, waiting for a notification that never really fills the gap anyway.

I miss feeling known. Not just “How’s work?” or “Did you see this show?” but the kind of connection where someone remembers the small things you said weeks ago, where you don’t have to translate yourself or worry you’re being too much. I can’t tell if I’ve forgotten how to let people in, or if everyone else is just as walled off and pretending better than I am.

Sometimes I wonder if this is just how adult life is now: everyone orbiting around each other, close enough to see but too far to actually touch. And then I start to question if this is just my own fault, like I missed some unspoken social lesson everyone else picked up along the way.
