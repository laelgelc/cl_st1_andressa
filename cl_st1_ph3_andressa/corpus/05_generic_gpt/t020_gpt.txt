I don’t really know how to say this in a way that doesn’t sound dramatic, but I feel like I’m living my life behind glass.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I respond to messages, I show up when I’m supposed to. People would probably describe me as quiet but “nice.” I make small talk about work, the weather, whatever show is popular. I smile, I nod, I laugh at the right times. Then I go home and it’s like I evaporate. No one notices that part.

What’s getting to me lately is this sense that I’m “around people” all the time but not actually *with* anyone. At work, everyone has their little groups. They talk about weekend trips, brunches, game nights. I’m always just close enough to overhear plans, never close enough to be included. I stand there pretending to be busy on my phone so it doesn’t look as awkward as it feels.

I’ve tried to put myself out there. I’ve gone to meetups, joined group chats, even forced myself to start conversations. It always ends the same way: a few surface-level interactions that slowly fade out, like I’m forgettable background noise. I don’t think people dislike me; it’s more like I’m just… not anyone’s first choice. Or second. Or third.

The worst part is how it messes with my sense of reality. I start wondering if maybe I’m actually really boring, or off-putting in ways I can’t see. I replay every conversation, every silence, every time someone’s eyes drift past me to someone more interesting. It’s exhausting to constantly feel like you’re auditioning for a role in other people’s lives and never getting the part.

Nights are the hardest. I come home, put my stuff down, and the silence just hits me. No one asking how my day was, no one to vent to, no one to sit next to on the couch doing nothing. I scroll through social media and it feels like watching a world I’m not part of. Everyone seems to have “their people.” I have… me.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to put it somewhere other than my own head for once.
