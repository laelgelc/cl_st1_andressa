I’m not even sure what I’m looking for by writing this, but I’m tired of hearing my own thoughts echo around in my head.

I’m in that weird place where my life looks “fine” from the outside. I have a job, a place to live, a couple of acquaintances I can make small talk with. But there’s no one who actually knows me. No one I can text something stupid to in the middle of the day. No one whose name popping up on my phone makes me feel relieved instead of anxious.

Most of my interactions feel like I’m playing a character I don’t fully believe in. I can be polite, even funny sometimes, but it’s like I’m watching myself from a distance. When the conversation ends, I go home and the silence is so loud it almost feels physical. I put on YouTube or a podcast not because I’m interested, but because I can’t stand hearing how quiet my apartment is.

I’m also starting to feel this strange disconnect with time. Weekdays blur together. Weekends are just longer stretches of being alone. Sometimes I’ll go an entire weekend without speaking out loud to another person. When I do finally talk, my voice sounds unfamiliar, like it belongs to someone else.

I don’t know if I’m lonely because I’m bad at connecting, or if I’m bad at connecting because I’ve been lonely for so long. I just know that I’m tired of feeling like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.
