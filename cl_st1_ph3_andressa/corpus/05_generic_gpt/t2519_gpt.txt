I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I interact with people every day, I can make small talk, I reply to memes in group chats. But it all feels like I’m playing a character that people vaguely like, while the real version of me is sitting alone somewhere in the background, untouched.

The weirdest part is how inconsistent it is. I can be in a room full of people laughing and still feel like I’m not actually there. I hear my own voice joining in, but it feels rehearsed, like I’m borrowing lines from someone else who knows how to be normal.

I’ve tried to reach out before, in small ways. Dropping hints that I’m not okay, saying “I’ve been kind of off lately” or “I’m tired all the time.” People usually respond with “same” or a laughing emoji, and the moment passes. I don’t blame them, but it always reinforces this idea that my loneliness is something I’m supposed to hide or turn into a joke.

What scares me is how used to it I’m getting. The empty evenings, the silence after my phone lights up and it’s just another notification that doesn’t actually mean anything. The way I hesitate before messaging anyone because I feel like I’m intruding.

I keep wondering if there’s something fundamentally off about me that other people can sense, something that makes them keep me at arm’s length while still being “nice” on the surface.

I don’t know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe just proof that I exist somewhere outside my own head.
