I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I answer messages, I show up to things when I’m supposed to. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice.” But the truth is I feel completely disconnected from everyone, like I’m just playing a role I don’t remember auditioning for.

I have acquaintances, but not people I can actually call when something is wrong. I scroll through my contacts sometimes and realize there isn’t a single person I feel comfortable being honest with. Even when I’m physically around others, I feel like I’m behind glass. I can see and hear everything, but nothing really touches me.

What makes it worse is that I’m not even sure how to fix it. I’ve tried joining groups, going to events, making small talk. I can do the surface-level stuff, but it never turns into real connection. I always feel like I’m too much or not enough, and I end up retreating before anything can really develop. Then I hate myself for retreating.

I’ve started to notice how much of my life is just filler. I go to work, come home, eat something, scroll or watch something until I’m tired enough to sleep. Weekends are the same, just with more hours to fill. I keep thinking, “This can’t be all there is,” but I don’t know what else to do.

It’s a strange kind of grief, being lonely while technically “not alone.” Like mourning relationships that never existed. I don’t really have a point to this post. I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
