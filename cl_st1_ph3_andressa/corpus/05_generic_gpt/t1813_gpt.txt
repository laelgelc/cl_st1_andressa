I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s changing who I am.

My days are basically work, commute, screen, sleep. I talk to people, but it’s all surface-level: “How was your weekend?” “Busy, you?” I can be surrounded by coworkers or family and still feel like I’m watching everything through glass.

I used to have friends I’d text without overthinking. Now I stare at my phone, type something, delete it, and convince myself I’m annoying. The longer I stay quiet, the harder it feels to reach out. It’s like my social muscles atrophied.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. I don’t even know what I’d say if someone actually asked, “How are you really?” I’m not sure I remember how to answer that honestly.
