I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and exposed lately.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, a few people I can text if I really need something. But it feels like my life is happening behind a pane of glass. I can see everyone else talking, laughing, making plans, but I’m not really in it. I’m just…near it.

What bothers me most is how hard it is to be honest about it in real life. If I say “I’m lonely,” people either rush to fix it with advice or they get uncomfortable and change the subject. So I end up pretending I’m busy, or tired, or “just introverted,” when the truth is I go days without a real, unfiltered conversation.

I miss feeling known. Not just “how’s work?” small talk, but the kind of connection where someone remembers the small details about you and cares enough to ask. Lately, I notice I’m censoring myself even more, like I’m scared that if I show too much of who I am, it’ll just push people further away.

I don’t really know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I guess I just wanted to put the feeling somewhere outside of my own head for once, instead of carrying it around in silence.
