I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to other people’s lives instead of inside my own.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I talk to coworkers, I answer “not much, you?” in group chats. I send memes, I use the right emojis, I reply “lol” at the right moments. But none of it feels like it actually touches me. It’s like I’m playing a character who is socially functional while the real me is sitting in a dark theater watching.

The strangest part is how invisible this loneliness is. No one sees it because I technically “interact” with people every day. I’m not physically isolated. I just feel like there’s this glass wall between me and everyone else. I can see them, hear them, even make them laugh sometimes, but I can’t feel that sense of being known or wanted in any real way.

I’ve tried to “fix” it by being more social, saying yes to invitations, trying hobbies, all the stuff you’re supposed to do. I end up standing in a room full of people, smiling and nodding, and somehow feel even more alone. I don’t know how to move a conversation from surface-level to something that actually matters without feeling like I’m being too intense or weird.

What scares me is how easy it’s becoming to just… withdraw. Go home, lie in bed, scroll, watch something, repeat. The less I try, the fewer chances there are to feel rejected or out of place. But then there are also fewer chances for anything to feel different. And I’m not sure which version of loneliness is worse.
