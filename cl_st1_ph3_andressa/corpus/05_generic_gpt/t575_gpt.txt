I’ve started to realize that I feel loneliest when I’m technically “not alone.” I can sit in a room full of people, laugh at the right moments, say the right things, and still feel like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else. They see a version of me that functions, but it doesn’t feel like me.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I can message about surface-level stuff, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something really broke inside me. I scroll through my contacts sometimes and realize there isn’t a single person I’d feel comfortable being fully honest with. It makes me wonder if it’s my fault—if I’m too closed off, too weird, too boring, or just fundamentally unlikable.

What scares me most is how normalized it’s becoming. I go to work, come home, eat, watch something, go to sleep. Repeat. The emptiness is just woven into the routine now, like it’s another chore I’m supposed to carry around.
