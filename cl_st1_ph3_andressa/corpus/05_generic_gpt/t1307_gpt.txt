I’ve been feeling like a ghost in my own life lately.

I’m surrounded by people on paper—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats that light up my phone—but I don’t feel like I actually *exist* to anyone in a real way. I can go days without anyone asking how I am beyond the automatic “how’s it going?” that doesn’t really invite an honest answer.

What’s messing with my head is that nothing is “wrong” in the obvious sense. I have a job, a place to live, I’m not in immediate crisis. But I come home, close the door, and it’s just silence. I eat alone, scroll alone, watch shows alone. If I disappeared from social media tomorrow, I’m not sure anyone would notice for a long time.

I try to reach out sometimes, but it always feels like I’m intruding. People are “busy,” they “should hang out sometime,” but it never actually happens unless I push, and I’m tired of feeling like I’m begging for scraps of attention. It makes me feel needy and pathetic, even though I know humans are supposed to want connection.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for writing this. Maybe I just needed to admit out loud that I’m lonely. That it hurts to feel this invisible. That I’m starting to forget what it feels like to be genuinely known by another person.
