I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, a few people I can text. I show up, I answer “I’m good, you?” when people ask how I am, I laugh when I’m supposed to. And then I go home and it’s like someone pulled the plug on everything. The silence feels heavy, not peaceful. I just sit there scrolling or staring at nothing, feeling this weird ache that I can’t justify to anyone.

The thing that bothers me most isn’t just being alone, it’s feeling fundamentally…unreachable. Like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else. I watch people form inside jokes, share memories, talk about their “people,” and I can’t imagine anyone ever thinking of me that way. Even when I’m physically with others, I feel like an extra in their movie.

I also feel like I missed some critical social development stage. I overthink every interaction: did I talk too much, too little, sound weird, seem needy? Then I pull back to avoid embarrassing myself, which just makes me more isolated. It’s like I’m always either too close or too far away, never at a normal distance.

What scares me is how normal this has started to feel. The empty weekends, the “maybe next time” messages that never turn into plans, eating alone, going to bed knowing no one really noticed I existed that day. I keep telling myself I’ll try harder “soon,” but the longer this goes on, the more it feels like this is just how my life is going to be.
