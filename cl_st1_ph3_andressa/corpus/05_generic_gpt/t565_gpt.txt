I’ve been feeling like a ghost in my own life lately.

I go through the motions—work, errands, scrolling on my phone—but it all feels like I’m moving through a world made of glass. I see people, I hear them, but there’s this invisible wall between us. I’m there, but not really there.

What’s been hitting me hardest is this weird combination of being “known” and completely unknown at the same time. People know my name, my job, my basic story. I can make small talk, crack a joke, even seem “fine” in group situations. But no one actually sees what’s going on inside. No one really checks in past the “how are you?” that expects a one-word answer. And I’m guilty of playing along, answering “good” or “tired” because I don’t even know how to explain what’s really going on without sounding dramatic or needy.

It’s not like I have zero people in my life. I do. But there’s this constant sense that I’m peripheral to everyone. Like I’m the backup friend, the person you invite when your first choices are busy. The one you text when you need something, not when you just want to share a random thought. I don’t think anyone would choose me as their “person” if they had to pick one.

Sometimes I wonder if this is just who I am now: someone who exists on the outer edges. I’ve tried being more open, tried suggesting plans, tried reaching out first. People respond, but the energy never feels mutual. It’s like I’m always swimming against the current, trying to stay in people’s lives while they’re barely treading water to stay in mine.

The worst part is nights. When everything is quiet and there’s no distraction, it hits me how long it’s been since I felt genuinely connected to someone. Not just talking, but feeling understood. Feeling wanted, not tolerated.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for posting this. Maybe I just needed to put it somewhere that isn’t my own head for once.
