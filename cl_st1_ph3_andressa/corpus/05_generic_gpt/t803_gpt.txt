I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I answer emails, I say hi to coworkers, I text people back (eventually). I’m not completely isolated, but everything feels thin, like I’m interacting through glass. Conversations are small talk and memes, and when they end I’m left with this heavy quiet that feels louder than any noise.

What really gets to me is how invisible it all is. No one sees “lonely” if you still show up and laugh at the right times. I’ve tried dropping hints—“yeah, I’ve been kind of down,” or “I don’t really hang out with people much”—and it either gets brushed off or turned into a joke. It makes me feel stupid for even trying.

I miss having someone who actually knows me. Not the curated version, but the messy, unedited parts. Someone I can send a random thought to without overthinking if I’m being annoying. Someone who notices when I go quiet and doesn’t just assume I’m busy.

Most nights I just scroll until I’m too tired to think, then wake up and do it again. I keep telling myself I’ll change something—join a group, message that person, whatever—but the idea of trying and being met with indifference again feels exhausting.

So I just…stay here, in this weird limbo where I’m surrounded by people and still somehow alone.
