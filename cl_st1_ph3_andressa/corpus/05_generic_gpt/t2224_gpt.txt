I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and “too much” at the same time, and it’s starting to mess with my head.

I have people around me technically—coworkers, a couple of acquaintances, family I text with sometimes—but I don’t feel known by anyone. Conversations stay surface-level: work, weather, memes, “how was your weekend?” I answer, they answer, and it all feels like we’re reading lines from a script we didn’t write.

I keep thinking about how there’s no one who would notice if I disappeared from social media, or if I stopped replying for a few days. People might eventually check in, but mostly out of habit or politeness. There’s no one who’d say, “Something feels off with you lately” because no one knows what “normal” even looks like for me.

I’m also tired of being the one who reaches out first. I’ll send messages, try to start conversations, suggest hanging out, and it’s either “yeah, we should sometime” (which never happens) or just silence. After a while it starts to feel like begging for scraps of attention. Then I feel pathetic for even wanting that so badly.

The worst part is I’m starting to doubt my own memories of being close to people. I used to have friends I shared everything with, late-night calls, stupid inside jokes, all of that. Now it feels like I imagined it. I scroll through old messages sometimes just to confirm it actually happened.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I’m just tired of carrying all of this in my head with nowhere for it to go, and no one in my real life who would actually want to hear it.
