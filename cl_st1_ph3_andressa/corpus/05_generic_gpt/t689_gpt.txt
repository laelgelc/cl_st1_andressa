I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really make sense on paper. I have people around me—coworkers, a couple of friends I see sometimes, family chats on holidays—but it all feels like I’m just playing a role. I show up, say the right things, laugh at the right moments, then go home and feel like I was never actually there.

What’s messing with me is how invisible it all feels. From the outside, I probably look “fine.” I go to work, answer messages, post the occasional meme. Inside, it feels like there’s this huge distance between me and everyone else, like I’m behind glass watching life happen to other people.

I want real connection so badly, but I don’t even know where to start anymore. I overthink every message, worry I’m being annoying, and then end up saying nothing. Then I sit there refreshing apps, hoping someone will reach out first, even though I know that’s not how it works.

It’s like I’m tired of being alone, but also exhausted by the idea of trying to be known.
