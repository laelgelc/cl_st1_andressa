I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into any neat category. I’m not completely isolated — I have coworkers, some acquaintances, family chats — but it all feels like background noise. I can go through an entire day “interacting” without feeling like anyone actually sees me.

What messes with my head is that I’m not even sure what I’m looking for. I want connection, but when opportunities come up, I freeze or feel like I’m pretending to be a person who’s more normal, more stable, more “together” than I really am. Then I go home and replay every small thing I said, convinced I sounded weird or boring.

I scroll through social media and see people doing ordinary things with friends — going for coffee, sharing inside jokes — and it feels like watching a life that never quite started for me. I’m old enough that it feels like I should have found “my people” by now, but instead it’s just me, my thoughts, and this constant low-level ache that doesn’t really go away.
