I’ve been feeling this weird combination of invisible and exposed lately. Invisible because no one really checks in, exposed because I’m constantly online, scrolling and lurking, watching everyone else live.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats—but it all feels like background noise. We talk about work, memes, errands. Nobody asks anything that goes past the surface, and I’m not sure I’d know how to answer if they did. When I try to open up, I feel like I’m oversharing or making things awkward, so I shut down again.

Most of my days are a loop: wake up, go through the motions, come home, distract myself with screens until I’m tired enough to sleep. I keep thinking I should “put myself out there,” but I don’t even know who “myself” is anymore. I’ve gotten so used to being alone that the idea of actually connecting with someone feels both desperately needed and almost impossible.
