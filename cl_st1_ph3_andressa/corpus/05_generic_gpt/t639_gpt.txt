I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and exposed lately, and I don’t really know where else to put it.

I’m surrounded by people most days – coworkers, neighbors, the usual small talk – but it feels like I’m acting out a version of myself on autopilot. I laugh at the right times, ask the right questions, make the right jokes, and then go home and feel like I didn’t actually exist all day. It’s like everyone knows “surface me,” but no one knows the person who goes home and just sits in the quiet.

I’m not totally isolated. I have a few people I could text, but I always talk myself out of it. I convince myself I’m bothering them, or that they’re just being polite when they respond. I replay old conversations and pick apart every awkward pause, every time I talked too much or too little. Then I feel stupid for overthinking it, and that just makes me retreat more.

The worst part is this sense that I missed some critical window where everyone else learned how to form real, lasting connections, and I didn’t. Friend groups seem to just…exist already. People have “their person,” or a circle that has history and traditions, and I’m standing outside the glass, watching. I don’t know how to go from acquaintance to “someone who actually matters” in someone’s life.

Nights are the hardest. I scroll, I lurk on places like this, I half-type messages and delete them. I keep thinking that if people really knew how empty things feel inside, they’d either run or feel burdened. So I just keep it in, and then I end up here, typing to strangers because it feels safer than saying it out loud to anyone who knows my name.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for posting this. Maybe I just needed to admit that I’m lonely instead of pretending I’m just “busy” or “tired.”
