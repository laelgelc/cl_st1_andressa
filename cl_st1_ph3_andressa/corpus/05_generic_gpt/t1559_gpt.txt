I don’t know when “being alone a lot” turned into “being lonely all the time,” but it feels like it crept up on me. I have people around me technically—coworkers, family chats, old classmates on social media—but none of it feels like it actually touches me. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes, “how’s it going?” followed by automatic “fine.”

I miss feeling known. I miss having someone who remembers the tiny, stupid details about me and actually cares. When something happens, good or bad, I hesitate before sharing because I don’t know who I’d even send it to. I scroll through my contacts and realize most of those names are just remnants of past versions of me.

It’s weird how crowded the world is and how invisible you can still feel in it.
