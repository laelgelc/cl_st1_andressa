I’m not even sure what I’m looking for by writing this, but I’ve been lurking here for a while and I guess I just want to put this somewhere.

I’m in that weird place where, on paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers I talk to, a couple of old friends I text once in a while, some family I see on holidays. But I feel like a ghost moving through all of it. I show up, say the right things, laugh at the right moments, then go home and it’s like none of it touched me.

I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely known by someone. Conversations feel like I’m playing a role: the “competent colleague,” the “chill friend,” the “okay, everything’s fine” version of myself. Underneath that, I feel this constant ache, like I’m standing behind glass watching everyone else live for real.

I try to reach out sometimes, but I always pull back at the last second. I’ll type a long message and then delete it because it feels too heavy, too needy, too much. I’m scared of being perceived as a burden, or worse, of opening up and getting a lukewarm response that confirms I was right to keep quiet.

Nights are the worst. The silence in my room feels loud. I scroll through my phone, hoping someone will message first, even though I know they won’t. Then I tell myself I should be used to this by now, that I’m an adult and I shouldn’t need people this badly.

I don’t even know if what I’m feeling is “normal loneliness” or if there’s something fundamentally wrong with me that makes it so hard to connect. It’s like everyone else got a manual on how to be close to people, and I missed that day.

Anyway, that’s it. Just needed to say it somewhere.
