I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m drifting further and further away from everyone and everything, and nobody even notices.

My days are basically a loop: wake up, work, scroll, sleep. I talk to people, but it’s all surface-level. Coworkers, small talk about projects, “how was your weekend?” with pre-packaged answers. I can go an entire week without anyone asking me something that isn’t obligatory or polite. Sometimes I’ll deliberately leave my phone in another room just to see if anyone messages me without me initiating. They don’t.

I have “friends,” I guess, but they’re more like people I used to be close to who now just exist on my social media. We like each other’s posts and send the occasional meme. When I try to suggest meeting up, there’s always a reason it doesn’t work out, or it gets pushed back indefinitely. After a while, I stopped asking because I got tired of feeling like I was begging for scraps of attention.

There’s this weird thing where I can be in a room full of people and feel more alone than when I’m actually alone. Everyone laughing, talking over each other, and I’m there nodding along, waiting for a moment where I can say something that doesn’t just die in the air. When I do talk, it feels like people listen just long enough to respond with something about themselves, and then the conversation moves on without me.

I’ve started to notice I’m quieter now than I used to be. I censor myself before I even speak, because I’ve internalized this idea that what I say doesn’t really matter. It’s like I’m fading out of my own life. The worst part is, I’m not even sure anyone would notice the difference between me being “busy” and me just disappearing.

I don’t know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe just to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
