I don’t even know how to describe what this feels like anymore. It’s not dramatic loneliness, just this low-level hum that never shuts off. I can go through a whole day talking to people – coworkers, cashiers, neighbors – and still feel like I didn’t actually exist for anyone.

Most of my “connections” are surface-level. We joke, we talk about work or random stuff, but it never goes deeper. I don’t feel like I can show the parts of me that are actually scared, insecure, or just weird without worrying I’ll push people away. So I keep it light, then go home and wonder why nobody really knows me.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through my phone, debating whether to message someone, but I overthink every word and usually end up not sending anything. It’s embarrassing how much I replay old conversations, trying to figure out if I said something wrong or came off as annoying. Then I convince myself they’re just being polite and don’t actually want me around.

I’m also at that age where everyone seems to be pairing off, getting married, having kids. I’m happy for them, but it just highlights how stuck I feel. I miss having someone I can text about nothing, or someone who actually notices when I disappear for a few days.

I keep wondering if this is just how my life is going to be: surrounded by people, but somehow completely separate from them.
