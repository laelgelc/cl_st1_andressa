I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing in slow motion.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, some acquaintances, a family that isn’t terrible. I’m not completely isolated in the literal sense. But I go through entire days where I don’t feel genuinely seen or known by anyone. Conversations are mostly surface-level: work updates, memes, “how was your weekend?” with answers like “pretty good, you?” when the real answer is “I felt like a ghost in my own life.”

What bothers me most is how invisible this kind of loneliness is. I’m physically around people but emotionally completely detached. I can laugh, make small jokes, appear normal, and then go home and feel this heavy emptiness that’s hard to even put into words. I scroll through chats where my name barely appears unless I start the conversation, and even then, it dies quickly. It’s like I’m always slightly out of sync with everyone else.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” more, but it feels like I’m forcing myself into spaces where no one is actually looking for me. I overthink every interaction afterward, replaying what I said, wondering if I came off weird or needy. At some point, I just stop trying because rejection, even the quiet, subtle kind, is exhausting.

What scares me is how used to it I’m becoming. I’m getting better at distracting myself—YouTube, games, random browsing—until it’s late and I realize I haven’t had a real, honest conversation with anyone in weeks. I’m starting to forget what it feels like to be truly comfortable around another person, without performing or editing myself in real-time.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by posting this. Maybe just somewhere to say it out loud, since I don’t have anyone in my life I can say it to.
