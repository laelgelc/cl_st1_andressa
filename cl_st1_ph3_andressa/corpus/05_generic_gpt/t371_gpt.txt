I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like my life is happening behind glass.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I respond to messages, I show up to things when I’m invited. But it all feels like I’m playing a role someone else wrote. I can be funny, polite, “easy to talk to,” and yet when I go home, it’s like I vanish. No calls, no “how are you really,” just silence and the hum of my computer.

What messes with my head is that I’m not totally isolated. I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I can text about memes or work stuff. But I don’t have that person I can message at 2 a.m. when my brain won’t shut up. I don’t have someone who actually notices when I withdraw, or who knows my history beyond surface-level facts.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like everyone says—joined group chats, tried hobbies, forced myself to be more social. I can make a decent first impression, but it never seems to go deeper. People already have their established circles, their “best friends,” their partners. I always end up orbiting around other people’s lives, never really part of the center of anything.

Sometimes I wonder if there’s just something fundamentally off about me that other people pick up on subconsciously. Like I’m missing whatever it is that makes people think, “Yeah, I want that person in my life long-term.”

I’m tired of feeling like a background character in my own story, but I honestly don’t know what else to try.
