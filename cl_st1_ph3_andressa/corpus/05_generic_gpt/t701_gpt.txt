I don’t really know how to talk about this in my real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people but feel weirdly invisible. I have coworkers, acquaintances, a family chat that pings sometimes, but there’s no one I feel like I can message at 2am and say, “I’m not okay, can you just exist with me for a bit?” Even during the day, conversations feel like I’m playing a role—polite, mildly funny, “doing fine.” Then I go home and it’s just this heavy silence.

What bothers me most is how ordinary everything looks from the outside. I go to work, pay bills, occasionally meet people for a drink, post something harmless on social media. Nobody would guess that I can go entire weekends barely speaking out loud. I’ll go through a whole Sunday where the only words I say are “thank you” to the cashier.

I’ve tried things people recommend—clubs, hobbies, online communities, even apps for making friends. I can start connections, but they never seem to deepen. It’s like there’s a glass wall: I can see people’s lives, but I’m not really in them. I keep wondering if there’s something fundamentally off about me that others can sense but I can’t see.

What scares me is how used to it I’m becoming. I plan my days around avoiding the sting of it—long walks with headphones, scrolling until my eyes hurt, going to bed early just to make the day end. It’s like I’m slowly shrinking my life down to something small enough that the loneliness fits without spilling over.
