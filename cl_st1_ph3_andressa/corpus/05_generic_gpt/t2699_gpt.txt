I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like my life is happening behind glass.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text occasionally, family I see on holidays. But I don’t feel connected to any of them. Conversations feel like I’m reading from a script: “How was your weekend?” “Busy.” “Yeah, same.” Then silence, or some joke I laugh at a second too late.

I keep thinking there must have been some moment where everyone else got the instructions on how to be close to people and I just…missed it. I can mimic it for a while. I can be “on” at work or at social events. People say I seem friendly, even funny. But then I go home and it’s like someone pulled the plug. The silence is so loud. I scroll, watch something, lie in bed too long. I don’t even know what I’m waiting for.

What hurts most is that the loneliness is starting to feel like part of my personality instead of a situation. Like I’m “the person people like but don’t choose.” The one who gets invited if someone else cancels. The one people open up to but never really ask about in return. I’m always the supporting character in my own life.

I try to reach out sometimes. I’ll type a long message and then delete half of it because it feels “too much.” I don’t want to be the needy friend. I don’t want to make anyone uncomfortable with how empty I feel. So I send something casual, get a casual reply, and that’s the end of it. Then I feel stupid for expecting more.

It’s weird how you can be so starved for connection and still push people away at the same time. I say no to plans because I’m tired or anxious, then sit at home wishing someone would insist I come anyway. But nobody does, and I can’t really blame them. I guess people can’t see a need if you’re always trying to hide it.
