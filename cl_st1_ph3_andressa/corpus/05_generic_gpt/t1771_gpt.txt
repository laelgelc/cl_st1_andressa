I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m fading out of my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I answer emails, I show up to meetings, I say “good, you?” when people ask how I am. I even make people laugh sometimes. But the second I’m alone again, it’s like the whole world goes quiet and I realize there’s nobody who actually sees me beyond the surface.

I have acquaintances, not friends. People I can talk to about work, TV shows, random stuff—but not about the way my chest physically hurts when I realize no one would notice if I just stopped reaching out. I’m always the one sending the first message, suggesting plans, trying to keep threads of connection alive. When I stop, everything just…stops.

What gets to me the most is the feeling of being emotionally “unreachable.” Even when I’m around others, I feel like I’m behind glass. I watch people talk about their partners, their group chats, their “we” plans, and I can’t think of a single person who would list me as part of their “we.”

I’ve tried joining clubs, going to events, talking to strangers, but it all stays shallow. I don’t know if it’s something about me—my personality, my face, my energy—that makes people keep their distance, or if I just missed the window where people form real bonds.

Nights are the worst. That’s when the silence feels heavy and I start wondering how long someone can live like this without it breaking something permanently inside them.
