It’s weird how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re on mute.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I can talk to about projects, TV shows, random news. But I don’t feel like there’s anyone I can be fully honest with without worrying I’m being “too much.” So I keep most things on the surface, and then complain internally that nobody really knows me. It’s like I’m the one holding the door shut and then wondering why nobody comes in.

I scroll social media and see people casually hanging out, going on trips, having “game nights” and group chats. I don’t even know how people get to that stage. My connections feel like weak signals that never quite turn into anything stable. I’m always “nice,” “easy to talk to,” but never the person someone chooses first.

Nights are the worst. That’s when it really hits that if I disappeared, my absence would be an inconvenience, not a loss. People would notice eventually, but I don’t know if anyone would feel it in the way I wish someone would.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for writing this. Maybe just proof to myself that I still want to be seen, even if I don’t know how to let that happen.
