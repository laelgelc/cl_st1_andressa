I don’t really know how to explain this in a way that doesn’t sound dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… empty space with tasks in it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, I’m not in crisis. I talk to coworkers, I answer emails, I make small jokes in meetings. People probably think I’m normal, maybe even “doing well.” But as soon as I close my laptop, it’s like the world just folds up and I disappear into this quiet, airless bubble.

I don’t really have anyone who checks in on me. No one messages just to say hi. Group chats I’m in are muted, and honestly I could stop responding and I’m not sure anyone would notice beyond a “lol where’d you go” once every few months. I scroll through my phone sometimes just to prove to myself that I’m not imagining it—no unread messages, no missed calls, just spam.

The worst part is how used to it I’ve become. I’ll go entire weekends without speaking out loud, except to say “thank you” to a cashier. I’ll watch shows or YouTube videos just to hear other people’s voices in the background. I eat dinner alone, at my desk, and half the time I don’t even taste it because I’m distracted by this heavy, dull feeling that this is just… it.

I think what hurts most is that I can remember times in my life when I did have people. Friends I saw regularly, conversations that lasted hours, inside jokes that made me feel like I belonged somewhere. Now it feels like I’m standing outside a house I used to live in, looking through the window at a life that moved on without me.

I keep telling myself I should “put myself out there,” join something, message old friends, whatever. But there’s this mix of exhaustion and fear—like I’ve forgotten how to connect, and if I try I’ll just confirm that there really isn’t a place for me in anyone’s life anymore.

So mostly I just go through the motions, do what I’m supposed to do, and then come back to my quiet apartment and sit with this feeling that I’m here, but not really part of anything.
