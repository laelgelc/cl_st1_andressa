I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… background noise.

I go through the motions: work, commute, cook something half-decent, scroll on my phone, sleep. Repeat. I talk to people every day—at work, in stores, in group chats—but it all feels like I’m acting out a script instead of actually connecting. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else live.

What gets to me most is that I don’t think anyone really knows me. They know the “functioning” version: polite, helpful, maybe even “funny” sometimes. But the stuff that actually sits on my chest at 2 a.m.? No one sees that. And I don’t know how to let them. I’m scared if I open up, I’ll either be dismissed (“everyone feels that way”) or it’ll just make things awkward and people will slowly drift away.

I have “friends,” but they’re all surface-level. We send memes, talk about shows, complain about work. When I try to steer things a bit deeper, it gets deflected or joked away. Everyone’s busy, everyone’s tired, everyone has their own lives. I get that. I just wish I felt like part of someone’s “own life” instead of an optional extra.

Nights are the worst. I’ll put on a podcast or a show just to have voices in the background. I’ll scroll and see people hanging out, traveling, having inside jokes in the comments, and I feel this weird mix of jealousy and numbness. I want that, but I also feel like I’ve forgotten how to be close to anyone. Like if someone actually did want to know me, I wouldn’t even know what to say.

Sometimes I catch myself thinking, “If I disappeared, how long until anyone really noticed?” Not in a dramatic, movie way. Just in a practical sense. A few people would eventually text, “Hey, you alive?” and that’s probably it.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere that isn’t my own head for once.
