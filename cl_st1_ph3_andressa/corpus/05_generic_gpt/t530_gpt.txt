I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I answer emails, I nod in meetings. I even have a few people I’d technically call friends. But there’s this constant, low-level ache that I can’t shake, like I’m living behind glass watching everyone else actually participate in the world.

The weirdest part is how invisible this feels. I can go an entire weekend without anyone messaging me first. If I didn’t show up to work, people would notice, but if I quietly stopped existing outside of that, I’m not sure anyone would. I scroll through my contacts sometimes, staring at names, trying to figure out who I could text without it feeling forced or awkward. Most of the time I just lock my phone again.

I’m not completely socially inept. I can make small talk, I can be “pleasant,” but everything feels shallow. I don’t know how people get from “we talk sometimes” to “we actually matter to each other.” I see people online talking about their “group” or “best friend” or “people who get them,” and it feels like some secret game I never learned the rules to.

I’ve tried joining things—servers, group chats, hobby groups—but I always end up drifting to the edges. I either talk too little because I’m scared of being annoying, or I overshare just enough to feel stupid afterward. Then I slowly back away until I’m just another silent member no one remembers.

Nights are the worst. That’s when it hits me that no one really knows what’s going on in my head or my life. If something good happens, there’s no one I feel comfortable excitedly messaging. If something bad happens, I just swallow it and move on. It’s like everything just gets absorbed by this blank space.

I don’t even know exactly what I’m looking for writing this. Maybe just proof that I’m still here, that I said something out loud instead of just thinking it and going to bed.
