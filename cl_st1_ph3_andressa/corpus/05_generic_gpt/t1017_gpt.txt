I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it’s felt like I’m living beside my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I exchange small talk with coworkers about weekend plans I don’t actually have. My family is alive and we text sometimes. I’m not completely isolated; there are people around me. But I feel like I’m behind glass all the time, watching everyone else actually connect while I just… hover on the edges.

I keep thinking about how long it’s been since I’ve had a conversation that felt real, where I didn’t feel like I was performing some version of myself that’s easier to digest. I’ve had friends before, but we’ve drifted, or I’ve pulled away, or life just moved on without me. I don’t even know if it was my fault, but it’s hard not to assume it is.

The worst part is the way the loneliness makes me second-guess everything. If someone takes a little too long to reply, I assume I’m annoying them. I replay old interactions and convince myself I said something weird, that this is why people don’t stick around. So I stay quiet to avoid messing things up, and then I end up even more alone. It’s like I’m trapped between wanting to be seen and being terrified that, if I am, people will confirm all the things I already suspect about myself.

Nights are the hardest. I scroll through social media and see people doing normal things—group photos, inside jokes, casual hangouts—and it hits me how far I am from that. I’m not even sure what I’d talk about if someone invited me out. I feel out of practice at being a person.

I don’t know what I’m looking for by posting this. Maybe just somewhere to say “I’m lonely” without having to dress it up as a joke or pretend it’s not that bad. It is that bad. It’s this constant dull ache that never really goes away, just shifts into the background until something small brings it crashing back into focus again.
