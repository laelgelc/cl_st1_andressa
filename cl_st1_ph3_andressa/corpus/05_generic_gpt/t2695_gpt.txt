I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s changing who I am.

I spend most days in my room, scrolling, lurking, half-listening to podcasts just so there’s some kind of voice in the background. I have acquaintances, people I can joke with, but no one I’d feel comfortable calling at 2 a.m., or even 8 p.m., honestly. When something good or bad happens, I open my messages and then close them again because I don’t know who to send it to.

It’s weird how you can be surrounded by people at work or school and still feel like you’re behind glass. I watch other people make plans, have group chats, share inside jokes. I end up wondering what’s wrong with me that I never quite “stick” with anyone.

It’s like I’m permanently on the outside of my own life, watching it go by.
