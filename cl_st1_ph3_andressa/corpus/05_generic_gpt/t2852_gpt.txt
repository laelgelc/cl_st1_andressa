It’s weird how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re on mute.

On paper, my life doesn’t look that bad. I have a job, a place to live, coworkers who are polite, a family that occasionally checks in. But everything feels like I’m watching it through glass. I participate in conversations, I answer emails, I nod and smile at the right times, but I don’t feel connected to anyone. It’s like I’m playing a character that people tolerate but never really know.

I think what hits hardest is the absence of “my person.” Not necessarily a romantic partner, just someone who thinks of me first sometimes. Someone who texts me memes because they remind them of me, or calls just to rant and then asks, “How are you, really?” I don’t have that. I’m always the extra, the backup plan, the “if no one else can make it, you can come too.”

I try to reach out. I message people, suggest meeting up, try to keep conversations going. Most of the time I get slow replies, or one-word responses, or “we should totally hang out sometime” that never turns into an actual plan. After a while it starts to feel pathetic to keep trying. So I stop. Then the silence makes me feel worse, and I start again. It’s a loop.

The loneliness has crept into everything. I don’t sleep well. I overthink every interaction. I replay conversations and convince myself I said something weird, or that I’m just fundamentally uninteresting. I look at other people’s friendships and relationships and it feels like there’s some basic social skill or quality I missed out on. Like everyone else got a manual I never received.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put it into words somewhere that isn’t my own head. It’s exhausting to keep pretending I’m fine when most days I feel like I’m fading into the background of my own life.
