I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t make sense on paper. I have people around me. I go to work, I talk to coworkers, I send memes in group chats. But somehow it all feels like I’m acting out a version of myself instead of being a real person anyone actually knows.

Conversations feel shallow, like I’m skimming the surface of everything. I listen to people talk about their lives, but when it’s my turn I freeze up or keep it light. I don’t even know if it’s because I don’t trust them or because I don’t trust that my real thoughts are worth hearing. Then I go home and replay every small interaction, convinced I came across as awkward or boring.

I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely understood. Not “oh, that sucks” understood, but the kind where someone really gets what you’re trying to say even when you’re not saying it well. I miss that feeling, even though I’m not sure I’ve ever actually had it.

Most nights I just scroll until I’m too tired to think. I see people posting pictures with friends, partners, families, and it feels like I’m watching life through a window. I’m there, but not really there. And the longer it goes on, the harder it is to imagine it being any different.
