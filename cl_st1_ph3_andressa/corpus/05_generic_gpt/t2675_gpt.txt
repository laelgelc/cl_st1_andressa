I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore.

I’m not completely isolated in the technical sense. I have coworkers, a family chat that pings occasionally, a couple of old friends I exchange memes with. But it all feels like background noise. Nothing really reaches that place in me that’s starving for actual closeness.

I’ll have a normal day at work, talk about projects, joke a bit, then come home and it’s like I hit a wall of silence. I sit there scrolling, half-hoping someone will message me, half-dreading having to respond if they do because I’m out of practice being real with anyone. It’s like I forgot the language of intimacy and only remember small talk.

What makes it worse is the feeling that I’m partly to blame. I’ve turned down invitations because I was tired or anxious. I’ve let conversations die because I didn’t know how to keep them going without sounding needy. I’ve watched friendships slowly fade and just… let them. And now I’m here, wondering when exactly I drifted this far away from everyone.

I keep thinking, “If people really knew me, they wouldn’t want to stick around anyway,” and that thought is like a filter over everything. So I keep the “acceptable” version of myself on display and then feel hurt that no one sees the parts I never show them.

There’s this weird mix of wanting someone to check in on me and also not wanting to burden anyone with how empty I feel most of the time. So I just keep going through the motions, answering “I’m fine” when I’m really not, and then lying awake at night trying to remember the last time I felt genuinely understood.
