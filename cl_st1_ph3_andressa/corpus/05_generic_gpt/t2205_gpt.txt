I’m starting to wonder if I’ve just… aged out of belonging.

I’m in this weird place where I’m not young enough to be in the chaos of early 20s anymore, but I’m not “settled” like the people posting baby photos and mortgage updates either. Everyone seems to have migrated into their own little family units or tight friend circles, and I’m just orbiting around them, watching.

I have people I can text, but no one I feel like I can call without rehearsing what I’m going to say first. I overthink every message: “Am I being too needy? Too negative? Too much?” So I pull back, wait for others to reach out, and then feel invisible when they don’t.

Weekends are the worst. I do all the “right” things—go outside, work out, try hobbies—but it still feels like I’m just killing time between workdays. I can go an entire weekend without hearing my name spoken out loud by another person.

What scares me most is how normal this is starting to feel. Like I’m slowly adjusting to the idea that maybe this is just how my life is going to be: functional on the surface, but quietly, constantly, alone.
