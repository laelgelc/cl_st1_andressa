Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine” – job, place to live, people I interact with – but everything feels thin, like I’m behind glass watching other people actually connect. I have acquaintances, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something really bad (or really good) happened. I scroll through my contacts and realize there isn’t a single person I could text without overthinking it.

What’s messing with me is how invisible this feels. I laugh at the right times, make small talk, show up where I’m supposed to be, and everyone assumes I’m okay. I don’t know how to bridge that gap between “seems fine” and “actually pretty lonely most of the time” without sounding dramatic or needy.

I miss feeling known. Not just “how’s work?” but the kind of familiarity where someone remembers the small, stupid details about you. I don’t even know when I stopped trying to build that. It just kind of faded, and now I’m not sure how to start again.
