I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

I feel like I’m living beside my own life instead of in it. I go through the motions: work, errands, scrolling, sleep. I talk to people every day, but it’s all surface-level stuff. I can be joking with coworkers and still feel like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else. They see the outline of me, but not much inside.

What gets to me is how long it’s been like this. It’s not one bad week; it’s years of feeling like I never quite “land” with anyone. I’ve had friends, sort of. People to hang out with sometimes. But I’m always the one who cares more, who initiates more, who notices when the contact fades. I tell myself to stop trying so hard, but if I don’t try, everything just quietly disappears.

I’ve also started to feel weirdly out of practice with being close to people. When someone does show interest, I get awkward and stiff, like I’m trying to remember lines from a script I never learned properly. I overthink every message, every pause, every time someone takes a bit too long to reply. It’s exhausting.

Nights are the worst. That’s when it hits me that nobody really knows me in the way I want to be known. All the stuff in my head—fears, memories, stupid jokes, random thoughts—just stays there. I keep imagining what it would be like to be someone people naturally think of, instead of the person you remember only when you need something or when you happen to cross paths.
