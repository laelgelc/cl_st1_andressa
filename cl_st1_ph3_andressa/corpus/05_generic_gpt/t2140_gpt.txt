I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s hitting harder as I get older. I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I can chat with about surface stuff, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something went wrong. I watch other people slip so easily into friendships and relationships, and I feel like I’m missing some basic instruction manual everyone else got.

I overthink every interaction. If someone doesn’t reply quickly, I assume I said something weird. If I try to open up, I feel like I’m oversharing. So I end up staying quiet, then feeling invisible, then resenting myself for not trying harder.

Most days I tell myself I’m fine with being alone—headphones on, doing my own thing—but there’s this constant background ache. Like I’m standing outside a house with all the lights on, hearing people laughing inside, and I can’t figure out how to get through the door.
