I’ve been feeling this really specific kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t quite fit into the usual “no friends / no partner” box. I technically have people in my life—family, a couple of acquaintances, coworkers—but I still feel like I’m behind glass all the time, watching everyone else actually live.

Conversations feel like I’m playing a role instead of being myself. I crack the expected jokes, ask the polite questions, nod at the right times, and then go home and feel completely drained, like I’ve done a performance instead of having a connection. When I try to be more honest or vulnerable, it either gets brushed off or I get that uncomfortable silence that makes me regret saying anything real.

I scroll through my messages and realize most of my chats are one-sided: me reaching out, me initiating plans, me checking in. When I stop initiating, everything just…stops. It’s like finding out you’re only in people’s lives because you keep knocking on the door.

Nights are the worst. I’ll lie in bed with my phone next to me, fully charged, no notifications, feeling this heavy, physical ache in my chest. I keep thinking how strange it is that I could disappear from social media or group chats and most people would just assume I’m “busy” and move on.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
