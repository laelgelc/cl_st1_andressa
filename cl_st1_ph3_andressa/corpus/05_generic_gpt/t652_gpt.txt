I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness that doesn’t really match the surface of my life.

On paper, I’m “not alone.” I have coworkers I talk to, family I text with, a couple of old friends I exchange memes with. But none of it feels like it actually touches me. It’s like I’m always slightly behind a glass wall, watching myself interact instead of actually being there.

I miss having someone who really knows me. Not just my favorite shows or what I do for work, but the stuff underneath—what I’m afraid of, what I secretly want, the way my brain spirals at 2 a.m. I don’t have anyone I can message without overthinking whether I’m being annoying or “too much.”

Most days I function fine. I go to work, I smile, I make small talk. Then I come home, put my phone down, and realize no one is going to reach out unless I do first. And when I do, the conversations feel shallow and fizzle out quickly. It makes me feel like I’m easy to forget.

There’s also this shame attached to it. I’m an adult, I “should” know how to make and keep friends, right? But I genuinely don’t know how to move from acquaintance to real connection anymore. I feel like I missed some crucial life class everyone else attended.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe just to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
