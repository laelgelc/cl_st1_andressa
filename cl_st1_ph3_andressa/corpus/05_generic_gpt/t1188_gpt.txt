I’ve been feeling like I’m fading out of my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, a few casual friends I see now and then. But I spend most days feeling like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else actually participate in the world while I just…exist near it.

I don’t have anyone I can text without overthinking it first. Every time I consider reaching out, I run through this whole mental script: “Am I bothering them? Do they actually like me? Is this weird?” By the time I’m done, I’ve usually talked myself out of sending anything at all. Then I sit there, phone in hand, feeling stupid and even more alone.

I’ve noticed that when people do invite me somewhere, I get anxious and awkward, and afterwards I replay everything I said, convinced I came off as boring or strange. Part of me desperately wants more connection; another part seems determined to sabotage it. It’s like I’m stuck between craving people and being terrified of them at the same time.

The worst part is the silence at night. When the day is over and I’m back in my room, it hits me that no one is really “in” my life. If I disappeared from social media or stopped showing up, I’m honestly not sure who would notice beyond a passing, “Huh, haven’t seen them around.”

I keep asking myself if this is just how adulthood is now, or if there’s something fundamentally wrong with me that makes it so hard to form close bonds. I see people talking about their group chats, their best friends, their partners, and I can’t even imagine what it feels like to be someone’s first choice instead of an afterthought or a backup plan.
