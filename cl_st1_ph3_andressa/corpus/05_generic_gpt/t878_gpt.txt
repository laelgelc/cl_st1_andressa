I’ve been feeling like I’m slowly fading out of everyone’s peripheral vision.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family I text sometimes—but I don’t feel *known* by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else actually participate in life.

What messes with my head is that I used to have close friends. Late-night talks, stupid inside jokes, the kind of comfort where silence wasn’t awkward. Somewhere along the way, schedules changed, priorities shifted, and I just…fell off the map. Now I don’t even know how to reach out without feeling needy or weird.

I miss being someone’s first choice to text. I miss being expected, not just tolerated. Lately I catch myself dragging out errands just to be around other humans, even if no one talks to me.

It’s starting to feel less like a phase and more like the new normal.
