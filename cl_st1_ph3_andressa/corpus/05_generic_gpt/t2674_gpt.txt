I’ve been feeling this weird, quiet kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into the usual “I have no friends” narrative. I technically have people in my life. I reply in group chats, I show up to work, I make small talk, I say “we should hang out sometime” like everyone else. But I feel like I’m just loosely attached to everything, like a sticker that’s been peeled off and stuck back on too many times.

Most days feel like I’m playing a character I’ve been improvising for years and I’ve forgotten what the original script was. I laugh when I’m supposed to, ask questions when the silence gets awkward, and then go home and replay everything I said, convinced it came off weird or annoying. There’s this constant sense that I’m not quite “in” with anyone, just orbiting around other people’s real connections.

What makes it worse is how hard it is to admit this to anyone. Saying “I feel lonely” out loud feels like confessing to being fundamentally unlikable. I always imagine the other person thinking, “But you seem fine” or “You’re overthinking it.” So I keep it to myself, and the gap between how I appear and how I actually feel just keeps getting wider.

I miss having conversations that don’t feel like I’m auditioning. I miss feeling known without having to carefully explain myself. I don’t even know exactly what I’m looking for—friends, a relationship, just one person who really sees me—but I know that whatever this half-connected, half-detached existence is, it’s starting to feel less like a phase and more like the default.
