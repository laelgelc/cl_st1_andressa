I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

Lately it’s been hitting me how “extra” I feel in my own life. Like I’m the side character in everyone else’s story. People seem to like me well enough on the surface – I’m “nice,” “easy to talk to,” all those vague compliments – but when plans are made, I’m almost never the one people think of first. I’m either invited as an afterthought or I find out about things after they’ve already happened.

What makes it worse is that from the outside I probably look fine. I have a job, coworkers who chat with me, some acquaintances. I can be funny in short bursts, I can hold a conversation. But then I go home and it’s just… silence. No one to text about my day, no one who actually notices if I go quiet for a few days. When my phone buzzes, it’s usually a notification from an app, not a person.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there.” Joined a few group activities, tried talking more, even forced myself to host something once. It went okay, people came, we laughed, and then afterwards everything went back to normal. No deeper connection, no sense that I’d moved from “person in the room” to “person in someone’s life.”

What messes with my head is how replaceable I feel. Like if I disappeared, people would say “oh, that’s sad” for a minute and then just slot someone else into the space I used to fill. There’s no one I’d call “my person,” and I’m very aware that I’m nobody’s first call either.

It’s a strange kind of loneliness where you’re not completely isolated, but you might as well be. I can go an entire weekend without speaking out loud to another human being. Sometimes I catch myself rehearsing what I’d say if someone actually asked, “How are you really?” and then remembering that no one is going to ask.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to admit out loud that I feel like a ghost in my own life.
