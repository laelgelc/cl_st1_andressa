I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my life on paper. I have coworkers I chat with, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family group chats that light up on holidays. But I still feel like I’m watching my own life from the outside, like I’m the extra in everyone else’s movie.

I don’t think anyone really knows me. They know the version who cracks jokes at work, the “I’m fine, just tired” version, the one who listens to their problems. But the stuff that actually keeps me up at night never really makes it into conversations. I start to share, then feel stupid or dramatic and change the subject.

Weekends are the worst. I scroll through social media and see people doing normal things together—dinners, game nights, trips—and it makes me feel like I somehow missed a step everyone else understood. I try to reach out sometimes, but I overthink every message, every silence, every “sorry, been busy.”

What scares me most is how used to it I’m getting. I go days without a real conversation and it almost feels normal now, like this is just how life is going to be.
