I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m living in parallel to everyone else, not really with them. I go to work, I talk to people, I can be funny and “on” when I need to, but the second I’m alone it’s like the air changes. Everything feels hollow and strangely loud, like the silence is shouting at me.

What messes with my head is that, on paper, I shouldn’t feel this alone. I have coworkers, acquaintances, a group chat that occasionally pings. But it all feels… surface-level. I don’t have that person I can message at 2 a.m. just to say, “I feel awful, can you just be here with me, even virtually?” I don’t have anyone who actually notices when I go quiet for a while.

I’ve tried to “fix” it in all the usual ways—joining clubs, saying yes to invitations, trying apps, being the one who reaches out first. It works for a bit, but then I hit this invisible wall. People stay at arm’s length. I start overthinking every interaction, replaying conversations and cringing at things I said or didn’t say, and then I pull back before they can pull away.

There’s also this weird shame attached to it. I feel embarrassed admitting I’m lonely, like it’s some personal failure, proof that I’m not “worth” sticking around for. When people say “you just have to put yourself out there,” I want to scream, because I have, and it’s exhausting to keep auditioning for a role I never get.

Most days I just go through the motions, come home, put something on in the background so I don’t have to sit in silence, and scroll until my eyes hurt. Then I go to bed, wake up, and repeat. I keep wondering if this is just how it’s going to be for me.
