I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s rewiring my brain.

I have people around me technically—coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats—but I don’t feel *known* by anyone. Conversations feel like I’m playing a character: the “fine, busy, tired but hanging in there” version of me. I can’t remember the last time I said what I was really thinking without editing it to seem normal or acceptable.

Nights are the worst. I just sit with my phone, scrolling through other people’s lives, feeling like I’m watching humanity through glass. I want to reach out, ask someone to talk, but I get stuck on, “Why would they want to?” and “What would I even say?” So I don’t.

I keep wondering what’s wrong with me that I can’t seem to form the kind of connection that feels mutual and safe. It’s like everyone else got a manual on how to be a person with other people, and I missed that day.
