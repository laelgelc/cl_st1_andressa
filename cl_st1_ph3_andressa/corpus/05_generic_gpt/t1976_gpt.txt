I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, coworkers I can chat with, a family that isn’t abusive or anything, a couple of online acquaintances. But it all feels like background noise. No one really knows me. I don’t even think I know me anymore.

The worst part is how invisible I feel in social situations. I’ll be in a group conversation and it’s like I’m there physically but not actually part of it. I make a comment, people kind of nod or talk over it, and then the topic moves on. I scroll through my messages and realize most of my conversations are me reaching out first, me asking how someone is, me trying to keep something alive that clearly died a while ago.

I’ve started to dread weekends. Everyone else seems to have plans, people to see, little rituals with friends or partners. I just sort of…exist in my room. I try to fill the time with shows, games, endless scrolling, but there’s this constant heaviness that follows me around. It’s like I’m waiting for something to happen without really believing it ever will.

I don’t know if I’m doing something wrong socially, if I’m just fundamentally unlikeable, or if this is just how adulthood is for some people. I’m tired of feeling like an extra in my own story, but I don’t really see a path out of it.
