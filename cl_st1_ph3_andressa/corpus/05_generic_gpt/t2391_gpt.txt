I’m starting to realize that my loneliness isn’t about being literally alone; it’s about feeling like I’m permanently on the outside of everyone else’s life.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, even a couple of old friends I text sometimes—but I still feel weirdly invisible. Conversations feel like I’m playing a role: I say the right things, I laugh at the right moments, but it never feels like anyone actually sees me. When I get home, it’s like the whole day didn’t really involve me, just some version of me I put on for others.

I scroll through chats that are mostly dead, reread old messages, hover over the “hey, how are you?” text and then delete it because I don’t want to seem needy. I keep thinking there must have been a point where I missed some social checkpoint everyone else passed. Now it feels like I’m just orbiting other people’s lives without ever really landing anywhere.
