I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to my own life instead of inside it.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, acquaintances, a couple of people I could technically text if I really needed something. I show up to work, make small talk about TV shows, complain about the weather, laugh at the right moments. People would probably describe me as “nice” or “quiet but friendly.” But once I get home and close the door, it’s like the world just forgets I exist, and honestly, I kind of forget too.

What messes with my head is that I don’t even know how to bridge the gap between “friendly” and “actually close.” I watch other people slip into friendships so easily—inside jokes, group chats buzzing all day, weekend plans that just seem to form out of nowhere. Meanwhile, I’m sitting there overthinking whether sending a meme to someone is “too much” or if I’ll come across as annoying.

I’m tired of being the person who only gets invited as an afterthought, or not at all. I’m tired of realizing that if I stopped initiating with the few people I do talk to, the conversations would just die. It makes me feel like a placeholder in other people’s lives, someone they don’t dislike but don’t really think about either.

It’s not like I haven’t tried. I’ve joined clubs, gone to meetups, forced myself into situations that are uncomfortable for me as an introvert. I show up, I smile, I try to be interested in others. But the connections never seem to stick. People are polite, then they drift back into their established circles, and I’m left orbiting on the outside again.

The worst part is the way loneliness seeps into everything. It’s not just about not having plans on a Friday night. It’s eating dinner at my table and realizing I haven’t heard my own voice out loud all day. It’s having something funny or upsetting happen and instinctively reaching for my phone, then remembering there’s no one I feel close enough to share it with. It’s scrolling through my contacts and realizing there isn’t a single person I’d feel comfortable calling just to talk.

I don’t even know what I’m expecting by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put it into words somewhere, because keeping it all in my head makes it feel even heavier.
