I’ve been feeling like I’m slowly fading out of everyone’s peripheral vision.

I have people in my life on paper—coworkers, acquaintances, a few old friends I text every now and then—but I don’t feel *known* by anyone. Most conversations are surface-level: work, weather, memes. The second I try to go a bit deeper, I feel the energy shift, like I’ve broken some unspoken rule.

Nights are the worst. I scroll, I lurk, I watch other people’s lives unfold in photos and comments, and it just reinforces this sense that I’m an extra in everyone else’s story. I’m present, but not really included.

What messes with my head is that I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m behind glass. I don’t know if the problem is me being too closed off, too awkward, or just… not someone people think about when they make plans.

It’s a weird mix of being tired of my own company and not knowing how to be anything but alone.
