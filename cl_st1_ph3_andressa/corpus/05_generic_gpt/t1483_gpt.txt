I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

I’m surrounded by people in the most technical sense. Coworkers, neighbors, people on the train, family chats that light up my phone. But there’s this constant sense that I’m not actually *in* any of it. Like I’m just watching everyone else live, and I’m stuck on the outside, knocking quietly so I don’t disturb anyone.

I have “friends,” but most of them are surface-level. We send memes, we might see each other every few months, but I don’t feel like anyone really knows me. When I try to open up, I either feel like I’m oversharing or like I’m boring them. I end up changing the subject or making a joke so they don’t see how much I actually want someone to stay and listen.

What makes it worse is that I’m starting to forget how to connect. Conversations feel like small talk scripts. I replay what I said afterward and cringe at every sentence. I see other people slide into easy, comfortable banter and I can’t figure out what I’m doing wrong.

Nights are the hardest. I’ll scroll through social media, watching people post about parties, relationships, inside jokes, even mundane stuff like “Sunday brunch with the gang,” and it feels like I’m reading a language I used to know but can’t speak anymore.

I’m not even sure what I’m asking for here. I just needed to put this somewhere that isn’t my head.
