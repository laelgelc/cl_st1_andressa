I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into any of the usual categories people talk about. I’m not completely isolated. I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family I see on holidays. On paper it looks like I’m “connected,” but most of the time I feel like I’m standing behind glass watching everyone else actually live.

Conversations feel scripted. I know the right responses, the right jokes, the right “haha yeah, same,” but it’s all surface-level. When I try to go deeper, either I back out at the last second or the other person does. It’s like we’re all scared to admit how lonely we are, so we just keep floating on small talk.

What makes it worse is that I don’t even know how to explain this to people in my life. If I say “I’m lonely,” they’ll point to the people around me, or say “you just need to get out more.” But it’s not about being physically alone. It’s more like no one really knows me, and I don’t know how to let them.

I’ve noticed I edit myself constantly. I downplay my interests, hide the parts of me that feel “too much” or “too weird,” and then I’m surprised when no one feels close to the version of me I’m presenting. But I’ve been doing it for so long that I’m not even sure what the unedited version looks like anymore.

Some nights it hits really hard. I’ll be scrolling through social media, seeing people hanging out, traveling, getting married, whatever, and I feel like I missed a step somewhere. Like everyone else got the instructions for how to build a life with real connections and I just… didn’t.
