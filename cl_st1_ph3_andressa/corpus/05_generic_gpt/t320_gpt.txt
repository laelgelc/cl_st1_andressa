I’ve been feeling this weird combination of invisible and exposed lately, and I don’t really know what to do with it, so I’m dumping it here.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers I talk to, a couple of old friends I text with sometimes, family group chats that light up with memes. But none of it feels like it touches whatever part of me is actually starving. I go through the day saying the right things, making small jokes, nodding at the right times, but it all feels like I’m acting out a version of myself that people are comfortable with, not the one that’s actually in my head.

The moments that hit the hardest are the small ones. Coming home and realizing no one noticed I was gone. Having something mildly good or bad happen and realizing there’s no one I feel comfortable messaging right away. Walking past people laughing together and feeling like I’m behind glass, watching a world I can’t quite enter.

What makes it worse is this sense that I missed the window where people build those deep, default relationships. The “I can call you at 2 a.m.” type of connection. Everyone seems to already have their established circles, their inside jokes, their shared histories. I feel like a late arrival to a party where the groups are already formed and I’m just hovering near the snack table, pretending I’m fine.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like people always say. Joined a couple of things, went to some meetups. I can talk to people, but the conversations never seem to leave the surface. I walk away with a phone full of quiet contacts and no one I’d actually feel okay being vulnerable with. It’s like I’m collecting puzzle pieces that don’t fit together.

The worst part is how it’s changing me. I can feel myself becoming quieter, more hesitant to reach out because I’m tired of feeling like an afterthought. I second-guess every message: am I being annoying, too intense, too needy? So I say nothing, then feel even more isolated, and the cycle continues.

I don’t really have a point or a plan. I just needed to put into words that I exist in this weird, empty in-between where I’m surrounded by people and still feel completely disconnected.
