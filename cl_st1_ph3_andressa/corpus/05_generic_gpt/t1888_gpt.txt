I’ve been feeling this weird combination of invisible and “too much” at the same time, and I don’t really know where else to put it, so here it goes.

On paper, my life looks fine. I have a job, a place to live, people I can technically contact if I need something. But I don’t feel actually *known* by anyone. Most of my interactions are surface-level: work small talk, occasional memes in group chats, the “we should catch up sometime” messages that never turn into anything real. I keep thinking I’m part of people’s lives, then I realize I’m more like a background character.

I’ve noticed that when I’m around people, I feel lonelier than when I’m alone. It’s like there’s this glass wall between me and everyone else. I can see them laughing and connecting, but I can’t seem to join in properly. I either talk too much because I’m nervous and trying to fill the silence, or I shut down completely and just listen, then go home and overanalyze every word I said.

I also feel like I missed some critical social development stage. Other people seem to just know how to maintain friendships, how often to text, how to ask to hang out without sounding desperate or annoying. For me, every message feels like I’m imposing. I wait for people to reach out first, they don’t, and then I use that as proof that I don’t matter that much to them.

Nights are the worst. I lie in bed scrolling, seeing people posting photos with their friends, partners, families. I know social media is curated, but it still stings. I keep thinking, “No one is thinking about me right now. If I disappeared, it would take a while before anyone noticed.”

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
