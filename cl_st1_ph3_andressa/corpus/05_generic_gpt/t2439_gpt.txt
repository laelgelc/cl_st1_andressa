I’ve been feeling like I’m drifting further away from everyone, even when I’m technically “around” people. I go to work, I make small talk, I answer “I’m fine” when people ask how I’m doing, and then I go home and it’s just…silence. Not peaceful silence, more like an echo chamber where every thought sounds louder than it should.

What messes with me is that on paper I’m not completely alone. I have coworkers, a few old friends I text occasionally, family that I see on holidays. But I never feel like I actually belong anywhere. Conversations stay at surface level, and I can’t tell if that’s because I don’t know how to open up or because no one really wants to go deeper with me.

I scroll through social media and see people hanging out, going on trips, having group chats, and I feel like I missed some crucial step in life where you learn how to connect and be included. I try to initiate sometimes—message someone, suggest plans—but when it fizzles out or gets postponed indefinitely, I take it way too personally and just retreat again.

I also feel weirdly invisible. I can be in a room and still feel like a background character. People seem to have their “person,” their close circle, their go-to people. I don’t know who my person is. I don’t even know if anyone thinks of me as theirs.

Part of me wants to reach out more, join things, talk more honestly. Another part is just tired of feeling like I’m forcing myself into spaces where I’m not really wanted. So I end up doing nothing, stuck in this loop of wanting connection and simultaneously expecting rejection.
