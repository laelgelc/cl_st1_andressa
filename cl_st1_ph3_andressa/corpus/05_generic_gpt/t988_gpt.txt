I don’t even know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing in plain sight.

I have people around me — coworkers, acquaintances, family I text occasionally — but it all feels paper-thin. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, random memes. Nobody really asks how I am in a way that suggests they want the real answer, and I’m too tired or scared to volunteer it.

I watch other people’s lives from the outside: group photos, inside jokes, people casually mentioning “my best friend” like that’s just a normal thing everyone has. I can’t remember the last time I felt like someone’s first choice. I’m always the backup plan, the “if you’re free” invite, the person you can cancel on without guilt.

What’s messing with me is that I’m not completely isolated. I “seem fine.” I show up, I smile, I function. But then I go home, shut the door, and it’s just silence and this heavy feeling in my chest, like I’m stuck behind glass watching life happen to other people.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I just needed to say out loud that I’m lonely in a way that doesn’t show up on the outside, and it’s starting to feel less like a phase and more like the default setting of my life.
