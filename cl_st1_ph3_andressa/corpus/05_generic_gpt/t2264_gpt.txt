I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing in slow motion.

My days are technically “full” — work, commuting, errands, scrolling, sleep — but nobody really knows me. I have people I talk to, but not people I can actually say things to. Every conversation feels like a performance: small talk, jokes, “I’m good, you?” and then back to silence. I keep thinking, “If I vanished tomorrow, who would actually notice for real, not just in a ‘huh, that’s weird’ way?”

I watch coworkers talk about their weekend plans, group chats lighting up on their phones. I go home to my quiet apartment where the loudest thing is the fridge. I eat dinner alone, watch something, check my phone out of habit, knowing there’s nothing there. I still check anyway.

I’ve tried to fix it in the ways you’re “supposed” to. Joined clubs, went to meetups, tried talking more, tried talking less, tried being “myself,” tried being what I thought people wanted. I can get along with people on a surface level, but it never seems to move past that. It’s like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else. I can see them, hear them, but I can’t get through.

What makes it worse is the feeling that this is somehow my fault, like there’s something fundamentally off-putting about me that I can’t see. I keep wondering if this is just how life is going to be now: surrounded by people, but basically on my own, watching from the outside.
