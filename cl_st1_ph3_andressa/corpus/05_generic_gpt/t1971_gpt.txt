I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people but feel completely disconnected. I have coworkers, a few “friends,” family I talk to occasionally. On paper, I’m not isolated. But it feels like I’m watching my own life from behind glass. I show up, smile, make small talk, and then go home feeling weirdly hollow, like I was acting the whole time.

I don’t feel like anyone actually knows me. Not the parts that are scared, bitter, or tired. I always censor myself because I’m afraid if I show too much, people will pull away. But then I end up resenting that no one sees me. It’s this loop: I hide, then feel invisible, then hate myself for being invisible.

I see other people forming these easy, effortless connections—inside jokes, group chats that actually stay active, people who can call someone when they’re having a bad day. I can’t think of a single person I could text at 2am without feeling like I’m bothering them. Honestly, I hesitate to text people even at 2pm.

I keep thinking maybe I’m just fundamentally unlikeable or hard to connect with. I overthink every interaction, replay conversations, analyze tone. It’s exhausting. And then I’m too drained to even try to reach out next time.

I don’t know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to admit out loud that I’m lonely, instead of pretending I’m fine all the time.
