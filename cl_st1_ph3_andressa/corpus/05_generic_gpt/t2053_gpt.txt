I’ve been feeling this really specific kind of loneliness lately that’s hard to explain to people in my life. It’s not that I have absolutely no one – I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up on holidays. But I feel like I’m just…adjacent to everyone, not actually connected.

Most of my days are the same: work, go home, scroll, maybe watch something, sleep. I might exchange a few messages, but it all feels surface-level. I can be in a group chat and still feel like I’m standing outside a house, looking through the window at everyone else having a conversation I’m not really a part of.

I don’t know how to bridge the gap between “acquaintance” and “someone I can actually be vulnerable with.” Whenever I try to open up, I feel like I’m oversharing or making things awkward, so I pull back and keep things light and casual. Then I end up feeling invisible because no one actually knows me.

It’s starting to mess with my sense of who I am. If no one really sees me, am I actually “me” or just whatever version of myself is easiest for other people to tolerate? I don’t have an answer.
