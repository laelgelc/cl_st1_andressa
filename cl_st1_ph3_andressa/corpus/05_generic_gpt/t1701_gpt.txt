I’ve been feeling this really specific kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t seem to fit into the usual categories people talk about. It’s not just “I don’t have friends” (I do, technically), and it’s not “no one texts me” (my phone goes off enough). It’s more like: there is no one I feel truly *known* by.

I can hang out with people, laugh, talk about work, games, memes, whatever. I can play the role I’m supposed to play. But the second I leave, it feels like I was just a placeholder version of myself. I come home, shut the door, and it’s almost like I vanish from the world’s memory until the next time I’m needed.

What really messes with my head is that from the outside, nothing looks “wrong.” I have a job, I show up, I respond to messages, I’m not completely isolated. So when I feel this heavy emptiness, I immediately start doubting myself: “Maybe I’m being dramatic. Maybe this is just adulthood. Maybe this is what everyone feels and they just deal with it better.”

I don’t really open up to people in my life, and when I try, I feel stupid halfway through and change the subject. There’s this constant fear that if I actually show the messy parts, people will either pull away or just not get it, and that might feel worse than keeping it all inside. So I stay on the surface, and then I’m lonely because no one sees below it. It’s a loop I can’t seem to break.

I don’t even know exactly what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe just to put it somewhere outside my own head for once. Right now it feels like I’m standing in a crowded room, waving my arms, and somehow I’m still invisible.
