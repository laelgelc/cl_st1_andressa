I’m starting to feel like I’m disappearing in plain sight.

My days are full on paper — work, errands, messages to reply to — but none of it feels like real contact. I talk to people all the time, but it’s all surface-level: “How’s it going?” “Busy, you?” “Same.” Then we go back to our separate lives. I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everything instead of participating.

I don’t really have “a person.” No one I can text without overthinking, no one who’d notice if I went quiet for a while and actually check in. I’m always the one initiating, and after a while it starts to feel like begging for scraps of attention. I cancel plans less and less because there are fewer plans to cancel.

What scares me is how normal it’s starting to feel. I come home, put my phone face down, and just zone out. Sometimes I realize I haven’t heard my own voice all day except for “thank you” at a checkout counter. I don’t know how to bridge the gap between “acquaintance” and “someone who actually knows me,” and I’m tired of feeling like I’m the only one who can’t figure this out.
