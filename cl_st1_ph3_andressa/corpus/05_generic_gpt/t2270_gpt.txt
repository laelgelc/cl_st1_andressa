I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m alive in “observer mode” instead of “participant mode.” It’s like I’m pressed up against the glass of my own life, watching everyone else interact while I stand there holding a drink I don’t even want.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, acquaintances, a couple of old friends I talk to every few weeks. But there’s this constant sense that I’m not actually *in* anyone’s life. I’m just sort of...adjacent. The person you invite if there’s extra space, the one you reply to eventually, the one you like well enough but don’t think about unless they message first.

What really gets me is the silence. Not literal silence—I have music, podcasts, background noise—but the silence of not being known. The silence of no one asking follow-up questions, or remembering something I said months ago. I can go days without anyone using my name out loud. That does something weird to your sense of existing.

I try to initiate. I send the “hey, how have you been?” texts, suggest meeting up, comment on people’s posts. Sometimes it works, sometimes it just fizzles. Every time it fizzles, it reinforces this idea that I’m forcing my way into people’s lives instead of being naturally included. It’s exhausting to always be the one knocking on the door.

The worst part is how it messes with my perception of time. Weeks blur together because nothing emotionally significant happens. No shared jokes that turn into stories, no random late-night conversations, no “remember when we…” moments. Just work, commute, screen, sleep. Repeat.

I don’t even know exactly what I’m asking for. I don’t think there’s a fix-it answer. I just wanted to put this somewhere: that I’m tired of feeling like a background character in scenes that never really involve me, and I don’t know how much of that is my fault and how much is just how things are.
