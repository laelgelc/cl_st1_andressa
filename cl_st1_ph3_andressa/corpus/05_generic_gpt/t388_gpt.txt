I keep catching myself rehearsing conversations that will never happen.

Like I’ll be walking home from work, imagining telling someone about my day, the small annoyances, the weird thing my coworker said. In my head there’s someone who laughs at the right spots, asks follow-up questions, remembers names. In reality I get home, put my bag down, and the silence just swallows everything.

I’m not completely isolated. I have coworkers I talk to, a couple of old friends I text every now and then. But it all feels…surface-level. I can’t remember the last time I felt like I could be fully myself with someone without editing every sentence as I speak. I always feel like I’m playing a role: the “fine” person, the “easygoing” one, the one who doesn’t need anything.

It’s strange how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re behind glass. I go to social things sometimes, but I always end up feeling more alone afterwards. Everyone seems to already belong to someone, like they have their established circles and shared histories. I’m just this extra person hovering on the edge, laughing at jokes I wasn’t there to witness the beginning of.

I’ve tried being more proactive: messaging people first, suggesting plans, joining groups. Sometimes it works once or twice, but then it fizzles out and I’m left wondering what I did wrong or what I’m missing that makes connections not stick. It’s exhausting to keep trying to be “interesting” enough to be kept around.

What bothers me most is how loneliness changes the way I see myself. I start assuming I’m boring, or too much, or not enough. I overanalyze every delayed reply, every “sorry, I’m busy,” as proof that I’m fundamentally forgettable. And then I get quieter, which probably makes it even harder for anyone to really know me.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by writing this. I just needed to put it somewhere outside of my own head for once.
