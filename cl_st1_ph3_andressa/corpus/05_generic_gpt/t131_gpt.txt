I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing while still being physically here.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, some acquaintances, a family chat that lights up with memes. But none of it feels like it actually reaches me. I go through my days doing the normal things—work, errands, scrolling—but it’s like I’m watching someone else live my life from a distance.

What messes with my head is that I’m not completely isolated. I can text someone if I really need to. I can make small talk. Sometimes I even laugh. But the second I’m alone again, it’s like all the noise dies down and I’m left with this heavy, echoing silence. It feels like nobody really knows me, and I’m not even sure I remember how to let someone in far enough to change that.

I’ve started to dread weekends because they stretch out in front of me like this big empty space I have to fill by myself. I try to schedule things, but plans fall through or feel shallow. I come home to a quiet room, make dinner for one, open my phone hoping someone messaged first, and then just…nothing.

I keep thinking there must be something wrong with me that I can’t seem to form the kind of connections other people have naturally. I’m tired of being the person who looks “fine” from the outside while feeling completely alone inside.
