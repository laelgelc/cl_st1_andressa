I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s hitting me harder as I get older. I’m surrounded by people every day – at work, on public transport, in stores – but it feels like I’m moving through a different layer of reality than everyone else.

I have acquaintances, people I can talk to about projects or memes or the weather, but no one I’d call if something really bad happened. I don’t think anyone would naturally think of me either. I’m “nice,” “easy to work with,” “chill,” but those are all surface-level traits. It’s like I’m a background character in my own life.

What makes it worse is that I’m not even sure what I’m doing wrong. I try to listen, ask questions, show interest. I say yes to invitations when they rarely appear. But there’s always this invisible wall where things just don’t deepen. People have their “real” friends, and I’m the extra.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through chats that are mostly dead, open and close apps, tell myself I should reach out to someone, then can’t think of anyone it wouldn’t feel weird to message. The silence starts to feel heavy, almost physical.

I keep wondering if this is just how it’s going to be: present, but not really part of anything.
