I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, I just know how to perform being “fine.”

I have friends on paper, group chats that ping, coworkers who say I’m “easy to talk to.” But no one really knows me. When conversations get close to anything real, I panic and either crack a joke or turn it back on them. Then later I lie in bed replaying everything I didn’t say, feeling hollow and weirdly invisible.

What makes it worse is that people assume I’m okay because I function: I show up, I smile, I ask about their day. Meanwhile I haven’t had a genuine “how are you really?” in months, maybe years.

Sometimes I wonder if I missed some crucial stage where people learned how to move from acquaintance to actual friend. It feels like I’m always stuck at the surface, watching everyone else swim.
