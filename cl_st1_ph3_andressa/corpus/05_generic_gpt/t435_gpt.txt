I’m starting to feel like I’ve become transparent to the world.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, even family—but it all feels like background noise. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, TV shows. No one actually asks how I’m doing in a way that feels like they want the real answer. And when they do, I hear myself say “I’m fine” before I can stop it.

What scares me most is how used to it I’m becoming. Nights blur together: scrolling, watching random videos, pretending I’m too “tired” to go out when the truth is I don’t know who I’d even call. I crave connection but dread the awkwardness of trying.

Sometimes I wonder if I’ve forgotten how to be close to anyone at all.
