I’ve been feeling like a background character in my own life lately.

I go through the motions—work, commute, eat, sleep—but it all feels like I’m watching someone else’s routine from behind glass. I talk to people every day, but it’s all surface-level: “How was your weekend?” “Busy, you?” and then silence. No one really knows what’s going on with me, and I’m starting to wonder if that’s partly my fault or if I just never had the right people around.

What bothers me most is the contrast. Online, I’m “social.” Group chats, memes, the occasional voice call. In reality, my phone is mostly silent. No one asks to see me in person. If I disappeared from social media, I’m not sure how long it would take for anyone to notice.

I try to initiate plans sometimes, but people are “swamped,” “tired,” or they just forget to follow up. After a while, it feels pathetic to keep trying. I’ve started telling myself I prefer being alone, but that’s not really true. I just don’t want to keep being reminded that I’m optional in other people’s lives.

I don’t even know what I’m expecting by writing this. Maybe I just needed to say it somewhere.
