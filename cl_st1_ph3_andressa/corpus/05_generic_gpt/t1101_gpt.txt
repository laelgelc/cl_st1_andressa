I’m starting to realize that I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m behind glass.

I have coworkers, acquaintances, even a few people I’d technically call friends, but everything feels surface-level. We talk about work, shows, memes, random stuff, but it never goes deeper. I don’t know if it’s them or me. I try to open up a bit and then immediately feel like I’ve overshared or made things awkward, so I retreat again.

Most evenings I just scroll my phone or watch something until I’m tired enough to sleep. The silence in my place is so loud. I keep thinking I should join a club, go to events, “put myself out there,” but the idea exhausts me before I even start. It’s like I’m stuck in this loop of wanting connection and then sabotaging every chance at it.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. Like I’m slowly accepting that no one really knows me, and maybe no one ever will.
