I’ve been feeling like a background character in my own life lately.

I’m surrounded by people on paper—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats that light up my phone—but none of it feels like connection. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes. I can’t remember the last time someone asked how I really am and actually wanted to know.

What messes with my head is that I look “fine” from the outside. I show up, make small talk, crack a joke. But I go home to silence, scroll through social media, and see everyone else doing things in groups, even if I know it’s curated. It still stings.

I’ve tried joining groups, messaging old friends, being the one to reach out. It usually fades after a few exchanges, like I’m forgettable once I’m out of sight. I’m starting to wonder if there’s something fundamentally off about me that other people can sense, even if I can’t.
