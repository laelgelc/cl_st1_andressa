I don’t know when “being alone a lot” turned into “being lonely all the time,” but it feels like it snuck up on me.

Most days are just… quiet. I wake up, go through the motions, maybe exchange a few surface-level messages with coworkers or people online, and then the rest of the day is me in my own head. I don’t really have anyone I can text just to say, “Hey, today sucked,” without feeling like I’m bothering them or overstepping some invisible boundary.

What really messes with me is that from the outside I probably look fine. I can joke around, I can talk about work or random stuff, but as soon as the interaction ends, it’s like the room gets ten times emptier. I keep thinking, “If I disappeared, how long would it take for anyone to actually notice?” and I hate that my brain even goes there.

I’ve tried joining groups, talking more, being “out there,” but it feels like I’m always orbiting other people’s lives, never actually part of anything. Like I’m the extra in everyone else’s movie.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to say it somewhere.
