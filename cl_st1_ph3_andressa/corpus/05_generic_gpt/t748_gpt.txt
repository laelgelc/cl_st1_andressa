I’ve been feeling like a background character in my own life for a long time.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family I text sometimes—but it all feels thin, like plastic wrap over everything. Conversations stay on the surface. Jokes, small talk, “how was your weekend?” and then everyone returns to their real lives, and I’m not in them.

I keep thinking that if I disappeared, people would notice in a logistical way, not an emotional one. “Oh, that person’s gone, we need someone else to cover that shift.” That kind of thing.

I’m also weirdly ashamed of being lonely. It feels like an admission that I’m somehow defective, that other people got handed a manual for making and keeping close friends and I missed that day. So I pretend I’m fine and stay quiet when plans are mentioned that I’m not invited to.

Most days I just go home, scroll, watch something, and try not to think about how repeatable it all is.
