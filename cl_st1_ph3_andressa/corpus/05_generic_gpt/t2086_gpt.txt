I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually know how to connect with people, even when they’re right in front of me.

On paper, my life doesn’t look that lonely. I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats. But it all feels like I’m playing a character who’s “fine,” and the real version of me is stuck somewhere behind glass, watching.

I go through entire days where I don’t say anything that feels honest. I make small talk, I laugh at the right parts, I ask how people are doing. And the whole time there’s this heavy feeling in my chest, like I’m screaming into a void that no one can hear. Not because they don’t care, but because I don’t know how to let them.

When I do try to open up, I feel stupid, dramatic, or like I’m burdening them. I end up downplaying everything: “Yeah, it’s been a bit rough, but I’m okay.” I’m not okay, but I also don’t know what “okay” is supposed to look like anymore.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through my phone hoping someone will message me first, because I’m tired of being the one who reaches out. Then I hate myself for feeling needy and put the phone down, but I still keep checking it like something might change.

It’s weird how you can be “known” by people and still feel like no one actually sees you. I’m not even sure what I’m asking for. I just wanted to put this somewhere it might make sense to someone.
