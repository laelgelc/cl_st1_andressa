I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that doesn’t seem to go away, even when I’m around people.

I have friends, technically. Group chats, occasional hangouts, memes sent back and forth. But it all feels…surface-level. I can be sitting in a room full of people and still feel like I’m behind a glass wall, watching everyone else connect while I just mimic what connection is supposed to look like.

I don’t really know how to let people see the real me without feeling like I’m burdening them or being too much. So I stay on the safe topics, make jokes, ask questions, and then go home and replay every conversation in my head, convinced I was awkward or boring.

Nights are the worst. The silence feels heavy, like it’s pressing on my chest. I scroll, I lurk, I read other people’s lives, but it doesn’t translate into feeling known. It’s like I’m slowly disappearing from everyone’s mind, including my own, and I don’t know how to reverse that.
