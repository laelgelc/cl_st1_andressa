I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I exist behind glass. I go to work, I respond when spoken to, I smile at the right moments, but it all feels like I’m watching someone else do it. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice,” which sounds harmless, but it’s become this cage. No one really asks anything deeper, and I don’t know how to push past that without seeming weird or needy.

I have acquaintances, group chats, coworkers I joke with, but nobody I’d actually call if something really bad happened at 2 a.m. When plans get cancelled, I pretend I’m relieved to have “me time,” but the truth is I just sit there with this heavy, physical feeling in my chest, scrolling through feeds of people doing normal, connected things.

What messes with my head is that I don’t think I’m completely unlikable. I can be funny, I listen, I care. But friendships feel like sand in my hands—either we drift apart, or I end up being the one who cares more, and then I pull back before it hurts.

I’m not even sure what I’m looking for posting this. Maybe just proof that I said it out loud somewhere, that I admitted I’m lonely instead of pretending I’m just “independent” all the time.
