I’m starting to feel like I’ve slowly slipped out of everyone’s life and no one noticed, including me until recently.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, acquaintances, family chats. But everything feels…surface-level. I can go days without anyone asking me how I *really* am. And when someone does, I panic and default to “I’m fine” because I don’t even know where to start.

What scares me is how used to it I’m becoming. I wake up, go through the motions, scroll through other people’s lives, then go to bed feeling like a background character in my own story. I watch friend groups on social media and realize I’ve never had that kind of “we just show up for each other” connection.

I keep wondering if it’s something fundamentally off about me. I second-guess every message I send. I replay conversations and convince myself I talked too much, or too little, or just wrong. So I stop reaching out, then feel worse when no one reaches out to me.

It’s like I’m quietly disappearing while still being physically present.
