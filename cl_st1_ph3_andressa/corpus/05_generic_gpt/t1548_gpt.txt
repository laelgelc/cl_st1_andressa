I’m starting to realize I’m not just “introverted,” I’m lonely in a way that feels like it’s hollowing me out.

I have people around me—coworkers, family chats, the occasional meetup—but it all feels like background noise. No one really knows me beyond the surface stuff. I keep conversations light because I’m scared if I’m honest about how isolated I feel, I’ll make things awkward or push people away.

Nights are the worst. I scroll, watch videos, lurk on here, and pretend I’m “relaxing,” but it’s mostly distraction. I can’t remember the last time someone texted first just to check in, or when I felt genuinely excited to see a message.

It’s like I’m living beside my own life instead of in it.
