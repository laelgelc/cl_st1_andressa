I’m starting to feel like I don’t exist anywhere outside my own head.

My days are basically a loop: wake up, go through the motions, scroll, lurk, sleep. I “talk” to people online but it’s mostly surface-level stuff—memes, comments, the occasional DM that dies after a few messages. In real life it’s even emptier. Coworkers make small talk with me, but no one actually knows me. I don’t even know if I’d know how to let them.

What messes with me most is how invisible this feels. From the outside, I probably look fine. I show up, I laugh at the right times, I answer emails. Inside, it feels like I’m screaming into a soundproof room. I’ll go entire weekends without a single real-time conversation, and then feel weirdly ashamed for wanting someone to just… notice I’m there.

I keep thinking about how I’ve somehow reached this age without any real “core group,” no one who’d text me first just to check in. I see people post about friends they’ve known for years, and I can’t even imagine what that kind of consistency feels like.

I don’t know if I’m the problem—too quiet, too closed off, too late to everything—or if this is just how life shakes out for some of us. I just know I’m tired of pretending I’m okay with being this alone when I’m really not.
