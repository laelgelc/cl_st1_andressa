I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

I’m lonely in this weird, quiet way. Not the dramatic movie kind where you’re crying on the floor every night, but the kind where everything feels slightly muted all the time. I go through my days, do my work, make small talk, answer emails, scroll through feeds, and it all looks normal from the outside. But it feels like I’m living behind glass. I can see everyone, but nothing really reaches me.

I have “people” in my life, technically. Coworkers, a few old friends I text every now and then, family chats that light up on holidays and birthdays. But I don’t feel known by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, shows, memes, “how was your weekend?” I don’t know how to move it from that shallow level to something real without feeling like I’m being too intense or needy.

I think what scares me most is how used to it I’m becoming. I used to crave connection so badly it hurt. Now it’s like my brain has numbed itself. I still want it, but I don’t expect it anymore. I just assume people are busy, already have their circles, and I’m extra. So I keep it light, keep it casual, laugh at the right times, and then go home and sit in the quiet.

There’s also this shame attached to it. I feel like I should have figured this out by now. I’m not a teenager. I’m at that age where people are getting married, having kids, or at least have “their group,” and I’m still trying to find someone I can text without overthinking every word. It makes me feel defective in some fundamental way, like there’s something about me that makes me forgettable.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to say it somewhere.
