I don’t really know how to talk about this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m just… not part of anything.

My days are basically a loop: wake up, work, come home, scroll, sleep. I talk to people at work, but it’s all surface-level stuff—projects, deadlines, “how was your weekend?” where no one really wants an honest answer. When I get home, the silence hits me so hard it almost feels physical. No messages waiting, no one to call “just because,” no plans to look forward to.

I used to have friends in school and university. We’d hang out, stay up late, overshare about everything. Then people moved, got partners, started families, drifted into these separate lives that I watch from the outside now, mostly through social media. I hesitate to text because I feel like an interruption in their “real” lives, like I missed some invisible deadline where people solidified their circles and I just… didn’t.

What bothers me most isn’t even being alone physically, it’s this constant feeling that I’m not really known by anyone. No one knows my daily routines, my small frustrations, the stupid jokes I’d normally share, or the random thoughts that just sit in my head because there’s nowhere for them to go. I can go days without saying anything that isn’t functional or polite.

I keep thinking, “I should join something, message someone, try harder,” but the thought of forcing myself into spaces where everyone already seems to have their people is exhausting. And when I do push myself to be social, I feel like I’m performing a version of myself that’s acceptable, not actually me.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere instead of letting it bounce around in my head all the time.
