I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I’m not in immediate crisis. I talk to coworkers, I answer emails, I say “have a good weekend” and “how was your holiday?” and I can fake normal small talk pretty well. But as soon as I walk out of the office and the door closes behind me, it’s like the volume of everything drops to zero. No messages. No calls. No one expecting me. Just the sound of my keys and my footsteps and then silence.

I scroll through my phone way too much. Not even looking for anything specific, just flicking between apps like maybe this time there will be a notification from someone who actually wants to talk to me, not just a spam email or a random app alert. Most of the time, it’s nothing. I watch other people’s stories of dinners, parties, “just hanging out,” and it feels like I’m staring through a window into a world I somehow missed the entrance to.

I’m not completely socially inept. I can make people laugh in short bursts. I’ve had moments where I thought, “Okay, this person might become a real friend.” But it never seems to stick. Things just fade. People get busy. I don’t want to be the one constantly asking to hang out and feel like I’m forcing myself into someone’s life, so I back off. Then time passes, and suddenly it’s been months and reaching out feels weird.

Nights are the worst. The quiet feels heavy. I talk out loud sometimes just to hear a human voice, even if it’s my own. I eat dinner alone, watch something, pretend I’m enjoying my “me time,” but there’s this constant background ache that this isn’t how it’s supposed to be, at least not all the time.

I don’t really know what I’m expecting by writing this. Maybe I just needed to say it somewhere where it doesn’t feel like I’m burdening anyone I know in real life, which, ironically, is basically no one.
