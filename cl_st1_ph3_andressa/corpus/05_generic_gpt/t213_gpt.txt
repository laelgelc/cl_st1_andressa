I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing in plain sight.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I reply to messages, I show up when I’m supposed to. People would probably describe me as quiet but “doing okay.” What they don’t see is how much of my day is just…silence. Not peaceful silence, more like the kind that echoes.

I have acquaintances, but no one I feel I can actually be unfiltered with. Conversations stay on the surface: work, shows, memes, “how was your weekend?” I find myself editing every sentence so I don’t come off as needy or weird, and then I go home and wish someone actually knew me well enough to notice that I’m not okay.

I scroll through chats and realize I’m always the one reaching out, and even then the talks die quickly. I’ve started to delay replying just so I don’t look desperate. It’s like I’m afraid of being alone but also ashamed of wanting connection so badly.

Nights are the worst. I lie there replaying old interactions, wondering if I missed my chance with people who might’ve become real friends if I hadn’t been so guarded. Now I’m stuck in this loop where I want closeness but have no idea how to bridge the distance I’ve helped create.
