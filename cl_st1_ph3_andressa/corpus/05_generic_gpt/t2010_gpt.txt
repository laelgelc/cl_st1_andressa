I don’t really know how to put this into words, but I feel like I’ve been slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, I talk to people every day. But it all feels like background noise. I go to work, make small talk with coworkers, answer “How are you?” with “Good, you?” and it’s all just…hollow. No one actually knows me. I don’t even know if I’d know how to let them.

I scroll through group chats that I’m technically part of, but the conversations seem to move on without me. If I don’t message, nobody checks in. If I do message, it feels like I’m interrupting a flow that doesn’t really have a place for me. I’m always “around,” but never really included. Like I’m an optional extra in other people’s lives.

Nights are the worst. The silence is so loud. I’ll have YouTube, music, podcasts going just so I don’t have to sit with my own thoughts, but somehow the loneliness pushes through all of it. It’s not even that I want constant socializing; I just want to feel like there’s at least one person who would notice if I went quiet.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” the way everyone suggests—meetups, hobby groups, talking to people more—but I always end up feeling like I’m orbiting around other people’s already-formed circles. They’re polite, even friendly, but there’s a difference between being welcomed and being wanted, and I can feel that difference very clearly.

What scares me is how normal this has started to feel. Like I’m getting used to the idea that this is just how my life is: being surrounded by people but still feeling like a ghost in every room I walk into. I keep wondering if this is something I can actually change, or if this is just who I am now.
