I’m starting to wonder if there’s something fundamentally wrong with me, because loneliness feels less like a phase and more like my default setting.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family I message now and then—but I don’t feel actually known by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes. I try to open up a bit and it either gets brushed off as “you’ll be fine” or the topic changes. After a while, you just stop trying.

Nights are the worst. I finish whatever I need to do, and then it’s just me, my phone, and the awareness that nobody is thinking about me specifically. Not in a “how are you really?” way. I scroll, I lurk, I watch other people’s lives play out in photos and comments, and it feels like I’m behind a glass wall.

I’m not even sure what I’m asking for writing this. I guess I just needed to say out loud—somewhere—that I’m tired of feeling like an extra in my own life while everyone else seems to have a role and a script.
