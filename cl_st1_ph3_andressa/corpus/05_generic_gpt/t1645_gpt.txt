I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, coworkers who are polite, a couple of old friends I text a few times a month, a family group chat with occasional memes. But the actual day-to-day reality is just… empty. I wake up, go to work, come home, scroll, sleep. No one really notices if I disappear for a few days, as long as I answer work emails.

The weirdest part is being surrounded by people and still feeling completely disconnected. At work, people joke around, talk about their weekends, their kids, their partners. I can chime in with small talk, but there’s this invisible wall. I don’t know how to cross from “person who’s here” to “person someone actually cares about.” I leave conversations feeling like I played a part instead of being myself.

I’ve tried the usual things: joined a couple of local meetups, went to a hobby group, even tried some apps. I show up, talk a bit, and then just fade into the background. Other people seem to click so easily. I walk home thinking about every awkward pause, every time I said something stupid or too quiet or too much.

What scares me is how used to it I’m becoming. I used to feel a sharp ache about being alone; now it’s more like a dull hum in the background, always there. I’ll have moments where I want to reach out, send a message, ask someone to hang out, but I sit there staring at my phone until the impulse passes.

I don’t know what I’m expecting by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to put into words that I exist, that I’m here, even if it doesn’t really feel like it most days.
