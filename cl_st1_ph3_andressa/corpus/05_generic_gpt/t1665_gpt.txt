I’m starting to realize I don’t really know how to connect with people, and it’s getting harder to pretend I’m okay with it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I answer messages, I show up when I’m supposed to. But everything feels like I’m playing a character who’s loosely based on me. I can make small talk, I can be “funny” in a group, but the second I try to say anything real, my brain screams that I’m being weird or too much, and I shut down.

I watch other people slide into friendships so easily. They share inside jokes, make plans, send each other random memes. I overthink sending a single text. I’ll type something out, stare at it, then delete it because I’m convinced they’ll see it as annoying or clingy. Then I sit there, feeling both relieved and disappointed.

The worst part is the quiet moments. Coming home to an empty place, eating alone, realizing there’s no one who’d really notice if I disappeared for a while. I have people I “know,” but I don’t feel known by anyone. Not in the way I want to be.

I’m not even sure what I’m asking for. I just needed to say this somewhere, because I don’t have anyone in my actual life I feel comfortable saying it to.
