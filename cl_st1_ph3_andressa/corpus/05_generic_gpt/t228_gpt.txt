I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m watching my own life from the outside. I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about TV shows and the weather, and then I come home and just… exist in silence. I have “people” around me, but I don’t feel like I actually *belong* to anyone.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, a couple of old friends I text now and then, but there’s nobody I can message at 2am and say “I feel like I’m falling apart” without worrying I’m being too much. I’m always the one who listens, who keeps it light, who makes sure everyone else feels comfortable. Somewhere along the way that became my entire identity, and now I don’t know how to be anything else.

What makes it worse is that on paper my life looks fine. I’m not completely isolated. I go out sometimes, I show up to birthdays, I answer group chats. But I always feel like a guest in other people’s lives, never someone whose presence actually matters. If I stopped showing up, I’m not convinced anyone’s day-to-day would really change.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like people always say. Joined a couple of groups, tried to be more social, forced myself to initiate plans. Half the time it fizzles out; the other half, I end up feeling like I’m auditioning for the role of “friend” and getting quietly rejected.

What scares me is how used to this I’m becoming. The silence when I come home doesn’t shock me anymore. The weekends with no plans don’t either. It’s just this low, constant ache of realizing that nobody really knows me, and I don’t know how to let them, even if they wanted to.
