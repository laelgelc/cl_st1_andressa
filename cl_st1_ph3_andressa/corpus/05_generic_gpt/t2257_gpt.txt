I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s getting harder to pretend otherwise.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have coworkers, acquaintances, people I can message if I really need something. But there’s no one I feel truly close to, no one I can text without overthinking every word, no one I can call when I’m having a bad day without feeling like I’m imposing.

I keep replaying past interactions and cringing at how awkward I must have seemed. I worry I come across as boring or emotionally flat, so I default to listening, nodding, asking questions. People seem comfortable around me, but they don’t really *see* me. And I rarely volunteer anything personal because I’m terrified it’ll be “too much” or just… irrelevant.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through chats where I’m always the one who stopped replying first, terrified of being annoying, and then I wonder if people just assumed I didn’t care. I do care. I just don’t know how to show it in a way that doesn’t feel forced or needy.

There’s this weird contradiction where I want to be invited, included, remembered—but when it actually happens, I feel out of place, like I’m just temporarily filling a gap until the “real” friends show up.

I don’t know what I’m expecting writing this. Maybe I just wanted to see my own thoughts somewhere outside my head for once.
