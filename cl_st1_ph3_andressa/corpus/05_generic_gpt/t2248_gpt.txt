I’m starting to realize that I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m sealed behind glass.

I have coworkers, acquaintances, people I chat with online. I can be funny, helpful, “easy to talk to.” But it all feels like I’m playing a character who’s loosely based on me. When the conversation ends, it’s like I snap back into this empty, echo-y version of myself.

What really gets to me is the lack of “default people.” No one I can text without overthinking. No one who’d notice if I went quiet for a while and actually check in, not out of obligation, but because they care. I scroll through my contacts and realize I don’t know who I’m allowed to reach out to without feeling like a burden.

I keep wondering if I’m the problem—too distant, too weird, too quiet, too much. I don’t even know what I’m craving exactly. Just… someone to exist next to, without feeling like I have to earn my place every second.
