I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into the usual “I have no friends” box. I technically have people in my life—coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up my phone—but I still feel like I’m standing behind glass watching everyone else actually live.

Most of my days are a loop: wake up, work, come home, scroll, sleep. I try to initiate conversations, but everything feels surface-level. It’s like everyone else has their own established circles and I’m the extra seat at the table no one really needs. I get invited sometimes, but I can’t shake the feeling that if I stopped showing up, nothing would really change for anyone.

What messes with me the most is not having “my person.” Not even in a romantic sense—just someone who genuinely wants to know how my day was, unprompted. I’m tired of always being the one who reaches out, the one who asks the questions, the one who listens.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for posting this. Maybe I just needed to say out loud that I feel invisible, even when people are technically around me.
