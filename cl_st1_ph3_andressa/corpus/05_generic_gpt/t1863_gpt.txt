It’s weird how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re behind glass.

On paper my life looks “fine.” I have a job, coworkers who are polite, a couple of old friends I text once in a while, family group chats that light up on holidays. But most days I feel like I’m just spectating my own life, like everyone else is actually in it and I’m just… near it.

I don’t think I’ve had a truly vulnerable, unguarded conversation in years. Everything feels surface-level: work talk, memes, “we should catch up sometime” that never becomes an actual plan. When I try to open up even a little, I get this immediate fear that I’m being “too much,” so I pull back, make a joke, change the subject. Then later I lie in bed replaying the conversation, cringing at myself anyway.

I scroll through social media and see people hanging out, traveling, getting married, having kids, and it’s like I’m stuck in this waiting room where my number never gets called. I’m in my own apartment, in my own silence, and the quiet is so loud it almost feels physical. I’ll put on a podcast or YouTube just to have human voices in the background, but it doesn’t touch the part that hurts.

I’ve tried things: meetups, hobby groups, talking more to coworkers. I always end up feeling like the extra chair at the table—technically there, but not really needed. People are friendly enough, but I don’t feel chosen by anyone. No one’s first call. No one’s “I thought of you when…”

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. Like I’m slowly adjusting to the idea that this might just be it: existing parallel to everyone else, close enough to see them, too far to actually reach.
