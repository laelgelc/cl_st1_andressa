I’ve hit that point where I’m not sure if I’m lonely because I’m isolated, or isolated because I’m lonely.

My days are a loop: work, come home, scroll, sleep. I talk to coworkers about tasks and deadlines, but nobody actually knows what’s going on with me. I don’t think anyone would notice if I stopped showing up, at least not beyond “who’s taking over this project?”

I’ve lost the muscle for connection. When I get invited somewhere (which is rare), I panic and overthink: what do I say, how do I act, will I just sit there like furniture? So I usually decline. Then I sit at home feeling worse, because I know I did this to myself, but I also don’t know how to do anything different.

It’s like watching life through a window. People have group chats, plans, inside jokes. I have unread notifications from apps I don’t even open anymore. I’m tired of my own company, but it feels like the only one I’m allowed to have.
