I keep realizing that most of my “connections” are just context-based. When the context disappears, so do the people.

At school it was classmates. At work it’s coworkers. We talk, we joke, we complain about the same stuff. It feels like friendship in the moment, but the second we’re not sharing the same space or problem, the thread just snaps. No one texts. If I don’t message first, the chat stays dead. If I stop showing up, I’m just… gone.

I watch other people move so naturally into each other’s lives—group trips, regular hangouts, “let’s catch up this weekend” that actually happens. For me, it’s like I’m always the extra chair at someone else’s table. Useful, but never really part of the set.

The weirdest part is how invisible the loneliness is. I function. I make small talk. I seem “fine.” There’s no obvious crisis, just this constant low-level ache that I’m nobody’s first choice, or second, or maybe even tenth. My phone is silent unless I force it not to be, and forcing it just makes me feel more pathetic.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for posting this. I’m not looking for advice; I know all the usual tips. I just wanted to put into words how it feels to be surrounded by people and still feel like I don’t actually exist in anyone’s life once they go home.
