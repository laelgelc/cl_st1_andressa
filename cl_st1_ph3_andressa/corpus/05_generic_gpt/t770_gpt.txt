I don’t really know how to describe what I’m feeling other than this weird combination of being “known” and completely invisible at the same time.

On paper I’m not isolated. I have coworkers who chat with me, a family group chat that pings every day, some old friends I send memes to. My phone isn’t empty. But everything feels like it’s happening a few inches away from me, like I’m watching my own life through a window.

I keep realizing how much of my personality is just…surface-level. I can talk about shows, work, news, random jokes. But when it comes to saying, “I’m actually not okay, I feel really alone,” my brain just shuts down. I imagine the awkward silence, the “you’re not alone, you have us” replies, and I feel stupid for even wanting more.

It’s not that people are cruel. They’re just busy. They have partners, kids, weekend plans, group photos. I’m the person they like, but don’t think to invite first. I’m “we should hang out sometime” that never materializes. I’m the extra chair at the table that’s fine to have but not necessary.

What scares me is how normalized this has become. I wake up, go to work, come home, scroll, sleep. The days blend together. I tell myself I’m just tired, just in a phase, but there’s this quiet panic underneath, like I’m drifting further away from everyone and I don’t know how to swim back.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for. A best friend? A partner? Just one person who actually notices when I go quiet for a few days and doesn’t assume I’m “just busy”? Someone I don’t have to perform around, where I don’t feel like I’m auditioning to be worth their time.

I’m starting to feel like I’ve forgotten how to genuinely connect, and I’m not sure when that happened, or if I’d even recognize real closeness anymore if it showed up.
