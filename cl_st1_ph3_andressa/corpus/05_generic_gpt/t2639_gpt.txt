I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and “too much” at the same time.

On paper, I’m not completely alone. I have coworkers I chat with, family I text sometimes, a couple of old friends I send memes to. But none of it feels like it really counts. It’s like my life is made of small talk and half-connections, and the parts of me that feel the heaviest never see daylight.

I watch other people make plans so casually. “Let’s grab dinner,” “Come over this weekend,” group photos, inside jokes. I’m always on the outside looking in, liking the posts, leaving comments, but somehow never being the person anyone thinks to invite first. Or at all.

What’s messing with my head is that I’m not sure if I’m the problem. I overthink every message, worry I’m being annoying, and then end up saying nothing. When I finally do reach out, I feel like I’m asking for a favor instead of just… existing naturally in someone’s life. I feel needy for wanting more than surface-level contact.

The nights are the worst. That’s when everything gets quiet and I realize there’s no one I can call without rehearsing what I’m going to say and wondering if I’m bothering them. I keep imagining what it would be like to have one person who actually notices when I disappear for a few days.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just needed to say it somewhere.
