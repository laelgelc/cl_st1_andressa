I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

I have people around me—coworkers, some acquaintances, family I text every now and then—but I don’t feel *with* any of them. Conversations stay at “How was your weekend?” and “Did you see that show?” and I play my role, say the right things, laugh in the right places. Then I go home and it’s just silence and the feeling that none of it actually touched me.

What really messes with me is that on paper I’m “fine.” I get things done, I show up, I’m not completely isolated. But I can’t shake this emptiness, like I could disappear from everyone’s lives and it would barely change their routines. I’m not even sure how much of this is in my head and how much is real.

I’ve tried to reach out a bit more, message people first, suggest plans. Sometimes it works, but even when I’m physically with others, I still feel weirdly separate, like I’m observing instead of participating. It’s like there’s this invisible barrier between me and everyone else that I don’t know how to break.

I don’t really have a point to this post. I just needed to put it somewhere. It’s exhausting carrying around this constant sense of being “outside” of everything, and not knowing how to let anyone actually see me.
