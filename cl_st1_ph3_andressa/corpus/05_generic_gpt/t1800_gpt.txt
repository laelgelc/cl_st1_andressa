I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and “too much” at the same time, and I don’t really know where else to say it out loud.

I’m surrounded by people on paper. Coworkers, acquaintances, family chats, the usual “how’s it going?” small talk. But it’s like everything is happening one layer above where I actually am. I can function, crack jokes, get my work done, but the second I’m alone again it feels like I just disappear into this heavy quiet.

What gets to me most is the lack of being *known*. People know facts about me—where I work, what I like, random trivia—but nobody really sees the parts I’m actually struggling with. And I can’t tell if that’s because I don’t show them, or because they’re not interested, or both. I start to open up, then I feel stupid and pull back, because I don’t want to be the person who’s “too emotional” or “too needy.”

I watch friendships around me that seem so natural and easy—people who can just text “I’m sad, can we talk?” without rehearsing it ten times in their head. I envy that. For me, reaching out feels like I’m imposing, like I have to justify why I deserve someone’s time.

I’ve also hit that stage where I’m tired of meeting new people only to stay stuck at the “we should hang out sometime” level that never actually goes anywhere. It makes every interaction feel temporary before it even starts.

I don’t really know what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put this somewhere instead of letting it bounce around in my head all night. It’s exhausting to feel this alone while technically not being alone at all.
