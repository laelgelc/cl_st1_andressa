I’ve been feeling this weird, quiet kind of loneliness that doesn’t really make sense on paper.

I have people around me. I talk to coworkers, I send memes in group chats, I show up to family stuff when I have to. But it all feels like I’m playing a role I learned a long time ago. I say the right things, laugh at the right moments, ask the usual questions. It’s like I’m there, but not actually *there*.

What I’m missing is that feeling of being known. Not “what’s your favorite movie” known, but the kind of knowing where someone can tell when you’re not okay just from the way you say “I’m fine.” I don’t really have anyone like that. And I’m not that person for anyone else either, which makes me wonder if I’ve just stayed too surface-level with everyone for too long.

I don’t open up much in real life because I’m scared of being “too much” or making things awkward. So I keep things light, and then I end up going home and feeling this heavy emptiness that’s hard to explain. It’s like being homesick for a place I’ve never actually been.

Sometimes I catch myself thinking that if I disappeared from social media and stopped reaching out, most people would just… stop too. Not out of cruelty, just because there isn’t really a strong reason not to. And that thought sticks with me more than I want to admit.
