I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too exposed at the same time.

On paper, my life looks “fine.” I go to work, I respond to messages, I say “not much, you?” when people ask what’s new. I’m surrounded by people often enough that I can’t even use the excuse of being physically isolated. But it all feels like I’m playing an NPC in someone else’s game.

Conversations are mostly surface-level. I crack jokes, I listen, I nod in the right places, and somehow walk away feeling emptier than before. No one really knows what’s going on in my head, and I’m not even sure how to start explaining it without feeling like I’m burdening them or being dramatic.

The worst part is how my standards for connection have quietly lowered. I’ll cling to a random long text exchange or a slightly deeper chat and replay it in my head for days, like proof that I’m not completely forgettable. Then things drift back to normal, and I’m left wondering if I imagined the whole thing.

It feels like I’m constantly on the verge of crying or just going numb, but I keep choosing numb because it’s easier to function that way. I don’t really know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe just to see my own thoughts somewhere outside my head.
