I’m starting to feel like I’ve slipped out of everyone’s peripheral vision.

I have people in my life on paper—family, a few friends, coworkers—but I don’t feel *known* by any of them. Conversations stay on the surface: work, shows, weather. I try to open up a bit and it’s like I’ve made things awkward, so I backtrack and joke it off. Then I go home and replay it in my head for hours.

What really messes with me is how invisible this feels. I function fine: I show up to work, answer emails, smile when I’m supposed to. No one sees that I go days without a real, honest conversation. My phone only lights up for group chats or logistics. If I disappeared, it would probably take a while for anyone to notice in a concrete way.

I’m not even sure what I’m asking for by writing this. Maybe I just needed somewhere to admit it.
