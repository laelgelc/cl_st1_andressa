I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living in a glass box. I can see people, I can talk to them, I can even laugh at the right times, but there’s this invisible wall between me and everyone else.

On paper I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers I chat with, a family group chat that pings every day, a couple of old friends I send memes to. But none of it feels like it touches anything real. It’s all surface-level, like I’m playing a character who knows the right lines to say, and then I go home and it’s just…quiet. The kind of quiet that makes you notice the sound of your own breathing.

What bothers me most is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped texting, if I stopped showing up, I honestly don’t know how long it would take before anyone noticed in a meaningful way. I’m the “reliable one,” the listener, the person people vent to. But when I need to talk, I scroll through my contacts and realize there isn’t a single person I feel safe being completely honest with.

I’ve tried joining clubs, group chats, even going to events alone, but I always end up feeling like an extra in other people’s lives. They already have their inside jokes, their shared history, their group photos. I’m just…there, on the edge of the frame.

It’s a weird kind of grief for something I’ve never really had: that feeling of being known and still chosen. I’m starting to wonder if some people just never get that.
