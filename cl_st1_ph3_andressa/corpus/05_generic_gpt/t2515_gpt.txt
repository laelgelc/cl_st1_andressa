I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m just drifting through my own life as a side character.

I technically “have people” around me. Coworkers, some old friends I text every now and then, family I see on holidays. But I don’t feel actually known by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes, random complaints. I can be physically surrounded by people and still feel like I’m in a soundproof box watching everything happen from the outside.

What’s messing with me is how invisible this feels. From the outside, I probably look fine. I show up to work, I respond in group chats, I say the right things. Inside, I’m just… tired. Tired of being the one who listens, asks questions, remembers details, but rarely gets that same energy back. When I try to share something real, it either gets brushed off, turned into a joke, or people get uncomfortable and change the subject.

I keep thinking about how long it’s been since someone asked me a question that wasn’t just small talk. When was the last time someone noticed I was quiet and genuinely wanted to know why? I can’t remember. I think I’ve become “the functional one” in everyone’s mind, the person who’s fine by default. Meanwhile, I come home and just sit with this heavy, dull feeling in my chest that I don’t know what to do with.

I also feel stuck in this weird loop: I crave closeness, but I’m scared to reach out too much because I don’t want to be “too much” for people. So I hold back, then feel lonely, then resent myself for not trying harder, then get even quieter. It’s like I’m slowly training myself to expect nothing from anyone.

Nights are the worst. I scroll, I watch stuff, I stare at the ceiling, and it hits me how no one would really notice if I just withdrew a little more. Not dramatically disappear, just gradually answer slower, cancel plans more often, fade into “oh, we just lost touch.” And the scariest part is how easy that feels.
