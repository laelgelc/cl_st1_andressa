I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding overdramatic, but lately I feel like I’m slowly fading out of my own life.

I’m surrounded by people on paper: coworkers, acquaintances, family chats buzzing on my phone. But none of it feels like it actually touches me. I can go days without anyone asking me a real question about how I’m doing, and when someone does, I hear myself give the same “I’m fine, just tired” answer, because I don’t even know where to start if I tried to be honest.

The worst part is how invisible this kind of loneliness is. From the outside, I probably look okay. I show up to work, reply to messages, post the occasional meme. But then I come home, shut the door, and it’s just this heavy silence. I’ll sit on my bed scrolling, hoping someone will message me first, and when no one does, I tell myself I’m being pathetic and put the phone down. Then I pick it back up again five minutes later.

I’ve noticed I’ve started avoiding people more, even when I’m craving connection. I’ll get invited somewhere and say no because I feel awkward and out of place when I’m actually there. I watch everyone else slip into easy conversation, inside jokes, shared histories, and I feel like I’m just… there, orbiting around the group instead of being part of it.

There’s this constant feeling that I’m “too much” when I open up and “not enough” when I stay quiet. So I end up stuck in the middle, saying nothing real and then feeling resentful that no one really knows me.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for posting this. I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
