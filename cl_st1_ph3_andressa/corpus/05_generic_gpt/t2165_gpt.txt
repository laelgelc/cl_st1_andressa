I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living in a parallel world next to everyone else instead of with them.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I answer messages, I show up when I’m supposed to. People would probably describe me as quiet but normal. But it feels like I’m just acting out some version of myself that other people expect, while the real me is somewhere far away, watching.

I have “friends,” but most of our conversations are surface-level: memes, work complaints, random updates. When I try to steer things deeper, it either gets awkward or the moment passes. I don’t blame them, but it leaves me feeling like no one actually knows me. Not the stuff I’m scared of, or what I’m proud of, or how tired I am of pretending I’m okay.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through my phone, re-reading old chats, hovering over people’s names and then not messaging because I don’t know what I’d even say. I tell myself I’m too tired to talk, but the truth is I’m more afraid of feeling unwanted than I am of being alone.

I don’t think I’m looking for advice. I just wanted to put this somewhere outside my own head, because it’s starting to feel like an echo chamber in here.
