I’ve been feeling this heavy, quiet kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my life on paper. I have people around me, chats going on my phone, group calls from time to time. But it all feels like I’m watching my own life through glass.

When I try to open up to friends, it somehow gets turned into a joke, or they change the topic, or they say something like “same lol” and move on. I don’t blame them exactly, but it makes me feel like I’m too much whenever I’m honest, so I go back to being the “chill” version of myself. Then I end up feeling fake and even more disconnected.

What bothers me most is that I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely understood. I miss that feeling of being able to message someone without overthinking if I’m annoying them. I overanalyze every interaction: how long it takes them to reply, whether I said something weird, whether I’m forcing the conversation. It’s exhausting.

I also notice that when I’m actually with people, I’m already dreading the moment I go home and the silence hits again. Sometimes I drag out errands or sit in my car just to avoid going back to my room. And when I do get home, I just scroll, lurk, and watch other people’s lives instead of living my own.

I don’t really know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere instead of carrying it around in my head all the time.
