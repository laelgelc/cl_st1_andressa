I’m starting to wonder if I’ve just… aged out of belonging.

Most of my connections now feel like weak Wi-Fi. Technically “connected,” but nothing really loads. I have coworkers I chat with, a family I text, a couple of old friends I exchange memes with, but there’s no one I feel I can really call and say, “I’m not okay, can you just stay on the line for a bit?”

I’m in that weird stage where everyone seems to have moved into their “real life” – partners, kids, stable friend groups, weekend plans that are standing traditions. I’m the one who’s always “free whenever,” which somehow translates to never being anyone’s first choice. I get invited sometimes, but more as an extra, not as someone the plan is built around.

The worst part is how invisible it feels. On paper, I look fine. I work, I function, I answer emails, I make small talk. I’ve gotten pretty good at sounding normal. But I’ll come home to an empty place, heat up something frozen, scroll through feeds of other people doing things with other people, and it hits me that no one is thinking, “I wonder how they’re doing tonight.”

I try to reach out. I suggest coffee, send “how are you?” messages, try to be that consistent person. But if I stop initiating, everything just… stops. Conversations die. Plans never happen. It makes me feel like I’m forcing my way into people’s lives instead of existing there naturally.

There’s also this shame that comes with it. I’m not supposed to admit I’m lonely because it sounds like a personal failure, like I’m unlikable or too much or not enough. So I keep it to myself, which obviously doesn’t help, but I don’t know how to explain this without sounding desperate.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for writing this. Maybe just a place to say, out loud (or on screen), that I feel very alone most of the time, and I don’t really see that changing anytime soon.
