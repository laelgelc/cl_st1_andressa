I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from everyone’s life, including my own.

Most days are just… quiet. Not peaceful quiet, but the kind where you realize no one has messaged you all day, and if your phone buzzes it’s probably a notification from some app, not an actual person. I scroll through chats that used to be active and see the last message was months ago, usually from me, trying to keep a conversation alive that clearly died a while back.

I’m surrounded by people technically—neighbors, coworkers, random faces—but I feel like I’m behind glass. I can hear them, see them, even nod and smile at the right times, but there’s no real connection. I don’t know how to move from “hey, how’s it going?” to something that feels like a real friendship. Every attempt feels forced or awkward, and then I replay it in my head later and cringe.

It’s weird how loneliness makes you doubt basic things about yourself. I start wondering if I’m just fundamentally unlikeable, or too boring, or too quiet, or too much. I overthink every message I send, then usually delete it. On the rare occasion I do reach out, I feel like I’m bothering people, like I’m asking for more space in their life than they actually want to give.

Nights are the worst. That’s when it hits me how small my world has become. Just me, my thoughts, my phone, and this heavy feeling that I’m not really part of anyone’s “important people” list. I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe just proof that I still exist somewhere outside my own head.
