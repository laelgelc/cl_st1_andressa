I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too visible at the same time.

Invisible, because no one really checks in. Days go by where the only notifications on my phone are spam emails and random app updates. Too visible, because when I’m around people, I feel painfully aware of how out of place I am, like everyone can see that I don’t quite belong.

I do have people in my life, technically. Co‑workers, a few old friends I text maybe once a month, family chats where we mostly send memes and logistics. But there’s this huge gap between “having people” and “feeling known,” and I’m stuck in that gap. I can talk about work, shows, news, whatever—but the second it gets close to how I actually feel, I freeze up or make a joke.

It’s like I’m always on the edge of the group, listening, nodding, laughing at the right times, but never really letting anyone see the parts of me that hurt or feel ashamed or scared. And then I go home and replay every interaction, convinced I said something weird or boring, and that’s why I don’t get invited next time.

I’m not even sure what I want from posting this. Maybe I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
