I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about TV shows I don’t watch. I say “have a good one” and “see you later” to people I never actually see later. I’m surrounded by others almost every day, but I still feel like there’s this thick glass wall between me and everyone else.

I don’t have anyone I can text something small and stupid to, like “look at this meme” or “you won’t believe what just happened.” I scroll through my contacts and realize most of them are numbers I haven’t used in months or years. When something good happens, I sit with it alone. When something bad happens, same thing. It’s like my life has no witnesses.

I’ve noticed I’m starting to talk less because I feel like I’ve forgotten how to be interesting. When people ask “how are you,” I just say “good, you?” even when my chest feels heavy and my head is buzzing. I can’t imagine actually answering honestly. I don’t even know what I’d say if someone genuinely wanted to know.

Nights are the worst. There’s this silence that feels loud, if that makes sense. I keep wondering if this is just how it’s going to be.
