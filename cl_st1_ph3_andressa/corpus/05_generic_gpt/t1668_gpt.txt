I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to be close to people, and it’s hitting me harder as I get older.

On paper, my life looks fine. I have a job, I talk to coworkers, I can make small talk at parties when I’m forced to go. But everything feels like I’m acting in a play where everyone else got the script years ago and I’m just improvising lines that sound right.

I don’t have anyone I’d call in the middle of the night. No one really knows what’s going on in my head. I’m that “reliable” person others come to for help or advice, but when I need something, I stare at my contacts list and realize there’s no one I feel comfortable bothering. It’s like I’ve built this image of being fine and now I’m trapped behind it.

Most evenings I just go home, scroll, watch something, and try not to think too much. But the silence eventually gets loud. I’ll see people posting about their group chats, best friends, partners, inside jokes, and I feel like I’m watching life through glass. I’m there, but not really in it.

I don’t know if I’m the problem—too quiet, too guarded, too “weird”—or if I just missed the window where everyone else formed their circles. I keep telling myself I’ll try harder “next week,” but weeks keep passing and nothing changes.

It’s a strange feeling to be surrounded by people and still feel like nobody actually sees you.
