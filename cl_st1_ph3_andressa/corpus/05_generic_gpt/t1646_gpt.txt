I’m starting to realize I’m not “busy and independent,” I’m just alone all the time. I go to work, come home, scroll, eat, sleep. I might talk to coworkers, but no one really knows me. Weekends are the worst—two empty days that feel like a test I keep failing.

I’ve tried joining groups, messaging old friends, even dating apps. It all feels like I’m auditioning for a role I never get. People seem to already have their circles; I’m the extra standing off to the side.

What bothers me most is how invisible it feels. No one texts first. If I disappeared from social media, I don’t know who would notice. I’m not in crisis, just stuck in this dull, heavy loneliness that never really goes away.
