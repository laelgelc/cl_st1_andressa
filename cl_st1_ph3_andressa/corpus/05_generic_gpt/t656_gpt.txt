I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by others on paper. I have coworkers, acquaintances, family chats that light up on holidays. But I feel like I’m behind a glass wall. I can see everyone, I can even talk and joke and “function,” but nothing actually reaches me. It all feels like I’m playing a character that people tolerate, not a person they’d choose if they really knew me.

I don’t have anyone I can message when something small but important happens. No one I can send a dumb meme to at 1 a.m. without overthinking whether I’m being annoying. When something goes wrong, I just… sit with it. Not because I’m strong or independent, but because there’s literally no one I feel close enough to bother.

When I try to open up, I feel like I’m oversharing or being dramatic. People say things like, “You just need to get out more” or “Everyone feels lonely sometimes,” and I shut down again. It’s like there’s no safe amount of honesty. Too shallow and I stay invisible, too real and I scare people off.

I keep wondering what’s wrong with me that other people seem to naturally form connections and I just can’t. I’m not interesting enough? Not funny enough? Too quiet? Too much? I replay every interaction in my head and find new reasons why someone probably doesn’t actually like me.

Most days I just go through the motions: work, home, screen, sleep. I talk more to podcasts and YouTube videos than to actual people. It’s starting to feel less like a phase and more like the permanent shape of my life.
