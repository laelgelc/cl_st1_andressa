I’ve started to realize that I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s eating through everything else.

On paper, my life looks fine. I have a job, coworkers I can chat with, a few people I’d call acquaintances. I answer “not much, you?” in group chats, I like memes, I show up to stuff when invited. But it all feels like I’m playing a role where everyone else got the script years ago and I’m still improvising badly.

What I’m really missing is that feeling of being known. Not just “oh, you like this band” known, but the kind of knowing where someone can tell from one text that you’re not okay and actually cares enough to ask. I don’t have that. I’m always the one who reaches out first, and if I stop, things just go quiet. It’s like I only exist in other people’s lives when I’m the one knocking on the door.

I’ve tried to fix it. Joined hobby groups, went to meetups, tried to open up more. I can get through small talk, even have what looks like a decent conversation, but it never seems to go deeper. People already have their circles and I’m this extra satellite orbiting around them, close enough to see the warmth but not close enough to feel it.

What makes it worse is how it messes with how I see myself. I start wondering what’s wrong with me that no one seems to choose me as their “person.” I tell myself I’m being dramatic, that everyone feels lonely sometimes, but the feeling doesn’t care about logic. It’s there when I wake up, when I go to bed, when I scroll through my phone pretending I’m just “browsing” and not actually hoping someone, anyone, will message first.
