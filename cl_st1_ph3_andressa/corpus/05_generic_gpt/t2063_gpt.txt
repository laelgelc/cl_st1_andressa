I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly dissolving from the edges inward.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my rent, I respond to emails, I say “good, you?” when people ask how I am. But there’s this constant sense that I’m just acting out the role of a person, not actually being one. Like I’m an understudy in someone else’s life.

Most days I barely talk to anyone outside of work. The conversations I do have are functional: “Did you see that email?” “What’s the status on this?” “Have a good weekend.” It’s like living in a world made of small talk. When I get home, the silence is almost physical. I put on YouTube or a podcast just to have voices around, but it’s not the same as someone actually knowing me.

I scroll through chats and old messages and see a graveyard of “we should hang out sometime” that never turned into anything. I’ve had friendships, but they seem to fade quietly, like everyone else is slowly moving on to the next stage of life and I missed the train without realizing it. I’m always “nice,” always “easy to talk to,” but somehow never the person anyone thinks of first, or even second.

It messes with my sense of self. When no one really sees you, it’s hard to feel like there’s anything there worth seeing. I catch myself editing my personality depending on who I’m around, trying to be more interesting, more funny, more something. Then I go home and feel like I don’t actually know who I am when no one’s watching.

What scares me is how normal this has started to feel. The empty weekends, the solo meals, the pretending I like being “independent” when really I just don’t know how to not be alone anymore. I keep thinking I should do something about it—join a group, reach out to old friends, whatever—but the weight of the loneliness itself makes it hard to move. So I just… stay here, stuck in this loop, watching my own life from the outside.
