I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly fading out of other people’s lives while still being physically present.

I have “people” around me — coworkers, family chats, the occasional group hangout — but I don’t feel *with* any of them. Conversations feel like I’m reading lines from a script I barely remember. I laugh in the right places, ask the usual questions, but it’s like I’m watching myself from the outside. When it’s over, no one really knows anything more about me than they did before, and I don’t really know them either.

I try to reach out sometimes. I’ll type a long message and then delete half of it because I don’t want to be “too much.” I end up sending something short and casual, and when the reply is just as shallow, I feel stupid for expecting more. Then I pull back again and tell myself I’m just busy, or tired, or whatever excuse sounds least pathetic.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. Coming home, putting my phone face down, sitting in silence because there’s no one to call and nowhere I’m expected to be. I used to mind the emptiness. Now it just feels like the default setting, and I’m not sure how to change it, or if I even remember how.
