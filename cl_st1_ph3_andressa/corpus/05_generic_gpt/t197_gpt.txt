I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here because it feels safer to say it to strangers.

Lately I’ve had this weird double feeling: I’m “fine” on paper, but I feel completely disconnected from everyone. I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, acquaintances, a few old friends I text every now and then, family chats that light up my phone. But I don’t feel like I actually exist in anyone’s mind when I’m not physically in front of them. Like I’m just background noise.

I’ll have these moments where I’m in a room full of people, everyone talking and laughing, and I’m there, nodding and smiling at the right times, but inside it’s like I’m standing behind glass. I see everyone, but I can’t quite reach them. The conversation never goes past the surface. I leave and think, “No one really knows me. And I didn’t really know them either.”

What messes with me is that I don’t even know how to fix it. I try to open up a little more, ask deeper questions, be more present, but it feels like people get uncomfortable if things stop being light and easy. Or they’re “so busy” and I end up feeling like I’m asking too much just by wanting real connection. So I back off again, then feel even more alone.

I also catch myself editing everything I say, even in texts. Deleting and rewriting simple messages because I’m scared of sounding needy or weird. Then I send something super neutral and get a neutral reply back, and the distance just stays there. It’s like I’m helping to build the wall that’s suffocating me.

I’m not sure what I’m hoping for with this post. I don’t have some big insight or plan. I just needed to say out loud that I’m lonely in a way that doesn’t have anything to do with how many people I know. It’s more like I’m missing some invisible bridge that everyone else seems to walk across without thinking.
