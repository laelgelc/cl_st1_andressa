I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

Lately it feels like I’m surrounded by people but not actually connected to anyone. I go to work, make small talk, answer “how’s it going?” with “good, you?” and then go home and just… vanish. If I stopped showing up, I’m pretty sure people would notice for a week or two and then I’d just become a “remember that person?” footnote.

What messes with my head is that on paper I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family group chats that light up with memes. But none of it feels like it touches the part of me that’s actually lonely. It’s like everyone knows the surface version of me and the rest is kept behind glass.

I’ve also realized I’ve forgotten how to be close to people. When someone shows interest in being my friend, I freeze. I’m scared I’ll be too much, or too boring, or that they’ll eventually see who I really am and quietly back away. So I stay distant, then feel lonely, then hate myself for it. It’s a loop I can’t seem to break.

Nights are the worst. That’s when it hits that no one is really “my person” and I’m not anyone’s either. Just kind of drifting, filling time with screens and noise so I don’t have to sit in the silence and feel how empty everything actually is.
