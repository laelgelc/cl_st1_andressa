I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to other people instead of with them.

I have coworkers, classmates, acquaintances. I can make small talk, I can crack jokes, I can even seem “social” from the outside. But every interaction feels like I’m playing some character who’s loosely based on me. When it’s over and I go home, it’s like I vanish. No one checks in, my phone is mostly silent unless I’m the one starting the conversation, and even then it feels like I’m intruding.

What bothers me most is the sense that I’m forgettable. People will say “we should hang out sometime” and then never follow up. Or I’ll be invited to a group thing once and never again. I replay everything I said, wondering what I did wrong, if I was too quiet, too awkward, too boring. I’m constantly half-convinced that everyone secretly tolerates me instead of actually liking me.

I try to put myself out there: joining servers, going to meetups, messaging people first. It always fizzles. There’s never that mutual “oh, this person gets me” feeling. I don’t even know what it would look like to feel genuinely known by someone anymore.

Most days I just distract myself with work, internet, and sleep. The loneliness isn’t always loud; sometimes it’s just this dull, constant ache in the background. Like this is how it’s going to be, and I’m slowly getting used to it in a way that scares me.
