I don’t really know how to explain this feeling without sounding dramatic, but lately it’s like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I make small talk at work. People would probably describe me as quiet but normal. But I go days without having a real conversation with anyone, the kind where you’re actually known. Most of my interactions are surface-level: “How was your weekend?” “Good, yours?” and that’s it. I can’t remember the last time someone asked me how I really am and actually wanted to hear the answer.

What bothers me is how invisible I feel. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like a ghost. I listen, I nod, I laugh in the right places, but it’s like I’m watching everyone else participate in something I’m not invited to. I scroll through my phone pretending I’m busy so I don’t look as out of place as I feel.

I’ve tried reaching out here and there, but I always end up feeling like I’m intruding or being too much. I overthink every message, every pause, every time someone leaves me on read. Eventually I just stop trying and tell myself I prefer being alone, even though I know that’s a lie.

I don’t even know exactly what I’m looking for by writing this. Maybe I just wanted to put it into words somewhere, so it’s not only in my head. Right now it just feels like I’m drifting, and no one has noticed I’ve gone missing.
