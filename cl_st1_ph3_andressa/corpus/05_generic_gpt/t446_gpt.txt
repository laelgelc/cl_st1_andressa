I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and exposed lately. Like I’m walking around with this huge neon sign over my head that says “alone,” but somehow no one seems to notice.

On paper, I’m not isolated. I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family group chats that light up with memes and random updates. But the actual feeling of being understood, of being really seen, has been missing for so long that I’m starting to wonder if I’ve forgotten how to connect at all.

I go through these phases where I try to “fix” it. I say yes to more invitations, I push myself to talk more, I join new group chats, Discord servers, whatever. And it always ends up the same way: I’m there, physically or virtually, but I still feel like I’m standing behind glass. Everyone else seems to slip into conversations so easily, share inside jokes, talk about plans. I just… float around the edges, trying not to sound awkward, trying not to say something that makes the silence heavier.

What messes with me the most is how ordinary everything looks from the outside. I go to work, I run errands, I answer “I’m fine” when people ask how I am. Nobody sees the way the evenings stretch out, how the quiet in my room feels almost loud, how I sometimes keep the TV or a podcast running just so it doesn’t feel like I’ve disappeared.

I don’t know if I’m lonely because I’ve pulled away from people, or if I pulled away because I was already lonely. It’s starting to feel less like a phase and more like a permanent state I’m just supposed to get used to.
