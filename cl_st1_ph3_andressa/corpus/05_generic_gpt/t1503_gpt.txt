I’ve been feeling like I’m fading out of everyone’s peripheral vision.

I’m not completely alone on paper. I have coworkers, some old friends I message every now and then, family chats that light up on holidays. But it all feels like watching life through glass. I’m there, but not really in it.

I keep trying to “fix” it in all the standard ways—joining group chats, going to meetups, saying yes more often—but I always end up standing on the edge of conversations, smiling, nodding, and leaving with that same hollow feeling in my chest. People are kind enough, but it never seems to go any deeper.

What really gets to me is how invisible this feels. No one sees it as a problem because I look “fine.” I show up, I work, I reply to messages. And then I go home, sit in the quiet, and wonder if this is just how it’s going to be.
