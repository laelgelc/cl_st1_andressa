I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, I just know how to perform being “fine.”

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I joke with. Group chats that light up, but no one I’d call if something really went wrong. I scroll through my contacts and feel this weird wall between me and every name. It’s like I’m an extra in my own life.

When I do try to open up, it feels forced and awkward, like I’m oversharing or being dramatic. Then I replay the conversation in my head for days and decide it’s safer to stay surface-level. I show up, smile, make small talk, then go home and feel this heavy emptiness.

What really bothers me is how invisible the loneliness is. From the outside, I probably look “social enough.” But inside, it feels like I’m slowly disappearing and no one’s noticing, including me.
