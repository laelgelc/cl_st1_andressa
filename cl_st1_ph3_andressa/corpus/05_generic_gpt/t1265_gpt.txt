I don’t really know where to put this, so I’m dumping it here.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m just orbiting other people’s lives instead of actually being in anyone’s inner circle. I have “people” around me – coworkers, some old classmates I still have on social media, family chats that light up occasionally – but I don’t feel like I belong to any of it. It all feels like I’m watching through glass.

I’ve noticed how quiet my phone is. Days go by without a message that isn’t a notification, spam, or something purely practical. When it does buzz, I get this quick hit of hope that it’s someone actually wanting to talk to me, and then it’s… not. It’s embarrassing how often I still check it anyway.

When I’m around others, I can play the role. I can make small talk, crack jokes, ask questions so they keep talking and I don’t have to. People probably think I’m “fine” or even social. But as soon as I’m home, the silence hits so hard it almost feels physical. I put on YouTube or a podcast just to have voices in the background, like I’m trying to trick my brain into thinking I’m not completely alone.

What bothers me is how replaceable I feel. Like if I stopped showing up, most people would just… adjust. Maybe a “hey, haven’t seen you in a while” once or twice, and then nothing. I don’t feel like anyone’s first choice. I’m the “if no one else is free” person, or the “oh yeah, we should hang out sometime” that never actually gets scheduled.

I keep thinking it’s my fault. I overthink every interaction – did I talk too much, too little, say something weird? Sometimes I cancel plans because I feel so drained and awkward, and then I hate myself for it because that just makes the loneliness worse. It’s like I’m both craving connection and pushing it away at the same time.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for writing this. Maybe just somewhere to say “I feel invisible” without having to dress it up as a joke.
