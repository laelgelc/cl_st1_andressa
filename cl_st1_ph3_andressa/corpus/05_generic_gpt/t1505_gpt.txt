I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too exposed at the same time, and I don’t really know where else to put it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about weekends and weather. People would probably describe me as polite, maybe even friendly. But it all feels like I’m acting in a play where everyone else got the script and I’m just improvising lines that sound right.

The part that bothers me most is how inconsistent it is. I can be surrounded by people and feel completely alone, and then at home, when I actually am alone, I feel this heavy, dull ache in my chest, like my body is reminding me that something important is missing. I’ll scroll through chats, see conversations I never answered or ones that just slowly faded, and I can’t tell if I pushed people away or if they just quietly stopped trying.

I don’t think I’m horrible to be around, but I also don’t feel like anyone’s first choice. I’m the “if no one else can make it, I’ll ask them” person. I’m the one people vent to once in a while, but no one really checks in on. If I disappeared from social media tomorrow, I honestly don’t know who would notice beyond a “huh, haven’t seen them post in a while” thought that passes in a second.

The worst part is how it messes with how I see myself. I start wondering if I’m boring, or emotionally flat, or just fundamentally uninteresting. I rehearse conversations in my head before I send a text, then delete it because it suddenly feels needy or awkward. Then I sit there staring at my phone, feeling stupid for wanting connection so badly.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just wanted to admit out loud that I’m lonely instead of pretending I’m just “busy” or “tired” all the time.
