I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I talk to coworkers, I respond to family texts, I sometimes go out for a drink with people from work. But none of it feels like it touches me. It’s like I’m acting out the role of a normal person while the real me is locked in a room somewhere behind my eyes.

I don’t have anyone I can message when something small but important happens. No one who knows the ongoing “story” of my life. Every conversation I have feels like starting from scratch: shallow, surface-level, context-free. I miss the feeling of being known so much that it physically hurts sometimes.

I’ve tried to fix it. I’ve joined groups, gone to meetups, tried hobbies, even forced myself to be “more open.” People seem to like me well enough, but it never goes beyond a certain point. They already have their people, their history, their inside jokes. I’m always the extra, the temporary addition.

What scares me is how used to it I’m becoming. I can go whole weekends barely speaking out loud, just scrolling or watching shows, and it feels normal now. Then some random moment will hit me—seeing a group laughing together, or hearing someone talk about “my best friend”—and it’s like my chest caves in.

I don’t know how to bridge the gap between being around people and actually mattering to anyone.
