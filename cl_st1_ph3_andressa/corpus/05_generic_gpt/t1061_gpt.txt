I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s getting harder to pretend otherwise.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I respond to messages, I show up when I’m supposed to. But everything feels like I’m acting out a version of myself that other people might tolerate, not who I actually am. I can make small talk, but as soon as the conversation drifts toward anything real, I freeze up or deflect with jokes. Then I go home and replay every interaction, picking apart what I said and assuming that’s why no one gets closer.

I watch other people form friendships almost accidentally—inside jokes, spontaneous plans, group photos—and it feels like I’m pressing my face against the glass, looking in from outside. Even when I’m physically with others, I feel like an extra in their lives, not a character in the story.

The worst part is how tired I am of my own company and still not knowing how to be anyone else.
