I’ve been feeling like a ghost in my own life lately.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, acquaintances, family chats that light up my phone. But I don’t feel like I’m actually *with* anyone. It’s like I’m always one layer of glass away from real connection. I show up, I talk, I laugh at the right times, but I leave every interaction feeling weirdly hollow and drained, like I’ve just performed a role instead of been a person.

What really messes with me is the sense that no one actually knows me. Not the heavier thoughts, not the random things I care deeply about, not the parts I’m ashamed of. Everything feels surface-level. I don’t even know how to break past that anymore. Whenever I try to open up, I hear myself and it sounds awkward, too intense, or just… wrong for the moment. So I pull back. Then I complain to myself that no one understands me. It’s a loop I can’t seem to get out of.

Nights are the worst. I can be exhausted and still not want to go to bed, because as soon as the lights are off and it’s just me, that emptiness gets louder. I scroll mindlessly, half hoping someone will message me first, even though I barely talk to anyone. I keep thinking, “If I disappeared, who would actually notice in a meaningful way?” People might be sad, sure, but would it really change anything for them?

I’ve realized I’m scared of being seen and scared of not being seen at the same time. I want someone to look at me and say, “I get it,” but I also feel like if they really saw everything, they’d back away. So I stay in this middle space: not fully alone, not fully connected, just existing in this vague, numb in-between.

I don’t really know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe just to put it somewhere outside my head for once.
