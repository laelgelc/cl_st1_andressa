I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m disappearing from my own life.

I’m surrounded by people on paper—coworkers, acquaintances, a family group chat that sends memes—but I don’t feel like I actually exist to anyone in a way that matters. Conversations feel like small talk on repeat. I can be standing in a room full of people and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everything instead of being part of it.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like everyone suggests. I go to events, I ask people questions, I try to be interested and present. But it rarely goes deeper than surface-level stuff. People already seem to have their circles, their “person,” their group chats full of inside jokes. I’m the extra, the one who gets invited as an afterthought, or not at all.

What messes with my head the most is that I can’t tell if it’s something about me, or if this is just how adulthood works for some people. I start overanalyzing everything I say, every silence, every time someone doesn’t text back. Then I get tired of trying, pull back, and the loneliness just gets heavier.

I keep thinking there must be a version of my life where I feel genuinely seen and wanted. Right now it just feels like I’m orbiting everyone else’s lives, but never landing anywhere.
