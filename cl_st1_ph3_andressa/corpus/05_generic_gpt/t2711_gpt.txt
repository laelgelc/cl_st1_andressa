I’m starting to feel like I’ve outgrown my own life, but there’s nowhere else to go.

On paper, I’m “fine.” Job, roof over my head, a couple of people I can text. But it all feels like I’m living behind glass. I’m physically present in conversations, but mentally I’m a few steps back, watching myself nod and smile and say the right things. Nobody really knows me, and I’m not even sure I’d know how to let them.

I keep thinking about how long it’s been since someone has looked genuinely happy to see me. Not polite-happy, not “oh hey, you made it,” but that unfiltered warmth you see in movies or between people who actually matter to each other. I can’t remember the last time I felt like I was anyone’s first choice.

I try to put myself out there—meetups, casual chats with coworkers, even messaging old friends—but it always fizzles out. People are busy, conversations die, plans fall through. It’s hard not to take it personally when you’re already convinced you’re forgettable. I feel like background noise in my own relationships.

The worst part is the quiet moments at night. I lie there scrolling, half-hoping someone will message me first, half-knowing they won’t. I keep thinking, “If I disappeared, who would actually notice, and how long would it take?” And I don’t have a good answer.

I don’t really know what I’m looking for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to put into words that I feel profoundly alone, even when I’m surrounded by people.
