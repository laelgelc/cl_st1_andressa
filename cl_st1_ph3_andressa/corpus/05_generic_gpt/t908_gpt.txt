I’ve been feeling this weird kind of invisible lately. I talk to people every day—coworkers, cashiers, neighbors—but it never feels like anyone actually knows me. Conversations stay on safe topics: weather, deadlines, “How was your weekend?” where no one really wants the real answer.

What bothers me is how practiced I’ve become at pretending I’m fine. I’ve got my “I’m good, you?” smile down to muscle memory. Meanwhile, I go home to silence and scroll through chats that are mostly dead, rereading old conversations with people I’m not close to anymore.

I keep wondering if there’s something fundamentally off about me that people sense and just…keep a distance from. I try to initiate plans sometimes, but when you’re always the one asking, it starts feeling pathetic. And when plans fall through, it hits harder than it probably should.

There are days where I feel like if I quietly disappeared from everyone’s life, it would take a long time before anyone really noticed. Not in a dramatic way—just in that slow fade-out kind of way, like a background character written out of a show without explanation. And I don’t know how to change that, or if I even can.
