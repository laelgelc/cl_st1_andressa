I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore. I’m surrounded by coworkers, neighbors, random faces on public transport, but it all feels like watching life through a glass wall. I can talk about work, weather, TV shows, but the moment the conversation should go deeper, my mind just… blanks. Or I overthink every word and end up saying nothing.

I scroll through my phone at night pretending I’m “busy,” but really I’m just avoiding the feeling that no one is thinking of me. Messages are almost always ones I initiate, and even then they fizzle out fast. I tell myself everyone else is just “busy,” but that excuse is wearing thin.

What scares me most is how normal this is starting to feel. The silence, the empty weekends, the pretending I prefer being alone. I don’t know if I’m lonely because I’ve withdrawn, or if I withdrew because I was lonely. Probably both.
