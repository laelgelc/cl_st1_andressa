I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, or if I ever did.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I joke with in group chats. But it all feels like I’m playing a role. I can be “funny” or “helpful” or “the good listener,” but no one really knows me. When the conversation ends, I’m left with this weird emptiness, like I was never actually there.

What bothers me is how invisible this kind of loneliness is. From the outside my life looks normal: job, responsibilities, a few social events here and there. Inside, it feels like I’m walking through a world made of glass—close to everyone, but separated by something I can’t break.

I’ve tried “putting myself out there,” going to events, messaging people more. It always ends in small talk that fizzles out, or friendships that never move past the surface. I don’t even know what I’d say if someone actually asked how I really am.

Sometimes I wonder if I’ve already missed the window where people form those deep, effortless friendships, and now I’m just orbiting everyone else’s lives from the outside.
