I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from everyone’s life, including my own.

I’m in that weird place where I technically have “people” around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family I text now and then—but I don’t feel genuinely connected to anyone. I can go days without speaking out loud outside of work. When I do talk, it’s surface-level stuff: “How was your weekend?” “Did you see that show?” It all feels like I’m playing a character that’s loosely based on me, but not actually me.

I’ve tried to be more social. I’ve said yes to invitations, joined group chats, even went to a couple of meetups. But I always end up feeling like an outsider watching everyone else already have their in-jokes and shared histories. I stand there, smiling, nodding, hoping something I say will land, and then I go home and replay every awkward moment in my head until I can’t sleep.

There’s this particular kind of loneliness that hits when you’re physically around people but mentally completely alone. Like you could vanish mid-conversation and no one would notice until it became inconvenient. I feel that a lot. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m on the other side of thick glass.

What bothers me most is that I’m starting to doubt my own perception of myself because there’s no one who really knows me well enough to reflect anything back. I don’t know if I’m kind, annoying, interesting, boring, or just… there. It’s like my identity is fading because it’s not anchored in any real, close connection.

I keep thinking that maybe I missed some crucial stage where people learned how to form deep friendships or relationships, and now I’m just permanently behind, watching everyone else move forward while I stay stuck in the same place, feeling more and more invisible.
