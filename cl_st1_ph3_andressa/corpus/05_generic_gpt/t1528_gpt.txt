I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m drifting further away from everyone while technically being “around people” all the time.

On paper, I’m not isolated. I have coworkers, a few old friends I still text sometimes, family chats that light up with memes and random updates. But I feel like a ghost moving through all of it. I show up, I respond, I laugh at the right times, and then I go home and it’s like I never really existed in those spaces.

What scares me is how used to it I’m getting. I used to feel this sharp, painful loneliness, like something was missing and I needed to fix it. Now it’s more like background noise. I scroll, I watch shows, I do my routines, and there’s just this quiet, constant sense that no one really knows me, and I’m not sure I know how to let them.

I’ve noticed I edit myself in every interaction. I keep things light, avoid saying anything that might seem “too much.” I ask people questions about their lives and let them talk, because it keeps the spotlight off me. Then I go home and feel weirdly resentful that no one asks deeper questions, even though I purposely make it hard for them to see anything real.

I don’t think I’ve had a truly honest, unfiltered conversation in a long time. The kind where you say the embarrassing, messy stuff without worrying how it lands. I don’t even know who I’d have that with anymore.

It feels like I’m slowly fading into this version of myself that’s “fine,” “busy,” “doing okay,” and I’m starting to worry that one day that’ll be the only version left, and no one will ever notice there was anything underneath it.
