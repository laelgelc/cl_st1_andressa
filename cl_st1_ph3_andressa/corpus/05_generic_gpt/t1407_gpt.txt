I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness that doesn’t always make sense from the outside.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers I chat with, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family that checks in. But it all feels like background noise, like people talking in another room with the door half closed. I’m there, but not really there.

I keep noticing how often I censor myself. I don’t say the thing I actually think, I say the thing that keeps the conversation light and safe. Then I go home and replay all the moments where I could’ve been more honest or vulnerable, but wasn’t, and I feel like I’m the one building the wall that’s trapping me.

I scroll through social media and see people posting pictures with “their people” and I can’t tell if that’s real or just a highlight reel, but either way it makes me so painfully aware that I don’t have anyone I’d call “my person.” There’s no one I’d text immediately if something good or bad happened. I just sort of… sit with it.

I’ve also noticed I’ve started avoiding invitations, even small ones. I tell myself I’m tired or busy, but the truth is I’m scared of feeling lonely while I’m with other people. That specific feeling is worse than being by myself.

I don’t really know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once, instead of pretending I’m fine all the time.
