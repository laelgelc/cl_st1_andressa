I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of inside it.

On paper things are “fine.” I have a job, coworkers who are polite, a family that isn’t abusive, a couple of old friends I text with every now and then. But I feel like a ghost around everyone. I show up, I say the right things, I make small jokes, and then I go home and it’s just silence. Not peaceful silence, more like the sound of a TV that’s been unplugged.

What’s messing with me is that I’m not completely isolated, but I still feel totally alone. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else. They’re laughing, talking about their weekends, their partners, their plans. I’m standing there trying to figure out when to chime in so I don’t look weird, and when I do, it feels forced and out of sync.

I miss having someone who actually knows me in the present tense. Not just “Oh yeah, we went to school together” or “We used to hang out a lot,” but someone who knows what my days look like now, what I’m worried about, the stupid little things that made me laugh this week. I miss being able to send a random message without overthinking whether I’m being annoying.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like everyone says. Joined a couple of groups, tried talking more at work, even downloaded some apps. It always feels like I’m auditioning for a role I never quite get. People already have their circles. I’m the extra chair at the table that no one really needs but no one moves, either.

I don’t even know what I want exactly. Just to feel like I belong somewhere that isn’t purely out of obligation or convenience. Right now it just feels like I’m filling time.
