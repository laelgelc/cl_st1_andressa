I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too exposed at the same time, and I don’t really know how to explain it to people in my life without sounding dramatic.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, a few people I can text if I need something. But it feels like there’s this glass wall between me and everyone else. I can see people laughing, connecting, building memories together, and I’m just… there. Present, but not actually part of anything.

I’ve noticed that most of my conversations are surface-level. Work stuff, memes, “how was your weekend?” kind of talk. I can’t remember the last time I felt like I could say what I really think or feel without worrying I’d make things awkward or be “too much.” So I keep it all inside. Then I feel even more disconnected because no one really knows me, but it’s also my fault for hiding.

Sometimes I scroll through my messages and realize I’m almost always the one reaching out. If I stop, things just go quiet. It makes me wonder if I actually matter to anyone or if I’m just a convenient person to respond to when they’re bored.

Nights are the worst. I’ll be lying in bed, phone next to me, wanting to talk to someone but not knowing who wouldn’t find it annoying. I replay conversations in my head and overanalyze every word, like I’m trying to find proof that I’m not as unwanted as I feel. It’s exhausting.

I don’t know what I’m looking for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
