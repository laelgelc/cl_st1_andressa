I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, or if I ever did.

I have acquaintances: people I chat with at work, classmates from years ago I still follow on social media, neighbors I nod to. But there’s no one I’d feel comfortable texting at 2 a.m. if something really bad happened. I don’t think anyone would pick me as their “person” either.

I keep trying the usual advice—join groups, say yes to invitations, be open—but it all feels like I’m just passing through other people’s lives as background noise. They already have their circles, their shared histories, their inside jokes. I’m the polite extra who laughs along and then goes home alone.

The worst part is how this makes me doubt my own worth. I start wondering what people see (or don’t see) when they look at me. Am I boring? Too quiet? Too awkward? Or just…forgettable?

I’m tired of pretending it doesn’t bother me. I’m tired of saying “I’m fine” when I’m not. I just want to know what it feels like to be genuinely missed by someone when I’m not around.
