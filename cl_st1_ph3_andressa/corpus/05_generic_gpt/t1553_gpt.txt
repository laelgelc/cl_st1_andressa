I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

I feel like I’ve quietly slipped out of everyone’s life and no one noticed. Not in a dramatic way, just… gradually. Group chats that used to be active for me are now just muted notifications I’m not part of. People still post pictures of parties, trips, game nights, whatever, and I’m not angry I wasn’t invited, I just feel weirdly invisible. Like at some point I faded from “friend” to “someone we used to know” and there was no moment where it happened.

What messes with my head is that I’m not completely isolated. I have coworkers, acquaintances, people I can text about random stuff. But it all feels surface-level. I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching myself pretend to be fine. I laugh at the right moments, ask the right questions, and then go home and realize no one actually knows what’s going on with me.

I’ve tried to “fix” it. Joined a couple of online communities, went to a few meetups. I can handle the small talk, but there’s this wall I can’t get past. I don’t know how to turn “we chat sometimes” into “you’re someone I could call if I was really falling apart.” And I’m scared of coming off as too intense or needy, so I hold back, which just keeps everything shallow.

It also feels like everyone else has moved on to the next stage of life—relationships, kids, stable friend groups—and I’m stuck in this weird in-between, like a background character who never got written into the main storyline. I scroll a lot, lurk a lot, read other people saying they’re lonely, and it’s comforting in theory, but when I close the app, it’s just me again in a quiet room.

I don’t even know what I’m expecting by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
