I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… background noise.

I talk to people every day – coworkers, the barista, neighbors – but it all feels like I’m playing a role. I can be funny, polite, “fine,” but the second I’m alone again it’s like someone turns the sound off. No one actually knows what my days look like, what I think about, what I’m scared of. If I disappeared from my usual places, I’m not sure anyone would notice for a while, and that thought sticks with me more than I want to admit.

I scroll through chats where I’m the one who always reaches out first, then close the app because I’m tired of feeling like I’m asking to be remembered. I’m not even sure what I’d say if someone genuinely asked how I was. “Lonely” feels too big and too vague at the same time.

What makes it worse is that I can remember times when I did have real connections, when I could send a stupid meme at 2 a.m. and know someone would get it. Now I mostly just watch other people live those lives through their posts and stories.

I’m not sure if I’m isolating myself or if I’ve just become easy to forget. Probably both. I just needed to put this somewhere that doesn’t immediately vanish into small talk.
