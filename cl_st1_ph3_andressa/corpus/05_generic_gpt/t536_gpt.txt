I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, and it’s starting to scare me.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, people I can technically message if I need something. But most days feel like I’m just drifting around in my own little bubble, watching everyone else live in some parallel universe where friendships and relationships are just…normal.

I scroll through chats and see conversations that ended months ago with me being the last one to reply with something awkward. I overthink every message I send, then get exhausted and don’t send anything at all. Then I feel rejected when nobody reaches out, even though I’m the one who disappears.

The worst part is how invisible I feel in real life. I go to work, I do what I’m supposed to do, I make small talk about weather and deadlines. But nobody really knows me. I don’t even know how I’d let them, because I’ve gotten so used to keeping everything surface-level that being honest feels like I’m breaking some unspoken rule.

Nights are the hardest. The silence feels heavy, like it’s pressing on my chest. I keep thinking, “This can’t be all there is,” but I don’t know what to do differently. I’m tired of feeling like a background character in my own life, but I don’t see a clear way out of this pattern.
