I’m starting to realize that I feel lonelier *around* people than when I’m physically alone.

I have friends, technically. Group chats, occasional invites, coworkers who ask about my weekend. But I always feel like an extra in other people’s lives. I’m there to fill space, not because anyone actually needs me there. If I stopped replying, I’m not sure how long it would take for anyone to really notice.

What gets me is how invisible the loneliness is. From the outside, I look “fine”: job, hobbies, social media presence. Inside, it feels like I’m behind glass. I can see everyone else connecting, joking, forming those easy, natural bonds, and I can’t figure out what I’m doing wrong. I don’t think I’m awful to be around. I listen, I care, I remember details. But the connection never seems to go deeper than surface-level.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through my phone trying to think of someone I could text without it feeling weird or needy. I usually end up locking the screen and just lying there, feeling this heavy, dull ache in my chest.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for anymore—one close friend, a partner, a group, some sense of belonging. I just know that whatever this is right now doesn’t feel sustainable.
