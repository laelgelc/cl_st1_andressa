I’ve been feeling this weird combination of invisible and “too much” at the same time, and I don’t really know where else to say it out loud.

On paper, I’m not isolated. I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text occasionally, family chats that light up my phone. But none of it feels like it reaches me. It’s like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else. I can see people, hear them, even joke around, but it never feels like anyone is actually with me.

I’m always the one who listens, the one who asks follow-up questions, remembers details, checks in. But when I go quiet, nobody really notices. If I stop initiating, things just… stop. I’ve tested it a few times and the silence that follows is honestly worse than any argument could be. It confirms the thing I keep trying to talk myself out of: maybe I just don’t matter that much to anyone.

I’ve tried joining group chats, hobbies, online communities, even going to events alone and forcing myself to talk to people. The surface-level interactions are fine, but they never turn into something deeper. I leave, go home, and the emptiness hits even harder because it proves that “putting myself out there” didn’t magically fix anything.

What really messes with my head is how normal I must look from the outside. I go to work, answer emails, make small talk about TV shows, laugh at the right times. Inside, it feels like I’m walking around with this heavy, constant ache that no one else can see. And I don’t know how to bridge that gap without sounding needy or pathetic.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. I think I just needed to say it somewhere that doesn’t immediately move on to the next notification.
