I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’ve been slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, coworkers I get along with on a surface level, a family that isn’t abusive or anything. I even have a couple of people I could technically text if I really needed something. But I don’t feel actually known by anyone. Not in that “deep, someone sees me” way. Most of my interactions feel like I’m playing a character who’s supposed to be “normal” and “doing okay.”

I’ve noticed how much of my day is just silence. I wake up, go to work, do the small talk thing, come home, and then it’s just me and the noise in my head. I’ll scroll on my phone for hours, not because I’m interested, but because it’s better than sitting in the quiet and realizing how alone I actually am. I’ll see group photos, inside jokes in comment sections, people tagging each other, and it just reminds me how replaceable I feel.

The worst part is that I’m not completely isolated. I do see people. I can sit in a room full of them and still feel like I’m behind a glass wall. I don’t know how to move conversations past the “How are you?” “Good, you?” stage without feeling like I’m burdening someone or oversharing. And when I do try to open up even a little, I end up overanalyzing every word for days afterward.

It’s starting to affect how I see myself. When you go this long without feeling emotionally close to anyone, you start wondering if maybe you’re just not the kind of person people connect with. I can’t tell if I’ve given up trying, or if I’m just tired. Probably both.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
