I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match what my life looks like from the outside. I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, some family I text with—but I don’t feel *known* by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, shows, memes. I can go the whole week without anyone asking how I *really* am, and honestly, I’m not sure they’d want the real answer.

What bothers me most is how replaceable I feel. If I disappeared from our group chats or stopped showing up, I think people would notice for a bit, then adjust. There’s no one whose life I feel woven into in a meaningful way. I’m just… there. Background character energy.

I’ve tried joining things—online communities, hobby groups—but I always feel like I’m intruding on friendships that already exist. I’m friendly, I try to listen, but I never seem to move from “that person who shows up” to “someone people actually think about.”

It’s starting to feel like there’s something fundamentally off about me that other people can sense, like an invisible barrier I can’t figure out how to break.
