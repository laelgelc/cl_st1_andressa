I don’t really know how to describe this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living in a glass box.

From the outside, my life probably looks fine. I go to work, I answer messages, I make small talk about shows and the weather. I even “hang out” sometimes, but it all feels like I’m playing a role someone else wrote for me. I can see other people, hear them, but there’s this invisible layer between us that I can’t break through.

I don’t have anyone I’d call if something really bad happened at 3 a.m. I have people I could text, sure, but no one where I’d feel like I wasn’t burdening them or overstepping. I scroll through my contacts and realize most of the names are people I only exist to in group chats or at work meetings.

What messes with my head is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped showing up, I think people would notice for a bit, maybe ask “hey, where’d you go?” and then just… move on. I’m not the person anyone thinks of first. I’m not the person anyone misses.

I’ve tried to “fix” it: joining clubs, being more social, saying yes more. But the conversations stay shallow. I don’t know how people make that jump from acquaintance to someone you can actually be real with. Everyone already seems to have their circles, their person, their history.

I’m just tired of feeling like an extra in my own life, watching everyone else have connections I can’t seem to reach, no matter how close I stand.
