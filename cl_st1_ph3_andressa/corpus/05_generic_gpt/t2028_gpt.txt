I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

On paper I’m “not alone.” I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family I see on holidays. But I feel like I’m living behind glass. I’m physically present in conversations, I say the right things, I laugh in the right places, but it never feels like anyone is actually connecting with *me*, just with the version of me I’ve learned to perform.

It’s weird because I can be social. I can do small talk, I can go to a party and not seem awkward, I can be “funny” in group chats. And then I go home and feel this heavy emptiness, like I was surrounded by people all day and still somehow starved for actual human contact. It makes me feel broken, like I’m missing some piece that everyone else just naturally has.

I also don’t really know how to let people in without feeling like I’m bothering them. I’ll type out a message like “hey, want to hang out?” and then stare at it until I delete it because I convince myself they’re just being polite when they say we should meet up. I wait for other people to initiate, and when they don’t, I tell myself it’s proof they don’t really care. Then I feel even more isolated, and the cycle repeats.

The worst part is nights. When everything is quiet and there’s nothing to distract me, it hits me how replaceable I feel. Like if I disappeared, people would be sad for a bit, then just… adjust. There isn’t really anyone I feel like I could call at 2 a.m. and say “I’m not okay” without feeling like I’m crossing some invisible line.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to say it somewhere out loud, since I can’t seem to say it to anyone who actually knows me.
