I’m starting to realize that I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m in a soundproof box.

I have coworkers, acquaintances, people I can text memes to, but it all feels like background noise. I go through the motions: small talk in the office, “how was your weekend,” nodding and laughing at the right moments. Then I go home, close the door, and it’s just this heavy quiet that almost has a physical weight.

What bothers me most is how invisible I feel. Not in a dramatic way, just… like if I stopped reaching out, most people wouldn’t notice for a while. I’m “the reliable one,” the listener, the one who remembers birthdays, but I can’t think of many people who actually know what my life feels like from the inside.

I’ve tried to fix it the “normal” ways: joining clubs, saying yes to more invitations, forcing myself to be more open. But the conversations stay on the surface, and I’m starting to feel like there’s something fundamentally off about me that other people can sense.

It’s getting harder to imagine a future where this isn’t just… how it is.
