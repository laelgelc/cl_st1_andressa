It’s weird how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re behind glass.

I have friends, technically. Group chats, occasional hangouts, coworkers I joke with. But it all feels surface level, like I’m playing a role that’s close enough to “me” to pass, but not close enough to feel real. I leave social situations feeling more drained and somehow more alone than before.

What makes it worse is that I can’t seem to bridge the gap. I want deeper connections, but I don’t know how to get there without feeling like I’m oversharing or burdening people. So I stay in this safe, shallow zone and then resent it. It’s like I’m the one locking the door and then complaining I’m stuck inside.

Nights are the hardest. The silence feels heavy, and I end up scrolling endlessly, watching other people’s lives and wondering what switch I missed. I keep thinking, “If people really knew me, maybe they’d leave anyway,” and that thought just kind of sits there.
