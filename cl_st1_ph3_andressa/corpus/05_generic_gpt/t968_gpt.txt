I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know what “having a life” feels like, beyond what I see other people doing.

My days are technically full: work, errands, scrolling, sleep. I talk to coworkers, I answer emails, I nod and laugh at the right times. On paper, I’m “social enough.” But it all feels like I’m playing a background character in everyone else’s story. No one really knows me, and I don’t know how to let them.

I’ve had friendships, but most of them faded in that slow, quiet way where you can’t point to a specific fight or moment. Just fewer messages, longer gaps, until it’s been months and reaching out feels weirdly formal, like contacting an old teacher. I tell myself I should text first, but then I overthink the wording and end up doing nothing. Then I feel hurt that nobody reaches out to me either, even though I’m doing the same thing to them.

The worst part is how loneliness makes me feel unlikeable and then that belief makes me act distant, which probably makes me harder to like. I second-guess every message I send. I reread conversations and convince myself I sounded annoying or needy. I’ll type a long reply and then delete it because “it’s too much.” I end up sending something short and bland, then wonder why the conversation dies.

Nights are the hardest. I lie in bed with this heavy feeling that if I disappeared, my absence would be more of an inconvenience than a loss. People would adjust, fill the space, move on. I know that sounds dramatic, but it’s how it feels.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by posting this. I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head, because carrying it around in silence is starting to feel like the only thing I’m actually committed to.
