I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people in a way that lasts.

I have acquaintances. People I can joke with at work, classmates I can talk to about assignments, neighbors I can nod at. But after the small talk ends, there’s this wall I can’t seem to get past. I don’t know if I’m the one building it or if it’s just always been there.

I watch other people form friendships almost accidentally—inside jokes, group chats, weekend plans that just happen. For me, everything feels forced and awkward, like I’m always a guest in someone else’s life, never part of the main cast. If I stop reaching out, the connection just quietly dies. No one seems to notice the silence except me.

The worst part is how invisible the loneliness is. From the outside, I probably look “fine.” I show up, I smile, I function. But I go home to a quiet place where no one is waiting, and there’s no one I can text about my day without feeling like I’m bothering them or overstepping what the relationship actually is.

I keep wondering what’s wrong with me that I’m this forgettable. I replay conversations, analyze every word I said, trying to figure out if I came off as weird or boring. I tell myself I’m used to it, but it still hits hard when I realize that if I disappeared from most people’s lives, their routines wouldn’t really change.
