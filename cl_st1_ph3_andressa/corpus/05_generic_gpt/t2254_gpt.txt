I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life without sounding dramatic, so I’m putting it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people on paper—coworkers, family I text sometimes, old friends I still follow on social media—but I feel weirdly invisible, like I’m walking through everyone else’s life as an extra. I’m “there,” but not really *in* anything.

The thing that gets to me most is how long it’s been since anyone has really known me in detail. People know surface facts—my job, my hobbies, the usual small talk stuff—but nobody knows what actually keeps me up at night, or what I’m secretly proud of, or what I’m terrified of losing. I don’t even know who I’d choose to tell, if I had to pick.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” more, but it always feels like I’m forcing a version of myself that’s more digestible. I laugh at the right times, ask questions, act interested. People seem to like me well enough, but then they go home to their people, and I go home to silence.

It’s starting to feel less like a phase and more like the default setting of my life.
