I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and “too much” at the same time.

I’m surrounded by people on paper—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats—but it all feels like background noise. I can go days without anyone asking how I really am. When I try to open up, I feel like I’m oversharing or making things awkward, so I shut down again and go back to small talk.

I miss having someone who actually knows my day-to-day life. Not a “how’s work?” check-in, but someone who remembers the small stuff I mentioned last week. I’m tired of being the one who reaches out first, the one who suggests plans, the one who keeps conversations alive.

What messes with my head is that from the outside, I probably look fine. I laugh at jokes, reply in group chats, show up when I’m supposed to. But it feels like I’m acting out a version of myself that’s easier for everyone else to handle, while the real me just sits in the background, getting quieter.
