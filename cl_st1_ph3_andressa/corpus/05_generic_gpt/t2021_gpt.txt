I’m starting to realize that I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that doesn’t seem to go away, no matter how much I try to distract myself.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I talk to coworkers, I reply in group chats. But it all feels like background noise. I don’t feel like there’s anyone who really knows me, or even wants to. I keep conversations surface-level because I’m scared that if I open up, people will get bored or uncomfortable and slowly disappear.

I watch other people make plans so easily. They’ll say “we should hang out” and then it actually happens. When I say it, it feels like I’m imposing, like I’m forcing myself into someone else’s already full life. So I stop asking. Then I resent the silence that follows, even though I created it.

Nights are the worst. The day winds down, the messages slow, and I’m left with this heavy feeling in my chest, scrolling through feeds full of people doing things together. I can go days without hearing my name spoken out loud by another person.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for posting this. I guess I just wanted to say it somewhere, because saying it out loud to anyone in my life feels like admitting I’ve failed at something everyone else figured out years ago.
