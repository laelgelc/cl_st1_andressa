I’ve been feeling this weird, hollow kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into any of the usual categories people talk about. It’s not just “I don’t have friends” or “I’m single.” It’s more like I’m permanently out of sync with everyone around me.

On paper, I’m not isolated. I have coworkers I chat with, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family I see on holidays. But even when I’m physically with people, there’s this invisible glass wall. I can smile, joke, make small talk, but it feels like I’m playing a character that vaguely resembles me, not actually being me.

When I try to be more honest or vulnerable, it usually lands wrong. Either I overshare and the other person gets uncomfortable, or I say things so cautiously that it just sounds like nothing. I walk away replaying every sentence, wondering if I came off as weird or needy. So I default back to being “fine” all the time, and that just makes the distance feel bigger.

The strange part is that I don’t even know what I want from other people exactly. I don’t have a clear picture of “the kind of friend” or “the kind of relationship” I’m missing. It’s more like a constant ache that something’s off, but I can’t quite name it. I scroll social media and see people casually hanging out, sending each other memes, going on trips together, and it all feels like watching a life that’s running parallel to mine but never intersects.

I’ve noticed I’ve started avoiding invitations because they make me anxious. I worry I’ll show up and feel more alone in a group than I would at home. Then I stay home and feel pathetic for not going. It’s like I’m stuck between wanting connection and being terrified I don’t actually know how to do it.

Most days, I just go through my routine: work, commute, eat, phone, sleep. I keep telling myself I’ll “figure it out” eventually, but the older I get, the more it feels like this is just how things are going to be.
