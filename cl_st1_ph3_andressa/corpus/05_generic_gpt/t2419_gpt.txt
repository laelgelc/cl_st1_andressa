I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my real life without sounding dramatic, so I’m dumping it here.

I’m surrounded by people but feel completely disconnected. I have coworkers, some acquaintances, a family that checks in occasionally, but it all feels like I’m playing a role instead of actually being known. I crack jokes, ask about their weekends, nod at the right times. Then I go home and it’s just…silence. Not peaceful silence, more like the sound of nothing echoing.

What gets to me the most is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped showing up, I think people would notice logistically (missed work, unanswered messages), but I don’t think anyone’s day would be emotionally different. No one is waiting for me, specifically. I’m just “someone,” not anyone.

I also feel like I’ve forgotten how to connect. When I’m with people, my brain constantly analyzes: “Am I being weird? Am I talking too much? Not enough? Are they bored?” It’s exhausting. I leave social situations feeling more lonely than before, because I know they only saw this curated, awkward version of me.

I miss the feeling of being known. Like when someone remembers the small, random details you never thought mattered. Or when you can be quiet with someone and it doesn’t feel like a failure. I don’t have that with anyone right now.

Most days I just go through the motions, hoping something will shift, but it never really does.
