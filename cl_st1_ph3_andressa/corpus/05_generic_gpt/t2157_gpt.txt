I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to my own life instead of inside it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about the weather or some TV show I don’t actually watch. People would probably describe me as quiet but normal. No one would guess how much of my day is just… emptiness with a face on.

The weirdest part is I’m not completely isolated. I have coworkers, some acquaintances, a family I text here and there. But I don’t feel known by anyone. Conversations stay at the surface. I’m either playing a role (the “reliable one,” the “good listener”) or just nodding along, waiting for it to be over so I can go back to my room and scroll my phone until I’m tired enough to sleep.

I keep thinking about how long it’s been since I felt genuinely close to someone. The kind of close where you can send a stupid meme at 2am and know they’ll get why it’s funny, or call them when you’re falling apart without rehearsing what you’re going to say. I don’t have that. I don’t even know how to get from here to there anymore.

I’ve tried things people always suggest: joined a couple of groups, forced myself to go to social events, tried chatting more. It all feels like speed dating for friendships where I never make it past the introduction round. People already have their circles. I always feel like an extra, never part of the main cast.

Sometimes I wonder if there’s just something off about me that other people can sense and avoid getting close to. Like I’m missing some social ingredient everyone else got by default. I don’t know how much of this is in my head and how much is real, but the end result is the same: I go to bed feeling like I’m the only person in the world who’s this alone, even though logically I know that’s not true.
