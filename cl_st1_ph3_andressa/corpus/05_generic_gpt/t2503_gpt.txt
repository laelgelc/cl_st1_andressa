I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and exposed lately. Invisible in the sense that no one really sees me, but exposed because I’m constantly aware of how alone I am. It’s like walking through a crowded room where everyone’s in their own little bubble, and mine is just…empty.

I have people around me technically—coworkers, acquaintances, family I text sometimes—but it all feels surface-level. I can talk about work, shows, random news, but there’s no one I feel safe being fully honest with. Whenever I try to open up even a little, I second-guess myself: “Is this too much? Am I being dramatic? Are they just being polite?” So I pull back, then feel even more disconnected.

Nights are the worst. I scroll, I lurk, I watch other people joke around and talk about their lives, and it feels like I’m pressing my face up against the glass of everyone else’s existence. I want someone to message just because they were thinking of me, not because I started the conversation first for the tenth time in a row.

What scares me is how normal this has started to feel. I’m getting used to the silence, the one-sided conversations in my head, the sense that if I disappeared, the world would just quietly rearrange itself without leaving a gap.
