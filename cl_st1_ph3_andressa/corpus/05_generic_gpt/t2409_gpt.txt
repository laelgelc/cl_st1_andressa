I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I talk to coworkers, occasionally go to gatherings, answer “how are you?” with “good, you?” like everyone else. But I feel like I’m living behind a sheet of glass. I watch myself interacting, laughing at the right times, saying the right things, and none of it feels like it actually touches me.

I keep realizing that no one really knows me. Not the version that lies awake at 2 a.m. replaying every awkward moment from the last decade, or the version that feels this constant ache in the chest that doesn’t go away even when I’m technically “not alone.” I think I’ve made myself so easygoing and low-maintenance that I’ve become optional in everyone’s life.

I try to reach out sometimes, but it feels like begging for scraps of attention. I’ll type out a long message, then delete half of it because I don’t want to be “too much.” I watch people I know make plans with each other and I’m either an afterthought or not considered at all. It’s like I’m permanently in the outer ring of everyone’s life.

What scares me is how normal this has started to feel. The silence, the weekends where my phone doesn’t light up, the way I automatically assume I’m bothering people if I text first. I don’t know if I’ve isolated myself or if I was just never really wanted in the first place.
