I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it’s like I’m surrounded by people and still completely alone.

I have coworkers, a couple of acquaintances I see every now and then, family chats that light up my phone. On paper, it looks like I’m connected. But most of the time I feel like I’m just acting out a role: “normal person making small talk.” I can do the jokes, the “how was your weekend,” the “we should hang out sometime” that never actually happens. Then I go home, close the door, and it’s just…silence.

What hurts the most is that I don’t feel known by anyone. Not really. People know the surface-level version of me, the one who keeps things light and doesn’t burden anyone. I’ve tried to open up a few times, but the conversation always drifts back to safer topics, or I get brushed off with “everyone feels that way” and it shuts me down. So I stop trying.

I think I’ve also forgotten how to let people get close. I overthink every message, every pause, every time someone takes a little too long to reply. I tell myself I’m fine being independent, but there’s this constant ache for someone I can text without rehearsing what I’m going to say, someone who’d actually notice if I disappeared for a while.

Most days I just go through the motions and hope no one can tell how empty it feels inside.
