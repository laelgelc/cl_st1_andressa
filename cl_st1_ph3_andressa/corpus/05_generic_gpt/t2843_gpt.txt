I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s getting harder to pretend I’m okay with that.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I joke with. Online friends I game with sometimes. But no one who really knows me. No one I’d call if something genuinely bad happened. I keep imagining my phone breaking and realizing there’s no one I’d be desperate to contact.

I’m always “fine” on the outside. I make people laugh, I listen to their problems, I give advice. Then I go home and sit in silence, scrolling through chats that are all surface-level. When I do try to open up, I feel like I’m oversharing or making things awkward, so I backpedal and change the subject.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. I’m getting used to the background ache of being left out, of not being chosen, of watching other people form deeper bonds while I hover on the edge, smiling, acting like that’s where I want to be.
