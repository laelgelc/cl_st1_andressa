I don’t really know where else to put this, so here it goes.

Lately I’ve had this weird combination of being surrounded by people and still feeling completely disconnected. I have coworkers, acquaintances, people I chat with online, but it all feels like I’m just playing a role. I can be funny, helpful, “easy to talk to,” but it never turns into something deeper. As soon as the situation ends, I go back home and it’s just silence.

What messes with me most is that no one really knows me, not in the way I wish someone would. They know the surface-level stuff: my job, my hobbies, the “safe” opinions. But the things that keep me up at night – the fears, the regrets, the weird little thoughts – those stay locked up because I don’t trust that anyone actually wants to hear them. Or maybe I’m afraid they’ll think I’m too much and slowly back away.

I see other people forming friendships that seem so natural. They make plans, they have group chats, they tag each other in things. I’m always the “extra” person, the one who gets invited when someone cancels or when they need to round out the numbers. It’s hard not to take that personally after a while.

Sometimes I wonder if this is just how my life is going to be: present but not really seen, known in fragments but not as a whole person. And I don’t know what to do with that thought.
