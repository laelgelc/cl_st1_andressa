I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about weather and TV shows. People would probably describe me as “nice” or “quiet.” But when I come home, it’s just this heavy silence that feels louder than anything that happened during the day.

I have people I interact with, but I don’t feel like I have people who actually know me. The version of me that exists around others is like a watered-down, censored character. I don’t talk about what actually scares me, or what I really think, because every time I try to open up even a little, it either gets brushed off, joked away, or the conversation awkwardly dies. So I just stop trying.

There’s this weird shame that comes with loneliness. Like I must be fundamentally flawed if everyone else manages to form these effortless connections and I’m stuck on the outside. I watch coworkers talk about weekend plans, group chats, partners, and it feels like I’m looking through a window at a world that I somehow missed the instructions for.

I keep telling myself I’ll “put myself out there,” join something, try harder. But when it comes down to it, I’m exhausted before I even start. It feels like I’m constantly auditioning for a role in other people’s lives and never getting a callback.

Most days I just go through the motions and hope the emptiness doesn’t swallow me whole.
