I’m starting to realize that I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s changing who I am.

My days are basically work, commute, screen, sleep. I talk to coworkers about tasks and deadlines, maybe crack a joke, but nobody really knows me. When the day ends, everyone goes off to their own lives and I go back to my apartment, which is quiet in a way that feels heavy, not peaceful.

I’ve lost the habit of reaching out. I stare at my phone, scroll through chats that went cold months or years ago, and can’t think of a way to restart anything without it feeling awkward or desperate. I tell myself I’ll join a club, or a class, or something “social,” but the thought of showing up alone and trying to break into existing groups makes me feel exhausted before I even try.

What bothers me most is how it’s affecting my sense of self. I used to see myself as funny, caring, even interesting. Now I mostly see myself as someone other people forget. I catch myself editing my personality down in conversations, keeping things surface-level because I assume they won’t stick around long enough to see anything deeper anyway.

It’s like I’m slowly fading out of my own life, and I don’t really know what to do about it.
