I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about weekends and TV shows. People would probably say I’m friendly. I can be funny in short bursts. But it’s all surface-level, like I’m playing a character who knows the right lines, while the real me is sitting somewhere way in the back, watching.

The weirdest part is that I’m not completely isolated. I have acquaintances, group chats, coworkers, some old friends I check in with every few weeks. But I don’t feel close to anyone. There’s no one I can message at 2 a.m. just to say, “I feel awful and I don’t know why, can you just exist with me for a bit?” I keep imagining what it would be like to be someone’s first choice, instead of an option they remember once in a while.

I’ve tried to “fix” it the way the internet says to: join clubs, go to events, say yes more, ask people questions about themselves. I do all of that and still come home feeling like I’ve been orbiting everyone else’s lives without actually entering them. People seem to already have their circles, their in-jokes, their shared history. I’m the extra piece that doesn’t quite fit into any puzzle.

What bothers me most is how it’s starting to change me. I catch myself holding back in conversations because I assume they won’t last. I downplay things I care about so I don’t seem “too much.” I tell myself I’m better off alone, but it doesn’t feel like a choice. It feels like I’ve slowly faded into the background and no one noticed, including me, until it got really quiet.
