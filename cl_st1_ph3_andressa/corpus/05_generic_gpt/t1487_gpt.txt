I’m starting to feel like I’ve forgotten how to connect with people. I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I nod to, but no one I can text when something small happens, good or bad. I scroll through chats that went quiet months ago and can’t tell if I drifted away or if everyone else did.

I try to initiate conversations, but they fizzle out, and I end up overanalyzing every word I sent. I tell myself I’m “busy” or “independent,” but the silence in my apartment at night feels heavy, like it’s pressing on my chest.

It’s strange to feel invisible and yet embarrassed about wanting to be seen. I don’t even know what I’m hoping for anymore—just that this can’t be all there is to human connection.
