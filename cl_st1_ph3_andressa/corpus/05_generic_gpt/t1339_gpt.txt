Lately I’ve been realizing that I’m not just “alone,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s rewiring my brain.

My days look normal from the outside. I go to work, make small talk, answer emails, come home. People would probably describe me as “nice” or “easy to get along with.” But no one really knows me. The version of me that exists in other people’s heads feels like a character I’m playing, and I don’t know how to step out of it without making things weird.

I have friends, technically. Group chats, occasional meetups. But when I’m with them I still feel like an extra in someone else’s story. Conversations stay on the surface. I don’t know how to move them deeper without sounding needy or intense. So I laugh at the right times, say “same” a lot, then go home and feel like I’ve just watched my own life from the outside.

The worst part is how it’s starting to affect what I think I deserve. I’ve caught myself assuming people will eventually get bored of me, so I keep my distance first. I’ll type out messages and delete them because I don’t want to be “too much.” Then I complain to myself that no one reaches out.

It’s like I’m stuck between wanting to be seen and being terrified of actually letting anyone see me.
