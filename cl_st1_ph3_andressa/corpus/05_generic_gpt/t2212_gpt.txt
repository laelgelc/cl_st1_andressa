I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s getting harder to pretend otherwise.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I chat with. I can make small talk, crack jokes, even seem “normal” in short bursts. But the second I’m alone again, it feels like I just stepped off a stage. None of it feels like it belongs to me.

I watch other people slide into friendships so naturally—inside jokes, group chats, casual hangouts that somehow just happen. For me, everything feels forced and fragile, like if I don’t keep performing, they’ll forget I exist.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through my phone, wanting to message someone, then convincing myself I’d be bothering them. So I don’t. I just sit there, feeling weirdly invisible, like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.
