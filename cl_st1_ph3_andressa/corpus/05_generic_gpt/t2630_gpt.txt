I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really make sense on paper. I’m not totally isolated: I go to work, I talk to people, I answer “how are you” with the usual “I’m fine.” But it all feels like I’m acting out a version of myself that everyone expects, while the real version is sitting a few feet behind glass.

What bothers me most is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped showing up, I think people would notice in a vague “oh, where’d they go?” kind of way, then just keep moving. I don’t feel like anyone’s “person,” the one they’d actually miss in a specific, painful way. I’m just… there.

I’ve tried to open up a few times, but it either gets brushed off with jokes or people get uncomfortable and change the subject. After that, you start editing yourself: don’t say too much, don’t be too honest, don’t be “dramatic.” Then you end up with connections that are technically there but feel hollow.

Nights are the worst. That’s when the silence feels loud and you realize there’s no one you can text without worrying you’re intruding. I keep thinking I must be doing something wrong, like there’s some basic social skill or warmth I’m missing that everyone else just naturally has. And it’s hard not to wonder if this is just how it’s going to be.
