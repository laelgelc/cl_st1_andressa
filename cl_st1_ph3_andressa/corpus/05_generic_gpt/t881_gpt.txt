I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living in a parallel world to everyone around me.

From the outside, I probably look fine. I have a job, I show up, I make small talk about weekend plans and TV shows, I laugh at the right times. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice.” But when I go home, there’s this sudden drop-off, like walking off a cliff into silence. No messages, no calls, no one to debrief the day with. Just me, my phone, and that awkward feeling of realizing I have no one I can text without it feeling forced or weird.

It’s not that I’ve never had friends. I did, once. School made it easier because proximity did half the work. Now, as an adult, everything feels transactional or temporary. People have their partners, their long-term friend groups, their kids, their routines. I feel like an extra in the background of everyone else’s movie.

What really bothers me is how much it’s starting to change me. I’ve gotten used to not being included, so I pre-reject myself. I don’t reach out because I assume I’m annoying. I don’t suggest plans because I’m almost sure they’ll say they’re busy, and I don’t know if I can handle pretending I’m okay with that one more time. When people cancel, I say “no worries at all” but inside it’s like confirmation of something I already believed about myself.

The worst part is the way loneliness makes you question your own memory. Were the times I felt connected ever real, or did I just imagine the closeness because I wanted it so badly? I scroll through old conversations and there’s this weird distance, like I’m reading someone else’s life.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for writing this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once, instead of letting it echo around in there like it usually does.
