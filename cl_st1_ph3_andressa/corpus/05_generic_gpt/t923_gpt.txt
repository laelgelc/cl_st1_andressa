I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my circumstances on paper. I have coworkers I get along with, a couple of old friends I text with sometimes, family chats that light up my phone. But most of it feels…thin. Like I’m playing a role in conversations instead of actually being there.

I’ve noticed I censor myself a lot. I keep things surface-level because I’m scared that if I show more of who I am—my actual opinions, my weird interests, the parts of me that feel messy—people will quietly drift away. So I stick to safe topics, safe jokes, safe versions of myself. And then I end up feeling invisible and misunderstood, which is kind of ironic since I’m the one hiding.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through social media and see people doing things in groups, having “their people,” and I can’t tell if I ever had that or if I just imagined it. I don’t even know what I’m looking for, exactly. Just something that doesn’t feel so hollow.
