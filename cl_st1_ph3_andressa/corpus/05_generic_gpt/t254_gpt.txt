I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’ve hit this weird point where I’m surrounded by people but feel completely disconnected from them. I go to work, I make small talk, I answer “how are you?” with “good, you?” and it all feels like I’m playing a character who vaguely looks like me but isn’t actually me.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I can text memes to. But I don’t have anyone I feel like I can call at 2 a.m. and say “I’m not okay, can you just stay on the line for a bit?” I don’t even know if I’d be able to form the words if I tried. It’s like I’ve forgotten how to be close to people.

What makes it worse is that on paper, my life looks fine. I have a job, I pay my bills, I’m not completely isolated. So when I start to feel this heavy, hollow loneliness, I also feel guilty, like I don’t have the right to feel this way. Other people have it worse, so why can’t I just be grateful?

Social media makes it feel more intense. Everyone seems to have “their people” – group photos, inside jokes, spontaneous hangouts. I scroll past it all and realize I don’t have a single person I regularly make plans with. If I didn’t initiate contact, I’m not sure anyone would really notice my absence, beyond maybe a “hey, where’ve you been?” weeks later.

I’m also tired of always being the one who reaches out first. It makes me feel needy and annoying, even when people respond kindly. I start to pull back, then I feel even more alone, and then I hate myself for pulling back in the first place. It’s like I’m stuck in this loop I don’t know how to break.

I can’t tell if I’ve become boring, or if I’ve always been this way and I’m just now noticing. I don’t know how adults are supposed to make real friends anymore, the kind where you don’t have to constantly perform or filter yourself.

Mostly I just feel invisible. Like I could quietly disappear from my own life and it would take a long time before anyone realized something was actually wrong.
