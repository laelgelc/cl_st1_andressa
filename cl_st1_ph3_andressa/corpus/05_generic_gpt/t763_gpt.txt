I don’t really know how to describe this other than feeling like I’m living behind glass.

My life looks “fine” from the outside. Job, small apartment, a couple of acquaintances I can grab a drink with. But no one actually knows me. Not the version that’s awake at 2am replaying conversations and wondering if I sounded weird, or the version that stares at their phone hoping someone will text first.

I’m surrounded by people all day and still feel like I’m on mute. I can talk about work, shows, surface stuff. The second it gets personal, my brain just shuts down. I either overshare and regret it, or keep everything locked up and walk away feeling like I missed another chance to connect.

What scares me is how normalized it’s become. I go through the routines, laugh at the right times, and then come home to this heavy quiet that feels less like peace and more like disappearing a little bit more every day.
