I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

Lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass. I go to work, say the right things, make small jokes, answer emails. People would probably describe me as “nice” or “easy to get along with.” But when I come home and close the door, it’s like I vanish. My phone barely buzzes unless it’s a notification from an app. If I didn’t text first, I’m not sure anyone would remember I exist.

What gets to me is that I *have* people around me, technically. Coworkers, acquaintances, even a couple of “friends,” but everything feels shallow and temporary. No one really knows what’s actually going on in my head. I’ve tried to open up a few times, but I always feel like I’m oversharing or making things awkward, so I pull back and pretend I’m fine.

Weekends are the worst. I’ll scroll through social media and see everyone doing things together—trips, dinners, game nights—and I’m just lying on my bed, watching another show I won’t remember. I tell myself I like being alone, but it’s more like I’ve adapted to it because I don’t know how to do anything else.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for exactly. A close friend? A partner? Just someone who’d notice if I disappeared for a while. Right now it feels like I could quietly slip out of my own life and nothing around me would really change.
