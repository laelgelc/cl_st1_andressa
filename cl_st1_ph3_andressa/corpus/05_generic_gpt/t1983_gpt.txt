I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s getting harder to pretend otherwise.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I chat with online, but it all feels like I’m acting out a role. I can say the right things, make small talk, even joke around, but once the conversation ends, I’m back in my head, replaying everything and feeling weirdly hollow.

I watch other people form friendships so effortlessly—inside jokes, group photos, plans that just “happen.” For me, everything feels forced or one-sided. If I don’t initiate, nothing happens. If I do, I overthink every word and usually talk myself out of it.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through my phone, wanting to message someone, realizing there’s no one I feel close enough to bother. It’s not dramatic, just this low, constant ache that never really shuts off.
