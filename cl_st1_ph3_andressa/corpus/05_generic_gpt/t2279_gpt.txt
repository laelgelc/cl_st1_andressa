I’m starting to realize that I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m in a soundproof box.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, some acquaintances, family I text with. But most of my interactions feel like small talk on autopilot. I can joke around, ask about their weekend, talk about work, but it never goes deeper. As soon as the conversation ends, it’s like I snap back into this empty quiet where nobody actually knows me.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” more: joined group chats, went to a few meetups, tried being more active online. I always end up feeling like the extra person in the photo, the one who could be cropped out and nothing would really change. I don’t think anyone would describe me as a close friend. I’m not even sure what someone would say if they had to talk about me beyond surface traits.

The weirdest part is how exhausting it is to keep pretending I’m fine. I keep my loneliness to myself because I don’t want to seem needy or dramatic. But then I resent that no one notices how disconnected I feel. It’s like I’m waiting for someone to prove me wrong, to show that I actually matter to them, but I don’t know how long you’re supposed to wait before you accept that maybe you just don’t.
