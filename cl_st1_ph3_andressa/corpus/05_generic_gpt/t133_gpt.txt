I’ve been feeling like a background character in my own life lately.

I go through the motions: wake up, work, come home, scroll, sleep. I talk to people every day—coworkers, cashiers, neighbors—but it all feels like acting. Surface-level conversations about weather, deadlines, “How was your weekend?” where no one really wants the real answer.

What I miss is the feeling of being known. Not just “How’s it going?” but someone who actually notices when my voice sounds off, or when I disappear for a few days and checks in because they care, not because it’s polite. I’m surrounded by people and still feel like I’m sitting behind glass.

I’ve tried to put myself out there: joined a couple of group chats, went to a meetup, even forced myself to start a few conversations online. It always seems to fizzle. People already have their circles, their history, their inside jokes. I end up feeling like an extra chair someone’s being nice enough to keep around, but no one really prefers.

What messes with my head is how loneliness makes me second-guess everything. I start assuming I’m boring, too quiet, too much, not enough—sometimes all at once. Then I withdraw more, which obviously doesn’t help, but it feels safer than risking that awkward realization that I’m the one people forget to invite.

I don’t even know exactly what I’m hoping for writing this. Maybe just to say out loud that I’m tired of feeling invisible, even when I’m technically “not alone.”
