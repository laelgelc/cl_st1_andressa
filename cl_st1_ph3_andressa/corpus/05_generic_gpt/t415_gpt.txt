I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know what “having friends” feels like in the way other people seem to mean it.

I have people I talk to: coworkers, a couple of old classmates I text occasionally, family group chats. On paper, it looks like I’m not alone. But almost all of my interactions feel…transactional? Surface-level? I can’t remember the last time someone messaged me first just to ask how I’m doing without needing something.

Weekends are the worst. I watch everyone else make plans, post pictures, talk about “the group,” and I’m just…not in any group. If I disappeared from social media, from work, from everything, I honestly don’t know who would notice beyond “hey, did you see where that file went?”

I try to reach out, but it always feels like I’m intruding. I overthink every message: “Am I being annoying? Am I asking for too much?” So I end up not sending anything, then feel hurt that no one reaches out to me either. It’s this loop I can’t seem to break.

What really gets to me is the lack of emotional intimacy. I don’t have that person I can send a random thought to, or call when I’m overwhelmed. I censor myself constantly because I’m scared of being “too much” for people. It makes me feel like I’m only acceptable in small, carefully controlled doses.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by posting this. I just needed to put it somewhere that isn’t the inside of my own head for once.
