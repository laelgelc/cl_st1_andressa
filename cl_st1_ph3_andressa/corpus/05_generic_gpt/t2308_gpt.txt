It’s weird how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re not really there.

I have friends, technically. Group chats, occasional hangouts, the usual surface-level stuff. But I don’t feel close to anyone. Conversations feel like I’m playing a role I learned a long time ago and never updated. I laugh at the right times, ask the right questions, but it’s like I’m watching myself from a distance.

I keep thinking I’m “too much” or “not enough,” so I filter everything I say. By the time I’ve edited my thoughts into something “acceptable,” the moment to speak has passed. Then I go home and replay every interaction, convinced I sounded awkward or boring.

I crave that feeling of being known without having to explain every detail of myself. Someone who actually notices when I’m quiet and doesn’t just move on to the next topic.

Instead, I scroll, I lurk, I read other people’s posts and think, “Yeah, that’s me,” and then close the app and sit in the same silence.
