I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone,” I’m actually lonely. I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats—but it all feels like background noise. I can go days without anyone asking how I really am. When I try to open up, conversations slide back to small talk, like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else. I’m tired of pretending I’m fine when I feel this invisible, even in rooms full of people.
