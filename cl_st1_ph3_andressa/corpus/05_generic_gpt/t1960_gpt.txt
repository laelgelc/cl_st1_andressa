I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my life on paper. I have coworkers I talk to, a couple of old friends I text with sometimes, family group chats that ping every now and then. But it all feels like I’m watching my own life from a few feet away.

The hardest part is that I don’t feel truly “known” by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, memes, small talk. I can’t remember the last time someone asked me how I *really* am and actually had the time or interest to listen. And I’m not innocent in that either—I’ve built up this habit of saying “I’m fine” because it feels safer than risking awkwardness or rejection.

Social media makes it worse. I scroll and see people doing things in groups—game nights, trips, casual hangouts—and it just highlights how replaceable I feel. Like if I disappeared from everyone’s life, they’d notice for a day or two, then adjust.

I keep thinking I should “just put myself out there,” join clubs, talk to people more. But there’s this tiredness, like I’ve already tried and it never really leads to that deeper connection I’m craving. So I go to work, come home, distract myself with shows and the internet, and repeat.
