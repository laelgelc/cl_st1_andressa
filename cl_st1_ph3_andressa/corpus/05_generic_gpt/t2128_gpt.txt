I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too exposed at the same time. I’m surrounded by people almost every day—at work, on the commute, in group chats—but it all feels like background noise. I can talk about tasks, memes, random stuff, but the moment it gets close to anything real, my brain just… shuts down.

I keep thinking I’m “too much” or “not enough,” so I end up editing myself mid-sentence, then later replaying the conversation and cringing anyway. It makes me pull back even more. People assume I’m fine because I function: I show up, I smile, I make small talk. But nobody actually knows me, and I don’t know how to let them.

Nights are the worst. That’s when the silence feels heavy and I start wondering if anyone would really notice if I just quietly drifted out of their lives.
