I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a small apartment, a couple of people I can technically call friends. I show up, I answer messages, I smile when I’m supposed to. But I feel like I’m acting out a version of myself that everyone else expects, and the real me is sitting somewhere in the back, silent and unseen.

The weirdest part is how invisible the loneliness is. No one looks at me and thinks “that person is lonely.” I’m good at small talk. I can crack a joke, ask about someone’s weekend, talk about shows or work. But there’s this huge gap between what I say and what I actually feel. I’m surrounded by people and still feel like I’m on the other side of soundproof glass.

I don’t really have “my person.” Or even “my group.” I have acquaintances I see occasionally, but no one I can text at 2 a.m. when my brain won’t shut up. No one who knows the small details—my fears, my weird habits, the stuff that makes me, me. When something good happens, I hesitate before sharing it because I don’t know who would genuinely care. When something bad happens, I just… swallow it.

I’ve tried to put myself out there: meetups, online chats, hobbies. I always end up feeling like I’m hovering at the edge of things, never quite crossing that line into real connection. People already seem to have their established circles, their in-jokes, their shared history. I’m the extra chair at the table—useful, but not necessary.

It’s starting to feel less like I’m alone and more like I’m fundamentally un-connectable. Like there’s some invisible barrier I can’t identify, let alone fix. And I don’t know how long you can go on feeling like this before it just becomes who you are.
