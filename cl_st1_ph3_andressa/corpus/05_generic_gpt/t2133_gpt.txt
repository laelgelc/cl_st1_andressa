I’m starting to realize that I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s changing who I am.

On paper, my life looks fine. I go to work, I pay my bills, I make small talk with coworkers, I answer “how are you?” with “good, you?” and nobody questions it. But I come home to silence every day, and the silence feels heavier than it used to. It’s like the walls are learning my routines better than any person does.

I’ve tried the usual advice: hobbies, gym, online groups, saying yes to invites. I do all that, but it never really crosses that line from acquaintance to “someone who actually knows me.” I’m always the extra person at the table, never the one anyone specifically asks for. If I stop texting, most conversations just die.

What bothers me most is how it’s affecting the way I see myself. I’m starting to assume people won’t be interested in me, so I hold back, then I seem distant, and that just confirms my own fear. It’s like I’m accidentally proving myself right.

There’s also this weird grief for a life I haven’t lived. I’m in this age range where people are getting married, having kids, building friend groups that feel like chosen family. I’m watching it all happen from the outside, scrolling through photos of events I’m not part of, realizing no one even thought to invite me.

I don’t really know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to put into words that this is where I am, instead of pretending it’s not.
