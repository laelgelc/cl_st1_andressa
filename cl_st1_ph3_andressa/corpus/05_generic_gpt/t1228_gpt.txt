It’s strange how you can go through an entire day without anyone really seeing you.

I have people around me—coworkers, a few acquaintances, family I text sometimes—but it all feels like background noise. Conversations are about tasks, logistics, jokes that skim the surface. I can play along, smile, even make them laugh, and still walk away feeling like I don’t actually exist to anyone once I’m out of their line of sight.

Nights are the worst. The silence in my room feels heavy, like it’s pressing on my chest. I scroll, lurk, watch other people talk about their lives and friendships and drama, and I feel like I’m peering through a window at something I somehow missed the instructions for.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for. I just needed to say out loud that I’m lonely in a way that doesn’t make sense on paper, but feels constant and exhausting in my head.
