I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I respond to emails, I show up to things when I have to. I even have people I can technically call friends. But most of the time I feel like an extra in everyone else’s movie. I’m there, but blurry in the background. No one really looks directly at me long enough to notice that I’m not okay.

I keep thinking about how long it’s been since someone has genuinely asked, “How are you?” and actually wanted more than the default “I’m good.” Conversations feel like small talk marathons: weather, work, memes, “we should hang out sometime” that never turns into an actual plan. I go home, close the door, and it’s like I just disappear from the world until I clock back in the next day.

What messes with my head is that I’m not completely isolated. I text people. I’m in group chats. I respond with “lol” and “that’s wild” and send reaction gifs like everyone else. But it’s all surface-level. I don’t feel like there’s anyone I could message at 2am and say, “I feel really empty right now,” without worrying that I’m being too much or making things awkward.

I’ve tried putting myself out there more—saying yes to invites, joining a couple of online communities, trying to talk a bit more at work—but I always end up feeling like I’m forcing myself into spaces where no one was really saving a seat for me in the first place. So I pull back again, and the cycle continues.

I don’t know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just needed to admit, somewhere, that I feel incredibly alone even when I’m technically not.
