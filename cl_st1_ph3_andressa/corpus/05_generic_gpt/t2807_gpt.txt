I’ve started to realize that I can be surrounded by people and still feel completely disconnected. I have coworkers, acquaintances, family I text with occasionally, but it all feels like I’m playing a role instead of actually being known.

Whenever I try to open up, I get this voice in my head saying, “You’re being weird, you’re oversharing, they don’t actually care.” So I pull back. Then I feel even more alone and resentful that no one “sees” me, even though I’m the one hiding.

Social media makes it worse. I scroll through everyone else’s group photos and inside jokes and feel like I missed some crucial step in learning how to form those kinds of bonds. I’m not completely isolated, but the connections I do have feel thin, like they’d disappear if I stopped putting in effort.

What scares me is how used to this I’m becoming. I fill my time with distractions, but there’s this constant background hum of “no one really knows you.” I’m not even sure what I’d say if someone genuinely asked how I’m doing. I think that’s what bothers me most: I don’t know how to bridge the gap between wanting to be close to people and not really knowing how to let anyone in.
