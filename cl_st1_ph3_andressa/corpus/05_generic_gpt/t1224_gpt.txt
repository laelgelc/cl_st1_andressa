I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… background noise.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats that ping on my phone. But none of it feels like it really touches me. Conversations stay on the surface: work, memes, “how was your weekend?” I answer, they answer, and it all feels rehearsed. I can’t remember the last time I said exactly what I was thinking without editing it down first.

What gets to me most is the silence after I share something real. I’ll open up a little—say I’m tired, or overwhelmed, or just not okay—and people either joke it away or change the subject. I don’t blame them; I’m not exactly good at sitting with other people’s feelings either. But every time that happens, I feel myself pulling back more.

Nights are the worst. I’ll scroll through chats and feel this weird mix of wanting to message someone and being convinced I’d just be bothering them. So I don’t. I just lie there, replaying old conversations, thinking about people I used to be close to and how we just drifted without any big fight or reason.

I keep wondering if there’s something about me that makes it hard for people to actually connect with me, or if I’m just expecting too much from everyone. I don’t really have an answer. I just know I’m tired of feeling like a ghost in my own life.
