I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from everyone’s life while still being physically here.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text now and then, family chats that light up on holidays. But none of it feels like it touches me. It’s like I’m on the other side of thick glass watching everyone else live, and when they look my way, they just see a reflection of themselves.

I’ve realized I’m always the “easy” friend: the one people come to when they’re bored or need something, the one who listens, gives advice, laughs at the right spots. But when I need someone, I freeze. I’ll open the chat box, stare at the cursor, and then close it because I don’t know how to say “I’m lonely” without feeling pathetic or needy. So I say nothing, and then I resent myself for not trying and resent them for not noticing.

The worst part is how routine it’s become. I wake up, go through my day, make small talk, come home, scroll, maybe play something, maybe watch something, then go to bed feeling like I’ve eaten a bunch of empty calories socially. I’m “full” of interactions but not nourished by any of them.

Sometimes I catch myself rehearsing conversations in my head that will never happen—imagining someone asking how I really am and actually wanting the real answer. I don’t even know what I’d say anymore. I’ve smoothed out my personality so much to fit in and not be “too much” that I’m not sure what’s left that’s actually me.

I keep thinking there must be a version of my life where I feel known by someone. But each year that passes, that version feels more like a story I made up than something that could actually happen.
