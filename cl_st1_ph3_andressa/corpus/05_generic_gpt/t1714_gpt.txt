I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living in the “background layer” of life.

On paper, I’m not completely alone. I have coworkers I talk to, family I text with occasionally, a couple of old friends I still send memes to. But none of it feels like it actually touches me. It’s like I’m watching my own life from behind glass, doing all the “normal” things, but never really being known by anyone.

What gets to me most is the silence after a small moment that should feel warm. I’ll send something a little vulnerable in a chat, they’ll respond with a joke or a quick “lol same,” and I immediately feel stupid for trying to go a bit deeper. So I pull back. Then I complain to myself that no one knows the real me, when I’m the one constantly hiding anything real.

I miss having someone who notices patterns—like, “You’ve been quieter lately, are you okay?” Or someone who remembers small details I mentioned weeks ago. I feel weirdly jealous when I see people casually talking about “my best friend” or “we’ve known each other for years.” I don’t even know when I lost that kind of connection, or if I ever actually had it in a stable way.

Most days I just go through my routine, headphones on, scrolling, working, sleeping. I tell myself I’m “used to it,” but there are these random moments—standing in the grocery store, sitting on the bus, lying in bed—where the emptiness hits so hard it almost feels physical. Then it passes, and I get up the next day and do it all over again.
