I don’t really know how to start this, but I’ve been feeling like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I answer emails, I say “have a good one” to the cashier. I even go out with coworkers sometimes. But it all feels like I’m playing a character who’s imitating what a normal person would do. The weird part is that no one seems to notice. Or maybe they do and just don’t care enough to say anything.

The loneliness isn’t about being physically alone. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else. I hear them laughing, talking about their weekends, their relationships, their families, and I just stand there nodding at the right times, throwing in a comment here and there. Inside, it feels like I’m screaming into a void. But nothing comes out, so I just smile.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there.” I’ve joined group chats, Discord servers, hobby groups. I make small talk, I ask questions, I respond when people message me. But it never seems to move past that shallow level. I’m always the person people talk to when they’re bored, not the person they think of first when something actually matters.

Sometimes I scroll through my contacts and realize there’s no one I could text and honestly say, “I’m not okay. Can we talk?” Either it would be too awkward, or I’d feel like a burden, or I just know they wouldn’t understand. So I don’t send anything. I just put my phone down and lie there, feeling heavy and empty at the same time.

What scares me most is how used to it I’m becoming. The silence after I come home. Eating alone. Going days without a real, honest conversation. Telling myself it doesn’t bother me that much. Then suddenly it hits me out of nowhere, like a wave, and I realize how much it actually does.

I don’t really have a point to this. I just wanted to say it somewhere.
