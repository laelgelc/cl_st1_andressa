I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into the usual “I have no one” narrative, because technically I *do* have people in my life. I have family I could call, a couple of old friends I message every now and then, coworkers I chat with. But I still feel like I’m floating just slightly outside of everyone’s lives, like a background character in my own story.

I keep noticing how most of my interactions are surface-level. Work talk, memes, the occasional “we should hang out sometime” that never turns into an actual plan. I’ll open chat apps, stare at people’s names, and then close them again because I don’t know how to bridge the gap between “hey” and anything real. It feels like I missed some crucial stage where people learned how to move from acquaintance to actual closeness.

The worst moments are when something small but meaningful happens—good or bad—and I realize there isn’t anyone I feel comfortable messaging right away. Not because they’d be cruel or anything, but because I’m not “that person” for anyone, and they’re not that person for me. It’s like everyone already has their people, their group chats, their default person to call, and I’m just… extra.

I also feel like I come off as boring or emotionally flat, so I end up censoring myself a lot. I worry that if I try to open up, I’ll be too much, or not enough, or just awkward. So I stay quiet, then complain to myself that no one really knows me. It’s a loop I don’t know how to break.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just needed to put into words that specific kind of loneliness where you’re not completely alone, but you still feel like no one is really “with” you.
