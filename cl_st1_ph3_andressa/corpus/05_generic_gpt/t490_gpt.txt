I’m starting to realize that I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that’s starting to feel like a personality trait.

My days are basically work, commute, screen, sleep. I talk to coworkers, but it’s all surface-level stuff—projects, weather, “how was your weekend?” where nobody really wants a detailed answer. I have acquaintances, not friends. People I can message about a meme, but not about how I’m actually doing.

What gets to me is how invisible it all is. From the outside, my life probably looks fine: job, small apartment, hobbies I do by myself. Inside, it feels like I’m constantly hovering at the edge of everyone else’s lives, watching them have group chats, trips, inside jokes I’ll never be part of.

I try sometimes—sending the first text, suggesting plans—but I always feel like I’m forcing my way into spaces where I’m not really wanted. After a few rejections or “maybe another time,” I just stop trying for a while.

Then the silence stretches out, and it’s like I forget how to be around people at all.
