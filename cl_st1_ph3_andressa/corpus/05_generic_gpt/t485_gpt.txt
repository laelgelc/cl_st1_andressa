I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening in a soundproof room.

I’m “around” people a lot. I go to work, I answer emails, I have surface-level banter with coworkers about the weather, TV shows, whatever. I send “lol” in group chats. On paper it looks like I’m connected. But I don’t feel like anyone actually knows me, and I don’t know how to bridge that gap anymore.

I used to think loneliness meant literally having no one. Now I’m realizing it can mean being surrounded and still feeling like a ghost. I can be in a room full of people and feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else live in real time while I’m on some kind of delay. By the time I think of something real to say, the moment has already moved on.

I’ve also noticed I’m starting to censor myself more. I don’t talk about what actually bothers me because I don’t want to be “too much” or push people away. So I keep it light, nod along, make jokes. Then I go home and replay every interaction, convinced I sounded weird or boring or needy. It’s exhausting.

The worst part is when I do try to open up a little and it just… lands flat. People say, “Oh, that sucks,” and then change the subject. I know everyone has their own problems, but it makes me feel stupid for even trying. After enough of those moments, it’s easier not to risk it.

I miss feeling close to someone. Not even in a romantic way necessarily, just that sense that there’s at least one person who really sees you and wants to know what’s going on in your head. Right now it feels like if I just quietly disappeared from my social circles, it would take a long time for anyone to actually notice.
