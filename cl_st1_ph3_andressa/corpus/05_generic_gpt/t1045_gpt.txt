I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding melodramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay rent, I answer emails, I make small talk about the weather and whatever show people are watching. I even laugh at the right moments. But it all feels like I’m playing a character who’s loosely based on me, and the real version is stuck somewhere behind glass.

The weirdest part is that I’m not technically alone. I have coworkers, some acquaintances, a family group chat that sends memes and birthday messages. But I don’t feel known by any of them. Conversations never get past surface level. If I disappeared, they’d probably notice eventually, but I don’t think anyone would feel like a piece of their world was missing, if that makes sense.

I’ve tried putting myself out there—meetups, hobbies, even forcing myself to message people first—but it always fizzles out. People already seem to have their circles, their shared history, their “remember when” stories. I’m always the extra, the new person who never quite becomes anything more.

It’s starting to mess with how I see myself. I catch my reflection and think, “No wonder nobody sticks around.” Rationally I know that’s not fair, but the feeling is there anyway.

I don’t really know what I’m looking for writing this. Maybe I just wanted to say it somewhere: I’m here, I exist, and I’m tired of feeling like a background character in my own story.
