I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness that doesn’t really match my life on paper, and it’s starting to mess with my sense of reality.

I’m not completely isolated. I have coworkers I chat with, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family group chats that light up with memes and random updates. But none of it feels like it actually touches me. It’s like I’m always standing just outside the circle, watching everyone else be “in” something that I can’t seem to access.

I’ve noticed that most of my interactions are surface-level. “How’s work?” “How was your weekend?” “Did you see that show?” I know how to play the part, respond, even be funny sometimes, but the whole time I’m thinking: if I disappeared from this conversation, from this group, from this city, would anything actually change for anyone?

I think what I’m missing is that feeling of being known. Not just “oh, you like this band” known, but the kind where someone understands how your brain works, what you’re afraid of, what you’re hoping for, and still chooses to stay. I don’t have that. I’m not sure I ever really did.

I also feel like I’ve forgotten how to let people close. When someone starts to get even a little bit deeper with me, I panic internally. I change the subject, make a joke, or just kind of emotionally shut down. Then I go home and complain in my head that nobody really knows me. It’s like I’m building my own prison and then crying about the bars.

Nights are the worst. The silence gets so loud. I scroll, I watch stuff, I listen to music, but under everything there’s this constant ache, like something important is missing and I don’t even know how to look for it anymore.

I don’t really know what I want from posting this. Maybe I just needed to say it somewhere.
