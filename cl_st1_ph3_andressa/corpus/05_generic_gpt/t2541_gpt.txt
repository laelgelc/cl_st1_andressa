I don’t really know how to say this out loud to anyone in my life, so I’m dumping it here.

I’m lonely in a way that doesn’t make sense on paper. I have coworkers, a couple of people I can text, family I talk to sometimes. I’m not completely isolated. But I feel like I’m watching my own life from behind glass. People see me, they interact with me, but it never feels like they actually *know* me, and I don’t know how to let them.

Most of my days are some version of: wake up, go to work, make small talk, come home, scroll on my phone, maybe watch a show, go to sleep. I keep thinking I’ll “fix” it next week or next month by being more social, joining something, reaching out. Then the day comes and I’m too tired or anxious or just numb, and I repeat the same loop.

What bothers me most is the silence when I turn everything off. When I put my phone face down and realize no one is going to message me unless I start the conversation. And even if I do, it’s usually surface-level: memes, work complaints, random comments. I can’t remember the last time I had a real, unhurried conversation where I felt emotionally safe enough to be honest.

I see other people my age forming friend groups, moving in with partners, planning trips together, talking about “their people.” I have acquaintances and old friends I’ve drifted from. I don’t know if it’s my fault for not trying harder or if I just never really fit anywhere in the first place.

Sometimes I wonder if there’s something fundamentally off about me. I rehearse things I might say in a deeper conversation and then imagine the other person being bored or weirded out, so I don’t say them. I play the “easygoing” version of myself because that’s what people seem to like, and then feel invisible because no one sees what’s underneath.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for here. I think I just wanted to say, somewhere, that I’m tired of feeling like an extra in everyone else’s story, and like my own life is happening at a distance I can’t quite close.
