I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my life on paper. I have coworkers I talk to, a couple of old friends I text once in a while, family group chats that light up with memes and random updates. But I feel like I’m watching all of it from behind glass.

What’s messing with me is how hard it is to actually say “I’m lonely” to anyone in my real life. It sounds dramatic, or like I’m fishing for pity. So I end up making jokes, acting “busy,” and then going home and scrolling for hours just to feel like I’m near someone. Half the time I don’t even read what I’m looking at; it’s just noise so my head doesn’t feel so empty.

I don’t think I ever really learned how to let people see the real version of me. I can do small talk, work talk, surface-level stuff. But as soon as a conversation starts feeling real, I panic internally. I worry I’ll say too much, or sound needy, or make things awkward. So I pull back, keep it light, and then later feel this ache because nobody really knows me. It’s like I’m contributing to my own isolation and can’t stop.

I miss having someone I can message without overthinking it. Someone where the conversation doesn’t need a reason. Lately even sending a simple “hey” feels like I’m imposing on people’s lives. Everyone seems busy, moving forward, building relationships, posting pictures with groups of friends or partners. I’m just… here. Background character energy.

I don’t know what I’m expecting by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere instead of letting it rattle around in my head. It feels a bit less suffocating when I turn it into words, even if nothing actually changes.
