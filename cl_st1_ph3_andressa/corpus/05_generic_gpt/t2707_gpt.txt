I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

I feel lonely in this weird, layered way. On paper, I’m not “alone” — I have coworkers, some acquaintances, a family chat that pings occasionally. But I can go entire weekends without speaking out loud to anyone, and no one notices. It’s like I exist as background noise in other people’s lives.

What bothers me most is how performative all my interactions feel. At work, I’m the “reliable one.” With family, I’m the “doing fine” one. I know my roles, but I don’t feel known. Nobody sees the version of me that gets home, drops my bag, sits on the bed, and just stares at the wall because I can’t think of a single person I could text without feeling like I’m bothering them.

I try to reach out sometimes, but it’s always me initiating. People are “busy,” or they say “we should hang out sometime,” and then nothing happens. After a while, it starts to feel pathetic to keep trying. I tell myself I’m just “independent,” but the truth is I’m tired of eating alone, going home alone, and having no one who really checks in.

The worst part is how it messes with my sense of worth. I start wondering what’s wrong with me that I’m so easy to forget. I scroll through social media and see everyone else in group photos, inside jokes, tagged posts, and I’m just… not there. It feels like I’m watching life from the outside, pressed up against the glass, and no one’s looking back.
