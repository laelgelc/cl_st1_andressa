I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, I just know how to perform being “fine.”

On paper I’m not isolated: I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats. But it all feels like background noise. When I’m actually in the middle of a conversation, I feel like I’m watching myself from the outside, saying the “right” things and then going home to silence.

The weirdest part is how ashamed I feel about being lonely. It’s like I’ve failed some basic life skill everyone else picked up in middle school. I overthink every interaction: “Did I talk too much? Too little? Was that joke awkward?” And then I retreat, which just makes everything worse.

Nights are the hardest. I’ll scroll, lurk, listen to podcasts just to fill the air, but the second I pause it’s like the emptiness rushes back in. I don’t even know what I’m looking for anymore—friends, a relationship, or just one person who actually sees me and doesn’t disappear.
