I’ve been feeling like I’m watching my own life from the outside lately. I go to work, I come home, I scroll on my phone, and somehow entire weeks disappear without a single real conversation that isn’t about tasks or logistics.

What messes with my head is that I technically “know” people. Coworkers, old classmates, family group chats. But it all feels surface-level, like I’m standing in a crowded room behind glass. I can see everyone, they can see me, but there’s this invisible barrier I can’t push through.

I’ve tried joining group things – hobbies, meetups – but I always end up feeling like an extra in someone else’s movie. People already have their circles and inside jokes. I laugh along, then go home and realize nobody actually knows anything real about me.

The worst part is how it’s changing me. I catch myself holding back even more, saying less, assuming no one is really interested anyway. It’s like I’m slowly disappearing while still being physically here.
