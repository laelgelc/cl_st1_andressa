I’m starting to feel like I’ve become transparent in my own life.

I have people around me—coworkers, a few acquaintances, family I text sometimes—but I don’t feel *known* by any of them. Conversations never seem to get past surface-level stuff. I listen, I nod, I ask questions, but when it’s my turn to talk, it’s like the moment passes and we move on. I end up editing myself down to whatever feels easiest for everyone else.

Nights are the worst. I scroll, I watch things, I stare at my phone hoping someone will message first. When they don’t, I tell myself I shouldn’t bother them. It’s this weird mix of wanting connection desperately and being terrified of coming across as needy or annoying.

What really scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. Like isolation is becoming my default setting, and the idea of actually letting someone see the real me feels more and more unrealistic.
