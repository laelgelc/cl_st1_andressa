I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m living behind glass. I go to work, I make small talk, I respond to “How’s it going?” with the usual “Good, you?” and everyone seems satisfied. But there’s this constant sense that nobody actually knows me, and I don’t know how to let them.

I technically have “people” in my life—coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up on holidays—but it all feels thin, like background noise. I can go days without speaking out loud beyond the bare minimum. When something good or bad happens, my first reaction is, “Who would I even tell?” and then I just…don’t tell anyone.

What messes with my head is that I don’t think I’m completely unlikable. I can be funny, I listen, I try to be considerate. But I feel like I’m always performing a version of myself that’s easy to digest, because the real stuff feels too heavy or too weird or just not worth other people’s time. Then I get resentful that no one sees me, even though I’m the one hiding.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through social media and see people at dinners, game nights, trips, all those “candid” photos where everyone looks like they belong. I know it’s curated, I know it’s not the full story, but it still hits that raw nerve. I can’t remember the last time someone invited me to something that wasn’t out of obligation.

I don’t really know what I’m looking for posting this. Maybe I just wanted to put into words that I’m tired—tired of being “fine,” tired of feeling like an extra in my own life, tired of pretending that this level of disconnection is normal.
