I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone,” I’m actually lonely, and it’s a weird shift. I have coworkers, a family chat, people who’d say they like me, but everything feels surface-level. No one really knows what’s going on in my head, and when I try to open up, I feel like I’m oversharing or making things awkward.

Most days I go through the motions: work, small talk, come home, scroll, sleep. I keep thinking I should “put myself out there,” but I’m exhausted before I even start. It feels like everyone else already has their people, their inside jokes, their shared history. I’m just orbiting around other people’s lives, never really part of anything.
