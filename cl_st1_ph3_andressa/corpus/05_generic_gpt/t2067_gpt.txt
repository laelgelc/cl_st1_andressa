I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

Most of my days feel like I’m watching my own life from the outside. I go to work, make small talk, answer emails, smile when I’m supposed to. People would probably describe me as “pleasant” or “easygoing.” But I go home and it feels like all the noise just shuts off and I’m left with this heavy silence that almost has a physical weight.

I have acquaintances, but not the kind of people you can text at 2 a.m. when your brain won’t shut up. I scroll through my contacts and realize there’s no one I’d feel comfortable calling without an excuse. The people I used to be close to either moved away, got married, or just slowly drifted out of my orbit, and I never really replaced them.

What makes it worse is that I’ve started to feel awkward even when I’m around others. I overthink every word, every pause, every facial expression. I leave conversations replaying them and cringing at things I said that probably didn’t even register for anyone else.

I keep telling myself I should join something, reach out, try harder. But the longer this goes on, the more it feels like I’ve forgotten how to actually connect with anyone, and I’m not sure where to even start anymore.
