It’s weird how you can go through an entire day surrounded by people and still feel like you’re not really there.

I talk to coworkers, I reply to messages, I laugh when I’m supposed to, but it all feels like I’m playing a character who’s better at being human than I actually am. As soon as I’m alone again, it’s like the silence rushes in and I realize how disconnected I really feel.

I don’t have anyone I’d call “my person.” No one I can text without overthinking, no one I can call when I’m spiraling at 1 a.m. I have “friends” in the sense that we share memes and occasionally meet up, but I don’t feel known by them. If I disappeared from their lives, I’m not convinced it would create more than a mild inconvenience.

What bothers me is how hard it is to bridge the gap. I try to open up sometimes, but the words feel heavy and dramatic, so I end up making a joke or changing the subject. I worry that if I show the real level of emptiness I feel, people will either back away or think I’m being needy. So I keep it light, and then I resent that no one sees past the surface I’m carefully maintaining.

There’s also this constant sense of being out of sync with everyone else. People talk about their partners, their kids, their weekend plans with close friends, and I nod along while thinking, “How did everyone else just… figure this out?” I’m stuck in this loop of wanting connection, feeling unworthy of it, and then withdrawing so I don’t have to face that feeling.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe just proof that I exist somewhere outside my own head.
