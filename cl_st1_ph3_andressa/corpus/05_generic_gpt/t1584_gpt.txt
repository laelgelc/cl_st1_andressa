I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t make sense on paper. I have coworkers I get along with, a couple of old friends I still message sometimes, family chats that ping on my phone. But I still feel like I’m sealed off behind glass.

Conversations feel scripted. I know when to laugh, when to say “oh wow, that’s crazy,” when to ask follow-up questions. People seem to think I’m “easy to talk to,” but no one really knows me. I don’t even know if I’d recognize what it feels like to be truly known anymore.

I’ve tried to open up a few times and it always feels like I’ve gone too deep for the situation, like I’ve broken some unspoken rule. The other person gets quiet, changes the subject, or gives a quick “you’ll be okay” and moves on. After that I feel embarrassed, like I overshared, and I pull back even more.

Most days I go through the motions: work, small talk, commute, screen, sleep. I keep thinking that if I just “put myself out there” more, something will click. But it’s hard to keep trying when every interaction reinforces this sense that I’m always going to be just slightly outside of everyone else’s lives, watching them connect from a distance I can’t seem to cross.
