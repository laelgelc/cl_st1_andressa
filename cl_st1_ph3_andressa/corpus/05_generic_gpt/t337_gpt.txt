I’m starting to realize I’m not “temporarily lonely,” I’m just… living a lonely life.

Most days are a loop: wake up, work, scroll, sleep. I talk to coworkers about tasks and deadlines, crack a joke here and there, but when I leave the office it’s like I cease to exist for anyone else. My phone barely lights up unless it’s a notification from some app or spam. If I disappeared, it would take a while for anyone outside of work to notice.

What bothers me most is that it’s not like I’ve never had people. I’ve had friends, relationships, group chats that were busy for a while. But everything seems to fade. People move, get partners, have kids, get busy, and I just… don’t seem to make the cut when they decide who to keep close. I’m always the one who cares more, the one sending the “hey, how are you?” texts that go unanswered or get a reply three days later with some half-hearted “sorry, been busy.”

I keep asking myself if I’m doing something wrong. Am I boring? Too quiet? Too intense? Not enough of anything? I’ll replay conversations in my head, trying to figure out what I said that made someone pull away. Sometimes I try to be more outgoing and I feel fake. Other times I lean into being quiet and I feel invisible.

The loneliness feels physical now. It’s like this heavy pressure in my chest when I see groups of friends laughing together, or couples doing normal things like grocery shopping. I feel like I’m behind a glass wall watching everyone else live in a world I can’t access. Even when I try to join things—meetups, online communities—I end up feeling like an extra in other people’s lives, not someone they’re actually excited to know.

I’m not even sure what I’m looking for writing this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere that isn’t the inside of my head for once.
