I don’t know when “being alone a lot” turned into “being lonely all the time,” but it’s like a switch flipped and never flipped back.

I have people in my life on paper—family, a couple of old friends, coworkers—but I feel like an extra in their stories. I’m there in group chats, but my messages are the ones that don’t really get a reply, or just a reaction emoji and then the conversation moves on. When I’m with people in person, I laugh and talk like I’m fine, and then go home and feel this weird hollow drop in my chest, like I was never really there.

What makes it worse is that I can’t seem to bridge the gap. I don’t know how to move from “acquaintance” to “someone people think about when they make plans.” I hesitate to text first because I don’t want to be annoying, and then I resent that no one reaches out. It’s this loop of wanting connection and being terrified of asking for it.

Nights are the hardest. I scroll through my phone, not even sure who I’d call if something really bad happened. I keep thinking that if I disappeared quietly, it would take a while before anyone actually noticed. And that thought just sits with me more often than I’m comfortable admitting.
