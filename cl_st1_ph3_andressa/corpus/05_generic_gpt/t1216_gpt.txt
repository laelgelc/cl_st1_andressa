I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and “too much” at the same time lately.

I’m surrounded by people a lot of the time—work, public transport, family chats—but it feels like I exist behind glass. I talk, I joke, I nod along, but almost nothing feels like it lands anywhere. It’s like I’m playing a role that everyone’s used to, and if I step out of it, the whole thing will fall apart.

What makes it worse is that I can’t shake the feeling that I’m somehow fundamentally out of sync with everyone else. Other people seem to move so easily from acquaintances to friends to “let’s hang out,” and I’m stuck at the small talk stage, exhausted and awkward, replaying every interaction later and cringing at how I came across.

I’ve tried the usual things—joining group activities, messaging old friends, saying yes more often—but most of the time I end up feeling lonelier in a group than I do by myself. Being physically present while feeling emotionally absent is a special kind of empty.

There are days when I don’t speak to anyone beyond basic functional stuff, and no one notices. No “how are you really,” no check-ins unless I initiate them, and even then the conversation drifts back to surface-level topics.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for exactly. Just something that doesn’t feel this hollow.
