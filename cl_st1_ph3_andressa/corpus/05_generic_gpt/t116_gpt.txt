I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it scares me.

On paper I’m “fine”: job, roof over my head, no major disasters. But the days feel like I’m just moving through scenes where I’m technically around people but never really *with* them. At work I can do small talk, even crack a joke, but it’s like I’m playing a character that knows the right lines. As soon as I get home, the silence hits and it feels like I just vanish.

What’s messing with me most is that I don’t even know what I’m missing in concrete terms. I see others texting friends, making plans, talking about “their people.” I can’t think of a single person I could message without feeling like I’m bothering them. When my phone lights up it’s always spam, notifications, or some app reminder. Never someone choosing to talk to me just because.

I’ve tried joining things—online groups, hobby meetups—but I always end up on the edge, watching everyone who already seems to know each other. I leave early, go home, and then hate myself for not trying harder. It feels like there’s this invisible barrier between me and everyone else that I can’t explain and definitely don’t know how to break.

Sometimes I wonder if this is just how my life is going to be: functioning, visible to others, but emotionally sidelined. Like I’m the background character in my own story, mostly there to fill space.
