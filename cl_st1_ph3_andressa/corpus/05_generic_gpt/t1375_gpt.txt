I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living in a glass box.

From the outside, my life probably looks fine. I have a job, I make small talk with coworkers, I respond to family group chats with the usual emojis. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice.” But I don’t feel connected to anyone. It’s like I’m acting out a version of myself that other people can tolerate while the real version just sits somewhere in the background, waiting for someone to actually notice.

I can go entire weekends without speaking out loud to another person. I’ll scroll through social media, watch people post about dinners, trips, “game nights with the best people,” and I feel this weird mix of envy and shame. Envy because I want that. Shame because I’m old enough that I feel like I should have figured this out by now.

What hurts the most is the lack of anyone who would notice if I just disappeared socially. If I stopped texting, nothing would really change. No one checks in unless I initiate. And even when I do, the conversations feel surface-level, like we’re both just filling silence. I don’t feel like anyone is really curious about me, and I’m not sure if that’s because I’m not interesting or because I’ve become so used to shrinking myself that there’s nothing visible to grab onto.

I’ve tried joining clubs, going to meetups, being “more open.” I always end up feeling like an outsider who got invited by mistake. I stand there, holding a drink, listening to everyone talk like they’ve known each other forever, and I just… fade out. Eventually I leave early and tell myself I tried.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. The quiet apartment, eating alone, watching shows to fill the noise, pretending I’m okay with it. I don’t know if I’m lonely because there’s something wrong with me, or if there’s just no place where I really fit. It’s getting harder to tell the difference.
