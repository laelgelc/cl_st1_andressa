I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about TV shows I don’t actually watch. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice.” No one sees that when I go home, the silence is so loud it almost feels like a physical thing pressing on my chest.

I have acquaintances, not friends. People to grab a drink with after work sometimes, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something was really wrong. I scroll through my contacts and realize there isn’t a single person I feel like I wouldn’t be bothering. It’s like I’m everyone’s second or third choice, never the person they think of first.

What really messes with me is how replaceable I feel. If I disappeared from my usual places, I think people would notice for a week or two, then just… adjust. Someone else would fill the space I used to take up in conversations, and that would be that.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for by writing this. Maybe I just needed to admit out loud that the loneliness isn’t some background feeling anymore. It’s kind of becoming the main thing.
