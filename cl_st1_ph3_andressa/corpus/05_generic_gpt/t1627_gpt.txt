I’m starting to realize that my life is technically “full” of people and still feels completely empty.

I have coworkers, I answer emails, I sit in meetings, I say “good weekend?” on Mondays and “see you tomorrow” in the evenings. I have family group chats where I send the occasional thumbs-up or “lol.” On paper it probably looks like I’m functioning fine. But I don’t feel like there is a single person who really knows me, or even wants to.

The worst part is how ordinary the loneliness looks from the outside. It’s just me going home, heating up food, scrolling on my phone, listening to the same playlists, and then suddenly realizing I haven’t said a real sentence out loud to anyone all day. I keep thinking I should reach out more, but when I actually consider calling someone, it feels like I’m forcing myself into their lives. Like I’m an interruption, not a person they’d be happy to hear from.

I also feel stuck between wanting connection and not knowing how to handle it when it appears. I get invited somewhere once in a while and I either panic and cancel, or I go and feel like I’m watching myself from the outside, trying to act like a normal, social person. I leave those situations exhausted and somehow lonelier than before.

What scares me is how used to this I’m becoming. The silence after I close my front door doesn’t shock me anymore. It’s just the default. And every time I tell myself I’ll change something, the day ends the same way: me, in my room, lights off, phone in hand, wondering if this is just how my life is going to be.
