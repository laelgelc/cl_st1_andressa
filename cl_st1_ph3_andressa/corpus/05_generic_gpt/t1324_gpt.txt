I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, some acquaintances I can message about TV shows or memes. But I don’t feel like I’m actually in anyone’s life in a real way. I’m like a background character who occasionally has a line, then disappears off screen.

Most days are the same: work, come home, scroll on my phone, maybe play a game, go to sleep too late. I talk to people at work, but it’s all surface-level: projects, deadlines, “how was your weekend?” followed by “oh, it was good” lies. I can’t seem to bridge the gap between small talk and something that feels like an actual connection. When I try, I get awkward, overthink every word, and then retreat because I’m convinced I’m being too much or too weird.

What really hurts is the one-sidedness of everything. I’m always the one who reaches out first. I send the “hey, how are you?” texts. I suggest meeting up. If I stop initiating, the conversations just die. People say, “We should hang out sometime,” but “sometime” never becomes a plan unless I push, and I’m tired of feeling like I’m begging for scraps of attention.

It messes with my head. I start wondering what’s wrong with me that no one seems to choose me. I see people posting pictures with their friends, going on trips, having game nights, and I’m just here, trying to convince myself that sitting alone in my room isn’t as bad as it feels.

The worst part is that I don’t even know how to fix it. I’ve tried hobbies, meetups, online communities. I meet people, but it never seems to move past that initial “new person” phase. It’s like everyone already has their circle, and I’m just orbiting on the outside, close enough to see it but not close enough to be in it.

I’m not even sure what I want from writing this. Maybe I just needed to say it somewhere.
