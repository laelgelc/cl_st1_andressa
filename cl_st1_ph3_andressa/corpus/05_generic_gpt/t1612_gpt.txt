I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

I’ve hit this weird point where I’m surrounded by people but feel like I don’t actually exist to any of them. I go to work, make small talk, answer emails, laugh in the right places, but it all feels like I’m playing a character someone else wrote. When the day ends, everyone goes back to their lives and I go back to my apartment that’s just…quiet.

What bothers me most isn’t the lack of people, it’s the lack of being known. I’ve had “friends” for years who don’t know basic things about me because I never say much, and when I finally try to open up even a little, the conversation somehow shifts back to them. I don’t blame them exactly; I just end up feeling like I’m not important enough to stick with.

There’s also this shame about being lonely as an adult. It feels like something I should have figured out already, like a life skill I somehow missed. I see people forming relationships so easily—partners, close friend groups, whatever—and I can’t understand what invisible rulebook they got that I didn’t.

I keep thinking I should “put myself out there,” but I’m tired of being the one who reaches out, initiates, follows up, and then watches things slowly fade. It makes me feel clingy and pathetic, even when I’m just trying to maintain normal human connection.

Mostly, I’m just tired of feeling like a background character in my own life.
