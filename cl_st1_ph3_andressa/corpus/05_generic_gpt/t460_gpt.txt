I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too visible at the same time.

Invisible, because no one really knows me. People know the surface stuff—job, hobbies, the polite answers. But the parts that actually hurt, the parts I think about at 2 a.m., never make it into conversations. I don’t even know how to bring them up without feeling like I’m making everything awkward.

Too visible, because everywhere I go I feel like I’m being silently judged for being alone. Eating alone, walking alone, going home alone. I see groups of friends laughing, couples leaning into each other on the train, and it feels like there’s some secret rulebook everyone else got and I missed.

I try to reach out sometimes, but it always feels like I’m intruding. People already have their circles, their “person,” their group chats. I’m the person people remember when they need something, or when they’re bored, but not the one they think of first. I’m not anyone’s favorite person. I’m just… optional.

What scares me is how normal this has started to feel. Waking up, going through the day, barely speaking to anyone beyond small talk. Pretending I’m fine because saying “I’m lonely” never seems to land anywhere. It just hangs in the air and then the topic changes.

I don’t even know what I’m expecting by writing this. Maybe I just needed to put it somewhere that isn’t my own head for once.
