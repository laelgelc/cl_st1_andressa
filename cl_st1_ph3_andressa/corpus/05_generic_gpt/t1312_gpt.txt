I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, or if I ever did.

I have “people” in my life on paper—coworkers, some old classmates on social media, family I text on holidays—but it all feels like background noise. No one really knows me, and I’m not sure anyone wants to. I keep conversations surface level because I’m terrified that if I show too much, they’ll quietly step back and I’ll have to watch it happen.

The weird part is that I’ve gotten good at performing “fine.” I can make small talk, crack a joke, play the role of the competent, functioning adult. Then I go home, close the door, and it’s just this heavy silence. I’ll scroll through my phone hoping someone messaged me, even though I already know they didn’t. I keep re-reading old chats just to remember what it felt like when someone seemed interested in me.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for—friendship, intimacy, just someone who actually asks follow-up questions. I just know that I’m tired of being the extra in everyone else’s story and never the person someone is genuinely excited to see.
