I’ve started to realize that I feel most alone when I’m technically “with” people.

I have coworkers, acquaintances, people I can message if I really need something. But I don’t feel like anyone actually knows me. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, shows, “how was your weekend?” I answer with some generic line and then feel this weird mix of frustration and relief that we didn’t go deeper.

I used to think loneliness meant having no one at all, but it’s more like being invisible while standing in the middle of a crowd. I’m there, but not really. People know the version of me that’s easy to digest: the one who jokes, who says “I’m fine,” who doesn’t make things awkward by being honest.

Part of it is on me. I hold back a lot. I don’t know how to go from small talk to “this is what actually scares me” without feeling like I’m oversharing or burdening someone. And when I do open up a little and the other person doesn’t really respond, I retreat even more.

Nights are the worst. That’s when I notice there’s no one I can text just to say, “Hey, I feel really off today,” without overthinking it for an hour first. I keep wondering if this is just adulthood, or if I somehow missed the point where everyone else learned how to actually connect.
