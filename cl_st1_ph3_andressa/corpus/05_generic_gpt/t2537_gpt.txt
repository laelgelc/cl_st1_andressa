I don’t really know how to explain this feeling without sounding dramatic, but I feel like a background character in my own life.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family I text occasionally—but I don’t feel *known* by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes, random small talk. Nobody asks follow-up questions, and I’ve started noticing that I don’t either, because I’m scared of seeming too intense or needy.

I scroll through group chats where I’m technically included, but I’m the one who chimes in every 30–40 messages with something that gets left on read. I’ll send something I think is funny or personal and then just watch the typing bubble appear for someone else replying to someone else. It’s like being in a room full of people facing slightly away from me.

I’ve tried putting myself out there—meetups, hobbies, online communities—but I always end up orbiting the edges. People are polite, sometimes even friendly, but it never crosses into “you matter to me specifically.” I can’t tell if I’m doing something wrong socially or if this is just how adulthood works for some of us.

The weirdest part is how invisible this feels. From the outside, my life probably looks fine. Inside, it feels like I’m slowly fading into the background and no one would really notice the difference.
