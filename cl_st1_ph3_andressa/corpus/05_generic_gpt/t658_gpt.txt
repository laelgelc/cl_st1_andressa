I’ve been feeling this really specific kind of loneliness lately that’s hard to explain to people in my life without sounding dramatic. It’s not just “I don’t have plans this weekend” lonely. It’s more like “I could disappear from everyone’s daily life and nothing would really change for them” lonely.

I have people around me, technically. Family, coworkers, a couple of casual friends I see sometimes. But I don’t feel known by any of them. Conversations are all surface-level: work, weather, memes, “how was your weekend?” and the usual “good, you?” script. I can be in a room full of people, even laughing, and still feel completely disconnected, like I’m behind glass.

What makes it worse is that I don’t really know how to fix it. I try to open up a bit and it either gets brushed off, or people do that awkward “oh damn, that sucks” and then change the subject. After enough of those, you just stop trying. It starts to feel safer to keep everything to yourself, even though that’s exactly what keeps the loneliness going.

I also have this constant fear that I’m too much and not enough at the same time. Too quiet, too awkward, too intense when I care, not interesting enough, not funny enough. I overthink every message I send, every pause in a conversation, every time someone leaves me on read. It all turns into “they don’t really want me around; they’re just being polite.”

What really hits me is the lack of someone who checks in unprompted. I’m always the one reaching out. If I stop initiating, my phone goes silent. Days can pass with no messages that aren’t from work or spam. It makes me wonder what it would feel like to be someone’s first thought instead of their afterthought—or not thought of at all.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for writing this. Maybe just proof that I exist somewhere outside my own head.
