I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, coworkers I can make small talk with. I can go through an entire day interacting with people and still come home feeling like I haven’t actually been seen or known by anyone. It’s like I’m a background character in other people’s stories.

What gets to me most is the silence when I get home. No one to message “I’m back,” no one to send a dumb meme to, no one who’s waiting to hear how my day went. I open my phone over and over like something might have changed in the last five minutes, but it’s always just group notifications or random spam. I scroll through conversations that faded out months ago and feel stupid for wanting to revive them.

I try to reach out sometimes, but I overthink every text. I worry I’m being annoying, or that they’re only replying out of politeness. When people cancel plans, I tell them it’s “no problem at all” but inside it feels like confirmation that I’m not really a priority to anyone. I watch other people just… exist together so easily, making plans, hanging out, having inside jokes. I feel like I missed some crucial step where everyone else learned how to be part of something.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. Waking up alone, doing everything alone, going to bed alone. Days blur together. Sometimes I wonder if I vanished completely, how long it would take before anyone actually noticed in a concrete way, not just “oh, they haven’t liked my post in a while.” And I don’t really know what to do with that thought.
