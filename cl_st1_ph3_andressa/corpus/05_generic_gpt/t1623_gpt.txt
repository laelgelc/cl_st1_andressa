I’ve been feeling like a background character in my own life lately.

I go through the motions—work, commute, errands—but it all feels like I’m moving in a world where everyone else already has their people. At work, conversations stop at small talk. I’m “nice,” “reliable,” but never the person anyone thinks to text on a weekend or invite to something that matters.

I try to reach out sometimes, but I end up overanalyzing every message I send. If someone takes a while to respond, my brain immediately jumps to, “They’re just being polite. You’re bothering them.” So I pull back, and the distance just gets wider.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through social media, watching other people’s group photos and inside jokes, and it feels like standing outside a house with the lights on, hearing laughter through the window. I’m not sure what’s wrong with me—whether I’m too quiet, too awkward, or just fundamentally forgettable—but it’s starting to feel less like a phase and more like who I am.
