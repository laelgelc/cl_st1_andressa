I’ve been feeling like I’m drifting further away from everyone, even when I’m technically “around” people.

I have coworkers, classmates, acquaintances I message sometimes, but it all feels like background noise. I can joke around, talk about work, comment on memes, but the second I try to say anything real, it’s like the conversation quietly dies. I don’t know if I’m oversharing, or if I’m just not the kind of person people want to know deeply.

What bothers me most is that I can’t remember the last time someone asked me a follow-up question about my life that wasn’t just small talk. I listen to other people, I remember details, I check in on them. But when it’s my turn, it feels like I’m talking into a void. I start editing myself down before I even speak, because I’m already expecting disinterest.

I’ve tried joining clubs, group chats, even going to events alone. I show up, I smile, I try to be approachable. People are polite, but it never seems to move past that. I leave feeling more alone than when I arrived, like I’ve just confirmed that I’m forgettable.

At home, the silence is loud. I’ll catch myself opening apps over and over, hoping someone messaged me, even though I know they didn’t. Sometimes I wonder if I’m the common denominator, if there’s something fundamentally off about me that I can’t see but everyone else can.

I don’t know what I’m looking for by posting this. Maybe just proof that I said it out loud somewhere.
