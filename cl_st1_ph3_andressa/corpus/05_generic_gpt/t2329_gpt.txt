I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels baked into my life now.

On paper I’m doing okay. I have a job, I pay my bills, I can make small talk with coworkers. But it all feels like I’m acting out a role instead of actually being part of anything. I go home, close the door, and it’s like I disappear from the world until the next workday.

What really gets to me is how invisible I feel. I can go days without anyone saying my name. If I don’t message people first, my phone is just silent. I scroll through chats where I used to talk to people regularly and see how the conversations just slowly faded out, like we both quietly agreed I wasn’t worth the effort.

I’m not completely socially inept, but I feel like I’m always “almost” part of things. Almost a friend, almost invited, almost included. People seem to like me in low doses, but no one seems to choose me when it actually matters—when plans are made, when deeper conversations happen.

The worst part is how it’s changing me. I’m starting to hesitate before reaching out because I’m tired of feeling like I’m intruding. I second-guess every message, every “hey, how are you?” because I don’t want to be that annoying person who doesn’t get the hint.

I don’t really know what to do with this, so I’m just writing it here.
