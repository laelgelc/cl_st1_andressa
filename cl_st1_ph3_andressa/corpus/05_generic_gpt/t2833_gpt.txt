I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my circumstances on paper. I have people around me. I go to work, I make small talk, I answer “how are you?” with “fine” and even manage to sound convincing. But it all feels like I’m acting in a play where everyone else got the script and I’m just improvising lines that sound close enough.

What hits me the most is how replaceable I feel. If I disappeared tomorrow, people might notice for a bit, but their lives would keep moving the same way. I don’t feel like a “main character” in anyone’s life, more like an extra in the background of other people’s stories. I’m there, but not really needed.

I try to reach out sometimes, but conversations feel shallow. I don’t know how to move from “how’s work?” to “I’m actually not okay” without feeling like I’m burdening someone. And when I do open up a little, I start second-guessing everything I said and convince myself I overshared or sounded needy.

Nights are the worst. That’s when the silence feels loud. I scroll through my phone, stare at chats where the last message is from me, left on read or just ignored, and I tell myself they’re busy, but it still stings. I start wondering what’s wrong with me that I can be surrounded by people and still feel like I’m on the outside of a glass wall, watching everyone else live.
