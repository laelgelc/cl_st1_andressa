I’m starting to realize I’m not just “going through a phase,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels structural, like it’s built into my life now.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers I chat with, family I see on holidays, a couple of old friends I text every few weeks. But it all feels like background noise. I don’t feel known by anyone, not in the way I secretly wish I could be. Most of my conversations stay on the surface: work, memes, complaining about being tired. I can’t remember the last time someone asked me how I really was and actually wanted the full answer.

Nights are the worst. I come home, make dinner, scroll my phone, watch something, and suddenly it’s midnight. Hours pass without my voice being used. It’s like I’m fading into the walls of my own life. Even when I’m around people, I feel like I’m watching myself from a distance, playing the role of “normal person” while something tight and heavy sits in my chest.

I’ve tried to “fix” it in all the usual ways—hobbies, clubs, apps, small talk with strangers. I can get to the “friendly acquaintance” stage, but there’s this invisible barrier I can’t seem to cross. I either overshare and feel stupid afterward, or I hold back and stay vague and then feel fake. I end up replaying every interaction in my head, picking apart things I said, convincing myself that I sounded weird or needy.

What hurts the most is feeling like I’m always the one who cares more. I initiate, I check in, I remember birthdays, and if I stop doing that, the connection just… dies. It makes me wonder if I’m fundamentally forgettable, like I only exist when I’m useful or entertaining.

I don’t really know what I’m looking for by writing this. I just needed to put into words that this constant quiet, this feeling of being adjacent to everyone else’s life, is starting to feel less like a mood and more like who I am now.
