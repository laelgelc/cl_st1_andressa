I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s changing who I am.

My days are basically work, commute, phone, sleep. I talk to coworkers about tasks and deadlines, then go home and maybe exchange a few memes in group chats. No one really knows what’s going on with me beyond “busy” or “tired.” I don’t even know how to answer “How are you?” without lying or oversharing.

What messes with my head is that I technically “have people” in my life. Old friends I see a couple times a year, family that checks in occasionally. But there’s no one I can text at 2 a.m. and say, “I feel empty and I don’t know why.” I scroll through social media and it feels like I’m watching life through a window, like I’m an extra in everyone else’s story.

I’ve also started dodging invitations because I feel out of practice being around people. Then I feel more isolated, which makes it harder next time. It’s like I’m slowly erasing myself from other people’s lives without really meaning to.
