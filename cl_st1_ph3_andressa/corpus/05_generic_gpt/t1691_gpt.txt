I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too exposed at the same time.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I talk to coworkers, I answer messages in group chats, I say “yeah, we should hang out sometime” to people I barely know. But when I go home and shut the door, it’s like the whole day collapses into this heavy silence. I don’t feel connected to anyone in a way that actually matters.

I miss the feeling of being known. Not “how’s work?” known, but the kind of known where you don’t have to explain your backstory or justify why you’re quiet, or tired, or just not okay. Lately I’ve realized I don’t really have that with anyone. I’m always playing a role: the competent coworker, the “I’m fine” friend, the person who laughs at the right times so nobody asks questions.

I keep thinking it’s my fault. I’m not interesting enough, not funny enough, not spontaneous enough. I overthink every interaction, replaying conversations and cringing at things I said or didn’t say. The result is I pull back even more, which just makes everything worse. It feels like I’m watching life through a window instead of actually living it.

What makes it harder is that I can’t point to one big reason. Nothing dramatic happened. It’s just this slow, quiet drifting away from everyone, including myself. I look at my phone and there’s nothing there I’m really wanted for. Just notifications that don’t mean much.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I just needed to admit, somewhere, that I feel really alone.
