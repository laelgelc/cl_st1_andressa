I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, no huge crisis going on. I talk to people every day: coworkers, the barista, neighbors in the hallway. But it all feels like background noise. Nothing sticks. No one actually knows me, and I’m starting to feel like maybe there’s nothing worth knowing.

I scroll through my phone and realize I don’t have anyone I can text without overthinking it. Even with the few people I might reach out to, I rehearse what to say, delete it, rewrite it, then end up sending nothing because I convince myself I’m being annoying. I watch the “typing…” bubble when they do respond and feel guilty for taking up their time. It’s exhausting.

Weekends are the worst. I wake up, check my notifications, see nothing, and then just kind of… drift. Sometimes I’ll pretend I’m busy if anyone asks what I did, but honestly it’s usually just me sitting in my room, scrolling, listening to the same songs, staring at the same walls. I’ll hear people outside laughing or heading somewhere together and it feels like there’s this invisible glass between me and the rest of the world.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like everyone suggests. Joined a couple of group things, tried talking more at work, even forced myself to go to a social event alone. I end up standing there, feeling like an intruder in other people’s conversations, nodding and smiling until I can leave without it being too obvious. People are polite, but it never goes beyond that. I walk home feeling more alone than before.

The thing that scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. I’m getting used to not expecting messages, to eating alone, to having entire days where the only voice I hear is my own. And when I do finally talk to someone, I feel rusty, like I’ve forgotten how to be a person around other people.

I don’t know what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just wanted to say it somewhere, because keeping it all in my head is starting to feel heavier than the loneliness itself.
