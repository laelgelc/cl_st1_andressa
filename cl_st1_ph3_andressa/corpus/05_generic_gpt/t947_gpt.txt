I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening in a soundproof room.

I have “people” in my life—coworkers, acquaintances, a couple of old friends I text every now and then. On paper I’m not isolated. But it all feels so shallow. I show up, I smile, I make small talk about work and TV shows and “how was your weekend,” and then I go home and it’s like I vanish. No one really notices the gap between the version of me they see and the one that comes back to an empty room.

What’s been bothering me most is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped reaching out, most conversations would just quietly die. If I disappeared from social media, I don’t think anyone would do more than a quick “hey, you okay?” once, maybe twice, and then move on. I’m not angry about it, just… weirdly resigned.

I miss feeling known. Not in a romantic way necessarily (though that would be nice), but just having someone who actually remembers small details, who asks follow-up questions, who notices when my tone changes. Sometimes I try to open up a bit more, and it usually gets brushed off with a joke or a “same lol,” and I retreat back into safer, surface-level stuff.

The worst part is how loneliness starts to mess with your sense of self. I second-guess everything I say, I overanalyze messages, I delete texts before sending them because I don’t want to be “too much.” I’ve started to talk less and less, online and in person, because it feels like I’m always the one trying to keep the connection alive.

Anyway, I don’t really have a point. I just needed to put this somewhere that doesn’t feel like I’m burdening anyone.
