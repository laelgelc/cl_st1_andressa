I don’t really know how to explain this feeling without sounding dramatic, but lately it’s like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, a couple of people I can text if I really need something. I’m not completely isolated in the stereotypical sense. But I feel like a ghost in every room I walk into. I show up, I do what’s expected, I make the right small talk, and then I go home and it’s just… silence. The kind that feels heavy, not peaceful.

I’ve noticed that I’m always the one who reaches out. I’m the one who sends the “hey, how are you?” messages, the one who suggests meeting up. If I stop initiating, everything just stops. No one checks in. Days go by, then weeks, and my phone is just this quiet rectangle that might as well be a paperweight. It makes me start to wonder if people like me only when I’m convenient or useful, not because they actually want me around.

What makes it worse is that I’ve become hyper-aware of how “extra” I feel when I try to connect. I replay conversations in my head and pick apart every word, every expression from the other person. Did I overshare? Did I talk too much? Did I sound boring? There’s this constant fear that I’m somehow too much and not enough at the same time.

I keep seeing people online talking about their “group” or “best friend since forever” or spontaneous plans, and I genuinely cannot relate. I don’t have that person I can message at 2am when my brain is spiraling. I don’t have anyone who knows the day-to-day details of my life. I just sort of… exist parallel to everyone else.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head, because carrying it around alone all the time is starting to feel like dragging a weight I can’t set down anywhere.
