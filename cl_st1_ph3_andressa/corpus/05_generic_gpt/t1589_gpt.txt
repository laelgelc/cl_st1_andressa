Lately I feel like I’m watching my own life from behind glass. I’m “busy” enough—work, errands, small talk with coworkers—but nothing feels like a real connection. I go home, unlock the door, and the silence hits harder than any noise ever could. I scroll through chats I’m too scared to start, stare at group photos I’m not in, then convince myself people already have enough friends and don’t need another awkward extra.

What bothers me is how invisible this all is. From the outside, I probably look fine: functioning, independent, “doing well.” Inside, it feels like I’m slowly fading out of focus. I miss being truly known by someone, having a person who asks how I am and actually wants the long, messy answer.
