I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into the usual “I have no friends” narrative. I technically have people in my life: coworkers I get along with, a couple of old friends I message every now and then, family I can visit. But I still feel like I’m completely on my own, like I’m living parallel to everyone instead of with them.

The part that gets to me is how invisible it all feels. From the outside, I probably look fine. I show up to work, I make small talk, I answer “I’m good, you?” automatically. Then I go home and there’s this silence that feels louder than any noise. I scroll through my phone hoping someone messaged me first, and when no one has, I just… put it down and pretend I don’t care.

I’ve tried reaching out more, but it feels forced. I overthink every message: “Am I being annoying? Am I asking for too much attention? Do they actually want to talk to me or are they just being polite?” So I end up backing off, then feel hurt that no one chases after me. It’s like I’m creating my own isolation and still resenting it.

What bothers me most is the sense that no one really knows me. People know the version of me that’s easy to digest: quiet, helpful, “chill.” But the parts that feel messy or needy or sad stay hidden, because I’m scared that if I show them, people will pull away. And at the same time, not showing them means no one can actually get close.

I don’t even know what I’m expecting by writing this. I just needed to put it somewhere that doesn’t immediately bounce back with “you’ll be fine” or “just go out more.”
