I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my life on paper. I have coworkers, some acquaintances, group chats that light up all day, but I still feel like I’m standing behind a glass wall watching everyone else actually live.

When I do try to open up, it’s like the conversation skims across the surface and then drifts back to memes, weekend plans, whatever. I don’t think I’ve had a conversation in a long time where I felt truly seen, where I could say what’s actually going on in my head without worrying I’m being “too much” or killing the vibe.

I scroll a lot, lurk on places like this, and it’s strange how reading other people’s loneliness makes me feel less alone and more alone at the same time. Like, there are clearly so many of us, but we’re all stuck in our own separate rooms.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to put the feeling somewhere outside my own head for once.
