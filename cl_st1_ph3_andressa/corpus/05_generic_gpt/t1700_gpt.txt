I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match what my life looks like on paper. I have people around me. I talk to coworkers, I respond in group chats, I send “lol” and emojis like everyone else. But I don’t feel actually connected to anyone. It’s like I’m acting out the role of a social person without ever being one.

I keep noticing how replaceable I feel. If I stopped messaging, I think most people would just… stop too. No one really checks in unless I initiate, and even then it feels surface-level. I can’t remember the last time someone asked me a question that went deeper than “How’s work?” or “What are you up to?” and actually waited for a real answer.

I’m also guilty of hiding. I downplay everything because I don’t want to be “too much” or drain people. When I do try to open up, I feel stupid halfway through and change the subject. I’ve trained myself to be “easy to be around” and now I’m stuck with this version of myself that no one really knows.

The worst part is how lonely I feel even when I’m physically with others. I can be sitting at a table, laughing at the right times, and still have this hollow sensation in my chest like I’m watching my own life from the outside. Then I go home and replay every interaction, convinced I was awkward or forgettable.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for exactly. A person who actually wants to know me? A place where I don’t feel like I have to constantly perform? I just know that whatever this is right now doesn’t feel like being alive so much as just continuing.
