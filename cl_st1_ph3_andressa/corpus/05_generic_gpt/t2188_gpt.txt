I don’t even know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… background noise. Like I’m there, but not really part of anything.

I have people around me technically. Coworkers, some old friends I text once in a while, family I see on holidays. But there’s this constant sense that I’m always slightly outside the circle, watching everyone else actually connect while I just hover nearby, trying not to seem too desperate or weird.

I keep catching myself rehearsing conversations in my head before I send a text, then deleting it because it feels like I’m bothering people. I’ll type “hey, how’ve you been?” and then think, “If they wanted to talk, they would’ve messaged me first,” and I just erase it. Then I sit there feeling lonely and also stupid for not reaching out. It’s like I’m stuck between wanting connection and being terrified of rejection, and I end up choosing silence every time.

Nights are the worst. I get home, scroll through social media, and it’s just photos of people doing life together. Group dinners, weekend trips, game nights. I’m happy for them in theory, but it also feels like there’s this invisible world everyone else got invited to and my invitation got lost in the mail. I eat alone, watch some show, and try not to think too much, but the quiet is so loud.

What messes with me is that I don’t even know who I’d call if something really good or really bad happened. Like, who is my “first person”? I don’t think I have one. I have people I could technically contact, but no one where I feel fully safe just showing up as I am, without editing myself or worrying I’m “too much.”

I’m not even sure what I’m looking for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to say out loud that I feel invisible most of the time, and it’s starting to scare me how normal that’s beginning to feel.
