I’m starting to feel like I’m disappearing in plain sight.

I have people around me—coworkers, family, a couple of “friends”—but I feel like an extra in everyone else’s movie. Conversations stay on the surface. I listen to other people’s problems, but when I try to open up, the vibe shifts or the topic changes. It makes me feel… embarrassing, somehow.

I go days without a genuine, unfiltered conversation. I overthink every message I send, then delete it because I don’t want to be “too much.” Then I complain to myself that nobody checks in.

I miss feeling known. Not liked, not tolerated—actually known. The version of me that laughs at work or makes small talk at gatherings feels like a mask that’s getting harder to peel off when I’m alone.
