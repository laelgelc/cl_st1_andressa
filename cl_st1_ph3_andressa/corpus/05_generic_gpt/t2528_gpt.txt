I keep realizing that most of my “connections” are just context-based. I talk to coworkers at work, classmates in class, neighbors in the hallway. But once we step out of those spaces, it’s like I vanish from their minds. No one texts just to check in. If I stop initiating, everything goes silent.

What bothers me isn’t being physically alone; it’s feeling like there’s no one who actually knows me. People know the version of me that jokes in meetings or helps with projects, not the one who goes home and eats dinner in front of a screen because there’s no one to talk to.

I try to be open, but it always feels one-sided. I listen to other people’s problems, remember their important days, send messages when they seem down. When it’s my turn to struggle, I stare at my contacts list and realize there isn’t a single person I feel comfortable bothering.

It makes me question whether I’m just fundamentally forgettable, like background noise in everyone else’s lives.
