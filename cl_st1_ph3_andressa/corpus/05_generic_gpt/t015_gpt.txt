I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into the usual “I have no friends” box. On paper, I’m not completely alone. I have a few people I talk to, coworkers I get along with, family I can text if I need something. But I still feel like I’m living behind glass.

I notice it most in the small moments. I’ll see something funny or interesting and my first instinct is to share it, and then I realize there’s no one I feel comfortable sending it to. Or I’ll open a chat app, scroll through the list of names, and close it again because I can’t think of anyone who would actually want to hear from me without it feeling forced.

I’m tired of being the “background character” in my own social life. I’m always the one invited as part of a group, rarely someone’s first choice. If I stopped reaching out, I have this sinking feeling that most people would just…never notice. It’s not that they’re cruel. It’s more like I’m just not significant enough to register.

What makes it worse is that I struggle to really let people in. I can do small talk, I can be “pleasant,” but when it comes to saying, “Hey, I’m actually not okay,” I freeze up. I don’t know if it’s fear of burdening people or just not trusting that they’ll stay if they see what’s really going on in my head. So I keep it all surface-level, and then feel unseen because no one knows the deeper parts of me. It’s a loop I don’t know how to break.

Nights are the hardest. I’ll lie in bed scrolling, not even taking anything in, just trying to distract myself from the quiet. There’s this ache that shows up then, like a physical weight in my chest. I imagine what it would be like to have someone I could text, “I can’t sleep, my brain won’t shut up,” and actually believe they’d want to answer.

I don’t really have a point or a plan. I just needed to put this somewhere, to admit out loud that I feel disconnected and kind of invisible. It’s strange how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re standing alone in an empty room.
