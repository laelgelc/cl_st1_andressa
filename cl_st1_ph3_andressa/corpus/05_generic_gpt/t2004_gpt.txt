I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m putting it here instead.

Most days feel like I’m watching my own life from the outside. I go to work, I make small talk, I answer emails, I laugh at the right spots. People would probably say I’m “fine.” But when I get home and close the door, it’s like the whole world just powers down and I’m left with this heavy, dull silence that feels louder than anything.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, acquaintances, a few old friends I text every now and then. But I don’t feel like I belong to anyone’s life in a real way. I’m always the one who can be replaced at plans without anything changing. If I don’t message people first, my phone can go days without lighting up for anything that isn’t an app notification or spam.

I keep thinking I must be doing something wrong. I try to be kind, I listen, I show up when people need help, but it doesn’t translate into closeness. It’s like there’s a pane of glass between me and everyone else. I can see them, hear them, but I can’t actually get through.

The worst part is how loneliness messes with my sense of self. When you go long enough without being really seen or chosen, it starts to feel like maybe there just isn’t much there to see. I second-guess every message I send, every invitation I consider extending, because I’m half-convinced I’m just bothering people.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to put these feelings somewhere outside my own head for once.
