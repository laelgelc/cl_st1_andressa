I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m just drifting through my own life as a background character.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family chats that light up my phone. But I don’t feel *connected* to any of it. It’s like watching a group conversation through a window. I can see everyone laughing, reacting, being part of something, and I’m just… pressing my face against the glass.

What messes with my head is that from the outside, I probably look fine. I go to work, make small talk, send memes, answer “how are you?” with “I’m good, you?” like it’s automatic. No one sees that once I get home, it’s just silence. I put my bag down, sit on the bed, and there’s this heavy, hollow feeling. Like I’m missing something I can’t even name.

I don’t think I’ve ever had that “person” people talk about. The one you can message without overthinking, the one who actually checks in because they want to, not because it’s polite. I have acquaintances who are nice enough, but I always feel like a temporary guest in their lives, never someone whose absence would really matter.

I overanalyze everything I say. I replay conversations and convince myself I sounded weird or annoying. Then I pull back a bit, respond slower, become “busy,” and that just reinforces the distance. I know I’m part of my own problem, but knowing that doesn’t magically fix it. It just adds guilt on top of the loneliness.

Sometimes I scroll through social media and see people doing the most mundane things together—grocery shopping, watching movies, sitting on the floor eating takeout—and it feels like another universe. I’m not even craving big parties or constant attention. I just want to feel like I belong somewhere specific, with someone specific.

Most days I just go through the motions, hoping something will eventually shift, but I honestly don’t know what I’m waiting for or what I’m supposed to do differently. It just feels like I’m slowly fading out around the edges and no one’s really noticing.
