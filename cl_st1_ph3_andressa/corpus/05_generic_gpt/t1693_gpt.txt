I feel like I’ve hit that stage where I’m not even sure what kind of connection I’m looking for anymore, I just know that what I have isn’t enough.

On paper I’m “fine.” I go to work, I’m polite, I answer “not bad, you?” when people ask how I am. I have coworkers I can chat with, a couple of old friends I text memes to. But there’s this constant, low-level ache under everything, like I’m permanently slightly homesick for a place I’ve never actually been.

What bothers me most is how invisible it feels. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m pressed up against thick glass, watching everyone else actually participate in life. They laugh, they lean into each other’s space, they have shared stories and inside jokes. I’m physically there but I don’t feel like I exist in the same way they do.

I’ve tried the usual advice: hobbies, meetups, apps. I can go, I can hold a conversation, but it never seems to move past that shallow, surface level. I leave with a few new numbers in my phone that I never end up using, because I don’t know how to bridge that gap from “we talked once” to “we actually matter to each other.”

The weirdest part is how it messes with my sense of self. When no one really knows you deeply, you start wondering if there’s even anything there to know. I catch myself editing my personality down to whatever seems most acceptable in the moment, then going home and feeling even more hollow because I’ve just reinforced the idea that the real me doesn’t belong anywhere.
