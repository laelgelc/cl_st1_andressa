I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m fading out of my own life.

I go through the motions: work, errands, scrolling, sleep. I talk to people every day, but it’s all surface-level stuff—weather, tasks, memes. No one really knows what’s going on in my head, and I don’t know how to let them in without feeling like I’m burdening them or oversharing.

What hurts the most is that I used to have “my people.” Group chats that were active all the time, late-night calls, inside jokes. Now those same chats are dead or I’m just the person who reacts with an emoji while everyone else actually talks. When I try to start a conversation, it fizzles out so fast that I start wondering if I’m just bad at being a person.

I keep thinking that if I disappeared, it would take a while before anyone noticed. Not in a dramatic, self-harm way—more like I’ve become background noise in other people’s lives. And I don’t know how to move myself back into focus.
