I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m living behind glass. I go to work, I respond to messages, I make small talk, I even laugh at the right times. But it feels like I’m watching someone who looks like me going through the motions, while the real me is somewhere else, untouched by any of it.

What messes with my head is that from the outside, I don’t look lonely. I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I still text, family I see on holidays. But none of it feels like connection. Conversations stay on the surface. I can’t remember the last time I said something real and didn’t immediately regret it or feel like I’d overshared.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like people always say. Joined a hobby group, went to a few meetups, tried talking more in group chats. Every time, I end up feeling like the extra person no one would really notice missing. People are friendly, but I don’t feel chosen. I feel tolerated.

The worst part is this growing sense that I’m becoming harder to know. The longer I go without genuine closeness, the rustier I get at opening up. I second-guess every message I send, every invitation I think about extending. I assume I’m annoying, or too much, or not enough, depending on the day.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I guess I just wanted to put into words that very specific kind of loneliness where you’re surrounded by people, but still feel like no one is really “with” you, and you’re not sure if it’s them, or you, or something in you that quietly broke a long time ago.
