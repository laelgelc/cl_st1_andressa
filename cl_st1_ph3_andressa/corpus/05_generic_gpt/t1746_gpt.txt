I’ve been feeling like a ghost in my own life lately.

I’m surrounded by people—coworkers, neighbors, family chats buzzing on my phone—but nothing feels like real connection. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else live in real time while I’m on some kind of delay.

I have acquaintances, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something went wrong. I scroll through old conversations and realize most of them died because I stopped reaching out first. It’s like I’m always “too much” when I try, and “not enough” when I pull back.

I’ve started talking less, even when I’m with others, because I feel like I’m boring or draining. Then I go home and replay every small interaction, convinced I said something weird or off. It’s exhausting.

Sometimes I wonder if there’s just something fundamentally unlikable about me that I can’t see but everyone else can. I keep thinking, “Next month will be different, I’ll put myself out there,” and then another month goes by and nothing changes.

I’m not even sure what I’m looking for writing this. Maybe just proof that I still exist to someone, somewhere, even if it’s just as words on a screen.
