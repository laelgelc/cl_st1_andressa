I don’t know when “being alone” quietly shifted into “being lonely,” but it feels like I missed the moment where it became a problem.

My days are technically full—work, commuting, scrolling, podcasts in my ears—but I can’t shake the sense that I’m just background noise in my own life. I have acquaintances I can joke with, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something really went wrong. Even the people who say “let’s hang out sometime” never follow through, and I’m tired of wondering if that’s about them or about me.

What makes it worse is how invisible it all is. From the outside, I probably look functional. Inside, it’s like living in a house with all the lights off. I keep thinking I should “put myself out there,” but I don’t know where “there” even is anymore, or if anyone would notice if I showed up.
