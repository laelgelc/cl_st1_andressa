I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into any neat category. I’m not totally isolated: I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up on holidays. But it all feels… thin. Like I’m living behind a pane of glass and everyone else is on the other side, muffled.

I notice it most in the small moments. Walking home and realizing I don’t have anyone I’d naturally call just to talk about nothing. Sitting in my room at night, scrolling, watching other people’s lives play out in little squares—friends hanging out, couples cooking together, group photos—and feeling like I’m watching a show I was never cast in.

When I do talk to people, I feel like I’m playing a role: the “fine” version of myself. I don’t know how to bridge that gap between surface-level conversation and actually letting someone see that I feel disconnected and kind of empty most of the time. It’s like I’ve forgotten how to be close to anyone, and the longer it goes on, the harder it is to imagine changing it.

There’s also this shame attached to it. I’m an adult; I’m “supposed” to have a solid circle by now. Admitting I’m lonely feels like admitting I’ve failed at something basic that everyone else figured out. So I just keep going through the motions and hoping it bothers me less than it actually does.
