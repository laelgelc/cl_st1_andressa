I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper things aren’t even that bad. I have a job, an apartment, some acquaintances, a family that isn’t terrible. But most days I feel like I’m watching everything through a window. I go to work, I make small talk, I answer emails, I come home, I scroll, I sleep. There’s almost no one I can text just to say, “Today sucked,” and actually expect a real conversation, not just a thumbs up or a “same.”

What bothers me most is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped showing up anywhere, I think people would notice in a logistical way, not an emotional one. Like, “Who’s covering this shift?” rather than “Is [my name] okay?” I’m not anyone’s first choice for anything. Not first call, not first invite, not first thought.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like everyone suggests—meetups, hobbies, talking more at work—but everything feels shallow. I can make people laugh, I can be friendly, but it never crosses that invisible line into actual closeness. I leave events feeling more alone than when I arrived, because it just reminds me that I’m always on the outside of other people’s already-formed circles.

What scares me is how normalized it’s becoming. I used to get hit with waves of sadness about it; now it’s more like a dull background noise I live with. I don’t even know what I’d do with real connection if it showed up. Part of me wants it desperately, another part has started to assume it’s just not for me.

I’m not even sure why I’m posting this. I guess I just wanted to put into words that weird, quiet kind of loneliness where nothing is obviously “wrong,” but everything feels slightly empty and like it’s going to stay that way.
