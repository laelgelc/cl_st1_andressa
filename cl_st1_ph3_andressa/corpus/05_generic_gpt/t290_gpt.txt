I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m disappearing from my own life.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I answer emails, I show up to meetings, I make small talk about weather and TV shows. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice.” The weird thing is, I’m almost never actually seen beyond that. It’s like I’m an NPC in my own story.

I go entire weeks without a single conversation that goes past surface level. No one asks how I really am, and I’m not sure I’d know how to answer if they did. When something good happens, there’s no one I feel comfortable messaging first. When something bad happens, I just sort of… absorb it alone, like a sponge that never gets wrung out.

I try to reach out sometimes, but it always feels like I’m interrupting other people’s real lives. I send a message, they respond hours or days later with “sorry, been so busy!” and then it dies. I can’t tell if I’m boring, or if everyone is just exhausted, or if I somehow missed the part of life where you’re supposed to form deep connections.

Nights are the worst. I scroll through my phone, not because anything is interesting, but because the silence feels too loud. I keep thinking about how, if I just stopped showing up anywhere, it would take a long time for anyone to actually notice in a concrete way, not just “oh, haven’t seen you online.”

I’m not sure what I’m looking for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted proof that I existed somewhere outside my own head, even if it’s just as text on a screen.
