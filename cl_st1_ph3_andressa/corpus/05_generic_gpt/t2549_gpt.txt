I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore.

I have acquaintances: coworkers I joke with, neighbors I nod at, people who’d say “hey, long time” if we ran into each other. But there’s no one I can text at 2 a.m. when my chest feels too tight and my brain won’t shut up. No one who really knows what my days look like beyond “work was fine.”

I scroll through chats where I was the last one to send a message months ago and never followed up because I didn’t want to seem needy. Then I get stuck in this loop: I’m lonely, but I’m terrified of being annoying, so I stay quiet, which just makes the distance worse.

Even when I’m around people, I feel like I’m behind glass. I’m there, laughing at the right spots, but it’s like I’m performing a version of myself that people tolerate, not someone they’d actually choose to be close to.

I keep wondering if this is just how adulthood is for some of us: everyone busy, everyone tired, and some of us slowly disappearing in the spaces between other people’s real lives.
