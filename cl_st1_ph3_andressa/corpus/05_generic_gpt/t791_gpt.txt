I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family chats that light up my phone. But it all feels like background noise. I go through the motions—work, small talk, scrolling, sleep—and there’s this constant, low-level ache that never really goes away. It’s like being homesick for a place that doesn’t exist.

I keep thinking how strange it is that I can be “reachable” 24/7 and still feel like I don’t actually exist to anyone in a real way. No one really asks how I am beyond the polite version. And when they do, I don’t know how to answer without sounding like I’m dumping too much on them, so I just say I’m fine and change the subject.

I’ve noticed I’ve started editing myself down in conversations—keeping things light, not sharing anything that might make me seem “too much.” The result is that the version of me people interact with feels like a cardboard cutout. Then I go home and realize no one has seen the real me in a very long time, maybe ever.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just needed to put into words that I feel invisible, even when people are looking right at me.
