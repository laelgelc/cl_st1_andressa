I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into any of the usual categories people talk about. It’s not that I’m completely isolated. I have a job, I talk to coworkers, I respond in group chats, I say “fine” when people ask how I’m doing. On paper, it probably looks like I’m functioning. But it feels like I’m just acting out the role of a person who’s okay, instead of actually being okay.

What’s bothering me most is that I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely seen. Conversations feel like passing a ball back and forth without anyone really looking at the person throwing it. We joke, we talk about shows, work, small annoyances. But the moment I try to go even slightly deeper, I feel this invisible wall go up—either in them or in me. I back off, change the subject, and then later I’m stuck replaying it and wondering why I even tried.

I think I’ve slowly made myself smaller over time. I don’t share much. I don’t initiate plans. I wait to be invited, and when I’m not, I tell myself I didn’t want to go anyway. Then I sit at home scrolling through people’s stories and feeling like I’m pressed up against the glass of everyone else’s life.

There’s also this shame that comes with it. Like I should have figured out how to make and keep close friends by now. Other people seem to naturally fall into groups, relationships, routines. I’m just… drifting. I keep telling myself I’ll try harder “soon”—message someone first, suggest meeting up, be more open—but when the moment comes I freeze and do nothing.

So the days blur together, and I just keep carrying this quiet, dull ache around, not really knowing what to do with it.
