I’m starting to realize that I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s changing who I am.

On paper, my life looks okay. I have a job, a place to live, people I can text if I really need something. But I don’t feel like I exist in anyone’s daily life. No one checks in just because. Weeks go by without my phone lighting up unless it’s a notification from an app.

When I’m around people, I feel like I’m watching myself from the outside, trying to say the right things so I don’t seem weird or needy. I leave conversations replaying everything I said, convinced I overshared or was boring. Then I pull back, which just makes the distance worse.

It messes with my sense of reality. I start wondering if my memories are even accurate, because there’s no one else who shares them with me. No “remember when we…” with anyone. Just me, scrolling, working, eating, sleeping.

The worst part is how small my world has become. I used to have hobbies I cared about, but doing them alone all the time drained the joy out of them. Now I mostly just numb myself with the internet and background noise so I don’t have to sit in the quiet and feel how empty everything is.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. I just needed to say it somewhere.
