I’m starting to realize that I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s rewiring my brain.

I have people around me in the basic sense—coworkers, neighbors, some old friends in group chats—but I don’t feel *known* by anyone. Every interaction feels like I’m playing a character: slightly funny, slightly competent, never too honest. If I try to be real, I feel like I’m oversharing or making it awkward, so I retreat again.

Nights are the worst. I’ll scroll, watch videos, lurk on here, and feel this weird mix of numbness and panic. I want to reach out to someone, but I don’t know who wouldn’t see it as a burden or just…weird. My phone almost never lights up unless it’s a notification from an app.

What scares me most is how quickly I’m getting used to this. Like I’m slowly accepting that this is just what my life is going to look like.
