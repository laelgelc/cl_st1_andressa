I’ve been feeling like a background character in my own life lately.

I’m surrounded by people on paper — coworkers, acquaintances, even family — but it all feels thin, like I’m talking through glass. Conversations stay on safe topics: work, weather, random memes. I can’t remember the last time I said something honest and messy to someone in person and felt like they actually wanted to hear it.

What makes it worse is that I’m starting to forget how to connect. I overthink every message, every invite, every silence. I replay interactions in my head and convince myself I came off weird or annoying, so I retreat more. Then I feel even more disconnected, and the cycle just keeps looping.

Nights are the hardest. That’s when the “no one really knows you” feeling hits. I scroll, I lurk, I watch other people joke and vent and share, and I feel like I’m pressed up against the window of a party I was never actually invited to.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this.
