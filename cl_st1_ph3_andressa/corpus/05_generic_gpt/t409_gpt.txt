I don’t even know where to start with this, but I’m so tired of feeling like a background character in my own life.

I’m surrounded by people in the most technical sense—coworkers, neighbors I nod at, family chats that mostly share memes—but I don’t feel like anyone actually knows me. Not the real me, the one that has thoughts at 2 a.m., the one that’s scared and confused and sometimes just wants someone to sit in silence with.

I keep thinking I’m the common denominator. Like, if I were more interesting, more funny, more something, people would actually want to stick around. I’ve had friendships fade so many times that I’ve started preemptively distancing myself because I assume it’s only a matter of time before they get bored or busy or find better people. It’s like I’m watching everyone else move on to the “next stage” of life—relationships, kids, tight friend groups, group vacations—and I’m stuck in this loop of work, home, screen, sleep.

I try to initiate things: messages, invitations, “hey, we should catch up sometime.” People say yes in theory, but it rarely turns into anything. When it does, it’s surface-level small talk. I walk away feeling more alone than when I was just by myself, because it reminds me how far away real connection feels.

The worst part is how invisible the loneliness is. From the outside I probably look fine—functional, put together. Inside it feels like an empty room with the lights off. I honestly can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely understood by someone, without having to downplay parts of myself or pretend I’m okay when I’m not.
