I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life without sounding dramatic, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people on paper. Coworkers, family chats, a couple of old friends I exchange memes with. But I feel like I’m behind glass all the time. I can see everyone, hear them, even interact, but nothing actually touches me. It’s like I’m playing a character who has “enough” social contact, while the real me is somewhere in the back, untouched and starving.

I’ve tried to fix it in all the usual ways. Joined groups, messaged people first, said yes to plans even when I was exhausted. I can have decent conversations, even laugh, but afterwards I walk home feeling weirdly hollow, like I was an extra in someone else’s life.

What I miss isn’t just “friends” in the abstract. I miss being known. I miss someone noticing when my voice changes and asking what’s actually wrong. I miss not having to translate everything into small talk.

The worst part is how it starts to feel like it’s my fault—like there’s something fundamentally off about me that people sense and quietly step away from. And I don’t know how to prove that wrong when I’m the only one here.
