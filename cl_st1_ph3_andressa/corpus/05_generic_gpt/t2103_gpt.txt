I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone,” I’m actually lonely, and it’s leaking into everything.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats—but I don’t feel known by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, memes. No one asks how I really am, and I don’t know how to answer honestly anymore without feeling like I’m oversharing or being dramatic.

Nights are the worst. I scroll, watch random videos, stare at my phone hoping someone will message first. They usually don’t. When I try to reach out, I second-guess every word and sometimes delete the message entirely.

I keep wondering if there’s something fundamentally off-putting about me, or if I just missed the window where people form real, lasting connections.
