I don’t really know where to put this, so I’m putting it here.

I’m not “alone” on paper. I have coworkers, a small family, people I can text. But it all feels like background noise. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else actually live while I just…exist.

The worst part is how repetitive it’s become. Wake up, work, come home, scroll, sleep. I might exchange a few messages with someone, maybe a meme or two, but it never goes any deeper. The surface-level stuff is exhausting, like I’m constantly performing “normal human” and nobody notices that I’m just improvising.

I’ve forgotten what it feels like to be genuinely excited to see someone. To know that if I disappeared for a week, someone would notice quickly and not just when I’ve failed to reply to something they needed from me.

I also feel like I’ve lost the ability to connect. When I try to open up, I hear myself talking and I sound weird, needy, or too intense. So I pull back, and then I get lonely again, and the cycle just keeps looping. It’s like I’m both starving for closeness and weirdly allergic to it now.

I’m not sure what I’m looking for by posting this. I just needed to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
