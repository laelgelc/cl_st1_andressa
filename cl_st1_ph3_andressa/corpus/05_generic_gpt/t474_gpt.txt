I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone a lot,” I’m actually lonely in a way that feels like it’s changing who I am.

I have people around me technically—coworkers, a couple of old friends I text once in a while, family chats—but I don’t feel known by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface. I can be in a room full of people and feel like I’m behind glass, watching myself pretend to be fine.

I miss the feeling of being someone’s first choice. Of having a person I can message without rehearsing the text ten times or worrying I’m being annoying. I overthink every interaction, then retreat, then feel even more disconnected.

What scares me is how used to it I’m becoming. Days pass where I don’t really talk to anyone, and it’s starting to feel normal, but not in a good way—more like I’m slowly fading out of my own life.
