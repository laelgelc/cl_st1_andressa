I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m fading out of my own life.

On paper, I’m “not alone.” I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up on holidays. But everything feels thin, like I’m behind glass watching other people actually live. I go through the motions: work, errands, streaming shows, scrolling social media. The days blur together so much that sometimes I have to check my phone to remember what day it is.

What really gets to me is how invisible I feel. People will talk to me, but rarely *with* me. I listen, nod, make jokes, but no one really asks follow-up questions. When I do share something real, the conversation just… slides off it. “Ah, that sucks anyway…” and we’re back to surface stuff. It makes me feel like my inner world is this locked room no one even realizes exists.

I’ve tried joining groups, going to events, talking more. I always end up on the edges, exhausted from trying to seem normal and interesting. Then I go home and replay every awkward moment, every time someone’s eyes drifted away while I was talking.

I’m starting to wonder if this is just how it’s going to be: surrounded by people, but somehow not really *with* anyone.
