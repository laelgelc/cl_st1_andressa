I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is just… background noise.

I’m not “alone” in the obvious sense. I have coworkers, a few old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up on holidays and birthdays. I go to work, say the right small talk things, send the right emojis, answer “how’s everything?” with “busy but good.” From the outside it probably looks fine. But I feel like a ghost in my own life.

The weird part is how invisible this feels to everyone else. I can sit in a room full of people and feel more alone than when I’m actually by myself. I watch them joke, talk about plans, share inside stories, and I’m there physically but not really part of it. I’m like the extra in a movie scene—present, but not important to the plot.

I’ve tried to “fix” it the way people suggest. I joined a hobby group, tried to be more active in group chats, forced myself to say yes to invitations instead of defaulting to no. But it all feels like I’m performing a version of myself that people might find acceptable, not actually being me. When I leave, I feel drained and somehow lonelier, because it reminds me that even when I try, I still don’t feel connected.

What really gets to me is the lack of a “person.” I don’t have that one friend I can text at 2 a.m. when my brain won’t shut up, or someone who’d notice if I went quiet for a while and actually check in. If I disappeared from social media tomorrow, I’m not convinced anyone would do more than maybe think, “Huh, haven’t seen them post in a bit,” and then move on.

I keep wondering if this is just adulthood now—everyone busy, everyone tired, everyone lonely but pretending they’re not. Or if it’s specifically me, something off-putting or forgettable that I can’t quite identify. I don’t know which answer feels worse.
