I don’t really know where to put this, so I’m putting it here.

Lately it feels like everyone else is living in a world I can only watch through glass. I go to work, I say the right small talk phrases, I smile when I’m supposed to, and then I come home to silence that feels heavier than it should. My phone lights up with group chats that I’m technically in, but somehow I’m always on the outside of the actual conversation.

I keep thinking about how most of my interactions are transactional: coworkers because of work, family because we’re related, old friends who occasionally like a post but never actually reach out. I don’t really have a person. No one who’d notice if I went quiet for a while, or who’d ask how I really am and actually want the honest answer.

What makes it worse is that I’m not completely isolated. I see people every day. I laugh, I contribute, I function. From the outside it probably looks fine. But the second I’m alone, it feels like the bottom drops out and I’m just… untethered. I don’t know how to move from “acquaintance” to “someone who matters” in anyone’s life.

I try to initiate plans sometimes, but I either get vague “we should hang out sometime” responses or last-minute cancellations. After a while it starts to feel personal, like there’s something fundamentally off about me that people can sense even if they can’t name it.

I don’t really have a point to this. I just needed to say it somewhere.
