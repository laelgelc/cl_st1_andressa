I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living in a parallel world to everyone around me.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I respond to messages, I show up to things when I’m invited. But it all feels like I’m acting. I can make small talk, I can crack jokes, but there’s this constant sense that I’m not really connected to anyone, like I’m just a background character in other people’s lives.

I’ll scroll through my contacts and realize there isn’t a single person I feel like I can text and say, “I’m not okay, can we talk?” without feeling like I’m burdening them or overstepping what the relationship actually is. I have “friends,” but they feel like acquaintances who share a group chat and the occasional meme. No one really knows what my days are like, what I’m actually thinking about, what I’m scared of.

The weird part is how invisible the loneliness is. I show up to work, I answer “good, you?” when people ask how I am, and then I go home and it’s just…silence. I eat alone, I watch something, I scroll until my eyes hurt, and then I go to bed feeling like I’m wasting my life but also too tired to change anything.

I keep wondering if the problem is me. Maybe I’m too guarded, or too boring, or too quiet, or too much. I replay conversations and analyze all the little awkward moments, like that’s proof I’m not meant to be close to anyone. I see people forming deep friendships, moving in together, getting engaged, and I can’t even imagine someone choosing me as their “person.”

There’s this heavy, dull ache that comes from realizing that if I disappeared from the internet and stopped showing up places, it would probably take a long time for anyone to really notice.
