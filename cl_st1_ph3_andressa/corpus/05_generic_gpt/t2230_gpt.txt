I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

On paper I’m “fine” – job, roof over my head, a couple of people I text sometimes. But I don’t feel actually connected to anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, shows, memes. The second I try to say something real, I feel like I’m making it awkward, like I’ve broken some unspoken rule that everything has to be light and easy.

I watch other people slide into friendships so effortlessly. Group photos, inside jokes, trips together. I’m always the extra, the “let me know if you’re free” person who never actually gets the invite. When I’m with people I feel invisible; when I’m alone I feel painfully aware that nobody’s thinking about me.

I’ve tried the usual advice – hobbies, meetups, messaging old friends – but it always fizzles out. People are friendly enough, but it never crosses that line into “you matter to me.” And I’m starting to wonder if there’s just something fundamentally off about me that other people pick up on.

What scares me most is how normal the loneliness is starting to feel. I come home, make dinner, scroll, maybe throw on a show, go to bed. Days blur together. I don’t know how to break out of this, or if I even remember what it feels like to be genuinely known by someone.
