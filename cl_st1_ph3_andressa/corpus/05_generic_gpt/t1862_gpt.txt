I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately I feel like a ghost in my own life.

On paper I’m “fine”: I have a job, a place to live, a few people I can text if I need something. But there’s this constant, low-level ache of not actually belonging anywhere. I can be in a room full of people and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else connect while I just mimic the right reactions.

I’ve noticed that most of my conversations are surface-level. Work updates, memes, “how was your weekend?” stuff. No one really knows what’s going on in my head, and I don’t know how to let them in without feeling like I’m burdening them or oversharing. I’ve tried to open up a couple of times and it either got brushed off or turned into a joke, so now I just shut down again.

The weird part is that I’m not completely isolated. I’ll get invited to things sometimes, but I feel like the “optional” friend — the one you invite if there’s extra space. I show up, smile, laugh at the right moments, and then go home and feel emptier than before. It’s like eating junk food when you’re starving: it fills the time but not the actual hunger.

I miss feeling genuinely seen. I miss having someone who checks in without me prompting it. I miss feeling like my presence makes a difference to someone’s day.

Most of the time I just keep it all to myself, because I don’t really know what anyone could do about it anyway.
