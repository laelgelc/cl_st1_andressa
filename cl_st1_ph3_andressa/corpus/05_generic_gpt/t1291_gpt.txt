I’m starting to realize I’m not just “alone,” I’m actually lonely, and it’s getting harder to pretend otherwise. I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I joke with, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m., no one who really knows what’s going on in my head. I keep my phone close, hoping for messages that rarely come. Weekends feel especially heavy—too much silence, too much time to think. I keep wondering if this is just how my life is going to be.
