I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my real life without sounding dramatic, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people, technically. I have coworkers I chat with, a few old friends I text sometimes, family group chats that light up on holidays. But it all feels like I’m playing a role in someone else’s life, not actually living my own with them. I’m “the reliable one,” “the listener,” “the one who’s always okay.” No one really looks closely enough to see that I’m not.

I can go days without anyone reaching out first. If I stop texting, things just…go quiet. Then I cave and send a meme or a “how are you?” and the conversation starts up again, but it feels like I’m resuscitating something that would’ve otherwise stayed dead. And it’s so hard not to interpret that silence as proof that I’m optional.

The weirdest part is that I’m not completely socially inept. I can be funny, I can hold a conversation, I know how to ask questions and be interested. But when I go home, it’s just this heavy emptiness. No one to debrief the day with, no one who knows my routines or my little quirks, no one who notices if I’m quieter than usual. I could disappear for a while and I’m honestly not sure who would actually notice in a concrete way, not just “oh, haven’t heard from you in a bit.”

I also feel like I missed some crucial “how to make and keep close friends as an adult” manual. People talk about their “group” or “best friend since forever,” and I just have this patchwork of acquaintances and old connections that never quite deepened. I don’t know how to ask for more closeness without feeling clingy or pathetic.

I keep myself busy so I don’t have to sit with it too much—scrolling, games, podcasts, anything. But there’s always that moment before bed where everything goes quiet and it hits me again how alone I actually feel, and I just lie there, staring at the ceiling, wondering if this is just how it’s going to be.
