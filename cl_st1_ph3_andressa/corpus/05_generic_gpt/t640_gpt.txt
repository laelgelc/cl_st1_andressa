I’m starting to realize that I’m not just “between friends” or “in a quiet phase” anymore. I’m actually lonely, in a way that feels like it’s becoming part of my identity.

My days are technically full: work, errands, scrolling, maybe a show before bed. But there’s no one threaded through any of it. No one I text random thoughts to, no one who’d notice if I disappeared from social media for a week. I’m surrounded by people and still feel like I’m watching life through glass.

When I do get invited somewhere, I feel like an extra, not part of the main cast. I laugh, I talk, but the connection never seems to leave the room with me. Everyone already has “their people,” and I’m just… a temporary guest.

I keep thinking it’s my fault: I’m too quiet, too awkward, too late to the party. I rehearse messages in my head and then don’t send them because I’m scared of being annoying. Then I sit there, phone in hand, pretending I don’t care, even though I really, really do.
