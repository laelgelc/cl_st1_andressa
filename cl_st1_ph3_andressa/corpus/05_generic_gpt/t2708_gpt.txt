I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s getting harder to pretend I’m okay with that.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I respond in group chats sometimes, I say “not much, you?” when people ask how I’m doing. But most days I feel like I’m watching life happen through glass. I’m there, but not really there.

I can be around people and still feel completely alone. I’ll sit in a room full of coworkers joking around and feel like I’m on mute, like my presence doesn’t register. When I do talk, it feels forced, like I’m playing a character. Then I go home and replay every tiny thing I said, convinced I sounded weird or annoying.

I’ve had friends in the past, but everything seems to fade. People move on, get busy, find partners, new friend groups. I’m happy for them in theory, but I’m always the one left watching their lives through social media, wondering why it never seems to be my turn to belong somewhere.

What scares me most is how used to this I’m becoming. I’m getting comfortable with silence, with weekends where no one texts first, with eating every meal alone. Part of me wants to reach out, make plans, try harder. Another part is tired of being the one who always initiates and then feeling like an afterthought anyway.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to put it somewhere that isn’t the inside of my head for once.
