I’ve been feeling this strange, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t always look like what people imagine. I have people around me, chats going on, group threads I’m part of. I answer messages, share memes, do all the usual “I’m still here” maintenance. But I keep realizing that nobody really knows what’s actually going on in my head.

When I try to open up, I feel like I’m talking too much, or being dramatic, or I’ll ruin the mood. So I cut it short, make a joke, change the subject. Then I end up feeling even more disconnected, because I had this tiny chance to be real and I backed away from it.

I miss feeling genuinely seen. Not just “how was your day,” but someone noticing when my answers sound off, or remembering small things I mentioned weeks ago. I feel replaceable a lot of the time, like if I disappeared from these chats or circles, people would notice for a bit and then just… adjust.

Most days I function fine. Work, chores, scrolling, sleep. But underneath, it’s like I’m living parallel to everyone else, not really with them. I don’t know how to bridge that gap without feeling needy or weird, so I just keep drifting.
