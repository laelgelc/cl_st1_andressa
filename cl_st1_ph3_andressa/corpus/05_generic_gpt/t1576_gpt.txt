I’ve been feeling like a ghost in my own life lately.

I technically “have people” around me—coworkers, a few old friends I text every now and then, family chats that light up my phone—but none of it feels like it actually reaches me. It’s like there’s this thick glass wall between me and everyone else. I can see them, I can hear them, but nothing really touches me and nothing I say seems to land.

The weird part is that from the outside I probably don’t look lonely. I go to work, I make small talk, I answer “I’m good, you?” automatically. I even laugh at the right times. But the second I’m alone again, it’s like all the noise drains away and I’m left with this heavy, hollow feeling that I don’t know what to do with.

I keep thinking about how long it’s been since I felt genuinely understood. Not just listened to, but understood. I don’t remember the last time I said something vulnerable and didn’t immediately regret it or minimize it afterward. Most of my “connections” feel like performances where I’m playing the role of a normal, functioning person.

I scroll through my contacts sometimes and realize there isn’t a single person I’d feel comfortable calling if I were really falling apart. Not because they’re bad people, but because I don’t feel like I exist in that way for anyone. I’m always the acquaintance, the coworker, the old classmate, the background character.

I’m starting to worry that I’ve forgotten how to actually let people in, or that I never really knew how in the first place. And the longer this goes on, the more I feel myself withdrawing, which just makes everything worse. It’s like I’m slowly erasing myself from other people’s lives while still desperately wanting to be seen.
