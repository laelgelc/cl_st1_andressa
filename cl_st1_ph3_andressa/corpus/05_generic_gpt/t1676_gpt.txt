I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match what my life looks like from the outside. I’m not completely isolated: I have coworkers I talk to, a few old friends I text sometimes, family chats that light up my phone. But I still feel like I’m standing behind glass watching everyone else actually live.

What bothers me most is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped reaching out, I’m not sure how long it would take before anyone seriously noticed. People would probably assume I’m “busy” and move on. There’s this constant sense that I’m an optional extra in other people’s lives, but for me they’re the main cast.

I miss feeling known. Not the small talk, not the “how’s work?” stuff, but the kind of connection where someone remembers the little things you said weeks ago, or notices when your tone is slightly off and asks what’s really going on. I don’t have that. I’m always the one adjusting, listening, making space. When the conversation ends, it’s like I disappear again.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like everyone says—meetups, online communities, being more social at work—but the connections stay shallow. I leave feeling more drained and somehow lonelier, like I’m failing at something that seems to come naturally to everyone else.

Some nights I just sit with my phone in my hand, wanting to message someone, and realizing I don’t actually know who I could send anything real to. So I end up saying nothing at all.
