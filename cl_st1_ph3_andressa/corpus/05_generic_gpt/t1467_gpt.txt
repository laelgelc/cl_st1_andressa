I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

I feel lonely in this weird, layered way. On paper I’m “not alone” — I have coworkers, a family that texts sometimes, a few online friends I joke with. But it all feels thin, like tracing paper versions of connections. I go through the motions: small talk at work, group chats, liking people’s posts. Then I come home, shut the door, and it’s like the silence suddenly gets really loud.

What messes with my head the most is that I’m not completely isolated, but I still feel like I’m standing behind glass. I can see everyone, they can see me, but there’s this invisible barrier. I keep thinking, “If I disappeared, who would actually notice in a way that mattered?” Not “oh that sucks,” but that deep, aching kind of missing someone. I don’t feel like anyone has that with me.

I’m also tired of being the one who reaches out first. If I stop messaging, most conversations just die. People say, “You should tell others how you feel,” but I don’t know how to say, “I feel like a background character in my own life and yours,” without sounding dramatic or making things awkward. So I keep it light, keep it surface-level, then resent how surface-level everything feels.

I see people who have “their person,” or even just a stable group that checks in, hangs out, remembers details. I want that so badly it almost feels embarrassing to admit. I’m not even sure what’s wrong with me that I can’t seem to build that kind of bond. I start wondering if I’m just fundamentally forgettable, like I pass through people’s lives without leaving much of an imprint.

Anyway, I don’t have a neat conclusion. I’m just really lonely, and it’s starting to feel less like a phase and more like a permanent state I’m supposed to get used to.
