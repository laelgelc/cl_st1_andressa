It’s weird how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re sealed off behind glass.

I have friends, technically. Group chats, occasional meetups, work colleagues. But everything feels…surface-level. Jokes, memes, small talk about shows and weekend plans. I participate, laugh in the right places, but it’s like I’m playing a character of myself. When I try to say something real, I either water it down or backspace it before hitting send.

I’m not completely isolated, but I feel profoundly unseen. No one really knows what my days look like when I get home. The silence in my apartment feels heavier lately. I scroll, I watch videos, I tell myself I’ll reach out to someone “later.” Then it’s 1 a.m. and I’ve spoken maybe 20 meaningful words all day.

Sometimes I wonder if I missed some developmental step where people learned how to form deep bonds. I can be there for others, listen to their problems, give advice. But when I think about who would notice if I just quietly withdrew, the list feels uncomfortably short.

It’s not a crisis, exactly. Just this constant low-level ache I don’t know what to do with.
