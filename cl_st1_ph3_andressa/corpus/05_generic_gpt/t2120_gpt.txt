I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I’ve been feeling like I’m slowly fading out of other people’s lives, and no one’s really noticing.

On paper I’m “fine.” I go to work, I respond when people message me, I make small talk. But it all feels like I’m playing a role that doesn’t belong to me. I’m always the extra person in a group, the one who gets invited as an afterthought, the one who can disappear from a chat for days and no one checks in.

I scroll through my phone sometimes just to see who I could text, and I realize how many conversations are just me asking questions to keep things going. If I stop, it just…stops. It makes me wonder if anyone actually wants me there, or if I’m just a convenient person to respond to when they’re bored.

What really gets to me is the silence. Not the literal silence, but that feeling when you have something you want to share—some small victory, a random thought, a stupid meme—and you realize there’s no one you feel close enough to send it to without overthinking it. Either I worry I’m bothering them, or I know they’ll just leave me on read.

I’m not completely isolated. I see people, I talk to them. But the connections feel shallow, like we’re all standing behind glass. I can see everyone’s lives moving forward—relationships, group trips, inside jokes—while I’m stuck watching from outside, trying not to press my face too close to the window.

I keep wondering if this is just adulthood, or if there’s something specifically wrong with me that makes me forgettable. I don’t know how to fix it. I’m tired of feeling like a placeholder in my own life, like if I vanished tomorrow, the only real difference would be one less name in a group chat that no one scrolls up far enough to notice anyway.
