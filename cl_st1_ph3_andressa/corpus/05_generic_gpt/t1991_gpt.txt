I don’t really know where to put this, but I’m tired of hearing my own thoughts echo around in my head, so here I am.

I’m at this weird point where my life looks “fine” from the outside. I have a job, I pay my bills, I make small talk with coworkers, I answer “good, you?” when people ask how I’m doing. But underneath that, it feels like there’s this constant, low-level ache of loneliness that never really shuts off. It’s not dramatic, just… always there.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers, acquaintances, the occasional group thing I get invited to. But I don’t feel close to anyone. I can be sitting in a room full of people and still feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else connect while I’m just sort of… present, but not part of it.

I miss having someone who actually knows me. Someone I don’t have to “perform” around. I censor myself so much—what I say, how I react, what I show—because I’m scared of being too much, or not enough, or just plain boring. So I stay on the surface, and then I complain to myself that all my relationships feel shallow. It’s like I’m the one locking the door and then wondering why nobody comes in.

I’ve also hit that age where everyone seems to be pairing off, getting engaged, having kids, moving in with partners. My evenings are just me, my phone, and this heavy awareness of how quiet my place is. I scroll through social media and feel like I’m watching a world I’m not really part of, like I missed some crucial step everyone else understood.

I don’t know how to fix this. I’m not even sure what I’m asking for. I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
