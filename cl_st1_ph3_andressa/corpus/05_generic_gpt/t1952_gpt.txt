I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I say “hey” to coworkers, I send the occasional meme in the group chat. But it all feels like I’m performing a role I never auditioned for. I can talk about work, weather, TV shows, but the second the conversation goes deeper, I freeze or deflect. It’s like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else, and I can see them, hear them, but I can’t step through.

What bothers me most is how invisible I feel. If I stopped initiating contact, I’m pretty sure most people in my life wouldn’t notice for a long time. Sometimes I test it—wait to see who texts first, who asks how I’m doing without needing something. The silence stretches on and on, and I end up caving just to prove to myself that I still exist to someone.

I also feel this weird shame about being lonely. I’m an adult; I’m “supposed” to have figured this out by now. When people talk about their close friends or partners, I nod and smile, and inside I’m thinking, “What’s wrong with me that nobody chooses me like that?” I don’t think I’m awful to be around, but I’m clearly not someone people prioritize.

Most nights I just scroll on my phone until my eyes hurt, then lie in bed replaying conversations I didn’t have, imagining connections that don’t exist. Then I get up the next day and do it all again, like I’m stuck in a loop that I can’t quite justify and can’t quite escape.
