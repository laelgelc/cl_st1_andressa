I’ve been feeling like a background character in my own life lately. I go through the motions—work, groceries, scrolling on my phone—without really existing in anyone’s mind once I’m out of sight. People are “friendly” with me, but no one actually knows me. I don’t even know how to let them.

The weirdest part is how busy everything looks from the outside. Group chats keep pinging, people post photos with friends, coworkers talk about their weekends. I stand there, laughing at the right times, and it feels like I’m pressing my face against a window.

I’ve tried joining things—clubs, online servers, whatever—but I always end up hovering at the edge, not quite in, not quite out. I overthink every message, every silence. Eventually I just disappear and no one notices.

It’s not dramatic loneliness, just this constant low-level ache. Like there’s a glass wall between me and everyone else, and I can’t figure out if it’s them keeping me out or me keeping myself in.
