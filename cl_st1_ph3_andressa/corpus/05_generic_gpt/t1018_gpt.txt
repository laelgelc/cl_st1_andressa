I don’t even know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m just… drifting through life with no real point of contact.

I have people around me, technically. Coworkers I chat with, family I text sometimes, a couple of old friends I send memes to. But none of it feels like it actually reaches me. It’s like there’s this invisible glass wall between me and everyone else. I can see them, hear them, even laugh with them, but I don’t feel connected. I walk away from conversations feeling emptier than before.

What really gets to me is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped showing up, I don’t think anyone’s life would really be different. Maybe I’d get a “hey, long time no see” text after a few weeks, but that’s about it. I don’t feel like I matter in the day-to-day of anyone’s life. No one really checks in just to ask how I’m actually doing. If I don’t initiate, things just go quiet.

I’ve tried to “fix” it the usual ways. Joined some group activities, forced myself to be more social, tried to open up a bit more. But it always stays on that surface level: small talk, shared jokes, nothing deeper. When I do try to be honest about how lonely I feel, people either change the subject, offer quick advice, or give that awkward silence that makes me regret saying anything.

The worst part is how this is starting to change how I see myself. I’m more hesitant to reach out because I assume I’m bothering people. I overthink every message I send. I replay conversations in my head and pick apart what I said, wondering if that’s why people keep their distance. 

I don’t even know what I’m looking for writing this. I guess I just needed to put it somewhere, to admit that I feel incredibly alone even when I’m technically “not alone” at all.
