I don’t really know how to say this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m fading out of my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, people I can technically contact if I need something. But most days it feels like I’m just floating around other people’s lives, not actually in them. I’m the coworker you can chat with, the acquaintance you invite if you’re trying to round out a group, the person in the group chat who reacts with an emoji but rarely gets a direct message.

The weird thing is I’m not completely isolated. I talk to people every day. I make small jokes, I smile, I ask how they’re doing. But it’s like I’m performing a version of myself that people find acceptable, and the real me is sitting behind glass, watching. Whenever a conversation gets close to anything real, I panic internally and steer it back to something light. Then I go home and wonder why no one really knows me.

Nights are the worst. I finish whatever I’m supposed to do, then just sort of…exist. I scroll, I watch random videos, I stare at the ceiling. The silence feels heavy, but I don’t even know who I’d call. I have contacts, but not people I feel like I can be messy around. I’m always worried I’ll be “too much” if I open up.

What messes with my head is that other people seem to move through social stuff like it’s breathing. They make plans, they keep in touch, they share things about their day without overthinking every sentence. For me, every interaction feels like a test I’m quietly failing. I replay conversations hours later, picking apart every awkward pause.

I don’t think I want a huge friend group or constant social activity. I just want to feel like there’s at least one person who would notice if I went quiet for a few days and actually care enough to check in. Right now it feels like if I disappeared, people would just assume I got busy and move on.
