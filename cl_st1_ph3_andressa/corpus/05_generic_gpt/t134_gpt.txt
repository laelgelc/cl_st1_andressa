I’ve been feeling this weird combination of invisible and exposed lately.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I respond to messages, I show up when I’m supposed to. But it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it. I go through the motions, say the right things, but there’s this constant sense that no one really knows me, and I’m not even sure I know how to let them.

I have acquaintances, group chats, people I can send memes to. But I don’t have that person I can text, “Hey, my brain is not okay today,” without feeling like I’m being dramatic or burdensome. I end up editing myself down to something more palatable: the “I’m good, just tired” version of me that everyone seems comfortable with.

Part of it is on me. I’ve pulled back so many times that now it feels impossible to push forward. I overthink every interaction: “Did I talk too much? Not enough? Was that weird?” After a while, it’s easier to just not try. But then the silence gets loud, and I start scrolling through old conversations, watching them fade from daily to weekly to nothing.

What really gets to me is how replaceable I feel. Like if I disappeared from the group chat or stopped showing up, people would notice for a moment, then just…adjust. I know that’s how life works, but there’s something painful about realizing how easy it is to slip out of the frame.
