I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers I talk to, a couple of old friends I text sometimes, family I see on holidays. But all of that feels like background noise. I go to work, say the right polite things, laugh in the right places, then come home and it’s like I vanish. No one really notices if I go silent for days. If I don’t start the conversation, it just doesn’t happen.

What bothers me most is the sense that I’m permanently on the outside of everyone else’s “real life.” People talk about their group chats blowing up, spontaneous plans, someone checking in when they’re having a rough day. I can’t remember the last time someone reached out to me first just to see how I was doing, without needing something.

I’ve also reached this weird point where I don’t even know how to be close to people anymore. When someone actually shows interest or tries to get to know me, I feel awkward and exposed, like they’re going to realize I’m boring or too much or not enough, and then I pull back. Then I’m alone again and angry at myself for doing exactly what I was afraid would happen.

There’s this constant ache of wanting to be known by someone and simultaneously not knowing how to let anyone close enough. I scroll through social media and see people posting pictures with their friends, partners, families, and it just emphasizes how replaceable and forgettable I feel.

Most days I just go through the motions and try not to think about it too much. But then it hits me randomly—on the bus, in the grocery store, lying in bed at night—that no one really knows me, and I’m not sure anyone ever will.
