I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living in a parallel world to everyone around me.

On paper, my life looks “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, coworkers I can make small talk with. I can go a whole week and no one would guess anything is wrong. But underneath that, there’s this constant, heavy feeling that I’m just… not actually connected to anyone.

I have people I can message, technically. A few old friends in group chats, some acquaintances I see every now and then. But it’s all surface-level. Memes, “how’s work?”, “we should hang out sometime” that never gets scheduled. I can’t remember the last time someone checked in on me just to see how I really am, without me prompting it or turning the conversation toward them first.

The worst part is how invisible it all is. If I say “I’m lonely,” people respond with “but you’re not alone, you have us!” and I don’t know how to explain that being physically surrounded or virtually connected doesn’t fix the feeling of being emotionally on an island. I can be in a room full of people and feel like I’m behind glass, watching everyone else actually experience life.

I’ve also started noticing how much I censor myself. I don’t say what I really think or feel because I don’t want to be “too much” or “too depressing.” So I keep it light, and then feel resentful that no one knows me deeply, even though I’m the one hiding. It’s a stupid loop: I’m lonely, I withdraw, then I’m lonelier.

Nights are the hardest. When the distractions are gone and it’s just me and the silence, it hits how replaceable I feel. Like if I disappeared from everyone’s life, they’d be sad for a bit, then adjust without much trouble. And I don’t know what to do with that thought.
