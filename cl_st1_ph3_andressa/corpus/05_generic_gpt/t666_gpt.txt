I’ve been feeling this weird combination of invisible and exposed lately.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, a place to live, some acquaintances I can message if I really need something. But most days it feels like I’m just orbiting around other people’s lives without ever actually being part of anything.

I’ll go through an entire day without anyone saying my name out loud. People talk to me, but it’s surface-level stuff: “How’s it going?” “Good, you?” “Good.” Then nothing. I walk away and it’s like I immediately stop existing for them. When I get home, I sit in silence and realize no one is expecting me, no one is wondering how my day went, and no one would really notice if I just turned my phone off for a week.

I scroll through chat apps and see old conversations that just… stopped. No fight, no falling out, just this slow fading where I replied a little slower, they replied a little shorter, until there was nothing left to say. I don’t even know if I pushed people away or if they were never really there in the first place.

What makes it worse is that I’m starting to feel awkward in situations that used to feel normal. I overthink every message I send, every joke I make, every pause in conversation. I feel like I’m performing “being a person” instead of actually being one. And afterwards, I replay everything in my head and cringe.

It’s like I’m stuck between wanting someone to really see me and being terrified that if they did, they’d decide I’m not worth the effort. So I end up here instead, typing into a box.
