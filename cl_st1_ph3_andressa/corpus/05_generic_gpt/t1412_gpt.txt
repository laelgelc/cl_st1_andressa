I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my life on paper.

I have people around me. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I text with sometimes, family chats that light up my phone. I go to work, I answer “How are you?” with “Good, you?” and it all looks normal. But I can go whole days without feeling like I’ve actually connected with another human being.

What gets me is the sense that no one really knows me, and I’m not even sure I remember how to let them. I notice I edit myself constantly—keep things light, crack a joke, change the subject if it gets too personal. Then I go home and feel resentful that no one sees past the surface, even though I’m the one building the walls.

Nights are the worst. That moment when you put your phone down and realize there’s no one you can message without feeling like you’re bothering them, or like you’re “too much.” I scroll through chats with people I used to be close to and stop myself from saying anything because I don’t know how to bridge the gap anymore.

I miss having “my person.” Not necessarily a romantic partner, just someone who wants to hear the boring details of my day, who I don’t feel like I have to perform for. I don’t even know where to start to find that again as an adult.

Mostly I’m just tired of feeling like I’m always half-present in my own life, surrounded by people but still somehow on the outside looking in.
