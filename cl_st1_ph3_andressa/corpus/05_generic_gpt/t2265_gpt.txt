I don’t really know where I fit anymore.

I’m not completely isolated – I have coworkers I make small talk with, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family group chats that light up on holidays. But it all feels like I’m watching my own life from behind glass. I’m “there,” but I’m not *in* it.

The part that messes with me is how invisible it feels. I can go days without anyone messaging me first. If I stop initiating, things just go quiet. People say “you should reach out more” but it’s exhausting to always be the one knocking on the door, never really sure if you’re wanted or just tolerated.

I’ve tried hobbies, online communities, going to events alone. I can have surface-level conversations, even seem social, but there’s this wall when it comes to actually connecting. I don’t know if it’s me being guarded, or just that no one is really interested in knowing me beyond the polite version.

Nights are the worst. That heavy feeling of “no one is thinking about me right now.” Not in a dramatic way, just in a factual, empty way. I scroll, I watch stuff, I lie there and replay awkward moments from years ago like they just happened.

I keep wondering if this is just how adulthood is for some people: surrounded by others, but somehow completely separate from them.
