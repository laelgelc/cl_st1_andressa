I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually know how to connect with people, and it’s getting harder to pretend that I do.

On paper, my life looks fine: job, small apartment, a couple of hobbies I can list if someone asks. But most days feel like I’m watching my own life through a window. I go to work, talk about projects and deadlines, make small jokes, seem “normal,” and then come home to a silence that feels heavier every year.

What gets to me is how invisible this kind of loneliness is. People see me laughing at work or making conversation and assume I’m okay. But nobody sees the way I drag out errands just so I’m not back in my empty place too soon. Or how I open social media, scroll through other people’s group photos and couple selfies, and then close it because it just makes the quiet feel louder.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” like everyone says. Joined a club, went to a few meetups. I can hold a conversation, but it never seems to move past surface level. It’s like there’s a glass wall: I can see everyone, they can see me, but nothing really passes through. I leave those events feeling more alone than when I arrived, because it reminds me that I’m clearly missing something other people seem to have figured out.

What scares me is how used to it I’m becoming. I’ve built routines around being alone—eating dinner while watching something, talking out loud to myself just to hear a voice, going days without a real, honest conversation. When someone does show a bit of interest, I either cling too hard in my head or shut down because I’m convinced they’ll eventually disappear.

I don’t even know exactly what I’m looking for anymore. Just… some proof that it’s possible to feel like you actually belong somewhere, with someone, and not just be an extra in everyone else’s story.
