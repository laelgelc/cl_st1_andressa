I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind a glass wall.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I talk to coworkers, I answer messages in group chats, my family checks in sometimes. But none of it feels like it actually touches me. Conversations are small talk about weekends, memes, work tasks. I laugh, I respond, I seem normal. Then I go home and it’s just silence and this heavy feeling in my chest that I can’t put down.

What gets to me is how replaceable I feel. If I disappeared from every chat and stopped showing up, I’m not sure anyone’s routine would really change. Maybe a “hey, you okay?” once or twice, then life would move on. I don’t feel like I’m anyone’s first choice. I’m always the “if no one else is free” option, the extra chair at the table, never the reason the gathering is happening.

I also catch myself editing everything I say because I’m scared of being “too much” or “too needy.” So I stay on the surface, then resent that no one knows me deeply. But when I try to open up even a bit, I feel exposed and stupid, like I’ve broken some unspoken rule.

It’s a strange mix of wanting to be seen and wanting to disappear. I keep thinking there must be some point where it stops feeling this empty, but right now I just move through the days, do what’s expected, and go back to my room, where it’s just me and the noise in my head.
