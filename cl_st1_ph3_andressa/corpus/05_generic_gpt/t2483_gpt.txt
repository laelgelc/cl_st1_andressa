I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into the usual “I have no friends” box. I technically have people around me—coworkers, some old friends I text every now and then, family chats—but I still feel like I’m walking around inside a glass container. Everyone can see me, I can see them, but there’s this silent barrier where nothing actually reaches me.

I’m tired of being “fine.” I’ve become so good at small talk that it almost feels like a costume I put on. I go through the motions, laugh at the right moments, ask people about their weekends, but I can’t remember the last time I said something real and didn’t immediately regret it or feel stupid afterward.

What scares me most is how used to it I’m getting. The empty evenings don’t feel dramatic anymore; they’re just routine. I scroll, I watch something, I eat, I sleep. I keep thinking, “I’m too old to be this disconnected,” but I don’t actually know how to change it without feeling like I’m burdening people or coming off as desperate.

Mostly, I just feel like I’m fading into the background of my own life.
