I’ve been feeling like a background character in my own life lately.

I go through the motions—work, errands, scrolling on my phone—but most days I don’t really talk to anyone beyond surface-level stuff. Coworkers know “work me,” my family knows the version of me that shows up for holidays, but it feels like nobody actually knows what’s going on in my head. I’m not even sure I do.

The weird part is I’m not completely isolated. I have people I could text, group chats I’m in, social media accounts with “friends.” But when I think about reaching out, I freeze. I either convince myself I’m bothering them, or I stare at the message box until it feels too awkward to send anything. Then I end up watching their stories instead of having an actual conversation.

Nights are the worst. That’s when the silence feels loud. I replay old conversations, old friendships I let fade, and I wonder if this is just what adulthood is—everyone drifting into their own little islands, busy and tired and quietly lonely.

I don’t know what I’m expecting posting this. I guess I just wanted to put it into words somewhere. It’s strange how you can be surrounded by people, physically or online, and still feel like you’re sitting alone in an empty room.
