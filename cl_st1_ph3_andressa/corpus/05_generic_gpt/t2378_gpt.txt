I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m watching my own life from behind glass.

On paper I’m “fine” – job, roof over my head, a couple of people I can text if I need something. But there’s no one who actually knows me. Conversations feel like small talk stacked on top of more small talk. I show up, I smile, I say the right things, and then I go home and it’s just…silence.

What’s messing with me is that I can be surrounded by people and still feel completely isolated. Group chats buzzing, coworkers chatting about their weekends, family asking if I’m eating well. None of it feels like connection. It’s like I’m playing a role I never auditioned for.

I’ve tried joining clubs, going to events, being “more social.” I can manage a decent first impression, but it never seems to go deeper. People already have their circles, their shared histories, their inside jokes. I end up on the outer ring, smiling and nodding.

I’m starting to wonder if there’s something about me that people just…don’t latch onto. Like I’m forgettable in some fundamental way. I go days without a message that isn’t about logistics. It makes me question whether anyone would actually notice if I just quietly stopped trying.
