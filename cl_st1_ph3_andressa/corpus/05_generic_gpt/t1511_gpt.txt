I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly fading out of my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay my bills, I answer emails, I say “not much, you?” when people ask what’s new. I even laugh in the right places during small talk. But under all of that, there’s this constant, heavy awareness that no one actually knows me and I don’t really know how to let anyone in anymore.

I used to have friends. Not a huge group, but a few people I could text randomly, send memes to, call when something good or bad happened. Over time it just… thinned out. People moved away, got partners, had kids, got busier. I get it; that’s life. But it feels like I somehow missed the off-ramp where everyone else merged into “real adulthood” with relationships and support systems, and I just kept driving alone.

The worst part is the silence after I turn off my phone at night. There’s no one who would notice if I disappeared from social media for weeks. No one checks in unprompted. If I don’t start the conversation, there is no conversation. I find myself dragging out meaningless chats just because I’m so desperate to feel like I exist to someone.

I’ve tried joining things—Discord servers, hobby groups, even going to a couple of meetups—but I always end up on the edges, watching other people slip into easy banter and inside jokes. I feel like a guest star in everyone else’s show, never part of the main cast.

What scares me is how normalized this has become. I wake up, go through the motions, and the loneliness is just there, like background noise. I can’t tell if I’m getting used to it or just shutting down more. I don’t know which is worse.
