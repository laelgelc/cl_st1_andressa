I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I reply to emails, I show up to meetings, I make small talk about weekends and weather. People at work probably think I’m quiet but normal. The weird thing is, I can be surrounded by people all day and still feel like I’m sealed behind glass. I hear myself laugh at jokes, but it feels like I’m watching someone else do it.

My phone is full of old group chats that have gone silent except for the occasional meme or birthday message. I scroll through them like I’m walking through a museum of friendships that used to feel real. I don’t know when exactly the shift happened—from “we should hang out sometime” actually meaning something to it just being polite noise.

I’ve tried reaching out a few times. I’ll send a “hey, how’ve you been?” and they’ll answer, and it’s pleasant, and then it just…dies. I can’t tell if I’m boring, or if everyone is just tired, or if this is how adulthood is for most people and no one admits it.

The worst part is evenings. That moment when you close the door behind you and realize no one is expecting you, no one is waiting to hear how your day went. I end up putting on a show I’ve already seen just to have familiar voices in the background. Sometimes I catch myself talking back to the TV under my breath, just to break the silence.

I keep thinking I should join something—clubs, classes, whatever—but the idea of walking into a room full of strangers who already know each other feels exhausting. And then another week goes by, and nothing changes.
