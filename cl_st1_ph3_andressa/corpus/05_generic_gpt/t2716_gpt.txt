I’ve started to realize I’m lonelier around people than when I’m actually alone. I can be in a room full of coworkers, chatting about nothing, and still feel like I’m behind glass. Everyone seems to have these invisible threads tying them to others—inside jokes, shared histories, group chats—and I’m just… extra. 

I try to initiate conversations, but they always feel surface-level, like we’re both waiting for a polite exit. I go home, scroll through my phone, and there’s no one I can message without overthinking it. It’s strange how heavy silence can feel when there’s no one expecting you anywhere.
