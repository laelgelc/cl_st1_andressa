I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too exposed at the same time.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, coworkers I can chat with, a couple of group chats that light up sometimes. I can go whole days exchanging messages and still feel like no one actually sees me. It’s like I’m playing the role of “functional person” and everyone buys it, so no one looks closer.

What really gets to me is how small my life feels when I’m not around other people. I come home, sit on my bed, scroll through my phone, maybe watch something, and the silence just kind of presses in. There’s no one who expects me to show up. If I disappeared from social media, I’m honestly not sure who would notice quickly.

I find myself holding back when I talk to people, because I’m scared that if I show the “too much” parts—neediness, anxiety, the constant feeling of being on the outside—they’ll pull away. But then I end up feeling even more alone because no one actually knows me.

I don’t even know what I’m asking for. Just needed to put this somewhere that doesn’t feel like I’m burdening anyone in my real life.
