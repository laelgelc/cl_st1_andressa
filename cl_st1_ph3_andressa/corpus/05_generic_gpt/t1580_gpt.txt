Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m watching my own life from the outside, like I’m a side character in everyone else’s story.

I technically “have people” around me — coworkers, acquaintances, family I text occasionally — but I don’t feel known by any of them. Conversations stay on the surface: work, weather, TV shows. I can’t remember the last time someone asked how I really was and actually wanted the full answer instead of the short, socially acceptable version.

I’ve tried to change things. I’ve joined group chats, gone to meetups, forced myself to be more “social.” I can talk, I can joke, I can play the part. But when I go home, it all feels fake, like I just performed a version of myself that people might tolerate more than the real one.

What hurts most is that people seem to already have their circles. They have their “best friend since childhood,” their partner, their group chat that’s constantly buzzing. I’m always the extra person, the one you invite if there’s room, not the one anyone builds plans around.

It’s starting to affect how I see myself. I keep wondering what’s wrong with me that I’m always the one left out, the one people forget to update, the one who finds out about events only after seeing photos online.

I’m tired of pretending it doesn’t bother me. It does. It makes everything feel a bit pointless when there’s no one who would really notice if I just quietly disappeared from their life.
