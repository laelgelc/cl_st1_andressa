I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to be close to anyone.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family I text sometimes—but everything feels like I’m watching my own life from a few steps back. I can make small talk, crack a joke, pretend I’m fine. Then I go home and it’s just this heavy silence that feels louder than anything.

I scroll through chats I never start because I don’t know what I’m “allowed” to say without being weird or needy. I rehearse messages in my head until the moment passes and it’s too late. I tell myself I don’t need anyone, but the truth is I’m constantly imagining what it would be like to have that one person who actually wants to know how my day really was.

It’s like everyone else got some manual on how to form real connections and I missed that class. I’m tired of feeling like an extra in other people’s stories, but I genuinely don’t know what to do differently.
