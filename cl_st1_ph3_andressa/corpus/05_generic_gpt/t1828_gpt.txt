I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people most days – coworkers, acquaintances, family chats, group messages – but I feel like a ghost walking through someone else’s life. Conversations stay on the surface: work, memes, small complaints. Nobody really knows what’s going on in my head, and I don’t know how to let them in without feeling like I’m oversharing or making things weird.

I keep trying to “fix” it with the usual advice: join clubs, message people first, say yes to plans. I do some of that, and the result is still the same: I go home afterward and feel even more alone, like I’ve just acted out the role of a normal person for a few hours. It’s exhausting.

What scares me most is how used to it I’m becoming. I can feel myself withdrawing, answering slower, cancelling more often, telling myself it doesn’t matter. But it does. I miss feeling genuinely connected to someone, not just being the extra person in the group photo who could disappear and nothing would change.
