I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m just drifting through my own life, watching it from the outside.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, even a few “friends”—but it all feels thin, like cardboard cutouts. We talk about work, shows, random stuff, but there’s this invisible wall that never comes down. I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely seen by someone, or like my presence actually mattered to them.

Nights are the worst. I finish whatever I’m supposed to do, sit down, and then there’s…nothing. No one to message, no one who’d notice if I went offline for days. I scroll, I lurk, I overthink. I replay old conversations and wonder if I somehow pushed people away without realizing it.

Part of me wants to reach out more, but another part is just tired of feeling like I’m forcing my way into other people’s already-full lives. So I stay quiet, and the silence just gets heavier.
