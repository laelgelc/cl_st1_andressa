I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my life on paper. I have coworkers I get along with, a couple of old friends I text with now and then, family group chats that light up sometimes. But it all feels like background noise instead of connection.

Most days I go to work, talk about tasks and deadlines, smile in the right places, then come home and just… disappear into my room. I scroll, watch random videos, lurk here, and then suddenly it’s midnight and I realize I haven’t had a real conversation all day. I mean the kind where you can say, “I’m not okay,” without feeling like you’re ruining the vibe.

I keep wondering if I somehow missed the point where everyone else learned how to form close bonds. People talk about “their people,” group trips, weekly hangouts, inside jokes. I’m always the peripheral person: invited sometimes, forgotten other times, rarely the first one anyone thinks of. When I’m actually with others, I feel like I’m acting out a version of myself that’s more acceptable, less… needy, I guess.

What scares me is how normal this has started to feel. The silence after I close the door behind me. Eating alone, again. Telling myself I prefer it that way, even though my chest hurts when I see other people laughing together. I keep thinking I should “put myself out there,” but I don’t even know where “there” is anymore, or what version of me I’m supposed to bring.
