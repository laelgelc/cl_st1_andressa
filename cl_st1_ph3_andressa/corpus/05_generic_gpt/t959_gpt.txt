I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m watching my own life from the outside, like I’m some side character in everyone else’s story.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I say hi to coworkers, I answer emails, I nod in meetings. I even crack jokes sometimes and people laugh. But the second I leave, it’s like I disappear. No one texts. No one asks what I’m doing this weekend. My phone is mostly notifications from apps and random spam, and I catch myself checking it over and over like maybe this time there’ll be something there.

I’ve had friends before, but everything seems to fade. People move, get partners, get busy, and I don’t know how to keep myself relevant in their lives. I feel like I’m always the one reaching out, and eventually I get tired of being the person who keeps conversations on life support. Then the silence just becomes permanent.

What really messes with me is how invisible this feels. I can sit in a room full of people and still feel like I’m behind glass. I listen to others talk about group trips, family dinners, partners who “won’t stop texting,” and I just nod along. I don’t know how to say, “I go home to nothing and no one,” without killing the vibe or sounding needy.

I’ve tried joining clubs, going to events, being “more social,” but it always feels like I’m orbiting other people’s already-formed circles. They’re polite, but I’m not really *in* it. I leave, and nobody notices I’m gone.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for writing this. I guess I just wanted to say it somewhere, to admit that I’m lonely in a way that feels less like a phase and more like a personality trait at this point.
