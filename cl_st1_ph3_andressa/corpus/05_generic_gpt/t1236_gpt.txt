I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, an apartment, a few people I can text if I need something practical. But I don’t feel known by anyone. I go entire days without using my real voice for more than “thank you” at a checkout or “have a good one” on a work call.

I scroll through chats with people I used to be close to and it’s just this graveyard of half-finished conversations. We all got busy, or tired, or awkward, and I never figured out how to say, “Hey, I miss you, and I don’t know how to fix this.” So instead I send a meme every few weeks and pretend that counts as staying in touch.

The worst part is how much of this feels like my fault and also completely out of my control. I overthink every message, every invite. I talk myself out of reaching out because I don’t want to be “too much” or “needy,” and then I sit there feeling hurt that no one reaches out to me. It’s like I’ve built my own cage and then convinced myself I’m locked in.

Nights are the hardest. There’s this heavy silence when I turn off my phone and realize no one is expecting to hear from me, no one is wondering how my day went. I keep imagining what it would be like to be someone people naturally think of, instead of someone they remember only when they’re bored or need something.

I’m not even sure what I’m hoping for posting this. Maybe just proof that I exist somewhere outside my own head.
