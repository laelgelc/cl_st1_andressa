It’s weird how you can be surrounded by people and still feel like you’re on mute.

On paper, I’m not “alone.” I have coworkers, some acquaintances, a family that texts in a group chat, people who know my name. But it all feels like I’m just passing through scenes in other people’s lives, like an extra who never gets any lines.

Most of my day is just…quiet. I wake up, scroll, go to work, make small talk about nothing, come home, and then it’s me and the silence. I keep trying to “put myself out there” like everyone says: join a hobby group, message people, say yes to invitations. But even when I’m physically there, I feel disconnected, like I’m watching myself from the outside trying to act normal.

What gets to me is how one-sided everything feels. I’m always the one reaching out, starting conversations, checking in. If I stop, everything just stops. No one seems to notice unless I make the first move. It makes me wonder if people actually like me or if I’m just…convenient when I appear.

There’s also this constant fear of being “too much” or “too needy,” so I hold back a lot. I edit my messages, delete them, rewrite them, then sometimes never send them at all. I want to say, “I’m lonely, I need someone,” but I don’t know who I’m allowed to say that to without scaring them off.

Nights are the worst. That’s when the thoughts really pile up: that maybe this is just how my life is going to be, that I somehow missed the point where everyone else learned how to form real, mutual connections. I’m tired of pretending it doesn’t bother me, but I also don’t know what to do differently anymore.
