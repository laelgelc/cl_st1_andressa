I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into any simple box. I’m not completely isolated. I have coworkers I talk to, a few old friends I message once in a while, family chats that light up my phone. But it all feels like I’m watching my own life through a window instead of being in it.

I go through the motions: work, errands, scrolling, sleep. I make small talk about shows, weather, deadlines. But the second I try to say anything that’s actually real—like “hey, I feel disconnected and tired of pretending I’m fine”—it’s like the conversation just can’t hold that weight. People either joke it away, change the subject, or give a quick “yeah, same” and move on.

What’s messing with me is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped texting first, most conversations would just die. If I disappeared from social media, I’m not sure who would actually notice beyond a passing “oh, weird” thought. It’s like I exist as an optional extra in everyone’s life, not a person they’d naturally think to reach out to.

I’ve tried joining things—online communities, group chats, even local meetups—but I always end up on the edges, the person people are “nice” to but don’t really see. I overthink every message I send, worry I’m being annoying, then go silent and feel worse.

The strangest part is how normalized it all feels now. I wake up, feel that hollow space inside, and just carry it around with me. I keep hoping something will shift, but most days it just feels like more of the same.
