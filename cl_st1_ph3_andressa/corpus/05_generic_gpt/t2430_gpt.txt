I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I go to work, I respond when people talk to me, I make small jokes, I’m “easy to get along with.” But it’s like I’m just a placeholder person. No one really knows me, and honestly I don’t think anyone is particularly curious. I’m the quiet one, the reliable one, the one who listens. Then I go home and there’s just this heavy silence that feels louder than any noise.

I’ve tried to reach out more: joining group chats, going to events, making small talk. The conversations always stay surface-level. I keep hoping one of them will naturally deepen, but they never do. People already have their circles, their “best friends,” their group photos. I’m the person who takes the picture, not the one in it.

What scares me most is how normal this has become. I can go days without a meaningful conversation and barely react. It’s like a slow erosion of something I can’t name. I’m not sure if I’ve given up or if I’m just tired.
