I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really match my life on paper. I have coworkers, a couple of friends I see occasionally, family chats that ping all day. But I still go to bed most nights with this feeling that I’m not actually connected to anyone.

I can talk to people, joke around, even be the “easygoing” one. But it feels like I’m performing a version of myself that’s easier for everyone to handle. When conversations start getting close to anything real, I either shut down or steer it back to something light. Then I walk away thinking, “No one knows me,” but I’m also the one not letting them.

What messes with my head is how replaceable I feel. If I stopped showing up, I think people would notice for a bit, then just… adjust. There’s no person I could call at 2 a.m. if I really needed to talk. I’m not that person for anyone else either. It feels like I’m orbiting other people’s lives instead of being part of something.

I keep wondering if this is just adulthood, or if I missed some critical step where everyone else learned how to form real, lasting bonds. I want to be seen and understood, but when I get the chance, I freeze. Then I go home, overthink every interaction, and end up feeling even more alone than before.
