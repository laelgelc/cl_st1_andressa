I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to be close to people.

On paper, my life looks “fine.” I have coworkers I chat with, a family group chat that sends memes, a couple of old friends I text every few weeks. But none of it feels like it touches whatever empty place is in the middle of my chest.

I scroll through social media and see people posting pictures with “their people,” inside jokes, casual hangouts, trips. I can’t even picture who I’d tag along with. If I disappeared, I think people would notice in a “huh, haven’t seen them in a while” way, not a “where are they, I’m worried” way.

When I try to open up, I feel like I’m oversharing or being weird, so I pull back. Then I get resentful that no one really knows me, even though I’m the one hiding. It’s like I’m pressing my face against the glass of other people’s lives, watching connection happen from the outside.

Nights are the worst. The day is noisy enough to distract me, but when everything gets quiet, the loneliness hits like a physical thing. I put on podcasts or YouTube just to hear voices, but it’s not the same as someone actually choosing to be there with you.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. Maybe I just needed to admit out loud that I’m tired of feeling like a background character in my own life.
