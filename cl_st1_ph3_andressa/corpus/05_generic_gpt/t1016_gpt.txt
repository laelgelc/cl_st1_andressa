I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people on paper. Coworkers, family chats, old school friends in group DMs. My phone has notifications all the time. But I still feel like I’m floating slightly outside of everything, like I’m watching my own life from just behind a glass wall.

I have “conversations,” but they’re mostly about work, memes, logistics, surface-level stuff. No one really asks how I *actually* am, and when they do, I panic and say “I’m fine” because I don’t even know how to start explaining this weird, heavy emptiness without sounding dramatic or broken.

I’ve tried to initiate deeper connections. I invite people out, I check in, I remember their birthdays, I listen. But it feels so one-sided. If I stopped reaching out, I’m honestly not sure who would notice for more than a moment. The thought of that is kind of sickening.

What makes it worse is that I’m not even sure who “the real me” is anymore. I have this version I present to people—competent, mildly funny, “doing okay.” Inside, I feel like a hollow outline of a person, just going through motions.

Nights are the worst. Everything gets quiet and it hits me how little I’m truly known by anyone. And I don’t know how you’re supposed to keep caring about your own life when it feels like you could disappear without really interrupting anything.
