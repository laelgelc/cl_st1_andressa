I’ve been feeling like a ghost in my own life lately.

I technically have “people” around me—coworkers, family I text occasionally, a couple of old friends I talk to every few weeks—but I don’t feel actually known by anyone. Conversations stay on the surface: work, shows, small jokes. No one asks how I’m really doing, and I don’t even know how I’d answer if they did.

What’s weird is that I’m not completely isolated. I’m in group chats, I scroll through social media, I sit in meetings and nod along. But I feel like I’m watching everyone else live from behind glass. They seem to have inside jokes, shared histories, reasons to show up for each other. I just feel… extra. Like if I disappeared, they’d notice for a second and then the group would close around the empty space.

I’ve tried to “put myself out there” more, but I always end up feeling like I’m intruding. I overthink every message I send and usually delete it. When I do speak up, I replay it afterward and cringe at how awkward I sounded.

Most days I just go through the motions: work, home, internet, sleep. It’s a strange kind of emptiness—busy on the outside, hollow on the inside.
