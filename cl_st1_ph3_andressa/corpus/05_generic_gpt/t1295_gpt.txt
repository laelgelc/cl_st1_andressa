I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life without sounding dramatic, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people on paper – coworkers, acquaintances, family I see on holidays – but I still feel like I’m walking around in a glass box. I can see everyone, hear the conversations, laugh at the right times, but there’s this invisible wall where nothing really reaches me and nothing real gets out.

I’ve tried being more “social.” I go to events, say yes to things, force myself to make small talk. It always stops at the surface. I don’t know how to bridge that gap between “we chat sometimes” and “you actually know me.” When I do try to open up a bit, people either change the subject, throw out a cliché, or act uncomfortable. After a while, it starts to feel easier to just shut down again.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. The quiet apartment, the one-sided group chats, the way days blur together because nothing emotionally significant happens. I’m starting to forget what it felt like to be genuinely excited to see someone, or to feel like my presence mattered to them in a specific, personal way.

I’m not sure if I’m doing something wrong, or if this is just how adulthood is for some of us. I just know I’m tired of feeling like a background character in my own life.
