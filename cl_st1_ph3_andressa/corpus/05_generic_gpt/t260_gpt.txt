Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m on the outside of a glass box, watching everyone else live their lives together while I just… observe.

I technically have “people” in my life – coworkers, a couple of old friends I text every few weeks, family chats where we send memes – but I don’t feel known by anyone. Most conversations are surface-level: work, TV, “how was your weekend?” I answer, they answer, and then we both move on like nothing mattered.

What I miss is feeling like someone genuinely wants to hear what’s going on in my head. Not just the sanitized version. The messy, insecure, repetitive stuff. I filter myself so much that when I’m finally alone, I’m not even sure what I actually think or feel anymore.

I try to reach out sometimes, but I overthink every message. “Am I being annoying? Too needy? Too quiet?” So I end up deleting half of what I write and sending something bland. Then I feel even more disconnected because I’ve hidden the parts of me that might actually create a real connection.

It’s strange how you can be “available” all the time online and still feel like there’s nowhere you really belong.
