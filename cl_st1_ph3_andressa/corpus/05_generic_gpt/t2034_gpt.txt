I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and “too much” lately.

I have people in my life on paper—family, coworkers, a couple of casual friends—but I don’t feel *known* by any of them. Conversations stay on the surface: work, shows, random memes. I can laugh and play along, but it feels like I’m acting out a version of myself that’s easier to digest.

When something actually hurts, I freeze up. I either make a joke or say I’m “just tired.” Part of me wants to open up, but another part is terrified that if I really show who I am—how needy, insecure, or awkward I feel—they’ll quietly pull away. So I keep it all inside and then resent them for not reading my mind.

Nights are the worst. I scroll, lurk, maybe send a message and delete it before it’s read. I keep thinking, “If I vanished, who would actually notice and reach out, not just comment ‘oh no’ later?”

It’s strange to feel surrounded by people and still feel like I’m standing behind glass, watching everyone else live.
