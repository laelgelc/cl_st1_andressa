I don’t really know how to talk about this in my “real” life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m surrounded by people and still completely alone. I go to work, make small talk, answer “how are you?” with “fine,” laugh at the right moments, but it all feels like I’m playing a character. As soon as I’m back home and the door closes, it’s just silence and this heavy feeling in my chest.

I have acquaintances, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something was really wrong. I scroll through my contacts and realize most of them are people I haven’t spoken to in months, or only interact with through memes and surface-level conversations. I keep thinking, “If I disappeared, how long would it take anyone to actually notice?”

What bothers me most is how hard it is to be honest without feeling like a burden. When I’ve tried to open up in the past, people either change the subject, give some quick “you’ll be okay” response, or vanish slowly afterwards. It makes me feel like my loneliness is something shameful I should keep hidden.

I’m also starting to forget what it feels like to be genuinely known by someone. Not the curated version of me, but the messy, insecure, awkward parts too. I miss being able to sit with someone in silence and not feel like I’m failing at social interaction.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just needed to say out loud that I’m tired of feeling invisible while pretending I’m not.
