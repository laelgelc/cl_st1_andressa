I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

I’m not completely isolated on paper. I have coworkers I chat with, a family group chat that sends memes, a couple of old friends I text every now and then. But it all feels like I’m playing a role in someone else’s story. I’m there as “the coworker,” “the sibling,” “the old friend,” but I don’t feel like anyone actually knows what’s going on in my head or would notice if I quietly pulled away.

Most days I go through the motions: wake up, work, come home, scroll, sleep. There’s no one I can message just to say “today sucked” without worrying I’m being a burden or too much. When I try to open up, I feel this wall inside me, like I’m suddenly hyper-aware of how I sound and I end up turning everything into a joke or changing the subject. Then I go home and feel resentful that no one “gets” me, even though I’m the one who won’t let them.

I watch people my age building lives: partners, close friend groups, plans, traditions. I feel like I missed the boat somewhere. I’m always the person on the outside of the group photo, the one invited out of politeness, the one people forget to tag. I tell myself it doesn’t matter, but it does. It really does.

What scares me is how used to this I’m getting. The silence after work doesn’t feel shocking anymore; it just feels normal. I’ve started talking out loud to myself in my apartment because it’s the only voice I hear that’s actually directed at me. I catch myself stretching out errands just to be around people, even if we never speak.

I don’t know if I’m lonely because I’m bad at connecting, or I’m bad at connecting because I’ve been lonely for so long. I keep thinking if I were more interesting, more funny, more something, people would naturally stick around. Instead I just feel like background noise in everyone’s life, including my own.

I don’t have a neat takeaway or a plan. I just wanted to put this somewhere where maybe someone will at least recognize the feeling.
