I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

I have people around me—coworkers, acquaintances, family chats that light up my phone—but I don’t feel *with* any of them. It’s like I’m playing a character that says the right things, laughs at the right times, and then goes home and just…powers down. The second the door closes behind me, the silence feels almost physical.

I keep catching myself rehearsing conversations in my head that I’ll never actually have. Imagining telling someone, “I’m really not okay,” and them actually staying, listening, asking questions. In reality, when I hint at feeling off, people either crack a joke, change the subject, or say “same” and move on. I get it, everyone’s busy, but it makes me feel like I’m asking for too much just by wanting to be seen.

What scares me is how normal this is starting to feel. Coming home, scrolling, eating alone, pretending I’m fine, repeating. Days blur together. I’m starting to forget what it felt like to be genuinely excited to see someone, or to have someone be excited to see me.

I don’t want to be “the lonely one” as some kind of identity, but that’s honestly what I feel like now. Like loneliness is this extra layer of skin I can’t peel off, no matter what I do.
