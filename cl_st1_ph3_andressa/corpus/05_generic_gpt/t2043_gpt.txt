I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life without sounding dramatic, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m surrounded by people and still feel completely alone. I have coworkers, a couple of casual friends, family I talk to on holidays. On paper I’m “not isolated,” but none of it feels like it touches the part of me that’s actually lonely. It’s like I’m always just slightly outside the glass, watching everyone else actually connect.

What’s messing with me is how… invisible I feel. I can be in a room with people laughing and talking, and I’m there, nodding, smiling in the right places, but if I stopped existing I’m not sure anything about that room would change. They’d still have their inside jokes, their group chats, their “remember when we…” stories. I’m just the extra.

I try to reach out sometimes, but I overthink every message. “Am I bothering them?” “Did I say something weird?” If they take a while to respond, my brain immediately jumps to, “They don’t actually like you, they’re just being polite.” So I retreat, and of course that just makes the loneliness worse. It’s like I’m stuck between craving closeness and being terrified of it.

Nights are the worst. That’s when everything gets loud in my head. I scroll through social media and see people posting pictures with friends, partners, families, and I know it’s curated and not the full story, but it still feels like there’s this whole part of life I never got the instructions for.

I’m not even sure what I’m asking for here. I just wanted to say this somewhere without having to make eye contact, without someone telling me to “just join a club” or “put yourself out there.” I know all the advice. I just feel too tired and too far behind to believe it applies to me.
