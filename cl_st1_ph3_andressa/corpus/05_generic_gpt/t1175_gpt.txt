I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

Lately I’ve been hit with this really specific kind of loneliness that doesn’t even make sense on paper. I have people around me. I talk to coworkers, I respond in group chats, I send memes, I say “have a good one” to the barista. But it all feels like I’m playing a character who has a social life, while the real me is locked somewhere else.

I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely “seen.” Most of my conversations stay on the surface: work, shows, random news, complaining about being tired. I feel like there’s this entire inner world—my fears, the way my brain spirals at night, the weird little things that make me happy—that no one knows, because I don’t know how to let it out without feeling like I’m too much.

I used to think I just hadn’t found “my people” yet, but I’m starting to wonder if I’m actually the problem. I overthink every interaction. If I message first, I feel annoying. If I don’t, I feel forgotten. When people cancel plans, I act like it’s fine but inside it confirms this idea that I’m always optional, never a priority.

Nights are the worst. I scroll endlessly, half-hoping someone will message me, half-hoping no one does so I have an excuse to just disappear into my phone. I see people posting about game nights, dinners, trips, and it’s like watching life happen from behind glass. I’m there, technically, but not really part of anything.

I’m also weirdly ashamed of feeling this way. I tell myself I’m an adult and I should be able to handle being alone, that needing connection this badly is some kind of weakness. So I act “fine,” and then feel even more disconnected because no one realizes I’m not.

I don’t even know what I’m looking for by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
