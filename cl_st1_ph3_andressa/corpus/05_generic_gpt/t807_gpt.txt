I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly dissolving from everyone’s life and no one has noticed.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I answer emails, I show up where I’m supposed to. I make small talk about weather and TV shows and “how busy things are.” But when I go home, it’s like I unplug from the world and nothing and no one is waiting on the other side.

I scroll through my contacts sometimes and realize how many names are basically fossils. People I used to talk to daily are now just… historical records. We didn’t even have a big falling out. Life just drifted and I didn’t know how to swim after them. Now it feels too awkward to reach out, like I’d be intruding on lives that moved on.

I’m not completely isolated. I have “people around” – coworkers, acquaintances, family members who text on holidays. But I don’t feel known by anyone. It’s like I’m playing a character whose only lines are “I’m good, you?” and “No worries!” and “Yeah, that’s crazy.” I can’t remember the last time someone asked how I really am and actually wanted more than the default answer.

What scares me most is how normal it’s starting to feel. Waking up, going through the motions, eating alone, scrolling, sleeping. Days blur together. If something good happens, I have no one I genuinely want to share it with. If something bad happens, I just sit with it until it shrinks enough to be ignored. There’s no witness to my life besides me.

I also feel this weird shame about being lonely, like it’s proof that I’m defective somehow. Everyone else seems to form friendships, relationships, group chats that actually stay active. I overthink every interaction, convinced I’m too quiet, too boring, too “something” for people to actually want around.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I just needed to put into words that I exist, that I’m here, and that being this alone all the time is starting to feel less like a phase and more like the whole story.
