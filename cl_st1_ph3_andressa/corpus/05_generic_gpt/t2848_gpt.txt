I’m starting to realize that my life is technically “full” of people and still feels completely empty.

On paper, I’m not isolated. I have coworkers I get along with, a family chat that pings all day, and a few old friends I can message if I really need to. But I don’t feel *known* by anyone. It’s like I’m playing a character in every interaction and no one ever meets the person actually running the show inside.

I get invited to things sometimes, but I always feel like the extra chair at the table. People talk to me, laugh with me, but I can’t shake the feeling that if I stopped showing up, nothing would really change for them. The group would just close the gap where I used to sit.

What messes with my head is how invisible the loneliness is. From the outside, I probably look fine. I go to work, answer “I’m good, you?” on autopilot, send memes, show up to birthdays. Inside, I’m constantly thinking, “If I disappeared, who would actually notice the *person* that’s gone, not just the role I filled?”

I’ve tried to open up a few times, but it always feels like I’m making things awkward or being “too much.” People either rush to fix it with platitudes or go quiet because they don’t know what to say. So I end up shrinking back, making my feelings smaller, convincing myself I’m being dramatic.

Most nights I just scroll, watch other people’s lives, and feel like I’m pressing my face against a window. So close to connection, but never really inside. I don’t even know what I’m looking for anymore: a best friend, a partner, or just someone who sees me and doesn’t immediately look away.
