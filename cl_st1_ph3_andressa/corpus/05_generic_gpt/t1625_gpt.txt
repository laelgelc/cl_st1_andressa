I’ve been feeling like a background character in my own life lately.

I go through the motions—work, commute, cook something half-decent, scroll on my phone until my eyes hurt—and somehow never really talk to anyone. I have “people” in my life on paper: coworkers I joke with, family chats that light up sometimes, a couple of old friends who like my posts. But when it comes to actually saying, “I’m lonely,” I just freeze.

What makes it worse is that I used to be more social. Somewhere along the way, everyone paired off or got busy or moved, and I just…didn’t. Now it feels like I missed some invisible deadline for forming real adult connections.

I’m tired of pretending I’m fine with being independent all the time. I’m not. I want someone who notices when I go quiet, who messages first sometimes. I want to feel like someone’s default person, not an afterthought or a backup plan.

Lately I catch myself wondering if this is just how it’s going to be.
