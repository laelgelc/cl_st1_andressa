I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness lately that doesn’t really fit into the usual “I don’t have friends” box. I technically do have people in my life: coworkers I can joke with, a couple of old friends I text every now and then, family chats that send memes. But I still feel like I’m standing behind glass, watching everyone else actually live their lives.

What gets me most is the lack of being known. I can be around people and still feel like I’m completely invisible. Conversations stay on the surface, and I never know how to move them deeper without feeling like I’m being “too much.” I end up playing a role: the listener, the calm one, the “I’m fine, don’t worry about me” person. Then I go home and realize nobody actually knows how tired or disconnected I really am.

I keep thinking about how long it’s been since someone checked in on me first without needing something. Or asked a follow-up question instead of just letting my answer slide away. It makes me wonder if I’m just fundamentally forgettable, like I’m background noise in other people’s lives.

I try to reach out sometimes, but there’s always this fear I’m annoying them, interrupting their “real” relationships. I’ll type out a message and then stare at it, debating whether I’m being weird or needy. Half the time I delete it and tell myself it’s not worth the awkwardness.

The strange part is I don’t even know what I’m hoping for. I don’t have some clear picture of a perfect friend group or relationship. I just know that this constant, quiet ache of not really belonging anywhere is starting to feel less like a phase and more like the default setting of my life.
