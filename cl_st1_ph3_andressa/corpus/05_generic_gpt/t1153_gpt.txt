I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people, even when they’re right in front of me.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I chat with online, but it all feels like I’m playing a role. I say the right things, laugh at the right moments, and then go home and feel like I was never really there. It’s like everyone else is living in color and I’m stuck behind glass, watching.

I try to reach out, but I overthink every message. If someone doesn’t reply quickly, I assume I’m annoying them. So I pull back, then feel even more isolated. It’s this loop I can’t seem to escape.

What scares me most is how normal it’s starting to feel—this quiet, constant emptiness that I can’t explain to anyone in my life.
