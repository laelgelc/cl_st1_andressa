I’m starting to feel like I’ve slipped out of everyone’s periphery vision.

I have people in my life, technically. Coworkers, a couple of old friends I message every so often, family chats that light up on holidays. But it all feels like I’m just orbiting around other people’s real connections, never actually part of the center of anything.

I go entire weekends without speaking out loud to another person. I’ll scroll through my phone, stare at conversations I could start, and then convince myself I’d be bothering them. They have “their people,” and I’m… extra.

What bothers me most is how invisible this feels from the outside. I look “fine.” I function, I work, I smile when I’m supposed to. But I can’t remember the last time I felt genuinely wanted somewhere, not just tolerated.
