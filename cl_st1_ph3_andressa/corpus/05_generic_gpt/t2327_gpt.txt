I’m starting to realize I’m not just “introverted,” I’m actually lonely. My days are a loop: work, commute, screen, sleep. I talk to coworkers about tasks, weather, deadlines, but no one really knows me. I don’t even know how to let them.

I scroll through chats where I’m the one who stopped replying months ago, then feel hurt no one checks in now. When people invite me out, I overthink everything—what to wear, what to say, how awkward I’ll be—and usually bail. Then I sit at home, wishing someone would insist I come anyway.

What scares me is how used to it I’m getting. I can go whole weekends barely speaking out loud. I’m not sure if I miss people, or just miss the version of myself who believed I’d eventually find my place.
