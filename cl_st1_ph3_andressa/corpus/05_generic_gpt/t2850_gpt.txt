I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, I respond to emails, I show up to meetings, I make small talk about weekends and TV shows. People would probably describe me as quiet but “nice.” But when I go home, it’s like someone hits mute on the world. I just sort of… exist in my room, scrolling, eating, sleeping, repeating.

What’s messing with me is that I’m not completely isolated in the literal sense. I have “people” in my life. Old friends in a group chat that’s mostly memes and surface-level stuff. Family who check in every now and then with “how are you?” and I automatically say “good, you?” because that’s the script. Coworkers who invite me to lunch sometimes. I’m not abandoned. I’m just not really seen.

I don’t know how to move conversations beyond the shallow layer without feeling like I’m oversharing or being weird. I don’t know how to say “I feel empty and disconnected most of the time” in a way that doesn’t kill the vibe or make people uncomfortable. So I keep it all inside, and then I feel even more distant because everyone thinks I’m okay.

There’s also this weird shame around being lonely. Like I’m failing at something that everyone else figured out in high school. I watch people talk about their “best friends,” their group trips, game nights, people who know their history and inside jokes and little quirks. I can’t think of a single person who knows me that deeply. I’m not sure I even know myself that deeply anymore.

I’m tired of my own company, but I’m also scared of being rejected if I try to reach out more. So I stay stuck in this in-between space: surrounded by people, but somehow alone in every room I walk into.
