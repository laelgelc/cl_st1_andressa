I’ve been feeling this weird mix of invisible and too visible at the same time.

I’m almost always around people – coworkers, classmates, family – but it’s like I’m just… there. I listen, nod, laugh at the right times, make small talk about work or the weather or some show everyone’s watching. But when the conversation ends, I walk away with this hollow feeling, like nothing about me actually reached anyone.

I don’t really have “my person.” No one I can text randomly without overthinking it. No one who’d notice if I went silent for a while and actually ask what’s wrong, not just “you good?” in passing. I’m that person people come to when they need advice or a favor, but when I need something deeper than “how’s it going,” I freeze up.

I’m scared of being “too much” if I open up, and at the same time I feel like I’m “not enough” to be worth sticking around for. So I end up editing myself constantly – making sure I’m easy to be around, not complaining, not asking for too much. Then I go home and wonder why no one really knows me.

Nights are the worst. I lie there scrolling, seeing everyone else’s group chats, trips, inside jokes. I know social media isn’t real life, but it still hits. I keep thinking, if I disappeared from these people’s lives, would anything actually change for them in a real way?

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe just to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
