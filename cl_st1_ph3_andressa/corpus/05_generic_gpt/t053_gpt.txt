I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness that doesn’t really fit into any of the usual boxes. I have friends, technically. Group chats, people I can text, coworkers I joke with. But most of it feels like I’m playing a role I learned a long time ago, not actually being myself.

When I try to open up, I either feel like I’m oversharing or like I’m boring the other person. I watch their face, their typing pauses, and immediately start editing myself down. Then afterward I replay the whole thing and feel stupid for even trying. So I end up keeping things on the surface, then resent that all my connections feel shallow. It’s like I’m the one locking the door and then complaining I’m stuck inside.

What really messes with me is how invisible this feels. No one sees it because I’m “fine” on the outside. I show up, I laugh at jokes, I do my work. Meanwhile there’s this constant background noise of “no one actually knows you, and if they did, they’d leave.”

I don’t even know what I’m asking for by writing this. Maybe I just wanted to put it somewhere that isn’t my own head for once.
