I’m starting to realize that I don’t actually remember what it feels like to feel “included,” not just present. I have people around me – coworkers, acquaintances, family I text sometimes – but I still feel like I’m watching everyone else live from behind glass.

I’m in my late 20s and it feels like everyone else somehow got the manual on how to form real, lasting connections. They have group chats that are actually active, people who invite them places without being prompted, inside jokes that stretch back years. I have a handful of scattered interactions that never seem to grow into anything more. We’ll talk for a bit, maybe hang out once or twice, and then it just fades. I’m never the person anyone thinks of first.

What hurts most is how invisible it feels. I can disappear from social media, from group chats, from mutual spaces, and nothing changes. No one checks in. It’s like I only exist when I’m physically in front of someone, and as soon as I’m not, I’m erased.

I try to put myself out there – hobbies, events, even apps meant for making friends – but I always end up feeling like an extra in someone else’s story. They already have their “core group”; I’m just the temporary addition until their real friends show up.

I also notice I’ve started to censor myself a lot. I worry that if I show too much of who I am, it’ll push people away, but hiding parts of myself just makes me feel more disconnected. It’s like I’m lonely even in conversations because the version of me that’s talking isn’t fully real.

I don’t know if this is just how adulthood is for some people, or if there’s something fundamentally off about me that I can’t see. I just know I’m tired of feeling like a background character in my own life.
