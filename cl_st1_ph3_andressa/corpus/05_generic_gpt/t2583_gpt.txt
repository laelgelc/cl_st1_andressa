I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing while still technically being here.

On paper, my life isn’t terrible. I have a job, I pay my bills, I answer emails, I say “good weekend?” on Mondays and “have a good one” on Fridays. I have people I can message if I really need something. But I don’t feel like I actually exist in anyone’s mind when I’m not physically in front of them or useful for something.

What’s been getting to me is the quiet in between. I’ll go the whole weekend without my phone lighting up unless it’s spam or some app notification. I’ll scroll and see everyone talking to someone, tagging friends, making plans, and I’m just… there, watching. I don’t even know who I’d tag if I wanted to be that person.

I’ve tried to “fix” it in the usual ways. Joined a couple of group chats, tried to be more responsive, said yes to more social things. But I always feel like an extra. People are kind enough, but I can see the difference between how they talk to their actual friends and how they talk to me. I’m filler. A backup plan. The person you invite when you need to even out the numbers.

What hurts the most is that I used to have people. Not a ton, but enough. Late-night calls, stupid inside jokes, that feeling of being woven into someone else’s life. Somewhere along the way, everyone drifted into relationships, careers, families, and I just… didn’t. It wasn’t one big fight or a dramatic falling out. Just a lot of unanswered “we should catch up soon” messages that never turned into anything.

I keep wondering if I did something wrong, or if this is just how adulthood is for some of us. The thought that this might just be it—working, coming home, eating alone, going to bed, repeating—makes my chest feel heavy in a way I don’t really know how to talk about offline.

Anyway, I don’t have a neat conclusion to this. I just needed to put it somewhere.
