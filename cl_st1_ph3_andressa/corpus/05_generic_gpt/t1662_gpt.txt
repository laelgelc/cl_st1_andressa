I’m starting to realize that most of my “social life” exists in my head.

I replay old conversations, imagine new ones, think of jokes I’ll never say out loud. Meanwhile, in real life, days go by where the only words I say are “thank you” to a cashier or “have a good one” to a delivery guy. It’s like I’m a ghost that can interact just enough to function, but not enough to actually connect.

I scroll through chats where the last message is always from me, months old. I hover over people’s names, thinking about sending something, then convince myself they’re too busy or that I’d just be annoying. Then I feel worse because I’ve basically talked myself out of my own chances.

What makes it confusing is that I don’t *look* lonely from the outside. I go to work, I answer emails, I nod in meetings. But there’s this constant sense that nobody really knows me, and maybe nobody wants to.

Sometimes I wonder if I’ve already missed my window to build real friendships, and now I’m just watching everyone else live from the sidelines.
