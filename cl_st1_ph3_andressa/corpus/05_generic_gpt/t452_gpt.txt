I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer messages, I show up when I’m supposed to. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice.” I can make small talk, crack a joke, ask about someone’s weekend. But it all feels like I’m playing a character I don’t fully believe in.

The weirdest part is that I’m not completely isolated. I have acquaintances, a group chat, coworkers who like me well enough. Yet I feel completely disconnected from everyone. I can be in a room full of people and feel like I’m not really there, like I’m watching them from a few feet outside my own body.

I crave deeper conversations, the kind where you can say “I’m not okay” and not feel like you’re ruining the vibe. But every time I get close to opening up, I panic and default back to “I’m good, just tired.” I’m scared of being too much, or of being met with that blank look that says, “I don’t know what to do with this.”

It’s also starting to mess with how I see myself. When nobody really knows you, it’s hard to know who you are. I feel like I’m fading into this vague, background version of a person. I go home, scroll, watch something, sleep, repeat. Days blur together.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. Maybe I just wanted to put it somewhere outside my own head for once.
