I don’t really know how to explain this to people in my life, so I’m dumping it here instead.

I’m not “isolated” in the obvious way. I have coworkers, a couple of old friends I still text sometimes, family group chats that light up on holidays. On paper it looks like I’m connected. But I feel like I’m living behind glass. I can see everyone, hear them, even laugh with them, but I can’t actually reach them.

Most of my days are a loop: wake up, work, come home, scroll, sleep. I’ll go the entire day without anyone saying my name out loud unless it’s for something practical. No one really asks follow-up questions when I say I’m “tired” or “fine.” I’ve started to realize how easy it is to disappear without actually going anywhere.

What really messes with me is that I don’t know how to fix it. I’m not great at small talk, and when I do get invited somewhere I feel like a guest actor in other people’s lives. I’m there, but I’m not part of the main story. I watch groups who clearly have shared history, inside jokes, that ease with each other, and I feel like I’m pressing my face up against a window.

I keep thinking about how long it’s been since someone really knew what was going on with me. Not the surface-level stuff, but the actual fears, the random thoughts, the things I care about. I can’t tell if I’ve become boring, or if I just never learned how to let people see anything real without feeling like I’m burdening them.

Anyway, that’s where I’m at.
