I’m starting to realize I don’t actually know how to connect with people anymore, or maybe I never did.

I have acquaintances, coworkers, people I joke around with, but no one I’d call at 2 a.m. if something really bad happened. When I scroll through my contacts, there isn’t a single person I feel comfortable texting just to say, “I’m not okay.” It’s like I exist on the edge of everyone’s life, close enough to be visible but not close enough to matter.

What makes it worse is that from the outside I probably look fine. I show up, I smile, I make small talk. People would be surprised if I said I feel lonely almost all the time. I’ve tried joining groups, going to events, being “more social,” but it all feels like acting. I don’t know how to move from surface-level conversations to anything real without feeling like I’m being too much.

Sometimes I wonder if I missed some crucial stage where everyone else learned how to form deep friendships and I just…didn’t. It’s a strange feeling: being surrounded by people and still feeling completely alone.
