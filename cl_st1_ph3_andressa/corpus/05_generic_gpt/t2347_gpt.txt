I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I answer emails, I show up to meetings, I say “not bad, you?” when people ask how I am. I even laugh at the right moments. But as soon as I’m alone again, it’s like someone turns the volume down on the world and I’m just… there. Not sad in the crying way, more like this dull ache that never leaves.

What gets me is that I’m not completely isolated. I have people I text sometimes, coworkers I chat with, family I see on holidays. But none of it feels like connection. It’s like standing in a room full of people and watching them through glass. I can see them, hear them, but I can’t seem to step through and actually be with them.

I overthink every interaction. I replay conversations and convince myself I was awkward, boring, too quiet, too much. I cancel plans because I’m exhausted, then sit at home wishing I’d gone. I scroll through social media and see everyone else doing things “together” and I feel like I missed a class where they taught how to be a person people naturally keep around.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for. Not some big friend group or constant social life, just one or two people I can message without planning it for hours in my head first. Someone who’d notice if I went quiet.

Most days I just go through the motions and tell myself this is just a phase. But it’s starting to feel less like a phase and more like the default setting. And that thought sits with me a lot longer than it probably should.
