I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing in slow motion.

On paper, I’m “fine.” I have a job, an apartment, people I can technically text. But most days I go from morning to night without having a real conversation with anyone. Just surface-level stuff: “How was your weekend?” “Good, you?” and then silence. It’s like I’m surrounded by people but somehow not actually *with* any of them.

The part that messes with my head is that I used to think loneliness was just “having no friends,” but it’s not that simple. I have people I could message, but I feel weirdly out of sync with everyone. They’re busy with partners, families, group chats I’m not really part of, lives that seem to be moving forward while mine just… loops. I hesitate to reach out because I don’t want to be the person who only messages when they’re lonely. So I wait for someone else to start, and they don’t, and then I take that as proof that I don’t really matter that much.

I’ve also noticed I’m starting to forget how to be around people. When I do hang out with someone, I overthink everything: Am I talking too much? Too little? Do I sound boring? Are they just being polite? I replay the interaction afterward and cringe at random things I said. It makes me want to retreat further, which obviously just makes the loneliness worse.

Even small things hit harder now. Seeing people laughing together in public, coworkers talking about weekend plans, couples arguing over grocery lists. It all feels like a world I’m watching through glass. Close enough to see, but not something I’m actually part of.

I don’t really have a point or a solution. I just wanted to put this somewhere, to admit that I feel invisible most of the time and I don’t know how to fix it without feeling like I’m begging for attention.
