I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m living next to my own life instead of in it.

On paper I’m “fine” – job, roof over my head, a couple of people I’d technically call friends. But it all feels like background noise. I show up, I do the expected things, I laugh in the right places, and then I go home and it’s like someone turned the sound off. I scroll, I eat something, I stare at the ceiling. The only time I feel anything sharp is when I realize how replaceable I am in everyone’s day.

I keep trying to figure out what’s wrong with me socially. I can hold a conversation, but I never seem to move past “acquaintance” territory. People will tell me I’m “easy to talk to,” but no one actually chooses me when it matters. I’m the person who gets invited if someone else cancels, or if it’s a big group thing and they’re just throwing the invite around.

What really messes with my head is how invisible this all is from the outside. If you looked at my social media, you’d think I had a decent life. There are photos with people, with smiles. But I’ve never had that feeling of being someone’s first thought. No “hey, I saw this and it reminded me of you” texts. No one checking in just because.

I’m starting to worry this is just how it’s going to be: physically present, emotionally peripheral. Like I’m a side character in every room I walk into, including my own apartment.
