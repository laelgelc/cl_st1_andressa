I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing from my own life.

On paper I’m “fine.” I have a job, I pay rent, I reply to emails, I make small talk about weather and TV shows. People would probably describe me as “quiet but nice.” What they don’t see is how much of my day is just… silence. Not peaceful silence, but the kind that hums in your ears and makes you aware of how alone you are.

I wake up, scroll my phone, see other people’s group photos, inside jokes, “can’t believe we’ve been friends for 10 years” posts, and I can’t even think of one person I’d feel comfortable texting, “Hey, want to hang out?” without worrying I’m bothering them. I have acquaintances, but no one I feel like I really belong with. I’m always the extra person, never the person someone thinks of first.

Sometimes I realize I haven’t heard my own voice out loud all day, except for “thank you” to a cashier or a quick “have a good one” to a coworker. When I get home, it’s just me, my phone, and the sound of my fridge. I eat alone, I watch shows alone, I go to sleep with podcasts on just so there’s some kind of human sound in the background.

What messes with my head is that I don’t even know how I got here. I had friends in school. I used to be invited places. Then life happened, people moved, I moved, work got busy, and somehow the gap never closed again. Now the idea of trying to make friends as an adult feels exhausting and humiliating. I feel like I missed some invisible deadline where everyone else formed their circles and I just… didn’t.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by writing this. I guess I just wanted to put into words that I exist, that I’m here, that I’m lonely in a way that feels like it’s eating through the walls of my life. I don’t really talk about this anywhere else.
