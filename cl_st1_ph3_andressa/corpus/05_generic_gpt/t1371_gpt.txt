I’ve been feeling this weird, heavy kind of loneliness that doesn’t quite fit into any simple box. It’s not just “I don’t have friends” loneliness, because technically I do. I have people I can text, a few I can meet for coffee, coworkers I can joke around with. But I still feel like I’m at arm’s length from everyone, like there’s a glass wall between me and the rest of the world.

I scroll through group chats and see conversations I wasn’t part of, inside jokes I don’t get. When I do meet up with people, I often feel like I’m performing a version of myself that’s easier for them to handle—less intense, less honest, more agreeable. I leave, go home, and feel more drained and disconnected than before I saw them.

The worst part is I don’t even know what I’m missing exactly. I say I want “real connection,” but what does that even look like in practice? I imagine having someone I can message without overthinking every word, someone who actually notices when I go quiet, who doesn’t just assume I’m busy and move on. But when chances come up to get closer to people, I freeze. I hold back, I make myself small, then later I complain to myself that no one really knows me.

It’s like I’m stuck between wanting to be seen and being terrified of it. If I open up, I worry I’ll be too much, or boring, or just… disappointing. If I stay quiet, I feel invisible. Either way, I end up alone with my thoughts, replaying conversations and wondering what’s wrong with me that I can’t seem to connect the way other people do.

I don’t know if this is just how adulthood works now, or if I’m doing something fundamentally wrong. I keep hoping it’ll “click” at some point, that I’ll stumble into a friendship or relationship where it feels natural and safe, but so far it just feels like I’m watching life happen through a window I can’t open.
