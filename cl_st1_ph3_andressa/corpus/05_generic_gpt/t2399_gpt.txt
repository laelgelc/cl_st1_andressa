I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like I’m disappearing in plain sight.

My days are technically “full” — work, errands, scrolling, sleeping — but I don’t feel connected to any of it. I talk to coworkers, I answer emails, I say “have a good one” to the barista. On paper, that’s interaction. In reality, it’s like I’m just an NPC in everyone else’s story.

What gets me is how invisible this kind of loneliness is. From the outside, I probably look fine. I show up, I smile, I make small talk. I even crack jokes sometimes. But there’s this constant background noise in my head: no one really knows you, and no one really needs to.

I miss having people who actually check in, not out of obligation, but because they’re genuinely curious about how I’m doing. I miss the kind of conversations where you lose track of time and suddenly it’s 2 a.m. and you’ve overshared half your life but feel lighter instead of stupid.

Now I overthink every interaction. If someone doesn’t reply, I assume I’m annoying. If they do reply, I convince myself they’re just being polite. I’ve pulled back from reaching out because I’m tired of feeling like I’m always the one initiating and then worrying I’ve pushed too hard.

It’s not like something terrible happened. It’s more like a slow erosion — friendships fading, routines changing, people moving on. And I’m still here, stuck between wanting to be seen and not wanting to burden anyone with the fact that I feel this empty.
