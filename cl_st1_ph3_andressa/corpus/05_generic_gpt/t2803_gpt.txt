I’m in that weird place where I’m not technically “alone,” but I feel completely disconnected from everyone around me.

I have coworkers, acquaintances, people I can send memes to. But there’s no one I feel I can message at 2am and say, “I’m not okay, can you just talk to me for a bit?” I scroll through my contacts and realize most conversations are shallow or one-sided. I’m always the one reaching out, and when I stop, everything just goes quiet.

I’ve tried joining clubs, going to events, making small talk. It all feels like speed‑running the same surface-level script: “What do you do?” “Where are you from?” Then we never speak again. I leave feeling more invisible than before.

What bothers me most is how embarrassing it feels to admit I’m lonely. Like it’s some personal failure, proof that I’m unlikeable or defective. So I keep pretending I’m fine, while this heavy, constant emptiness just sits there, taking up more and more space inside my life.
