I don’t really know how to talk about this in real life, so I’m dumping it here.

Lately I’ve been feeling like I’m slowly fading out of everyone’s life, including my own. I’m “around” people pretty often — work, family chats, group texts — but I still feel completely disconnected, like I’m watching my own life through a glass wall.

I have people I could technically message, but I overthink every word. I’ll type something, delete it, retype, then decide I’m being annoying and just close the app. Then I sit there staring at the empty screen, feeling stupid for wanting connection so badly. I keep telling myself, “If they wanted to talk to you, they would,” and the silence kind of proves that to me over and over.

The weirdest part is the contrast: people say I seem “put together,” “calm,” even “funny.” No one sees that I go home and just lie on my bed scrolling until my eyes hurt because I don’t know what else to do with myself. I eat alone, I watch shows alone, I go to sleep alone, and it all blends into this gray routine that feels less like living and more like waiting.

There’s this heavy feeling when I wake up in the morning, like I’m already behind on something I can’t name. I’m tired of my own company, but I also feel like I’m not really someone others would choose to be around. I’m not interesting enough, not attractive enough, not anything enough.

I don’t even know what I’m hoping for by posting this. I guess I just wanted to put into words that I feel invisible, even to myself, and it’s starting to scare me how normal that’s beginning to feel.
