I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but I feel like I’m slowly disappearing from my own life.

On paper I’m “fine.” I go to work, I answer emails, I make small talk about the weather or TV shows I don’t watch. People would probably describe me as quiet but normal. What they don’t see is that as soon as I’m alone again, it’s like the volume gets turned down on the whole world and I’m just… there. Not sad enough to cry, not busy enough to be distracted, just existing in this weird, heavy silence.

I have “friends,” but they’re more like people I used to be close to. We text on birthdays or send each other memes once in a while, but I can feel the distance. I hesitate before messaging anyone because it feels like I’m intruding on their real lives—lives with partners, kids, plans, group chats I’m not in. I’m stuck in this loop of wanting to reach out and then convincing myself they’re only being polite when they respond.

The worst part is how lonely I feel even when I’m around others. I can be sitting at a table full of people and still feel like I’m watching everything through glass. I laugh at the right times, nod along, but it all feels like acting. No one really knows what my days look like, what I think about when I can’t sleep, or how long it’s been since someone genuinely asked how I’m doing and waited for a real answer.

I keep hoping something will change on its own, but most nights it’s just me, my phone, and the same quiet room.
