I don’t really know how to explain this without sounding dramatic, but lately it feels like my life is happening behind glass.

I’m surrounded by people on paper. I have coworkers I chat with, a family group chat that pings occasionally, a few old friends I exchange memes with. But when I stop moving, when I go home and close the door, it’s like everything just…drops off a cliff. No one is really “there.” No one is checking in because they actually want to know how I’m doing. And I don’t really reach out either, because I don’t know where to even start.

What bothers me most is that I can’t point to a single big reason. Nothing “bad” is happening. I get up, go to work, come back, scroll, watch something, sleep. Repeat. I laugh at things, I function, I get stuff done. But under all of that there’s this constant, low-level ache that I don’t belong anywhere in particular. Like I’m just a background character in everyone else’s life.

I’ve tried the usual advice: joining clubs, messaging people, saying yes more often. I can be sociable in short bursts. People seem to like me well enough, but it never goes deeper. The conversation stays on the surface and then fades, like I’m forgettable once I’m not physically in front of them.

There’s also this weird shame attached to all of it. I feel embarrassed that I’m this lonely as an adult. It makes me feel defective, like there’s some invisible rulebook everyone else got about how to form close connections, and I missed that day.

I think what I want is stupidly simple: to feel like someone is actually glad I exist, not just “Oh, you’re nice to have around when convenient.” To have a person I could text without overthinking every word, without worrying I’m being a burden or too much.

Right now it just feels like I’m drifting, and nobody would really notice if I quietly floated off.
