I grew up moving around a lot – different cities, different countries, constantly changing schools and even universities. I was “the new kid” so many times that it just became part of my identity. I’d make friends, sometimes pretty quickly, but almost every time, as soon as I moved again, those friendships would slowly fade away.

I’ve realized that for me, it’s always been easier to maintain relationships with people I see in person regularly. Once it requires effort to keep in touch online, it turns into this “out of sight, out of mind” thing. Not in a cold or intentional way, just… life moves on, and we drift. After years of this, it feels like my brain has internalized the idea that people are temporary.

What’s weird is that I’ve now been in the same place for about five years. No big moves, no new schools. But my relationships still feel like they’re on a short timer. I make acquaintances, have surface-level friendships, maybe a few months of hanging out here and there, and then it fizzles out. I struggle to find the emotional energy or even the desire to deepen those connections or to start new ones.

I keep wondering if this is some kind of subconscious self-protection. Like, if I don’t get too attached, it won’t hurt when things inevitably end. Or maybe I just genuinely believe, deep down, that people don’t stay, so why invest?

The result is that I feel really unmoored and lonely. I want deep connections, the kind where you feel known and rooted somewhere, but my habits and mindset seem to be working against that.

Has anyone else gone through something similar, especially if you moved a lot growing up? Did you manage to change this pattern, and if so, how? What helped you find the energy or courage to actually invest in people again?
