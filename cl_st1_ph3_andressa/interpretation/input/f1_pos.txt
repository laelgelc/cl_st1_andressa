Days blur together and I’m starting to realize most of my “interactions” are with screens, not people.

I technically have people in my life. Coworkers I chat with, a family group chat where we send memes, a couple of old friends I text every once in a while. On paper, that probably looks fine. But the reality is that everything feels surface-level, like I’m playing a character who’s “doing okay” instead of being honest about how hollow I feel.

I’ll have moments where I want to message someone and say, “I’m lonely. Can we just talk about nothing for a bit?” But I always end up deleting it because I don’t want to sound needy or make things awkward. I overthink every possible response they could have and convince myself I’m burdening them. So I stay quiet, scroll, watch videos, listen to podcasts—anything to fill the silence without actually breaking it.

What makes it worse is how disconnected I feel even when I’m around people. I’ll be in a group conversation, laughing at the right times, making jokes, and still feel like I’m standing behind glass, watching it all happen. Everyone else seems to have these natural, effortless bonds, and I’m just… adjacent to them.

I miss feeling known. Not just “how’s work?” known, but the kind of known where someone notices when you’re off, or remembers something small you said weeks ago. I don’t really have that right now. I don’t know if it’s because I’ve pulled away over time, or because I never really had it to begin with.

Most days I just go through the motions: wake up, work, come home, distract myself until I’m tired enough to sleep. It’s functional. It’s fine, technically. But it also feels like I’m slowly fading into the background of my own life.
