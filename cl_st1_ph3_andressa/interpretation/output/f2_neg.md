Interpretation of Factor f2 (negative pole)

1. Which group drives this pole?

- Mean scores:
  - generic_gpt: **-0.48**
  - human: **+0.78**
  - summary_guided_gpt: **+0.51**

The **negative pole** of f2 is clearly driven by the **generic GPT posts**. Human and summary‑guided GPT texts cluster on the positive side, so this pole is characterising a *distinctively generic‑GPT discourse style* rather than the dominant pattern of human self‑disclosure.

So: f2_neg is best understood as a **LLM‑generic, stylised discourse of loneliness**, not the core human pattern.

2. What do the loadings tell us?

Negative loadings (strongest first):

- script (-1.04)
- gap (-0.71)
- noise (-0.61)
- role (-0.52)
- errand (-0.51)

These cluster semantically into:

- **Staged / theatrical interaction**: script, role, conversation  
- **Background / low‑salience presence**: noise, people, acquaintance  
- **Disconnection / distance metaphors**: gap, window, glass  
- **Routine / going through motions**: errand  
- **Internal state packaging**: mix, emptiness  

This is a lexicon of **performed, scripted sociality and perceived distance**, with life framed as routine “errands” and social contact as “background noise” behind a pane of “glass”.

3. What do the examples add?

All 10 high‑scoring texts are **generic_gpt**. They consistently:

- Use **highly conventional metaphors**:
  - “background noise”
  - “conversations feel scripted”
  - “playing a role”
  - “watching life through a window / behind glass”
  - “weird mix of invisible and too visible”
  - “emptiness gets louder”

- Emphasise **surface social contact vs. lack of depth**:
  - “I’m surrounded by people… but I feel like a ghost”
  - “coworkers, small talk… but it all feels like background noise”
  - “friends are more like acquaintances”
  - “I’m never the person anyone thinks to call first”

- Highlight **routine and going through motions**:
  - “work, errands, scrolling, sleep”
  - “I go through the motions—work, errands, scrolling on my phone”

- Frame self in **performance terms**:
  - “actors reading from a script”
  - “auditioning for a role I’m not quite right for”
  - “playing a role I never auditioned for / never learned properly”

- Use **meta‑emotional packaging**:
  - “this weird mix of invisible and ‘too much’”
  - “weird mix of invisible and too visible”
  - “weird mix of being visible and utterly unseen”

The discourse is **highly generalised, metaphor‑heavy, and stylised**, with relatively little concrete, idiosyncratic detail. It reads like a *template* for describing loneliness in modern life.

4. Discourse characteristics of this pole

Putting loadings and examples together, this pole encodes a discourse that can be characterised as:

- **Theatrical / scripted social life**: Social interaction is framed as performance (“script”, “role”), not authentic engagement.
- **Ambient, impersonal presence of others**: Others are “background noise”, “people”, “acquaintances” rather than close ties.
- **Mediated distance**: The self is behind “glass”, looking through a “window”, separated by a “gap” that cannot be bridged.
- **Routine, de‑personalised daily life**: Life is “work, errands, scrolling, sleep” – a sequence of errands and motions rather than meaningful events.
- **Packaged emotional states**: Feelings are summarised as “a weird mix” and “emptiness”, often in formulaic constructions.

Crucially, this is not just “loneliness” in general; it is **a particular, stylised way of narrating loneliness**, strongly associated with the generic GPT condition.

5. Possible labels for this pole

Any label should capture both the *content* (loneliness as scripted, distant, backgrounded) and the *style* (generic, metaphor‑driven, performance framing). Here are several plausible labels, with justification:

1. **“Scripted Social Disconnection”**

   - Captures:
     - script, role, conversation → “scripted social”
     - gap, window, glass, noise, acquaintance → “disconnection”
   - Fits the examples where life is “background noise” and interactions are “actors reading from a script”.

2. **“Performed Loneliness in Routine Life”**

   - “Performed” → script, role, auditioning
   - “Loneliness” → emptiness, invisible, ghost, no one to call
   - “Routine life” → errand, going through the motions, work/errands/scrolling
   - Highlights that loneliness is narrated as something enacted within a repetitive, errand‑like everyday.

3. **“Background‑Noise Existence”**

   - Directly echoes the recurrent metaphor “background noise”.
   - Emphasises the sense of being present but low‑salience, replaceable, not foregrounded in anyone’s life.
   - Supported by noise, people, acquaintance, emptiness.

4. **“Behind‑Glass Social Distance”**

   - Draws on window, glass, gap.
   - Captures the “watching life through a window / behind glass” imagery.
   - Emphasises the felt barrier between self and others despite physical proximity.

6. Recommended primary label

If one label is needed, I would prioritise:

**Scripted Social Disconnection**

Because it:

- Directly reflects the **strongest loading** (“script”) and the cluster with “role”, “conversation”.
- Encodes the **central experiential theme**: being surrounded by people but only in a scripted, background‑noise way, with a persistent “gap” and “emptiness”.
- Is clearly **driven by generic GPT texts**, marking a distinctive LLM‑generic discourse style where loneliness is narrated through theatrical and background‑noise metaphors rather than through highly specific, situated detail.

In sum, the negative pole of f2 represents a **generic‑GPT, metaphorically “scripted” account of loneliness**, where life is reduced to errands and background noise, and the self is cast as a performer behind glass, disconnected from authentic social intimacy.
